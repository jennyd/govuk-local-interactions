What to do if you think your Housing Benefit calculation is wrong

About our decisions

We want to be sure you are receiving the right amount of Housing Benefit. When we work it out you will be sent a decision letter which will show the information we've used to assess your claim. You should check the letter carefully and let us know if anything is incorrect or has been missed. There will also be a leaflet enclosed explaining how to read decision letters.

If you do not understand our decision letter, or want to know more, you should contact us for an explanation.

You also have the right to ask for a full statement of reasons explaining what we have done. You need to ask for this in writing within one calendar month of the date on our decision letter.

What if you think our decision is wrong?

If you think we may have made a mistake, you can ask for more information to help you decide.

You can also ask us to:

  • look at your claim again to reconsider your entitlement
  • appeal against our decision to an independent tribunal

You must put either of these requests in writing, stating clearly why you think the decision is wrong. You can send us additional evidence to help us review your claim, or we may ask you for additional evidence.

If you have asked for a review of your claim and still disagree with our decision, you can then appeal. You need to do this in writing, at which point we will look at our decision once again before writing to the Tribunal Service.

Who can appeal?

Only the person affected by our decision can appeal. This includes:

  • you (the person making the claim)
  • someone acting on your behalf (chosen by the courts)
  • someone we agree can act on your behalf
  • your landlord or agent - but only for certain decisions (see below)
  • anybody we have asked to repay an overpayment

Landlords and appeals

If your Housing Benefit is being paid directly to a landlord, they can only appeal against the following:

  • our decision not to pay benefit directly to them
  • our request for them to pay back any benefit we've overpaid you

A landlord cannot appeal against the amount of benefit we give a tenant.

Decisions you can't appeal against

You can ask us to look again at any decision you think is wrong.

There are some decisions about which you cannot lodge a formal appeal with us, for example:

  • what information and proof you need to give us
  • how we pay your benefit and how often it is paid
  • suspending or restoring benefit
  • which partner in a couple should be the claimant
  • paying outstanding benefit after a death
  • the capital limit or the tariff income rates
  • the 'Assessed Income Figure' (you would need to appeal to the Pension Service)
  • our right to recover an overpayment
  • how we recover the amount we have overpaid
  • if we will not look at our decision again because you asked us too late

You cannot appeal against the Rent Service's decision or the Local Housing Allowance Rates, but you can ask the Rent Service to look at its decision again. Contact us for advice on how to do this.

What happens when we look at a decision again?

The matter will be looked at by a different officer to the one who made the original decision.

If we change our decision - we will send you a letter explaining our new decision. You still have the right to ask us to look at this decision again or to appeal against this new decision.

If we do not change our decision - we will send you a letter explaining the reasons why.

If you still think our decision is wrong, you have one calendar month to appeal to the Social Security Appeals Tribunal for Housing Benefit, which you should do via us.

How do I appeal?

For Housing Benefit, if you are not happy with the decision we have made, you can appeal to The Social Security Appeals Tribunal Service - a body which is independent from us.

You need to put this in writing to the Council, clearly explaining your reasons. This needs to be done within one calendar month of our decision letter.

In accordance with tribunal rules, your appeal must include the following:

  • your name and address
  • the name and address of your representative (if any)
  • an address where documents can be sent to you
  • details of the decision being appealed
  • your grounds for your appeal (i.e. why you disagree with our decision)
  • your signature

We will collect all the details and evidence and the reasons behind our decision(s) and send it on to the appeals service within three months. We will also send a copy to you.

The appeals service will contact you with the date they will hear your case.
