Community transport

Community Transport (CT) plays an important role in the county through a network of community transport schemes which provide access to services and social opportunities for those unable to use public transport.

Every parish in Herefordshire is served by a community transport scheme, based in the voluntary sector and supported by Herefordshire Council.

CT schemes provide a pre-booked, door-to-door transport service to help you get to local services, visit friends and enjoy a range of leisure activities.

Who can use community transport?

Community transport (CT) provides transport if you are unable to use public transport services because:

  • No public transport service is available
  • No alternative transport is available at the time you need to travel
  • You have limited mobility, which prevents you from using bus or train services

It can be used by:

  • Individuals or groups
  • Able bodied people as well as those with limited mobility and wheelchair users

There are schemes covering the whole of the county.

How can I volunteer for a community transport scheme?

Volunteer drivers are the lifeblood of Herefordshire's community transport schemes which offer an affordable door-to-door solution for anyone who is unable to use public transport.

The schemes provide transport for those who are without transport or access to public transport, enabling them to get to essential services like doctors and hospitals as well as social and learning events.

What community transport volunteers do

Some volunteer drivers use their own cars, and are paid a mileage rate to cover the petrol and running costs of their vehicle. Others drive scheme-owned minibuses and wheelchair-accessible vehicles, for which full training is given. If you can't drive, we need volunteers to help out in our busy booking offices.

By becoming a volunteer you can:

  • Volunteer as much or as little time as you want
  • Help people get out and about in their communities
  • Meet new people, learn new skills, and make new friends

If you're unemployed, you won't lose your benefit by volunteering.

Contact

All of Herefordshire's community transport schemes need more volunteers.

If you are interested in volunteering, or want to find out more about using the service, please contact your local CT scheme.

I live in a rural area with no bus service. What transport options are there?

Every parish in Herefordshire is served by a community transport scheme, based in the voluntary sector and supported by the council.

These schemes can provide pre-booked transport for those unable to use public transport or where such transport is unavailable. Passengers pay a contribution toward the cost of the journey, which is also subsidised by the Herefordshire Council.

Schemes are able to provide transport services for wheelchair users and those with limited mobility.

View the list of community transport schemes operating in Herefordshire

How do I book community transport?

To arrange community transport, please:

Find the community transport scheme which covers your area

  • Check there is no other public transport available or which you are able to use
  • Register and book your journey with at least 48 hours' notice - check booking office opening hours with the particular scheme

How can I hire a minibus for my community group?

hvoss (Herefordshire Voluntary Organisations Support Service) is operating a minibus sharing scheme throughout Herefordshire called MLS (Minibus Link Scheme).

Not-for-profit organisations with minibuses and MPVs lend out their vehicles when not needed to not-for-profit organisations requiring transport - the cost of the loan covering the running costs of the vehicle and administration of the loan.

This scheme is available to all organisations such as community and voluntary organisations, uniformed youth groups, youth and sports clubs, schools and colleges who properly constitute not-for-profit.

If you would like to be a part of the scheme visit the Minibus Link Scheme page of the hvoss website, email mls@hvoss.org.uk or phone 01432 343932.
