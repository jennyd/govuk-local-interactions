Electoral Register

Registering to Vote

You must be:

  • Over 16 (although you cannot vote until your 18th birthday)
  • A British or Commonwealth citizen
  • A citizen of the Irish Republic or of another European Union member state

Citizens of European Union member states other than UK or the Irish Republic are only eligible to vote in local government elections.

If you are not a British or Irish Republic citizen you must fill in a separate vote form to vote in European Parliamentary elections.

You can only vote in elections if your name is on the register of electors for our district.

As from June 10th 2014 the registration system has changed.  Under the old system, the 'head of every household' could register everyone who lived at their address, now individuals are responsible for registering themselves.  In order to register, you will require your National Insurance number and your date of birth.  To find out more about Individual Electoral Registration, visit www.gov.uk/register-to-vote

Credit reference agencies also obtain the electoral register to confirm the residency of people, so this may be another reason for you to ensure you are registered to vote.

Obtaining a copy of the Electoral Register

There are two versions of the Electoral Register: an 'open access/edited' version and a 'full' version.  The open access/edited version excludes all the electors who have chosen to 'opt out' and not have their details published in this version. The full version can only be purchased by particular organisations specified under law, such as Government departments or credit reference agencies.

Anyone can only purchase an open access/edited version, not the full version, of the Electoral Register and they can be supplied in either data or printed forms.

Please contact Electoral Services who can work out how much the Register will cost (it is based on a set fee plus extra costs depending on the number of entries in the Register).
