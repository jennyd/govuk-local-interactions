Noise nuisance - residential

Noise can impact on a person and affect their quality of life and enjoyment of their property. 

What you can do

Firstly you could try contacting your neighbour yourself. Explain what the noise is you are experiencing and how it is affecting you and ask if they could do something about it. In most instances they may just not be aware they are causing you a problem.

However if the problem continues you can contact us.

Can we investigate?

Noise from musical instruments  
Noise from loud arguments  

What we will do

In the first instance we will ask you to complete a noise diary detailing how and when you are being affected by the noise.

Once the diary form is returned to us we will contact the noise maker to make them aware that a complaint has been raised and we will provide advice on suitable noise control. If this is unsuccessful, we will need to carry out noise monitoring at your property to determine if a statutory nuisance exists.

If it is confirmed that a statutory nuisance exists, we have various legal powers to resolve the problem. This may include, serving enforcement notices on the person responsible for the noise nuisance, seizing equipment, and in some cases prosecution.

Taking private action

Section 82 of the Environmental Protection Act 1990  allows for private individuals to take their own action in the Magistrates' Court if they are aggrieved by the existence of a statutory nuisance. The Court can issue an Order requiring the abatement of the nuisance and can also issue a fine. Breach of such an Order is a criminal offence punishable by a further fine.
