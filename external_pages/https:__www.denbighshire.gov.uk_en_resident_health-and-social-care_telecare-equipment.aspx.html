Telecare is a personal alarm service to help vulnerable adults over the age of 18 to live safely and independently in their own home. 

How does it work?

We provide a button that you can wear as a wristband or a pendant. When you press the button, it automatically contacts our operators. They will know straight away who you are, what your name is, and where you are calling from. The operator can talk to you directly through a loudspeaker on your phone to find out what you need, and to let you know that help is on its way.

Once the operator has established what you need they will arrange for someone to come and help you. This could be a family member, neighbour, doctor, one of the emergency services or our out of hours response team.

Telecare provides a service 24 hours a day, 365 days a year, and it works in the garden as well as in the home. 

How much does it cost?

It costs £15 per month per household to rent the Telecare equipment. This is the standard charge, and there are no extra charges for installing or maintaining the equipment.

If you receive housing benefit, there is no charge for Telecare. 

How do I request Telecare equipment?

If you think Telecare could help you or someone you know, please give us a call on 0300 456 1000

If you would like to see the alarm system before agreeing to have it installed, you can visit the Telecare demonstration facility at:

Rhyl Community Fire Station
Coast Road
Rhyl
LL18 3PL

You can visit from 9am – 5pm, Monday to Friday.
