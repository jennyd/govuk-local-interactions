Parks

  Abney Hall Park

Abney Hall, built in 1847, is the focal point of the park. Its grand structure and landscaped gardens tell of the wealth of the first owner, a former Mayor of Stockport and cotton magnate.

  Adswood Park

Adswood Park opened to the public in June 1913 as Adswood Recreation Ground.

  Alexandra Park

Alexandra Park is an ideal location to provide a central focus for the local community.

  Brabyns Park

Brabyns Park is Marple's largest park and was once the estate attached to Brabyns Hall.

  Bramhall Park

This magnificent park of woodland and trails is the ideal location for an afternoon stroll.

  Bredbury Recreation Ground

Bredbury Park is located on the north-eastern side of Stockport town and is located adjacent to the A560 Stockport Road East.

  Brinnington Park

Brinnington Park has been transformed over the last six years as a result of the ‘Brinnington Park SRB Project’ (which runs in conjunction with Brinnington Community First).

  Bruntwood Park

Bruntwood Park provides recreational and sporting facilities to all local residents and offers a play area of excellence to the Borough of Stockport and to many visitors from the North West region.

  Cale Green Park

Cale Green Park is a formal Victorian style park enjoying close proximity to public transport making the park available to a wide community.

  Chadkirk Country Estate

The estate comprises a 60 acre farm and a 16th century chapel, all nestled in the picturesque Goyt valley.

  Crescent Park

Through engaging members of the local community and a strong partnership with the Friends of Crescent Park, real and lasting benefits for the local community have been achieved.

  Diamond Jubilee Park

Diamond Jubilee Park is situated within an area of high density population with local shops and is just some 10-15 minutes walk from Cheadle District Centre and all its amenities.

  East Avenue Park

East Avenue Park is a vibrant park that offers access to a range of different sports and activities.

  Etherow Country Park

Etherow Country Park lies at the heart of the Etherow/Goyt Valley and was one of Britain's first country parks.

  Gatley Carrs

Gatley Carrs is a designated nature reserve that is a key part of the local community.

  Hallam Coronation Gardens

Hallam Coronation Garden opened in 1902 and retains much of its original character, with its traditional gates, formal layout and ornamental planting.

  Happy Valley

Happy Valley is a designated nature reserve that is home to a wide variety of habitats and wildlife.

  Heaton Mersey Common

Heaton Mersey Common is a pocket of lush greenspace that is home to a wide variety of wildlife and plants.

  Heaton Mersey Park

Situated within the Heaton Mersey Village Conservation Area this park is split over two levels.

  Heaton Moor Park

Created in 1894, Heaton Moor Park offers a selection of sports pitches and courts as well as plenty of green space.

  Heaton Norris Park

Heaton Norris Park's elevated position gives stunning views of the Stockport town centre skyline and of the Cheshire plain.

  Manchester Road Park

Located one mile from Stockport town centre, this is a small (1 hectare) community park serving Heaton Chapel and South Reddish.

  Maple Avenue Park

Maple Avenue, Cheadle Hulme

  Marple Memorial Park

Marple Memorial Park was built on land donated by local families, and opened in 1922 to commemorate all the Marple soldiers who died during World War 1.

  Mersey Vale Nature Park

There is plenty to do and see at Mersey Vale Nature Park, whether it’s discovering wildlife, enjoying a picnic, walking, cycling or horse riding on the many paths and trails through the Park.

  North Reddish Park

The Friends and the North Reddish Bowling Club have recently raised enough funding to fence the bowling greens which will help protect them from damage and hopefully help them become one of the best bowling facilities in the borough.

  Oak Meadow Park

Oak Meadow Park in the centre of Cheadle Hulme is a valuable urban greenspace used by a wide variety of the local community.

  Poise Brook

Poise Brook is a designated nature reserve that is home to a wide variety of habitats and wildlife.

  Reddish Vale Country Park

Reddish Vale Country park spans 161 hectares along the River Tame in the heart of Stockport.

  Romiley Park

Romiley Park is a well-loved neighbourhood park close to the centre of Romiley village.

  Shaw Heath Recreation Ground

Shaw Heath Recreation Ground offers a range of facilities that few other small sites are able to.

  South Reddish Park

South Reddish Park is a well maintained park that is a great place to play sport and enjoy the outdoors.

  St Thomas' Park

St Thomas' Park is an important part of the local community, who have helped to rejuvenate the greenspace in recent years.

  Thornfield Park

At only approximately 1.8 hectares this small park offers a superb range of facilities.

  Torkington Park

Torkington Park is the main park in the Hazel Grove area and the home of the Secret Garden.

  Vernon Park

Stockport's historic Vernon Park received a £1.6 million Heritage Lottery Fund grant to give it new life and restore the Park to its former Victorian and Edwardian splendour.

  Woodbank Park

Woodbank Memorial Park is situated adjacent to Vernon Park, Offerton. It is ideal for a leisurely stroll in its wide open spaces.
