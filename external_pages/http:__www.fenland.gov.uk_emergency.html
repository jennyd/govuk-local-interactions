Emergency Planning

How to prepare and what to do in case of an emergency

Fenland District Council employs an Emergency Planning Team who co-ordinate planning, training and exercising within Local Authority Teams. This means if a major emergency happened, the right resources would work together to support the community and effectively manage the situation. Find out how the Council can help in an emergency.

  • Community Risk Register

    Community Risk Register

    Local Authorities undertake risk assessments, as well as maintaining and publishing them on a Community Risk Register. This is part of the Civil Contingencies Act 2004. As part of the Emergency Planning arrangements across Cambridgeshire, Fenland District Council has worked with the Cambridgeshire and Peterborough Local Reliance Forum to produce this register. The Community Risk Register outlines what hazards exist in the area and makes sure the plans we develop are proportionate to the risks. It is updated annually and whenever information about new risks is available.
