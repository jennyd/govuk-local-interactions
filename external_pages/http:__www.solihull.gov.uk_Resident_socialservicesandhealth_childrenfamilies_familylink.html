Family Link

Everyone needs a break occasionally, but for parents of a child with a disability it can be next to impossible to find the space or time.

Family Link is there to help. The scheme pairs up local people (‘link carers’) with disabled children and their families. Link carers look after the children for short periods (up to a total of 28 days per year), giving families the respite they need.

Family Link has something to offer everyone involved:

  • families can spend time together, concentrate on the other children or just recharge their batteries, knowing their disabled child is safe and happy
  • disabled children have a change of scene and benefit from new experiences and relationships
  • link carers and their families help out another family in a rewarding way, expand their horizons and build new friendships

Could I be a link carer?

Link carers are ordinary individuals, couples or families of all ages, cultures and backgrounds, who can offer care in their own home to a child with a disability. We provide training and support, and an allowance to help with costs.

If you think you’d like to help, we’d love to hear from you. We can visit you at home to explain more about the scheme and help you decide if it’s right for you. You can contact the Family Link team by calling 0800 073 0769 or contact us by email at ssplace@solihull.gov.uk.
