Sole Occupier & Other Discounts

You may be entitled to a 25% discount off your council tax bill if you are the only person (aged 18 or over) living in a property.

How can you claim the discount?

Please complete an application form found under the Documents section of this page or contact the Council's Customer Services section.

You may need to send us proof or the new address of anyone who used to live with you. Or, you can write to us with the details.

What happens then?

If we are satisfied that you qualify for a discount, we will send you a new bill. We send review forms to check that discounts still apply.

What happens if someone else over 18 moves into the property?

Please contact us with the details of the new occupier straight away as this may affect the discount.

What happens if you falsely claim the discount?

This is an offence and by law you may be fined up to £200 for giving false information.

PLEASE NOTE ANY INFORMATION SUPPLIED MAY BE USED TO COMBAT FRAUD.

Other discounts

We work out the council tax charge by assuming that at least two adults are living in the property. 50% of the council tax charge is for the property and 50% relates to the people who live there. We do not count some people when we work out how many adults live in the property. This may allow you to receive a discount even if you do not live alone. The people who are not counted are:-

  • full time students, student nurses, apprentices and youth training trainees;
  • 18 and 19 year olds who are at, or who have just left, school or college;
  • long-stay patients in hospital;
  • people being looked after in care homes;
  • people staying in certain hostels or night shelters;
  • care workers working for low pay, usually for charities;
  • people who care for someone with a disability who is not their husband or wife, partner or a child under 18;
  • members of visiting forces and certain international institutions;
  • foreign diplomats;
  • members of religious communities (monks and nuns);
  • people in detention (unless for non-payment of council tax or a fine); and
  • severely mentally impaired people.
  • If all members of the household fit these categories, they may be able to get a 50% discount.

Discounts may also be applicable in the following circumstances

  • If there is only one person over 18 living in the property who gets counted, the person who pays the bill may be entitled to a discount of 25% (see above).
  • If a property is a furnished second home then a 50% discount may apply for periods up to 31 March 2013. From 1 April 2013 there is zero discount.
  • All people over 18 are not counted (see above).
  • If a property is empty for more than six months (this only applies if the property is not already exempt); then a 50% discount may be available up to 31 March 2005, after which a 25% discount may be available up to 31 March 2007.  From 1 April 2007 the full charge will apply. Please note that if the property remains empty for more than 2 years then from 1 April 2013 a premium of 50% will be added making the charge 150%.
  • From 1 April 2013 if the property is vacant and undergoing major repair works or structural alteration to render it habitable then a 25% discount is available for a maximum period of 12 months.
  • From 1 April 2013 if the property is unoccupied and unfurnished a 25% discount is available for a maximum period of 6 months.

50% discounts

  • A 50% discount may apply if:-
  • a property is no-one's only or main home; and it is furnished. Please note that this discount is only applicable until 31 March 2013 after which there is zero discount.
  • a property is a second home; Please note that this discount is only applicable until 31 March 2013 after which there is zero discount.
  • all people over 18 are not counted (see above)

Council Tax Relief for troops on operations

  • Armed Forces Regular and Reserve Service (Full or Limited Commitment) personnel deployed to Afghanistan and other operational locations who pay Council Tax for a property in the UK will receive financial support through a flat-rebate payment over a six month tour of £140.
  • The rebate will be added to the tax-free Operational Allowance paid at the end of an operational tour.

For more information, select the link for the Ministry of Defence website within the Offsite Links section of this page.

Annexe Discount

From 1 April 2014 there is a 50% reduction in the amount of Council Tax payable for people living (as their sole or main residence) in an annexe where they are related to the person paying Council Tax for the main dwelling. There is also a 50% reduction available for people who use an unoccupied annexe as part of their main residence. If you think that this may apply to you please contact the Council for further details.

Privacy Notice 

Broxtowe Borough Council is required by law to participate in a National Fraud Initiative data matching exercise. Information held by the Council for council tax administration and electoral registration purposes will be used for comparison with other councils and systems to aid the prevention and detection of fraud and error.
