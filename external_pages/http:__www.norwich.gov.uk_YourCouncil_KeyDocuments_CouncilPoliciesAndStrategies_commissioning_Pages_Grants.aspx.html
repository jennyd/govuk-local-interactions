Grants

Page Content

Norwich City Council commissions a wide range of activities as well as directly delivering services, details of which can be found in the corporate plan for 2015-20:

Corporate Plan 2015-20

The council is also committed to working with, developing and supporting the voluntary and community sector. It recognises that there are many activities the voluntary and community sector is particularly well placed to undertake and can expertly deliver in Norwich.

Annual grants 2016-17 for the voluntary, community and social enterprise sector
 
The council has five key priorities for the period 2015-20 which are:
·         Safe, clean and low carbon city.
·         Prosperous and vibrant city.
·         Fair city.
·         Healthy city with good housing.
·         Value for money services.  
 
In addition to its existing grant awards, the council is seeking bids from organisations to help it to meet some of these priorities in 2016-17. Details of these are listed under the headings below. Unless otherwise stated the duration of each grant is one year from 1 April 2016. Any organisation from the voluntary, community and social enterprise sector interested in applying for funding should consider these outcomes before proceeding to the ‘how to apply’ section.
 
Prosperous and vibrant city
¨      Effective culture and leisure opportunities
o   Build confidence and raise aspirations through leisure and culture activities (for all ages and abilities).
 
¨      Maintain historic character through effective planning and conservation management
o   Heritage development, promotion and interpretation.
 
Awards of up to £10,000 for activities.
Awards of up to £10,000 for heritage conservation and development.
 
Fair city
A single award of £1,500 is available to support the council in making the city more accessible for residents and visitors with disabilities.   
 
 
Value for money services
 
¨    Developing web-based solution to support increased volunteering
 
¨    Developing a volunteer mentoring programme
 
You will find the detailed specification and outcomes required for this award here 
 
An award of up to £25,000 is available for delivery of these outcomes.
 
How to apply
 
üRead the guidance notes Annual Grants Guidance
 
üComplete the application form Annual Grants Application form 
 
üSubmit the application form and required supporting documents 
 
Applications should be sent by the stated closing date, preferably by email to commissioning@norwich.gov.uk or alternatively hard copy to ‘Commissioning’ at City Hall, St Peters Street, Norwich, NR2 1NH.
 

Dates for 2016:

The closing date for applications is 31 January 2016.
Applicants will be informed of the outcome by mid-March 2016.
Grants awarded will commence 01 April 2016.

Existing grant arrangements, housing service contracts and partnership arrangements are also in place to help the council meet some of its priorities. Details of these for 2016-17 will be available on this page in April 2016.
