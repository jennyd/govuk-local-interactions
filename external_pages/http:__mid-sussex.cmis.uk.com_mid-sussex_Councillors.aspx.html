Councillors

In this section...

You can find information on your elected representatives including the Councillors' allowances scheme, Code of Conduct (including Town and Parish Councils within the District) and copies of the Member Information Service bulletins dating back to 2002.

Mid Sussex District Council is made up of 54 elected Members:

  • Conservative - 54 Members 
   

The Council operates a "Leader" and "Cabinet" model. Six Councillors serve as Cabinet Members and Group Leader, making executive decisions. There is also a Group Leader for the other political groups. Committees with specific responsibilities carry out non-executive functions. Councillors also serve as representatives on outside bodies.

All Councillors meet together as the full Council. These meetings are normally open to the public. Members of the public may ask questions of Council.
You can search for the Councillor in your area by postcode or alphabetically.
A list of all the Mid Sussex District Councillors can be found here.


People like you are Councillors.  If you are interested in becoming a Councillor, we have produced a booklet on 'Being a Councillor' for further information about the role.
