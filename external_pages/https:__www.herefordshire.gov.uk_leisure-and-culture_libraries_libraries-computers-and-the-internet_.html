Computers in libraries

Herefordshire libraries offer free computer and internet access at most sites. You will only need to pay for printing.

Services available include:

  • Black and white printing is available at all sites at a cost of 10p per A4 side
  • Colour printing is available at Hereford, Ross, Leominster, Bromyard, Kington and Ledbury libraries at a cost of 20p per A4 side
  • You may use USB sticks to save work or download files, these can be purchased from the library at a cost of £6
  • Junior computers are available for under 15s

We apply filtering software to all library computers. If you feel that a site has been blocked by mistake, please use the online form to notify our IT department.

Can I use a computer if I am not a member of the library?

If you are not a member of the library, you can use a short stay computer to access the internet for a 15-minute session.

However, if you would like to use the computer for longer joining the library is free.

How do I access a computer as library member?

  • You will need your library card and PIN to access the computers - if you don't know your PIN, a member of staff will be able to help you
  • Computers are allocated for one hour sessions
  • You can have a maximum of two hours on the computers per day
  • To book a computer in advance contact your local library
