Where is the crematorium?

The crematorium is located within the grounds of Magdalen Cemetery, Oriel Avenue, Gorleston, Great Yarmouth, NR31 7JJ. Parking is available.

 Directions to the crematorium. [38kb]

How many people can be seated in the crematorium chapel?

The maximum number is 120 but this would be fewer if people with disabilities are coming.

When is the crematorium office open?

The crematorium office is open Monday to Friday from 9am to 4.30pm.

Who can help me organise a funeral?

Funeral directors are expert at organising funerals. You can find professional registered funeral directors and get advice on arranging a funeral from:

  • National Association of Funeral Directors (NAFD)
  • National Society of Allied and Independent Funeral Directors
  • Age UK

The funeral director will want to see a certificate for burial or cremation, issued by the Registrar. The funeral director should provide full details of all costs.

Funeral directors can arrange for repatriation of bodies from abroad or organise funerals in other countries.

Can I organise a funeral myself?

If you are thinking of making all arrangements yourself, you should read gov.uk. We can offer some advice and help with the planning process.

Cremation  

You will need to complete the following forms:

 Cremation form 1 [54kb] - application for the cremation of the body of a person. Usually completed by the nearest surviving relative or an executor.

 Cremation form 1A [202kb] - Environmental Policy to be completed and signed in all cases

 Cremation form 4 [331kb] - medical certificate to be completed by a registered medical practitioner

 Cremation form 5 [128kb] - confirmatory medical certificate to be completed by a registered medical practitioner of at least five years standing

 Cremation form 20 [85kb] - service arrangements

If the death has been referred to a Coroner, then the Coroner will complete a form 6 for the crematorium.

Burial

You will need to complete a  Notice of Interment form [130kb].

You can hold a non-religious service with your choice of words and music.

Can music be played at the service?

Yes, this can be supplied by the crematorium using the Wesley Music System that offers a wide-ranging choice of popular and classical songs and music. Our library is available on the Wesley Music website, if we do not have your chosen music this must be booked in advance  of the service, by your funeral director.

We can provide a recording of the complete service on CD for a fee of £57.50.

Recording requests must be made in advance.

Can I get help with funeral costs?

If you are the deceased's next of kin and receive income support or benefits, you might be able to get help with costs. You can find out more on gov.uk.

How much do cremation and burial cost?

See our  cremation fees [83kb] for options and charges.

See our  cemetery charges [58kb] - there may be extra fees involved, depending on your ceremony.

 

What memorial options do you offer following cremation?

To help commemorate the memory of a loved one we provide a rose or shrub dedication in our garden of remembrance, a book of remembrance, wall plaques, memorial benches and trees. See our  memorial fees [98kb] for options and charges.

How do I organise a memorial stone for a grave?

Visit the National Association of Memorial Masons (NAMM) website to find your nearest mason.

See our  rules and regulations [150kb] to find out what sizes we allow and to apply to have a memorial stone erected.

Can I reserve a grave for future use?

You can buy a grave before it is needed. You will get a deed to the grave, which is a legal document and should be kept safe.

How do I transfer ownership of a grave?

Read our  guidance [19kb] and complete the  form [152kb] to transfer grave ownership

Can I bury on private land?

You can, you will need to comply with all the legal requirements. You can find out more on the Natural Death Centre website.
