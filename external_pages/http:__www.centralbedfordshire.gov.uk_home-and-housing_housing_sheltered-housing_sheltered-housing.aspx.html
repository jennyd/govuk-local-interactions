Independent Living

What is sheltered housing?


Sheltered accommodation is purposely designed to meet the needs of older people who may need a little extra support to continue living independently. 

The schemes benefit from having an alarm service which is installed so that tenants can call for emergency assistance should they require help.  Also, Sheltered Housing Officers make contact with the tenants on a daily basis. 

Many of the sheltered schemes have communal lounge facilities which encourage social interaction and create a scheme community.

Find a sheltered housing scheme

Sheltered housing schemes are run by Central Bedfordshire Council in the south of the area.  You can find out about these on our website. 

Aldwyck Housing also provide some supported housing (similar to sheltered housing) and retirement housing in Dunstable.

Aragon Housing provide some supported housing (similar to sheltered housing) and retirement housing in the north of the area.

There are also privately run schemes across Central Bedfordshire. Details of privately run available properties can also be found in your local newspaper or on websites such as RightMove.

You can find out about other sheltered housing and retirement schemes on the Housing Care website.

Apply for sheltered housing

If you’ve had an assessment by social services and we have agreed that you need to move into a sheltered housing scheme and agreed the council will pay all of the costs or some of the costs then your social worker or housing officer will support you to find an appropriate new home. 

If you are meeting all of the cost yourself then you are free to approach privately run sheltered housing schemes and ask to be put on their waiting lists. 

For council sun schemes, anyone over the age of 55 years old living in Central Bedfordshire can apply regardless of whether your present home is rented or owned. For couples, at least one partner must be of retirement age. We also welcome applications from people who have not reached retirement age but feel this type of housing may be suitable for them due to a disability or significant health condition.

Unfortunately applications from households cannot be accepted if there is a non-dependent living with them, who has no support needs themselves (for example a son/ daughter/ grand child).  Sheltered housing and extra care schemes are reserved for older and frailer households.

Even if you live outside of Central Bedfordshire but fulfil the above criteria, you may be eligible if you have family living within the district or another strong local connection.

To apply, visit the Bedfordshire Homefinder website to download an application form, alternatively you can call 0300 300 8302 and ask for a housing application to be sent to you to complete.

Your application will be assessed, looking at your current circumstances and if you are eligible, your application will be placed in the appropriate band on the housing register. Your band will show you the level of priority you have been given for social housing, i.e. urgent, high, medium or low. We will write to you to tell you which band you are in.

Choice Based Lettings


The council allocates all social housing through the Choice Base Letting Scheme (CBL).
The Choice Base Letting Scheme enables customers to choose their own home (within the hosing band they have been allocated) and provides transparency about the selection and allocation of all social housing. 

All vacant properties are advertised.  All applicants waiting for housing can “bid” or express an interest in a property.  The person with the highest priority for that size property and waiting the longest with that priority will be allocated to the property.
You can read more about Choice Based Letting on our Finding accommodation page.

What is extra care?

Extra care housing provides a higher level of support than you might get in sheltered housing but it still allows you to retain some privacy, and remain as independent as possible.

In extra care housing you have your own flat but you also have support staff on duty in the building 24 hours a day, seven days a week. You will usually receive support at particular times of the day depending on your needs, and will have a support plan which is agreed between you and the support staff, but will have the added reassurance of knowing that there is always someone nearby of things go wrong when you are by yourself.

The support offered in extra care housing varies between different places and will depend on what support you need as an individual, but extra care housing will generally offer support with personal care (i.e. getting up, washing and dressing), preparing meals and other essential day-to-day tasks as well as access to help in an emergency.

There’s often be a programme of activities available during the day, which can be a good chance for you to meet other people living in the building and socialise. Some places will hold exercise classes and may even have a swimming pool.

Find an extra care scheme

Extra care schemes are run by Central Bedfordshire Council in the south of the area.  You can find out about these on our website. 

There are also privately run schemes across Central Bedfordshire as well as a few run by housing associations. You can find out about extra care schemes in Bedfordshire on the Housing Care website.

Details of privately run available properties can also be found in your local newspaper or on websites such as RightMove.

Apply for an extra care scheme

Extra Care housing is not let through Bedfordshire Homefinder website. You will need to apply directly and be assessed by the council for this type of housing. Telephone 0300 300 5421 or 0300 300 5384 for further information.

Emergency alarms

Personal distress alarms can be very helpful, they can call support in an emergency, give increased security, help you to remain independent and also give you and other family members peace of mind that if you need some help you can easily call for some help.

There are various types of alarms available - from detectors and sensors through to pendants and buttons connected to a control centre.

Lifeline

Lifeline is an emergency alarm service 24 hours a day, every day via a friendly 24 hr control centre.

The Lifeline alarm unit consists of a base unit and a remote pendant. The unit is plugged into a mains socket and a nearby telephone point. The pendant is usually worn around your neck so that you can activate the alarm from anywhere in or around your home.

When you activate your Lifeline alarm, the alarm unit automatically dials our 24 hr control centre, and within a short time you will hear the friendly voice of one of our operators calling out to you from the alarm unit to ask whether you need help. The operator knows who you are and where you live as soon as the alarm call is received, so all you need do is to tell them what the problem is. You do not have to be next to the alarm unit to do this because the alarm unit has a powerful microphone that can pick up your voice even if you are a considerable distance from the unit. Once the operator has found out the nature of the problem they will quickly arrange the right response to help you.

To have a Lifeline alarm unit installed, you will need a modern telephone socket and an electrical socket nearby. We will also ask you to give us names of at least two people who you trust, that live locally and who can hold keys to your home and act as key holders.

You can apply for a Lifeline alarm by completing this Lifeline referral form or you can call 0300 300 8146 for a demonstration or further information.

Lifeline+

In addition to Lifeline we also offer another service known as Lifeline+. This is additional service providing an early intervention flexible service that supports people

aged 55 and above in the south Central Bedfordshire area only.

If you would like a free, no obligation demonstration in your own home, contact us 0300 300 8146

If you already use Lifeline or Lifeline+ then you may find this Lifeline leaflet (PDF 4MB) useful.

Telehealth

There’s also special Telehealth equipment available, which can monitor your health in your own home. It can be equipment to measure your blood pressure, blood glucose levels or your weight. This can reduce the number of visits to your Doctor and unplanned visits to the hospital that you need to make. You’re taught how to do the tests on yourself and the measurements are automatically transmitted to your doctor or nurse, who can then see the information without you having to leave home. They will tell you about any concerns they may have.   The NHS provide this system so ask your Doctor about it.  You can also read more on the NHS website.
