I think you've made a mistake with my housing benefit

Sub heading

If you think your housing benefit has been calculated wrongly you can ask us to look at our decision again

Page Content

If you think our decision is wrong

Once we work out your benefit, we will send you letters telling you how we worked out your claim.
If there is something you don't understand, or would like a bit more information about, phone us on 03007900345 and ask us to explain it.


If you do not agree with the decision you can:
  • ask us for a written statement of reasons
  • ask us to look at the decision again
  • appeal to an independent tribunal

There are time limits and other special rules you must follow when you ask for any of these things. You can only ask for these things if you are a person affected by the decision. More details are shown below.

Ask for a written statement of reasons

A written statement of reasons provides more detail about the regulations we applied when we made the decision. It can help you understand why we made the decision, and might be useful if you are not sure what to do next.

Time limit:
You must ask for a written statement of reasons within one month of the date of the decision.
Other rules:
You must ask for this in writing, and the request must be signed. This means you cannot send your request by email.
You can only ask for this if you are a person affected by the decision.

How long will it take?

We will try to send your statement within 1 month of receiving your request.

Ask us to look at the decision again

If you don't agree with the way your benefit is worked out you can ask us to look at it again. We sometimes call this a "Reconsideration".

To ask for this, please write to us telling us why you think our decision is wrong and provide any supporting information and we will look at your claim again. This will be done by a different officer to the one who made the original decision. If the original decision is wrong we will change it.
Time limit:
You should ask for this within one month of the date of the original decision.
If you asked for a written statement of reasons, this time will be extended. The written statement of reasons will tell you the deadline for asking us to look at the decision again.
If you can't meet this time limit, we may be able to extend it by up to 12 months. If your request is being made late, you must also tell us the reasons why it is late.
Other rules:
You must ask for this in writing.
You can only ask for this if you are a person affected by the decision.
How long will it take?
We will try to look again at the decision within 1 month of receiving your request.
This may take longer if we need to ask you to provide extra information.
What happens if the decision can be changed?
If we change the decision, we will normally change it from the date of the original decision.
We will send you a letter explaining the new decision.
If you do not agree with the new decision, you can ask for it to be looked at again.

What happens if the decision cannot be changed?

We will send you a letter explaining why we think the original decision is right, but you can still appeal against it to the independent tribunal.
If you want to appeal, you have one month from the date of this new letter confirming our decision.

Ask for an independent tribunal to look at the decision

If you don't agree with the way your benefit is worked out you can ask for an independent tribunal to look at it again. This is also called an appeal.
The independent tribunals are managed by Her Majesty's Courts & Tribunals Service (HMCTS). For more information please visit the Ministry of Justice (opens in new window)

How do I appeal?

To make an appeal you need to write to us
Time limit:
You should ask for this within one month of the date of the original decision.
If you asked for a written statement of reasons, this time will be extended. The written statement of reasons will tell you the deadline for asking us to look at the decision again.
If you can't meet this time limit, the independent tribunal may extend it by up to 12 months. If your appeal is being made late, you must also explain the reasons why it is late.
Other rules:
You must ask for this in writing and the appeal must be signed by you. This means you cannot make an appeal by email.
You can only appeal if you are a person affected by the decision.
Your appeal must clearly identify the decision you think is wrong.
Your appeal must include the reasons why you think the decision is wrong.

How long will it take?

The whole appeal process usually takes between 3 months and 8 months.
We will try to look at your appeal and prepare it for the tribunal within 1 month.

What happens after I send you my appeal?

The first thing we will do is to look at our decision again.
If we can change it, and the new decision is better for you, your appeal will stop.
If we can't change it, or we can change it but the new decision is no better for you, your appeal will continue.
If your appeal continues, we will send your appeal, along with our response to the appeal to HMCTS.
We will also send a copy of our response to you, and to your representative if you have one.
When HMCTS receives your appeal and our response, they will send an enquiry form to you.
You must read this form carefully, answer the questions, and send it back to HMCTS. If you don't do this, your appeal will stop.
The enquiry form will ask you if you would like to attend the hearing or have the appeal looked at based on the reasons given in your letters.
If HMCTS accept your appeal they will advise you of the date and place of hearing. For Luton, appeals are normally held in Dunstable.

What happens at the tribunal?

Normally, one person will hear your appeal. This person will be legally qualified and not from the Council. The tribunal may include another person with financial qualifications.
If you go to the hearing you may be asked questions. You will also be able to ask questions. If you prefer, you can take someone with you to help you. A person from the Council may also be present.
If you wanted to go to the hearing but cannot attend you must inform HMCTS straight away. If you do not, your appeal may be heard without you.
If you have asked for a paper hearing you will not be asked to attend.
How do I find out the results?
Whether you go to the hearing or the appeal is decided from the documents, you will be sent a written statement explaining the tribunal's decision. A copy will also be sent to the Council.
What if I disagree with the tribunal’s decision?
You may be able to appeal to the Upper Tribunal. However, you can only appeal to the Upper Tribunal on a point of law. Your decision notice from HMCTS will tell you what to do if you are unhappy with their decision.

What do you mean by a "person affected by a decision"?

Only a person affected by a decision can ask for a written statement of reasons, ask us to look at a decision again, or appeal to an independent tribunal.
You are always a person affected by a decision if you are:

  • the benefit claimant
  • a person we have appointed to act on behalf of the benefit claimant.
  • You are also a person affected by a decision if you are a landlord, but only:
  • by a decision that there is a recoverable overpayment which can be recovered from you
  • or by a decision not to pay Housing Benefit directly to you.
