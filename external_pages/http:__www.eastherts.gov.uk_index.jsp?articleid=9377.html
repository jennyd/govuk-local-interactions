Council Tax Support Scheme

Council Tax Support replaced Council Tax Benefit on 1 April 2013.

Our scheme means that every 'working age' resident has to pay council tax even if they are claiming financial support. The support scheme is calculated on 91.5% of your council tax liability (after discounts for single persons etc) instead of 100% as previously. This means that everyone has to pay at least 8.5% of the net liability.

  • We automatically transferred your claim for Council Tax Benefit into the new Council Tax support scheme.

The amount you will have to pay depends on where you live and what council tax band your house is in.

Remember the easiest way to pay, is by monthly Direct Debit. You can download a direct debit mandate from Downloads section below.

If you do not want to pay by Direct Debit, you can pay in other ways, see How to Pay your Council Tax page for more information.

What does this mean for our customers?

If a customer is classed as a pensioner, they will be protected from the effects of the changes and get support on the same basis as previously.  If a customer is classed as 'working age', their benefit has been  transferred to the new scheme but they will get less help with their Council Tax bill, and some people will have to pay Council Tax for the first time.

How can I claim?

If you claim Income Support, the Benefits Agency (formerly the Department of Social Security) will normally tell the Council.

However, you must also complete the Council's Council Tax Support Application Form [507kb].  The form also covers Housing Benefit.

Once you have claimed Council Tax Support you may realise that you would have qualified before.  Council Tax Support can be backdated, but only if you have good reason for not claiming sooner. You must give your reasons in writing. How to claim backdated Housing Benefit and Council Tax Support [136kb].

Council Tax Support payments cover Council Tax only; it cannot cover court costs, bailiff fees or the cost of paying at a Post Office.

Council Tax Support is paid direct to your Council Tax account.

Information and evidence required for Council Tax Support claims

Details of what information and evidence is required is available on the Information and evidence for Council Tax Support claims page.
