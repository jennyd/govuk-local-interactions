Drainage – private property

The owner or occupant is responsible for the repair and clearing of blocked drains within private properties.

You may find that your toilet is blocked, grids that take water from sinks may overflow or there may be smells around the grid or inspection chamber.

Check if your neighbours are experiencing similar problems, if none of your neighbours are affected, it is likely that your drain is blocked and therefore the responsibility lies with you.

If you are unable to clear the blockage yourself contact a specialist drainage contractor, plumber or reputable builder to clear the drain or carry out any repairs, check with your insurance company to see if you are covered by your policy.

If you are a tenant you should contact your landlord or Housing Association to resolve the issue.

If your neighbours are also affected then the problem is likely to be with the sewer and responsibility lies with United Utilities. Use the United Utilities sewers and drains explained, to find out whether a sewer or drain is public or private. We have certain powers to force owners to deal with drainage issues on privately owned land.
