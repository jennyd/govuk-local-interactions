Closures and diversions

We can close sections of the road network in North Somerset on safety grounds where a problem prevents the safe passage of pedestrians or road users. The procedure is part of a statutory process which takes around eight weeks to complete. Roads can be closed off to traffic by using a traffic regulation order which can only be enforced with the written agreement of the police. To avoid being caught out read our planned road closures FAQs to see how you might be affected if we close a road.

Demonstrations and parades

We may close roads to allow events or parades to take place within North Somerset, but this is usually only done under special circumstances and we require at least six weeks notice and up to three months in some cases to close any routes. We do not deal with road closures for demonstrations where organisers must contact the police for permission. All costs involved in the closure of roads must be covered by the event organisers. For more information contact our streets and open spaces team.

Out of hours emergencies

If an emergency affecting the highway network occurs outside normal office hours we will aim to send a crew to the site within two hours of receiving a report of the problem. Any issues that can safely be left until the next morning will be. When you report a problem you need to tell us exactly what it is and where it has happened. To report an emergency out of hours contact our emergency contact answering service.

Flooding

We close flooded roads and issue warnings to safeguard road users. Once floodwaters recede we will investigate why an area has flooded and aim to improve drainage and clean road gullies once a year. We are not responsible for preventing flooding on private property. Find out more about flooding.

Road spillages

We are responsible for keeping roads and highways across North Somerset clean and free from spillages. Spillages of certain substances, such as oil, gravel, sand and dropped cargo, can cause traffic accidents and should be reported to us immediately. Report a spillage on the road. If possible, please provide us with details of the offender as this will help us recover any costs in clearing up the spillage.
