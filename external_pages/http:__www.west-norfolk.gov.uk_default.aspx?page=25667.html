Hazardous & Clinical Waste

This page contains information about common household products that are classed as hazardous waste and clinical waste and information about how to dispose of them correctly.

Hazardous waste

 

Hazardous waste is any solid or liquid that may cause harm to people or the environment if not disposed of correctly.

It is illegal to dispose of hazardous items incorrectly and can result in you being issued with a fine of up to £5,000. 

 

The following are examples of hazardous waste that you might have at home:

 

Household products:

  • Paint
  • Thinners such as white spirit
  • Paint brush cleaners
  • De-scalers
  • Stain removers
  • Drain cleaners
  • Electrical equipment
  • Gas cylinders
  • Batteries
  • Fluorescent light tubes or lamps
  • Glues
  • Photographic chemicals
  • Oven cleaners
  • Swimming pool cleaners
  • Used cooking oil
  • Items containing cathode ray tubes (e.g. televisions, computer monitors
  • Asbestos

Garden products:

  • Pesticides
  •  Fertilizers
  • Insecticides
  • Lawn treatments
  • Fungicides
  • Weed killers
  • Wood preservatives, treatments and varnishes

Car maintenance products:

  • Antifreeze
  • Brake fluid
  • Lubricants
  • De-greasers
  • Used engine oil
  • Automotive batteries
  • Tyres

 

Disposal of hazardous waste

 

It is important that you dispose of any hazardous waste items correctly.  Failure to do so may cause harm to other people or the environment and could result in you being fined up to £5,000.

 

Leftover chemicals should never be thrown into your rubbish bin or poured down your drains, sinks or toilets.

 

Residents may take some hazardous waste items to their nearest household recycling centre for disposal.  For further information visit the Norfolk County Council website or call 0344 800 8020. 

 

Norfolk County Council holds annual Hazardous Waste Amnesty Days at the main recycling centres where residents can take items such as paint to be disposed. These are published on Norfolk County Council's website.

 

If you have difficulty disposing of your hazardous waste items we may be able to provide a hazardous waste collection from homes in the borough. Please be aware that this collection service is limited to hazardous household waste which cannot be easily and appropriately disposed of elsewhere. To discuss the possibility of a collection of your household hazardous waste please contact us on 01553 616200.

 

Please note that we do not collect hazardous waste from businesses.  We can provide information about firms authorised to collect this type of waste. For further advice please call: 01553 616200.

 

 

Disposal of clinical waste

 

Clinical waste is any waste which poses a threat of infection to people. The term also includes drugs or other pharmaceutical products.

 

Incontinence pads, urinary catheter bags/tubes and stoma bags can be safely disposed of in your black rubbish bin. Please ensure that this waste is double bagged. If you require extra bin capacity because of this type of waste please call 01553 616200 to discuss this with a member of our team.

 

Clinical waste such as dressing, dialysis and blood catheters cannot be disposed of in your black bin and need to be collected separately. We provide a free weekly collection of this type of waste for domestic properties. Please call us on 01553 616200 to make arrangements for a collection.

 

We cannot collect needles, syringes, sharp instruments or sharps boxes. Please contact your local GP surgery if you have this type of waste.

 

If you find a syringe or other clinical waste in a public place, please notify the Cleanup Team on 0500 253 2687 or email: cleanup@west-norfolk.gov.uk so that they can arrange collection.
