Claims for housing benefit and other help

  • claim online for financial help with your rent and council tax
  • apply for free school meals
  • request discretionary housing payments or local welfare provision

Make a claim for benefit or financial support

Housing benefit and council tax support

To get housing benefit or council tax support, you need to make a claim. You can do this:

  • online
  • in one of our customer service centres
  • by post - download a form below, or contact us to have one sent to you

You can also claim housing benefit when you claim benefits from the Department for Work and Pensions, but you cannot claim council tax support this way.

However you decide to claim, please do not delay - you may miss out on money you would have been entitled to.

Free school meals

You can claim free school meals for your children by calling us on 0300 300 8306. We will usually be able to tell you straight away if you're entitled, and your child's free school meals will start from their next school day.

If you're making an online claim for housing benefit or council tax support, you can ask us to check if you're entitled to free school meals at the same time.

More about claiming free school meals

Discretionary housing payments (DHPs)

If you're already getting housing benefit or the Universal Credit housing costs element for rent, and need extra help, you can ask for a discretionary payment (DHP) to help with your housing costs. You need to apply in writing and tell us why you need extra help. You can do this:

  • in one of our customer service centres
  • by post - download a form below, or contact us to have one sent to you

Download a DHP form (PDF 71KB)

More about claiming discretionary housing payments

Local welfare provision

If you need money in a crisis, or you're vulnerable and in need of goods or services to help you live independently in your community, you may qualify for help. You'll need to meet our eligibility rules, and funds are limited.

More about local welfare provision

Benefit and support appeals

If we've made a decision about your housing benefit or council tax support, and you think that decision is wrong, you must tell us in writing straight away. You can do this:

  • in one of our customer service centres
  • by post

You need to tell us the decision you disagree with, and why you think it is wrong.

If you disagree with housing benefit and council tax support, you only need to write one letter, but there are different rules for how we have to deal with each.

Download additional benefit forms

Sometimes we need to ask you to fill in additional forms, so that we can decide how much housing benefit or council tax support you are entitled to. You can download copies of these forms below, to print and return to us.

Appointee form (PDF 55KB) - complete this form to become an appointee for a claimant. An appointee takes responsibility for the benefit and support claim for a person who cannot deal with their claim themselves.

Backdating request form (PDF 53KB) - complete this form to ask us to pay you from an earlier date. Find out more about backdating on our backdating page.

BACS payment details form (PDF 43KB) - complete this form if you are a private tenant and would like your housing benefit to be paid by BACS bank transfer, or if you want to change the account your benefit is paid into.

Change of circumstances form (PDF 78KB) - complete this form to report a change in your circumstances which could affect your benefit or support. Find out more about changes of circumstances on our change of circumstances page.

Proof of childcare payments form (PDF 56KB) - complete this form to prove how much you pay for childcare. You and your childcare provider both need to sign it. Your childcare costs can often be offset against your income if you are working.

Proof of earnings form (PDF 54KB) - ask your employer to complete this form to prove how much you are paid. We will normally ask you to have this form completed if you have recently started work or your pay has changed, or if you do not get payslips.

Proof of self-employed income form (PDF 84KB) - complete this form to declare your income from self-employment. We will normally ask you to complete this form if you have recently started your self-employment or if you cannot provide your accounts.

Rent details form (PDF 165KB) - complete this form if we have asked you for more information about your rent and property information.

Second property form (PDF 64KB) - complete this form if we have asked you for more information about a property you own, which is not the property you currently live in.

Student details form (PDF 76KB) - complete this form if we have asked you for more information about your student status or income from student loans, grants and bursaries.
