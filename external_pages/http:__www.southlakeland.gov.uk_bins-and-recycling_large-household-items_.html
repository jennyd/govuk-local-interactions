Large household items: Collection

Book a collection

You can book the collection of a large household item by calling us on 01539 733 333.

Collections are made by right2work, who will then call you to ask for more information about the goods to be collected; and arrange an appointment with you to collect the items.

Collection appointments are available between 8am and 4pm from Tuesday to Saturday, with evening collections until 8pm each Thursday.

You will also be offered the opportunity to receive a text message prior to collection, letting you know that the collectors are on their way.

The collectors will provide proof of identification. If you have any doubt about the identification of the collectors please call us on 01539 733 333. You should not allow any persons to enter your home or collect your items unless you are sure of their identity.

Payment is required when you make your request. Payment can be made by credit card, debit card, cheque or postal order.

Collectors are only able to accept items that have been booked and paid for. They will not be able to accept any other donations.

Collections can be made from inside the home, or from outside the property. However, the item must be dry. 


Costs

A single collection of up to three items costs £25. Extra items cost £8.00 per item, up to a maximum of ten items per collection.
For example: 
  • a mattress would count as one item and cost £25
  • a mattress, a bed, a chair and a sofa would count as four items and cost £33
So if you arranged for ten items to be collected the cost would be £81.

What will happen to your item?

Wherever possible the items collected will be refurbished for reuse, recycled or reprocessed.

right2work have opened a retail outlet at Dockray Hall in Kendal where you are able to buy items that have been refurbished.

Household Waste Recycling Centres

There are four Household Waste Recycling Centres in South Lakeland, which are owned by Cumbria County Council. These can be used for the disposal of waste that will not fit into a wheelie bin. There is no charge for householders wishing to use these centres. More on Household Waste Recycling Centres

Alternative ways to reuse and recycle

  • Freecycle groups match people who have things they want to get rid of with people who can use them.

  • Freegle promotes the keeping of usable items out of landfill.

  • recycle.co.uk is a platform that allows users to list items they are willing to give away for free in an attempt to help others within their community and stop items that are perfectly reusable from going to landfill sites

  • The Furniture ReUse Network  promotes the reuse of essential household furniture and electrical appliances to help people in need.

Disposing of waste within the law

You must make sure that your waste is collected by a licensed waste carrier. Licensed waste carriers are registered with the Environment Agency. You can check if a company is listed by contacting the Environment Agency on 08708 506506.
