Dangerous buildings and demolition work

From time to time buildings and other structures become dangerous.Â  These situations can present a risk to the general public as well as those directly involved.

We try, either with the co-operation of the building owner or through legal action, to make the situation safe or advise authorities such as the fire brigade upon the safety of their officers entering a building.

If you see a building or structure that looks to be unsafe please contact us with as much information as possible, including the names of owners if you have them.

We have legal powers to require the owners of buildings or structures to remedy the defects, or it may be necessary to appoint and direct our contractors to carry out works to make the building or structure safe at the owner's expense.

Please Note: Â The council's responsibility is to protect members of the public from dangerous buildings, we cannot enter into disputes between neighbours over the maintenance of shared premises or structures.

Demolitions

Anyone intending to demolish a building is, in most instances, required by law to serve a notice with their local council.

The notice should be accompanied by a location plan and method statement for the demolition.Â  Please note that construction design management regulations apply to demolition works and it is the responsibility of the contractor to carry out the works in a safe way.

Once the Section 80 notice has been completed, please return this to the Building Control Department along with your payment to cover administrative costs of Â£65.00.

The council is only responsible for the registering of the demolition and placing conditions on the state the land is left after demolition.Â  Building Control will then consult with the relevant parties and, where appropriate, consent to demolish will be issued together with any conditions, which must be complied with when the demolition is carried out.

It may also be necessary to obtain consent from Planning before the building is demolished.

All demolitions require notice, except:

  • Demolition due to a demolition order
  • Demolition of part of a building which is occupied and intended to continue been occupied
  • The building has a cubic content less than 49m3
  • An agricultural building, unless it is attached to a building other than above

NoÂ other demolition works must be started without a Section 80 Notice being received by the council.Â  If you are intending on carrying out demolition works, not described above, you must complete and return the Section 80 Notice.
