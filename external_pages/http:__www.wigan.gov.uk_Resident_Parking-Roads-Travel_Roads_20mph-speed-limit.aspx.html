20mph speed limit

We have made our roads safer by introducing a 20mph speed limit in residential areas.

We want all of our residents and particularly children and young people to be able to walk, cycle and play safely on our streets.

A 20mph speed limit improves road safety throughout the borough, saves lives and prevents serious injuries. A pedestrian, if struck by a vehicle at 20mph, is likely to suffer slight injuries. At 30mph they would be severely hurt and at 40mph or above are likely to be killed.

Changing driver behaviour

We want to change driver’s attitudes and behaviours when travelling on residential streets rather than prosecute speeding offences. However, the 20mph speed limit is legally enforceable by Greater Manchester Police (GMP).

We will work together with the police on community engagement activities with schools, local businesses and other local groups to help reinforce the message that driving at 20mph in residential areas is fast enough.

We are also looking to develop community speed watch schemes which involve members of the community being able to monitor traffic speed in their neighbourhoods, whilst being supported by the police.

Road signs

Road signs alert drivers when entering a 20mph area and they will also be placed at junctions where the speed limit changes.

Road humps will not be constructed as part of the 20mph speed limit on residential roads plan. Where the speed limit is lowered to 20mph it will be controlled by signs only. There are no plans to remove any existing road humps.
