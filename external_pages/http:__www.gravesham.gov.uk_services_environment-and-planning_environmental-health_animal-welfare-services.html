Animal welfare services

Animal control

Dogs allowed to roam and stray

Members of the public are advised not to approach or stroke dogs however mild their temperament may appear. The owner, or occupier of the premises where the dog is (or should be) kept, if known, can be warned of the consequences of their failure to keep their dog under proper control.

Although there are certain pieces of Council land designated where dogs are required to be kept on a lead e.g. Gordon Promenade, in order to make the areas more desirable to use, there are no regulations currently in force in Gravesham requiring dog owners to exercise their dogs on a lead in public areas. 

Collection of stray dogs from finders' premises

If a dog has been found within the borough or enters a property in the borough from which it is unable to escape, the finder should contact Regulatory Services on 01474 56 44 22 during office hours to arrange for the dog to be collected from the finder's premises by the kennelling service. If the dog is found after 5.00 o'clock or over the weekend, it should be held until the following working day, when collection can be arranged or it can be taken to the Civic Centre. The Civic Centre is able to receive dogs between the hours of 9.00am and 5.00pm Monday to Friday only.

If it is not possible to keep the dog until the next working day the finder should call the out of hours duty officer on 01474 56 44 22 and hold for the operator. A collection by the kennels can then be arranged from the finder's address. The above service is only available if the dog is found in the Borough.

If a dog is not in custody it is not possible to arrange a collection.

If you find the dog in another borough you should call that Council direct. The contact details are as follows:

  • Animal Control Officer, Dartford Borough Council 01322 34 30 88
  • Sevenoaks District Council tel no 01732 22 70 00
  • Medway Council tel no 01634 33 33 33
  • Tonbridge and Malling Borough Council daytime tel no 01732 87 61 81 and evenings and weekends tel no 01732 84 45 22

Lost & found dogs

Regulatory Services keep a record of dogs lost and found in order to help reunite owners with their lost pets. You may also be able to find your dog via the Missing Pets Bureau email info@missingpetsbureau.com or by post, Freepost SEA999, Primrose Lane, Croydon, Surrey, CR9 8WZ, or telephone 0870 1627 999. You should also consider contacting the veterinary practices in Gravesham and neighbouring districts in case the dog has been injured. It is also recommended that you contact the neighbouring Councils in case the dog has been found outside of Gravesham Borough.

On payment of the seizure, transportation and kennelling costs direct to the kennels and on agreement to pay the council's stray dog fee of £65 which includes the statutory penalty fee of £25 the dog owner can claim the dog back. The council does not have to give the dog back unless all costs incurred by the dog are paid. Please note that the person claiming the dog is the person who has to pay the fees, if you are doing this on behalf of the owner please ensure that they agree to pay you back.

Collar & Tag and Microchipping

It is a requirement that all dogs must wear a collar with an identity tag when in a public place.  If a dog is lost or strays this will enable the dog to be reunited with its owner more quickly, causing less distress to both the dog and owner.

It is strongly advised that in addition to your dog wearing a collar and tag you have it microchipped. You should consider getting this done as a matter of priority as it is a permanent form of identification. Most veterinary practices offer a chipping service. A 'chip' (about the size of a long grain of rice) is injected into the dog and carries a unique number that allows the dog to be traced to its owner once scanned. All of the owners contact details are stored on a National Database. The Council's kennelling service will scan the dog for a microchip and will contact the owner as soon as is practicable.

The Council has been advised that in the event of an accident whilst a dog is straying, if the owner's details are unavailable a veterinary practice will only carry out basic first aid. A microchip will enable quick tracing of the owner's details thus allowing in-depth medical treatment, potentially saving your dog's life. It is important that the details on the database with regards the owner's address and phone numbers etc are kept up to date as failure to do so may result in you not being reunited with your dog."

Could you give a dog a home?

Our kennels receive stray dogs from all over Kent. There are always ones looking for a new home. If you think you can give a stray dog a good home please contact our kennels on tel no 01732 88 34 68. They are open Monday to Friday 9.30 to 4.00 and Sat to Sun 12.00 to 4.00.

Dangerous dogs

A person who has either been bitten by someone else's dog or who has been a witness to an attack should report the details to the police. If the police are unable to pursue the matter they may pass the details to Regulatory Services for information. The Animal Control Officer will consider whether or not any further investigation is required by the Council. The Animal Control Officer is not empowered to seize dogs whilst they are on their owner's premises.

Dog Fouling

A new Dog Control Order came into force in April 2011 for dog fouling across the borough of Gravesham. It brings the Council's existing orders in line with the provisions of the Clean Neighbourhoods and Environment Act 2005 (CNEA).

Please go to the Dog Control Order for fouling page for more information.

Dogs Allowed To Foul On Private Land

It is important to remember that the Council's dog control order does not apply to private land such as open plan front gardens and rear accesses.  Owners of such areas should take appropriate measures to protect and control those areas.

Nuisances from the keeping of animals

The Animal Control Officer can investigate reports of nuisance from the keeping of animals on premises, including accumulations of animal waste, which the occupier has failed to dispose of properly. If the complaint is about a Council house you should report the matter to your Housing Officer.

Dogs barking and other animal noise

Reports of persistent noise from animals kept on premises, which may constitute a nuisance to neighbouring residents will be considered and investigation carried out where considered necessary.  For further information please see Noise Pollution.

Guard dogs

Enquiries about guard dogs on non domestic premises will be considered and investigation carried out where considered necessary.

Licensing of animal establishments

Any person who wishes to run an animal establishment or keep a dangerous wild animal must have a licence. Application forms must be completed, the relevant fee paid and an inspection of the premises carried out by the Animal Control Officer and the Council's authorised Veterinary Officer prior to any licence being issued. Further inspections are carried out at regular intervals. An investigation will be made if there are complaints against the establishment. This information is treated in strict confidence. See additional information on the Animal Establishment Page

Other animal related enquiries

There are other organisations and agencies who have certain responsibilities and functions with regard to dogs and other animals.  These include the RSPCA, the police, veterinary surgeons, and the Kent County Council.

  • Animal Health matters - Kent County Council Trading Standards
  • Animal Welfare matters - RSPCA
  • Dangerous Dog enquiries - Kent Police, telephone 01622 69 06 90

Dead animals

If you find a dead animal please contact the following:

  • If you find a dead dog or dead wild animal e.g. badger or fox, on the highway, pavement or verge please call the Council's Waste Management Unit on tel no 01474 33 74 49
  • If you find a dead animal (excluding farm animals) on private land you should contact the owner of the land.
  • If you find a dead or dying farm animal e.g. pig, sheep, horse or cow on agricultural land contact Kent County Council Trading Standards. (Kent Trading Standards).

Horses grazing on Council land

If you wish to discuss this matter or wish to make a complaint please call the Council on 01474 33 70 00 or use our general comments form.

For more information regarding animal control matters please contact Regulatory Services telephone 01474 33 73 34 or e-mail animal.control@gravesham.gov.uk
