Insurance

Claims Against the Council

The Insurance Section handles all the insurance needs of the Council, from arranging cover to providing a claims handling service.

Public Liability Claims

If you have suffered personal injury or if your property has been damaged and you wish to make a claim for compensation you must complete an Incident Report Form.   You can do this in the following ways:

  • Using our Online Incident Report Form 
  • Download a paper copy of the Insurance Incident Report Form (Claimant to Complete) 2014 (Word Document, 0.19 Mb)  for completion and return to the Insurance Section.
  • Telephone 01509 634566 and request a form to be sent to you for completion and return.

Please complete the form as fully as possible as the decision on your claim may be delayed if all the questions have not been answered. 

For your claim to be successful you will need to prove that the Council has been at fault in law. Although something may have happened there is no automatic right to compensation.

An acknowledgement will be sent to you on receipt of this form. If you do not hear from us within 10 working days of returning this form please contact us. 

Your claim will be passed to the Council's Public Liability Insurers and the Insurance Officer will  request a full report from the relevant Council service area.  That service area will complete an investigation and relevant documentation will then be passed to the Council's insurers via the Insurance Officer.

Once the Council's insurers have received your claim, they will acknowlege receipt and carry out their own investigations.  The insurers will correspond directly with you.

Motor Claims

If you have suffered vehicle/property damage or personal injury which has involved a Council vehicle a Motor Incident Report Form will need to be completed.

You can do this in the following ways:

  • By completing our online Motor Incident Report Form
  • By downloading a papercopy:  Motor Incident Report Form (PDF Document, 0.1 Mb) for completion and return to the Insurance Section.
  • By contacting the Insurance Section and requesting a form for completion and return.  

An acknowledgement will be sent to you on receipt of this form. If you do not hear from us within 10 working days of returning this form please contact us.

Highway Claims

Charnwood Borough Council does not deal with claims relating to slips, trips and falls on the public highway. This is the responsibility of Leicestershire County Council. If you have a claim please contact them on 0800 626203 or visit the county council’s website.

Refuse Contractors

If you have suffered personal injury or property damage which has been caused by operatives working for the Council’s refuse contractors you will need to contact the Council’s Environmental Services.

Tenants' Contents Insurance

If you are a Charnwood Borough Council tenant, your home will be insured by the Council.  However, this insurance cover is for the building only and not anything inside it.  You are free to find your own insurance but you may be interested in our Home Contents Insurance leaflet & application form which provides information about an insurance scheme which we recommend.  You can pay for this every week at the same time as your rent.

If you need further details contact the Housing Operations Business Support Team on 01509 634877.

Leaseholders

As a condition of your lease, the Council takes out building insurance cover for your property. An amount is included within your service charge to cover the insurance premium. The policy insures the structure of the building and its fixtures. The policy schedule gives full details of what is covered. It does not insure the contents of your home. This is your responsibility and you are strongly advised to make your own arrangements.

Making a Claim

When damage has occurred to your property and you need to make a claim you should request a claim form from the Council on 01509 634566.

Claims are to be notified as soon as possible and within seven days in respect of claims for malicious damage.

The Police must be notified as soon as possible in the event of theft or malicious damage.

You will need to return the form to the Insurance Section. An acknowledgement will be sent to you on receipt of this form. If you do not hear from us within 10 working days of returning this form please contact us.

More information can be found in the Leaseholders' Handbook (PDF Document, 20.96 Mb).
