Adoption

Thinking about Adoption?

Deciding to adopt a child is a life changing decision. Here at Cheshire East, we understand you’ll have lots of questions and have a team of experienced and dedicated Social Workers who are here to advise, support and help you every step of the way.

What is Adoption?

Adoption is the legal process that means you become the parent of a child or children who cannot - for whatever reason – live with their birth parents.

You’ll take on the same legal rights and responsibilities of a birth parent and provide a permanent home for the child or children you adopt. They will take on your surname and become a member of your family for life.

Who are the children waiting to be adopted?

There are many reasons why children need loving, adoptive homes. Last year 5,050 children in care were adopted and the children came from all backgrounds. It’s true that some will have suffered neglect or abuse, others may have emotional or behavioural difficulties, including attachment issues, health or educational needs. Some may have a physical or learning disability or experienced a number of placements and so be unsettled. There are very few babies placed for adoption, although children can range in age from a few months old upwards. The most predominant age range is 0-5 years.  The average age at adoption is 3 years 5 months. Each year the number of children adopted from Cheshire East is between 20 and 30.

Becoming an adoptive parent

We like to recruit the widest range of people possible to become adoptive parents because the children who need adoptive families are also of differing ages and have different needs. Last year Cheshire East approved 40 adoptive families. We are particularly keen to recruit adopters who could consider adopting children of school age, brothers and sisters together, children of mixed ethnicity and children with learning / and or physical disabilities. Our priority is to ensure we can find families for the children from Cheshire East, who need adoptive families.

Fostering to Adopt

Fostering to Adopt is a new initiative, where the local authority places a child for whom they believe that adoption is the right plan, with foster carers who are also approved adopters. The child will be placed before a Court has agreed a child can be placed for adoption. If the Court agrees that the child should be adopted, the placement then becomes an Adoption Placement. 

Fostering for Adoption brings it’s own unique challenges and rewards. It is not the right solution for every child or every family. In some cases it really can make all the difference in helping a child to thrive and grow in a safe and secure environment whilst we work together to find the very best way forward for the vulnerable children in our care.
