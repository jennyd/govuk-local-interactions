Home education

 

Choosing to Electively Home Educate
As a parent it is your responsibility to make sure your child receives an education from the age of five. Most parents do this by sending their child to school, but some parents choose to educate their child themselves. Â This is called Elective Home Education (EHE).

If your child is already on a school roll if you decide to home educate, it is your responsibility to put your decision in writing to the Head teacher. The school will pass this information with any concerns they may have about safeguarding or educational ability, to the EHE Team which is part of the Education Welfare Service.

If your child has an Education Health Care plan (Statement of Educational Needs) and you wish to EHE you must discuss this with the local authority for their agreement.

The Education you provide
You do not have to be a qualified teacher or follow the National Curriculum. The education provided must match the age, aptitude and abilities of the child. It should also be efficient and suitable, preparing the child for adult life and meet any Special Educational Needs the child has.

Support for EHE
EHE is a big commitment and there is advice and guidance online about resources or support groups in your area. Somerset County Council does not offer advice or guidance on EHE.Â 

Somerset County Council will intervene if it appears that parents are not providing a suitable education. The relevant section of the Act says:Â 

"If it appears to a local education authority that a child of compulsory school age in their area is not receiving suitable education, either by regular attendance at school or otherwise, they shall serve a notice in writing on the parent requiring him to satisfy them within the period specified in the notice that the child is receiving such education."

Return to Mainstream Education
If you want your child to return to school you can request a place at any time. You will need to go through the normal school admissions process because the school will not have kept a place open for your child.
