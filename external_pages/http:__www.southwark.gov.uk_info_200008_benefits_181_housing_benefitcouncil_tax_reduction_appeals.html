Housing benefit appeals

When you apply for housing benefit you will receive a decision notice from us. You can then use our online form to ask for an explanation of our decision or you can ask us to reconsider our decision.

If the decision is correct, we will advise you that we cannot change it but that you can appeal if you still think the decision is wrong. Any appeal must be received within one month of the decision date.

  • Your appeal must be in writing and signed by the affected person.
  • If you have had a reconsideration of your claim, any appeal must be made within one month of the reconsideration date.
  • You must clearly state the date of the decision you are appealing against and the reasons why you think the decision is incorrect.
  • If we have not already done so, we will reconsider your case. If we are unable to change our decision, we will refer your case to an independent tribunal administered by HM Courts & Tribunals Service.  

You can appeal against a decision by completing a decisions and appeals form. This must be printed, signed and sent to us - the form cannot be submitted online.

Council tax reduction appeals

When you apply for council tax reduction you will receive a decision notice from us.

You can then use our online form to ask us for an explanation of our decision or you can ask us to reconsider our decision.

If the decision is correct, we will advise you that we cannot change it but that you can appeal if you still think the decision is wrong .

If you still disagree, or if you have not heard from us within two months of requesting an explanation/reconsideration, you have a right to make an appeal to an independent body called the Valuation Tribunal Service.
