    Business continuity provides a framework for an effective response to disruptions that safeguards the interests of your key stakeholders, your reputation, your brand and your value-creating activities.

  • Keeping you informed

    In the event of an emergency, we consider it a priority to make sure you have access to the information you need to keep yourself and your staff safe.

  • Major Incident Cordons
  • Recovering after an emergency

    Working with partner organisations and the Square Miles business community, the City of London Corporation will to endeavour to facilitate a swift recovery after an incident.

The City of London's Security and Contingency Planning Group is available to assist businesses in the City with the development and exercising of their business continuity plans.

Business continuity advisory centre

Assistance is provided in such areas as:

  • Information on the major incident response arrangements within the City
  • Presentations to staff and management on any aspect of business continuity
  • The development of exercises and, if appropriate, participation in those exercises

As an independent third party, the Group is also available to discuss a company's plans and arrangements in strictest confidence. For more information email contingency.planning@cityoflondon.gov.uk

For organisations based within the City of London boundary there is no charge for these services.

The City of London's business continuity guidance booklet (335kb)provides introductory information on business continuity and some useful links to other sources of information. Another document businesses might find useful is the Business Resilience Planning Considerations document (479kb), published by the City of London as a result of a series of thematic workshops with input from City firms.

The City of London also contributed to the development of the Business Continuity for Dummies guide, including the production of a short animation. More information on this book can be found on the Wiley website and a sample chapter can be obtained through the Cabinet Office website.​​​​​
