Extra care and sheltered housing

 

Extra Care Housing
Extra Care Housing (ECH) is considered a specialist housing provision, mainly for older people or for people with a long-term care and support need or disability. ECH in Somerset offers support available on site 24 hours a day, 7 days a week with a designated on site care team This team delivers assessed care packages during the morning and evening, or whenever someone needs it. The aim of ECH in Somerset is to provide a cost-effective alternative to residential care, and there are examples of this working well in some schemes. 

Care and Support in Extra Care Housing – Consultation
On Wednesday 3 June, Somerset County Council Cabinet Members considered a report on proposals for the delivery of care and support in Extra Care Housing schemes from October 2016.

Following an extensive involvement and consultation exercise, officers have now been given permission by Cabinet Members to prepare a service specification for an integrated care and support service to be delivered in the existing Extra Care Housing schemes in Somerset. The most important thing for Extra Care Housing tenants is quality and consistency of care and support, provided by staff they can develop longer term relationships with based on trust and respect. 

Since the consultation was completed and the findings published, officers have received updated advice from solicitors which takes account of changes to Public Contract Regulations introduced in February 2015. The new regulations allow for commissioning of some care and support services under the newly introduced ‘light touch’ regime. This provides commissioners with greater flexibility to design and conduct competitive procurement in ways that best suits the particular circumstances and specific outcomes sought.

In light of this, it is now proposed that a procurement exercise is undertaken under the light touch regime. This will involve a competitive process designed to achieve the outcomes specified by tenants and their families through the involvement and consultation exercise. This approach is considered preferable to the direct award of contracts because it removes the possibility of legal challenge on the grounds of lack of transparency and competition in the procurement process.

Officers are now preparing a service specification and developing the timetable for the procurement process.
