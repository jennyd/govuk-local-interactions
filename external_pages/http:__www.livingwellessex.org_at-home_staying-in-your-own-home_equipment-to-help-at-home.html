Equipment to help at home

If you find that you're having difficulty with everyday tasks such as washing, dressing and cooking, or with moving round your home, there may be specialist equipment available which will allow you to do these things safely and independently.

Sometimes it may be necessary to make some significant changes to your property, to make it easier for you to move about and use your home safely and independently.

If you're the carer for someone with physical health problems, then having the right equipment in place can make your work both easier and safer.

Choosing and buying equipment

  • equipment factsheets, online catalogues and impartial advice are available from the Disabled Living Foundation
  • Rica provides consumer reports for older and disabled people on topics such as mobility aids, equipment and household appliances
  • local pharmacies, GPs and charities including Age UK, MS Society and the Red Cross also offer information and ideas for equipment to help you at home
  • the Red Cross can offer short-term loans of some kinds of equipment

  • Remap is a charity that designs and makes equipment free for the person in need, if it isn't commercially available

Mobility, medical and short-term equipment loans

Specialist equipment, including walking sticks and walking frames, can enable you to walk more safely, steadily and independently. Your local NHS providers (GP, district nurse or hospital medical staff) can help you source some equipment to meet your mobility needs, such as wheelchairs and walking aids.

Medical equipment such as hospital beds and pressure reliving equipment can also be provided, as well as short-term equipment loans (up to six months).

Depending on your situation, you may need a wheelchair permanently to get about. The NHS Wheelchair Service conduct assessments and can provide manual and powered wheelchairs to eligible patients for permanent use. Normally, your GP will refer you to the service.

You can also hire a wheelchair from the Red Cross.

You can undertake your own assessment - AskSARA

AskSARA is an online advice tool that will take you through a series of easy questions about your situation to give you advice on which equipment would be best for you.

It will provide you with a speedy assessment, links to buy equipment online and a personalised report you can print or email.

Support from Essex County Council 

The council will provide advice and can provide equipment to help manage everyday living tasks, such as washing, dressing and meal preparation, depending on the level of difficulty and risk you're experiencing.

To be eligible, you'll need to have an assessment. To enquire about assessments, contact the council on 0345 603 7630 (or 0345 758 5592 for Minicom users).

Essex Cares delivers sensory services on behalf of Essex County Council such as specialist equipment support and training, rehabilitation, and daily living skills training. Their equipment service includes demonstration, advice, and in some cases provision of equipment such as white canes, specialist smoke alarms, doorbells, and loop systems for hearing impairment. Their holistic assessment and support aims to help you maintain independence and quality of life. For more information, call the Essex Cares Sensory Service on 01245 256380 or 07825 995915, via textphone 01245 261715 or email sensoryservices@essexcares.org.
