Conservation areas

A conservation area is an area of special architectural or historic interest. Its character is made up of many elements, all of which go toward making a place unique and distinctive.

>

 

Why do we preserve certain areas of interest?

Some areas are important to our regional cultural heritage and are valuable assets in terms of the local economy and tourism. Old buildings and historic places enrich our quality of life and help preserve a sense of continuity in a changing world. We protect these areas from change or damage by making them a conservation area.

South Somerset has over eighty conservation areas and it's our aim to protect the unique character of each.

Go to our Mapping page to see conservation areas in South Somerset

Ilchester Conservation Area:

You are to invited to Contribute to the consultation about Ilchester Conservation Area.

Conservation areas are areas of special architectural or historic interest that are designated in order to conserve or enhance their special character. Historic areas are now extensively recognised for the contribution they make to our cultural inheritance, economic wellbeing and quality of life.

The Council is required to review the boundary of its conservation areas from time to time and to make proposals for their preservation and enhancement. In order that a Conservation Area's designation is effective in conserving its special interest, planning decisions must be based on a thorough understanding of the character that makes it special. We prepare appraisals to describe that character in detail so that whatever involvement you have, whether as a resident or decision-maker, the information is available to help to manage proposed changes in a way that will not harm the area.

At Ilchester the conservation area was designated in 1976 so a review is overdue.

A new character appraisal has now been prepared in draft for consultation along with some proposed amendments to the boundary.

If you have any observations you would like to contribute please send them by email to conservation@southsomerset.gov.uk or write to Andrew Tucker, Conservation Team, South Somerset District Council, Brympton Way, Yeovil BA20 2HT

The consultation closed on 27th January 2012.

 

 

How do I know if my property is in a conservation area?

You can view all conservation areas in South Somerset on our Mapping page.

I live in a conservation area, how does this affect me?

It is the responsibility of all those living within a conservation area to protect against poor workmanship and the use of inappropriate materials which can easily affect the quality of a building and the area as a whole.

Planning controls are stricter within a conservation area than other areas due to the fact that even minor changes to a building can harm the character of the area. As a result, there are fewer works that can be carried out as 'permitted development', that is, without the need for planning permission.

The planning regulations are too complex to be set out in full here so always seek advice from our Planning team.

Note: If the building is a listed building, separate listed building consent will be needed for most internal and external alterations.

Windows and doors- Original windows and doors should be retained and repaired where possible. If a new window is really necessary, have a replica made.

Article 4 Directions

What is an "Article 4 Direction"?

In some Conservation Areas, we have also served what's known as 'Article 4 Directions'.

An Article 4 Direction puts slightly tighter restrictions on the usual permitted development rights in order to control small-scale change, which if not monitored, could gradually eat away at the character of a conservation area.

An Article 4 Direction will usually only affect the 'face' of a property that fronts onto a highway - such as restricting the replacement of windows, doors and roof coverings.

Where permitted development rights are removed by an Article 4 Direction, planning permission will be required for the work. That means we are able to make sure the changes are done in a way that is in keeping with the area. However, there is no fee for planning permission if the work would ordinarily have been permitted development.

An 'Article 4 Direction' could affect you if your building is not already listed. This would restrict usual permitted development rights in order to control small-scale change which can gradually erode the character of an area. Usually it only affects the elevation of a property that faces a highway, for the replacement of windows, doors and roof coverings, and not parts of the home that are largely unseen from nearby.

Planning Permission will be required for this work but there is no fee payable if the work would ordinarily have been permitted.

See information on the new Article 4 Directions in Wincanton,

Article 4 Directions in Bruton and

It is always best to contact our Planning team first before undertaking any changes to your property.

What if I want to demolish part/all of the building in a conservation area?

Specific Conservation Area Consent is required for the demolition of unlisted buildings in a conservation area. There are certain exceptions, including demolition of small buildings of less than 115 cubic metres.
