Parking Permits: Car Parks

Permit options

Three types of permit can be purchased.  The cheapest option is to buy a Standard permit which has one registration number.  The other two options are the Transferable and Business permits.  With a Transferable permit, you get one permit and you can swap it between the two specified vehicles.  With a Business permit, you get one permit and it can be swapped between unspecified vehicles. 

Standard and Transferable permit-holders can use the Westmorland Shopping Centre Car Park in Kendal for an additional £30.00 charge.  This non-refundable annual administrative fee includes a card to use the entry/exit barriers.

When purchasing a Standard permit, you may be eligible for a reduction of £55.00 (pro rata) if you have an "A" rated vehicle.  "A" rated means the CO2 emissions are 100 g/km, or less.  This is stated in your log book, the V5 document, in section 4 or check with gov.uk Please contact Parking Services if you believe you are eligible.

New for 2015: Milnthorpe permit
A permit for two car parks in Milnthorpe, Park Road and Beetham Road, is available now.  This costs £200.00 for the full year.  The permit cannot be used in Milnthorpe Square or in other car parks within the district.

Payment by direct debit

It is possible to pay for your permit by monthly instalments, by completing the direct debit mandate.  We usually require the first instalment to be paid by credit/debit card, to allow time for the mandate to be set up.  We may call you to take this payment, so please provide a telephone number and email address.
There is no extra charge for paying by direct debit.  Please see below for our policy on surrendering a permit when paying by direct debit.

Owing to resource requirements, we cannot arrange a permit by direct debit for less than a six months' commitment.

What to do when you change your vehicle

Please let us know your new registration number.  You need to return the permit so we can change it to the new registration.  You can complete a permit change form at South Lakeland House reception, or send it to us by post.  To cover you for a few days please put a note on your dashboard showing the permit number which begins with the prefix SCX. 

I paid for my permit in full.  Can I get a refund if I no longer require the permit?

 Yes, you can.  If you surrender the permit before the end of a month, you will get the remaining months refunded pro rata.  Please return the permit by Recorded Delivery or hand it in to South Lakeland House Reception.  We will require your bank details as we only refund by BACS so please complete the refund form.   

I pay for my permit by direct debit.  What happens if I don't need the permit any longer?


Settlement date is always the last day of a month.  Further charges are liable if a permit is handed in when a new month has started.  Please do not cancel the direct debit before notifying this office.  We recommend sending the permit back by Recorded Delivery so you have a record of the date it was returned.  Alternatively, you can hand it in at South Lakeland House Reception, and you will get a receipt.

If the permit is returned later than the last day of the month then you will have to pay the next month's instalment.  (When the last day of the month falls on a weekend, we would require the permit back on the Monday following).  Once the permit has been returned, and there are no outstanding charges, the remaining instalments will be cancelled, and you will be advised to cancel the direct debit at that stage.

How do I renew my permit?

All permits expire at the end of March.  A renewal letter will be sent out in early March with full instructions on how to renew the permit.  Please ensure you renew you permit before the expiry date.
