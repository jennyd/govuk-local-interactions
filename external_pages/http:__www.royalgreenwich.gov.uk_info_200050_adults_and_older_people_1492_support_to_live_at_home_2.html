Support to live at home

Greenwich Telecare Service

View pages in this section

Telecare arranges help in an emergency. It is a 24-hour, seven-day-a-week service that helps people to live independently in their own homes. Several services are provided, including an alarm and emergency button, extra sensors and a home security package.

Watch our video about the Telecare service

Browse Telecare products and services

Telecare alarm

The Telecare alarm sits alongside your telephone and comes with a small button which can be worn as a pendant. When pressed, it connects you to a 24-hour response centre.

This service provides help and reassurance to people who:

  • are older and at risk of falls
  • are on occasion physically unable to move due to a disabling condition
  • have suffered an acute illness or medical emergency, such as a stroke or a heart attack
  • suffer from racial harassment, domestic violence, or are at risk of attack in their home.

The service costs Â£5.61 a week. If you have limited income and you receive Housing Benefit, there is no charge.

If you own your own home you may get help with the charge if you are on a low income with limited savings, and you request a financial assessment.

Telecare sensors

Telecare's high-tech sensors also help to support independence at home. These are additional sensors which trigger the Telecare alarm and are especially useful for people who have memory difficulties. These sensors will trigger a call when:

  • an unlit cooker has been left, or a lit cooker poses a risk
  • a bath or sink is about to overflow
  • a person fails to return to bed at night, or has been inactive for an unusually long time
  • an external door is opened and a person wanders from the property at night.

Home security package

Telecare also has a special home security package. The package includes:

  • an intruder alarm
  • a smoke detector
  • a bogus caller button (this button triggers an alert if someone pushes their way into your home).

Carbon monoxide detectors can also be supplied.

Who can apply?

Older people, people with disabilities and people who have been victims of hate crimes and domestic violence can use the Telecare Service.

To find out if you are eligible, contact the Greenwich Telecare team.

Make a referral

You can refer someone for a Telecare alarm using our Telecare referral form. Just download and print out the application form, and post or fax your completed form according to the instructions.
