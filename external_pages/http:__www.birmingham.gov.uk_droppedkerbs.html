Dropped Kerbs

Dropped kerbs allow vehicles to access a driveway across a pavement or footway.
Birmingham City Council must approve all dropped kerb applications. This process can take up to 12 weeks and will incur a charge.

To apply to have a dropped kerb in front of your home, please read the information below then follow the link to the online application form at the bottom of this page. You will then be asked to call our cashiers team to pay the non-refundable inspection fee before the application form can be completed.

More information

What is a dropped kerb?

A dropped kerb allows vehicles to cross the pavement or footway to and from a driveway. The work involves lowering and strengthening the pavement to create a ramp up to a driveway. A standard width crossing is 2.75m at the property boundary and 4.57m at the kerb edge.

Who pays for putting in a dropped kerb?

You must pay all costs involved in constructing a dropped kerb. We will inspect and provide a quote to complete the dropped kerb, we will also provide a list of approved contractors to allow you to seek further quotes.

Can I carry out the work myself?

No.

On what grounds might my application be refused?

Your application may be refused for the following reasons:
  • you do not have planning permission
  • you do not have permission from housing
  • the dropped kerb will not meet the Dropped Kerb Standards (see below)
  • there is a healthy well established tree in the way.

When do I need planning permission?

Before we can process your application, planning permission is required where your property is:
  • On a classified road (typically a main road. Attached below is a list of classified roads in Birmingham).
  • a building divided into separate properties e.g. flats
  • for commercial use
  • in a conservation area
  • a listed building.
Your drive must also be permeable to water or divert water to a drain otherwise planning permission is required. If you think you need planning permission advice, please contact planning by submitting the form at http://www.birmingham.gov.uk/preappadvice or make an application at http://www.planningportal.gov.uk/planning/applications
List of classified roads in Birmingham

My house is owned by Birmingham City Council, do I still need permission?

You will need permission from your local housing officer prior to applying for a dropped kerb. Housing can be contacted at http://www.birmingham.gov.uk/contact-housing.

What works are involved that I might have to pay for?

In addition to the construction, the following items may be included in your quote:
  • Removing a tree or shrub and replanting a new one
  • Pruning an existing tree or shrub to enable access
  • Relocating street furniture
  • Relocating pipes and cables
  • Strengthening manhole covers or other apparatus.

Will I own the dropped kerb?

No. The dropped kerb remains part of the highway, the construction fee is for building the dropped kerb. We reserve the right to alter the dropped if necessary as part of highway works.

How much does it cost?

The initial inspection fee of £77 is payable on application. Payment is currently by card only and instructions are contained at the top of the application form at the link below. If you have any queries please email droppedkerbs@birmingham.gov.uk.

Is there any help with the cost?

If you have blue badge or someone resident at the property holds a blue badge Then the application may be subject to a 50% reduction on construction of a standard width crossing. Any additional width over and above the standard crossing will be charged at full price. We do not offer instalment plans for crossings and all works must be paid for in advance.

How do I get a quote for a dropped kerb?

Ensure you have the correct permissions, then complete the online dropped kerb application form below.

How do I pay?

If you plan to apply please confirm all permissions are in place and contact our cashiers team to make a card payment of £77. You should give your name and address to the cashier. Once you have received your reference please attach this to your application in the field named payment reference.

Detailed requirements for dropped kerbs

  1. The dropped kerb must be positioned so that its use does not endanger or obstruct traffic.
  2. The dropped kerb is constructed using a similar material to that in the surrounding area; this may be tarmac, slabs or block paving.
  3. The standard minimum width of the dropped kerb is 2.75m at the property boundary and 4.57m at kerb edge.
  4. No dropped kerb will be constructed where the frontage is less than 4.75m from back of footway to front of building. Driveways on smaller frontages will not be granted permission.
  5. A tree assessment will be carried out by a tree professional in all instances where a tree may be affected.
  6. No dropped kerb shall be positioned closer than 1.5m to a tree. This may need to be more where specified by a tree professional. The tree professional may recommend access is refused where tree preservation is a concern.
  7. No tree will be removed for an extension or secondary access.
  8. No dropped kerb will be constructed where planning or housing permission is required and has not been granted.

Apply for a dropped kerb

You can apply for a dropped kerb online by completing our online application form. Before you can complete the form however you will need to have a two-part payment reference number, given to you when you have paid the non-refundable initial inspection fee.
