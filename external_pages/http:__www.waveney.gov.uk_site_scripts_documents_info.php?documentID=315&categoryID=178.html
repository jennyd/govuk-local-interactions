Helping to adapt your home

Do more...

  • Comment on this page
  • Print this page
  • Email this to a friend

A Disabled Facilities Grant is a means-tested grant offered by the Council to help towards the cost of adapting your home so that you can continue to live there.

An Occupational Therapist will look at your circumstances and assess what equipment and/or adaptations are essential and suitable to meet your needs and, if appropriate, send these initial recommendations to Waveney District Council.

Examples of these recommendations could include:

  • Providing access ramps
  • Widening doors
  • Provision of a stairlift
  • Replacing a bath with a level access shower
  • Providing ground floor toilet facilities
  • Making kitchen facilities wheelchair accessible

The maximum grant available is £30,000 per application, and will need to be supported by a recommendation by an Occupational Therapist from Suffolk County Council. The proposed works must be reasonable and practical depending on the age and condition of the property and must be necessary and appropriate to meet the disabled person's needs. The Local Authority can refuse a Disabled Facilities Grant if it believes the scheme does not meet the criteria.

We will look carefully at your needs, the works that would have to be done, the usable space within your home, and any other possible options.
