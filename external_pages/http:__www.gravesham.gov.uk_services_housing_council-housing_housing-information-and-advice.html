Housing - information and advice

Your Tenancy Information (including tenancy and estate management areas, caretaking service as well as tenancy conditions, mutual exchanges, succession etc).

Tenancy Conditions

Every one of our tenants will have signed a Tenancy Agreement upon commencement of their tenancy and will, therefore, be bound by the council's Tenancy Conditions. The current Tenancy Conditions were reviewed in 2011/12 after a period of 12 years to ensure they were relevant and fair. As part of this process, we followed a prescribed consultation process which involved tenants, partners and staff  and we issued the revised conditions to tenants from 30th July 2012. All existing tenancies at that time were bound by these updated conditions too, you can view the latest Tenancy Conditions here.

Tenants Handbook

We have reviewed the Tenants Handbook and brought it up to date, with greater accesibility. The handbook is now presented in seven sections covering all of the aspects of your Gravesham Borough Council tenancy.

You can view the individual sections by clicking on the links contained in the paragraphs below, but if you would like the information in a different language or format, please contact the council's Customer Services Section via the contact form

or by post to:

Customer ServicesGravesham Borough Council
Civic Centre
Windmill Street
Gravesend

Section 01 - General Information

This section covers most information tenants might need to know about the council's Housing Service, such as the Housing Officers, Data Protection and Freedom of Information, equality and diversity, customer care, making a compliment or complaint and there is a list of telephone numbers, both for Gravesham Borough Council as well as other local organisations which tenants might find useful. You can view the booklet here.

Footnote: since the Tenants Handbook was published, there has been a change to the legislation which affects the complaints process. There is now the possibility for persons who have complained about a repairs issue or a general housing management issue (but not a housing allocations complaint) and have exhausted the council's complaints process, to have their complaint considered by an intermediate stage in the complaint process, prior to the complaint being considered by the Housing Ombudsman. This stage is termed the 'Designated Person'.

Section 02 - Your Tenancy

This section explains your tenancy, your rights and obligations and gives further information on pest control, keeping animals, refuse and recycling services, garages and car parking and gardening. It also gives advice on tenancy issues such as ending your tenancy, succession, mutual exchanges and Right To Buy. You can view the booklet here.

Section 03 - Your Rent

The third section of the Tenants Handbook covers issues such as paying your rent, setting up a direct debit, benefits advice, debt counselling information, fraud and recharges advice. You can view the booklet here.

Section 04 - Repairs and Maintenance

You have responsibilities for various maintenance tasks within your home under the terms of your tenancy and the council as your landlord also has obligations which this section explains. There is also information on your alterations which you make makje to the property, the Right To Repair, rechargeable repairs, security, asbestos, condensation and safety matters such as the annual gas safety check and the access needed for this to take place. You can view the booklet here.

Section 05 - Living Together

This section covers community safety, CCTV and anti-social behaviour issues, harassment and domestic violence advice as well as information on vandalism, graffiti and waste management matters. You can view the booklet here.

Section 06 - Working Together

The sixth section of the Tenants Handbook covers the areas in which the council works with tenants to shape the Housing services that are provided by Gravesham Borough Council. The Tenant Particpation Team work with groups and individual tenants to set up and support residents associations, to run the People's Bank involvement process, the Living + Learning tenant training programme and to produce newsletters, Local Offers and the Annual Report to Tenants booklets.  You can view the booklet here.

Section 07 - Sheltered Housing

The final section of the handbook covers the specialist area of Sheltered Housing, which is available to tenants over 60 years of age (55 - 59 years old are able to apply if they are registered disabled) and who want to live as independantly as possible in small communities with healthy interaction between fellow tenants. There is minimal support within the community, provided by a Scheme Manager, the Out Of Hours service and the option of using the Community Alarm Service. You can view the booklet here.

Further information on any area covered by the Tenants Handbook, can be discussed with individual Housing Officers or Scheme Managers, to find the Housing Officer for your area, follow the link here or to talk to someone specifically about sheltered housing, call or e-mail the Sheltered Housing Team on 01474 337 375 or sheltered.housing@gravesham.gov.uk

Mutual Exchange

As a local authority tenant you have the right to carry out a mutual exchange (swap) with another local authority or housing association tenant. This could be anywhere within the borough or the country.

Housing Officers

If you have a query regarding your home, your tenancy or your neighbourhood, the first person to contact is generally your Housing Officer, they will be able to advise you or direct you to where you can get answers to your questions.

If you don't know who your Housing Officer is, you can contact the Civic Centre Customer Services Section (01474) 33 70 00 or you can search by address;(Alongside each address is listed the Housing Officer and the Housing Income Officer for that address with their contact telephone number).
