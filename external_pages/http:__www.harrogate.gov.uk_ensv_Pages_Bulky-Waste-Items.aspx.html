Bulky Waste Items

Page Content

The Council offers a chargeable collection service for bulky items of furniture from domestic properties. We can collect a maximum of two bulky domestic articles at a time such as a bed or cooker .

You will usually be contacted within 4 working days to arrange a suitable date and time for collection. Items will usually be collected within 10 working days.

How much will this service cost me?
The current cost is £37.50 for up to two items. However, the cost is reduced to £16.40 if you are in receipt of certain benefits,  (The Council may undertake a check to confirm that you are in receipt of qualifying benefits).
What is classed as 1 item?
Items that have more than one piece such as bed with a base or a 3 piece suite.
 

How do I arrange collection?
Please telephone 01423 500 600.You may pay for this service using a credit or debit card when you telephone. 

If you are in receipt of benefits our customer service advisors will let you know if you qualify for a collection at a reduced cost.

What can and can't be collected?

​Yes

  • Bed bases/mattresses
  • Chairs
  • Sofas
  • Wardrobes
  • Other household furniture
  • Fridges and freezers
  • Washing machines
  • Cooker
  • Microwaves
  • Dishwasher
  • Televisions
  • Computer items
  • Carpet and underlay
  • Bicycles
  • Fence panels
  • Wood
  • Interior doors
  • Plastic baths and basins
  • Garden furniture

No​

  • Car parts
  • Window units
  • Garage doors
  • Builder's rubble
  • Garden waste
  • Commercial waste
  • Hazardous waste
  • Lengths or wood/metal >2m
  • Any item that cannot be
  • safely carried by 2 operatives

 
Is there an alternative?
Yes, if the items are in reasonable condition, you could consider donating them to Essential Needs who operate a re-use scheme. They can take items of furniture and white good as well as small household items such as bedding, curtains, electrical items etc.

You could also consider donating your furniture items to any of the following charities; The British Heart foundation (they also take electrical items),  St Michael's Hospice or Disability Action Yorkshire.

Or you can visit freecycle for further information on recycling unwanted items.

Alternatively, you may take your unwanted bulky items to the local tip.  For more information on household waste and recycling centres in North Yorkshire, please see NYCC website

All waste carriers need to be registered with the Environment Agency.  Harrogate Borough Council's waste carriers licence number is PCD/UE5752FT. However, if you use a private contractor, check on the Environment Agency website to ensure that they can legally collect your waste.
