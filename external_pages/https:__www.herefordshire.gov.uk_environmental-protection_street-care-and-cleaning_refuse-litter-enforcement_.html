Litter

Littering in a public place is unsightly. It is also an offence. If you are caught dropping or leaving litter, you may be issued with a £80 Fixed Penalty Notice or prosecuted. Avoid this by using litter bins or taking your rubbish home.

Chewing gum and cigarette butts are also litter if they are dropped on the ground. It might not seem much, but when you add up all the discarded gum and cigarette butts, the effect is massive. This is why we take a robust approach towards littering.

We work closely with the waste and recycling team to keep Herefordshire clean and tidy.

How do I report litter?

To report build-ups of litter in the street or along a public highway, please contact us using the contact details below.

Trunks roads such as the A40 and A49 are contractually litter picked twice a year. Please do not report litter picking on these roads as we are unable to respond to additional requests. This is because specific traffic management is needed to protect the workforce. 

  • Online: Report litter service
  • Telephone: 01432 261800

How often are litter bins emptied?

This varies depending on the location of the bin, but bins may be emptied on a daily, weekly or fortnightly basis. We are carrying out work to determine the most suitable frequency for bins in different locations.

Can dog waste go in a regular bin?

Yes, dog owners are able to dispose of dog waste in regular bins however, they must ensure the waste is double bagged before it is put in the bin.

How do I report a full or damaged litter bin?

To report a full or damaged litter bin, please provide the location details to us at:

  • Online: Report a full or damaged litter bin
  • Telephone: 01432 261800

How do I request a new litter bin?

To request a new bin in your community please contact your parish council.

Search for your parish council by postcode

Community litter picks and litter picking packs

Following a significant reduction in the Herefordshire Council’s budgets there is a reduced level of street cleansing. Bins are emptied as before and we encourage individuals to take responsibility for their own rubbish. Litter can be taken home or put into litter bins provided.

We support community litter picks where we can and supply litter pickers and arrange for collection following a litter pick.   

Litter packs

Please contact 01432 349504 to request a litter picking pack which includes:

  • 5 litter picks
  • Gloves
  • High visibility vests
  • 2 hoops and black bags

On occasions, the litter packs can be in high demand and we would advise you to request a pack at least two weeks before you require them.
