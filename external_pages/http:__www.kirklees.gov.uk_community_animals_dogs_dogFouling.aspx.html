Dogs fouling

Last updated - July 2015

Dog fouling and the law

  • Dogs should be trained to go at home, with any fæcal deposits disposed of in a hygienic manner.
  • If a dog fouls while it is being walked, the dog owner or person in charge should clean up immediately.
  • To protect the public health and promote cleanliness, many public areas in Kirklees have been designated as 'poop scoop' areas.
  • This means that anyone in charge of a dog will commit an offence if they do not clean up any fæces left by that dog.
  • The person responsible can be issued with a fixed penalty notice, or prosecuted in the Magistrates Court where a fine of up to £1,000 can be made.

Reporting dog fouling

Report it

To report dog fouling contact the council on Telephone 01484 414700.

What the council will do

  • If you are reporting dog fouling, but have no details of the times and specific locations, or details of the owner then that information will be used to decide where we will target any future responsible dog ownership campaigns.
  • Any information you can give us about the dog owner(s) causing the problem, or the most common times that people walk their dogs, will be helpful to us and will allow specific enforcement action.
  • Any information given will be treated in confidence
  • The council will clear dog fouling as part of its normal planned street cleaning.

Evidence required and action that can be taken

  • If the problem is a an offence under the Dog Control Orders then formal action can be taken:
    • Someone has to see the dog(s) causing the fouling.
    • That person will need to give a witness statement and may be required to go to court as a witness on behalf of the council.
    • If an authorised council officer should witness anyone breaking the law, then we will take action.
  • If people are not prepared to act as witnesses, then we are restricted to advising offenders - a course of action which will not always give positive results in preventing nuisances of this type.
  • We will explain more about the best action to take when you are contacted.

'No dog fouling' signs, dog bins and poop scoops

'No dog fouling' signs

Some 15,000 signs have already been fitted to lamp-posts adjacent to roads in Kirklees, and in other places, but requests for more signs will always be considered.

Contact the Kirklees StreetScene Action Team and give the location where you think signs are needed.

  • Contact details

We will visit the area, check the location and assess the number of signs required. Signs will be fitted by the team as soon possible.

No dog fouling sign

Dog bins

There is no legal requirement for the council to provide dog bins. Dog waste bins are quite expensive to buy and fix, and also the costs of emptying and maintenance are high. Research has shown that where bins are provided, they are abused and often vandalised, rather than used properly.

There is no reason that you cannot place your properly bagged dog waste in any council litter bin

Poop scoops

The council in conjunction with some of the Parish councils provides "dog glove" dispensers these have a supply of "poop bags" in case you have forgotten or used the ones you set out with on your walk.

Please use these responsibly and only take as many bags as you need for that occasion.

They are also available from some pet shops and dog training classes.
