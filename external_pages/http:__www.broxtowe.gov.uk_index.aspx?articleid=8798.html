Missed Bin Collections

If your bin has not been emptied then it is possible to arrange for an additional one off collection.

 However please first check to ensure the correct bin was placed out on the right day. To check your calendar use the 'When is my collection' related article.

Before reporting a missed bin collection

Please read the information below to ensure that your bin will be collected when we return:

  • Bins are to be presented at the edge of the property, or other mutually agreed location by 7.00am on the day of collection.
  • Bins must be presented with lids fully closed.
  • The Council do not collect side waste.
  • No plastic bags of any description to be placed in the green or brown-lidded bins.
  • Please ensure that only the items listed for recycling are placed in the green-lidded bin. Check the list by using the link on the right hand side, 'Green-lidded recycling bin'. Items not listed that have been placed in the bin will need to be removed.
  • Bins that have been filled to such an extent that it is deemed unsafe to move or load on to the refuse lorry lift will be left. The householder will need to lighten the bin.
  • Black bins found to contain non household waste such as bricks, soil, stone, rubble or plaster will be left. The householder needs to remove these items.

Report a missed bin collection

Providing the above terms are met then to arrange to have your missed bin emptied either use the:

  • Online service by selecting the Report a Missed Bin.
  • Report a missed bin by sending a text message to 60075. Start the message with 'BROX MISSED' and include your postcode and house number - i.e. 'BROX MISSED NG9 1AB 32'. An acknowledgement text will be returned. 'Each message sent is charged at the user's standard network rate although some inclusive network plans exclude messages to short codes and therefore prices may vary.'
  • Contact our Customer Services through the details provided within the contact section.
  • Visit one of the cash offices.

N.B. This service is monitored to ensure fair usage.
