Garden waste recycling

This is a fortnightly service for subscribers.  It does not operate during Christmas week and New Year's week.  Check your collection day and this week's collections schedule.

There are various ways to dispose of garden waste in Epsom & Ewell.  You cannot dispose of garden waste in your green rubbish bin.

  • Home composting is the most environmentally friendly option
  • The Civic Amenity Site (the 'Tip') at Blenheim Road accepts garden waste and leaves for recycling seven days a week
  • If you only have leaves to dispose of you can subscribe to our fortnightly seasonal leaf recycling service 
  • You can subscribe to our fortnightly garden waste recycling service, which will recycle both garden waste and leaves. 
How much does it cost?

Your subscription is a one-off payment entitling you to one year's service.  You can choose between bins or reusable sacks.  Costs for the year 1 April 2015 to 31 March 2016 are as below.


240-litre brown wheeled bins cost £42 each per year for subscriptions paid by Direct Debit, or £46 if paid by any other method.  Request a Direct Debit form.


60-litre reusable sacks cost £10 each per year for subscriptions paid by Direct Debit, or £11 if paid by any other method.  Each sack is designed to hold a maximum 10 kilogrammes of material.  Request a Direct Debit form.

Leave your recycling out for collection in a clearly visible place at the edge of your property by 6:30am on your normal collection day every fortnight.

What can I recycle?

Garden
waste
    

Yes please:

• Grass cuttings
• Smaller twigs and branches
• Prunings and clippings
• Flowers
• Straw and hay (unless soiled by pet waste)
• Plants and weeds
• Root balls (shaken free of soil)
• Leaves.    

No thank you:

• Larger branches and logs (thicker than a wrist)
• Plant pots
• Soil, stones or ashes
• Pet waste, or soiled straw and hay
• Fallen fruit
• Any food, even if grown in your own garden.


How to subscribe

Call us on 01372 732000 to join and for details of how to pay, including by Direct Debit.  Please note:

  • Payments made by credit card are subject to a 1.5% charge
  • You can pay by cash, cheque or credit/debit card in person at the Town Hall
  • If posting cheques please attach a note clearly stating what the payment is for. Do not post cash
  • You can also join the service online.
If you wish to receive the 50% discount available to recipients of certain means-tested benefits, please visit the Town Hall to join the service as we will require proof of original documentation to confirm your eligibility. Please bring a recent official letter showing your name and address along with any document confirming that you receive means-tested benefits.

Subscriptions are based on a twelve month non-refundable agreement.

What about renewals?

The simplest and cheapest way to pay for your garden waste subscription is by Direct Debit. If you choose this method of payment, you need take no further action in order to renew your annual subscription. We will simply notify you each year that we are collecting a further yearly subscription and your service will continue uninterrupted.

If you subscribe using any other payment method, we will contact you in advance when your year’s subscription is nearing completion to ask whether you wish to renew it for a further year.

Who can join the kerbside garden waste recycling service?

All households in the Borough can join. If you live in a flat you may wish to consider communal collections where residents share a number of central recycling bins. Please contact us for details.

Collections are not available to commercial organisations.

Great news - bank holiday week collections just got easier!

To make your recycling and waste services more convenient, from now on we will make our normal Monday – Friday collections during bank holiday weeks. So you will no longer have to remember to put your bins out one day late. Just put them out on your normal collection day, even if that is a bank holiday Monday.

We will continue, as always, to work normally on Good Friday.

Christmas and New Year weeks will continue to be advised separately, to take account of the changing holiday dates each year.
