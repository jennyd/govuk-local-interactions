Household rubbish

Household rubbish is collected every week.

All rubbish for collection should be left at the edge of your property, clearly visible, before 7am on the morning of collection or as late as possible the night before.

Please refer to your collection calendar for your specific collection dates or use the link below.

Find out your collection day and zone

If you live in a property with a wheelie bin rubbish collection service:

You must put your wheelie bin and your recycling containers out for collection before 7am on your collection day, at the front of your property, clearly visible from the road but not on the pavement. The lid must be fully closed. Any additional rubbish that is left next to your bin will not be collected.

We can only collect rubbish from bins with Hounslow Council branding.You should mark your bin and containers with your house number if you have not already done so.

Recycle paper using your blue bag or place it in your recycling box along with flattened card, glass bottles and jars, tins and cans and other items like textiles, shoes and batteries.

You should also be using a food waste caddy to recycle your food waste. Both these containers are collected weekly.

Plastic bottles, tubs, pots and trays can be recycled using your white plastic sack. Use your green sack for garden waste in your green sack. These are collected on alternate weeks. You can check you collection days here.

If you live in a property with a black sack rubbish collection service:

You must put your black sacks and your recycling containers out for collection before 7am on your collection day, at the front of your property, clearly visible from the road but not on the pavement.

You must not place rubbish out in a wheelie bin if you have not been issued one by Hounslow Council as our vehicles can’t collect from these bins.

Recycle paper using your blue bag or place it in your recycling box along with flattened card, glass bottles and jars, tins and cans and other items like textiles, shoes and batteries.

You should also be using a food waste caddy to recycle your food waste. Both these containers are collected weekly.

Plastic bottles, tubs, pots and trays can be recycled using your white plastic sack. Use your green sack for garden waste in your green sack. These are collected on alternate weeks. You can check you collection days here.

You live in a property above a shop:

You should be using our special flats above shops collection service. This is a twice weekly collection of a clear sack for recycling and a purple sack for rubbish. You must not use black sacks for your rubbish.

The clear sack should be used to recycle paper, card, glass bottles and jars, tins and cans and plastic bottles only.

If you live in flat, which is part of a block:

You may have communal recycling and rubbish bins. The bins will be marked to show you what to put where. This includes paper, card, glass bottles and jars, tins and cans, and plastic tubs/ pots and trays, as well as non-recyclable rubbish.

If you do not have bins for any of the recycling materials listed, or any other way of recycling, please contact us. You can put recycling and rubbish into the bins whenever you need to, and don’t have to store material in your flat.

Please contact us if you see the bins overflowing, or a collection has been missed. For more information visit the 'recycling from your flat' page.

Assisted collections

If, for any reason, you think you might have difficulty using the wheelie bin please contact us. We may be able to set up an ‘assisted collection’ for you. This means we will work with you to make sure we can collect your rubbish and recycling. Email wheeliebin@hounslow.gov.uk or call 020 8583 2189 for more information.

Reminder about how to get rid of broken glass

We would like to remind all residents that broken glass should be wrapped up and placed in the black sacks for collection.

Please note: broken glass should not be placed in the recycling box. Loose broken glass placed in black sacks can cause serious injuries to the public and to our waste collection crews.

There was an incident where a member of the waste collection crews suffered a serious cut requiring stitches due to unwrapped broken glass being placed out in a black sack, despite wearing protective trousers.

Please wrap up broken glass securely in a newspaper and make sure that any broken glass is fully covered to prevent any injuries.
