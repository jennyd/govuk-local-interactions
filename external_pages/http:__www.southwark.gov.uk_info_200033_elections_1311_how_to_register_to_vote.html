1. The way we register to vote has changed: register to vote now

Individual electoral registration

Registration now takes place under a new and secure system called Individual Electoral Registration. You can now register to vote online. 

The new system means that everyone is now responsible for registering themselves. It is no longer the responsibility of the ‘head of the household’. 

Registering to vote with your council enables you to vote in local and European elections as well as UK Parliamentary elections.

How does the new system affect me?

Most people who were previously registered to vote will be registered automatically under the new system. Electoral Registration Officers have matched the previous electoral register against the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) database. Those people who matched were automatically transferred to the electoral register and do not need to do anything else.

For all sorts of reasons, some people did not match against these records and therefore cannot be transferred automatically to the new register. For example, there may have been a difference in the spelling of the two records. These people who could not be matched have been invited to re-apply. 

If you did not receive a letter to confirm you are on the new register, you may need to register to vote.

Am I already registered?

Do I need to do anything?

 Your situation Explanation and action required
I received a letter asking me to fill in a household enquiry form Complete and return the form to let us know who is living at your address. We will use this information to send them electoral registration forms to register using the new Individual Electoral Registration system.
I received a letter asking for more information The electoral services team have received your application but need to verify your application, please send the required information to Electoral Services to complete your registration.
I received a letter asking me to complete a new individual registration form Register online or complete and return the form
I have not received a letter You may not be eligible to vote in the UK or you may need to register under the new system
I have recently moved home If you have not already done so, please register to vote at your new address

If you do want to register to vote you will need your National Insurance number.

Who is eligible to Register?

You must register to vote if you are a UK resident, aged 18 years or over, and either a British, Irish, Commonwealth (those with leave to enter or those who do not require such leave) or European Union Citizen. If you are not a citizen of one of the countries listed on the electoral commission website, you cannot register to vote, even if you are allowed to live in this country. Citizens of European Union member states are not eligible to vote in UK parliamentary elections, but are eligible to vote in local or European parliamentary elections.

If you are unsure as to whether you are eligible to be on the Register, please visit About My Vote.

Special Category Voters

Young People

16 or 17 year olds should be registered to ensure that they can vote once they reach 18 years of age. 

Students

A student who has a permanent home address and a term-time address can be lawfully registered at both addresses. If an elector is registered to vote in two different electoral areas, they are eligible to vote in local elections for the two different local councils. However, you can only vote once in a UK Parliamentary Election.

Service Voters

If you are a member of the armed forces, or the spouse or civil partner of someone in the armed forces, you can register to vote either as a service voter or as an ordinary voter.

The way you choose to register to vote is up to you and will depend on your personal circumstances.

If you are based overseas, or expect to be posted abroad in the next year, it makes sense to register as a service voter.

This allows you to be registered at a fixed address in the UK even if you move around.

A service voter registration also lasts for five years, so, once you are registered, you should not have to worry about it while you are busy overseas.

You can register to vote as a service voter online

If you will be abroad or cannot vote in person, you can apply to vote by post or proxy.

Crown Servants

If you are working outside the UK as a Crown Servant or as an employee of the British Council, you can still register to vote. You can also register if you are married to, or are the civil partner of a Crown Servant or British Council employee and you are accompanying them during their employment abroad.

If you are employed by the EU, UN or any other organisation not directly in service of the Crown you cannot register as a Crown Servant.

You can apply to register to vote online.

Overseas voters

British citizens living abroad can vote in UK Parliamentary and European Parliamentary elections but cannot vote in local elections or elections to devolved bodies such as the Greater London Authority.

British citizens living abroad for more than 15 years are not eligible to register to vote in UK elections.

If you want to register to vote in Southwark, you can register online.

People without a permanent address

In certain circumstances people living in temporary accommodation can register at a temporary address or by making a declaration of local connection in order to vote.

If you think this applies to you please contact Electoral Services.

Applying to vote by proxy or post

You must be registered to vote on the new Individual Electoral Registration system before you can apply for a proxy or postal vote.

The Open Register

The open register is an extract of the electoral register, but it is not used for elections. It can be bought by any person, company or organisation. For example, it is used by businesses and charities to confirm name and address details. Your name and address will appear on the open register unless you selected the option not to be included in the open register when you registered. Removing your details from the open register does not affect your right to vote.

If you think you are on the open register and would like to be removed from the open register please contact Electoral Enquiries with the subject line ‘Remove from Open Register’.
