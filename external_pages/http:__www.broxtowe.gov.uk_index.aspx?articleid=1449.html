Council Housing

Broxtowe Borough Council owns approximately 4,700 homes. Over a third of our properties are for people of, or approaching retirement age.

The Housing department aims to ensure that a good quality home is available to all that are eligible for Broxtowe Borough Council housing and in the management of the Councils own stock.

Broxtowe Borough Council's housing department regularly reviews housing conditions across all tenures, identification of social housing needs and enabling people with special needs to live independently.

Management of the Councils housing stock involves;

  • Allocation of tenancies
  • Carrying out repairs and maintenance
  • Addressing anti-social behaviour
  • Administering the right to buy and collecting rents
  • Properties for rent

The Housing Waiting List

The number of homes we have to let at any one time is low compared to the number of people that need housing. We allocate properties via Homesearch, which is the Councils Choice Based Lettings Scheme. Properties are allocated to applicants on the waiting list who are in the greatest need for rehousing.

For more information on Choice Based Lettings and how to apply for Council housing, follow the related articles section.
