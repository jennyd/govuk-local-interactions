Discounts and Exemptions

Are you entitled to pay less or are you exempt from paying Council Tax?

Council Tax Discount on Annexes from 1 April 2014

A new Council Tax discount was introduced by the Government from 1 April 2014. It will be applicable to people living in a property with annexes. The annexe must either be in use by a resident of the property, or occupied by a relative of the person who pays Council Tax on the property with the annexe.

For full details follow this link to the Council Tax discount on annexes homepage

Discounts for Empty Properties

The Government has replaced the exemptions given to certain types of empty homes with a discount, the amount of which will be decided by local councils.

Teignbridge replaced Class C exemption with a discount of 100% which will be awarded to empty, unfurnished properties, this discount is restricted to a maximum period of 1 month, after the month has expired the full council tax is payable. Application for a 50% Discount_Uninhabitable Property [111kb]

A 50% discount can be awarded to empty, unfurnished properties for a maximum period of 12 months, whilst they require or are undergoing major repair work to render them habitable or are undergoing structural alteration or have undergone major repair work to render them habitable, if less than 6 months have elapsed since the date on which the alteration was substantially completed and the dwelling has continuously remained vacant since that date. Supporting evidence such as photographs can be emailed to revandbens@teignbridge.gov.uk.

This will be given for a maximum of 12 months following which full council tax will become payable.

Please note:

  • Works which are required to bring the property up to a standard to be commercially let would not necessarily meet the criteria for the discount.
  • If the property could be lived in whilst the works are being carried out (such as new bathroom, new kitchen, rewiring and plastering) it is very unlikely that the property would meet the criteria for the discount.

Empty Property Levy (Premium)

The Government has provided Councils with a discretionary power to charge an empty homes premium on properties which are empty and unfurnished and have been so for 2 years or more. Teignbridge has decided to charge an additional 50% on top of the council tax charged for these properties.

Some properties will be exempt from the Empty Homes Premium and will continue to pay council tax at the rate of 100%. These include:

1) a property which would be the sole or main residence of a person but which is empty while that person resides in accommodation provided by the Ministry of Defence by reason of their employment i.e. service personnel posted away from home;

2) an annexe in a property which is being used as part of the main residence or dwelling in that property.

Single Person Discount

If only one adult (aged 18 and over) lives in the property has their main home, the council tax is reduced by 25%.

Download and print the  Single Occupier Discount Form [96kb]

Discounts for people who aren't counted

Certain categories of people are not counted when working out the number of adults who live in your home. For example, full time students, student nurses and severe mentally impaired.

The following groups of people may not be counted subject to the necessary proof of entitlement being provided:-

  • The Severely Mentally impaired  Severe Mental Impairment Application Form [105kb]
  • Full time Students including 18 and 19 year olds still at school  Student Discount Application Form [60kb] and  Student Discount Information [13kb]
  • Care providers - caring for someone with a disability who is not a spouse, partner or child under 18  Carer Application Form [57kb]

    All the below groups need to complete the  All other Disregard Discounts Form. [8kb]
  • Persons in respect of whom Child Benefit is in payment
  • School and College leavers
  • Persons in detention
  • Student nurses / Project 2000 nurses
  • Apprentices
  • Youth training trainees
  • Persons resident in nursing or care homes
  • Care workers (conditions apply).
  • Residents of hostels for the homeless or night shelters
  • Religious Community Residents
  • Members and dependants of visiting forces
  • Persons with diplomatic privileges or immunities


Reductions for Disabilities Scheme

A permanently disabled person living in the property as their main home who requires at least one of the following may receive a reduction in the Council Tax:-

  • A room (other than a bathroom, kitchen or toilet) used mainly by the disabled person
  • An additional bathroom or kitchen for use by the disabled person
  • Extra space inside the dwelling to allow for the use of a wheelchair

If the property is eligible, the Council Tax will be reduced to that of a property in the valuation band immediately below that shown in the valuation list.

Download and print the  Disablement Relief Application Form [109kb] and the  Information on Disabilities Discounts [20kb]

Exemptions

Some dwellings are exempt and therefore no Council Tax is payable. The following is a list of exemption classes which have been simplified and regard must, therefore, be given to the primary legislation for the exact terms of any exemption.

CLASS B

A dwelling owned by a charitable body which has been unoccupied for less than six months.

CLASS D

An unoccupied dwelling where the liable person is held in detention.

CLASS E

An unoccupied dwelling where the liable person has gone to live in a care home.

CLASS F

An unoccupied dwelling where a person is acting as a personal representative of someone who has died and either no grant of Probate or Letter of Administration has been made or less than six months have elapsed since the day on which such a grant was made.

CLASS G

An unoccupied dwelling where occupation is prohibited by law.

CLASS H

An unoccupied dwelling awaiting occupation by a Minister of Religion from which to perform his/her duties.

CLASS I

An unoccupied dwelling where the liable person has his/her sole or main residence in another place to receive personal care because of old age, disablement, illness, past or present alcohol or drug dependence or past or present mental disorder.

CLASS J

An unoccupied dwelling where the liable person is now solely or mainly resident elsewhere to provide personal care for the reasons mentioned in Class I.

CLASS K

An unoccupied dwelling where the liable person is a Student and this was his/her previous sole or main residence.

CLASS L

An unoccupied dwelling where the liable person is the mortgagor (borrower) and the property has been repossessed by the mortgagee (lender).

CLASS M

Halls of Residence providing accommodation for Students.

CLASS N

A dwelling wholly occupied by Students or School Leavers.

CLASS O

A dwelling owned by the Secretary of State for Defence and is held for the purposes of Armed Forces accommodation.

CLASS P

A dwelling where the liable person is a member of Visiting Forces.

CLASS Q

An unoccupied dwelling where the liable person is acting in a capacity of a Trustee in Bankruptcy.

CLASS R

An unoccupied caravan pitch or boat mooring.

CLASS S

A dwelling occupied only by a person or persons aged under 18.

CLASS T

An unoccupied annexe to an occupied dwelling which may not be let separately without a breach of Planning Control within the meaning of Section 171A of the Town and Country Planning Act 1990.

CLASS U

A dwelling occupied only by person(s) who are severely mentally impaired, or by student(s) who are living with severely impaired person(s) and who would (under normal circumstances) be liable to pay the Council Tax.

CLASS V

Main UK dwelling of a visiting diplomat.

CLASS W

An annexe or similar self contained part of a property occupied by a relative of the other occupants who is over the age of 65 or severely mentally impaired or substantially and permanently disabled. (Effective 1st April 1997).

*Vacant is defined as unoccupied and substantially unfurnished
