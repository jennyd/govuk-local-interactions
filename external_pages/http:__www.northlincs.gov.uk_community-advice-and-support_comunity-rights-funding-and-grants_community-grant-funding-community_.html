Community Grant Funding

Community Grant Funding

We recognise the important role that community groups play in making North Lincolnshire an excellent place to work, live and play. We also wish to support talented individuals to become the very best that they can in their chosen activity. We have increased the funds available to support community groups and talented individuals. In return, recipients of grant funding will have to help to deliver key priorities for the area.

We will expect groups, where appropriate, to show that their projects will reduce the impact on the environment, tackle crime and the fear of crime, improve health, reduce health inequalities and provide support for vulnerable adults and children.

Applicant Organisations must have policies addressing all statutory responsibilities including health and safety, safeguarding children and vulnerable adults and equality and diversity, which meet recognised standards of good practice.

Queen's 90th birthday grants

To help communities mark The Queen’s 90th birthday this year, the council has set-up a fund to support local people and groups put on celebrations to mark this significant occasion.

Community groups can apply for a slice of this funding for everything from street parties and small events to supplying commemorative mugs or coins to young people.

Town and parish councils, community groups and even groups of neighbours can get support through the funding.

If you would like to apply for a share of the funding, please get in touch with Simon Seal, Partnership and Resources Manager, for an application form by emailing community.recreation@northlincs.gov.uk or calling 01724 297265.

Registered Youth Organisations

Registered Youth Organisations who have already submitted a constitution and safeguarding/child protection documents to register do not need to re-submit these with applications for financial support unless they have changed.
