Concessionary Travel Pass

This is a scheme which provides free-off peak bus travel by using a pass that is valid from 9:30am to 11pm Monday to Friday, all day over the weekend and on public holidays. If you use the pass before 9:30am you will get charged at the standard adult rate. Greater Manchester residents can also use the pass to travel on local rail services and on Metrolink.

Who is eligible for a pass?

Everyone can have a pass; the only criteria to qualify is that BOTH men and women must be older than the current pension age for women.  The following pension tool will allow you to work out the date for eligibility for the travel pass. Even if you are a man, in this instance please enter your gender as ‘female’.

Ready reckoner tool

You can pick up an application form from Stockport bus station. You will also need to provide:

  • A passport-sized photograph in colour;
  • Proof of your age, for example birth certificate, passport, driving , licence or medical card;
  • Proof of address, for example utility bills (gas, electric or water), Council Tax bills or letters from the DWP.

If you have a physical or learning disability, registered blind or partially sighted, profoundly deaf, or would otherwise be refused a driving licence under Section 92 of the Road Traffic Act 1998 you can also claim a pass.

Concession Plus

If you are disabled and qualify for a concessionary travel pass, you can also get a Concession Plus Pass. This gives you free transport on buses, local trains and the Metrolink in Greater Manchester at all times. It can also be used on local buses throughout England, from 9.30amto 11pm Monday to Friday, and all weekend and public holidays.
