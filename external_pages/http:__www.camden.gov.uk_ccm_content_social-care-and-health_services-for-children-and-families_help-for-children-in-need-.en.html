Help for children in need

The Caversham Family Centre is part of Social Services. We provide a special type of service to children under 11 years old in Camden, and to their families. We work with families to help protect children from harm and with parents and carers to help them learn the skills to make sure their children grow up and develop successfully.

What help can the centre provide?

Our services to help children and families:

Sessional care

To support families where children have particular needs because of speech or developmental delays, emotional problems, illness, disability, or because of concerns about a child’s welfare and safety.

Supported child minding

A day care service for children similar to those in sessional care. Registered child minders provide the service, each with specialist training.

Supervised contact

If children are not living with their brothers and sisters, parents or carers, they can meet up with them at the centre. Family workers supervise the meetings and can help if there are any problems.

Family assessment

For families with children up to 11 where we are worried about the child’s welfare. Over a six-week period a social worker will assess a family’s ability to meet the needs of the child. They will make recommendations in a report at the end. It helps plan for the child’s care either continuing to live at home or to be ‘looked after’.

Family support

There are five different parts to this service:

  • Direct work for children
    A social worker works directly with a child using play materials and art to help a child cope with, and make sense of their life experience.
  • Parents’ drop-in
    If your child is attending the nursery unit, you can visit the parents’ drop-in for company. You can talk about common issues, socialise and have fun.
  • Groups for parents and carers
    The groups vary depending on the need in the community. They can include groups on parenting skills, stress management and understanding children’s behaviour and needs.
  • Groups for children
    The groups vary but include play- and activity-based groups to help children socialise with each other or cope with a particular issue that is affecting them.
  • Family support work
    This is where a social worker meets with individual parents offering practical and emotional support. It helps parents learn and use more positive and effective strategies for caring for their children.

How and where can I apply for help?

You can contact one of the Children in Need Centres.

The centres are open Monday to Friday, from 9.00am to 5.00 p.m. However, if this is not possible, we can arrange to see you at other times.

If you need to see a social worker, we usually make an appointment for you. If it is an emergency, we can see you straight away, but if you are just visiting the office hoping to be seen, you will probably have to wait.

  • Contact the Safeguarding and Social Care Team (north)
  • Contact the Safeguarding and Social Care Team (south)

What will happen next?

We will ask you some questions to find out if seeing a social worker will help you. If we think it will, we will make an appointment to see you to find out more about what is worrying you and understand your needs.

When you ask us for help, we will respond immediately if it is an emergency, or otherwise within five working days.

After we talk with you we may decide that we need to do a more detailed assessment of your needs.

What is an assessment?

An assessment is where a social worker will see you to talk about your family’s needs and those of your children, and about the help you need.

They will then try to find ways of meeting your needs and may have to get advice and agreement to this. The social worker will talk to you about all the possible help you could get so that you can decide what would work for you.
