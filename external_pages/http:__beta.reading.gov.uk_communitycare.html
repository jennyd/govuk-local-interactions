Adult Social Care & Support

If you are finding it more difficult to take care of yourself or keep in touch with others because you are getting older or you have a disability or ongoing health condition Adult Social Care Services may be able to help.

We offer a range of information, advice and support to help you:

  • Stay living in your own home for as long as possible
  • Be as independent as you can be
  • Stay involved in social activities and
  • Live your life the way you want.

Some of the services we offer are free and available to all Reading adults, other services have specific eligibility criteria and people may be asked to contribute towards the cost - how much you pay depends on your financial situation.

See our leaflet " The help you need - care and support choices for older people and adults with disabilities [310kb]"

The Reading Services Guide has information on lots of community based services which could help you. There are also handy guides to help you manage your care and support needs.

Who are services for?

Our statutory services are for adults (18 years and over) who live within the borough of Reading who are dependent on others for care and/or support because of their age, a disability or ongoing health problems.

We also support carers who look after a dependent adult.

Our children's Learning Difficulty/Disability (LDD) team (0118 937 3697)  supports families with a child with additional needs and young carers.  

How do I contact Adult Social Care Services?

Older people and adults with physical disabilities, sensory needs, learning disabilities or ongoing health conditions - and carers of adults with any of these conditions - should call the Reading Adult Contact Team (ReACT) 0118 937 3747.

Adults with mental health needs - or their carers - should contact the Mental Health Team on 0300 365 0300

What support do we offer?

What we offer depends on your individual situation.

We will always try to help you to find community support to stay as well as you can and at home for as long as possible. 

When you first contact us for help we will ask you to complete an assessment to help us understand your needs. Assessments are free and available to everyone.

Once we are aware of your situation we may:

  • Offer information and advice
  • Refer you to our Reablement service for short term intensive support to help you regain your independence
  • Offer ongoing care and support if you are eligible.

 

What information and advice do we provide?

We can give you advice about your situation and provide information about local organisations which may be able to provide services and support you need.

If you prefer you can go straight to our Reading Services Guide to find:

  • Things to do
  • Support to get out and about
  • Services to help you live safely at home
  • Help with housing or finding a care home.

Information and advice services are FREE and available to ALL Reading adults, their families and carers. 

What is reablement?

This service offers up to six weeks of intensive support and therapy in your own home to help you rebuild strength, increase mobility and regain confidence following an illness or injury. Our aim is to help you regain all or most of your independence.

Reablement has a great track record - most people going through this service need little or no ongoing support.

This service is FREE and available to all Reading adults:

  • Whose initial assessment suggests they could benefit from this service and
  • Who are registered with or willing to register with a GP.

See our  reablement leaflet [104kb] for more information.

What is ongoing care and support?

If you are likely to need ongoing care and/or support we will carry out a more detailed assessment with you to:

  • Identify your needs and goals
  • Determine if you are eligible for this support from the Council.

See our leaflet  "Am I eligible for Ongoing Care and Support" [103kb] for more information about the assessment process and our eligibility criteria

If you are not eligible for this help we will offer advice about other organisations who may be able to help you

If you are eligible we will:

  • Calculate how much the support you need is likely to cost (this is called your Personal Budget)
  • Help you choose and plan the services and support you want to meet your assessed needs (this is called a Support Plan)
  • Arrange your support for you or give you the money we would put towards your Personal Budget so you can arrange and pay for your own care (this is called a Direct Payment) - if you prefer you can have a combination
  • Review your situation regularly to make sure the support you receive continues to meet your needs.

You can come back to us at any time if your needs change or you are unhappy with your Support Plan.

For more details see our leaflet  Choosing, arranging and managing and arranging your care and support. [474kb].

What sort of help will I get?

It depends on your individual needs and what you choose. For example, if you need help with personal care (washing, bathing, dressing) you may decide to employ your own personal assistant or you could ask a care agency to provide the support you need.

We will explain your options to help you make an informed choice when you are planning your services.

How do I get an assessment?

You can:

  • Complete our online Care and Support Needs Assessment Form. We will contact you to discuss the outcome, usually within 7 working days. 
  •  Download and print a paper version [191kb] and return it to the Civic Offices.
  • Call our contact teams:
  • Adults with disabilities/long-term illness - call Reading Adult Contact Team on 0118 937 3747
  • Adults with mental health problems - call the Common Point of Entry on 0300 365 030

We can support you through the process and complete your assessment over the phone.

If you have complex needs a social care worker will visit you at home to help you complete your assessment.

You can ask someone to support you through the assessment process to help make sure your needs and wishes are represented - this could be a family member, friend or we can find you an independent advocate.

 

How much will I pay for my ongoing care and support?

If you care and support assessment shows you may be eligible for ongoing care and support we will offer you a financial assessment to find out how much you would have to contribute towards your care.

We will never ask you to pay more that your assessment shows you can afford. People on low incomes do not have to pay anything.

If you the value of your income, savings and investments over the upper limit you will be expected to pay the full cost of your care.

You don't have to have a financial assessment - however, if you refuse an assessment you will be expected to pay the full cost of your care.

For more information see "Paying for Your Care and Support"


 

 



 

 

What if my home isn't suitable for my needs?

If you can't use essential facilities in your home (like your bathroom) you may be able to:

  • Get adaptations, equipment and alarms to help you manage at home 
  • Apply for a Disabled Facilities Grant to help pay for adaptations if you have a substantial disability 
  • Move to more suitable accommodation such as extra care housing or a residential or nursing home.

Contact Adult Social Care Services on 0118 937 3747 for advice. 

Can I get help to move into a care home?

Moving to a care home is a big decision. Before you decide anything please contact Adult Social Care Services (0118 9373747) for advice. 

We can carry out an assessment of your situation and explain your options which may include:

  • Adaptations, Equipment and Alarms to make your home safer and easier to manage
  • Getting practical help with personal care and support to get out and about
  • Moving to supported housing (sheltered or extra care housing).

If you have complex care needs that cannot be supported at home and your care assessment shows that you need to move to a care home we can offer advice about local homes that can offer the help you need.

For more information about finding a care home see Finding a Care or Support Service.

Where can I find out how Reading Adult Social Care Services are performing?

Our  Local Account Report [1Mb] gives you information about how we help adults with care and support needs in Reading.

The Local Account is a report of the Council's performance in Adult Social Care. It is a summary of what the Council is doing well and where we plan to do further work to improve the way we support people.

42% of the Council's net budget is spend on adult social care services. There are currently 19,400 people living in Reading aged 65 and over. It is estimated this will increase to 26,700 by the year 2030. Last year 2,890 people were supported, including 510 as carers, by the Council's Adult Social Care services.
