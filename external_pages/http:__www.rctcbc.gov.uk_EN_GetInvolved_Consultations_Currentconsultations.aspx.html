Current consultations

We are committed to working in partnership with our communities, stakeholders and partner agencies, using a range of consultation methods, so ideas and feedback can be shared and used to inform future service delivery.

Find out more about the many ways in which you can get involved, how and why we need your views and feedback and the specific service change proposals we are consulting upon, on this page.

RCT Together

RCT Together is the Council's forward-thinking approach to engaging with communities and individuals to explore alternative ways of delivering services and facilities.

Community ownership is a key theme of RCT Together, with the Council providing dedicated advice and support for those who wish to run facilities in partnership with the authority in the future.

This theme has already been successfully demonstrated through the community ownership of libraries, new businesses based in former Council buildings and childcare provision settings.

RCT Together forms a significant part of our approach to responding effectively to budget cuts by considering alternative ways to deliver services, while reducing Council spending in line with budget cuts.

A new online form has been launched to allow those interested in finding out more about  how to get involved and the support on offer. Find out more and enjoy the video showcase on the RCT Together web page.

The Budget Challenge

The Council faces a budget gap of £23.7m in 2016/17, with a further budget gap of up to £42.3m for the following financial year (2017/18).

Savings must be made across the authority, which will inevitably mean changes to the way in which services are delivered and maintained in the future.

We will consult and engage with communities and stakeholders to inform our work on the Budget Challenge in three main ways:

1. Annual budget consultation:

Our annual consultation on the Council's Budget Plan is an essential piece of work that allows us to ensure our spending priorities reflect those of our communities.

This gives us a firm foundation when it comes to identifying the services that matter the most.

Get involved in Annual Budget Consultation by contacting the Consultation Team or consider joining our Citizens' Panel to get the latest news and updates on consultation and engagement.

Find out more general information on the Budget Challenge Page.

2. Share ideas on how we can save money or generate income

We are committed to doing all we can to save money or generate income before considering making difficult decisions about the future of a Council service.

We know there are lots of residents and organisations in the county borough that have ideas on how the Council can save money or generate income and we want to hear from you.

All ideas, no matter how big or small, are welcomed and all reasonable proposals will be considered.

Share your ideas for money-saving or income-generation

3. Have your say on change proposals relating to specific services

When a decision has been made to consider changing the way a service is delivered, the Council is committed to consulting and engaging as widely as it can with as big a range of people as possible.

This can include an online survey, public information and engagement events, stakeholder briefings and the chance to share your views via email or letter.

You can view all completed Service Change Proposal consultations on the Completed Consultations Page.

Quality of life Survey

A huge range of partner agencies have come together to deliver a joint consultation for older people, aged 50 and over, to secure their views on quality of life issues.

Having a clear understanding of the issues people have, how they feel about life in their community, how aware they are of the services on offer to them and what service changes or improvements they would like to see is essential to all partner agencies.

This survey is a collaboration between the Rhondda Cynon Taf Community Safety Partnership, the RCT Strategy for Older People, Cwm Taf University Health Board, the Carers' Support Project and Age Connects Morgannwg (the new name for Age Concern Morgannwg).

By having a better understanding of the issues affecting you, partners will be  better-placed to plan and deliver the services you rely upon.

Working in partnership - Cwm Taf Community Engagement Hub

We are proud to be working in partnership with the above agencies on a Cwm Taf regional basis to make our consultation and engagement activity even more far-reaching.

Part of this work includes the launch of a new Joint Citizens' Panel as part of a joint European Social Fund project. Residents of Rhondda Cynon Taf and Merthyr are invited to join the panel, so they have the opportunity to give regular input into the planning and delivery of local services.

It is anticipated that feedback from the panel will be used to help improve a whole range of services in the region, including schools, waste collection, police services, health and more.

We need your views on our plans and priorities for older people’s health and social care services in Cwm Taf

Because of changes in the law, and the growing number of older people in our area, your local councils and health board are working together to look at new plans and priorities for older people’s health and social care services across Cwm Taf.

We want older people to have longer, healthier and happier lives, and to stay as independent as they can be for as long as possible.

We think this means we need to change the types of services we provide, and the way we provide them. We need your views on our plans and priorities for the future of older people’s services, to see whether you think they’ll achieve what we want for older people in our area.

We’ll be asking the public for their views from 27th July - 18th September 2015.

To have your say on our plans and priorities for older people’s health and social care services, visit www.cwmtafhub.com and take part in our online survey.
