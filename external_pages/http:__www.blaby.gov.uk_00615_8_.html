Community Grants

Summary

The Grants Scheme helps not-for-profit organisations or groups to improve facilities or provide services for the benefit of the local community. There are three types of grant available:

  •  Capital Grants up to £5000

  •  Revenue Grants up to £750       

  •  Staying Healthy Grants up to £1000

What can a community grant help with?

The different grants are designed to help with different costs. This can include things such as:

  •  Building works

  •  Equipment purchase

  •  Venue or transport costs

  •  Marketing and promotion activities

  •  One-off events

We can provide advice on which grant is most suitable for your group or organisation’s needs.

Who can apply for a community grant?

We can accept applications from groups or organisations that operate within Blaby District for the benefit of the residents of Blaby District. This could include:

  •  Youth Clubs                    

  •  Community Groups

  •  Sports Clubs

  •  Charities, not-for-profit organisations and social enterprises

  •  Schools/Academies (for extra-curricular activities)

  •  Religious and Faith-based organisations

  •  Parish or Town Councils

Your group or organisation will need to meet certain conditions to be able to apply for a community grant. Download the guidance notes below for the three grants to learn more about the eligibility criteria.

How to apply for a grant

Below are the guidance notes and application forms for each type of grant.  Complete the application form and email it to bmh@blaby.gov.uk or contact Beth Harriman on 0116 272 7718 to obtain an application pack or for further advice about the scheme and your eligibility.

Deadline for applications 2015-16:

 Friday 26th February 2016
