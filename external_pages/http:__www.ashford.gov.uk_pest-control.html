Pest Control

Ashford Borough Council is committed to keeping its council tax low, so it is essential that public funds are focussed on statutory services. With this in mind, the council has changed the way it provides pest control services.

From 1 January 2016, subsidies have been removed and charges introduced for services relating to rats, mice, bedbugs and cockroaches. Mitie, the council’s contractor hasn’t increased costs for three years, but as we move into year 4, they have reviewed their charges, illustrated below.

Local authorities are not legally required to provide a pest control service, although they do have a duty to keep their district free from rats and mice.

To find out more, read our FAQs

Charged Services

Please note that our contractor does not accept cheques.

Mitie also provide services for other common pests such as dog and cat fleas, ants, mites, moths, beetles, cluster flies, weevils and wasps but these are not classed as a health hazard and a charge is made for treatment.

We do not offer a service for any of the following: bees (all types), birds, foxes, moles, squirrels, rabbits, mosquitoes, woodworm, slugs, snails, snakes, or badgers.

Callers can contact the contractor on 0844 335 0330, but there will be a charge for any service they provide.

For information regarding foxes please visit the Fox Project website.

Concerns regarding protection or enhancement of wildlife please contact Natural England on 0845 600 3078 or DEFRA on  08459 33 55 77.

Questions or concerns regarding honey bees can be referred to the Kent Bee Keepers Association.

Pest Control Servies FAQs:

Why are you changing the way this service is provided?

Ashford Borough Council is committed to keeping its council tax low, so it is essential that public funds are focussed on statutory services. We are therefore reviewing our costs and services, bringing them in line with other Kent authorities.

So from now on I will have to pay for pest control services?

Yes, residents will need to source their own means of pest control.

Ashford isn’t the only local authority to introduce these charges - of 10 other Kent authorities, six do not offer any form of concession for those on means tested benefits.

When will the changes take place?

From 1st January 2016, all subsidies have been removed and charges introduced for services relating to rats, mice, bedbugs and cockroaches.

Should I still contact Mitie for pest control enquiries?

Yes, you can contact Mitie by calling 0844 335 0330.

Although Mitie are our approved contractors, residents are free to shop around to find their preferred supplier and at a rate that suits them, or purchase pest control supplies from their local DIY store.

Why are you trialling the proposed changes?  

We will be embarking on a joint procurement with Maidstone Borough Council and Swale Borough Council in 2017, so now is the ideal time to trial the new charging structure and monitor any environmental impacts prior to full procurement. Should an environmental issue occur, budget savings will provide a contingency fund.

Could this lead to rat infestations in the borough?

We certainly hope not, this did not happen in Swale when charges were introduced. We will be monitoring the situation closely and we do have contingency money set aside to offset any potential impact.
