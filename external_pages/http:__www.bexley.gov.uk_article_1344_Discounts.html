Discounts

Am I entitled to a council tax discount?

Introduction

The full charge assumes there are two (or more) adult occupiers and discounts may be given if there is only one adult resident. In some cases people are 'disregarded' - that is they are treated as if they are not resident and this can give rise to a discount.

The main discounts available are:

Single persons discount

If there is only one adult occupier, you may claim a 25% discount.

The Council will review awards for discounts and reserves the right to use credit reference agencies to validate any such claims.

We have recently commenced a review of Single Person Discounts. If you receive a form please return this without delay. If the form is not returned it may result in your Single Person Discount being removed. If you wish to speak to someone please call 0345 302 2311.

Family annexes

From 1 April 2014, a new Council Tax discount of 50% is being introduced for family annexes.

If you currently have an annexe which is lived in by a family member you can receive a 50% Council Tax discount on the annexe from 1 April 2014.

The new discount is not based on occupation or furniture but on use of the annexe by the resident of the main building or occupation by a family member. If a non family member occupies an annexe the discount will not apply.

If you believe you may qualify please contact Council Tax.

Students

How does Council Tax classify a student?

  • A person undertaking a full-time course of education, e.g. University and Higher Education students
  • A person undertaking a qualifying course of education, e.g. for A Levels and further education
  • A foreign language assistant registered with the British Council
  • A foreign partner/dependant of a full time student

People under 20 years old who leave school or finish a course at Further Education College, from 1 May and 31 October, are disregarded for Council Tax purposes provided they are still under 20 years old and they have become a full time student by 1 November (whichever is the soonest) and have not taken up full-time employment.

People studying evening classes, courses taken in connection with a person's job or day release courses are not classified as a student.

What is a full time course of education?

  • It is a full-time course of education at a prescribed educational establishment such as a university, polytechnic or college
  • Takes at least 24 weeks a year
  • Involves at least 21 hours of study a week
  • Includes student nurses studying academic courses at university or polytechnic and;
  • Students who distance learn and don't attend an educational establishment

Please note that if your educational establishment, for funding purposes, states you are a full time student on your certificate, but it also states you are studying less than 24 weeks a year or 21 hours a week, you are not classified as a full time student for Council Tax purposes. Therefore you will not be entitled to any Council Tax reduction.

What is a qualifying course of education?

  • It is a course, which lasts more than 3 months and at least 12 hours a week for any qualification up to A level, ONC or OND standard
  • Under 20 years old

How do I know if I'm entitled to a Council Tax discount or an exemption?

  • Dwellings where all the occupiers are full time students will be exempt
  • Where full time students live with a parent or share a home with non-students they could be eligible for a discount

How do I prove I am a student?

You will need to provide a copy of your student certificate and this is available from your place of education.

Please note letters which offer placements on courses are not acceptable as they do not prove a student has commenced studying.

Foreign Language Assistants

Foreign Language Assistants are not issued student certificates.

To qualify for a Council Tax reduction we require a letter from the British Council confirming that the applicant is registered with them.

Foreign partners or dependants

Foreign partners or dependants of a student are not counted for Council Tax on the condition that they are not a British Citizen and they are not allowed to work or claim benefit in this country. A copy of the student certificate and a copy of the passport of the student partner/dependant are required.

Annual reviews

The Council has a duty each year to review all student discounts and exemptions awarded. Therefore you will receive a review form to complete. This will ask you to confirm you are still a full time student and entitled to a Council Tax reduction and you will have to provide a copy of your current student certificate in order to continue to receive your reduction.

If you receive this form please complete and return it as quickly as possible if you wish your reduction to continue.

Other discounts for people who are disregarded residents and meet qualifying conditions 

  • People aged over 18 who have child benefit paid for them
  • Apprentices
  • Severely mentally impaired people
  • Long-term residents of hospitals, residential care homes or nursing homes
  • Volunteer care workers
  • People providing care for someone they live with
  • Members of religious communities
  • Members of international headquarters, defence organisations and visiting forces
  • People who are in prison or detention (except for non-payment of fines or local taxes)
  • People staying in hostels or night shelters

Please apply as soon as you believe there is an entitlement to a discount.

If you have been given a discount you must tell us immediately if your circumstances change. 

You may appeal against our refusal to award a discount for a specific period. However you may not appeal against the amount of the discount. You must put your appeal in writing to the council tax office stating your reasons. If you are not satisfied with the decision you can then appeal to the Valuation Tribunal Service.

Many services are now available by visiting Online Services .There are also frequently used discount forms available under related downloads, which you can print and post back to us with any supporting information, if required. If you cannot find or are uncertain which form is applicable please email bexley.ctax@secure.capita.co.uk providing as much information as possible.
