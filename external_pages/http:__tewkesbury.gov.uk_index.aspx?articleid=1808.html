How to register to vote

Changes to the Registration system

Individual Electoral Registration (IER) - from 10 June 2014

The way you register to vote has changed. A new system called Individual Electoral Registration or IER has been introduced. Read on for more information or go straight to https://www.gov.uk/register-to-vote (externally managed Government website) in order to register online.

How is the new system different?

Previously, the 'head of every household' was responsible for registering everyone who lived at each address each year. They would do this by completing or checking the annual canvass form that is posted to properties by local authorities every year. Now that the new system has been introduced unregistered people who want to register to vote will need to register by filling out an individual form.

Under the new system people can now also register to vote using a new user friendly online form via a central government run website: https://www.gov.uk/register-to-vote Each person newly applying to be added to the Electoral Register will be asked to give their National Insurance number and date of birth. This will allow each person's application to be verified against the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP) database before they are added to the Register.

Persons already registered to vote at an address in the Borough

If a person is already on the Electoral Register with Tewkesbury Borough Council their information will be compared to the DWP database. Those people who match the information held on that separate database will not be required to give any additional information to Tewkesbury Borough Council. They will be transferred automatically to the newly updated Electoral Register and sent a letter of confirmation letting them know that they need do nothing more until further notice.

A minority of people who are already registered to vote under the existing system (up until June 2014) will need to take action to make sure they are registered under the new system (June 2014 onwards). Those people whose name and address cannot be matched against the DWP database will be sent a letter by Tewkesbury Borough Council inviting them to apply to register again and provide further details (National Insurance number and date of birth) in order to support their application.

Empty properties

Those properties that are currently listed as empty under the existing system (2013/2014 Electoral Register) will receive a letter called a Household Enquiry Form. This form will ask whether or not any persons should be registered to vote at that address and will ask for names of those persons so that an individual voting registration form can be sent to them.

Why are these changes being made?

These changes, known overall as "Individual Electoral Registration" (IER), will bring registration into the 21st century with the introduction of online registration for everyone who is eligible.

These changes to the registration process aims to provide a more convenient, secure and modern way of registering. IER will address vulnerabilities in the current electoral registration process and will maximise both the accuracy and completeness of the Electoral Register. It will improve the integrity of the system and engagement with each potential voter.

Look out for more information in the post from mid June 2014 onwards telling you about Individual Electoral Registration.

To register online visit: https://www.gov.uk/register-to-vote (externally managed Government website)

For more information on voting visit: https://www.gov.uk/yourvotematters (externally managed Government website)

Registering to vote

  • Register online at https://www.gov.uk/register-to-vote (externally managed Government website).
  • Visit https://www.gov.uk/register-to-vote (externally managed Government website) and download and print a form for completion by hand, which should then be posted to this office at the address below.
  • Email Tewkesbury Borough Council Electoral Services and request that an individual voting registration form be posted out to you. You will need to provide your postal address in your email. The form that will be sent to you can only be used by one person, so if you need more than one form please say so and provide the names of all persons to receive a form.
  • Phone Tewkesbury Borough Council Electoral Services 01684 272025 and request that an individual voting registration form be posted out to you via second class post.

Please note that during the change over from the old registration system to IER there may be a slight delay in sending a form out to you. Please bear with us while we implement and get used to these new changes to the registration system.

You should ensure that your form is signed, that all sections have been completed (where applicable) and then post your completed form to:

Electoral Services, Tewkesbury Borough Council, Council Offices, Gloucester Road, Tewkesbury, Gloucestershire, GL20 5TT.

Please note the following before completing an individual voting registration form:

  • Persons wishing to register as service voters, crown voters, overseas electors or persons of no fixed abode (declaration of local connection) can also download and print off an application form for these purposes from the government website https://www.gov.uk/register-to-vote
  • There are deadlines for registering to vote in the run up to an election. If an election is imminent or if you are concerned that this may apply to you, please contact Electoral Services immediately 01684 272025 for more information.
  • There are monthly deadlines for return voting registration forms in relation to credit referencing. Residents are responsible for ensuring that completed voting registration form are returned to this department before such deadlines, IF relevant to them.
  • There are two versions of the Register of Electors. More information on this is provided below.

The two versions of the Register of Electors

The Electoral Registration Officer makes and keeps two versions of the Electoral Register - the Full Register and the Open Register (also known as the Edited Register).

The Full Register lists everyone who is entitled to vote and is available for general viewing at the borough council offices as a paper document. Only certain people and organisations can receive copies of the Full Register, and they can only use it for specified purposes. The main use of the Full Register is for elections and referendums, but it can also be used for other purposes such as the prevention and detection of crime and for checking your identity when you have applied for credit. Details of others who can have copies of the Full Register and what they can use it for are given in the Representation of the People (England and Wales) (Amendment) Regulations 2002 (SI 2002 No.1871). It is a criminal offence not to comply with the regulations. For example, it is a criminal offence either to pass the Full Register on to anyone or to use it for any purpose, except as allowed by the regulations. These regulations are available on the internet at www.legislation.hmso.gov.uk/stat.htm or you can buy a copy from The Stationery Office Ltd.

The Open/Edited Register leaves out the names and addresses of people who have asked for them to be excluded from that version of the Register. The Open/Edited Register can be bought by anyone and they may use it for any purpose. You can choose not to appear on the Open Register by ticking the relevant box on the individual voter registration form when you register to vote.
