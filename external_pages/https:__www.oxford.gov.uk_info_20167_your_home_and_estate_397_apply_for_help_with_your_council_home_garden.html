Apply for help with your Council Garden

Our garden maintenance scheme entitles some of our housing tenants to a maximum of one hour's work once a month to cut lawns, prune and cut hedges and borders. This equates to two gardeners attending to your garden for 30 minutes.

Some smaller gardens may receive fewer visits, at our discretion. The scheme is not for gardens that are excessively overgrown.

Eligibility

The scheme is limited to those in most need. In order to qualify you need to be; 

  • The tenant of the property
  • In receipt of Housing Benefit (full or partial).
  • Have no one else living at your property, or any family living in Oxfordshire, that could look after your garden for you

And also be EITHER

  • 60 or older and physically unable to cope with your garden; OR
  • Under 60 with a physically disability (Disability Living Allowance proof required).

You will need to send us additional information to verify 

How to apply 

To apply for next year's Garden Maintenance Scheme, check you meet the criteria below and then download the application form or contact us for a form:

Garden Scheme Application Form

The deadline for applications is Friday 18 March 2016. Applications received after this date will be put on a waiting list in date order of when the application was received.

Winter Clearance Scheme

Our Winter scheme runs over the winter period. The scheme is open to any of our tenants that need help getting their badly overgrown garden back under control.

It involves a one off visit to cut back overgrowth, brambles etc. to a level that you are then able to manage it yourself.

There are limited spaces available so please contact us on 0800 22 76 76 to register.
