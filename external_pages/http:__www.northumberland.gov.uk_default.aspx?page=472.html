Council tax - how to pay

We use council tax to fund essential services across Northumberland. Choose from one of the options below to pay your council tax.

  • Northumberland County Council

Pay council tax by direct debit

Using direct debit is a simple way to ensure your payments are paid automatically each month. Its efficiency means lower collection costs and lower council tax rises. You have the option to pay 10 or 12-monthly instalments on either the 1st, 15th or 28th of the month.

What you will need to set up your direct debit
Please have the following details ready when setting up your direct debit. You will be able to find most of this information in your cheque book or on your debit card:
  • bank or building society name
  • account number and sort code
  • branch address
  • council tax account number 
The direct debit guarantee
  • This guarantee is offered by all banks and building societies that take part in the direct debit scheme.
  • The efficiency and security of the scheme is monitored and protected by your own bank or building society.
  • If there are any changes to the amounts to be paid or the payment dates, Northumberland County Council will notify you 10 working days in advance of your account being debited or as otherwise agreed.
  • If an error is made by either Northumberland County Council or your bank or building society, you are guaranteed a full and immediate refund from your branch.
  • You can cancel a direct debit at any time by writing to your bank or building society.

Enquire about council tax

Can I pay my council tax over 12 months?
Yes. The option is available to pay your council tax and business rates over 12 months, or the number of whole months remaining in the financial year.
  • direct debit – you can pay on either the 1st, 15th or 28th of the month, set up your direct debit online here
  • cash – you can pay over 12 months but only with an instalment date of the 1st of the month
What happens if I fail to pay my council tax?
We will take action to recover money owed. A reminder notice will be issued and if a payment is still not made, we can apply to the magistrates’ court for the issue of a summons. Following this, if a payment is still not made the court will issue a liability order, allowing the council to enforce payment by either:
  • deducting money from your earnings
  • deducting money from income support, jobseekers' allowance and employment support allowance
  • asking an enforcement agent to collect the money or remove goods to the value of the debt
  • issuing a petition for bankruptcy
