Services for carers

Personalisation for carers means tailoring support to a person’s individual needs with the carer being part of the discussion about support for them and support for the person they are looking after.  Our offer to carers operates on two levels:

Level 1

Available for all carers.

Universal services

These are a wide range of services funded by the Council that people can access themselves. Services include:

  • emotional and practical support
  • counselling
  • advice and information
  • support groups
  • money and benefit advice
  • assisting hospital discharge
  • carers participation groups
  • befriending
  • Carers Support Fund.

You can access these services by contacting Carers Hub Plymouth on 01752 201890 or visit the Carers Hub Plymouth website.

Level 2

Available for all carers of people eligible for funding from the Council

Includes level 1 services, plus a proportion of the personal budget and support plan of the cared for and will focus on things that will allow the carer to continue in his/her caring role. The amount will vary from person to person depending on each individual situation.

Our assessment process takes into account the amount of informal care provided and the self-reported impact this has on the informal carer(s). This means that the carers’ entitlement to an assessment from the Council is built into the self-directed support process.

If a carer wishes to have their assessment separate from that of the person they provide care for or their cared for does not wish to have the involvement of our department then they can have a separate carers assessment with a social worker. This can be carried out in the home at a time that is convenient to them.

Customer feedback tells us that our current offer is confusing, fragmented and difficult to navigate. Therefore to better support the carer our commissioners are increasing the investment in universal services which includes:

  • Better integrated advice and information
  • Community based hardship fund
  • Befriending and peer support

You can find out more about these services on the Plymouth Online Directory website.

You can download regular Carers Newsletters to provide you with advice and information relevant to carers. It includes updates about new services available to support carers.
