Street Care and Cleansing

An efficient and effective street cleansing service is essential to provide the kind of environment that we would like to live in. The quality of the local environment, and in particular standards of street cleansing, are increasingly becoming the measure that the public uses to judge how well an area is being managed and its suitability as a place to live, work, visit or bring up a family.Â 

Our aim is to keep the District a clean, environmentally safe, well managed and pleasant place to be.

Cleaner streets cannot be achieved solely through better cleansing - we need people to take responsibility for litter and waste in the first place - and where this doesn't happen we will seek to take robust enforcement action.

How often do streets get cleaned?

Mid Suffolk District Council carries out routine and reactive street cleaning of adopted roads and footpaths within towns and villages throughout the district. Different areas of the districts are cleaned on different frequencies, but as a general rule, town centres are cleaned every day and rural areas as and when required.
It is important to note however, that our schedules are flexible and we do not clean a street that does not need cleaning. The emphasis of the Council is to do our best to keep the district clean. That means that if you report areas (or we find areas) that have become a problem, we will deal with them rather than sticking to a schedule just because it exists.
To report a problem with litter 0845 6066170 or click here

Does the Council sweep roads?

The Council is responsible for the scheduled mechanical sweeping of all kerbed and drained roads within the district. This is generally performed on a quarterly or half-yearly basis according to need.To report a street sweeping issue 0845 6066170 or click here

Who do I report a problem with a dog or litter bin to?

The majority of dog and litter bins across the district are supplied and maintained by the local town or parish council.  The street cleansing team empty bins mostly on a weekly basis, with bins in busy areas emptied more frequently.  Requests for new or replacement bins should be directed to the appropriate town or parish council

To report an overflowing bin 0845 6066170 or click here

Do the Councils collect dead animals?

All dead wild and domestic animals on the highway are collected by the street cleansing team. All domestic animals are scanned for identification chips and, wherever possible, owners are contacted. To report the location of a dead animal 0845 6066170 or click here. Dead animals causing an obstruction on the A14 should be reported directly to the Highways contractor Skanska on 01473 835842.

Is litter cleared from A roads?

Road side litter is one of the fastest growing litter problems. The high levels and speed of traffic on A classification roads makes clearing litter a dangerous proposition. The Environmental Protection Act 1990 makes allowance for this and as such litter clearance that might require road closure is to be performed when practicable and preferably incorporated in to other scheduled works. The litter clearance work for both Councils on the A14 & A12  is covered part of a partnership scheme with Ipswich Borough Council. To report a litter problem on an A classification road 0845 6066170 or click here.

Are the Councils responsible for emergency clearance?

Suffolk County Council is responsible for any emergency clearing of materials, including mud, that might be a hazard on the highway and storm water drains. Report a problem with flooding and drainage

Do the Councils clear leaf and blossom fall?

The council will remove leaf and blossom fall, however this is an unpredictable task and we would ask the public to report areas that are problematic.  To report a problem regarding leaves or blossom 0845 6066170 or click here.

What does the Council do about graffiti?

There is a common misconception that councils are responsible for clearing up graffiti and that this falls within the street cleansing service.  This is not the case - graffiti removal is the responsibility of whosever property it is on, it is criminal damage and should be dealt with as such.  The only graffiti that the Councils are responsible for clearing is that on its own property.Where the defacement is on private property, the council encourages property owners to remove graffiti.  However, if the defacement is offensive, the Councils are happy to work with the owners to remove the defacement as quickly as possible. To report an instance of graffiti 0845 6066170 or click here

 

Who is responsible for clearing fly-tipped waste?

The Council and the Environment Agency are responsible for clearing up/investigating fly-tipping on public land.  Clearing fly-tipping from private land is the responsibility of the landowner. To report a fly-tip incident click here.

What should I expect from the Council in respect of standards and response times?

The Council’s legal duty is set out in the Government's Code of Practice for litter, and this describes expected response times for different land use types. The full code can be viewed here

Does the Council supply dog anti-fouling signs?

Mid Suffolk District Council are not obliged to supply this type of signage.  Any requests for anti-fouling signs should be directed to town or parish councils for their consideration.
