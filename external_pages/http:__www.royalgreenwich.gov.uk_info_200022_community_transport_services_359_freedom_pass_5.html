Apply for an older person's Freedom Pass

View pages in this section

You can now apply for the older person's Freedom Pass online on the Freedom Pass website. 

More information about what documents you will need when you apply online can be found on the Freedom Pass website. 

If you are unable to apply online, you can pick up a paper application form, which you will need to complete and return in the post. Paper application forms can be collected from any library in Greenwich. 

Library staff can help you with applying online or on paper, but they will not be able to carry out the process for you. 

Documents you will need to apply 

You will need the following documents:

  • One colour passport photograph, which must comply with passport standards
  • One proof of your name and age:
    • Letter of pension entitlement
    • Birth certificate (unless your name has changed)
    • Current passport
    • Medical card
    • Driving licence (paper or photocard)
  • One proof of your address:
    • Current Council Tax bill, letter or payment book
    • Current council or housing association rent book
    • Current TV licence
    • Residential utility bill (excluding mobile phone bills) dated within the last three months
    • Bank or building society statement dated within the last three months
    • HM Revenue and Customs letter dated within the last three months
    • Council Tax bill dated within the last three months
    • Department of Work and Pensions letter dated within the last three months
    • Occupational pension letter dated within the last three months

What happens next?

The process depends whether you apply online or using a paper form:

Online applications

You will receive an email confirming your application has been received and will be provided with a reference number so that you can track your application online.

The Freedom Pass will normally be delivered to your home within 10 working days by second-class post.

Postal applications

You must complete and sign the form, enclose one colour photograph (which must be in accordance with passport standards), proof of identity and residency, and post to London Councils for processing. You will need to pay for the postage.

The Freedom Pass will normally be delivered to your home within 10 working days by second-class post.

Please DO NOT photocopy application forms because each form has a unique barcode assigned to it.

Lost, stolen, damaged or faulty pass?

Contact the Freedom Pass helpline on 0300 330 1433 (local call rate, open Monday to Sunday 8am to 8pm) or email info@freedompass.org and leave the following details after the tone:

  • Your name
  • Your date of birth
  • Your daytime telephone number
  • Whether the pass is lost, stolen or faulty

London Councils will deal with your request.

Freedom Pass holders who lose or damage their passes will be charged £10 for replacements.

The Freedom Pass is paid for by your local council and the scheme is managed by London Councils on behalf of the London boroughs.
