Do I qualify?

Do I qualify for a bus pass?

Bus passes are available for older North Lincolnshire residents or those with qualifying disabilities.

If you are unable to use public transport on your own because of a disability, you may apply for a companion to be added to your pass to help you on your journey.

Entitlement by age

Please select the date range in which your date of birth falls, to find out when you will be eligible for a bus pass.

Entitlement by disability

You are entitled to a pass if you are in one of the following groups and if your disability is either permanent or likely to last at least 12 months:

  • Blind or partially sighted
  • Deaf
  • In receipt of Disability Living Allowance (must be either low rate mobility with middle or high rate care or high rate mobility)
  • Limbless
  • Have a learning disability
  • Disability or injury preventing ability to walk (You will usually use crutches, walking frame or wheelchair)
  • Other physical disability
  • War Pensioner

Companion's Element

A companion element can be added to a disabled persons pass if they are unable to use public transport on their own at any time.

The disabled person’s pass will show a '+C' symbol in the top right hand corner. The companion may travel free on local buses within North Lincolnshire and journeys which start in North Lincolnshire and end elsewhere. The companion may also travel back free from places in North East Lincolnshire, Hull, the East Riding of Yorkshire and South Yorkshire but please note they are not able to travel back free from Lincolnshire.

Companions may buy half fare return tickets for local rail journeys or single tickets at one-third off.

Please note that we do not provide a companion’s entitlement for disabled children under 12 years of age.

How can I apply to have a companion entitlement added to my pass?

If your:

Disability Living Allowance letter shows you are eligible for High Rate Mobility and Middle or High Rate Care

or your:

Personal Independence Payment letter shows that you are eligible for enhanced mobility and enhanced daily living then you are automatically eligible for the companion element.  Take your letter into Shopmobility or to your nearest local link and it can be requested. 

If you do not automatically qualify then you can call us on  01724 297460 and ask for an application form. The form must be verified by your doctor (not the nurse or receptionist) and, once completed, should be taken to either Scunthorpe Shopmobility Office or to one of our Local Link offices.

If you have a pass already, send your completed form to Public Transport, Hewson House, Brigg, DN20 8XQ and we will send you a new pass which includes the companion symbol.

If you need any further information please contact us.
