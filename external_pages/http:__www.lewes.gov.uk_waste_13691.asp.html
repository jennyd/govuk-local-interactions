We operate a weekly "Sharps Bucket" Collection Service to safely dispose of sharp objects, like used and unused syringes.

We will collect sharps, properly contained in a yellow or Orange top sealed sharps bucket, from householders who are self-treating, (Please note we will not collect loose sharps or unsealed containers).

Where sharps waste is being generated by a health care worker, District Nurse or GP working in a private dwelling, the Primary Care Trust (PCT) must make their own arrangements for the appropriate disposal. This includes bottles and containers that contain unused, partially used or out-of-date cytotoxic products and vaccines and other equipment, for example gloves.

Sharps waste must not be placed in normal household waste containers or contaminated with household waste for normal refuse collection. They are collected separately as they are required to be sent to the appropriate licensed treatment facilities. We provide sharps buckets on request. Please ensure that you place your name and address on the label of the sharps bucket. This is a legal requirement and we will not able to collect sharps buckets without these details being completed.

Collection Day

Our weekly collection day within the district is every Tuesday.

Arranging Collections

New and Existing Customers

If you require our service please complete our online sharps collection form, or phone Customer Services.

We have a statutory duty to ensure that your waste is correctly described as a) infectious waste or b) non-infectious waste or c) sharps followed by disposal and treatment in an appropriate and legitimate manner.

When you make your first request - we will send you a letter by post (or email if desired) with a referral form which need to be filled in by your health professional e.g. health care worker, district nurse or GP. They are required to confirm the type of waste in question, sign and return the referral form to us within 30 calendar days from the date shown on the letter. On receipt of the referral form we will contact you to advise you of the collection arrangements.

If the referral form is not returned to us within 30 calendar days from the date shown on the letter we will assume that you no longer require our service.

If you require a collection/replacement please complete our online sharps collection form, or contact Customer Services.

Replenishments

If you require more sharps boxes please contact Customer Services and we will supply them on the day of collection.

Disposal Advice for Discarded Sharps

If you discover discarded sharps please take the following steps.

Do not touch it or allow anyone else to.  Do not kick it in the gutter or down a drain, throw it away amongst household refuse or flush it down the toilet. Please discard it safely by placing it in a metal can or strong plastic bottle.  Remember to wear thick gloves and use tongs or tweezers to pick it up.  Place the needle end of the syringe into the container first.

Make sure you wash your hands afterwards and store the container in a safe place. Report this to Customer Services to arrange collection.
