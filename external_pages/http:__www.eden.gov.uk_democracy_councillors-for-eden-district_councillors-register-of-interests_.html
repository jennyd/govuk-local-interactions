Councillors' Register of Interests

Which Councillors need to register their interests?

All Members and Co-opted Members of District and Parish Councils are required by law (Local Government Act 2000) to complete a register of interests form to register their financial and other interests. They have to complete their register of interests within 28 days of being elected.

What information is included in the register of interests?

  • Details of any employment or business carried on by them;
  • The name of their employer;
  • Details of people who have paid towards election expenses;
  • Contracts between themselves/their firm and the Council;
  • Interests in any land in the area; details of any directorships

View Councillors' register of interests

  • Select a District Councillor from Councillor's list to view their registered interests

  • Select parish from Parish Council list to see the interests for each of their Councillors

See the full register of District and Parish Councillors' interests

The full register of District and Parish Councillors' interests is maintained by the Monitoring Officer. The register is available for public inspection, by appointment, between the hours of 9am and 4.30pm, Monday to Friday at the Town Hall, Penrith.

To make an appointment to see the full register District and Parish Councillors:

  • Write to: Democratic Services, Town Hall, Penrith, Cumbria CA11 7QF
  • Email: cttee.admin@eden.gov.uk
  • Telephone 01768 212207 or 212142
