Find or renew a library item

 

You can find the library catalogue for Somerset on the LibrariesWest website. This lists all the items held by Somerset Libraries and the libraries within Bath and North East Somerset, Bristol City Libraries, North Somerset and South Gloucestershire. 

From the catalogue you can search for books, CDs, DVDs or any other item in stock in any of the libraries within the LibrariesWest Consortium. You can search by author, title or keyword. You can request items from the catalogue.

You can also borrow eBooks from our LibariesWest website.

You can choose to search the catalogue by:

  • title

  • author/composer/artist

  • ISBN / ISSN

  • keyword, if you are not sure of the title.

Renewing an item

You can renew your library books online on the LibrariesWest website by using your membership login.

Reserving an item
You can request or reserve any loan item such as books, spoken word, videos, DVDs, CDs or computer games.

You can make a request for an item by using a People's Network computer in a library, by going to the LibrariesWest website, or you can ask a member of staff to place the request for you.

There is a fee for each request, except for children's books and large print books. The charge for a request placed by you is less than if placed by a member of staff.

You will be notified by post or email as soon as the item is available for collection. If you would like to receive notifications by email (and this will usually be quicker) please tell staff your email address.

Children may request up to 6 books at any time free of charge.

If the item is held by one of our LibrariesWest partners you can request it via the LibrariesWest website. We aim to supply books that are on the shelves of another library in Somerset within 6 working days. 

Our web catalogue includes items that are in stock within the LibrariesWest Consortium. If the item is not in stock in Somerset or within the LibrariesWest Consortium, you can still request it. We will consider purchasing a copy if appropriate, or if a book title we will try to obtain a copy of it from another library within the UK through the Inter-library Loan system.

There is a small, non-refundable charge for requests. Nearly 90% of all book requests are met within 15 days.

To request multiple copies of performance material or play scripts you need to submit a request voucher. This can be purchased in advance from the Performing Arts Library. Requests made by telephone or by letter are only acceptable if the appropriate payment is then sent or enclosed.

Music sets can be borrowed on extended loan for rehearsal and performance. Drama sets can be borrowed for readings only. A charge is made for each request, except for large print books and children's books.

How do I use it?
Complete and hand in a request form at your local library or ask a member of staff to place the request for you. Requests can also be made by using a computer terminal in the library or through the LibrariesWest website. You will need your library card and PIN number. The charge for this is less than if you ask a member of staff to place the request.

There is a non-refundable fee for each request except for children's books and large print books. You will be notified by post or email as soon as the item is ready for collection. Children may request up to six books at any one time free of charge. If the title is not in stock we will consider purchasing a copy or borrowing it from another library authority.

What happens next?
You will be notified by post or by email when you item arrives.

When you visit the library to collect the reserved item, please bring your membership card and the notification slip. If you have asked someone else to collect a reserved item on your behalf, please ask them to take your membership card and the notification slip. Staff will ask that person for evidence of their identity and address, such as their library membership card or driving licence.

The notification slip asks you to collect the item within 14 days of the date the item becoming available for you. If you receive notifications by post, please note that you will receive this a few days after it became available. If you have not collected the item by the date shown, it will be made available for another borrower, although in the case of material borrowed from another library authority, staff will attempt first to contact you to check if it is still required You will be notified by post or email as soon as your requested item is ready for you to collect. The item will be kept for you at the library you nominate for 14 days.

To receive notifications by email, please give details of your email address to your local library, and ask them to set up email notification. Or, you can use the facility on the LibrariesWest website. Click on Personal Details. Here you will be asked to enter your membership number and PIN. Then choose to be notified by email, provided you have previously given your email address to library staff. 

How much does it cost?
There is a reduced rate for requests placed by you on a public access computer terminal or by using the LibrariesWest website.

Renewing items
You can renew library items online on the LibrariesWest website. You can renew library items up to twenty times without bringing them to the library provided no one else has requested them.

If the item you wish to renew is already due for return but has been requested by another borrower, you may be able to renew it for just two or three days to give you time to return it - please contact Libraries Direct on 0300 123 2224.

Renewals can be made in the following ways:

  • the internet, on the LibrariesWest website (card and PIN number needed)

  • returning them to the library for reissue/renewal

  • informing the library by post or fax

  • phoning Libraries Direct on 0300 123 2224 using a People's Network Terminal in the library (card and PIN number needed)

Renewing Performing Arts Library loans - requests may be made in person or by mail, phone, fax and email direct to the Performing Arts Library. The renewal of performance music sets will be permitted subject to advance requests placed by other users. 

To renew items when the library is closed you will need your library membership number and your PIN. You can renew items online on the LibrariesWest website.

To renew online:

  • go to the LibrariesWest website

  • click on Your Renewal

  • enter your membership number

  • enter PIN.


This gives you access to what you have out on loan and the option to renew.
