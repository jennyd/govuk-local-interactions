Join the library

How to join the library

You can join at any one of our libraries you will need address details. If you're under 16 you'll need a parent or guardian's consent and address details.  

Join online

If you join online you'll receive a temporary membership number and PIN which will allow you to reserve books.

To collect your reservations and borrow items you'll need to come into a library with proof of your address and your temporary membership number.

Joining Libraries Extra

Libraries Extra is now available at Portslade and Woodingdean Libraries. You need to join in the library and provide photo ID and proof of address

About library membership

Borrowing books is free!

Choose from adult and children’s fiction and non-fiction books and eBooks. You can also find LGBT (lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender) collections, rare books and special collections, music scores, local history books, essential skills collections, careers and job seekers reference resources. DVDs, CDs, audio books, language courses are available to hire for a small charge.

With a full adult membership you can borrow up to a maximum of 40 books plus eight audio-visual items (subject to age classification for young people). With a child membership, you can also borrow two toys from Whitehawk Library.

You'll have access to all our free online resources and access to public computers.

You can borrow the items for

Books and audio books 3 weeks
Language courses 3 weeks 
DVD  1 week
CD 1 week

Renewing items

Items can be renewed up to four times, all items can be renewed providing nobody else has reserved them. If you renew chargeable items you will pay a further hire charge each time you renew.

You can renew in any of these ways

  • using My Library online
  • in person at any library, membership card number required
  • phone the library, membership card number required
  • automated 24 hour renewals on 0303 123 0035, membership card number and PIN required

If you experience any difficulty renewing items online please contact us

Reserving items

You can reserve any item in person at a library or online. There is a small charge of 90p per item for making reservations (free for children and Reading Well Books on Prescription). Your charge is payable when you collect your item. We'll notify you when the item is in stock by email or post. To collect your reserved item, simply bring your library card and the reservation notification with you to the library.

Overdue items

Overdue items are subject to charges
