Conservation and Heritage

Conservation areas were first established by the Civic Amenities Act 1967. The statutory definition of a conservation area is one of "special architectural or historic interest, the character or appearance of which it is desirable to preserve or enhance."

They are designated by the council and relate to the quality and interest of an area, rather than just individual buildings.  

Where are the Conservation Areas?

You can find out details of the conservation areas in the district on the council GIS map interface: East Herts Online Maps

This system is in development, so we really appreciate comments and feedback from users.

You can select what type of information you want to see from the left hand menu (Conservation Areas should already be ticked). If you want to turn information off or on, just tick or untick the relevant box(es) and hit the "Apply" button at the bottom of the menu.

You can either zoom out of the map to see the boundaries of the conservation area, or simply hit the "Query Map" tool on the left hand toolbar of the map and then click on the property or location on the map that you're interested in.  If the location is inside a conservation area, the pop up results will tell you what conservation area and the date it was designated.  If the location is not in a conservation area, it will not show any conservation area information at all. 

You can also view the boundaries of the conservation areas in the district on the Interactive Local Plan (conservation areas are marked with a dark orange boundary)

IMORTANT. A number of revisions have been made to some of the Conservation Area boundaries and the process is ongoing. View these details under Conservation Area Appraisals.  Revised boundaries are shown on respective Plans  2.

What is a conservation area?

It is common for areas of historic towns and villages to be designated as conservation areas.

These areas can vary greatly in size and character, but generally it is the appearance of the area, rather than individual buildings, that justifies the designation.

Planning legislation makes it clear that local authorities should give "special attention" to those areas and their enhancement. One of the main purposes of designating a conservation area is to ensure that development proposals preserve and enhance the character of the area.

Conservation areas are a very important aspect of conserving the built environment.

Conservation Area Appraisals

East Herts Council  has commenced a programme of Conservation Area appraisal work. The purpose of this is to identify and document what factors are considered to make up the special character of these areas. Once initial appraisal work has been undertaken a draft Conservation Area Appraisal will be produced which will be subject to a period of consultation.

Listed Buildings

A "Listed Building" is one which is formally declared to be of special architectural or historic interest.

Under the Planning (Listed Buildings and Conservation Areas) Act 1990 there is a statutory duty to compile Lists of Buildings of Special Architectural or Historic Interest for the whole country. The List is a register. It identifies those buildings which are of special interest and gives the Council extra powers to protect them, as well as imposing extra responsibilities on their owners.

Please view Listed Buildings  for further information.
