Homelessness

If you are homeless or think you are at risk of being made homeless, contact us to see what we can do to help. Our housing options advisers at Greyfriars House will be able to give you advice and information on your housing possibilities.

Collapse allReveal all

What should I do if I think I may be made homeless?

You should first check whether you are being evicted legally. Our housing options team may be able to give you information that stops you becoming homeless or give you advice on finding alternative accommodation.

Read our I need somewhere to live pages for more information on housing options.

How can you help me if I am soon to be homeless?

If you are at risk of homelessness, you should come to Greyfriars House immediately. We will assess whether you:

  • Are homeless or threatened with homelessness
  • Are in priority need - if you have children, are pregnant or are vulnerable
  • Are eligible for help based on your immigration status

If you fit these criteria we will provide emergency accommodation while we investigate your application. We do not have to offer accommodation to everyone and we will give all decisions in writing.

Emergency accommodation may be bed and breakfast-type accommodation.
