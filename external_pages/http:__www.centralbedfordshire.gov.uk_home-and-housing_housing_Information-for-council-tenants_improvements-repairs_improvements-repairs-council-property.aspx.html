Repair reporting

Reporting of repairs by phone

Ring the free phone number 0800 074 0263 to report your repairs.

All repairs on our tenanted homes are reported direct to the council or the relevant contractor. A recorded voice will guide the caller on how they can report their repairs.

Option 1 – gas repairs,  including central heating and gas fires. For more information on our gas contractor Sun Realm

Option 2 – electrical repairs. Before reporting any electrical faults, the caller needs to make sure that the fault doesn’t relate directly to a faulty appliance (iron, fridge, light fitting etc.). For more information on our electrical contractor Purdy

Option 3 – general repairs

Please note, certain repairs are the responsibility of the tenant and a full list of these repairs is shown below under “Tenant responsibility repairs”. When you phone to report a repair, we will advise you if the repair is the responsibility of the landlord or the tenant.

Emergency out of hours repairs

All tenants are able to report repairs directly to our contractors between the hours of 8:45am and 5:15pm Monday to Thursday, and 8:45am and 4.45pm on a Friday, on 0800 074 0263, options 1, 2 and 3.

Outside of these hours, at weekends and during national holidays, your call will be diverted to our Out of Hours service. This service is for emergency or Health & Safety repairs only. All other repairs will need to be reported on the next working day.

Repair priorities and expected repair timescales

When you report a repair, it will be allocated a priority category depending on the type of repair.  Response times and repair timescales for priority categories are as follows:

Gas Repairs

Priority 0: Callout – out of hours
Repair to be started within 2 hours and completed or made safe within 24 hours

Priority 1:   Gas escapes and unsafe gas appliances
Attend unsafe installation immediately.  Follow-up call to be made within 4 hours to effect repair.

Priority 2:   No heating/hot water report
If reported before midday (Monday to Saturday) attend same day.  If reported after midday, call same day or next day before midday (Monday to Friday).

Priority 3: User defined
To be started and completed within specified period.

Electrical Repairs

Priority 0: Emergency callout – out of hours
Repair to be started within 1 hour and completed or made safe within 24 hours

Priority 1: Emergency callout – normal working hours
Repair to be started within 1 hour and completed or made safe within 4 hours

Priority 2:  Routine
Repair to be started and completed within 5 working days/7 calendar days

Priority 3:  Routine
Repair to be started and completed within 15 working days/21 calendar days

Priority 4: User defined
To be started and completed within specified period

General Repairs

Priority 0: Emergency callout – out of hours
Repair to be started within 1 hour and completed or made safe within 24 hours

Priority 1: Emergency callout – normal working hours
Repair to be started within 2 hours and completed or made safe within 24 hours

Priority 2: Urgent
Repair to be started and completed within 5 working days/7 calendar days

Priority 3: Routine
Repair to be started and completed within 20 working days/28 calendar days

Priority 4: Client defined
To be started and completed within specified period.

If you have any disability that you think the contractor needs to take into consideration, please let them know when reporting the repair.

Overdue repairs

A guideline on priorities for certain repairs (known as qualifying repairs) has been set by Central Government under the Right to Repair scheme. Qualifying repairs such as insecure windows and doors or unsafe power sockets and electrical fittings must be carried out by Central Bedfordshire Council contractors within the correct guidelines. Failure to do so can result in financial compensation. The repair timescales published by the council take account of these guidelines.


Tenant responsibility repairs

Central Bedfordshire Council is not responsible for the repair and maintenance of the following items:

  • broken windows
  • lock changes
  • doorbells
  • curtain rails
  • sink/bath plugs and chains
  • WC seat and covers
  • internal cupboard door catches and handles
  • electrical plug fuses from tenant’s own appliances, and light bulbs
  • smoke detectors (even if originally supplied by the council) and faulty worn out batteries
  • additional, lost, or broken keys
  • internal decorations
  • lockouts
  • blockages in the sink/wash-hand basin or wc (if caused by the tenant)
  • garden gates
  • replacing, cleaning showerheads
  • curtain battening
  • wooden sheds
  • greenhouses
  • repair to letter boxes or plates
     
Rechargeable repairs

A 'rechargeable repair' is any repair that is caused by misuse, abuse or negligence by the tenant, the tenant’s family, or visitors to the tenant’s property.  Rechargeable repairs will not be carried out by Central Bedfordshire Council’s contractors, unless there is a risk to Health & Safety.

Home improvements

As a council tenant, before you start any alterations to your home such as a new kitchen, bathroom, extension etc, you must get written permission from Central Bedfordshire Council.

If you make any alterations to your home, you may be entitled to compensation under a government initiative called the Right to Compensation, should you decide to move out. Further details, including qualifying improvements, can be found in: a better deal for tenants - your right to compensation for improvements, or by calling 0300 300 8302


Care and repair scheme

The care and repair scheme is designed to assist the elderly and vulnerable with enhancing their homes. The website is like a ‘one-stop-shop’ offering contacts and alternative websites for advice.

The details of the initiative can be viewed on the website Care and Repair England

Services and communal repairs

Estate Management Officers make regular visits to ensure that communal areas are clean and tidy.  They report any repairs they become aware of.

If you want to report an area that needs attention or repair, please call the repairs free phone on 0800 074 0263

Decoration and disturbance allowances

Vouchers towards decorating materials are available to Central Bedfordshire Council tenants in certain circumstances, such as damage as a result of a repair being required.  We will let you know if you qualify for decorating vouchers and how you can obtain them.

Empty council houses (Voids)

When a property is empty, even for a short time, it is at risk from damage, vandalism, theft and squatters.  Additionally, there is the constant threat of litigation under Health and Safety legislation.

The most effective method to manage these risks is to fit security doors and screens to cover the windows and entrance doors, until the repairs are complete and the property is allocated and re-occupied.

If you have concerns in respect of these risk avoidance measures, please call 0300 300 8302 and ask to speak to the Housing Voids Team.

Where possible, we will endeavour to provide you with a likely timescale that the security screen will be in position.

Surveying - major repairs

It may be necessary from time to time for the council to gain access to your home in order to undertake surveys.  Where possible, the council will telephone you first to arrange a convenient time to call.

Please note that all our council representatives carry identification cards.  You should not allow any person into your home unless they carry the relevant identification.

If in doubt, please contact us on 0800 074 0263 Option 3 for further clarification.

Council housing – decoration

We will decorate the outside of your home, shared landings and staircases in the communal areas on a 7 year programme.  We are not responsible for decorating the inside of your home, as this is one of your responsibilities to maintain.  We expect it to meet a reasonable standard, and especially if the property is about to be given up.

Housing Maintenance Plan

The Housing Major Works Annual Delivery Plan brings together all the various activities that we intend to carry out to maintain and improve our housing stock.

This plan provides a single point of reference for the planned programme of major works for the next year, as well as providing information on the rolling 5 year plan.

You can view the document here:  Housing Major Works Annual Delivery Plan (PDF 502KB)

You can also find out when planned maintenance is being carried out on your council home by calling us on 0800 074 0263 Option 3
