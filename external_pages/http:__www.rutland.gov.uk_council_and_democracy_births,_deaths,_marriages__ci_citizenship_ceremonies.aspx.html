Citizenship Ceremonies

Applicants for British Nationality under the Nationality, Immigration and Asylum Act 2002 can make a citizenship oath and pledge at a ceremony conducted by a Superintendent Registrar or their Deputy.

All applicants for British Nationality after 1 January 2004 are required to take part in the ceremony which is conducted by the local authority on behalf of the Home Office.

The ceremony enhances the process of becoming a British Citizen and provides a fitting and meaningful celebration of this significant life event. At the heart of the ceremony will be the traditional Oath of Allegiance, which has been coupled with a new and modern citizenship pledge.

The taking of the pledge will be the key moment at which the citizenship is conferred, and at which the new citizen pledges their commitment and loyalty to the Sovereign and the United Kingdom.

In Rutland we try to involve the Lord Lieutenant or the Chairman of the Council in the ceremony wherever possible.

For further details contact Superintendent Registrar on 01572 722577.
