Free early learning for two-year-olds

Page Content

Some two year olds are entitled to a free part-time early education place. Find out if you are eligible for a free place and how you can apply.

These places can be taken up at a nursery, children's centre, childminder or a selected number of Islington Primary Schools - details of participating providers are available at the bottom of the page (please note that settings have a limited number of places, so you are not guaranteed a place at your first choice).

If your child is eligible, you can start claiming free early education from the beginning of the term after your child turns two.

Who is eligible?

Families must be resident in Islington.

To be eligible for a place, parents/carers must be getting one of the following: 

  • Income support
  • income-based Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)
  • income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • support through Part 6 of the Immigration and Asylum Act
  • the guaranteed element of State Pension Credit
  • Child Tax Credit (but not Working Tax Credit) and have an annual income not over £16,190
  • Working Tax Credit and earn no more than £16,190 a year

  • The Working Tax Credit 4-week run on (the payment you get when you stop qualifying for Working Tax Credit).

    • Families will also be eligible if:
  • A child receives Disability Living Allowance
  • A child is looked after by the local authority
  • A child has left care under a special guardianship order, child arrangements order or adoption order.

    • Families may be eligible if a child has special educational needs and/or a disability. 

    When can I start receiving the free hours for my child?

              Child's birthday                     When you can start receiving free hours

              1 January to 31 March              the beginning of term on or after 1 April
              1 April to 31 August                  the beginning of term on or after 1 September
              1 September to 31 December    the beginning of term on or after 1 January

              When can I apply?

                    You can apply for an eligibility check one term before you would be able to start receiving free hours for your child. You can apply now for an eligibility check if your child was born between 1 January 2013 to 31 March 2014. You are able to apply for an eligibility check at the start of the term during which your child turns two. 

              How can I apply?

              You need to complete a form to find out if your child is eligible.

              If you are applying for a child who is looked after by the local authority, please contact your supervising social worker to discuss what is most appropriate for the child.

              Check my eligibility 

              Parents who do not have access to the internet can complete the paper form; please print up the Eligibility Check Form using the link in Related Documents at the end of this page. You can also ask for a copy of the paper form at your local children's centre.

              Once you have submitted your eligibility check, the Council will use the information you have provided to check if your child is eligible for a free part-time place and will notify you by post or email (depending on what you specify on the form). If you are eligible, you will then be able to choose which nurseries/childminders you would like to apply to. You can find a list of providers that are taking part in the scheme using the link in Related Documents at the end of this page.

              If you are not sure when or how you can apply, contact the Family Information Service on 020 7527 5959; fis@islington.gov.uk.

              What could I do while my two year old is learning?

              If you are eligible for free early learning for your 2 year old, you are also eligible for free support and free training courses to help you learn new skills and get ready to work.
              For more information on employment support see the Help for Parents pages. For information on free training courses see the Adult and Community Learning pages.
