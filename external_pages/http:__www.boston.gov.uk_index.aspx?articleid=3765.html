Waste and recycling information

We provide an alternate weekly collection of refuse and recycling to all properties in the borough, one week your blue bin will be emptied the next your green refuse bin will be emptied.

Items that go into your blue bin

Paper - newspapers, magazines, writing paper, junk mail, envelopes, directories (inc yellow pages) catalogues

Cardboard - cardboard boxes, corrugated cardboard, cereal packets, cardboard egg boxes (large pieces of cardboard should be flat packed and placed at the side of the blue bin)

Plastic bottles only (please remove all bottle tops and place them in the blue bin) - drinks bottles, shampoo bottles, washing up ;liquid bottles, plastic milk containers, detergent bottles, fabric softener bottles

Glass bottles and jars only (please remove all bottle tops and place them in the blue bin) - wine bottles, beer bottles, food jars, sauce jars

We cannot accept Pyrex, ceramic or window glass

Cans (steel or aluminium) - food tins, drinks cans, pet food tins, empty aerosol cans, foil, foil trays

Blue recycling bins may be rejected for collection if:

  • You have contaminated your bin with the incorrect materials
  • You have placed black bags in with your recyclables

If this happens, the refuse collectors will leave blue recycling bags for you to sort your recycling into and you should remove any non recyclable waste which should be put into the green bin. Once you have done this you can ring us to request a re-collection or extra bags to keep you going until your next blue collection day.

All other items must be put into your green Energy from Waste bin such as the following:

  • Food waste/animal faeces(double bag)
  • Polystyrene packaging
  • Waxed milk and juice cartons
  • Nappies
  • Plastic carrier/black plastic bin bags
  • Plastic film
  • Bubble wrap
  • Yoghurt pots
  • Ice cream containers
  • Crisp packets
  • Margarine tubs
  • Crockery/utensils

Green refuse bins may be rejected for collection if:

  • Your bin is too heavy

If this happens, the refuse collectors will leave your bin and a letter advising you to empty some of the contents out of your bin; once you have done this you can ring us to request a re-collection.

Building materials, that is to say tiles, cement, brick rubble, plasterboard, etc from your home is NOT household materials and must NOT be put into the green bin. Your bin will not be emptied if these items are in there.

In conjunction with the above, other items that should not be put into the green bin are:

  • Batteries
  • Oils
  • Paints
  • Electrical items (WEEE)
  • Gas cylinders
  • Tyres
  • Fluorescent tubes
  • Hypodermic needles
  • Soil / peat
  • Garden waste

DID YOU KNOW FACTS..........

  • Your bin must be left out by 5.30am on the day of your collection or the night before to guarantee collection
  • All changes to refuse and recycling collections due to bank holidays are publicised well in advance via calendar drops, stickers placed on bins, local paper and on the website;
  • Your bin must be placed at the edge of your property with the handles facing outwards and it would really help the bin men if you could stand your bin next to your neighbour's for collection
  • It is your responsibility to return your bin to your storage area as soon as possible on the same day as your collection
  • We can provide refuse and recycling leaflets in Portuguese, Polish, Lithuanian and Latvian
  • Dog poop scoop bags are available FREE of charge from calling in at our West Street offices.

If your bin has not been emptied

If your bin has not been emptied please get in contact with us so we can arrange a re-collection as soon as it is operationally possible.

Lost or stolen bin

If your bin goes missing or is damaged please call us on 01205 311112  where you will be added to our delivery list.

For information on our waste collection service standards please see the link opposite.

To find out bin collection dates and changes to collections due to bank holidays please see the calendar below.

Recycling bags

In addition to the twin bin system, we provide blue bags for recyclable materials. These are for residents who do not have enough storage space for a blue bin or who may need additional bags to put extra recycling out along side the recycling bin (these bags should not be used as bin liners or to bag the recycling up to put in the bin) A supply of these recycling bags is available by either leaving a note on your bin on day of your collection asking the crew to leave a roll, at the council offices or by calling us.

When putting confidential paper into the blue bags (or blue bin) we advise that personal details, bank details etc should always be shredded to minimise the risk of identity theft.

It is very important for members of the public to use these recycling bags rather than black sacks for recyclables, this is so the refuse collectors can identify what is in the bags. If black bags are put out for collection they will not be taken

If you have no space at all for bins, we will issue a supply a roll of bags for refuse and recycling, quarterly free of charge.

Assisted collections

We provide an assisted collection to residents who are disabled or infirm in some way, which would make it difficult for the occupier to pull their bin in and out for collection. If you require this service please call us on 01205 311112 where we can fill an application form out or arrange a visit.

Extra bin collections

We can provide an extra small bin to properties where there are six or more occupants living, an application form must be submitted. If a property has less than 6 occupants and are still struggling to manage their waste with 1 green bin then you can call us up to arrange a visit from an officer who will carry out a waste audit. If after the waste audit it shows that materials are being put into the correct container and being recycled, the officer will put your application through for a second bin to be delivered. If you feel you qualify for this service please call us, or see form below.
