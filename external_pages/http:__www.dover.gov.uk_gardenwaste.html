Garden Waste Collections

2015/16 subscriptions are now closed.

The new service will reopen for renewals or new subscribers early 2016

A garden waste licence costs £40 and is valid April-March

  • We will collect your garden waste once a fortnight and will empty up to six bags per collection.
  • You will receive up to 25 collections (one break over the Christmas period) a year for a cost of £1.60 per collection. 
  • New subscribers will be sent a letter containing information, your start date and a tag to secure to one of your bags.  The tag must be visible to the collecting crew.  Existing subscribers will continue to use their existing tag. If no tag is present the collection will not be made.  
  • A sack is not provided with the licence.

  • Once your payment has been received your collection service will automatically be renewed and no further confirmation letter will be issued. Receipts are available if requested.

We will collect

  • Grass cuttings
  • Twigs and small branches
  • Prunings and hedge clippings
  • Flowers and plants
  • Weeds (except Japanese Knotweed and ragwort)

We cannot take

  • Soil and turf
  • Rubble and stones
  • Cooked foods and kitchen waste
  • Wood
  • All non organic waste
  • Garden chemicals
  • Pet litter

 

Frequently Asked Questions

I receive an assisted collection for my recycling/refuse, will I get an assisted collection for my garden waste?

Yes you will receive an assisted collection. Please can you let us know if you currently receive an assisted collection when you subscribe so we can ensure it is carried out. How much should I put in my sack(s)? If you overload your sack it may not be collected. If you can’t lift the sack, we can’t lift it. Look after both your safety and our collection crew’s safety.

Where can I get additional sacks?

Additional sacks can be purchased from the main DDC Council office at Whitfield or you can use your own bags.

Do I have to use the sack supplied by Dover District Council?

No you may use your own sacks but you must ensure that the licence tag issued to you is visible when they are put out for collection. Please note that containers like builders sacks are too large and will not be collected.

Is there a limit to how much garden waste I can put in each sack?

For your safety and the safety of the crews please ensure you do not overload each sack. Any sack weighing over 20kg is deemed too heavy and the collection will not be made. If this happens you must remove the excess weight and present the sack/s on the next scheduled collection.

What if I wish to put out more than six bags per collection?

Additional licences can be purchased should you wish to have more than the allocated six bag collection. There is no limit to the number of licences a property may purchase. All licence tags issued to a property must be displayed on bag/s when placed out for collection.

What happens to the garden waste after it is collected?

Green Garden Waste is composted and produces a high quality soil improver that is taken to a local facility where it is composted and then used on the farmers land. Full terms and conditions for the garden waste service are available on request. Find out what happens next.

Alternative methods available for you to dispose of your garden waste

  • Home Composting which is better for the environment, saves you time and money and gives you a soil improver which you can use in your garden. Dover District Council is part of a scheme which provides reduced priced compost bins, water butts and accessories. To place an order please telephone 0844 571 4444 or visit www.dover.getcomposting.com
  • Take your garden waste to one of the three Household Waste Recycling Centres within the District operated by Kent County Council. These sites can be found in Honeywood Road (Dover), Southwall Road (Deal) and Ramsgate Road (Sandwich).
