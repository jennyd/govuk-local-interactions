Electoral Registration

Registering to Vote

Electoral Registration

The way we all register to vote has now changed. You can register to vote online at www.gov.uk/register-to-vote

The new registration system is called Individual Electoral Registration.

How is the system different?

You can now register to vote online at www.gov.uk/register-to-vote

Everyone is responsible for registering themselves. Under the old system the head of every household could register everyone who lived at their address.

You will need to provide a few more details to register - including your National Insurance number and date of birth. This is to help make the electoral register more secure.

How do I register under the new system?

Go to www.gov.uk/register-to-vote  fill in your name, address, date of birth and a few other details - you will need to have your National Insurance number which can be found on your national insurance card, or in official paperwork such as payslips, or letters about benefits or tax credits.

If you have any problems, questions or queries please call the Electoral Registration Team on 01782 742229 or 742249.

The Electoral Register is published on a monthly basis. We add the names of new applicants, remove the names of those who are no longer resident in the Borough and alter names, for example where electors have got married. If you do not appear on the register or believe your details are incorrect please contact us.

If you move house we need to know your new details. Please register online at www.gov.uk/register-to-vote or contact the Electoral Services Team.

Who Can Register to Vote?

You can register to vote if you are:

  • 16 years old or over and a British citizen or an Irish, qualifying Commonwealth or European Union citizen who is resident in the UK (except for service voters or overseas voters).
  • 17 year olds and some 16 year olds are entitled to be included on the register as ‘attainers’. They can vote once they are 18.

Further information can be found at www.aboutmyvote.co.uk

Only people who are on the Register are allowed to vote in elections. There are two versions of the Register:

  • The FULL REGISTER of electors lists everyone who is entitled to vote. It is available to check under supervision at the Guildhall in the centre of Newcastle-under-Lyme. It is also used by other organisations for the prevention and detection of crime and checking your identity when you apply for credit. The law states who can have a copy of the full register and what it can be used for.
  • The OPEN REGISTER is available for sale to anyone that requests a copy and can be used for any purpose. If you do not want your details to be sold on please contact the Electoral Registration Team or complete this form and email it to electreg@newcastle-staffs.gov.uk. All occupiers must make an individual request if they want to opt out of the open register.
