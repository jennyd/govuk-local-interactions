Housing advice

Below you will find advice on all housing issues, including how to prevent homelessness and what to do if you have been made homeless.

  • I need somewhere to live

    Your rights and the help available if you are homeless or if someone is trying to make you homeless.

  • I am having a problem with my home

    Your rights and the help available if you have somewhere to live but are having problems with your tenancy or mortgage, or are facing homelessness.

  • Relationship breakdown

    Your right to your home will depend on whether you are an owner or a tenant, whether you are married or cohabiting and whether you have dependant children.

  • I have been served notice

    Your rights and the help available if you have been served notice.

  • Where can I apply for housing in Sunderland?

    Here you can find information about how to apply to Sunderland City Council's housing register and what to do if you're considering renting accommodation.

  • No Second Night Out

    North East councils are working together on a 'No Second Night Out' approach for rough sleepers who are new to the streets or unknown to councils.

  • Housing advice - older people

    Sources of information and housing advice for older people, including Extra Care and care homes.

  • Housing advice - young people

    Leaving home is something that most people think about at some stage in their lives. It's the time when you leave your family and strike out on your own and gain some independence.

  • Housing benefit

    Housing benefit is a means-tested benefit (in other words it is determined after taking into account income available to you). It is designed to help people on low income pay their rent. You do not have to be entitled to other means-tested or social security benefits to qualify.

  • Housing - public health advice

    The Housing Health and Safety Rating System (HHSRS) is the set method of assessing housing standards.

  • Private property compulsory acquisition

    We will attempt to acquire private property following the declaration of a clearance area or when Compulsory Purchase Orders are raised.

  • Housing - improvement and repairs loans

    Sunderland's Home Improvement Agency provides a service to older people, people of all ages with disabilities and those who are otherwise vulnerable and need help repairing, maintaining or adapting their home.

  • Housing - mediation

    One of the things mediators help with is getting people to communicate better.

  • Affordable housing

    If you live in England and can't afford to buy a home, you may be able to get some financial help through a home ownership scheme. There are 3 types of scheme: First Buy equity loans; shared ownership; or NewBuy.

  • Local housing allowance (LHA)

    Local housing allowance (LHA) is used to calculate entitlement to Housing Benefit if you live in privately-rented accommodation.

  • Backdating housing benefit

    The rules for backdating Housing Benefit and Council Tax Benefit changed from 6 October 2008.

  • Housing benefit

    If you are on a low income you may receive support to help you pay your rent.

  • Changes which affect housing benefit

    The amount of benefit you have been awarded depends on your circumstances staying the same. In other words, how they are set out in your award letter.

  • Housing benefit overpayments

    An overpayment is when we have paid you too much benefit. This normally happens if you do not tell us about a change in time.

  • Housing benefit appeals

    What to do if you disagree with our decision on your benefit.

  • Commercial property

    Sunderland City Council publishes property information for council assets.

  • Credit unions

    Credit unions are independently-run local co-operative organisations that offer loans and saving accounts to people living or working their area. The money that is collected in through savings is used to lend out in the form of loans. Credit unions offer loans, savings and other financial products.

  • Crime - support and assistance

    We can offer you support and advice if you are a victim of crime.

  • Anti social behaviour and hate crimes

  • Housing - community safety

    For further information about community safety please use the links on this page.

  • Community safety

    The City Council co-ordinates the city's partnership activity aimed at reducing crime, fear of crime, anti-social behaviour and substance misuse in the city.

  • Home safety advice

    For more information about home safety advice please use the links shown on the right of this page.

  • Youth justice - action plan orders

    The action plan order is made by the court for young people aged between ten and 17 years, convicted of a relatively serious offence.

  • Housing - Care and Repair scheme

  • Statutory inspections and re-inspections

    We carry out routine safety inspections on houses in multiple occupation to ensure that they are free of threats to health and safety, and meet the above standards.

  • Licences for houses in multiple occupation

    Landlords or managers of certain houses in multiple occupation (HMO) are required to obtain a licence in order to operate.

  • Private rented accommodation

    What to do if you're considering renting accommodation in the private rented sector (including Accredited Landlords).

  • Empty residential properties

    We aim to bring empty properties back into use to help improve the quality of life in our city.

  • Homeowners

    If you are a homeowner or are thinking about buying a home, this page offers sources of information and advice to help you.

  • Private landlords

    Private landlords are individuals or companies who own property that is entirely independent of the local authority and the registered social landlord sector.

  • Low cost home ownership

    If you can't afford to buy a home, you may be able to get some financial help through a home ownership scheme.

  • Care homes

    If you can no longer live independently, there are a range of residential homes to choose from.

  • Palliative care

    People can help at all stages of the illness, from diagnosis, while treatment is in progress and at the end of life.

  • Home and money

    Here to help with enquiries such as council tax, housing and housing advice, planning and building control, and welfare rights advice.
