How do you determine the seriousness of a defect?

All defects, whether recorded in a safety inspection or those that may be reported to us are assessed on a risk basis.

This takes into account the extent of damage or injury likely to be caused by the defect, while also assessing the likelihood of road or footway users passing by or over the defect. The amount of traffic (vehicular or pedestrian), vehicular speed, and type of road or footway are important considerations in this assessment, along with the vulnerability of particular road users, e.g. cyclists.

For example if a defect of the same size were to occur in both a shopping area and a moderately trafficked footway, then the shopping area will be given the highest level of priority due to the increased likelihood of users passing by or over the defect.

What does the Borough do to repair defects?

Response times for repairing or making safe defects will vary depending on the risk to the public.

  • Where a defect is severe enough to present a serious safety issue it will be repaired or made safe within two hours with those being a high risk in twenty four hours
  • Defects considered to be a medium risk to safety will be repaired within seven calendar days
  • Defects considered to be a moderate risk will be repaired within 28 days
  • Defects that are considered a low risk will not be repaired. However they will be reviewed when the next inspection is undertaken

Which roads are maintained by the council?

We are responsible for maintaining over 340 kilometres of roads in the borough and our aim is to keep all carriageways in a usable condition for all users.

Which roads are not maintained by the council?

  • Red routes which are maintained by Transport for London.  These are easily identifiable by their red road marking.  To report Red route related faults, email londonstreets@tfl.gov.uk.
  • Private roads that are roads that have not been adopted.
