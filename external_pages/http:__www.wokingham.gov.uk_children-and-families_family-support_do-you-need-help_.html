Do you need help?

Getting help and advice from us

If you need help with the below issues, you can contact us for help and advice. A duty worker will talk to you and advise you of what will happen next.

  • Report a concern about a child

  • Expand / Collapse

If you are worried about a child or young person you should tell us immediately. Children and their parents may need urgent help.

Who to contact

  • If you believe a child is in immediate danger call the Police on 999   
  • Referral and assessment team - phone 0118 908 8002
  • Email  triage@wokingham.gcsx.gov.uk 
  • Emergency duty team - out of hours telephone01344 786 543
  • Thames valley police website - phone 0845 8505 505
  • National Society for Prevention of Cruelty to Children website - 24 hour helpline 0808 800 5000
  • ChildLine website - 24 hour helpline  0800 11 11

Call 999 in an emergency if you believe a child is in immediate danger

  • Does your family need support?

  • Expand / Collapse

If your family are experiencing difficulties, or feel you might need some extra support we may be able to help you. A worker can talk to you to see what kind of help you and your family might needs.

Sometimes someone who works with you and your family if might think you might need some extra help. This could be a GP, teacher or health visitor. If they do, they will talk to you first so you're aware of what's going on.

How does it work?

  • A worker asks you and your child some questions to find out out what sort of help and support you might need. 
  • We will then agree together what needs to be done to ensure that the right sort of help is put in place for you and your family.

You can call 0118 908 8002or email triage@wokingham.gov.ukto speak to someone. 

  • Does your child have a disability?

  • Expand / Collapse

To be eligible for services, children must:

  • Have a permanent and substantial disability
  • Be under the age of 18 years, and they and their family are resident in the Wokingham Borough
  • Require more attention and support than another child of the same age and gender

Read our eligibility criteria (PDF document) to find out more about eligibility criteria.

If your child is not eligible, there are a number of local organisations and charities that can help. Information about these can be requested from us or the CAN (Children with Additional Needs) Network.

Request an assessment

You can

  • call us on  0118 908 8002 
  • email  triage@wokingham.gov.uk  

When you contact the Team, you will

  • Be put through to the duty officer who will complete an initial enquiry form and ask you for some details. 
  • If you are requesting a service, we will need to carry out an assessment of need. 
  • This means that we will look at your child's and family's needs, and will work out a plan to provide services or support. 
  • The Duty Officer is available from 9am to 5pm, Monday to Friday. 

Assessments should be completed in 7 working days.  it may be quicker than this in an emergency situation. It can sometimes take longer, depending on the circumstances, but you will be kept informed throughout the process.

My child isn't eligible

If your child's needs can't be met by us then you can  search our children with additional needs directory to find other services which can help you. Some of these services are universal, meaning they don't have eligibility criteria.

If you disagree with a decision we've made,  visit our unhappy with the decision page to find out what to do next.
