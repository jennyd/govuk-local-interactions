Apply for primary school place

If you are a resident of the Borough you will need to apply for a school place through Stockton Borough Council, even if your preferred school is in another Borough.

We are responsible for processing all applications for school places in community schools, Voluntary-Aided (VA) schools, academies and free schools.

Places are allocated according to a strict set of admission criteria and each admission authority sets its own criteria.

  • Read the Admissions to Primary School 2016 booklet.
  • Read the Admission Policy for Community and Voluntary Controlled schools for September 2016.

All VA schools, academies and free schools have their own admission policy, which you can view on our web pages or on the school's own website. When deciding which schools to apply for, you should look carefully at the admission policies to see the category that your child will be considered under.

After the closing date, we will send applications to each VA school, academy or free school. They will consider the applications against their admission policy and will send us a list of all applicants, ranked in order of their over subscription criteria. We will do the same with applications for community and voluntary-controlled schools. Parents and carers who apply online will be informed of the outcome of their application by email and a letter will also be sent to every parent and carer.

Please be aware that children who have attended a school nursery will not have priority for a place at that school. Instead, our Borough is divided into areas known as 'admission zones'. Each school within our Borough has its own defined admission zone and where you live determines which school is your admission zone school. We will always encourage you to consider your admission zone school when applying for a school place for your child.

 

Key information for children starting primary school in September 2016

If your child was born between 1 September 2011 and 31 August 2012, he or she will start primary school in September 2016.

Applications can be made between 9 November 2015 and 15 January 2016.

Any applications received after this date will be classed as 'late' and will only be considered after places have been allocated to all of the applications received 'on time'. If your application is late, we may not be able to offer your child a place at your preferred school.

You will be informed of the outcome of your application on 18th April 2016, the 1st working day after the offer date.

Summer born children
If parent/carers of summer born children, born April - August 2012, are interested in deferring their child's start date for admission in September 2016, they must make this known and clear on the application form so that the LA can deal with the request.

 

Apply for a primary school place

If your child was born between 1 September 2011 and 31 August 2012, he or she will start primary school in September 2016.

The closing date for applications has now passed - please ring 01642 526605 to request a paper application. Please note that your application will now be classed as LATE.

If you have any queries please ring the Schools Admissions Team on tel: 01642 526605 or email: school.admissions@stockton.gov.uk

  • Visit the Applying to primary schools page for further information.

 

Key dates for primary school admissions for September 2016

09 November 2015
Letters and supporting information available for parents. The online application link will open.

15 January 2016
Closing date for all applications. The online link will close and any paper applications received after this date will be marked 'late' and will only be considered after places have been allocated to all of the applications received by the closing date.

16 April 2016
National Offer Day. Letters sent to all applicants via 2nd class post. Those who applied online will also receive an email on the 18 April 2016, the 1st working day after the offer date.

June and July 2016
Admission appeal hearings will take place.
