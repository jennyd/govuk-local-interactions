Allotments in Poole

Poole has eight allotment sites providing 499 plots for the residents of Poole to grow their own fruit and vegetables. As well as the social, recreational and health benefits they provide, allotments also give shelter to wildlife and help conserve open spaces.

  1. Alder Farm Allotments, Alder Crescent, Branksome BH12 4BD

Rents

The current rent for an allotment plot which includes water charges is £0.58 pence per square metre which will rise to £0.66 pence per square metre on 01 October 2016 for the 2016 - 2017 allotment year in line with the terms of the allotment policy agreed in 2011.

Access to Leisure & Learning discounts are available for allotment rents currently standing at a discount of 30% until the end of September 2016 providing you meet the required criteria. More details on how to apply can be found at Access to Leisure & Learning.

Waiting List

The waiting list is open to all residents of the Borough of Poole to apply for an allotment plot. There are approximately 509 people on the waiting list as of 17 December 2015, the actual number varies on each site.

The average waiting time for a plot is around six years before you may be offered a plot.

When a plot is offered you will have two weeks to respond to the offer, if no response is received the plot will be offered to the next on the list and your name will be removed from the waiting list.

The average size of a plot is 114.31 Sq metres.

During the last allotment year October 2014 to the end of September 2015, 13 new plots have been created by splitting larger plots as they become vacant but no new allotment sites have been constructed.

There are monthly inspections throughout the growing season of March to September each year.

If plots remain uncultivated to the standard indicated in the allotment agreement, the plot holder is given 4 weeks to bring the plot up to standard or they may face losing their plot (unless there are extenuating circumstances).


A further inspection is carried out at the end of this four week period. If no contact has been made to explain why the plot is in a poor standard, and no improvement to the plot is seen, a termination notice is issued.

This gives the tenant two weeks to clear any personal items and the plot is re-let to the next on the waiting list.

To apply for a plot please download and return the attached form or contact either us by email or phone as below:

Email: allotments@poole.gov.uk

Tel:  01202 262500

The Borough of Poole is responsible for:

  • managing current allotment plot holder records and waiting lists
  • determining and collecting annual rent / advising tenants of rent increases
  • developing the allotment sites with tenants and the grounds maintenance contractor
  • liaising with allotment associations, tenants and prospective tenants
  • allotment agreements are issued by the Allotment Manager
  • inspection of sites jointly with Allotment Association representatives if applicable
  • issue non-cultivation notices and termination of allotment agreements due to non compliance with the allotment agreement, non-cultivation or non-payment of rents etc 
  • providing funds for new and replacement facilities
  • maintaining allotment Information on www.poole.gov.uk

Community Gardens

As an alternative to holding an allotment plot, have you considered getting involved in a community garden?

Poole Housing Partnership currently run small community gardens in Poole.

These are located in Perry Gardens, Poole Town, Turlin Road, Turlin Moor and a smaller one at Bedford Road North, Alderney.

For further information please contact Clare Sutton at PHP on Poole 01202 264316 or at c.sutton@poole.gov.uk
