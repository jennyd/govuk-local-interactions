Information on who can claim free school meals and how to make a claim

Who can get free school meals?

Since September 2014 free school meals have been available to all children in Reception, Year 1 and Year 2.  Free school meals for all other school years is currently only available to certain children. 

You can claim free school meals for your child if the child is in full-time education, you are responsible for the child, and you are getting one of the following benefits:

  • Income Support
  • Income based Job Seekers Allowance
  • Income Related Employment and Support Allowance
  • Guaranteed Pension Credit
  • Child Tax Credit with an annual income each year of less than £16,190 (as assessed by Tax Credits)
  • Support under Part VI of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999

If you receive Child Tax Credit and Working Tax Credit, your child will not qualify for free school meals.

How do I claim free school meals?

To claim for free school meals you must complete a claim form.  You can download a form from this page and return it to us to make your claim. 

If you are claiming for Housing Benefit or Council Tax Reduction (or both), and free school meals, you only need to fill in the main Housing Benefit and Council Tax Reduction application form.

We can sometimes check whether you qualify by using the Department for Education's eligibility checking system, but we may have to ask you to provide some evidence of your income if this is not possible.

How do you pay free schools meals?

We send a list of children to the school secretary each week of the school term.  This tells them which children should have free school meals and which children are no longer entitled to them.
