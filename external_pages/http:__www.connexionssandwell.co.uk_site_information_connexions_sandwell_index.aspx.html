Our Vision Statement

Connexions Sandwell will enable vulnerable young people to participate in learning and to progress into employment.

Young people aged 16-18 will be supported to re-engage in learning if necessary, working in partnership with other providers of Sandwell Council "Early Help" services.
  • Please read our Mission and Vision Statement
  • For more details about the young people Connexions Sandwell supports, please also see our explanatory note

Connexions Sandwell encourages you to get the best qualifications you can by continuing learning after year 11. Connexions Sandwell will do this by providing all young people with information about post-16 options and careers information on this website. When young people need additional support to successfully participate in learning after year 11, guidance and practical help will be available.

All advice is impartial and discussions will remain confidential.

Are you in year 12, 13 or 14? Please let us know what you are doing ...

Sandwell Connexions has the responsibility on behalf of Sandwell Metropolitan Council, to track all young people who live in the borough on whether they are in education, employment or training, until their 20th birthday (or 25th birthday for young people with a disability or learning difficulties). We have to send this information to the government and it needs to be accuarate. You may have already told us what you are doing but we just need to make sure it's still the same after a certain period of time.

If you are a Sandwell resident in year 12, 13 or 14 and have not been contacted by Sandwell Connexions already, please can you contact us to let us know what you are currently doing? You can; call us on 0121 569 2955 , email us sandwell_connexions@sandwell.gov.uk or get in touch through Facebook or Twitter.

What about when our centre is not open?

Help and advice with careers and learning is available 7 days a week between 8 am and 10 pm from the National Careers Service. You can:

Phone: 0800 100 900 (calls from a landline are free)

Text: 07766 4 13 2 19

Textphone: 08000 968 336 (if you are deaf, hard of hearing or have speech difficulties)

You can also use web chat or email to talk to trained Careers Advisers

Using our information

If you need any of our information in a different format, such as large print, Braille, audio or another language, please let an Adviser or your Connexions centre know.
