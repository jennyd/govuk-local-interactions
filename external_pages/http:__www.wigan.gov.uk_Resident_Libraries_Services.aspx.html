Library services

Our libraries offer a wide range of services to meet your educational and recreational needs. You can get online for free, take part in activities and learn new skills.

There is a network of branch libraries throughout the borough where you can gain access to:

  • Books on all subjects - you can renew books online up to a maximum of three times
  • CDs and DVDs
  • Videos
  • Talking books
  • Newspapers and magazines
  • Online resources.

Reference and enquiry services and a range of events and activities for both adults and children are also available.

How to join

The library is free to join and open to all ages. Children aged under 16 need to have their application form signed by a parent or guardian. You will need to provide evidence of your identity and where you live.

Home delivery

Library users who find it difficult to access their local library may be eligible for our free Home Delivery Service.

If you are housebound, temporarily unable to get to your local library, have caring responsibilities or find it difficult to carry books home from your library, you may be able to access this service.

Simply answer a few questions about your reading likes and dislikes for access to library books to match your preferences.

We deliver and collect books on a regular basis.

  • Call 01942 486985

Bookchoice

Struggling for something to read? Tried everything by your favourite author and need new ideas? Bookchoice can help, simply fill in the online form and you will receive recommendations tailored to your tastes.

Talking books

If you enjoy reading books but have difficulty seeing them or holding them due to visual impairment or physical disability, the Talking Book Service may be able to help.

We have a large range of talking books in different formats for you to enjoy.

Talking books can be either collected from your local library or delivered via the Home Delivery Service.

  • Call 01942 486984

Other services

Our libraries also offer other services which include:

  • WorkClub
  • Get Online
  • Universal Credit
  • Health - some libraries will have information about health issues. Leaflets on local health services will be available and you can browse the NHS website for quality information and advice.
