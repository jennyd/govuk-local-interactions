Help with preparing meals

It is important to have a nutritious and balanced diet and make sure that you eat at least 1 hot main meal each day to stay healthy.

If you have problems preparing meals and cooking this could become a problem.

If you do not eat good meals you will run out of energy and could increase your chances of having a slip, stumble or fall.

Balanced and nutritious diets

It is important for older people to have healthy meals. A balanced and nutrition-rich diet would usually include:

  • starchy foods like bread, rice, pasta, unsweetened breakfast cereals, potatoes, yams and sweet potatoes. High fibre versions are healthiest (like wholemeal bread or pasta)
  • fruit and vegetables - try to eat your 5-a-day
  • protein such as meat, fish, eggs, beans and pulses - low fat options are healthiest
  • dairy products like milk, cheese and yoghurt in moderation - again, low fat is best

Reducing how much salt, sugar and fat you have in your food will also be healthier for you.

You can find out more about how to have a balanced diet and healthy meals with our healthy eating page, and from visiting the Community Advice Hubs in:

  • North Solihull at Chelmsley Wood Library
  • South Solihull at Solihull Connect Theatre Square

Kitchen gadgets and equipment

Microwaves are useful for cooking and are often the best way to cook ready meals for the elderly. They are also convenient for things like defrosting foods and warming up a cup of tea that has cooled down.

Other kitchen gadgets can also help to keep you independent.  Visit The Better Living Centre Solihull to get free and impartial advice from trained staff and demonstrations of useful gadets, adaptations and equipment.

There are plenty of popular shops selling kitchen equipment that can help. Useful gadgets products are also available to help.

Ready Meals

Ready meals are an ideal way to ensure you are eating well.

They are available from many shops and supermarkets as fresh or frozen meals and can be delivered to your door. Ready meals can be cooked in a traditional gas oven or electric ovens, as well as microwave ovens.

To benefit from the convenience of this pre-prepared food and meal service you must have an oven or microwave oven. You will also need a fridge, a freezer, or both.

It is also important you can use an oven or microwave to benefit from these ready meals. If you are unsure of this, you may require more help to live at home.

Home delivery for food and meals on wheels

There is no cooked meals on wheels service for Solihull but you can have good meals for older people delivered to your door. 

Frozen and ready-to-heat ready meals can be found in most supermarkets and online ordering also making it easy for them be delivered directly to your home.

To see how the internet could help you keep a good and healthy diet, and bring you many other benefits, you can visit the Support for Success guide to getting online.

You could also consider home meal and food delivery options from private firms. These companies deliver a large range of meals and desserts directly to your door.

They can be delivered frozen and ready to cook or hot and ready to eat.

Local organisations that provide meals on wheels, help with home shopping and home delivery for food can list in the Solihull Directory.

When things become difficult

Community based support is available to anyone aged over 65, people that are disabled or have learning difficulties and those finding it hard to cope at home.

As well as helping you develop skills to look after yourself at home, it can also help with:

  • taking medication
  • making meals and drinks
  • coming back home after being in hospital or residential care

If you are finding it very difficult to live safely at home and are concerned, you can contact us to request an assessment. A qualified worker will work with you, and your carer or representative if you would like, to discuss your needs and identify how to help.

To understand your level of care an assessment will be conducted. The assessment will take place in your own home or wherever is most convenient for you.

The assessment process will discuss what support you need to let you live your life in the best way possible, from understanding your care and support needs to helping maintain and improve your personal wellbeing.
