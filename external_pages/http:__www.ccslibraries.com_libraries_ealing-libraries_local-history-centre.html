Local History Centre

See the Local History Events 2016 list below.

  • Are you working on a school project?
  • Did your family come from Ealing?
  • Did any famous people live in Ealing?
  • What is the story of that interesting building you pass every day?

If you are looking for the answers to these questions, then Ealing Library is a good place to start. We have a wide range of local history resources and can offer you help and advice on researching local and family history. 

Please have a look at our Local History Blog which we hope to update once a month.

Please contact us and make an appointment in advance of a visit.  We are based at Ealing Central Library.  Email: ealing.localhistory@carillionservices.co.uk

If you wish to study the history of Ealing, or your own family history, then please search through the adult learning courses for a suitable class.

 

Local History Events 2016

Tuesday 12th January
Agatha Christie and Ealing  (40th anniversary of Agatha Christie's death)
Ealing Central Library
Forty years ago to this day Agatha Christie died. She had strong links with Ealing in her youth because he great aunt, who was the inspiration for Miss Marple, lived here. There are also several references to Ealing in her stories and two well known actors who played prominent parts in TV and film adaptations of the stories lived here.
Dr Oates
 
 
Tuesday 9th February
Ealing's Ancient Rectories and Vicarages
Ealing Central Library
The clergy once lived in opulent abodes, dating from the Middle Ages. These buildings were rebuilt over the years but in the twentieth century most were demolished and the clergy lived in humbler housing. Talk looks at the rise and fall of these once prominent buildings throughout the borough.
Dr Oates
 
 
Tuesday 29th March
Underground Ealing
Some of Ealing's history is under the surface. This includes air raid shelters and civil defence headquarters, rivers and streams, as well as the borough's first public toilets. The talk discusses these as well as imaginary tunnels, especially those in Acton.
Ealing Central Library
Dr Oates
