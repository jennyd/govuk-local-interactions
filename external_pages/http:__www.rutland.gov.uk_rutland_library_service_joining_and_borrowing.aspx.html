Joining and borrowing

 

Joining the Library

 

Anyone can join, however young, and it's absolutely free. All you need to do is:

  •  fill in our membership form
  • and provide one form of identification with your name and address on it.  If you are under 18 you will need a parent or sponsor to sign for you.

Join Online

If you are 18 or over, you can join online using our online registration facility. Access our online catalogue, click on 'Log In' and click on 'Register' to join online.

A temporary borrower number and PIN will be issued to you. (PIN is a personal identification number to enable use of library PCs and online services.) You can search our online catalogue , place up to 5 holds on our books & talking books, and use our online reference services immediately.

 

You will need to collect your permanant library card from any Rutland library in order to complete your membership and have full access to our services. Your temporary borrower number will expire after 3 months. Make sure you bring some identification, when you collect your library card. If you are under 16 you will need to come into the library and have a guarantor.

 

It's important to bring your library card each time you visit the library. Your library card and your PIN number is for your personal use only. Let us know immediately if you lose it.

 

What can I borrow?

 

Borrow for free:

 

Books (up to 24 at one go!) for up to 3 weeks.

Talking Books for up to 3 weeks.

Large Print Books for up to 3 weeks.

Information DVDs (Shakespeare, gardening, health DVDs, etc) for 1 week.

Energy Monitors.

Bierley Video Magnifiers.

Ebooks.

Books on Prescription titles.

Foreign language fiction (includes French, German, Polish, and more).

Classroom Support Resource loan boxes (for teachers)

Reminiscence support stock

 

Use for free:

Our computers for up to 2 hours per day (members) or 20 minutes (visitors).

Our Reference and local history collections.

Our online reference packages such as Theory Test Pro for practicing your driving theory test, Britannica Online, or Ancestry for family History research.

Our reservation service for items in stock in other Leicestershire or Rutland libraries but not currently in the Rutland library you use (you can reserve online from your home computer).

Our Work Club for jobseeking (provided by Rutland Adult Learning).

UK Phone Book collection (Oakham Library only).

 

Borrow for a small charge:

Information Books and academic journal articles from across the country using our Inter-Library Loan Service.

 

Hire for a small charge:

 

Music CDs for 1 week. (In stock at Oakham Library only)

Feature Films and TV Series Box Sets on DVD for 1 week.

 

 

Please do not be offended if we ask for proof of age identification when you borrow DVD's and computer games.

If you are going on holiday you can have a specially extended loan, unless items have been reserved by another borrower.

 

 

Returning items

 

Please return items to the library you borrowed them from, on or before the date stamped on the label.

If items are not returned by their due date a charge will be made, and we ask that all outstanding bills are paid before any more items are borrowed.

 

Renewing items

 

If you wish to keep Books after the due date and no-one else is waiting for them, they may be renewed. If you wish to keep charged for items (such as DVDs and CDs) after the due date an additional hire fee will be incurred. If you can't get to your library you can renew books by telephone.

 

Books can also be renewed via the library catalogue, using your library card and PIN number - just go to the 'My Library Account' page to log in:

 

 

 

How do I find the books I want?

 

We have a large selection of modern and classic fiction in alphabetical order by author. We also have special sections for crime, romance, westerns, science fiction and fantasy. Information books are given 'class' numbers. The books are on the shelves in number order. There are guides to the 'class' numbers in the library. Through the library catalogue you can access the complete holdings of Rutland and Leicestershire Libraries. For eBooks see our Ebooks page.

 

How do I request or reserve (place a hold on) a book?

 

You can place a hold on any book, whether it is available in the library or not, and we will try to obtain it for you. Holds where items are "in stock" on the Leicestershire & Rutland library catalogue are FREE. Other holds including Inter-Library Loan and British Library holds will incur a small charge.

You can place a hold on any book on the library catalogue online, by using your library card and PIN number. Alternatively you can reserve any book at any Rutland library, in person, by phone or by email. Our Inter Library Loans service enables us to look nationwide.

You can also request photocopies from serials and periodicals. Because of copyright restrictions we cannot request a photocopy of more than one article from the same volume. We are sometimes able to request the whole volume in this case for you to look at.

 

How do I get a PIN number?

 

You need a PIN number to make reservations online and to access the 'My Library Account' section of our online catalogue where you can check your account and renew your loans online.

To obtain a PIN number by phone, please contact your local library and a number will be posted to your home. If you have your library card with you when you visit, a PIN can be printed for you straight away.

Don't lend your card to anyone else, as you are responsible for its use.
