Emergency Planning

Emergency Planning

Emergency Planning is an activity which is intended to prevent and reduce harm to society from hazards produced by both man and the environment.

It involves assessing threats (Anticipate & Assess) and where possible preventing them (Prevent & Prepare), tackling major emergencies when they occur (Respond), carrying on business as usual and restoring normality afterwards (Recovery).

The Sussex Resilience Forum

The Sussex Resilience Forum (Sussex Resilience Forum - SRF) is a partnership, made up of all the organisations needed to prepare for and respond to any major emergency within East and West Sussex and Brighton & Hove.

The Forum covers the Sussex Police force area, and includes the emergency services, local authorities, the Environment Agency and health agencies along with voluntary and private agencies. Under the Civil Contingencies Act (2004) every part of the United Kingdom is required to establish a resilience forum.

The Role of Horsham District Council

Horsham District Council works alongside other responder organisations under the banner of the Sussex Resilience Forum (SRF) to prepare for and respond to any major emergency within Sussex.

Should a major emergency occur in the Horsham District, it would be the role of the District Council to:

  • Support the emergency services
  • Assist people who have been affected 
  • Support the community during the recovery process
  • Provide public information via the media 
  • Maintain essential Council services and as business as usual
