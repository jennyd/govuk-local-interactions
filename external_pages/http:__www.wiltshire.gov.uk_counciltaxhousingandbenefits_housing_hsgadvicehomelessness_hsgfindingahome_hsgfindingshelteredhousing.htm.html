Sheltered housing

There are different types of sheltered housing that may be of interest to you:

Sheltered accommodation:

For people over retirement age or with an assessed need for supported housing. Accommodation generally consists of bungalows or flats.

If you would like to find out more about sheltered housing, please contact your local Housing Options Team, or you can apply directly for sheltered housing through the choice-based lettings system, Homes 4 Wiltshire

Extra care housing:

For people needing help with domestic tasks or personal care, or who have mobility issues. Extra care housing can provide an alternative to residential care, nursing care, supported living and sheltered housing. It aims to provide ‘a home for life’ for many people even if their care needs change over time.

Find out more about extra care housing

Ridgeway Care & Repair

Ridgeway Care & Repair help older, vulnerable and disabled people to remain living in their own homes. They offer advice and practical support with organising repairs, improvements and home adaptations as well as project managing building extensions.

Care & Repair also run 'Handihelp', a not-for-profit scheme that is reliable and affordable. They offer help with small tasks around the home, such as mending a tap, replacing a light bulb, fixing a smoke alarm and other odd jobs.

For more information please contact Ridgeway Care & Repair North Wiltshire on 01249 460701.

Or you can visit their website at www.ridgewaycommunity.org.uk.

Telecare

There are pieces of equipment like lifelines, pendant alarms, smoke detectors, carbon monoxide detectors and many others that can help you to remain safe at home, 24 hours a day. This sort of equipment is called telecare or assistive technology.

The council and the NHS  have one company to provide this equipment across the county. This company is Medequip-UK, working with Aster Living.

Find out more about telecare.

You can apply directly for sheltered accommodation, extra-care housing and lifeline properties, through Homes 4 Wiltshire, and you can find out more about all these types of housing, including referrals to supported accommodation, from our Housing Options Teams.

Contact details

Wiltshire Council’s Housing Options teams offer free advice and assistance to people at risk of losing their home or who are experiencing housing problems. Our offices are open from 8.30am - 5.30pm, Monday to Friday:

If you would like to visit your local office it would be helpful if you could bring along the following documentation:

  • ID
  • Proof of income
  • Child benefit / Tax credit information
  • National Insurance number

Additional documentation may include;

  • Tenancy Agreement
  • Eviction Notice (e.g. s21 Notice)
  • Court Orders
  • Bailiff Warrant
  • Letters from mortgage lender
  • Mortgage statements
  • Any supporting medical evidence such as GP letter and confirmation of medication.
