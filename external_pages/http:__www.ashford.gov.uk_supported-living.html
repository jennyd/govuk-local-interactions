Supported Living

Sheltered Housing 

What does it mean to live in sheltered housing in Ashford?

Sheltered housing is independent living with the option of support for those who need it.

Within Ashford the council owns and manages 12 sheltered schemes run by a team of scheme managers who offer and deliver a wide range of support services.

The support offered includes looking after your health and well-being, providing tenancy support, and information and advice regarding your tenancy. The 12 schemes are covered by 10 scheme managers, who work 8.00am to 4.30pm Monday to Thursday and 8am to 4pm on Fridays. Most scheme managers work in one scheme, although  two members of staff each cover two schemes (close to each other).

We have approximately 440 sheltered flats and bungalows, and a further 730 dwellings spread throughout the rest of the borough specifically intended for elderly or disabled tenants. They have a range of numbers of bedrooms, and are offered to people aged 50 and over.

Many of our units have been adapted to focus on specific housing needs to make life a little easier for our tenants – this may include level access, extra hand rails, and flush floor showers.

Some schemes have regular visiting services for the benefit of our tenants such as hairdressing, book clubs and chiropody.

We also work with care managers and social services to help deliver personalised care packages where this can be accommodated within our schemes, as efficiently as possible.

Frequently asked questions about sheltered living

24-hour support

The alarm system functions on a 24 hour, 7 days a week basis and Ashford Monitoring Centre is able to contact family and/or emergency services when necessary.

Handyman

A new handyman role is being introduced. This will be a service dedicated to the sheltered schemes and will offer an improved range of repairs and maintenance work, including some grounds maintenance.

Telecare

As well as the support from the scheme managers, each scheme has a hard wired Telecare System installed. This is managed 24 hours a day, seven days a week by a team of trained staff based at the Ashford Monitoring Centre. This gives you and your family peace of mind and means that you have the assistance when you need it no matter what time of the day or night.

Service charges

The support is charged through the service charge which makes up part of your rent, in most schemes we operate a communal heating and water facility which is also paid through the service charge. This is calculated every year based on the previous year’s consumption.

Safety and security

Each of the sheltered blocks of flats has the added security of having a secure door entry system connected to the monitoring centre. Farrow Court and Gerlach House also have their own CCTV for the added safety of its residents.

Buildings and gardens

We look after the buildings internally and externally but tenants are encouraged to be actively involved in the communal gardens and we will happily promote vegetable patches and flower beds. The bungalow schemes have their own gardens.

How to get involved

If you are already a tenant within one of our sheltered schemes and you would like to get involved, there are a number of ways that you can do this. We currently have a sheltered housing forum group which meets every two months to discuss a wide range of topics relating to sheltered housing. We encourage a ‘have your say’ on our services. On most occasions we aim to have a guest speaker on topics in which the group has expressed an interest.

The scheme managers will also encourage tenants to be involved in estate Inspections within their scheme and will support any ideas for improvements.

Social activities

Sheltered housing schemes tend to have very active social clubs, although tenants obviously have the choice to be involved as much or as little as they wish.

Many of the social activities are arranged by the residents and include evening entertainment such as live music and ‘cheese and wine’ nights, fish & chip suppers, the traditional bingo and coffee mornings but in some units there are games of Wii and X-Box Kinect happening!

The scheme mangers practise positive engagement and will help organise the social activities where required.

Modernising our sheltered schemes

Ashford Borough Council have identified that some of our sheltered accommodation needs to be modernised and expanded. We have developed long term plans over a number of years to modernise and improve the facilities.

Farrow Court, Stanhope has been chosen as the first scheme to enter this programme of transformation and improvement. The plans are in the very early stages but updates will follow.

How to apply

If you are interested in sheltered housing and currently meet the criteria then please complete a housing application form.pdf[pdf] 1MB and return it to Ashford Borough Council.

For more information or if you would like to talk to someone about sheltered housing in Ashford phone the housing services team on 01233 330688 or email housing@ashford.gov.uk
