Trees in Sunderland

Sunderland City Council is committed to preserving and enhancing trees and woodland within its control.

Trees are an important part of the character of Sunderland, bringing with them many benefits.

Follow the links below to find out more about trees in Sunderland and how the council is working to provide a sustainable pleasant environment for future generations.

  • Protected trees

    Trees may be protected by either a Tree Preservation Order or by virtue of being within a Conservation Area - you may need permission before carrying out work

  • Caring for your own trees

    Choosing a tree surgeon or arborist can be a difficult proposition and if not done properly could lead to injury, damage to property and irrevocable damage to your trees

  • Benefits of trees

    Trees play an extremely important role in the landscape and environmental quality of an area however it is very difficult to quantify or justify these benefits

  • Who to contact

    Some times it's difficult to know who to contact if you have a question about trees. It depends on who owns the tree and where it is growing

  • Planning - high hedges

    If you have concerns about a high hedge the council may be able to help in certain circumstances

  • Downloadable forms

    Downloadable forms (including a brief description for each) that relate to trees and working on trees

  • Ash tree infection

    A highly contagious infection - Ash Die-back - can wipe out the affected tree and spread to other Ash trees extremely quickly.

  • Building control

    The Building Control service is Customer Service Excellence accredited - we aim to provide all our clients with a first class service.

  • Development Control

    Development Control deals with Planning applications, appeals and enforcement issues.
