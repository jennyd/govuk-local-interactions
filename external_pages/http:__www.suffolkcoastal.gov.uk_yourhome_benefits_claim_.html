Housing Benefit - who can claim?

Housing Benefit is a means tested benefit designed to help people on a low income pay their rent.

You can claim Housing Benefit if:

  • You are on a low income and have a rent liability.

You cannot get Housing Benefit if:

  • You, or your partner between you, have more than £16,000 in savings, even if your income is low, unless you are in receipt of Guarantee Credit.
  • You pay rent to a close relative who lives in the same house as you.
  • You own the place where you live.

Most full-time students cannot claim Housing Benefit unless they are:

  • Pensioners.
  • Lone parents.
  • Disabled.
  • Getting Income Support.
  • Getting income based Job Seekers Allowance.
  • Parents with dependent children or are responsible for a child boarded out with them.

A partner is someone you are married to or in a civil partnership with, or someone you live with as if you were married to them, or in a civil partnership with.

When previously held capital is still considered

If you deprive yourself of capital in order to qualify for Housing Benefit or Local Council Tax Reduction or to increase the amount of Benefit or Reduction you get, we can treat you as still having that capital. This might occur if you give money away, to members of your family for example, or buy expensive items, with the intention of claiming Housing Benefit or Local Council Tax Reduction. 

Local Housing Allowance

In April 2008 the Government introduced a new scheme to calculate housing benefit for people renting from private landlords, this is called the Local Housing Allowance. We will pay LHA to people who:

  • Make a new claim for help with paying rent.
  • Already get Housing Benefit but move to a new address.
  • Have a break of 1 week or more in current Housing Benefit.

Find out if you qualify

You call us on 01394 444838 or use an online calculator to get an indication of your entitlement to benefit but please note that the results returned should not be used as a definitive indication of your entitlement to benefit.

Please also be aware that the calculator will ask for the Local Housing Allowance rate (if renting from a private landlord) rather than your rent amount. Please ensure that you use the correct bedroom requirement and amount to get an accurate calculation. Follow this link to check the Local Housing Allowance rates.

Non-dependent deductions

The non-dependant deduction is a deduction in your weekly benefit to allow for 'keep' we assume your non-dependant pays you to stay in your home.

  • More about non-dependent deductions

Discretionary housing payments

If you get some Housing Benefit but are having difficulty paying the rest of your rent yourself, you may be able to get a discretionary housing payment.

Or, if you are considering moving to more affordable accommodation, you may be able to get a discretionary housing payment to help with your rent in advance, your rent deposit or to help with other costs associated with your move, such as removal costs.

  • More about discretionary housing payments

Help and advice

For general questions or advice please call us on 01394 444838 or email benefits.scdc@eastsuffolk.gov.uk.

You can also get help and information by visiting us in Woodbridge and Felixstowe.

For free independent help and advice on Housing and Council Tax Benefit contact your local Citizen's Advice Bureau.
