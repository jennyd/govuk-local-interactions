Public Space Protection Order Consultation – Dog controls 

A Public Space Protection Order (PSPO) is a new power introduced under the Anti Social Behaviour, Crime and Policing Act 2014 that allows controls to be placed on the use of public spaces.  PSPO’s will become the principal control relating to dogs in public places and will supercede existing controls such as Dog Control Orders.  

The Council have taken the option of reviewing its existing controls and produced the attached draft PSPO relating to the control of dogs.  The draft PSPO contains controls relating to fouling (similar to existing controls), introduces a new control to require dog owners to be equipped to pick dog foul e.g. carry of bag or similar, prohibits dogs from fenced play areas and requires dogs to be on leads while in cemeteries, graveyards, etc.

We are issuing the draft PSPO for consultation prior to final adoption in March 2016.  It is intended that the new PSPO will come into force in April 2016.

A draft copy of the PSPO is attached and we would welcome any comments that you have on the content.  All comments received will be considered as part of the finalisation of the document prior to final adoption.

The closing date for consultation responses is Friday 12th February 2016. Blaby District Council welcomes all representations from any person or body who may wish to comment on the application.

Representations should be addressed to; Environmental Services Manager, Blaby District Council, Desford Road, Narborough, LE19 2EP. Representations may be sent by email to ian.marriner@blaby.gov.uk
