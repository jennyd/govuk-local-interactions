What to Do in the Event of an Emergency

Preparing for an Emergency
  
It is unlikely that any of us will ever be caught in an emergency or disaster but they can happen anywhere at any time and when you are least expecting it.

Industrial accidents, severe weather, fires, transport accidents and many other emergencies can strike without warning and have the possibility of disrupting and threatening lives and the community.

The UK government sent out a leaflet in 2004 to all households called:- Preparing for Emergencies - What you need to Know'.

The booklet contains useful information and steps that you can take to help yourself and your family in the event of an emergency.

The Preparing for Emergencies website (see link opposite) contains all the information in the booklet plus any additional information that has been made available by the government.

If an emergency occurs try to remain calm and carry out the following: 

  • If immediate threat to life call 999 and request help from the Emergency Services.
  • Seek information from the Emergency Services, either in person at the scene, or via local television and radio.
  • Check on neighbours, especially those who are disabled or elderly and might not be aware of the situation.
  • Remember you may be advised to evacuate or to remain in your home.  Always seek advice from the Emergency Services.
  • If evacuation is required remember to take things such as medication, baby food etc with you.
  • If anyone is injured apply First Aid until professional medical assistance arrives.
  • If it’s safe to do so, check your home for any damage, including fires, leaks etc and be prepared to turn off the electric, gas or water supplies.
  • If there is a risk of a gas leak or any other explosive substance do not turn on any electrical switches, light candles or strike matches.
  • If there is an emergency and you have been asked to stay at home, go inside closing all doors and windows and any non essential vents.
  • Tune into local television or radio to find out any more information and advice.

You can help plan for an emergency by taking some simple steps and becoming more familiar with your home.
It is important that you learn some basics about your home so that if an emergency occurs you are in the best possible position to deal with it.

Ask yourself the following questions:

  • How would you switch off your electric, gas and water supplies?
  • What are the exit routes in and around your home?
  • Are your important household documents stored in a safe fire proof container?
  • Are you and other family members aware of the location of these documents if you needed to be evacuated?
  • Are your insurance documents up-to-date and do you have sufficient cover for your home and contents?
  • Do you have contact telephone numbers for family, friends and schools easily available if you had to leave your home?

During an emergency it is also useful to have the following items available, so make sure that they are accessible and can be easily located by all family members during an emergency: - 
 

  • First Aid Kit.
  • Torch + spare batteries.
  • Emergency contacts list + note book/pen & pencil.
  • A battery powered radio.
  • Bottled water in case the mains water is unavailable.
  • Warm clothing and blankets.
  • Supplies of ready to eat food.

See Related Documents to create your own Home Emergency Plan

Evacuation.
  
If you are asked to evacuate your home by the emergency services, follow their advice and leave as soon as possible.  You would normally be taken to a reception centre where you can be looked after until its safe for you to return to your home.

If you have any elderly or disabled neighbours check that they are aware of the situation and inform the emergency services.

If there is time, try to remember to take any medication, warm clothes, special foods, personal documents with you, and before you leave switch off heating, gas, electric, and water supplies. Lock all doors and windows before you go.
