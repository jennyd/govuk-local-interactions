Bulky waste

Bulky waste is household waste you can’t fit into your rubbish bin e.g. sofa, freezer, washing machine.

How to dispose of your bulky waste

This page provides information on how to dispose of your bulky waste items.

Choose an option below when disposing of your bulky waste
  • Take most of your bulky waste items to one of our household waste recovery centres for free.
  • Look for organisations and charities on our reuse and recycle page to see if any could make use of your items. Some will collect for free.
  • Use our bulky waste collection service which will collect items from the edge of your property. For further information read our bulky waste FAQs here. Please see below for a list of items that we collect.
For commercial waste (waste generated as a result of carrying out a business), please see here for more information.

Which bulky waste items do we collect?
Please check the list below to see if we can collect your bulky waste. All those listed are classed as one item. Loose materials must be bagged or bundled and light enough for one person to lift safely or it won’t be collected.

If you don’t see your bulky waste item listed, please see our list of commonly requested items we don't collect below, and whether we may still be able to dispose of the items for a fee.

Textiles:
We will collect textiles such as bags of clothes and curtains, free of charge if you are also paying for other things to be collected. This excludes duvets and pillows, which would be charged for per bag.

Special bulky waste collections
For free removal of small amounts of hazardous waste, e.g. asbestos and pesticides, please look at our hazardous waste collection service.

We can quote for removal of your plastic oil heating tanks but they must be cut in half, disconnected from pipe work and completely empty of oil. Alternatively, cut and clean as mentioned and take them to your household waste recovery centre.

Please contact us for more information.

For metal oil tanks you will need to contact a specialist company, for example, Atlantis.

How to book & pay for bulky waste collection
At the time of booking, choose a collection day for your bulky waste and we will arrange to collect it between 7am and 5pm. You must tell us if you are booking a concession collection, as you may be asked to provide evidence of eligibility.
  • Phone: 0345 600 6400 or use one of our local numbers. You can use a credit/debit card to pay at the time of booking.
  • In person: come to one of our customer information centres to arrange your collection. You can use a credit/debit card, cash or cheque at the time of booking. 
Cancelling/changing your collection
You must inform us if you wish to cancel your collection. Please do this as soon as possible, so we don’t waste time and fuel.

Also, please tell us if you want to amend your collection, so we can make sure there is space on the vehicle. 

Please have your reference number handy when contacting us.

If you need a refund, we will issue you a cheque.

Phone: 0345 600 6400 or use one of our local numbers. 
Assistance with putting your items out
If you need help putting your bulky items out, we can offer an assisted collection. Please let us know if you require assistance at the time of booking. You will need to give a short explanation of why you are unable to move your items out of your property. 
Important information for collection day
  • Put the items for collection outside your property.
  • Place items as close as possible to where your wheeled household waste bin is collected.
  • Items should be in an area that is easily accessible and not likely to cause injury/inconvenience.
  • We can collect waste from just inside your property if you are unable to place it outside or if this will cause an obstruction. You must be present and will be asked to sign an insurance disclaimer for any accidental damage that may be caused. Please inform us of this at the time of booking.
  • If you live in a flat, items must be left on the ground floor outside the building and easily identified.
  • Bag any loose materials, ensuring they are light enough for one person to carry, or unfortunately we will not be able to take them.
