Adaptations and repairs

We have teamed up with Solihull Independent Living (SIL) to offer people who are older, disabled or on a low income an affordable way to repair, improve or adapt their homes.

Help with payments

If you’re a home owner or rent privately, you may be able to get a Disabled Facilities Grant towards the cost of adapting your property, which may require a visit from an Occupational Therapist (OT) to assess your individual needs.

  • you will be eligible for an OT assessment if you meet all of the criteria.
  • children’s assessments are not subject to this assessment framework.

The grant is means tested, so the amount that you get will depend on your income. Contact SIL for further information.

Home improvements

Solihull Independent Living can help home owners and private tenants with improvements such as rewiring, re-roofing, enhancing security and updating kitchens and bathrooms. You may also be able to get a grant or loan towards the works.

For council tenants, a home improvement and maintenance service is offered by Solihull Community Housing (SCH).

Living away from home

Support could also be available if your son or daughter wants to move away from home. Planning financially and practically for this as they approach 17 is sensible, whilst contacting SCH can provide information about:

  • the housing register
  • housing associations

Equipment to help

You may also be able to get other equipment to help with looking after your child at home or away. Provided by social services, your health visitor, social worker, GP or occupational therapist will be able to arrange an assessment into your needs.

Following assessment, a paediatric occupational therapist may be able to suggest useful specialist equipment and advise how you can find items such as:

  • adapted bikes
  • buggies
  • pushchairs
  • trikes
  • wheelchairs

Other support services can also offer information and practical help.
