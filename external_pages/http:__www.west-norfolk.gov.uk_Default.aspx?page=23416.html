Housing Options and Advice Service

The Council has a duty to ensure that advice and information about housing, homelessness and the prevention of homelessness are available to anyone in the district.
 
Homelessness Services
 
Stonham Homestay
 
Domestic Violence
 
The Housing Options Service can offer advice and assistance on the following issues:
  • housing opportunities within the borough, including the private rented sector, opportunities for housing through the Housing Register and West Norfolk Homechoice
  • details of housing associations in the district that accept direct applications
  • details of landlords and letting agents within the district
  • tenants rights and rights of occupation
  • what to do about harassment and illegal eviction
  • how to deal with possession proceedings
  • make sure you are getting the income you are entitled to, for example, welfare benefits
  • rent levels and affordability issues
  • rent guarantee or deposit schemes
  • access to floating support services to help with housing
  • rent and mortgage arrears
  • how to tackle debt
  • the homelessness legislation
  • grants available for housing repair and / or adaptation
  • leaseholders rights and service charges
  • Sanctuary Schemes (security services for people experiencing domestic violence).
Specialist advice on dealing with drug and alcohol abuse and mental health issues is available from agencies linked with the Council.  Some agencies provide a floating support service to help with housing problems.
 
It is important that you seek help as soon as possible as we may be able to find solutions to your housing problem.
 
We operate a duty service at the King's Court council offices in King's Lynn. The Housing Options duty officer is available on Monday, Tuesday, Thursday and Friday between 9.00 and 16.00 and Wednesday between 13.00 and 16.00.
 
Please be aware that if the duty officer is busy with another client, you may have to wait to be seen.
 

Homelessness Services

 

The Council has responsibility to ensure that advice and information about homelessness and the prevention of homelessness are available to anyone in the borough.

 
When a household presents as homeless, the Council undertakes an investigation into their circumstances. The Council only has a legal duty to secure accommodation for certain groups of people who are in 'priority need'. The Homelessness Act 2002 states that certain people have a priority need for housing if they are homeless or threatened with homelessness.
 
These include people with dependent children, pregnant women, people made homeless by an emergency, and people who are vulnerable – this may include people who are vulnerable because of their age; a physical disability; a mental illness; a learning disability; in fear of violence, or leaving institutional care or prison.
 
The phone number for Homelessness Services is 01553 616675
 

Rough Sleepers

 

If you are aware of a rough sleeper and would like to help, you can visit the StreetLink website or call 0300 500 0914 where  you can provide information and report your concern. The StreetLink website also provides useful information about rough sleeping.
 
StreetLink can help link someone sleeping on the street with local advice and services.

 

Am I homeless?

 

People are homeless if they have nowhere to stay that night or are threatened with homelessness within 28 days.  Homelessness can happen for a variety of reasons.  If you are homeless or think you are going to be homeless soon, the Council will investigate your case.  We will work with you to resolve your housing problems and try to prevent you from becoming homeless.

 

We will interview you about your housing situation and we may need to see you at home.

 

Am I eligible for assistance?

 

You must be a British Citizen or a person from abroad who is eligible to receive public funds.

 

Do I have a priority need?

 

The Housing Act 1996 Part VII & Homeless Act 2002 states that certain people have a 'priority need' for housing if they are threatened with homelessness.  Whether you have a priority need depends on your personal circumstances.  For example, how old you are and who is in your household.

 

Am I intentionally homeless?

 

The Council will consider whether you are intentionally homeless.  People are intentionally homeless if they have done something or failed to do something, which results in them losing their accommodation.  This can include causing a nuisance to neighbours, committing criminal acts in their home, failing to pay their rent or voluntarily leaving a secure tenancy.

 

If you have a priority need but are considered intentionally homeless the Council may only have a duty to provide you temporary accommodation for a reasonable time to enable you to find your own home (normally 28 days).

 

What is a 'local connection'?

 

You must have a local connection with King's Lynn and West Norfolk for this Council to be able to assist you with looking for permanent housing.

 

You can contact the Housing Needs department for further advice.

 

If you are unintentionally homeless and in priority need but do not have a local connection with West Norfolk we will usually ask the Local Authority where you do have a connection to provide you with accommodation, depending on your circumstances.

 

If you would like any more information on any of the above then please do not hesitate to contact us on the numbers listed below.  Alternatively, please click on this link for information on the available Housing Options and Advice Service (pdf 345Kb).

 

Stonham HomeStay

 

What does Stonham HomeStay provide?
 
This service is free and confidential and provides low level housing-related support for up to 2 years to people who are homeless, at risk of homelessness or need extra help to maintain their independence irrespective of whether you rent or own a home.  Some examples of why a person may want help from the service are listed below:
  • They have mortgage, rent arrears or debt problems
  • They are at risk of becoming homeless
  • They are setting up home for the first time
  • They are suffering from or are at risk of domestic violence
  • They have physical or mental health problems
  • They have drug or alcohol problems
  • They have recently had a relationship breakdown or bereavement.

Stonham HomeStay provides vital assistance to people living in the community and help to set up a permanent home in the community together with associated support needs.  Please contact Stonham HomeStay on 0845 155 0390 for further information.

 

People can self refer for floating support as well as be referred by other agencies, for example a landlord.
 

Domestic Violence

 

The Housing Options Team can advise and help you if you feel you cannot stay in your home, or you just want to know about your housing options.  We can help you (and your children if you have any) find somewhere to stay at any time if it is not safe for you to remain in your home.  For further information, please see the Domestic Abuse - Don't suffer in silence booklet (pdf 669Kb).

 

Domestic Abuse Disclosure Scheme (Clare's Law)

 

This gives members of the public a formal way to make enquiries about an individual who they are in a relationship with or who is in a relationship with someone they know if there is a concern that the individual may be abusive towards their partner.

You can make an application under the scheme by any of the following means:

  • By telephone - contact the police on 101 and speak with a call taker who will record the details of the application;
  • In person - make an application to a member of staff at a police station;
  • By speaking with a police officer on the street

Visit the Norfolk Police website for more detailed information and an information leaflet.
