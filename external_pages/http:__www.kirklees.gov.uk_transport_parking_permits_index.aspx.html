Types of parking permit

There are several different stypes of parking permits, you can find the information for each type below.

Resident permits


  • Resident permits are issued for vehicles owned by someone who lives in a specified property.
  • They have the registration number of the vehicle and cannot be used in any other vehicle.
  • Each vehicle, owned by a permanent resident at the specified property, can have a resident permit.
  • The cost of the permit is £15.00 and will be valid for as long as you own the vehicle
  • If you change the vehicle or registration mark on your vehicle, a new permit will be required at a cost of £15.00

Visitor permits


  • Visitor permits are issued to the occupants of a specified property and are for use by their visitors, when they are actually on the premises.
  • They should not be used by someone as free parking whilst they go to work for example.
  • Each property can have up to two visitor permits:
    • Open Visitor - this is free of charge and valid for as long as you live in the property
    • Annual Open Visitor - a second permit which will cost £15.00 per annum (12 months) and will need to be renewed each year

Business permits (including landlord permits)


  • Business permits are for occupants of business premises that are within the permit parking area but where nobody lives at the property.
  • There is a charge of £200.00 for each permit which is valid for one year from the 1st of any month.

Special residential permits


  • Special residential permits can be issued to the National Health Service, local authorities and charitable organisations.
  • This type of permit is free but the application must be approved before permits are issued.

Worship permits


  • These permits are issued to places of worship that are within a residential permit parking area, to allow congregation members to attend worship.
  • Worship permits are free but the times and days they can be used are restricted.

Cancelling a business and landlords permit


  • Any request for cancellation or refund must be made in writing giving one full months notice to the Parking Office and is subject to approval.
  • Annual permit refunds will be based only for full months.
  • A cancellation of any permit may incur a fee.

Trader permits


  • These permits are issued to traders who are working at properties that are situated in a residential permit area.
  • You may need to complete an application form and include your vehicle registration number.
  • Permits can be issued for periods of one week up to a year.
  • A charge is payable upon application.

Pedestrian area access permits


  • In some areas, parking or driving on particular streets is restricted to certain times and days.
  • These areas are called 'pedestrian areas' where access to vehicles is restricted to allow pedestrians to move about freely.
  • Pedestrian area access permits can only be issued when there is a need to go through a pedestrian area to get to an off-street parking area.
  • They have the vehicle registration number on them and cannot be used for any other vehicle.
  • These permits do not allow parking within the pedestrian area and you must have a specific off-street parking area available for use.

How to display your permit

A valid permit must be clearly displayed (showing the expiry date) at all times whilst parked in a residential parking scheme.

Motorcycles are exempt from displaying a permit but a valid permit for the motorcycle must be available in case you are asked to provide it to the council.

Who can apply for a parking permit

Only those people who live in properties that are affected by a residential permit parking scheme.

Occupants of business premises that are within the permit parking area but where nobody lives at the property.

When a residential permit parking scheme is brought into force the council has to obtain a Traffic Regulation Order (TRO) through the courts to authorise the scheme. Within this order is a list of all the addresses of the properties that will be affected and only people who reside in these properties will qualify for the issue of a residential parking permit.

  • Traffic Regulation Order (TRO)

How long a parking permit lasts for

  • Resident - valid for as long as you keep the same vehicle, registered at the property
  • Open Visitor - valid for as long as you live in the property
  • Annual Visitor - valid for 12 months and will need to be renewed each year
  • Business - valid for 12 months and will need to be renewed each year

If you change your vehicle during the year

  • You will need to apply for a new permit at a cost of £15.00

What a residential permit parking scheme is

It is an area where parking is restricted or reserved for permit holders only.

Signs erected on the pavement or at the entrances to roads indicate a permit parking zone.

Restricted parking

  • Signs will inform you of the restriction, for example 'Parking for one hour only - no return within one hour except Permit Holders'.

Permit holders only

  • If signs say 'Permit Holders Only' then only the holders of valid and current permits can park in that area.

Apply for the introduction or removal of a residential permit parking scheme in your area

  • Permit parking scheme application form

Terms and conditions

  1. Applying for a permit, you no longer need to send in your proof of residency if you are named on the council tax bill. The Council is authorised to check your details against the Council Tax records. If you are not registered for Council Tax please provide one of the following:
    • A current tenancy agreement (signed and dated)
    • One utility bill (within the last 3 months and showing the address details)
    • Proof of vehicle ownership, please provide one of the following:
      • A VQ5 document (when applying for your vehicle)
      • A current vehicle insurance schedule
      • Proof of company vehicle and authorisation to be kept at the property
  2. A permit should only be used by a bona fide visitor and must not be used for any other reason.
  3. Supplying an incomplete application or insufficient supporting document may delay your application.
  4. If your permit is lost, stolen or damaged, you must apply for a replacement. A charge may be applied
  5. A permit that has been copied or altered in any way will be considered invalid
  6. The permit is the property of the council and may be withdrawn at any time at the Councils discretion.
  7. The permit is valid only for the area(s) and times shown on the permit and must be clearly displayed.
  8. A permit does not guarantee a space
  9. A Penalty Charge Notice may be issued for any parking contravention or for any suspected miss-use of a permit.
  10. For permits where payment is required, you will be sent an acceptance email for your application. A unique pin number will be given and you will need to make your payment using the pin number provided. Keep this pin number in a safe place as you may need this at a later date.
  11. Any alteration after the permit has been issued may be subject to an administrative charge
  12. No refund will be made if a permit is no longer required or returned to the council by the permit holder or if cancelled by the council (not applicable to all permits)
  13. The council may use the information you have provided to prevent and detect fraud. It may also share this information for the same purpose with other local authorities and organisations that handle and protect public funds.

If you lose your parking permit

Request a new permit.

There is a £20.00 charge for a replacement permit to be issued.
