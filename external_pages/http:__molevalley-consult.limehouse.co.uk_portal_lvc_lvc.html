Local Validation Checklist

Local Planning Authorities are required to publish a Local Validation Checklist.  The purpose is to set out information which must be submitted with different types of application in the District, alongside national requirements which apply throughout the country.  The aim is to make the process clear for anyone submitting an application as well as ensuring that the Council has the information it needs to determine it. 

The submission of the right information is a significant factor in making timely and good-quality planning decisions.  In producing this document, the Council has aimed to strike the right balance between ensuring that all those involved in consultation and decision-making have the right information before them, while avoiding placing any unreasonable or unnecessary burden on those preparing applications. 

Consultation Arrangements

This document seeks your views on Mole Valley's proposed Validation Checklist which will set out the documents required with applications submitted in Mole Valley.  The consultation is open to anyone.  All comments must be made not later than 23 August 2013.

We want your views on the proposed validation checklist.  Are the requirements:

  • Necessary? Are the requirements based on statutory requirements, national or local policy or guidance?
  • Precise? Is it clear what development requires which particular supporting information?
  • Proportional? Is the information required proportional to the nature, scale and sensitivity of the development?  Are thresholds clearly set out?
  • Fit for purpose? Is it clear what information is needed in order to satisfy the requirement?
  • Helpful? Is it clear where further information can be found or questions answered if required?

Your comments are important.  Once the Validation Checklist is finalised, applications will not be validated if the correct supporting information is not supplied. 

Once comments are returned they will be considered and the Validation Checklist will be amended as appropriate.  A summary of the comments will be published alongside the response by the Council.  The Checklist will be finalised and Officers will present it to the Council's Executive for their consideration.  Once approved it will be published on the Council's website and made available alongside planning application forms.
