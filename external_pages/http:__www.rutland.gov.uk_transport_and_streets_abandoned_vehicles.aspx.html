Abandoned vehicles

 

A vehicle will not be treated as abandoned simply because it is untaxed.

 

PLEASE NOTE THAT UNTAXED VEHICLES ARE NOT THE RESPONSIBILITY OF THE COUNCIL AND WILL NEED TO BE REPORTED TO THE DVLA (at www.gov.uk)

 

Rutland County Council has the power to remove abandoned vehicles under the Refuse Disposal (Amenity) Act 1978.

 

Abandoned vehicles are becoming an increasing problem in many areas. As well as being an eyesore, abandoned vehicles can attract anti-social behaviour including arson and vandalism which can endanger lives, property and the environment.

 

What is an abandoned vehicle?

For the council to investigate, an abandoned vehicle is likely to have one or more of the following characteristics:-

·         A vehicle that the keeper no longer wants

·         It is in poor condition, has sustained accident damage, is burnt out or has been vandalised eg flat tyres, missing parts, broken windows

·         It has not been moved for a long period of time

·         It has an invalid tax disc or no tax disc at all

·         It is not secure

 

If a vehicle is on private land (ie a private carpark, driveway etc) the landowner(s) will need to provide us with written confirmation that the vehicle has been parked on their land without their permission and they would like us to arrange its removal. If you are not the landowner please pass on details of the vehicle to the landowner(s) and ask them to contact us.

 

If an officer is unable to determine, after investigation, that a car can be classified as abandoned it will not be treated as such.
