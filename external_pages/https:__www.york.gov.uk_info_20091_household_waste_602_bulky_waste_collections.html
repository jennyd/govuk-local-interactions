How can we help you today?

Bulky waste collections

Planned maintenance...

We're making essential updates to 'Do It Online' on Saturday 6 and Sunday 7 February. Some online services may be unavailable during this time but you can still pay invoices using our 'WebPay' system.

www.york.gov.uk/WebPay

We can collect up to ten unwanted items of 'bulky items' from your property. These items can include furniture, bed bases, mattresses, garden chairs, and other household items.

Some special terms apply:

  • each small items count as one item
  • removing a bed that includes a base, a mattress and a headboard would count as three items
  • cast iron baths and pianos are assessed on an individual basis
  • if we need to 'assess' your bulky items we'll let you know the cost of removal before we arrange collection

Booking a bulky item collection

Bulky items collections £42.

Contact our environmental services team via our customer centre to arrange a collection and make a payment (over the telephone).

Please ensure you book a bulky item collection day appropriate to your ward/area.

Putting bulky items out for collection

Items for collection must not be placed on a public path, a highway or verge. They should be left outside in a suitable place where we can easily access them, for example, on your driveway or in your garden. For collections to happen:

  • items must be available by 8.00am on the day of collection
  • you may want to put items out a number of days before the collection is due
  • you do not need to be present at the time

Please note, we will not enter a property to remove items under any circumstances.

Fridges, freezers and asbestos

Find out how we can also help with removal or fridges and freezers.

Asbestos collections are not made at the same time as other bulky household waste, but separate collections are available.

Bulky items we cannot collect

As of June 2015, we will not collect any of the following items:

  • trade waste
  • fluorescent tubes
  • domestic household waste (for example bagged waste that goes into a wheelie bin or bin bag)
  • garden waste (for example bags of leaves, cuttings, bushes, tree branches)
  • builders waste (for example plasterboard, rubble, masonry)
  • gas bottles
  • whole or damaged panes of glass (for example glass in a window frame or loose window panes, glass in a door frame - we will take glass that is secure, for example in a microwave or old style TV as long as it is not broken)
  • any soiled products (for example mattresses, sofas or soft furnishings)
  • tyres

Consider reusing or recycling household items

There are a number of ways your household items could be reused or recycled:

  • the York Freecycle group is open to all residents of York who want to 'recycle' something rather than throw it away. Whether it's a chair, a fax machine, piano or an old door, feel free to advertise it. Or maybe you're looking to acquire something yourself. There's one main rule, though: everything given away must be free! For more information or to join the network visit the Freecycle website

  • collections of furniture and electrical items from British Heart Foundation (BHF) are free and can be arranged by contacting them by calling tel: 0844 334 1414, Mondays to Fridays 9.00am to 5.30pm, or by requesting a collection via the BHF website

  • the York Community Furniture Store, Unit 29 The Raylor Centre, James Street, York can arrange collection of furniture and electrical items if you call tel: 01904 426444.

The Community Furniture Store supplies furniture and electrical goods to households in the York area offering discounts for people with proof of low income. No gas appliances. All soft furnishings must meet 1988 fire regulation and be in a reasonable condition. Alternatively deliveries can be made by prior arrangement directly to the store which is open 9.00am to 5.00pm Mondays to Fridays, and 9.30am to 4.30pm on Saturdays.
