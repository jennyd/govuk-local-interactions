Noise pollution

Summary

This page details which noise issues are dealt with by our Environmental Health Department, and which are dealt with by our Anti-social Behaviour Department

The environmental health team can investigate the following complaints concerning noise:

  • Amplified noise (e.g. loud music or TV)

  • Animal noise (e.g. barking dogs or crowing cockerels)
  • Commercial noise (e.g. noise from shops, pubs, leisure facilities)

  • Construction or industrial noise (e.g. machinery, factories, building sites)

  • Intruder alarms (e.g. car and house alarms)
  • Mechanical noise (DIY)

Anti-social behaviour and noise reportedly being made on purpose

If you believe that the noise is being made on purpose and you are able to provide evidence in the form of completing diary sheets that we send to you, we will review the information and assess how best we can help you. We work very closely with our Anti-social behaviour (ASB) team colleagues and it is likely that we will refer your complaint to a member of the ASB team. The Anti-social behaviour team will assist with the following:

  • Noises reportedly being made on purpose
  • Noise associated with loitering, groups or gangs
  • Noise associated with anti-social behaviour
  • Abusive language directed at yourself or others
  • Swearing, shouting, slamming doors in excess

Noise that we are unable to deal with

Transport noise

Unfortunately, we have no jurisdiction to deal with road traffic noise, railway noise and aircraft noise; these are exempt from statutory nuisance law under the Environmental Protection Act 1990. We may work in conjunction with the Highways department at Leicestershire County Council if there is an identified noise problem associated with a particular road, especially if it is a new road or recently altered.

Banging noises, slamming doors, children playing, heavy footsteps

Unfortunately, we have no statutory powers to deal with people noise and noise associated with people’s behaviour. Please note that no home can be totally sound-proofed and it is expected to hear some neighbour noise, particularly if you live in a flat, semi-detached or terraced house
