Education other than at School

This is where parents provide for their children's own education by making alternative arrangements, rather than sending them to a local school.  

Some parents chose to educate their children at home, for a variety of reasons. This Local Education Authority (LEA) supports the right of parents to educate their children at home.

 

  • What should you do if you want to educate your child at home?
    If your child is attending school, you should inform the headteacher of your child's school, in writing.
    The headteacher should then inform the LEA.
    If your child has never attended school, you are asked to inform the LEA of your decision to educate your child at home.

 

  • What are parents' responsibilities?
    “The parent of every child of compulsory school age shall cause him/her to receive efficient full-time education suitable –
    (a) to his/her age, ability and aptitude, and
    (b) to any special educational needs he/she may have,

Either by regular attendance at school or otherwise”  (Section 7, 1996 Education Act).

In legal terms, an education is ‘efficient’ if it achieves what it sets out to achieve and ‘suitable’ if the education prepares the child for life in modern society and enables the child to achieve his full potential.

There is no requirement for parents to apply the National Curriculum, to operate school hours or to give formal ‘lessons’.

If parents choose to home-educate, they assume financial responsibility for their child’s education, including the cost of any public examinations that they may chose to take. The child must continue to receive suitable education until the end of ‘compulsory education’ (i.e. the last Friday in June in the academic year in which they reach age 16).

What if your child has a Statement of Special Educational Needs?


If your child has a Statement of Special Educational Needs which names a special school, you must obtain the consent of the LEA to withdraw your child from the school. This is to enable the LEA to ensure that the child receives the special educational provision specified in the statement.

What are the LEAs responsibilities?


The LEA needs to satisfy itself that children receive suitable educational provision. In order to do so, the officer with responsibility for home education will stay in contact with the family in order to discuss their plans and to offer support and guidance.
