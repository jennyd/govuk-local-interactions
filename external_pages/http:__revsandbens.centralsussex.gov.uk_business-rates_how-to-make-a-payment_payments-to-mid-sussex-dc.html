Payments to Mid Sussex DC

How to pay NNDR - National Non-Domestic Rates (Business Rates)

Mid Sussex District Council is open:

  • Monday to Thursday 8.45 am to 5.00pm
  • Friday from 9.00am to 4.00pm
  • Telephone calls are taken from 9.00am to 5.00pm

How to pay

  • Monthly instalments by Direct Debit, you can choose to pay on the 1st, 10th or 20th day of each month
  • Monthly instalments (other methods), instalments are due on the 1st of each month form April to January. The number of instalments will be reduced if your bill is issued from May onwards.
  • Annually in full not later than the 1st April
  • Payments by telephone or over the internet may be made by Direct or Credit Card. A fee of 1.5% will be charged for payments made by Credit Card.

Direct Debit

Direct Debit is the easiest, cheapest and most popular way to pay your Non-Domestic Rates. You may pay on the 1st, 10th and 20th of each month. Direct Debit authorises the Council to collect Non-Domestic Rates direct from your Bank or Building Society. We shall advise you within 10 working days of the amounts due to be collected. You can set up a Direct Debit by calling the Revenues Office. Alternatively a Direct Debit form can be issued on request or downloaded below.

Direct Debit Form

By Telephone

You may pay by phone using a Debit or Credit Card by telephoning the 24 automated payment line on 0800 090 2910. A fee of 1.5% will be charged for payments made by Credit Card.

On-line on the internet

The Council uses a secure server to receive your payment card details. Please ensure you have the account reference to which your payment relates. Click here to pay online

At the Post Office

If you do not have a bank account a personalised payment card can be issued for payments to be made at the post office.

If making payment by cheque, please note:

  • Cheques should be made to 'Mid Sussex District Council'
  • Please write your Non-Domestic Rate account number, name and address on the reverse of the cheque
  • Post dated or third party cheques are not accepted - they will be returned to you
  • Receipts will only be given if payment is accompanied by a stamped address envelope
  • Any foreign payments must also cover the cost of the Council's bank charges

Cash should NOT be sent through the post

Should you wish to change the way you pay your bill please:

  • Call the Revenues Office: 01444 477564 or
  • e-mail: revenue@centralsussex.gov.uk

Please note

If you opt not to pay by the Statutory scheme i.e. quarterly/half yearly, if unpaid, these amounts will be subject to recovery action in accordance with the Statutory scheme.
