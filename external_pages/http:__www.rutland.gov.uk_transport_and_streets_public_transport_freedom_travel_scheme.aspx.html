Freedom Travel Scheme

 

FREEDOM TRAVEL SCHEME APPLICATION FORM

 

1.      Who Can Apply?

 

You are eligible for the concessionary Travel scheme if you are:

Resident in Rutland AND Eligible by age for a state pension.

 

You will be entitled to a bus pass giving free off-peak travel on local buses when you reach 'eligible age'. If you were born after 5 April 1950, the age you become eligible is tied to changes in the State Pension age for women. This affects both men and women.

  • If you were born before 6 April 1950, you are eligible for a bus pass from your 60th birthday.
  • If you are a woman born after 5 April 1950, you will become eligible for a bus pass when you reach pensionable age.
  • If you're a man born after 5 April 1950, you will become eligible when you reach the pensionable age of a woman born on the same day.

You can check when you'll become eligible by entering your sex and date of birth into the State Pension age calculator

 

When applying for the first time you will be required to present proof of age and residency.  Photocopies are acceptable.

The following documents will be accepted as proof of age:

Birth certificate, driving licence, medical card, pension book or letter from the Department of Social Security confirming that the pension is paid directly into the applicants bank account.

The following documents will be accepted as proof of residency.

Rent book, council tax demand or utility bill.

 

2.     National Free Travel Pass

 

Eligible applicants are entitled to a free travel pass that can be used on all local bus services in England.

Passes are issued for a 3-year period.

If you suffer from a disability or cannot use public transport due to a restricted mobility you may be eligible for a Free Travel Pass or National Travel Tokens through the Access Travel Scheme (contact the Council for further details)

A passport-sized photograph must be provided if applying for the first time.  Please write your full name on the back of the photograph.

 

3.     When can I use my Travel Pass?

 

The Travel Pass can be used on any local bus service within Rutland at the following times:

Between 0930 and 2300 - Monday to Friday, and All day on Saturday, Sunday and Bank Holidays 

Passes may also be used on some socially necessary journeys (ie hospital appointments) booked through the Community Car Scheme operated by Voluntary Action Rutland. For journeys provided by VAR, holders of a valid Travel Pass are eligible for a 50% discount on the cost of the journey. For more details, please contact VAR on (01572) 724705.

 

 4.     Can I use my Travel Pass outside of Rutland?

 

Yes you can. The Travel Pass issued by Rutland is part of the English National Concessionary Travel scheme which entitles pass holders to use any local bus service in England within the time periods specified above. Additional benefits and unrestricted times may be available in other parts of the country but these will need to be checked with the bus operator or council in the areas that you are visiting.  

 

5.       How do I apply?

 

If you are applying for the first time you should send a completed Freedom Travel Scheme Application Form (pdf file) with the relevant documents relating to proof of age and residency to the address below

FREEDOM TRAVEL SCHEME

THE TRANSPORT DEPARTMENT

RUTLAND COUNTY COUNCIL

OAKHAM

RUTLAND

LE15 6HP

 

Alternatively you can bring your application into the council offices and present it to a member of our Customer Services Team.

 

6.       Is there a charge?

 

No, there is no charge for the initial pass. However, should you require a replacement, there will be a charge of £5 for a re-issue.

 

7.       How do I Renew My Concession?

 

Your pass will have an expiry date on it. You will receive a reminder before the current pass expires and you will need to request a new pass.
