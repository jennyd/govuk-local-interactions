Children with Special Educational Needs (SEN)

We provide a wide range of services for children with special educational needs and/or disabilities and their families. These services cover support with all aspects of care and development and specialist education support.

Our 0-25 SEN Team provides advice, support and guidance to children and young people with complex needs and disabilities and their families. They work closely with other services and agencies to make sure the needs of children are met and their families are appropriately supported.

You can find more information in these pages about short breaks services, specialist educational support and the Stockton United for Change, the local parent partnership group.

 

Changes to services for children with special educational needs and/or disabilities

 
                                                                 

A 'Local Offer' has been developed to enable children and young people with special educational needs and disabilities (aged 0 - 25) and their families to access information about all of the services available to them from one place.

                          Visit Stockton's Local Offer website                           

The Government's Children and Families Act 2014 sets out a series of major changes to how local councils and their partners provide services to children and young people with special educational needs and disabilities. The Act comprises the Government's vision for reform in relation to provision for children and young people aged up to 25 with special educational needs and disabilities and their families. The changes introduced by the Act are now being implemented. This will be a gradual transition rather than an immediate wholesale change.

Stockton Council is now introducing the changes, which include replacing statements for children with special educational needs and disabilities with a new integrated assessment and a single Education, Health and Care Plan (EHC Plan).

Importantly, the new approach will focus on ensuring coordinated services are planned across different services for education, health and care.

Parents and carers are being informed about and consulted on the changes along with a range of Council partners including education providers, Catalyst and parents' participation group Stockton United for Change.

Carole Wilson, a parent involved in Stockton United for Change, said "The reforms will put parents, carers, young people and children right at the heart of decision-making."

Children and Young People’s Disability Register Stockton

This is a confidential record of information about children and young people with disabilities living in the Borough and helps us to plan our services to meet the needs of these children.

Children’s names are added to the register at their parent’s request, and in return parents can receive information about services and special events available in their area.

To be able to register children and young people must:

  • Have a learning difficulty, mental health, medical condition or a physical disability which has a substantial adverse effect on their ability to live and develop as other children of a similar age
  • Be 0 to 17 years old and live in the borough of Stockton-on-Tees

The information is held on a secure computerised system, it conforms to The Data Protection Act 1998. Only authorised staff can access this information.

Register your information on the Stockton Children and Young People's Disability Register.

 

Child and Adolescent Mental Health Service (CAMHS)

Stockton CAMHS work together to promote a sense of wellbeing, by helping children and young people and their families to cope with any difficulties they may be experiencing. They work with young people under 18, including young people who are looked after by Stockton Council.

  • Visit the Tees, Esk and Wear Valleys NHS website for further CAMHS information.
  • YoungMinds is the UK’s leading charity committed to improving the emotional wellbeing and mental health of children and young people. Visit the YoungMinds website for further information, alternatively contact the parents helpline on 0808 8025544.
