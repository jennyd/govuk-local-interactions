Garden Waste Bin (Brown Bin)

Popular Tasks for Waste & Recycling

 

 Apply for a Brown Bin

 

 View your Bin Collection Calendar

Since the brown bin garden waste collection service was introduced in 2006, over 21,700 homes have joined the service. 

 

Each year we collect over 8,000 tonnes of garden waste from across the borough which is turned into nutrient-rich compost for farms and gardens, instead of being dumped into a landfill site.


For just *£42 a year, paid by direct debit, you can do your bit for our local environment by joining the scheme. You can even share a bin with your neighbour, if you both agree. 

 

Just complete the brown bin application form  

 

After we have received your application form, you will be sent:

  • A letter of acknowledgement;
  • A Direct Debit mandate form to be completed and returned, and,
  • A calendar of your future collection dates. 

Once the Direct Debit form is returned and processed, your bin will be delivered within 10 working days. 

 

We start collecting at 7:00 a.m., so it's important that you put your bin out at the boundary of your property before then (but after 7.00 p.m. the previous evening).  Please make sure we can see it - we'd hate to miss it!  If you leave your bin out on the pavement it may become a nuisance to elderly or disabled people, or parents with prams and pushchairs, so please return your bin back onto your property by the end of the collection day - thank you.

 

We'd also advise you to put your house number or name on the bin, just to be on the safe side, and don't hesitate to contact us if you have problems putting out your bin - we are only too happy to help you if you find it a bit of a struggle.

 

As your contract is renewed, you will be sent a letter of notification for the coming year. If you wish to continue with the scheme, your payments will be collected by *direct debit.

 

* Please note:

  • From 1 April 2016 - a £5 administration charge will be payable for non-direct debit payments,
  • The annual charge is non-refundable, however, you can take your brown bin with you when you move house within the Borough - just let us know your moving date.

We will collect:

  • Grass & hedge clippings
  • Twigs and small branches
  • Cut flowers
  • Windfall fruit
  • Leaves
  • Prunings from shrubs
  • Shredded paper

We cannot collect:

  • Soil
  • Stones or gravel
  • Seed trays or flower pots
  • Kitchen or food waste
  • Pet litter

Other ways to dispose of garden waste

 

Garden waste cannot be disposed of in your black bin. If you do not wish to join the brown bin scheme you can compost your garden waste at home or can take it to a Norfolk County Council household recycling centre.
