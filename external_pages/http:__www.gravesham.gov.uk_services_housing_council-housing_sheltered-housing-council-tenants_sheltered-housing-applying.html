Sheltered housing - applying

What is Sheltered Housing? Who can apply and other useful information, including Useful Contact numbers.

What is Sheltered Housing?

Sheltered Housing allows independent living but gives the extra reassurance that assistance or help is at hand if there is an emergency.  A Scheme Manager is available to advise residents on a daily basis and will help arrange support from various agencies if this is needed to help ensure the health and well being of residents.  A Support Plan will be agreed with you when you move into Sheltered Housing.

Residents have their own self contained accommodation within a sheltered housing scheme allowing them to enjoy additional security and peace of mind whilst being able to enjoy the facilities available at the scheme.

Some of our schemes have laundry rooms, TV rooms and guest rooms. Many have the benefit of door entry systems, smoke detectors and fire alarms. All of our sheltered housing is fitted with pull cords so that help can be summoned in an emergency situation at any time day or night.

A Scheme Manager is based at each sheltered scheme to give support, advice and help to residents and to ensure as far as possible, the safety and well being of residents.

Who can apply?

Any person over 60 whatever their circumstances can apply. You will need to complete a Common Housing Register Form (see link below). Any person aged between 55 and 59 can also apply if they are registered disabled.

Applicants will be visited at home by one of our Scheme Managers. They will give you information on our Sheltered Housing Service and advise you as to what may be available to you. You will be able to ask the Scheme Manager any questions you may have about sheltered housing. If you are interested in viewing any schemes they will help you to arrange this.  The Scheme Manager will carry out an assessment as to whether Sheltered Housing is the right choice for you and you will be asked a number of questions so that an assessment can be made.  You will be advised of the outcome of your assessment.  If we find that Sheltered Housing is not the best choice for your individual circumstances you will be given advice on better alternatives.  You have the right to appeal this decision.

I'm a homeowner can I apply?

Yes, but we will ask you for evidence of the value of your property, details of your income and savings and we will carry out a financial assessment. We will also ask for details of your current circumstances and any health or disability problems.

Your application will be considered and you will be advised as to whether you can be offered Sheltered Housing or whether you should seek privately rented or privately owned Sheltered Housing.

Equal Opportunities

Gravesham is committed to providing housing services to the whole community and seeks to ensure that no-one applying for housing will be discriminated against on the basis of their ethnic origin, nationality, religion, gender, sexual orientation, race, or disability.

Can I choose where I want to live and the type of property I live in?

Yes, by telling us on your application form. We will discuss your chances of being re-housed at your preferred schemes. We recommend you visit the schemes you are interested in so you can check whether they meet your needs.

A guide to our Sheltered Housing schemes can be obtained from our allocations team who can be contacted on 01474 33 73 66. If you wish to visit a scheme you can do this by contacting the Scheme Manager at the scheme you are interested in or by contacting the Sheltered Housing Co-ordinator on 01474 33 7272.

Can I bring my pet with me?

This may be possible, but depends on what kind of pet you have and which scheme you are moving to. Ask for a copy of the scheme information leaflet for details or ask the Scheme Manager who should be able to advise you.

How do you let your sheltered housing units?

When a property becomes available for letting it will be offered to the applicant with the highest housing needs score, following a Housing and Support Needs assessment.

What size of property can I register for?

We automatically register singles/couples for one bedroom accommodation. We do have a small number of bed-sitters at some schemes which we may offer to single applicants, if you have expressed an interest in this type of accommodation. We also have a small number of two-bedroom units which are offered to applicants needing separate bedrooms for medical reasons.

How can I increase my chances of being re-housed?

You can increase your chances by selecting a number of schemes you wish to be considered for. Some schemes are in high demand, but other schemes have only a limited number of applicants, which will increase your chance of being re-housed and reduce the length of time you will have to wait.

There may also be some current vacancies where there are no applicants. Details of these vacancies can be obtained by contacting the Allocations team or the Sheltered Housing Co-ordinator.

Can you re-house me if I'm homeless?

If you are threatened with, or are actually homeless you should contact our Housing Needs Team. They can be contacted on: 01474 33 73 11. They will assess your application. If you are accepted you will be given priority for re-housing.

Do you exclude or suspend applicants from your list?

Yes. Those excluded are:

  • Persons subject to immigration control.
  • Applicants living abroad who have no date of return to this country will not be offered a tenancy, but may register to establish a date priority for the future.
  • Applicants who have been guilty of unacceptable behaviour.
  • Applicants who have a poor rent payment history/current arrears.
  • Transfer applicants who have damaged their existing property.
  • Applicants who have the financial means to purchase a sheltered housing dwelling.
  • Applicants who have an Introductory Tenancy.

What happens if I refuse an offer?

We will make you an offer of housing based on what you have asked for and assume that you have a real interest in moving to those areas. If you refuse an offer we will discuss this further with you.

What happens if I accept an offer?

We expect you to be in a position to accept a tenancy at very short notice and to arrange to collect keys for the property and sign a tenancy agreement.

If you are an existing tenant and are downsizing your property (moving from a larger to a smaller property), we will assist you with the payment and arrangements for your move. We will also advise you if you are entitled to receive a 'Tenants Incentive payment'.
