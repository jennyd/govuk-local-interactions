Dangerous structures

 
Download as PDF
Last Modified January 07, 2016
Share this page


 

Blackpool building control| Yates Wine Lodge, Talbot Square

The Building Act 1984 empowers local authorities to deal with a building in a dangerous condition. Building Control has the responsibility for making sure that reported dangerous buildings and structures are dealt with to prevent injury to the public.

These can include: 

  • loose or falling roof tiles
  • walls that are in danger of collapse
  • unstable chimneys
  • buildings that become unstable because of storms, fire damage or vehicle impact. 

Once a report is received, one of our building control surveyors will visit the site to assess the danger and associated risks.

If immediate action is needed to remove the danger, Section 78 of the Act gives us the power to ensure that work is carried out immediately to protect the public.  The cost of this work is chargeable to the building owner.

If the inspection shows the building/structure is not imminently dangerous but is in a state that cannot be left indefinitely, then the Council can seek a Court Order under Section 77 of the Building Act, which will make the owner carry out works to ensure its long term stability.

If you are concerned about a dangerous building, please contact the Building Control team on 01253 476219 (or out of hours please call 01253 477600).
