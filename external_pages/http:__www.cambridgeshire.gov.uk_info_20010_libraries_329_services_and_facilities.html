Computers, internet and use of media in libraries

Public computer access may not be possible in libraries due to intermittent IT issues.
We apologise for any inconvenience

You can make use of:

  • free computer use
  • free internet access
  • Microsoft applications for word processing, spreadsheet and presentation work
  • free WiFi in libraries (where available)
  • self-service scanning (in selected libraries)

Book a library computer

Free WiFi

Free unlimited WiFi is available in most libraries (check your library for availability). To use it, you need to:

  1. enable WiFi and connect to the network called 'CambWifi'
  2. enter your email address and click 'Get Online'

You will be logged in automatically whenever you are in a CambWifi network location.

Printing facilities are not available through the libraries WiFi network.

Watching TV in libraries

The law states that you need to be covered by a TV Licence to watch or record television programmes as they're shown on TV - on any device including a TV set, computer, laptop, tablet, mobile phone, games console, digital box, or DVD/VHS/Blu-ray recorder.

Breaking this law may lead to a maximum fine of £1,000. You may NOT watch live TV using devices we provide. You may watch live TV here using your own devices only, provided (a) the device is powered by internal batteries and not plugged in, and (b) you have a current TV Licence at another address.

Access to non-live TV pictures, such as the Channel 4 On Demand service, or the BBC iPlayer service, do not require a TV licence.
