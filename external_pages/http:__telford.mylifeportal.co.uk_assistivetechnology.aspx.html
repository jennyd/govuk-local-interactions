Assistive Technology

New Assistive Technology campaign

A new campaign called AT Home began in December 2015 to encourage people to think about how new technology and devices can help people to continue to live independently in their own home. This type of support, known as assistive technology, is becoming increasingly important as councils’ social care budgets come under increasing pressure due to cuts in Government grants.

People approaching older age often aren’t aware of how assistive technology could help them remain independent and in their own home later in life.

Telford & Wrekin are joining forces with 13 other councils in the region to promote AT Home and raise awareness of the simple technology that is available from the high street to assist people to remain independent at home.

Telford and Wrekin Council are holding road shows at the following venues:

5 February from 9.30 to 2pm at Newport Library

8 February from 9.30 to 2pm at Princess Royal Hospital entrance (close to League of Friends cafe)

You can watch real life stories by visiting the AT Home video page, featuring four Telford residents who explain how assistive technology has helped them.

For more information please view the AT Home flyer or the AT Home website.

Assistive technology or gadgets – Is equipment that helps you to carry out everyday tasks with less effort and less risk. It ranges from simple gadgets such as:

A motion detector light bulb
which comes on automatically
when movement is detected.
This can help to prevent falls...

...to very sophisticated equipment that helps severely disabled people to open and close doors,windows and curtains or use their television.

You can find examples of gadgets that can be used in the home to help promote independence using the links below.

  • Gadgets to help support every day tasks
  • Gadgets that can alert you if someone needs help
  • Gadgets to help with communication
  • Gadgets to help with memory

To see what gadgets could help make life easier take a guided tour around Mi Smarthouse or visit the Disabled Living Foundation and AskSara websites to identify the right solution for you.

There are lots of gadgets that you can buy on the high street or online a few examples of retailers are on our information about purchasing gadgets page.
