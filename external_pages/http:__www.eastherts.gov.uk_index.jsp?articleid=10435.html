Community Safety

East Herts Council is celebrating 10 years of working in partnership to: 'Reduce levels of crime and anti-social behaviour and increase people's sense of community safety'

How do you rate this information / service?

  • CCTV
    East Herts Council along with our partners operates and maintains a comprehensive CCTV system across the district in the three towns of Hertford, Ware and Bishops Stortford.
  • Designated Public Place Orders (DPPO)
    The Criminal Justice and Police Act 2001 allows local authorities to take action against anti-social drinking by applying for a Designated Public Place Order. The Order provides Police Officers and Police Community Support Officers (PCSO) the power to require a person to stop and surrender their alcohol for disposal, if they have been, are or intend to drink alcohol in public places when its consumption is associated with anti-social behaviour.

  • Community Safety Plan
    The Crime and Disorder Act 1998 made it a statutory duty for local authorities to work with the Police and other partners to reduce and address crime and disorder in their areas.
  • Community Safety Action Plan
    Every year the Community Safety Partnership will carry out strategic assessments on the Community Safety Plan.
  • Hate Crime
    The Police APCO defines Hate Crime as: 'any crime where the perpetrator's prejudice against an identifiable group of people is a factor in determining who is victimised'.
  • Safer East Herts Newsletters
