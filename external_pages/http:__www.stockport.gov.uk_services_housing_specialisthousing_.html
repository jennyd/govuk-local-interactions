Supported and Older Person's Housing

There are different types of housing available for people who may have additional needs. This housing is available for older people, people who have specific disabilities or people who need extra support.

Strategic Housing works with a number of partners in housing, Adult Social Care, health and the voluntary sector to enable the development of housing providing people with choice and independence.

  • Care Homes
  • Sheltered Housing
  • Supported Housing

Extra care housing

Extra care housing provides self-contained accommodation for older people who have care and support needs, with the care and support available 24 hours a day.

Unlike sheltered housing, the care and support is available on a more flexible basis to respond to your changing circumstances.

Stockport currently has extra care schemes in Edgeley, Reddish, Marple and Heald Green. These are managed by a partnership between Stockport Council, Registered Providers - housing associations and Stockport Homes - and a contracted care provider.

All the schemes consist of self-contained apartments and are available to rent (there are a limited number of shared ownership options available at Smithy Croft scheme in Heald Green).

Some of these apartments are adapted for people with disabilities or for wheelchair users. The extra care schemes benefit from a mix of able and less able older residents and they allow couples and friends to stay together.

Please note that the schemes often have waiting lists and eligibility criteria.

How to apply:

Extra care housing is available to all older people whether they own or rent their property following a social work assessment.

Please contact Libby Spencer, Extra Care Housing Co-ordinator on 0161 474 3706, libby.spencer@stockport.gov.uk or Adult Social Care Contact Centre on 0161 217 6029 for further details of extra care housing.

The Housing Learning and Improvement Network website provides useful case studies and good practice guidance around extra care housing.

The Elderly Accommodation Counsel (EAC) is also a good source of information about extra care.

There are also a number of Extra Care schemes available for outright sale on the private market operated by McCarthy & Stone (Dutton Court) and Gladman (Adlington House).

Residential care homes

Residential care is for people aged over 18 years old who are no longer able to remain living independently at home due to physical disabilities, sight or hearing loss, frailty or illness.

In Stockport residential homes are all independently owned and managed, so charges will vary. All registered care homes are regulated by the Care Quality Commission (CQC) and are given star ratings. You can check the star rating of a particular care home by visiting the CQC website .

How to access residential care:

You may contact the Adult Social Care Contact Centre on 0161 217 6029 to find out if you are eligible for financial support from the local authority for residential care.

You will be able to receive help from Stockport Council if you fall within the 'critical' or 'substantial' categories of the Fair Access to Care Criteria, as set out by the Department of Health. You can still receive information and advice on other services available to help you if you are not eligible for help.

If a residential home is found to be the best option, the Council can recommend which ones would best meet your needs and advise on any vacancies. They can help you find one in your chosen area of Stockport.

Alternatively you can search for your own care home on the My Care My Choice website.

Retirement and sheltered housing

There are a number of sheltered & retirement housing schemes in the borough available for rent, shared ownership and market sale. Some of these are owned and managed by our partner Registered Providers (formerly known as housing associations) and some by private housing providers.

To access sheltered housing for rent, please contact the Homechoice Team at Stockport Homes, who can register you with Homechoice and advise on vacancies in both Stockport Homes and selected Registered Provider properties throughout Stockport.

In addition, you can search through a database of properties from the HousingCare website, which is a sister site of Elderly Accommodation Counsel.

The Elderly Accommodation Counsel (EAC) provides useful advice on the legal and financial points to consider before making a purchase.

Which? Elderly Care is a source of free and independent information and advice on care choices for older people across the UK.

More information is also available on My Care My Choice website.

Supported housing

Sometimes people require extra support to live in the community. Supported housing is usually self-contained accommodation with on-site or visiting support.

Supported housing may include housing specifically developed for:

  • People with a learning and/or sensory disability
  • People with mental health issues
  • People with a physical  disability
  • People who are homeless
  • Older people (please see separate retirement housing and Extra care Housing web pages)

Typical models of supported housing include:

  • Supported tenancies - A range of accommodation which enables people to live independently, in a small group or alone, with an appropriate level of support.
  • Shared houses
  • Temporary accommodation for homeless people.
