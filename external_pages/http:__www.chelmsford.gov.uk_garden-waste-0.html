Garden waste

    Chelmsford Council provides a brown wheelie bin to residents for the recycling of garden waste.


    This is a fortnightly service and continues throughout the year. For details of your collection day please see the 'Find your collection day' link (right).


    No more than two brown bins will be emptied on each collection day.


    Please recycle

     

    • Grass and hedge cuttings
    • Leaves 
    • Small bracken and twigs
    • Shrub cuttings and small branches
    • Windfalls


    Contamination of the green waste can result in the whole lorry load being rejected and subsequently going to landfill rather than for composting. So please only put the material described above into your brown bin.


    Please keep out

     

    • Large stones, bricks, paving slabs and soil
    • Kitchen waste such as raw fruit and vegetable peelings (these can be placed in your food waste caddy)
    • Tea bags, egg shells, cardboard (these can be placed in your food waste caddy)
    • Animal waste or litter
    • Large branches and household timber with nails and screws inserted
    • Nappies
    • Plastic bags, waxed packaging and polythene
    • Toxic waste e.g. pesticides


    If in doubt - leave it out.


    For insurance purposes we are only able to empty wheelie bins which we have supplied.


    How to use your brown bin


    Placing a bed of twigs or dry bracken in the bottom of the bin before the grass cuttings will help the bin's contents to be discharged more easily.


    If you currently receive an Assisted Collection for your black bin, you will automatically receive one for your brown bin.


    Unfortunately we are unable to provide a bin cleaning service - details of these can be found in the yellow pages.
