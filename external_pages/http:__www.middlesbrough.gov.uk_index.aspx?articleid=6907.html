Housing benefit & council tax reduction scheme

Who is eligible for benefits and/or reduction, and how to apply

Housing benefit is a social security benefit, administered by the Council, for people on low income to help meet the cost of rent. The Council also administers council tax reduction (CTR) under the local council tax support scheme. Both are means tested so the amount of help you maybe entitled to will depend on the money you earn and the money you have saved, together with your household circumstances.

If you are in receipt of a state benefit such as income support or job seekers allowance, you must still make a claim for housing benefit and/or council tax reduction as it is not awarded automatically.

An overall limit is planned for working age households that includes housing benefit. You can read more about this on the Benefit cap page.

Whether your landlord is the council, a housing association, or a private individual or company - you can still claim. However, you cannot normally claim if you are living with a member of your family and paying them rent.

Middlesbrough Council has approved the Council Tax Support scheme for 2015/16 . Details of the new scheme from 1 April 2015 is available to download on the right hand side of this page.

Housing benefit

The amount of rent eligible when claiming this benefit is different, according to whether a property is rented from a private landlord and whether any of the following circumstances apply:

  • Renting from a registered social landlord
  • Living in a hostel
  • Living in board and lodge accommodation
  • Living in a 'protected property' (e.g. supported housing)
  • Excluded tenancies (pre 1989)
  • Exceptional cases (i.e. caravans, houseboats)

Benefit for tenants who are renting from a private landlord in circumstances not listed will reflect the appropriate Local Housing Allowance. More details are available in the .

To find out your household needs you can use the bedroom entitlement calculator.

Important note for tenants renting from a registered social landlord

Working age claimants of Housing Benefit living in accommodation provided by a registered social landlord or a housing association who have more bedrooms than their household needs will have their eligible rent reduced.

You can read more about this on the Under-occupancy page.

Council tax reduction

If you are liable to pay Council Tax you can apply for CTR. It is available to people on low incomes to reduce the amount of council tax payable. This includes people who work but are on a low wage.

For Council Tax payers of pensionable age, a second adult rebate based on the circumstances of a second adult may be claimed, irrespective of your own financial circumstances. The second adult cannot be a partner or paying you rent.

Middlesbrough Council has approved the Council Tax Support scheme for 2015/16 . Details of the new scheme from 1 April 2015 is available to download on the right hand side of this page.

How to claim

Benefit is usually paid from the Monday following the date the council receive the request to claim benefit. It is therefore important that you do not delay in making your claim to avoid the risk of losing entitlement. Any evidence needed to support your claim can be provided later.

You can make a new claim for Housing Benefit and Council /Tax Reduction by . Once you have made a claim you may need to report changes if your circumstances change. You can do this easily online by clicking here. You can call Middlesbrough House on 01642 726005, email housbens@middlesbrough.gov.uk or visit Middlesbrough House or one of the four Service Contact Points.

Please note Middlesbrough Council may share your information with, and receive information from, other government bodies and credit reference agencies to prevent and detect crime or protect public funds. If you want to know more about how Middlesbrough Council can use your information visit the Data protection page.
