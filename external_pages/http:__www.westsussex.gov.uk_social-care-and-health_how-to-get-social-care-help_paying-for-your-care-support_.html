Paying for your care support

Find out the different ways to pay for your non-residential support with a personal budget.

  1. Direct payments
  2. Council-managed budget

1 Direct payments

Direct payments are where we make payments directly to you or an appointed suitable person. This is someone who can act on your behalf, such as a family member.

If you choose this method, you can arrange and pay for your own support, instead of using the services we provide. 

The money is paid into a bank account that will need to be set up specifically for this purpose. 

You will need to give us bank statements for the direct payment account and you will be expected to keep a record of how you have spent the money.

Spending your direct payment

Direct payments can only be used to pay for support set out in your support plan. It cannot be used for other things, such as:

  • health care
  • general household bills or living costs
  • residential or nursing care home fees, unless it's for short periods of care.

If you have more than eight weeks of your personal budget allocation which you have not spent, you will be expected to return this to the County Council. We will work with you to understand the reasons why the money has not been spent.

2 Council-managed budget

A council-managed budget is where we manage your personal budget and arrange your support. 

This can be because:

  • you wish to use services provided by the County Council, as our services cannot be purchased directly
  • you do not wish to manage the money yourself
  • you are not able to consent to receive direct payments due to lack of mental capacity, and there isn't a 'suitable person' who can manage the personal budget on your behalf.
