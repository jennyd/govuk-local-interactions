Road and footway maintenance

The Council keeps roads and footways in a safe condition for highway users, and to withstand damage caused by wear and tear.

Bexley's roads and footways are being damaged in a number of ways.

These include: 

  • Wear and tear from high traffic levels on busy roads
  • Statutory undertakers works i.e. repairs to water, gas and electricity supplies
  • Wearing out through the effects of natural weathering
  • Damage to footways by parked cars
  • Theft

The Council is committed to carrying out highway maintenance works in order to meet three main objectives:

  • Keep the borough's roads and footways in a safe condition for highways users
  • Extend the service life of the road or footway
  • Enable the road or footway to withstand damage caused by wear and tear

In achieving these objectives the key principle is to ensure 'best value' from the available maintenance resources. This is achieved through a balanced maintenance programme, which aims to undertake the following activities at the optimum time.

To report any defects, including theft on the highway submit the online form or by contacting the Public Realm Management customer services team.

Major planned maintenance

Structural surveys are undertaken on all streets maintained by the London Borough of Bexley over a three year period. From the results of these surveys a shortlist of streets in the poorest overall structural condition is produced and a programme of works is put forward for inclusion in the Annual Major Works Programme. The programme is split into three types of work:

  • Major carriageway works - this involves resurfacing of the carriageway. The roads chosen for this years programme are the busier roads in the borough where conditions are deteriorating.
  • Major footway reconstruction works - this involves reconstructing the footway, usually replacing slabs that have suffered from vehicle damage with a composite construction (concrete foundation with a flexible surface) which is more durable and reduces the need for future maintenance works. Residents are advised about the change in material when advisory letters are sent out in advance of major works.
  • Patching works - where only sections of a road have deteriorated and fairly large-scale repairs are required. Areas of the footway are repaired using 'like for like' materials where there is less risk of future damage, or composite construction if there is a risk of future damage occurring.

Reactive maintenance

Regular inspections are carried out to ensure that the borough's roads and footways are maintained in a safe condition for highway users. In addition, defective areas reported by residents are also inspected. From these inspections, reactive type repairs are carried out in order of priority. These repairs include filling potholes, replacing damaged paving slabs etc. Numerous footway and carriageway emergency repairs are carried out by the borough emergency response operatives.

Cyclic maintenance

This is where a regular cycle of maintenance is undertaken, including cleaning of gullies, ditches, soakaways, replacement of lamps in street lights and illuminated signs etc. This ensures that systems and equipment are maintained in good working order.
