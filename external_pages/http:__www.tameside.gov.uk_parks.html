Parks in Tameside

Tameside Parks Moving Forward

This is an exciting time for Tameside's Parks with improvements being made across the Borough.

In 2008, Tameside was delighted to be awarded 10 Green Flag Awards. This year we secured an further Green Flag with Cheethams Park, Stalybridge now joining the extensive list of local parks and green spaces to be recognised under the national scheme.

This year's Green Flag winners are:

  • Victoria Park, Denton
  • Ryecroft Hall, Audenshaw
  • Haughton Green Playing Fields
  • Hyde Park
  • Oxford Park, Ashton-under-Lyne
  • Cedar Park, Ashton-under-Lyne
  • Park Bridge Country Park, Ashton-under-Lyne
  • Werneth Low Country Park, Hyde
  • Lymefield Country Park, Broadbottom
  • Denton Cemetery
  • Cheetham Park
  • Dukinfield Cemetery

Victoria Park has also been recognised by being awarded the Green Heritage Award in recognition of the special work to preserve and enhance the heritage of the park.

The Council also supported 4 community groups who received Green Pennant Awards for managing high quality green spaces.  The Green Pennant winners are:

  • Bailey's Backs, Brushes Estate, Stalybridge
  • Gorse Hall, Stalybridge
  • Bowling Green and Community Centre at Haughton Green Playing Fields
  • Tame Valley Community Green, Dukinfield

In Ashton, Cedar Park, Oxford Park, Waterloo Park and West End Park have seen improvement works over the past year.

Mossley Park and Dukinfield Park are currently having major improvement works carried out whilst Granada Park and Thornley Park in Denton and Floral Gardens in Droylsden have had improvement works carried out in the last 12 months.

Stamford Park is set for a major restoration based on proposals developed through extensive consultation and historical analysis of the park.  This project is funded through Heritage Lottery Fund and the Council.
