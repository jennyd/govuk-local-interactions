Appealing a housing benefit decision

If you want to apeal a housing benefit decision you can contact us to ask for an explanation regarding the decision.

  • We will explain how we made the decision and what information was used.
  • If you are not satisfied with the explanation, you can ask us to look at the decision again. You must let us know within one calendar month of the date of the decision letter.

Where to find help

  • Online form
  • Email: benefits@cheshirewestandchester.gov.uk
  • By post: Revenues and Benefits, PO Box 187, Ellesmere Port, Cheshire, CH34 9DB  
  • Telephone: 0300 123 7021

Ask us to look at the decision again

If you disagree with a decision we have made you have one calendar month to:

If the decision can be changed:

  • We will tell you in writing what our new decision is.
  • If you do not agree with this new decision, you can ask us to look at it again.

If the decision cannot be changed:

  • We will tell you in writing that we cannot change the decision and will advise if you can appeal against it.
  • If you appeal, the one calendar month time limit starts again from the date of the letter telling you that the decision cannot be changed.

Still not happy and want to appeal

If, after we have explained our decision, you still think it is wrong, you can appeal to an independent tribunal. You should send your written appeal to us, giving full reasons for your appeal.

If you appeal against a Housing benefit decision, we will submit your appeal to the Independent Tribunal Service.

The time limit for appeals is one calendar month from the date of the decision letter, or one calendar month from the date of the letter telling you that the decision cannot be changed.

Late appeals

If you appeal after one calendar month, you must say why it is late. Late appeals are only accepted in special circumstances, and cannot be accepted if made 12 months or more after the date of the decision letter. We can choose to extend the time limit, but only if there are special circumstances.

Appeal a Council Tax Reduction decision

You can get in touch with us by phone, in writing or calling into your local council customer service centre to ask for an explanation regarding your Council Tax Reduction decision.  This should be within one calendar month of the date of the original decision.

We will explain how we made the decision and what information was used.

Following the explanation, if you are still not happy, you can ask us to look at the decision again. This should be within one calendar month of the date of our reply.

However, if we do not respond to you within two months, or if you are still unhappy with our decision you may appeal to the Valuation Tribunal. Contact details relating to the Valuation Tribunal will be provided to you as part of this process or on request.
