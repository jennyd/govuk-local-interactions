What noise issues can we investigate? 

Excessive noise can be very distressing and badly affect people’s quality of life.  There are no set noise levels in law as to when a noise is unacceptable.  Each case is individually assessed.  If you find you are unable to resolve the noise problem, we may be able to help.

We can investigate the following types of noise nuisance if the noise causes you unreasonable interference with the use and enjoyment of your property.  (This is not a complete list.  There may be other activities that cause noise that we can advise on):

  • Loud music or television (on a regular basis/for long periods of time).

  • Intense prolonged noise e.g. all night party.

  • Barking dogs (frequent and regular barking).

  • Commercial premises e.g. factory and business units.

  • Licensed premises e.g. pubs and restaurants.

  • Construction noise including prolonged DIY at anti-social hours.

        The guidance times for noise related to construction activities are:

        8 am to 6 pm Monday to Friday

        8am to 1pm Saturday

        and no noisy work on Sundays or Bank Holidays.

  • Building/car alarms going off.

  • Noise from individual vehicles e.g. delivery lorries, car stereos.

  • Cockerels and other animals.

What noise issues are we unable to investigate?

Due to restrictions in the law we are unable to deal with the following:

  • Noise from aircraft (e.g. civil aircraft and Police helicopter). These complaints should be made direct to the operator concerned, e.g. Bournemouth Airport www.bournemouthairport.com.

  • Noise from road traffic.

  • Noise from naval, military or air force activities.

  • People shouting in the street (however, if they are associated with a licensed premises this may be resolved through their premises licence).

     

It is unlikely that we will be able to take legal action for

  • noise caused by children

  • noise from normal domestic activity due to poor sound insulation (e.g. footsteps, talking, door closing etc.), and

  • noise from emergency works by gas, electric and water companies

What can you do to address a noise issue? 

We all have to accept a certain level of manmade noise in our community. However, if you are experiencing noise at a level that you believe is unreasonable, there are a number of things you can do to try to improve the situation.

  • Try discussing the situation with the person/people causing the noise. This may resolve the problem as they may not be aware that what they are doing is causing a problem

  • Should talking with them not resolve the problem, or you feel unable to approach them, you could write a letter. Here is a Sample Letter on which you can base your own

  • If you have tried talking and/or writing to the person and this has not resolved the situation, mediation may be a useful option to consider

You can contact Mediation Dorset by calling 01305 257717.

  • If none of these options solves the problem we may be able to help as long as the cause of the noise is something that we can investigate

How will we assess a noise complaint?

If we agree to investigate your noise complaint it will need to be assessed to see whether any intervention is necessary.

It is essential for us to be able to discuss your complaint with you. This is to agree an approach to making an assessment. During the investigation the following will be taken into account:

  • The type of noise and the characteristics of your locality

  • What time it occurs

  • How often it occurs

  • How long it lasts

  • How loud it is

  • How it affects you

  • How many other people are affected.

If we think it may be necessary to intervene, the first thing we are likely to do is to try to resolve your complaint informally.  We do this by contacting the person making the noise to try to get them to reduce it to an acceptable level.

If this approach does not work further actions may be considered such as serving an abatement (warning) notice. If this also fails, then in certain situations it may be possible for us to take legal action.

What is your involvement if you make a complaint to us?

In the majority of cases we will need to assess the noise from within your property. This may be from the living room, bedroom or garden depending on where you are affected by the noise. It will be necessary for you to assist us in gathering the evidence needed for the assessment. This may include some or all of the following:

  • When noise occurs continuously for long periods (e.g. for a noisy commercial fan that operates all day/night) it is likely that we will arrange a time to visit you in order to assess the noise

  • When the noise occurs on a frequent basis for periods longer than half an hour (e.g. loud music or when a dog is barking) you will be asked to call us when the noise is happening so that we can visit

  • When the noise is intermittent and of short duration (e.g. loud banging), or the noise occurs outside our daytime working hours (e.g. delivery vehicles) it is likely that we will install one of our electronic recorders for you to use.

The Noise App

  • You may find it convenient to use The Noise App to record the noise nuisance.  The Noise App is available free of change on Android and iOS and will allow you to make an audio recording of  the noise disturbance, enter details, add a comment, and  email it to us for analysis. The audio recordings you make with the App, together with the details you record about the nature of the disturbance, make up an electronic noise diary.

  • Additionally you may be asked to keep a diary of when the noise occurs to show how regular the noise is, for how long it lasts and the impact it has on you

You will need to provide your contact details so that we can discuss your complaint with you and explain how we will investigate it. Initially your complaint will be treated as confidential. However, you may be needed later as a witness should legal action be taken. If this is the case we will discuss this with you before proceeding.

If you wish to report a noise complaint anonymously you may not see any change in the situation.  This is because we will be unable to undertake a full assessment, or provide you with feedback on any decisions or actions taken.

How do you contact us?

There are a number of ways to contact us to discuss a noise complaint. If the noise is happening at the time the best way is to call us on the appropriate number below:

During Office Hours           Tel: 01202 261700 

            8:30-17:15 Mon-Thurs, and

            8:30-16:45 Fri

Outside Office Hours         Tel: 0800 506050

An officer should be available between

           20:00-02:00 Mon-Sat, and

             9:00-17:00 Sat-Sun

(this includes Bank Holidays except Christmas Day, Boxing Day and New Years Eve).

Should you wish to receive a call back to discuss your complaint at another time please use our Report It on-line form. (Please be aware that there will be a delay as on-line reports are not checked outside of daytime office hours).

What reasonable steps can you take to prevent noise you make from annoying your neighbours?

The best way is to talk to your neighbours and tell them if you are planning on doing something out of the ordinary that will cause noise, e.g. a party, buildings works, car maintenance etc. It is a good idea to give them a rough timescale of how long you will be making the noise for and ask them whether there are any specific times to avoid.  Further information is available in our guidance sheet Noise Nuisance Additional Information.
