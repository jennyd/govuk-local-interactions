Older People

Older People

How can we help you?

We are committed to supporting our older residents across the District. We want to:  

  • help you to have your say
  • provide support for you to stay in your home for longer
  • improve availability of information and advice.
  • empower you
  • support your well being.

We recognise that some people are unable to take their own decisions and care for themselves. We also want to support your right to lead a life based on self determination and personal choice.  

How do we do this?

The Council works in partnership with a number of statutory and voluntary organisations. Here below are some of projects and activities that we support:

Supporting Horsham District Older People's Forum                                                    

The Horsham District Older Peoples Forum was formerly known as the Horsham District Senior Persons Council and changed its name in December 2014.

They have 4 public meetings a year, at differing locations around the district and on different days of the week. Anyone aged 60 or above who resides in the Horsham District is welcome to attend.

They would also welcome representatives of any local group, charity or organisation that include older people to become involved with HDOPF and attend our meetings. If you would like to attend and / or have your groups' links on this website or have information to disseminate please contact me.

The HDOPF is a non-political, voluntary body that acts as the 'ears and voice' for issues of those aged 60 and over who reside in the Horsham District Council area. It also exists to be a consultative group for anyone seeking the views of older people in the district on particular topics or schemes such as from Horsham District Council (HDC) and West Sussex County Council (WSCC).

 More information can be found on the HDOPF website here, or on their Facebook page here.

Age With Confidence

The West Sussex Cooperative has launched 'Age with Confidence' to help ensure that residents, families and communities are confident in preparing for and living well in old age. Early intervention is fundamental to this approach.

Emergency Alarms for the Elderly and Vulnerable

Through the Community Link service we provide a telephone-based personal alarm system (on a rental basis) for anyone in their own home. The alarm provides added security, peace of mind and reduces feelings of isolation. Click here to visit their website.

Horsham Museum Reminiscence service

Horsham Museum runs a service which loans out themed boxes containing different artefacts to care homes, nursing and residential homes. The boxes help to encourage discussion and conversation between residents. Click here to visit the Horsham Museum website.

Activities for the Over 50's

We offer a wide variety of sports for older people across the District which can be booked through our Leisure Link team. These include:

  • 50+ multi-sport clubs such as Young at Heart that takes place at The Holbrook Club, Horsham on Monday mornings
  • short mat bowls
  • yoga sessions
  • health walks
  • Nordic walking programmes via delivery partners.

We Leisure Link team also offers the hire of low intensity sports equipment to residential care homes and sheltered housing units. These can be hired with or without a staff member to deliver a sesstion, and include:

  • New Age Kurling
  • Table Based, and Floor Based Skittles
  • Boccia, and Target Boccia
  • Scholen

Older Persons Directory

This directory contains a list of facilities and services available to older people in the Horsham District. The Directory is an important tool for providing older people with vital information that supports them to lead healthy, active and independent lives.

Shopmobility

Horsham Shopmobility provides a selection of manual and powered wheelchairs along with motorised scooters to anyone, young or old, who needs help walking around Horsham town centre. We also offer a Highway Code for Mobility Scooter Users Guide.

Working in Partnership

We work closely with the Horsham District Community Partnership and the Horsham District Older Person's Forum in a multi-agency group that aims to promote the interests of older people and improve their local facilities and services.

Need more information?

For more information on any of the initiatives please contact call 01403 215642 or email community.development@horsham.gov.uk
