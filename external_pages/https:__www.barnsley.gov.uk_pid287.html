Personal budgets

If you qualify for some help from us to arrange and pay for your care, you will receive an amount of money known as a 'personal budget'.

The idea of a personal budget is to give you more choice and control over how your care and support needs are met.   You can either manage it yourself through a 'direct payment' or it can be managed by someone else in line with your wishes, such as a relative or friend.  In some cases we can manage the budget for you, and arrange your services on your behalf.   We'll tell you more about the options for managing your personal budget at your assessment. 

How do I get a personal budget?

To have a personal budget you will need an adult social services assessment .  This is to find out if you qualify for some help from us to arrange and pay for your care.

See our short film about assessments.

 Your assessment is to:

  • identify your needs
  • decide if you qualify for some help from us
  • find out about how much you will need to contribute towards the cost of your care
  • discuss your personal budget amount and how you are going to manage it
