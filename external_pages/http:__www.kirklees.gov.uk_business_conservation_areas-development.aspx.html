Development in conservation areas

Planning (planning.contactcentre@kirklees.gov.uk) Last updated - May 2011

What a conservation area is

A conservation area designation enables local planning authorities to protect the character and appearance of an area of special architectural and historic interest.

  • About conservation areas

Conservation areas have higher design standards for new developments, and stricter controls over demolition (either in full or part) and works to trees, together with some alterations and advertisements. These controls are not intended as a hindrance to change, but as safeguards to the character of an area as a whole.

About development and conservation consent

If your development is to take place within a conservation area you may require conservation area consent if demolition is involved, even if planning permission is not required.

When you need conservation area consent

  • Conservation area consent is only required for demolition works.
  • In considering these, account will be taken of the building's contribution to the architectural or historical interest of the area in which it is sited.
  • The general presumption is in favour of retaining buildings that make a positive contribution to the character or appearance of a conservation area.
  • If consent is granted for demolition the council may impose a condition to make sure that the demolition does not take placed until a contract for redevelopment work (which should have planning permission) has been entered into.

Information you need to supply for conservation area consent

All applications

You should supply accurate drawings of the proposed works, ideally at a scale of 1:50. These drawings should show the position and size of buildings nearby and should ideally be supplemented with good quality photographs.

You should check whether planning permission is required:

  • Do you need planning permission?

Applications for demolition

All applications for demolition must include a justification statement to demonstrate that the tests set out in Government guidance (Planning Policy Statement 5) can be met.

  • Planning Policy Statement 5: Planning for the Historic Environment (external site)

The demolition of buildings in conservation areas will be strongly resisted unless it can be demonstrated that:

  • the listed building is beyond realistic repair
  • every possible effort has been made to continue its present use or to find an alternative use, including offering the building for sale for a reasonable period of time at a realistic price reflecting the building's condition.

When you should apply for conservation area consent

You should make you application for conservation area consent at the same time as your planning application.

  • Planning Portal: Make a new Listed Building / Conservation area appeal
  • How to complete your Listed Building / Conservation area appeal form

Development that affects trees in conservation areas

If your proposals affect trees within conservation areas you must give the council six weeks written notice before commencing work.

  • Trees in conservation areas
  • Making a tree work application

Advertisements in conservation areas

All outdoor advertisements affect the appearance of the building or the neighbourhood where they are displayed and consequently all applications relating to advertisements will require a high standard of design appropriate to the building and location.

New developments in conservation areas

Design is expected to be of a high standard to ensure that your proposals both preserve and enhance the character and appearance of the conservation area.

Conservation and design contacts

We operate an appointments system. To make an appointment please contact Kirklees Direct on 01484 414746 or email dc.admin@kirklees.gov.uk.
