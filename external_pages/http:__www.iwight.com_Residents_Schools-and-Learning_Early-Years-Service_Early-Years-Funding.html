Early Years Funding

Early Years - Free Early Education and Childcare Funding

Free Early Years Flexible Entitlement (FFE) Funding

Early year’s education takes place in pre-schools, day nurseries and with accredited child minders.  All children are entitled to receive free education from the term after their 3rd birthday up until compulsory school age.  The scheme is a Government funded initiative to support development and learning for young children.

Please see below and check our FAQ's for information about Early Educational Funding which is provided by the Isle of Wight Council.

The funding offers a free place with childcare provider’s allowing all children between the age of 3 and 4 to access good quality early year’s care and education.  All 3 and 4 year olds are currently entitled to 15 hours of funded Early Years’ Education per week. From September 2014, this will be extended to around 40% of two year olds nationally. 

Free Early Education - Funding for 2 Year Olds

From September 2014 the law is changing to allow more two year olds to benefit from free childcare than ever before.

From September 2013, local authorities have a statutory duty to extend their nursery education scheme to eligible 2 year olds living on the Isle of Wight. Two Year Old Funding is not available for all two year old children.  Please note: Families must meet strict criteria to be eligible.  The Department for Education (DfE) stipulates that the funding is for children whose families are on a low income and meet the criteria for free school meals.

Criteria for a 2 Year Old to be eligible for a Funded Place in September 2014

From September 2014, the Government is extending the current scheme to offer Free Early Education to 40% of 2 year olds from the funding period after their second birthday to families who meet the Government’s criteria for Free School Meals.  For a child to be eligible a parent/carer must have an income below £16,190 and be claiming the one of the following state benefits.  The law has changed and criteria have been extended to allow more two year olds to benefit from free childcare than ever before.

Parents who are in receipt of one of the following:

  • Income Support - for more information please click here.

  • Income based Job Seekers’ Allowance - for more information please click here.

  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) - for more information please click here.

  • Support under Part V1 of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999 - for more information please click here.

  • Guaranteed Element of the State Pension Credit - for more information please click here.    

  • Child Tax Credit, provided you have an annual gross income of no more than £16,190, assessed by Her Majesty’s Revenue & Customs. For more information please click here.

Or if the two year old has a current statement of Special Educational Needs (SEN) or an Education, Health and Care plan:

  • A form counter-signed by the relevant professional involved with the child is required for children who meet the following criteria.

  • Has left local authority care through a Special Guardianship Order, adoption or a Residence Order.

  • Is currently a Looked After Child, for example in foster care.
  • Is in receipt of Disability Living Allowance (DLA).

How to Apply for a Funded Place

If your child is three or four years of age your chosen childcare provider will claim on your behalf.  Parents and carers need to complete a funding form if claiming for a two year old.  If your child is two years old and you believe you may be eligible to claim for funding please click here to download an application form.

If an application is being made on the basis of tax credit or DLA you will need to provide proof that you receive these benefits. Applicants should send in a copy of the relevant document (this is specified on the form).  If you need help completing your form visit your local children centre or the Family Information Zone (next to Newport Bus station) who will be able to help you.

Complete the application form which should be sent to the address printed on the form.  Check out our FAQ's for further information.

Government Policy

As part of the government policy of improving the quality and range of education and childcare from birth to 5 years, the Department for Education have created the Funded Early Education Choice Framework in May 2013; to download a copy of the framework please click here (PDF, 52.47KB, 2 pages).  This document explains how a child can take up a funded early education place, and what information is available to parents.
