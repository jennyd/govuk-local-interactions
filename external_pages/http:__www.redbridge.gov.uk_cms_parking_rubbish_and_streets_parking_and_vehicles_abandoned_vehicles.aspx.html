Abandoned and Untaxed Vehicles

Do it online

Report an abandoned vehicle online 

Due to government changes on the 1st October, vehicles are no longer a required to display a tax disc. Therefore a vehicle not displaying a tax disc, is not necessarily abandoned.

Please see the government information on vehicle tax changes

The following list can help you to help determine whether a vehicle is abandoned, however vehicles that are reported to us are always investigated.  

  • Has the vehicle been parked without moving for two weeks or more?
  • Are there any flat tyres or removed wheels?
  • Are the windscreen or any of the windows broken?
  • Is the vehicle mouldy or rusty? 
  • Is there waste inside the vehicle?

Vehicles aren't classed as abandoned

  • if your neighbour has gone on holiday (or in dispute with them)
  • if parked across your driveway (this is known as an 'obstruction')

If you're unsure, please give our Customer Contact Centre a call.

Report an abandoned vehicle

  • Exact location
  • Registration number
  • Make
  • Colour
  • Brief description of the state of the vehicle

If you have found an abandoned vehicle you can report it online 

How we deal with abandoned vehicles

All abandoned vehicle reports received by the council will be investigated. If the vehicle is believed to be abandoned, a notice maybe placed on the vehicle and attempts will be made to contact the last registered keeper.

If the vehicle remains unmoved after the notice has expired and no owner comes forward, the abandoned vehicle officer will visit. If the vehicle is deemed to be abandoned by an officer it will be removed and stored for 28 days. If the vehicle remains unclaimed after this period, it will be destroyed.  Can I be fined for abandoning my vehicle?

Yes and you may also get a criminal record.

Can the Council remove a vehicle from private land?

We can investigate these vehicles however we must have permission from the land owner as we will have to go on to the property to investigate it.  If need be we will contact the land owner.

Untaxed vehicles

Every vehicle registered in the United Kingdom must be taxed if used or kept on a public road. If the vehicle is kept off-road it must either be taxed or have a SORN (Statutory Off Road Notification) in force. If not it could be wheel-clamped or removed without notice by either Redbridge  Council or the Driving and Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA).

How to claim your vehicle

If your vehicle has been removed by Redbridge Council, contact:

Redcorn Ltd, Denver Industrial Estate, Ferry Lane North, Rainham, Essex, RM13 9DD

Telephone 01708 526760

You will need proof ownership.
