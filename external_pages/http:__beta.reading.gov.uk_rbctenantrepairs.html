Repairs, Maintenance & Home Improvements

The maintenance of Council properties is very important to us, but we are only responsible for some repairs. We need to work together to ensure you have a pleasant home to live in.

Questions

Close allOpen all

How do I book a repair on my Council home?

Use our online repair booking form and we will call you back to confirm details and book your appointment.

You can call us on 0800 318296 (freephone) and describe the exact nature of your repairs. We will then send the right tradesman out to do the repair.

If it is an emergency call 0118 937 3737 and we will arrange for the repair to be carried out.

For further details please refer to our  factsheet explaining how to report a repair. [275kb]

How do I report an issue in a communal area?

If you want to report an issue with a communal area near your flats, you can request a repair or contact our team using the details on the side of the page.

What repairs and maintenance am I responsible for and what are you responsible for?

We are responsible for carrying out essential repairs to:

  • The structure and exterior of the building (for example, roofs, walls, floors and window frames)
  • Kitchen and bathroom fixtures (for example, basins, sinks, toilets and baths)
  • Electrical wiring, gas and water pipes and water heating equipment
  • Any communal areas around your home.

If something you have done - or not done - has caused us to have to carry out the repair we will charge you for it.

You are responsible for:

  • Paying for any repairs that we have to carry out on your behalf which are your responsibility
  • Paying for any repairs that we are responsible for carrying out but have been caused by someone accidentally, deliberately or through neglect 
  • Carrying out small repairs caused by you, someone living with you, or someone visiting you whether caused accidentally, deliberately or through neglect
  • Keeping everything clean and tidy - this includes your home, garden, shed  and garages
  • Painting and decorating inside your home
  • Carrying out minor repairs (for example, lock changes, gain entry, internal doors and unblocking of sanitary items)
  • Looking after your garden (for example, ensuring grass is cut, and bushes and trees are maintained at a reasonable level)
  • Repairing any damage caused by you, someone living with you, or someone visiting you, whether caused accidentally, deliberately or through neglect.

For more information please refer to our  looking after your home factsheet. [244kb]

If you are struggling to carry out repairs due to disability or age we may be able to help. Contact Tenant Services on 0118 937 2761 to discuss what help we may be able to give you.

My Council home is damp, what can I do about it?

If your home is damp, mouldy, or has excess condensation, it can cause health problems. We may have to carry out some work to sort out some problems or you may have to make changes to the way you heat and live in the property to sort out others.

Penetrating damp

This is caused by water seeping into the property usually from heavy rain, leaking gutters, or overflows running. If you think you have penetrating damp contact the Repairs Team.

Rising damp

This is caused by moisture in the ground being drawn up  the walls of the property. This is because of a defective or missing damp proof course. This will only affect walls up to a height of 1m above the ground floor or external ground levels. If you're in a first floor flat or above it won't be rising damp. If you think you have rising damp contact the Repairs Team.

Condensation

This occurs when warm moist air comes into contact with a cooler surface, like a wall, WC cistern or a window. You can reduce the condensation in your home by trying to reduce the amount of water in the air, letting any moist air out and keeping your home warm.

You can do this by drying clothes outside, keeping lids on pans and venting your tumble dryer outside.

A well-insulated house is easier to heat and less likely to have condensation problems. It is important to heat and ventilate your home.

Allow air to circulate to reduce the build-up of condensation and mould. Leave a gap between any outside walls and furniture if you have a damp problem.

In almost all cases if you have black mould present you probably have a condensation problem.

We have produced a booklet on  reducing condensation in your home [218kb] to help.

How often do you carry out gas safety checks on your properties?

We have to carry out an annual gas safety check. The check itself takes less than an hour. We check for leaks in the supply and ensure all the gas appliances in your home are working safely.

We will write to let you know when the check is due and you can arrange the visit for a time that is convenient for you. We must do the inspection within one year of the last visit.

As part of your tenancy agreement, you agree to give us access to carry out the inspection. The inspections and any servicing are free.

If you refuse to let us carry out the annual safety check we will either force entry and charge you for any damages or apply to repossess the property.

Can I make alterations to my Council home?

If you are an introductory tenant, you must not carry out any alterations or improvements to the property (except painting and decorating).

If you are a secure tenant you must get written permission before doing any work in or outside your home. For full details you can refer to parts 3.2.8-3.2.13 in your  tenancy agreement. [125kb] Contact your Housing Officer and give them as much detail about the proposed work as you can. They will decide whether to give you written permission or not.

Do you have a planned programme of improvements and maintenance for your housing stock?

To keep our housing stock in good repair we carry out a rolling programme of maintenance and improvement work.

This includes:

  • Central heating
  • Boiler replacements
  • Rewiring
  • New windows
  • New doors
  • New kitchens
  • New bathrooms
  • Planned maintenance every seven years.

We aim to give you several weeks' notice of any maintenance planned for your home. We will write and tell you what the work involves and the planned dates.

If there is a choice in styles or colour we will give you a chance to select which you want.

For some planned work we may require you to move out of your home temporarily. If we ask you to do this and you have no family or friends you can stay with, we will arrange temporary accommodation for you and help with moving costs.

What is Right to Repair?

Secure Council tenants are entitled to have certain repairs carried out within a specified amount of time.

If we cannot complete the work during the first visit we will make it safe and carry out temporary repairs. We will then arrange an appointment to complete the work.

If our contractor does not complete the work within the set time frame you have the right to ask us to use a different contractor. If the second contractor doesn't complete the work on time you can claim compensation.

These are the given time scales and qualifying repairs:

Within four hours

  • Insecure property
  • Gaining emergency access to property
  • Dangerous flooring or stairs
  • Uncontrollable water leaks
  • Unusable taps in the 'on' position
  • Blocked toilets or soil and vent pipes (if only one of these is in the property)
  • Blocked drains (if backing up)
  • Missing inspection chamber covers
  • Electrics sparking or smoking, or bare wires
  • Blocked chimney to open fire
  • Making safe any dangerous structure, including glazing.

Within one day

  • Total loss of electricity
  • Unsafe light or electrical fitting
  • Total loss of water supply
  • Total or partial loss of gas supply
  • Blocked flue to an open fire or boiler
  • Total or partial loss of heating or hot water (1 November to 30 April)
  • Blocked or leaking drain pipe for a toilet or water pipe
  • Toilet not flushing (if only toilet)
  • Leaking water-heating pipe, tank or cistern
  • An outside window, door or lock that is not secure.

Within three days

  • Partial loss of electricity
  • Partial loss of water supply
  • Total or partial loss of heating or hot water (1 May to 31 October)
  • A blocked sink, bath or basin
  • A tap that will not turn
  • A loose or detached banister or handrail
  • Rotten wood flooring or steps on the stairs.

Within seven days

  • A leaking roof
  • A broken door-entry phone
  • A broken mechanical extractor fan in the kitchen or bathroom.

Within 20 days

  • All other repairs that are the responsibility of the Council
  • Repairs to frames and doors
  • Repairs to worktops and sink tops
  • Repairs to window frames.

What should I do if I think there is asbestos in my home?

Asbestos is safe if you do not disturb it and it does not look like it is damaged, flaking or producing dust.

Do not try to remove the asbestos yourself.

You might find asbestos in:

  • Garage or shed roofs and wall panels
  • Drain pipes
  • Insulation boards soffit
  • Bath side panels.

Contact the Repairs Team on 0800 318296 (freephone) if you think you have unsafe asbestos in your home.

We will send a surveyor out to inspect the material and take a sample. If there is a problem we will arrange for the damage to be repaired. If that isn't possible we will arrange for the asbestos to be removed and disposed of safely.

Why have I been charged for a repair?

If we have carried out work that is your responsibility or the work needed was caused accidentally, deliberately or as a result of abuse or neglect we will have to recharge our costs to you. 

You can find out more about these costs and why you have to pay them in our  costs you must pay factsheet [207kb].
