Disabled facilities grants

Disabled Facilities Grants (DFGs) are mandatory grants administered through the local housing authority (the council). Grants are means tested and are therefore targeted at people on low incomes.  However, some applicants in receipt of certain benefits may be awarded 100% funding. There is no means test for disabled children under 19 years and, in all cases, necessary adaptations can be funded up to the grant maximum of £30,000. 

As part of the initial assessment to see if you are suitability for a disabled facilities grant, you must complete a 'Test of Resources' form outlining your financial circumstances. If it is decided that you must make a contribution towards the cost of the work you will need to consider how you will pay for this - this may be through savings, additional mortgage or charitable funding.  More information is available in the DFG fundraising factsheet [pdf].

If you're unable to access funds from a reputable lender, savings, family or charitable organisations to fund your contribution towards the cost of essential work required as part of the grant, the council may be able to assist homeowners with a low cost loan or adaptations loan. Both types of loan are secured on the property by means of a charge at the Land Registry.

Low cost loan 

This is a loan of up to £5,000, repayable over a period of up to 60 months, assuming that the applicant has been assessed as being able to afford the repayments. The loan charge is equivalent to 5% of the amount that is borrowed. In addition, the council charges an administration fee (currently £100) to set up the loan.  This fee goes towards the costs incurred by the council in providing the low cost loan [pdf], such as Land Registry and bankruptcy searches, obtaining proof of identity to satisfy money laundering regulations, registering the charge at the Land Registry and the loan agent fees.

Adaptations loan

Where the amount required by the applicant exceeds £5,000, the council may offer an adaptations loan [pdf] to homeowners. This is an equity share loan, which means that the council takes a share of the ownership of the property in return for lending the applicant the money needed for their contribution towards the cost of essential works. A property valuation report prepared by an independent chartered surveyor will be required, for which a fee will be payable. The loan is repayable on sale or transfer of the lease or on request by the property’s owner. This would require a property valuation undertaken by the council’s surveyor. The amount repayable would depend on the % increase (or decrease) in the value of the property.

Relocation loan

If a home is unsuitable for the adaptations needed to support a disabled person, the council may consider offering a relocation loan, in certain circumstances, to assist the disabled person to move to a more suitable property within the borough which may or may not need to be adapted.  This option is only available to owner-occupiers as the council would need to place a charge on the property at the Land Registry to protect its loan. 

Further information is available from the council through your occupational therapist or from the private sector housing team.
