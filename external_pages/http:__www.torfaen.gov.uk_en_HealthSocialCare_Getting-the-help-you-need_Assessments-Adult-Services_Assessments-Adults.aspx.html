Assessments - Adult Services

Torfaen encourages fair and equal access to social care services for those who really need them.

Community care services are available to people over 18 with a wide range of needs.

Once you have contacted us, we will arrange for a member of our staff (a care manager) to visit you to assess your needs.

This care manager might be a social worker, a community care support worker, a nurse, an occupational therapist or a physiotherapist.

Your assessment may take place in your own home or in hospital before you are discharged, or perhaps in another community setting. You can involve your family/a carer if you wish, or ask someone to speak on your behalf.

We will work with you to find out what you are able to do and what you experience difficulty with. For example, we will see how you manage with everyday activities like washing and dressing, preparing hot drinks and meals and getting around.

We might also want to speak to other people, like your doctor, if you are happy for us to do so.

Throughout the assessment process, it is important that you share your views and wishes with us so that everyone involved in your assessment can work together effectively to achieve a positive outcome for you.

Once this assessment has been completed, the social worker will discuss with you how best to meet your needs.

We use specific criteria to establish whether people are eligible to be considered for services and to ensure that those with the greatest needs are given the highest priority. For more information, see our leaflet Eligibility Criteria - Getting the services you need .

There is no charge for an assessment of needs, however if as a result of the assessment certain services are offered, you may have to contribute or pay for these.

More information about our charging policy is available in the leaflet Paying for home (domiciliary) and day care services .

People who provide regular, informal, unpaid care for another person in their own home also have a right to a carer’s assessment .

For more information, or to make a referral to social care services, telephone 01495 762200.
