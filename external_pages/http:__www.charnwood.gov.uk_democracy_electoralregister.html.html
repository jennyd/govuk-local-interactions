Electoral Register

Top tasks:

  • Open Electoral Register: Opt Out or Back In
  • Register to Vote

Registering to vote means your name goes on the electoral register.  The register is a public document, mainly used as a list of voters for elections. but it is also used in other circumstances such as crime detection, for jury service and by organisations checking people’s addresses when doing credit checks.

Why It’s Important to Register

  • To exercise your democratic right to vote in any public election
  • You may not be able to open a bank account, have a mobile phone contract or get credit. 

If you are not on the electoral register, register to vote online now 

There are two versions of the electoral register, the full register, and the open register

The Full Register

The main use of the full register is to show who can vote in elections and referendums.  Credit reference agencies can use it, but only to check your name and address if you are applying for credit or other purposes specified in law.

The Open Register

The open register is an extract of the electoral register, not used for election purposes.

When you go on the electoral register you automatically go on the ‘open’ register. This version of the register can be bought by anyone who asks for it including businesses and charities who wish to use it for marketing and commercial reasons.

You can ask to be removed from the open register. You’ll stay on the full electoral register, and your right to vote is the same. If you opt out of the open register, you can opt back in again at any time.

How to Opt Out of the Open Register

If you do not want your information included in the Open Register you can do one of the following:

  • Opt out of the Open Register online.
  • Telephone 01509 634546

Frequently Asked Questions (12)

Electoral Registration: What if I Move House or Change My Name?

If you move house you will need to re-register.

If you change your name, please contact us:

  • By email at electoral.services@charnwood.gov.uk
  • Telephone: (01509) 634546
  • Electoral Services, Council Offices, Southfield Road, Loughborough, LE11 2TX

Electoral Registration: What Is Individual Electoral Registration (Ier)?

IER is a new national system for registering to vote. As from 10th June 2014 people registering to vote need to do so individually, rather than by household as before. 

Electoral Registration: Is My Personal Information Safe?

Your information is subject to Data Protection rules, and the online registration service is operated by the government and follows strict data security requirements.

Electoral Registration: Why Do I Need to Give More Information Than Before?

The new system includes checks to confirm that people are who they say they are to reduce the risk of voting fraud.

Electoral Registration: How Do I Register to Vote?

The quickest and easiest way is to register online at www.gov.uk/register-to-vote

Alternatively, you can register:

  • By telephone (01509) 634546
  • By post: Electoral Services, Council Offices, Southfield Road, Loughborough, LE11 2TX

Electoral Registration: What Information Do I Need to Give?

To register you will need to give your:

  • Name
  • Address (and previous address if applicable)
  • Nationality
  • Date of birth
  • National insurance number

Electoral Registration: Further Information or Questions?

Please contact us:

  • By email at electoral.services@charnwood.gov.uk
  • Telephone: (01509) 634613
  • Electoral Services, Council Offices, Southfield Road, Loughborough, LE11 2TX

Electoral Registration: How Do I Get My Name Removed From the Open Register?

The Open Register is the version of the Electoral Register which can be purchased by the general public. To opt out of the Open Register use our online form or telephone 01509 634546.

Opting out of this version of the register will not affect you right to vote.

For more information about the Electoral Register go to: https://www.charnwood.gov.uk/pages/electoralregister

Electoral Registration: I’m a Student Living Away During Term-Time, Where Do I Register?

Students may be entitled to register at both their home address and their university or college address, but can only vote once in any single election.

If in doubt, please contact us for advice:

  • By email at electoral.services@charnwood.gov.uk
  • Telephone: (01509) 634613

Electoral Registration: Can My Details Be Kept Private on the Electoral Register?

Every person registering is asked to choose whether to have their details excluded from the ‘open register’, which can be purchased by mail order companies etc. Not being on the open register won’t affect your voting rights or credit status.

Electoral Registration: What if I Have a Postal Vote or a Proxy Vote (Where Someone Else Votes for You) Already Set Up, or if I Want to Arrange for One?

If you already have an ongoing postal vote or proxy vote set up, then this will automatically transfer when you move across to the new system.

If you want to arrange for a postal vote or a proxy vote for the first time, then please go to our where & how you can vote page for a form and more details.

Electoral Registration: Will There Still Be an Annual Check on Voters’ Details Like Before?

Yes. Each year from summer 2015 a household enquiry form will be sent out to all addresses in the Borough asking if people have moved away or if there are new people who need to register. Any new voters will then be invited to register.
