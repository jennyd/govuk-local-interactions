reporting an abandoned vehicle

Listen

Broadland District Council has a duty to remove abandoned vehicles within the district under section two of the Refuse Disposal (Amenity) Act 1978. Vehicles can be removed from both public and private land.

A vehicle is only removed once a Council officer has carried out a number of checks and concludes that it has been abandoned without lawful authority.

Please consider...

If a vehicle is parked dangerously or is obstructing the highway, please report it to Norfolk Police by calling 101 or via email: enquiries@norfolk.pnn.police.uk

You can also visit the Norfolk Police website.

If a vehicle is parked in contravention of on-street parking restrictions, including yellow lines and time-limited spaces, please report it by calling 01553 616222 or email: parking.operations@west-norfolk.gov.uk.

If you suspect the vehicle is abandoned

  • Click here to check the vehicles current Tax and MOT status via the DVLA's vehicle enquiry website.
  • Report untaxed vehicles to the DVLA

If the vehicle is not taxed and has also been stationary for more than 21 days, it may be abandoned and should be reported to Broadland District Council to investigate.

Other indications that may show a vehicle has been abandoned are:

  • It is significantly damaged or un-roadworthy
  • It has weeds/vegetation growing around the vehicle, indicating that it has not moved for some time
  • It has mould on either the inside or outside of the vehicle
  • It is lacking number plates
  • It contains waste

further information

If you are confident that the vehicle is abandoned

Please report it to the Environmental Contracts Team by calling 01603 430534 or emailing environ.protection@broadland.gov.uk. To assist our investigation please try and give as many details as possible, registration number, make, model, colour, exact location.
