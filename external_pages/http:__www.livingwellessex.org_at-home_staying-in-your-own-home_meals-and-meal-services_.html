Meals and meal services

If you're experiencing health problems and are struggling to get out to the shops or prepare healthy meals for yourself at home, there will be ways in which you can overcome these difficulties.

On this page we have provided some suggestions on how you can continue to have a healthy, balanced diet, and to enjoy your food, even if you find it hard to get out and about, or are no longer able to prepare a meal for yourself.

If you are also struggling with other day to day tasks, and feel that you may need some support, you may want to contact the council for an assessment. The assessment will look at how you are managing at home, and help decide whether you need support from your local authority. To request an assessment, contact Essex County Council Adult Social Care on 0345 603 7630. You can read more about the assessment process on our page Requesting an Assessment. You may also find the below pages useful:

  • Regaining your independence 
  • Help with walking and mobility 
  • Equipment to help at home 

Equipment in your kitchen

There are a whole range of gadgets and devices which have been specially designed to allow people with disabilities and health problems to be more independent in the kitchen. You may find that these can make all the difference in allowing you to prepare food for yourself rather than relying on others to do it for you. 

Go to our Equipment to help at home page to see what's available.

Ready meals

If you're finding it difficult to prepare a hot main meal for yourself, and you're able to use an oven or microwave, you could consider buying ready meals.

These pre-prepared meals simply need cooking or heating up in the cooker or microwave. They come chilled or frozen and you can buy them from any supermarket, online or even over the phone (see below).

Ready meals cater for people with all dietary needs. These can include diabetic, low fat, low salt, low sugar, gluten free, vegetarian, and egg-free meals.

Although ready meals can be very handy, you should always read the label to make sure you're maintaining a healthy, balanced diet.

You could order ready meals online from supermarkets who have a home delivery service:

  • Waitrose
  • Marks and Spencer
  • Sainsburys
  • Tesco
  • Asda
  • Morrisons

Or you could order frozen meals from a dedicated frozen meals supplier who will deliver them to your door and could even place your meals in your freezer ready for you to cook.

The following are just some of the companies found online who can deliver batches of frozen meals to your door:

  • Wiltshire Farm Foods can deliver batches of frozen meals to your home on a regular basis, and provide a wide variety of options for people with different tastes or dietary needs
  • Oakhouse Foods provide a range of home-delivered frozen meals across the UK
  • Appetito deliver hot daily meals, or frozen meals in batches, to your door

All these companies offer special diets for people with food allergies and intolerances.

Problems getting to the shops?

If you're having trouble getting out and about, there are ways to ensure that you get the food and other shopping you need.

Community shopping and other transport services

Many councils run a Community Shopping Service which will help you to get safely to and from the shops.

And you may be able to use services such as Dial-A-Ride or the Taxicard scheme to get to or from the shops.

Help from your supermarket or local store

Many of the big supermarkets will help you shop if you have difficulties - either by pushing you around the supermarket if you are using a wheelchair, or by collecting your shopping for you. Contact the supermarkets direct to find out more.

And some local food shops will deliver food to your home for a small charge. Give them a ring and see what they say.

Deliveries from local restaurants and cafés

Consider which local restaurants will deliver take-away food to your door and if that's a viable option for you.

For example, if you live in South Benfleet, Enjoy Café deliver home-cooked food for people unable to cook or get out themselves. Breakfast, lunch and dinner meals are available for takeaway, delivery and eat-ins, with meals ranging from £1 to £6.

If you'd like to know more about their service, call 01268 569094 for more details.

Getting someone else to help

Don't be shy to ask trusted friends or neighbours to collect shopping for you. Many will be very happy to help and will be pleased to be asked.

Or you can arrange for someone to go with you to the shops, or to do your shopping for you. See our Support in your home page for information on how to arrange this.

Meal services

If you are unable to heat up frozen meals yourself, and have no-one to prepare meals for you, you may be able to receive assistance from the Meals on Wheels service currently delivered by Sodexo. This service will provided you with a daily hot meal delivered to your home at lunch on days you require.

If you would like to be referred for the meals on wheels service, please contact Essex County Council on 0345 603 7630 (or 0345 758 5592 for Minicom users).           

If you are eligible for the meals on wheels service you will have to pay towards the cost of each meal. Meals are available to people with special diets, food allergies and intolerances, people with specific medical needs, and meals for people with particular cultural or religious requirements.

If you are living alone and are isolated from family and friends then the people who deliver your meal will look out for you, and can let others know if you're experiencing any problems.

Alternatively if you don't want to use Essex's meals on wheels service, there are alternative local providers of hot meals:

  • Tiptree Meals Service; hot meals and tea tray service delivered county-wide
  • Mobile Meals; hot meals and tea tray service delivered in Colchester and Tendring. Call 01255 223003
  • Dinner's Direct; hot meals delivered in Colchester, including Brightlingsea, Elmstead and Wivenhoe
  • Creative Chefs; hot meals and tea tray service delivered in Tendring including Clacton, Frinton and Harwich
  • Mersea Island Meals on Wheels; hot meals delivered on Mersea Island, Tuesdays, Wednesdays and Thursdays only
  • Herts Meals; hot meals and tea tray service delivered in Harlow and Epping
  • Traditional Home Meals; covering all of Tendring
  • CTC Freshway; covering all of Tendring and surrounding area including Manningtree and Dedham. Phone 01206 827087
  • Royal Volutary Service Essex; covering areas around the Essex/Suffolk border

Food and poverty

Food poverty, social isolation and unemployment are currently affecting a lot of people across the UK sadly. Some people end up being unable to afford to buy food for themselves and their loved ones. But if you find yourself in this situation there are some organisations out there who can help.

The Trussell Trust food banks provide a minimum of three days' emergency food and support to people experiencing crisis in the UK.

FoodCycle is a UK charity that combines volunteers, surplus food and spare kitchen spaces to create tasty, nutritious meals for people at risk of food poverty and social isolation.
