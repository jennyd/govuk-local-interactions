Collection of large items

    Chelmsford City Council offers a collection service for the removal of large items from outside of your home. This is to assist residents who are unable to take their items to the Recycling Centre.


    Large items are things such as:

     

    • Television
    • Fridge/freezer
    • Washing machine
    • Sofa
    • Bed
    • Tables & Chairs
    • Carpet

     

    Some items may still have life in them, so think about donating them for reuse, rather than throwing them away. ECORRN (Essex Community Reuse and Recycling Network) run furniture reuse projects across the county and work with 40 different charities. 

     

     

    Fees


    There is a fee for these collections depending on the number of items being collected. These fees are for the period covering 1 April 2015 to 31 March 2016.

     

    Number of ItemsFull FeeConcession

    4 to 6 items£33.50£16.75

    Hourly Rate£69.50£34.75

     

    Concessions


    Residents that are receiving the following benefits are eligible for concessions:

     

    • Council Tax support(not single occupancy)
    • Disability Living Allowance
    • Incapacity benefit
    • Housing benefit
    • Income Support
    • Job Seekers Allowance
    • Working Tax Credit
    • State Pensions
    • Employment and Support Allowance
    • Personal Independent Payment (PIP)

     

    To apply for a collection or for an estimate requests:

     

    We are currently working on an online booking and payment facility however until this becomes available please telephone 01245 615800 to arrange a collection.

     


    When you contact us, you will need to provide the following information:

     

    • Details of items to be collected, and where the items will be left
    • Your name and address, and a daytime phone number

     

    Payment can be made by credit/debit card or cheque made payable to Chelmsford City Council. If you are eligible for the concessionary fees, we will need your national insurance number.

                

    Collection guidelines

     

    • Items for collections must be left outside your property close to the boundary. If you are unable to do this, please contact us on 01245 615800 for advice
    • Large items such as sheds and green houses must be dismantled prior to the collection. Please make safe any pieces of glass
    • Appliances must be disconnected from the service supply
    • Only items that we have agreed will be removed

     

    We cannot collect:

     

    • Concrete, builders' rubble, oil storage tanks or asbestos
    • Bricks, blocks, tiles or slates
    • Florescent light tubes and sun bed UV light tubes
    • Commercial waste or Clinical Waste
    • Liquids, toxic waste, paint or oil
    • Engines, tyres, car batteries, or any rechargeable batteries

     

    Apart from asbestos, clinical waste and toxic waste which requires removal by a specialist, all the above can be taken to your local Recycling Centre for Household Waste which is operated by Essex County Council.
