Sheltered and supported housing

Housing with Support

There are a number of supported housing schemes for people who need a bit of support to become more independent. These schemes provide housing related support so that in time residents can move to their own home.

There are schemes for learning disability, physical disability, mental health, domestic violence and homelessness.

Referrals can be made through a Central Access Point run by Cumbria County Council. Housing related support can help with:

  • becoming more independent
  • applying for benefits, budgeting and managing debt
  • finding somewhere to live and setting up home
  • access to other support or community services
  • support to access work or training
  • dealing with other professionals, family and neighbours and access to advice and guidance
  • support to maintain health and well being
  • security of the home and personal safety

There are a number of different supported housing providers offering accommodation for people with support needs in South Lakeland.


Sheltered and extra care housing

South Lakes Housing is one of the main providers of sheltered housing schemes for rent for older and disabled people across South Lakeland. There are schemes in Ulverston, Windermere, Ambleside, Sedbergh, Kendal, Kirkby Lonsdale and Grange over Sands. Vacancies are advertised on Cumbria Choice. Applicants need to register on Cumbria Choice and make bids on any vacancies. Additionally, there are a number of other sheltered housing providers offering accommodation for older people across South Lakeland.

Extra care housing schemes provide an on-site care team along with a Scheme Manager. Care is provided to residents who need it and can be adjusted according to a resident’s needs. Extra care schemes for rent are located in Kendal and Ambleside managed by Impact Housing Association, who also have a shared equity scheme in Kendal. South Lakes Housing has a scheme at Windermere that has recently been upgraded to include dementia friendly elements.
