Report seat, railing or bikestand problems

Swindon Borough Council repairs and maintains street furniture that is publicly owned.

This includes the following:

  • Street seats and benches
  • Cycle stands
  • Barriers and bollards
  • Railings
  • Bus-stop posts
  • Footbridges
  • Some gates and fences

Problems with street furniture on private land is the responsibilty of the land-owner however.

Report a problem with a seat, railing or bikestand online

Following a report of a problem with street furniture, we prioritise problems as follows:

  • If an item has been damaged in a way that makes it potentially dangerous, or if it has graffiti of an offensive or racist nature, we will inspect it within one hour with a view to resolving the issue quickly.
  • Where there is a minimal hazard but no issue for public safety, we will inspect and assess the problem within 10 working days.

It is sometimes necessary to have items temporarily removed whilst a replacement item is ordered or supplied. Because of the varying nature of street furniture items, and because we use a number of different suppliers for the various items, we cannot be specific about the timescale for replacement.
