Disabled parking and Blue Badges

Disabled parking permits are known as Blue Badges in the UK and Europe.

Portsmouth City Council is responsible for the issue of all Blue Badges in the city, covering all postcodes between PO1 and PO6.

  • if you live outside of this area, for example Waterlooville, Fareham or Gosport, you will need to contact Hampshire County Council to apply for a Blue Badge

The Blue Badge scheme provides a national range of parking concessions for disabled people with severe mobility problems who have difficulty using public transport - it is designed to help severely disabled people to travel independently, as either a driver or passenger, by allowing them to park close to their destination.

Further information on disabled parking and applying for a Blue Badge can be found below - in addition you can browse our disabled parking frequently asked questions or contact us:

  • email bluebadges@portsmouthcc.gov.uk
  • write to Transport and Environment, Civic Offices, Guildhall Square, Portsmouth, Hampshire, PO1 2NE
  • phone 023 9284 1176

Applying for a Blue Badge

  • You can apply for or renew a Blue Badge online at gov.uk. Alternatively, contact us for an application form.

The application process can be tracked online, and includes questions to check your eligibility for a Blue Badge. All badges are issued by the council and cost £10 to cover administration and production.

How to collect your Blue Badge

We no longer send Blue Badges out by post due to problems encountered with postage and to remove the risk of fraudulent applications being processed.

We contact applicants to let them know that their Blue Badge is ready for collection - please ensure that you provide us with a daytime telephone number.

Applicants will need proof of identification (birth certificate, passport or driving licence) to be able to collect their Blue Badge from Portsmouth Shopmobility, 56 Arundel St, Portsmouth, PO1 1NL. 

  • you can also follow the link to report a lost or stolen Blue Badge online

Blue Badge independent mobility assessments

If you do not meet the automatic qualification criteria when you apply for a Blue Badge, you will need to attend an independent mobility assessment (IMA) at the Frank Sorrell Centre, Prince Albert Road, Southsea, PO4 9HR.

Applicants will will be contacted to arrange an appointment when their application has been processed.

Disabled parking bays

Portsmouth City Council is also responsible for issuing and enforcing disabled parking bays. Disabled parking bays are not reserved for the sole use of an individual and may be used by any Blue Badge holder.

Disabled parking bays feature a sign next to them (on a wall, fence or post) which reads 'Disabled badge holders only'. It is a requirement to display a valid Blue Badge at all times whilst parked in an enforceable disabled parking bay.

Any motorist found parked in a disabled bay without displaying a valid Blue Badge is liable to receive a Penalty Charge Notice.

Disabled bay holders may not be able to display a Blue Badge at all times whilst parked in their allocated bay - they can request a permit, specific to their vehicle and address, enabling them to park in their allocated bay without displaying a Blue Badge.

Permit request forms are automatically sent to bay holders upon the issue of a disabled parking bay and can also be requested by contacting us:

  • email disabledbays@portsmouthcc.gov.uk
  • write to Transport and Environment, Civic Offices, Guildhall Square, Portsmouth, Hampshire, PO1 2NE
  • phone 023 9268 8304

A fee of  £18 is applicable for each permit, and an administration fee is also required for a change of vehicle, or for a lost or damaged permit.

A Traffic Regulation Order must legally be created for a new disabled parking bay before it can be installed. Portsmouth City Council will make every effort to deal with applications as quickly as possible, but due to the production and publication of Traffic Regulation Orders, an application will take a minimum of 3 months to process.

There is a £51.00 fee applicable prior to a bay being installed - if you can prove you are receiving Housing Benefit or Council Tax Support this fee will not be charged.
