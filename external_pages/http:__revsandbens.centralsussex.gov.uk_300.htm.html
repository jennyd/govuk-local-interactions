What is Council Tax Support?

The Council Tax Support Scheme is a means tested benefit administered by local authorities on behalf of the Government. The purpose of the scheme is to provide assistance with council tax payments and it does so in two forms. Claimants eligible for both forms are awarded whichever is the most valuable.

  1. Main Council Tax Support is the most common and it helps people on low incomes pay their council tax.
  2. Second Adult Rebate is not based on the claimant’s needs or resources but on the income of certain adults in the claimant’s household. There are two forms of Second Adult Rebate:
    • Student Only – the purpose being to compensate the claimant (council tax payer) for the loss of an exemption caused by the presence of the “second adult” in their home.
    • General – the purpose being to compensate the claimant (council tax payer) for the loss of a discount caused by the presence of the “second adult” in their home. It is worth up to 25% of the council tax liability on the dwelling.

The information on these pages does not cover all the rules for all the benefits for every situation, nor does it provide a full interpretation of the rules. It should not be treated as a complete and authoritative statement of the law. It is provided for guidance purposes only.

What is Housing Benefit?

The Housing Benefit Scheme is a means tested benefit which is administered by local authorities on behalf of the Government. The purpose of the scheme is to ensure that people have a sufficient income to afford to pay the rent of a reasonably priced accommodation which is suitable for their needs. If you are on a low income and you rent your home from the Council, a Housing Association or a Private Landlord, you may be able to get Housing Benefit. You cannot usually get Housing Benefit if you live in a close relative’s household and you cannot get Housing Benefit if you own your home or to help with mortgage payments.

When should I claim?

You should claim as soon as you think you may be entitled to Housing and/or Council Tax Support, as there are strict rules governing the time-limits for which claims can be submitted.

We can however accept advance claims; for example: - if you know you are moving into a new property in 4 weeks time then submit your claim as soon as you know the exact date. Advance claims can be accepted providing they are received within 13 weeks (17 weeks for pensioners) of the date of a change that will mean you may be entitled to Housing and/or Council Tax Support.

Help completing the claim form

We offer a visiting service for elderly or infirm customers wishing to make a new claim. Please call us on the numbers above and a member of staff will explain everything you need to provide with your claim form and a Visiting Officer will then visit you at home to complete the form with you and collect the relevant evidence.

You can also visit any of our offices listed above where a member of our staff will be happy to assist you with completing your claim form.
