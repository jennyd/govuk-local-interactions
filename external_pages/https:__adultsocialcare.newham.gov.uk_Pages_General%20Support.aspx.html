Big White Wall: Free digital mental health support 24/7:

Newham Clinical Commissioning Group (local NHS) have commissioned a free, confidential online support service for all Newham residents aged 16 or over experiencing mental health issues; such as anxiety and depression. The online service is called Big White Wall and it provides safe, anonymous, online support 24/7, with a supportive community, information and self-help resources, and trained counsellors online at all times.

For more information please visit the website:

Samaritans:

The Samaritans provides confidential non-judgemental emotional support, 24 hours a day for people who are experiencing feelings of distress or despair, including those which could lead to suicide.

Advocacy and Activities:

MIND in Tower Hamlets and Newham

MIND in Tower Hamlets and Newham provide a range of services to support people who are affected by mental health issues:

  • Impatient and community advocacy
  • Welfare benefits advice
  • Weekly men's group and women's group
  • Counselling (access via Talking Therapies referral)
  • The Mind advocacy service is an independent, confidential service providing practical support to enable individuals to:
  • Have a say in their care and treatment
  • Find out and safeguard their rights in the community

Access Services:
To access MIND services you can refer yourself or be referred by your GP/local doctor, your care coordinator or any other statutory and voluntary sector agencies.

Opening hours- 10:00 -17:00 Monday to Friday (by appointment only)
The counselling service is available on Tuesday evenings and on Saturdays.

Legal:

LawWorks Clinics Network:

The LawWorks Clinics Network is a nationwide network of free legal advice sessions which LawWorks supports. Clinics provide free initial advice to individuals on various areas of law including social welfare issues, employment law, housing matters and consumer disputes.
To find your local clinic, please visit the website: http://lawworks.org.uk/clinics

Carers:

Support for Carers of Adults with Mental Health Needs:

A support service for carers who may have mental health issues offering advocacy and support. A support group is also available for carers who are supporting/caring for someone with mental health needs. Support groups are an informal way to meet other carers, whilst gaining advice, information and support to help in your caring role.

Groups meet:
Shrewsbury Group: First Tuesday of each month; 6pm to 7pm
Stratford Group: 3rd Thursday of the month from 5.30-7.30pm
Cost: free to join the support group
Address: Passmore Edwards Centre, Shrewsbury Road, E7 8QR
Tel: 020 8475 8018

Bereavement:

Newham Bereavement Service:

Newham Bereavement Service is a team of trained volunteers who together with other staff are here to offer practical, social and emotional bereavement support.

Newham Bereavement Service offer a variety of services to help people as everyone's needs and feeling vary.
Our volunteers are available to support by providing:

  • Regular visits, company and a friendly chat.
  • Information including access to other services.
  • Emotional support at times of change or difficulty.
  • Encouragement and support to make a wider network of contacts.

Address: St Joseph's Hospice, Mare Street, Hackney, E8 4SA
Email: info@stjh.org.uk 
Tel: 020 8525 6000

Older People:

Age UK – Newham:

Service offered: Advice and information for older people in Newham. Housing and benefits advice surgeries. Home visits for income maximisation/welfare benefits checks.

CMHT (Older People):
Provided by ELFT, the CMHT (Older People) is an integrated mental health and social services team that provides psychiatric and social needs assessment, intervention and treatment. The service works with carers and families of older people to deliver care that meets the needs of individuals and acts as a single point of entry to all other ELFT older people’s services. Self-referral or referral by health professional available.

Address: 655 Barking Road, Plaistow, E13 9EX
