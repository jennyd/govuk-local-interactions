Anti-social behaviour

Safer Kirklees (antisocial.behaviour@kirklees.gov.uk) Last updated - November 2015

Report an anti-social behaviour incident

If you own your own home


  • Contact your local Neighbourhood Policing Team: Find your local Neighbourhood Policing Team
  • Or contact the Police on the non emergency number 101

If you rent your home from Kirklees Council


  • Contact Kirklees Neighbourhood Housing offices
  • Or contact the Police on the non emergency number 101

If you rent your home from a private landlord, agency or housing association

You must first report the problem to your landlord. If problems persist:

  • Contact your local Neighbourhood Policing Team: Find your local Neighbourhood Policing Team
  • Or contact the Police on the non emergency number 101

What happens next

When we receive a referral about persistent and serious anti-social behaviour, our first priority will be to establish all the facts.

We will discuss your concerns with you - in the strictest confidence, liaise with your landlord and partner agencies as appropriate to clarify actions already taken and conduct an investigation to determine what further actions could be taken to resolve the situation.

Types of action could include:

  • Setting up mediation sessions.
  • Referring those committing anti-social behaviour to diversionary activities or support.
  • Using acceptable behaviour contracts to deter the person or group from persisting with their activities.
  • Securing injunctions against individuals.
  • Using anti-social behaviour orders to prevent the person or group from persisting with their activities.

Community trigger: If you feel that no action has been taken

When you can use the community trigger


  • If you have complained three times in the last six months about separate incidents and no action has been taken;
  • or five people in the local community have complained separately in the last six months about similar incidents and no action has been taken;
  • or you have complained in the last six months about a hate crime incident and no action has been taken

If you have an ongoing complaint that is being dealt with, your case is not appropriate for the community trigger. It is not a complaints procedure. If you are making a complaint about an organisation, please use the organisation's own complaints procedures or report the matter to the ombudsman or Independent Police Complaints Commission.

  • How to use an ombudsman in England
  • Independent Police Complaints Commission complaints

Make a community trigger report


  • Fill in the Community Trigger form
  • or Contact us

You need to provide details of:


  • each time you have complained
  • who you have complained to (name, organisation and/or Incident Reference Number if you have one)
  • information about the anti-social behaviour.

What happens next


  • We will assess your application to ensure that the threshold has been met.
  • If it has, we will ask the agencies involved to provide details of your complaints and the actions that they have considered and taken.
  • A meeting will then take place between the appropriate agencies which may include Kirklees Council, Kirklees Anti-Social Behaviour Partnership, West Yorkshire Police or a registered housing provider (social landlord) and other partners (if they are involved) to discuss the anti-social behaviour and what actions have been considered and taken.
  • The group will review how the partnership has responded and make recommendations on how the problem can be resolved.
  • A response will be sent to you detailing the outcome of this meeting. You will be informed of actions already taken and what will happen in the future.

If you are unhappy with the response, you can request a further review:

  • 01484 221000
  • Chris.walsh@kirklees.gov.uk

What anti-social behaviour is

Anti-social behaviour includes things such as:

  • rowdy, noisy behaviour in otherwise quiet neighbourhoods
  • night time noise from houses or gardens, especially between 11.00 pm and 7.00 am
  • threatening, drunken or "yobbish" behaviour
  • vandalism, graffiti and fly-posting
  • dealing or buying drugs on the street
  • litter and fly-tipping rubbish
  • aggressive begging
  • drinking in the street
  • setting off fireworks late at night
  • abandoning cars on the street
  • neighbour disputes

Anti-social behaviour doesn't just make life unpleasant. It can ruin lives and make whole areas feel unsafe.

Things we cannot resolve

We do not resolve neighbour disputes about the location of boundaries, walls, fences and buildings or the ownership of land

If you think someone has built a wall, fence or building:

  • Contact Planning

Safer Kirklees

We believe that everyone who lives, works or visits Kirklees has the basic right to go about their business, feel safe and secure and not have to suffer or tolerate the anti-social activities of others.

Safer Kirklees brings together experienced staff from Kirklees Council, West Yorkshire Police and Kirklees Neighbourhood Housing and other partner organisations to effectively prevent and resolve anti-social behaviour.

Advice and assistance

If you would like advice and assistance about the anti social behaviour you are suffering from please contact our team for an informal chat or download our information pack.
