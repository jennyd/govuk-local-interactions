Managing your care with Direct Payments 

Sometimes Social Services can provide a cash payment directly to people whose needs have been assessed by Social Services as being eligible to receive services, so they can arrange and purchase their own support.

They might use the money to:

  • employ someone directly to help with their care (a Personal Assistant)
  • buy care from a private registered care agency
  • make their own arrangements instead of using Social Services day care or respite care

Direct Payments are just one option for care; if people prefer to receive services in the traditional way, they can.  Some people choose a mix of Direct Payments and services.

If you receive a Direct Payment, you may be asked to pay part of the cost towards your care.  This depends upon the type of care or service you are assessed as needing and may also depend upon how much income and savings you have.  If you have to pay something, this amount will be the same whether the services are arranged for you by Social Services or whether you choose Direct Payments.  The usual maximum you could be asked to pay for non-residential care is currently £60 per week.

Who can apply for a Direct Payment?

With a few exceptions the following people can apply for a Direct Payment:

  • Adults and young people over the age of 16
  • A person with parental responsibility for a disabled child and a disabled person with responsibility for a child
  • Carers aged 16 and over
  • An appointed ‘Suitable Person’

How do I get a Direct Payment?

In order for you to receive a Direct Payment, a Care Manager has to do an assessment of your care needs.  Please ask us for more information on the assessment process and eligibility criteria.

If you are eligible to receive services, they will agree a Care Plan with you.  They will talk to you about how you would manage the Direct Payment and with the Finance Officer agree how much money you would receive as a Direct Payment to put the Care Plan into practice and meet your assessed needs.

Social Services will then pay money into a separate bank account which you will need to set up.

If you have already had an assessment, and are receiving a service, you can apply to switch to Direct Payments instead.  Please speak to your Care Manager about this.

What can I do with a Direct Payment?

You can plan your own care and control your own arrangements. As you are the person who is employing your Personal Assistant, you are the one in charge.  You must still meet any outcomes/needs identified in your Care Plan – but you can do this in your own way.

Why choose Direct Payments?

Direct Payments can provide more choice, control and independence in the way your care is managed.  The support arranged maybe more flexible and responsive to your assessed needs because it is you arranging it for yourself.

Once you start Direct Payments, you will have regular reviews to check that they are meeting your needs.

If your needs change, you may be able to change your Direct Payment.

If you find that Direct Payments turn out not to be right for you after all, you can change to having services provided by Social Services.

What is involved in managing my Direct Payment?

With the choice and flexibility offered by Direct Payments there are also responsibilities.

You are in charge of administering the money and keeping records to show it has been spent in the right way.   However, you can have help with doing this from a friend or relative.

Bridgend Social Services, in partnership with two other Councils, fund Dewis Centre for Independent Living. They can provide you with free information, advice and support.  They can also provide practical help with tasks such as recruiting and employing staff, advice regarding tax and National Insurance and they offer a payroll service.

How do I find out more?

If you already receive support from Social Services, speak to your Social Worker or Care Manager.
