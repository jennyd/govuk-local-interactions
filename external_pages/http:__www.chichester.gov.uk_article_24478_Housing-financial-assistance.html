Disabled Facilities Grant

If you have a disability, you can apply for a Disabled Facilities Grant funded by the district council. Disabled Facilities Grants are available to homeowners, private tenants, or those occupying properties owned by a registered provider e.g. a housing association.

Disabled Facilities Grants can be given for works such as the following:

  • Providing access to your property;
  • Installing a stair lift;
  • Converting a bathroom to a level access shower room; or,
  • Providing a wheelchair accessible kitchen.

Before applying for a grant an Occupational Therapist must first assess your needs. If you do not already have an Occupational Therapist please contact either by phoning 01243 642121 or emailing OT.connect@westsussex.gov.uk or go to  WSCC Adult Care Point  for more options.

Once the council has received a recommendation for adaptation works from your Occupational Therapist, and if you are in receipt of a means tested benefit you can submit an application for a grant. However, if you are not in receipt of a means tested benefit you must first complete a means test questionnaire and send it to the Environmental Housing Team at Chichester District Council, and depending upon the outcome you may be asked to contribute towards the cost of the works. Please note that children under 16 years or in full time education up to the age of 19 are eligible for a full grant and are not required to be means tested.

It is important to note that before adaptation works are approved, they must be assessed by an officer within the Environmental Housing Team to determine that they are reasonable and practicable.

Empty Homes and Conversion Assistance

Empty Homes Assistance

If you own a house that has been empty for more than a year, and requires works to meet the Decent Homes Standard you can apply for financial assistance. The amount available is 50% of the cost of works, up to a maximum of £10,000. Conditions include:

  • works cannot be part of an insurance claim
  • the property must be at least 10 years old
  • works must be completed within 12 months of assistance being approved
  • following the works being done, we select the tenants for the next 10 years

Conversion Assistance

This is available to owners of buildings that are going to be converted to provide additional living accommodation. For example, this could for changing a property from non-residential to residential use or converting a house into flats. The assistance is 50% of the cost of works, up to a maximum of £10,000. Conditions include:

  • you must receive a number of quotations for the works, and carry out the work in line with the quotations
  • you must have the assistance approved before works are started
  • you must ensure that all homes created meet the Decent Homes Standard
  • following the works being done, we select the tenants for the next 10 years

Energy efficiency grants and discounts

There are a number of organisations, including the Government and energy companies, who can provide grants or discounts to help you:

  • make your home warm;
  • or introduce energy saving improvements.

These improvements could include:

  • installing cavity wall and/or loft insulation;
  • upgrading your central heating system; or
  • draught proofing your home.

Green Deal 

A Government-backed scheme to help you make cost-effective energy saving improvements. Instead of paying for the full cost of the improvements up front, you pay over time through a charge added to your electricity bill.

ECO (Energy Company Obligation)

Funding from the big six energy suppliers to support energy improvements for people on certain benefits, for those in solid wall properties and for households in the poorest parts of the country.

Feed-in Tariffs

On-going financial support for people generating electricity from renewable sources such as solar PV and wind.

Renewable Heat Premium Payment (RHPP)

A one-off payment to households installing renewable heating systems such as heat pumps, wood boilers or solar water heating. If you would like to find out more on these national schemes, please contact the Energy Saving Trust. The Energy Saving Trust  has also a list of recommended products, from appliances to lighting, which meet strict energy performance criteria and can help you save energy and save money.

The Home Trust Loan

The Home Trust Loan is a unique, low cost, home improvement service and lending scheme offered in partnership between Parity Trust  and Chichester District Council.

There are three borrowing options tailored to suit your circumstances.

Whichever one you choose, you can be assured of:

  • a low interest rate of 5.49% (5.63% APR);
  • a fixed rate of interest, guaranteed for the full term of the loan; and,
  • the option to make overpayments or settle the loan in full without penalty.

What's more, the scheme provides you with all the support and guidance you need to see the home improvement process through from start to finish. We offer:

  • the support of a dedicated Home Trust Loan Officer, who is able to visit you in your home;
  • advice on how to choose a reputable contractor; and,
  • once the work is finished, a final inspection can be arranged to ensure the works have been completed to a satisfactory standard.

Who is the Home Trust Loan For?

We have specifically designed this loan for home owners whose properties need repairs or maintenance, but who do not have the funds available to carry out the work or who are finding it difficult to access the financial assistance.

What can the loan be used for?

The Home Trust Loan is available for those who need improvement works to be carried out to their properties, for example if:

  • you have a leaking roof;
  • your boiler has broken;
  • your front door needs replacing;
  • your windows are draughty; or,
  • you don't have adequate heating and need a central heating system installed.

These are all prime examples of purposes for which a Home Trust Loan may be used. The eligibility criteria relating to appropriate work can vary, so please contact Parity Trust for more information.

How to apply?

Contact the Environmental Housing Team and an officer will visit your house to inspect the works that need carrying out. If the officer agrees the works are necessary and that the Home Trust Loan is potentially your best source of finance, a Home Trust Loan Officer will contact you to arrange a home visit. They will conduct a full financial review with you and provide information about the Home Trust Loan options available to you.

 

Housing renewal assistance

Decent Homes Assistance

If your property requires works to meet the Decent Home Standard, you may be eligible for a Home Trust Loan and/or subsidy to pay for the works.

To meet the Decent Homes Standard, a house should:

  • be free from health and safety risks
  • be in a satisfactory state of repair
  • have reasonably modern facilities
  • have adequate heating and insulation

We work in partnership with Parity Trust who administers Home Trust Loans. Parity Trust is a not-for-profit organisation. Please see the Home Trust Loan website for more information.

If you think you may be eligible for assistance, please contact the Environmental Housing Team to arrange for an officer to carry out an inspection of your home.

Hazard Prevention Assistance

To be eligible for this assistance you must either be over 60 or have a disability, and be on a low income.  It is aimed at dealing with low cost defects that could be a risk to health and safety. For example:

  • dangerous electrical wiring
  • badly rotted windows
  • broken front paths and steps
  • faulty boiler

In order to determine whether works are eligible, please contact the Environmental Housing Team who will arrange for an officer to visit and make an assessment.

It is important to note that this assistance is repayable when the property is sold.

Landlord accreditation assistance

Landlords owning a property within Chichester District Council area may wish to join the Chichester and Arun Landlord Accreditation Scheme.

  • Landlord Accreditation Scheme - Frequently Asked Questions [pdf 810kb]
  • Landlord Accreditation Standards [pdf 3Mb]
  • Landlords Guide - The Homefinder Scheme [pdf 154kb]

Landlords who apply to join the scheme are eligible for Landlord Accreditation Assistance, which provides financial assistance towards the cost of a wide range of works required to meet the Accreditation Scheme standards.

In order to join the scheme, landlords must complete an application form, which can be found within the Landlord Accreditation Scheme Standards booklet, which is available online. As part of the application form landlords must list all their properties and confirm they will accredit all their privately rented properties within the Chichester & Arun districts within a reasonable timescale.

Once an application has been received the Council will arrange to inspect the property and identify any required works. If necessary a schedule of works for the items eligible for Landlords Accreditation Assistance will be compiled. The landlord may then use this schedule to obtain at least two competitive quotes for each item.

Landlord Accreditation Assistance is provided at a rate of 50% of the total cost of the eligible works, up to a maximum of £4000. Financial assistance is available towards the cost of the majority of works required to meet the scheme standards. However portable items such as fire blankets and battery powered carbon monoxide detectors, etc. are not included.
