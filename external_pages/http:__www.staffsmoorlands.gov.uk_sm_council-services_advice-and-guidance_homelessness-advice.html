Homelessness advice

If you think that you may lose your accommodation then please contact a housing advisor. Every assistance will be provided to help you to keep your current accommodation. It is therefore vital that you contact us as soon as possible so we can try to prevent your homelessness. Housing advice and assistance is available, primarily to prevent homelessness, whether you live in a private rented, housing association or mortgaged property.

Advice and assistance includes;

  • Help to prevent the loss of your current home and address problems with current accommodation (i.e. liaison with landlord or housing benefits etc)
  • Establishing legal rights as tenant, home owner or landlord
  • Access to relevant support (i.e. tenancy support) or independent financial advice (i.e. money and debt advice)
  • Exploring ways to find and access alternative accommodation (i.e. within the private rented sector using the Rent Deposit Bond Scheme)
  • If single and under 35 years specialist advice and assistance is available through the 'Single Persons Housing Project'

To contact the Housing Advice Service Tel: 0345 605 3012 or complete the online form.

 

The following section explains the legal duty the council is likely to have towards you should you become homeless.

Eligible for assistance

You would not normally be eligible for assistance if you are a person from abroad and you are not able to claim state benefits. Your housing advisor will be able to make an assessment of your eligibility. You would need to bring in your passport or other immigration documents to enable a decision to be made if you think you may be in this category.

You are homeless or threatened with homelessness if:

  • You have a home but are at risk of violence or threats of violence likely to be carried out
  • You have nowhere to stay where your family can live with you
  • You do not have a legal right to remain in your accommodation
  • You have been given a valid notice of eviction or are facing court action due to mortgage arrears or landlord action
  • You have nowhere to stay tonight

You have a priority need if:

  • You have dependant children living with you
  • You are pregnant or live with a pregnant woman
  • You are age 16 or 17 (to be assessed with social services)
  • You are under 21 and were fostered or in care between 16-18
  • You are considered to be vulnerable because of your age or due to mental or physical disability or illness, or because you have suffered from violence
  • You are considered to be vulnerable because you have been in care, in custody or have served in HM forces
  • You are homeless due to an emergency such as a fire or flood

If you are eligible, homeless and in priority need, we may have a duty to offer you interim accommodation. Temporary accommodation while investigations are carried out may be hostel accommodation or bed and breakfast if no no other temporary accommdodation is available.

If you were not considered to be in priority need we would provide you with advice and assistance. We would also try to help you to find temporary accommodation - but we do not have a legal duty to do so. However in severe weather, where temperatures are below zero degrees Celsius or lower there may be exemptions.

We may be able to assist you with our Rent Deposit Bond Scheme (subject to affordability and local connection) to help you to find accommodation in the private sector. Please ask a member of the housing advice service for information about this scheme.

Intentionality

If your housing advisor decides that you have deliberately done or failed to do something which has caused you to lose your accommodation, then you are likely to be considered to be intentionally homeless.

If this is the case then we may only have a duty to provide you with interim accommodation for a certain period, so that you can find your own accommodation.

If you have accommodation in which it is reasonable for you to live, you should think carefully about giving this up and presenting as homeless as you are likely to be found to be intentionally homeless.

Please seek advice BEFORE you decide to leave.

Local connection

You have a local connection with Staffordshire Moorlands if:

  • You have lived in the Staffordshire Moorlands for 6 out of the last 12 months, or 3 out of the last 5 years
  • You have firstline family members that have lived within the Moorlands District area for 5 years
  • You have  employment here
  • You have other special reasons

Homelessness decision - what happens next?

Your housing advisor will check that there is no other suitable accommodation available for you. You could be asked what you have done yourself to find accommodation for yourself and your family. The advisor will advise you of the duty owed to you.

Depending on what duty we have towards you, we may find accommodation for you or provide advice and assistance such as the Rent Deposit Bond Scheme to help you find accommodation in the private rented sector.

Your homelessness application will be investigated, taking into consideration all relevant information given by the applicant and may also include information from external agencies, benefits agencies and even medical details. We endeavour to reach a decision as quickly as possible, however some cases will take longer than others. You will be given your decision in writing, and the reason for this decision. This decision will determine our duty towards you.
 

If we do have a duty to re-house you temporary accommodation may be secured pending permanent rehousing, this can be achieved by;

  • 'One offer' of social housing accommodation, through nominating you to a housing association vacancy within the Staffordshire Moorlands area. The areas of choice  include Leek, Biddulph or Cheadle and their surrounding village areas. There is limited availability of accommodation in the area, and it is not possible to to say how long it will be before a property becomes available. OR
  • A 12 month tenancy in the private rented sector can be acquired by either yourself or the Council to fulfil this duty.

If you do not agree with the decision you have been given

You have the right to a review of any decision taken on your application under the Housing Act 1996 ( Part VII) as amended by the Homelessness Act 2002.This includes the suitability of any accommodation you may be  offered  by the council in order to discharge its duty towards you under the legisalation.

Rough Sleeping

We support rough sleepers in the area and are working with StreetLink to do this. If you are sleeping rough or come across someone who is sleeping rough, please call the StreetLink Service on Tel: 0300 500 0914 or visit the website

Streetlink

StreetLink is a national rough sleeping website that enables the public to alert local authorities in England about rough sleepers in their area. The Service offers the public a means to act when they see someone sleeping rough and is the first step someone can take to ensure that rough sleepers are connected to the local services and support available to them.

Rough Sleepers and members of the public can contact the StreetLink service about people sleeping rough in Staffordshire Moorlands, alternatively they can report any concerns about a rough sleeper directly to the Rough Sleeper’s Outreach Service on 0800 015 1464

Rough Sleeper Outreach

The Staffordshire and Shropshire Rough Sleeper Outreach Service can help to find accommodation, claim benefits and find a doctor. To find out more about these services Tel 0800 015 1464 or visit Brighter-Futures website.

Community Matron Service

The Community Matron service for single homeless aims to make healthcare more accessible, enable management of mental/ physical health and well being, prevent unnecessary hospital admissions and reduce hospital discharges to no fixed abode. The service offers;

  • Drop-in sessions/ appointments at Leek venues
  • Treatment for single homeless clients (minor illnesses and longer term health conditions)
  • Signpost to relevant services (i.e. substance misuse/ mental health/ talking therapies)
  • Advocacy and negotiate health access

To find out more about these services Tel 0300 123 0980 ext 4259 (confidential voicemail) or visit Health Inclusion Team website.

Severe Weather

Good practice guidance recommends that all local authorities have emergency provisions in place to prevent the loss of life of rough sleepers in severe weather conditions. Provisions should be triggered when the night time temperature is predicted to be zero or below for three consecutive nights.

If you, or someone you are aware of, is facing the prospect of sleeping rough during severe weather, please contact the Housing Advice Service on Tel 0345 605 3012
