Traffic lights and pedestrian crossing beacons

Find out who maintains traffic and crossing lights and how to report faults.

  1. Permanent traffic lights
  2. Zebra crossings
  3. Temporary (portable) traffic signals

1 Permanent traffic lights

There are roughly 440 sets of permanent traffic lights at crossings and junctions in West Sussex.

Traffic light maintenance

We:

  • inspect traffic lights twice a year for routine repairs and maintenance
  • routinely replace non-LED lamps once a year to reduce the number of faults due to lamp failure
  • monitor the majority of our signalled junctions and will ask our contractor, Telent, to repair any that are not operating properly. 

Traffic light faults

You should report faulty traffic lights to our contractor Telent.

Telent will respond to 'Priority 1' faults, which could pose a road safety risk, within 2 hours, and to other faults within 24 hours. 

  • Phone Telent: 0845 7125 415 - calls will cost 4 pence per minute, plus your phone company’s access charge.

A27, A23 and M23

Highways England is responsible for traffic lights on the trunk roads - A27, A23 and M23 - and faults should be reported to:

  • Email: info@highwaysengland.co.uk
  • Phone: 030 012 35000

2 Zebra crossings

The flashing beacon lights on zebra crossings, as well as street lights and illuminated signs and bollards, are maintained by SSE. 

Report a fault with a flashing beacon to SSE

  • Phone: 0800 048 2435 (SSE)
  • Online: SSE lights on in West Sussex

Requesting a new crossing

Find out how to request a new pedestrian crossing for a road in your community.

3 Temporary (portable) traffic signals

If there is a fault with a temporary (portable) traffic signal call the emergency number displayed on or near the signals, or report the issue to us:

  • Phone: 01243 642105
