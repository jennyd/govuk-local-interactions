Garden waste recycling

Order new bin     Report missed collection

 

We provide brown bins or brown reusable bags to 39,000 properties in Hackney for collecting garden waste. On the day of collection your brown bin or bag should be placed at the front boundary of your home at street level by 7am, where it can be easily seen and accessed by the collection crew.

Enter your postcode and select your address to find out if you are entitled to a garden waste collection and to find out your collection day.

What can I recycle in my brown bin?


Flowers


Small plants


Grass


Small twigs


Hedge trimmings


Weeds, prunings


Leaves

 

 

What can't I recycle in my brown bin?


Food waste


Plant pots


Soil


Logs or branches over 8cm diameter


Paper


Plastic bags


Stones


Tins and cans

 

What should I do if I have large amounts of garden waste?

You can order an extra brown bin or a re-usable bag. You can also transport waste yourself to a household waste disposal site.

Japanese knotweed

You must not put Japanese knotweed in with your garden waste or general rubbish. The government has guidance on treating and diposing of invasive plants. If you have Japanese knotweed or other invasive plants on your land you should contact a specialist contractor to remove and dispose of it properly. 

What happens to my garden waste?

All food waste and garden waste collected in Hackney is delivered to a composting facility operated by London Waste Ltd in Edmonton, North London, where it is broken down into compost. This compost is then made available to Hackney to use on its parks and green spaces.
