How do I become a foster carer in Derby?

There are eight steps to fostering and our staff will be there to guide and support you every step of the way.

  1. Information session
  2. Screening call
  3. First home visit
  4. Invitation to foster
  5. Stage one of your application
  6. Essential checks
  7. Stage two of your application
  8. The fostering panel

 

Information session

You can come along to our fostering information sessions to learn more about the service we provide.

If you want to get the ball rolling then download our Fostering Information Pack and complete the online fostering enquiry form.

If you do not have easy access to a computer then please request a hardcopy of the fostering information pack and form by telephoning 01332 640880 or emailing fostering@derby.gov.uk. 

 

Screening call

Our social worker will then telephone you to have a chat about your interest.

 

First home visit

We will arrange for a social worker to meet you in person and help you decide if fostering is the way forward. It will be an informal chat to learn more about you.

 

Invitation to foster

If you meet the requirements you will be invited to apply to foster.

 

Stage one of your application

We confirm that we have received your application. 

 

Essential checks

We carry out routine checks including police and medical checks, interviews and request references.

We’ll keep in touch with you and will always discuss anything that comes up.

 

Stage two of your application form

If everything is satisfactory at the end of stage one, you will proceed to stage two where you’ll be assigned a social worker.

Our social worker will meet you regularly to get to know you better and prepare you for fostering.

You will discuss your skills and experience and you will be invited to a Skills To Foster training course. 

Everything is treated in confidence and we aim to let you know about your suitability to become a foster carer during these first meetings.

If you have already been approved as a foster carer by another fostering agency, or if you are a Friends and Family Foster Carer for a specific child, we can fast-track your application and stages one and two can run at the same time.

 

The Fostering Panel

When stages one and two are complete your social worker will submit a report to the fostering panel. 

You will be invited to attend a hearing where the panel will consider previous discussions and reports and make a recommendation regarding your approval.
