Housing with care and support

We recognise that wherever possible most people wish to remain in their own homes. However where this is not possible there are a range of options for people to consider. These include sheltered/retirement housing, housing with care and supported housing.

For most people Warrington Home Information and Improvement Agency can offer assistance and advice as you begin this process. Warrington Home Information and Improvement Agency is a non-profit advisory service for older, disabled or vulnerable people and families on low incomes who own their own homes or live in private rented accommodation.  Contact them for further information.

Sheltered/retirement housing

Sheltered/retirement housing is either purpose built flats or a group of bungalows or flats. Homes are usually only offered to people over the age of 55.  In most schemes, a scheme manager can be contacted via an alarm system from the person's home, to provide help if there is an emergency. If the manager is not on site this often links to a social alarm system, such as Carecall. Some scheme managers may offer additional support including making regular checks on your well-being, liaising with your local doctor and your relatives and managing the day-to-day running of the accommodation. Normally, scheme managers do not provide care services for residents or carry out tasks like shopping or cleaning.

If you think this level of support would best meet your needs please contact the Access Social Care team for an assessment of your needs. 

Housing with care

Housing with care is similar to sheltered/retirement housing but often offers a greater level of care, where up to 24-hour support with personal care is available. This can help people to retain their independence and choice while giving them support as required.  Housing with care can be an alternative to residential or nursing care and is also known as extra care sheltered housing or very sheltered housing. 

If you think this level of support would best meet your needs please contact the Access Social Care team for an assessment of your needs. 

Supported housing 

Occasionally complex needs cannot be fully met in standard accommodation. A range of accommodation is available that is specially designed for people with particular care and support needs, whilst ensuring that they can continue to live in their local community. This may include people with complex learning, physical or sensory needs.

If you think this level of support would best meet your needs please contact the Access Social Care team for an assessment of your needs.
