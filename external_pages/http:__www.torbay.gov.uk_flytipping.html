Fly-tipping

  • Report - Fly-tipping


Fly-tipping is a crime and is very expensive to deal with. It can attract further crime, is dangerous, unsightly, harmful to the environment and is affecting our quality of life. In the current economic climate its important now more than ever to tackle fly tipping incidents which place additional financial strain on the Local Authority and private land owners. 
Did you know?
  • Any waste which is discarded improperly by the public or businesses is illegal. You can be fined up to £50,000 and/or sent to prison for twelve months if you are convicted in a Magistrates’ Court.
  • Land owners have a legal duty to ensure that they take steps to secure their land and prevent fly-tipping.
  • Householders have a responsibility to make sure that their waste is disposed of legally.
  • If a householder wishes to dispose of waste such as bulky items, they must ensure that the person charged with its disposal is registered to do so.

Reporting Fly-tipping

In Torbay we are cracking down on fly-tipping and we need your help to do this. If we don’t work together the problem will continue to grow. If you see fly-tipping or have suspicions about a waste carrier you can report it in complete confidence to the council’s Enforcement Team.

Report - Fly-tipping

Remember that the more information you can provide to the council, the more likely it is that offenders will be caught and punished. The following details will help identify a fly-tipping offender:
  • The date, day and time you saw fly-tipping taking place.
  • How clearly you could see what was happening. Were there any obstructions to your line of vision?
  • How far away were you?
  • Was a vehicle used? Record the registration number; make, colour and model and any other noticeable features (such as logos, telephone numbers etc).
  • Describe the person/people, giving as much detail as possible.

Remember - DO

  • Visually inspect the waste and try to determine what the waste consists of and how much there is.
  • Take note of the exact location of the waste (and whether it is near water).
  • Report the offence.

Remember - NEVER

  • Touch the waste. The fly-tipped waste may be dangerous and contain hazardous items such as broken glass, asbestos, syringes and other harmful substances.
  • Disturb the site as there may be evidence that could help identify the offender(s) and lead to their prosecution.

Disposing of Waste

Residents of Torbay are reminded that if you have waste to dispose of Torbay Council will accept household items free of charge provided they are brought to the Household Waste and Recycling Centre (HWRC). This includes a variety of household waste, recyclable materials, discarded refrigerators, televisions etc as well as green waste but excludes food waste.
This is not a chargeable service to Torbay residents, but waste must be taken to the site. If transport is not available to you, TOR2 offers a Bulky Item Collection which is chargeable.
If you employ someone to remove waste from your property it is your responsibility to check with the Environment Agency that they are a Licensed Waste Carrier.

Search - Environment Agency Registers 

Monthly Garden Waste Collection Points operate in Brixham and Torquay every month between February and November. Torbay residents can take their household garden waste to the collection point between the hours of 9am and 1pm. The waste is then sent for composting.
