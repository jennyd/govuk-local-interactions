Adult social care - help with specific conditions

Portsmouth City Council provides services for people with particular conditions through adult social care. Read on to find out more about the council support provided for these different conditions:

  • autism
  • dementia
  • adult mental health
  • learning disabilities
  • physical disabilities
  • substance misuse
  • HIV/Aids
  • sensory impairment & deaf services.

Search for local services, organisations and support groups for all these conditions using our Healthwatch directory of services.

The council also offers help and support on a range of issues to carers.

For more information or to arrange an assessment, contact adult social care helpdesk on 023 9268 0810, or write to the adult social care assessment and support team at Portsmouth City Council Civic Offices, Guildhall Square, Portsmouth, PO1 2EP.

Autism     Open / Close

Autism is a lifelong condition affecting one in 100 people. It is a spectrum condition which means that, while all people with autism share certain difficulties, their condition will affect them in different ways.

Autism can affect how a person senses the world around them, and how a person communicates and relates with other people. People with autism can find it hard to tell other people how they feel, and may not find it easy to understand what others are thinking. This can make it hard to make friends, and meeting people can be difficult. To see how much you understand about autism, try our introduction to autism quiz. 

Portsmouth City Council asked people's views to help develop an autism diagnosis and support service. In the Associated documents section below, you can read both the survey results and the strategic plan.

Follow the links for local help and advice on autism:

  • Portsmouth Autism Support Network (PASN)  of parents of autistic children support each other
  • Pirates are Cooler than Ninjas offers help and support for young adults with autism, their parents and carers.

 or visit National Autistic Society.

Dementia   Open / Close

The term 'dementia' describes a set of symptoms that include loss of memory, mood changes, and problems with communication and reasoning. There are many types of dementia, including Alzheimer's disease and vascular dementia. It is progressive, which means the symptoms gradually get worse.

If you think you may have dementia, contact your GP in the first instance. If you look after someone with dementia, read our information for carers.

Enhancing the quality of life for people with dementia (and their carers) is a key priority for the council. Follow the link for information on the dementia action plan. For more information about the plan, email dementia.strategy@portsmouthcc.gov.uk.

Adult mental health     Open / Close

If you, or someone you care about, has mental health needs, there are services available in Portsmouth that could help, including:

  • counselling, social and emotional support
  • psychological and occupational therapy
  • rehabilitation and resettlement
  • community services
  • inpatient services

If you are concerned about your mental health, the first thing you should do is see your GP for diagnosis, treatment and advice, and if necessary a referral for specialist help.

Learning disabilities     Open / Close

Working with the Portsmouth clinical commissioning group, Portsmouth City Council supports people with learning disabilities in all areas of daily life, helping them to live as independently as possible. The council can help with everything from health, housing and managing money, to supporting people with learning disabilities to take part in everyday activities, such as going to work or college.

Phone 023 9268 4600 to contact the Kestrel Centre at St James Hospital, Locksway Road, Southsea, PO4 8LD for more information.

Physical disabilities   Open / Close

If you have a physical disability, we aim to support you to live at home as independently as possible. We have a range of services that could help:

  • occupational therapy - providing equipment or adapting your home to make daily living easier
  • help maximise your independence or rebuild your confidence after a fall or hospital stay 
  • respite care and short-stay accommodation.

Visit Disabled Go for accessibility information for venues in the city.

Substance misuse     Open / Close

Follow the Community Safety link to find information on local substance misuse services from the council.

To find out about accommodation, including supported accommodation for people with substance misuse problems, please email housing_options@portsmouthcc.gov.uk or phone Housing options on 023 9283 4989.

Cranstoun CDA under the Switch service offers a range of services for adults living in Portsmouth  who are either currently experiencing problems due to alcohol or other substance misuse or wanting continued support in maintaining their recovery. Services include assessment, one to one support, group work, auricular acupuncture, a women's service and specialist support to members of the LGBT community. It also provides a mobile needle exchange service.

HIV/AIDS   Open / Close

HIV is a virus that attacks the body's immune system, which provides a natural defence system against disease and infection. HIV can be passed on through:

  • unprotected sexual intercourse (vaginal, oral or anal) without using a condom
  • sharing contaminated needles, syringes and 'works'
  • from an infected mother to her baby during pregnancy, childbirth or breastfeeding
  • from contaminated blood or blood products.

If you are worried you may be HIV positive, you can call 0300 300 2016 to find your nearest place to get tested, or visit Sex Sense.

Being diagnosed with HIV affects you, your partner and your friends. Positive Action and Groundswell are two local support services for people living with HIV/AIDS include. For local NHS HIV support services visit NHS Choices.

Sensory impairment and deaf services      Open / Close

The sensory impairment & deaf services team works with Portsmouth residents who are deaf, deafened, hard of hearing, vision impaired or have dual sensory loss. We work with other departments and organisations to help people maintain or regain their independence, quality of life and ability to participate as fully as they choose in community affairs and activities. The services we offer include:

  • advice and information about equipment and services that support independence 
  • assessment by a specialist team member 
  • early intervention service based in the eye clinic at Queen Alexandra hospital 
  • individual rehabilitation training for people with vision impairment 
  • low vision service in partnership with the health service and local optometrists.

For more information or to make a referral, email sensoryteam@portsmouthcc.gov.uk, phone 023 9268 0810 or text 079 5653 5228 between 8.30am and noon Monday to Friday.
