Fly posting

Fly posters are adverts such as placards, advertising boards and banners that advertise businesses, events or services. Often fixed to existing signs or next to the road, they can distract drivers and cause accidents by covering traffic signs and signals.

Fly posting in public areas

We will remove fly posting from bollards, railings, streetlamps, trees, traffic signs and signals.

Dangersous or offensive fly posting is removed within two hours of notification (If you report it before 2.00pm we will remove it the same working day, otherwise we will remove it the following working day). Other fly posting will be removed within 5 working days of it being reported.

Request removal by using the fly posting report form.

The only way you can legally put up signs in public areas is to ask our traffic management team by calling: 0121 704 8000.

Fly posting on private land

We can’t remove fly posters from private land, but we can ask the person that put them there to remove them within 48 hours. The owner or occupier of private land that has fly posters displayed on it can also be told to remove them, but they’ll be given 28 days to do so.

 If fly posting is on private land please call Solihull Connect on 0121 704 8000 for advice.

Witnessing fly posting

If you see someone putting up fly posters in the borough, please tell us by calling: 0121 704 8000 and we’ll do our best to trace who is responsible and prosecute them.

We’ll need your details because we might need to take a witness statement from you, but your details won’t be released to anyone without your permission. In some cases, you might be asked to give evidence in court, but these occasions are rare.

If you see someone putting up a fly poster, you should:

  • note the details of any vehicle involved, such as the registration number
  • write descriptions of the people involved and the types of posters they’re putting up
  • record the time, date and location of where the fly posters are

You should never confront those responsible for fly posting or put yourself at risk. Instead, call us on: 0121 704 8000.

Penalties

Fly posting is an offence that carries a maximum fine of £2,500.

We have the power to issue fixed penalty notices to people that put up fly posters and first offences carry a £75 fine. This is reduced to £50 if it’s paid within 7 days. However, the case will be referred to the Magistrates Court if the penalty notice is not paid within 14 days.
