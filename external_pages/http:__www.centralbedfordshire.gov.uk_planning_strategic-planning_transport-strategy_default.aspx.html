myjourney

Introduction

myjourney (PDF 3.1MB) is the new Local Transport Plan covering all of Central Bedfordshire.

myjourney sets out Central Bedfordshire's aims and objectives for transport over the 15 year period, and how we plan to deliver them. myjourney reflects national Government guidance on transport, local transport priorities, local travel patterns and how much money there is available. It is probably the most important transport-related document for Central Bedfordshire.

myjourney is very important to how the Council delivers transport services, improvements, maintenance and management. The priorities in myjourney sets out in what areas we will focus most of our effort and resources, and what schemes and services that you will see on the ground. In fact, without these priorities, many transport schemes delivered in your area over the last 10 years may never have seen the light of day!

Please email us on myjourney@centralbedfordshire.gov.uk, or call us on 0300 300 6516 to find out more.

Local Area Transport Plans

Local Area Transport Plans

The 10 Local Area Transport Plans (LATP) set out the key transport issues and concerns of local people, and a programme of measures through which they will be addressed.

They forms part of the Local Transport Plan (LTP) for Central Bedfordshire covering the period between April 2011 and March 2026.

The LATP's draw upon a number of different sources of information to form a robust evidence base upon which the most effective and cost efficient transport schemes can be provided in the areas including:

  • Consultation with local residents and elected members
  • Census data and the Central Bedfordshire Householder Travel Survey
  • Previous studies and reports including the Town and Parish Plans
  • Feedback from working groups, town and parish councils and other stakeholders
  • Future growth predictions and site allocations in the Development Strategy
  • Travel Plans in place at schools, workplaces and new residential developments

What we are doing

Major transport schemes

The work of the Transport Strategy Team goes beyond policy and strategy writing and encompasses a range of elements including the delivery of major transport schemes as detailed below.

Our work involves regular contact with major transport providers such as the Department for Transport, Highways Agency, Network Rail and the train and bus service operators in our area.

Roads

Woodside Link Road

The Woodside Link is a new road which will connect Houghton Regis and the industrial estates in Dunstable to the planned new junction 11a (between existing junctions 11 and 12) on the M1 north of the town.  Junction 11a will be constructed as part of the A5-M1 road.

A421: Junction 13 to Milton Keynes dualling

The A421 corridor provides strategic transport access between M1 Junction 13 and Central Milton Keynes via the primary grid network. It is also a regional/ sub-regional priority for improved east-west links passing through the City to improve access to the heart of England via the A421 link from A1; M1 to M40 via Milton Keynes.

Milton Keynes Council and Central Bedfordshire Council are working together to deliver the improved section of the A421.

A5 - M1 link road

The Department of Transport are responsible for building the A5-M1 link road. Central Bedfordshire Council has contributed £5m as well as brokering a £45m contribution to the scheme from local developers.

The 2.9 mile dual carriageway will run east from the A5 north of Dunstable to the M1 at a new junction (J11a) improving journey time reliability and safety for those travelling on the A5 in the southern part of Central Bedfordshire.

Rail

East West Rail

East West Rail is a major project to establish a strategic railway connecting East Anglia with Central, Southern and Western England.

The 'Western Section' is now a committed, funded scheme to re-introduce passenger and freight services between Bedford and Oxford, Milton Keynes and Aylesbury. It involves upgrading and reconstructing sections of existing and 'mothballed' rail track, which is to be delivered by Network Rail.

The project is being promoted by the East West Rail Consortium – a group of local authorities and businesses with an interest in improving access to and from East Anglia and the Milton Keynes South Midlands growth area.

East Coast Mainline level crossing improvement programme

Network Rail has recently embarked upon a series of feasibility studies to explore options for the closure of various level crossings on the East Coast Main Line (ECML) between London and Doncaster.

Options may include simple closures, diversions of public and private rights of way in locations where a suitable alternative route already exists; or where this is not feasible, provision of new replacement infrastructure, either online (at the existing crossing) or offline solutions (further from the existing crossing) such as bridges or new footpaths, bridleways or road links designed to enable users to continue crossing the rail line with improved safety and connectivity.

The Luton Dunstable Busway

A unique and pioneering project, between Central Bedfordshire Council and Luton Borough Council. The Luton Dunstable Busway is the second longest Busway in the world and the longest in an urban environment.  The 6.1 miles of segregated bus-only road, a concrete track has been built along a 4.8-mile section to take the two small guide wheels fitted at the front of standard buses, so vehicles can be guided along smoothly and hands-free at speeds of up to 50mph. It also opens up a wealth of opportunities for speedy and hassle free journeys to work, university, college or school, shopping, the theatre or just catching up with friends.

See the Busway website for more details.

Householder Travel Survey 2014

In April of this year, we undertook a Householder Travel Survey of 2,000 local residents. It looked at transport use, people’s attitudes and experiences of transport and their travel patterns to places like town centres, places of work, education and healthcare provision. This was to provide a really comprehensive picture of what local people think about the transport network.

You can read the full survey here but some of the results include:

  • the majority of residents agree that good quality transport is important to them, and that investing in travel and transport and encouraging sustainable travel should be a priority for the Council
  • residents tend to not have a problem with transport related issues in their local area, although a notable minority think that some issues are serious or very serious problems especially a lack of car parking and speeding traffic
  • the majority of residents would look online when planning a journey using public transport
  • there has been a slight growth in car ownership between 2012 and 2014
  • a quarter of working residents travel 20 miles or more to work
  • the majority of residents travel to work by car, although those that live within 2 miles of their place of work tend to walk to work and many of those that travel 20 miles or more to work will use a train
  • the majority use of children sustainable modes of transport to travel to school such as walking or taking a bus, although car usage increases the further away the school is from home

The survey is the third to have been commissioned by the council and it has enabled us to compare data from 2010 and 2012. By building up a broader picture, we can chart changes in travel patterns and perceptions which in turn help to inform transport policies and schemes that we implement. It provides a really useful evidence base to make sure we are delivering the right schemes in the right places to improve the transport choices for local residents.

Travel Choices is a three year project funded through the Government's Local Sustainable Transport Fund (LTSF) to support the economy of Dunstable, Houghton Regis and Leighton Buzzard by providing residents and businesses with an improved choice for local journeys reducing car use, cutting costs and improving health.

Local Transport Plan Rural Area Matched Fund
What is the Local Transport Plan Rural Area Matched Fund?

The rural matched fund is a way by which local town and parish councils can fund transport infrastructure schemes in their area with the assistance of matched funding from Central Bedfordshire Council. This allows town and parish councils to deliver local priority schemes otherwise not prioritised as part of the Local Area Transport Plan process, and can bring extra investment in local transport infrastructure.

Who can bid?

The matched funding is being made available to town and parish councils. The focus of the fund is on the more rural areas of the authority, so the larger centres of Dunstable, Houghton Regis, Leighton-Linslade and Biggleswade are less likely to be successful if making a bid.

What contribution towards each scheme is expected?

A financial contribution of at least 50% is expected from the town or parish council. The higher the proportion of funding for a scheme provided by the town and parish council, the more likely the bid is to be successful.

Local Highways Maintenance Challenge Fund

The Local Highways Maintenance Challenge Fund was announced in the 2014 Autumn Statement. The purpose of the Fund is to enable local highway authorities in England to bid for major maintenance projects that are otherwise difficult to fund through the normal needs element allocations they receive.

Central Bedfordshire Council has applied for two grants under the scheme.

Local Highways Maintenance Challenge Fund - Footways. Renewal of Footways, Footpaths and Cycle Tracks in Houghton Regis. The proposed project will renew 80km of a total of 263km of footways, footpaths and cycle paths assessed as ‘structurally unsound’ within the Parkside and Tithe Farm wards of Houghton Regis.  Maintenance Challenge Fund Application Footpath Renewal (PDF 1.3MB).

Local Highways Maintenance Challenge Fund - Street Lighting. Upgrade of lighting assets within Central Bedfordshire. The proposed project will renew 12,749 life expired lanterns, 5,135 life expired columns and illuminated bollards with Central Bedfordshire. Replacement units will incorporate the latest technology and consist of LED light units to reduce maintenance and energy costs associated with their upkeep. Maintenance Challenge Fund Application Lighting (PDF 275KB).

More details on the fund can be found on the Department for Transport Website.
