Equipment and adaptations to help with daily living

You can use the online equipment assessment to help identify the adaptations and equipment that could make a difference to your everyday life. We know that each room in your home may present different challenges, and going through the process online allows you to identify those room by room. When you are finished, possible solutions will be identified for you to consider, with purchasing details if needed. This is free of cost to do.

Going through the process online can be helpful if you have problems like

  • getting in and out of the bath
  • get up and down from low places, like a chair/sofa
  • getting around the house safely.

If you need help using this online assessment, your local library can help, or you can visit your Surrey Hub. Trying this out is a great option if you pay for your own care and support.

If you are eligible for Adult Social Care, your care worker may ask you to visit your local equipment centre, or if you are unable to travel, an Occupational Therapist may visit you at home. The objective is the same: to identify what aids, adaptations and equipment is needed to support you living independently at home. Please speak to your local social care team for more information.

Organisations can access the Community Equipment Service.

Community alarms and sensors

Telecare – what is it?

Telecare is a 24-hour service using simple equipment to help potentially vulnerable people live more independently in their homes. The basic kit consists of a telecare unit together with a pendant for the user to wear aimed at providing quality services with a high standard of customer care, giving peace of mind to individuals and their families.

The unit is linked to a care centre by the telephone line and can be activated by either pressing the button on the pendant or remotely via another telecare sensor, for example, smoke alarms, fall detectors or pill dispensers.

Surrey Telecare

Surrey Telecare is a partnership initiative set up by the borough and district councils and Surrey County Council, to ensure residents can benefit from simple equipment that keeps people living independently at home giving family members peace of mind.

How much does it cost with the Surrey Telecare Offer?

All Surrey residents with at least one telecare sensor can benefit from the Surrey Telecare offer where additional sensors are offered free of charge to people who can benefit from them. There is a charge for the Community alarm, normally £4 - £5 per week.

Telehealth

Patients across Surrey with long term conditions such as chronic respiratory disease and heart failure are using telehealth to help them manage their condition more effectively and help them and their  clinicians  spot any possible problems as early as possible  so the correct treatment can be started as soon as possible. With telehealth the patient uses a range of simple equipment to measure things such as oxygen levels or heart rate. This data is uploaded via wireless and reviewed by specialist nurses. If the readings are outside normal for that person an alert is triggered and the patient contacted with advice. Where necessary the persons usual health care professional is contacted and will visit the patient.

If you would like more information about telehealth do please speak to your GP.
