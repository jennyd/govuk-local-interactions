Council and Housing Association Properties

To join the waiting list for Council and Housing Association properties you must join the Dorset Homechoice Scheme via Dorset Homechoice registration

If you already live in council or Housing Association property and would like to move, you may want to consider exchanging your home.

Council Tenancies

All council tenancies are managed by the Poole Housing Partnership, an Arms Length Management Organisation (ALMO), set up under the government scheme to bring investment for improvements to council homes across the borough of Poole. For further information please see contact details below:

Housing Associations

A Housing Association is a non-profit making organisation which provides accommodation either for people who can not afford their own home or who need special types of housing, for example elderly people. Associations usually have paid workers and are managed by a voluntary management committee. A list of sheltered housing schemes is listed in the downloads section.

Exchanging your home

If you currently live in a home provided by a social landlord and are looking for a new home then mutual exchange using Exchange Locata.

New applicants can register with the scheme, by completing one common application form. Once registered applicants will be able to bid for properties, across all areas within the partnership where they are eligible.

Applicants will need to choose one authority as their managing local authority (if they wish to apply to more than one of the partners), this is usually the authority they either live in or wish to live in.
