Dog Warden Service

Test Valley Borough Council has a Dog Warden and an Animal Welfare Officer whose work includes dealing with many varied dog related issues and promoting responsible dog ownership throughout the Borough. Below are listed some of the day to day tasks:

  • Education – visiting schools throughout the Borough giving talks on all aspects of dog awareness including health related issues. Taking part in Junior Citizen and Science Fair weeks. Rumble is the latest addition to the Dog Warden Team - read his story.
  • Patrols – talking to local dog walkers and enforcing the Clean Neighbourhoods and Environment Act 2005
  • Stray dogs – collecting and arranging kennelling for strays and arranging re-homing when necessary.
  • Advice – to members of the public on subjects such as problem dogs with behavioural problems as well as general responsible dog ownership.
  • Traffic light system – a pawprinted signage system in the parks and open spaces of Test Valley to help all dog walkers to make the best use of facilities provided.
  • Home visits – for national dog rescue organisations to assess the suitability and surroundings of prospective dog re-homers.
  • Talks – to bodies such as the Post Office and Housing Associations on dog issues that affect their working environment.
  • Local events – attending fêtes and fairs in the borough to promote responsible dog ownership.

Your local Dog Warden may be contacted on Andover 01264 368463 (Monday - Thursday 8.30 - 5.00 and Friday 8.30 - 4.30).  Out of working hours please ring 01264 368000.

Hampshire and I.O.W. Dog Wardens Group

The Test Valley Animal Welfare Team are members of the Hampshire and I.O.W. Dog Wardens Group. This group is made up of officers from all the Hampshire local authorities who meet quarterly to discuss issues relevant to their roles. The forward thinking group has become a voice in national dog related issues and is regularly consulted on changes in legislation and procedures. Follow the link below to see what Dog Wardens in Hampshire are doing and working on currently.

Agility Classes

UpAndOver - Whether for fun or the serious competitor, UpAndOver is a friendly, local club offering puppy classes, foundation, beginner, novice and grades.  All trainers are experienced handlers, with many years experience and Agility 1st trained.  All enquiries should go to Jane Price membership@upandoverdogagilityclub.co.uk tel: 07909 950745 www.upandoverdogagilityclub.co.uk.

Wag & Tone - Get fit and have fun with your dog!  A new fun dog class for owners and dogs aimed at building focus, concentration and to strengthen the bond between owner and dog.  Based on circuit training principles, to music, aimed to work both owner and dog.  Brilliant fun for all dogs - big, small, young and bouncy, naughty, old or lazy and for handlers of all ages!  Exercises are easy and low impact.  All dogs much be 12 months and over to attend.  Classes are on Wednesdays from 7pm to 8pm.
