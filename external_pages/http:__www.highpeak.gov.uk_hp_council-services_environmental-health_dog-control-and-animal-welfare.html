Dog control and animal welfare

Dog wardens and stray dogs

It is illegal to allow your dog to stray or to foul most footpaths, grass verges or open spaces. We employ dog wardens to encourage responsible dog ownership, and will take enforcement action, where appropriate.

If you find or lose a dog, please contact us and report this to our Dog Warden service.

My dog isn't a stray it's just escaped?

Any dog found wandering in a public place alone is classed as a stray and will be dealt with by High Peak Borough Council, under the Environmental Protection (Stray Dogs) Regulations 1992.  All stray dogs that are collected are checked for any identification such as a tag or microchip.

My dog is micro-chipped does it still have to wear a collar and tag?

Yes your dog should wear a collar and tag as this is a legal requirement and gives the finder the opportunity to return it to the owner without handing it in to the council as a stray.  Dogs must wear a collar and tag when in a public place and owners can be prosecuted if they do not do this.  The tag (or similar method of identification) should include your name and address and contact telephone numbers.  You can micro chip your dog so that it can always be identified.  The permanent chip is easy to update if your details change and can be read by a scanner.  It is advisable that the reading of a chip is checked by a vet once a year to ensure that the chip is operating correctly.

What should I do if I lose my dog?

In the first instance, you should contact our Contact Centre on 0345 129 4870. You will be asked for a full description of your dog, and details of where and when it was lost.  We may already have found your dog.  We keep a dog register which is updated throughout the working day.

We will endeavour to return the dog to its owner, if it is identifiable and the dog warden has not already taken it to the kennels a Statutory Fine £25.00 will be payable to the council within 21 days.

If there is no identification on the dog or we can not contact the owner, the dog will be taken immediately to our Council Kennels and kept for a maximum of three days, A release fee/fine & kennelling cost per night will be payable.  On receipt of payment the dog warden will arrange to return the dog to its owner. You will need to call 0345 129 7777 and make the payment via a debit or credit card or make alternative arrangements through our Contact Centre. Please let us know if the dog turns up, so we can update our dog register.

If the dog has not been claimed after 3 day's it is transferred to Manchester Dogs Home.

Manchester Dogs Home, Crofters House, Moss Brook Road, Harpurhey, Manchester, M9 5PG

Tel: 0844 504 1212

It is your responsibility to contact Manchester Dogs Home direct to arrange collection of your dog and you will be required to pay a fine and a release fee and any kennel costs incurred which is charged at a daily rate up to a maximum of 7 days.

What should I do if I find a stray dog?

You must either:-

  • Return the dog to the owner if it has a collar and identification tag or you know where it lives.
  • Inform us that you have found a stray dog and we will arrange for it to be collected as soon as possible

Please call 0345 129 7777.

Our Dog Warden Service operates from 8.30 am – 4:00 pm Monday-Friday

Should you find a stray dog after this time then you can take the dog to our acceptance point which is Manchester Dogs Home, we respectfully ask that you call the dogs home to advise that you are on your way so a member of staff can meet you on site. Stray dogs will be accepted at Manchester Dogs Home from members of the public at the following times:-

4pm-6pm in the evening (Monday to Friday)

10am – 3pm (Saturday/Sunday and Bank Holidays)

Christmas Day/Boxing Day 10am – 11am (By Appointment Only)

Manchester Dogs Home, Crofters House, Moss Brook Road, Harpurhey, Manchester, M9 5PG

Tel: 0844 504 1212   Option 1

 Please be aware that Manchester Dogs Home is a charity and we would ask that this is considered as their resources are limited and they rely heavily on donations and volunteers to provide a safe and clean environment for stray dog

What Happens to unclaimed stray dogs?

We have a statutory duty to keep the dog in kennels for a period of seven days.  If the dog is not claimed in this time, the owner loses all rights to its return.  The dog is then usually offered for re-homing.

If the Dog Warden still has the dog on the van, it may be possible for the dog to be returned before it is taken to the kennels, but this cannot be guaranteed as it is dependent on the warden’s workload. The owner must pay for the dog before the dog is returned.

If you find a stray dog which is not claimed, you may be able to keep it.  The dog warden will contact you, and you will be sent a letter outlining your legal obligations.

Safeguard your dog

Always make sure doors, gates and windows are kept secure.  This can sometimes cause a problem, especially during school holidays, as children may possibly leave doors and gates open. Make sure your fencing is adequate for keeping your dog in, thus keeping your pet safe.

Dog owners are legally required to make sure their dog wears a collar and with a tag carrying the name and address of the owner.  We also recommend that your dog is micro-chipped.
