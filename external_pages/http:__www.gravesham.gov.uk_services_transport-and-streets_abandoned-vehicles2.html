Introduction

To enable us  to remove an abandoned vehicle it is necessary to determine if a vehicle is truly abandoned or a nuisance vehicle.

What is an Abandoned Vehicle?

It is an offence to abandon a vehicle on a highway or on any land open to the air.  A person found guilty of this offence can face a fine of up to Â£2,500.

For guidance in determining a truly abandoned vehicle, please consider the following:

  • Has the vehicle remained in the same location unmoved for a minimum of 21 days.
  • Is the vehicle untaxed. (see below on the way to check this)
  • What is the general condition of the vehicle including tyres, wheels etc.
  • Is the vehicle dangerous, has it been left unlocked, broken windows etc.
  • Has the vehicle been vandalised

Report an abandoned vehicle

To report an abandoned vehicle, please see the 'Report an abandoned vehicle' section

An officer will then inspect the vehicle within 5 working days and if a vehicle is deemed as abandoned, depending on its condition, the council can issue three types of notices of removal; 24 hour, 7 day or 15 day notices. These notices will give the owner of the vehicle that period of time to remove it. If it is not removed by the expiry date/time of the notice the council will remove the vehicle.

Untaxed vehicles

The council do not deal with untaxed vehicles. To check whether a vehicle is taxed and to report an untaxed vehicle, visit GOV.UK - Report an untaxed vehicle

If you want to a report an untaxed vehicle visit the DVLA

You can check whether a vehicle has an MOT, visit GOV.UK - Check MOT history
