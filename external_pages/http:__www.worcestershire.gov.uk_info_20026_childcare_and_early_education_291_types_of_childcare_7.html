Types of Childcare

Out of School Care

Out of school care offers children of three years and over a safe and stimulating place to play outside school hours.

Key facts:

  • May be required to register with Ofsted on the Childcare Register, may choose to register, or may be registered as part of a school (see below)
  • Can run before and/or after school
  • In some cases will take children to school and/or pick them up at the end of the school day
  • Must have public liability insurance
  • May offer breakfast or dinner

Ofsted Registration for school-run clubs:

  • Out of school clubs provided by a school on a school site will be covered either by the schoolâ€™s Ofsted registration, or by the relevant registering/inspecting body if run by an independent (private) school.

Ofsted Registration for non-school run clubs:

  • Clubs that are caring for children aged between 3 and 5 for over two hours per day must register with Ofsted on the Early Years Register. You can ask to see an inspection report when you visit, or you can view them online at Ofsted
  • Clubs that are caring for children aged between 5 and 8 for over two hours per day must register with Ofsted on the Compulsory Part of the Childcare Register and may receive an Ofsted inspection.
  • Clubs that only care for children aged over 8, or only offer care for less than two hours per day, can choose to register on the Voluntary Part of the Childcare Register and may receive an Ofsted inspection.
