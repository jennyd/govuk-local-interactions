Disabled facilities grant

Grants for disabled facilities

Available to disabled residents to assist with the provision of adaptations, in order to ensure that they have greater access to, and use of their homes.

If you think you may qualify for this grant, you must first contact the Social Services Directorate who will arrange for the assessment of the needs of the disabled person.

They put forward recommendations to us, we will then evaluate the reasonableness and practicality of providing the adaptations or alterations.

Disabled Facilities Grants, which may be awarded to owners or tenants, have a maximum value of £30,000 and can be used for such adaptations as:

  • Providing Ramps and step lifts for wheelchairs,
  • Stair lifts and through floor lifts,
  • Level and low access showers,
  • Provision of a Ground floor bedroom/bathroom facilities,
  • Widening internal doorways,
  • Provision of hoisting equipment

Unless for the benefit of a child, these grants are subject to Test of Financial Resources to establish whether or not the applicant has a contribution to make towards the cost of the works. The test is prescribed by government and the council has no discretion over its use, or the results obtained from it.

If you are the occupant of a Registered Provider Property, (Housing Association) then your first request should be direct to your Registered Social Landlord.

Disabled Facilities Grants are subject to a Test of Financial Resources, to establish whether or not the applicant has a contribution to make towards the cost of the works. The test is prescribed by legislation and we have no discretion over its use, or the results obtained from it.

The Test Of Financial Resources is carried out on the disabled person but the parents of disabled children are not means tested.

If you are in receipt of means tested benefit such as Income Support or Guaranteed Pension Credit, it will not be necessary to carry out the Test of Financial Resources and your contribution is likely to be nil.

As part of an application for a Grant, a Test of Resources must be carried out on the disabled person in order to assess the amount, if any, that they must contribute towards the cost of the works. The applicant will be required to provide information about all sources of income, all savings and capital and produce documentary evidence to support the information given. 

The collection of this information and scrutiny of the supporting documentation will normally be undertaken as near as possible to the point of formal application: that is when the application form has been completed, estimates secured and proof of title or landlords approval is secured. This timing is to ensure that the information is current at the time the application is assessed.

Contact the Lancashire County Council Social Services Directorate for Pendle on 0845 0530009.
