First citizenship ceremony

This article was published on: 25-JUN-04


North Tyneside Council hosted the borough's first Citizenship Ceremony this week.
Six new British citizens pledged their allegiance to Queen and country and were presented with citizenship certificates at The Town Hall, Wallsend.
The Vice-Lord Lieutenant Lieutenant of Tyne and Wear John Fenwick joined Chairman of North Tyneside, Cllr Lawrence Goveas and North Tyneside Mayor Mrs Linda Arkley in congratulating and welcoming the new citizens.
In addition to the formal part of the ceremony, carried out by Superintendent Registrar Mrs Christine Lois, pupils from Wellfield Middle School sang songs.
Cllr Goveas said "It is a great personal honour for me to preside the at first citizenship ceremony in North Tyneside. Becoming a British citizen is something to be celebrated and I hope this ceremony adds to the significance of acquiring citizenship and marks the beginning of a long and happy association with North Tyneside."
