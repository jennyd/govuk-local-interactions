 Our Services 

  

Educational Psychology Service (EPS)

Educational psychologists (also called EPs or Ed Psychs) have professional qualifications in psychology and educational psychology – the study of how children develop, learn and behave. They help ensure that:

  • children with additional educational needs who are having difficulties at school receive the right sort of help; and
  • very young children, who may have additional educational needs and require extra help, get the support they need, either before or when they start school.

Each of the maintained schools in Hackney has an allocated EP who visits regularly to help with strategic planning and provision management.

Parent Advice Service

The EPS runs a Parent Advice Service for parents/carers from 2- 4 pm every Wednesday during term time. This Service is open to all children and families living in Hackney including those children not yet in school, those out of school and those children who live in Hackney and attend school in other boroughs. Parents or carers should telephone for an appointment on 020 8820 7519 if they would like an opportunity to talk about their child with an educational psychologist.

Inclusion and Specialist Support

The Inclusion and Specialist Support Team provides a range of services to schools, settings, families and children and young people with SEND:

Specialist Teaching

The highly trained team of teachers are qualified and experienced in meeting the needs of children with a wide range of SEND, including autism, dyslexia, speech, communication needs, behaviour, social difficulties and complex needs. They provide training, support and advice to schools and settings, as well as providing direct teaching and intervention.

Early Support and Area SENCO team

The team works with young children (0-4 years) who have special educational needs and their families and nursery education settings. They advise and support parents/carers and provide teaching and intervention to help development.

Visual Impairment Service

The service advises and supports parent/carers and children at home from diagnosis and continue to monitor children's progress when they enter early years settings and throughout school. They contribute to transition plans at important stages in the children's education including advice on transition to further education.

Deaf and Partially Hearing Service

The team consists of qualified teachers for the deaf and a specialist portage worker for sensory impairment. They advise and support parents/carers and children at home from diagnosis and continue to monitor progress when they enter early years settings, throughout school and college.

For further information about any of the Inclusion and Specialist Support services contact 020 8820 7326.

Integrated Speech and Language Therapy

The Speech and Language Therapy Service works with families and schools to develop and support children and young people’s speech, language and communication skills.

If you have concerns about your child’s speech, language or communication you can:

  • speak to your child’s class teacher and SENCO; or
  • contact us directly on 020 7683 4262 or sltinfo@homerton.nhs.uk.

If you live in Hackney, your child is under 5 years and not in school or nursery then you can access Speech and Language Therapy in Children’s Centres. Assessments are conducted via a drop in that we call ‘Talking Walk in’ held regularly across the borough.

If your child attends an Academy, maintained school or nursery in Hackney then your child can be seen by the school’s link Speech and Language Therapist (SLT)

The SLT will:

  • assess your child by talking to them and significant adults including yourself and teachers; and
  • put in place a programme of activities tailored to your individual child’s needs.

Portage

Hackney Portage is a home visiting service, which helps children with delayed development. Portage recognises that parents are the best people to teach their child and so relies on parental involvement in both the planning and carrying out of the child’s learning development goals and activities.

Portage caters for any child from birth to three years who appears to be behind in certain areas of development as well as children who have a recognised disability e.g. Down’s syndrome.

Most children are referred to Portage through the Multi Agency Referral System meeting that is held weekly at Hackney Ark. Parents can also refer themselves directly.

For more information contact the Portage Manager Fiona Jones on 020 8820 7588

Transport Solutions

We transport children and young people with special educational needs to and from school, using our buses and other sub-contractors.

  • Download the SEND Home to School Travel Assistance Guide

We also offer our services to other voluntary groups and organisations during school holidays and can provide transportation for other purposes including trips in and around London to swimming pools, museums, and other venues.
