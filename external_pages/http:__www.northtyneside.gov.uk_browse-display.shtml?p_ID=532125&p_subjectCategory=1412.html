Ways to receive your Personal Budget

How can I choose to receive my Personal Budget?

There are different ways you can choose to receive and manage your personal budget, to meet your eligible needs agreed in your wellbeing assessment.

You can choose to manage the money directly so that you or people you choose can have access to it to organise your own support. This is called a direct payment.

Here are the ways you can choose to receive your direct payment:

A direct payment paid directly to you. You are then responsible for ensuring the people providing your support are paid on time and that all agreed expenses and employment related costs are dealt with.

You can get help with all aspects of managing a direct payment from the council or we can put you in touch with organisations that can help you every step of the way.

A direct payment paid to a suitable person. With this option you are still able to organise your social care and support as you choose but you have the support of another person to help you arrange and manage this. A benefit to you would be that you do not have the responsibility of managing your direct payment.

A direct payment paid to a support organisation working on your behalf. This is called a managed account. With this option you are still able to organise your social care and support as you choose but you have the support of an agency to help you manage your direct payment. However there is a cost for this service which may be paid for by the Council or may need to be paid from your direct payment money itself, depending on your circumstances. In some cases the Council can assist you with paying invoices, but you would retain the choice and control to make decisions about your care and support services.

A mixed budget of a direct payment and support arranged by the Council or service provider. This is where you choose from the range of options above. It will help maximise your overall control in the areas you want but give you more support where you need it. This also allows people to try out direct payments, before deciding whether to move to a "full" direct payment.

Details about how you plan to receive your Personal Budget is set out in your Support Plan.
