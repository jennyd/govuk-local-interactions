Making an Application for Tree Work on a Protected Tree

This page provides information regarding how to apply to prune or fell protected trees and the procedure for removing hedgerows.
 

Once you have ascertained that a tree is protected an application form must be submitted to the Borough Council of King’s Lynn and West Norfolk. 

 

There is a standard application form  (Form No 26) with attached guidance notes available for completion. 

 

You can also make an application for tree works online via The Planning Portal.

 

The simplest way to submit a planning application is online via the Planning Portal

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Alternatively you can write to Environment and Planning, King’s Court, Chapel Street, King’s Lynn, Norfolk, PE30 1EX.

 

All applications must contain enough information and details in order for us to process the application, otherwise it may be returned to you to ask for further details. 

 

It is NOT sufficient to state “pruning” when registering a tree work application.

 

The applicant will need to specify one or more of the following:

 

Fell/Remove    

  

This is straight forward and needs no further details.

 
Crown Clean      

This is also straight forward but they MAY specify a minimum size of deadwood.

 
Crown Reduce     

This must be specified in a linear distance, stating the length (radius) of crown remaining after the reduction.

 
Crown Thin          

This must be specified in a percentage of the original crown to be removed.

 
Crown Lift       

This must be specified in a linear distance from the ground to the branch height, following the tree work.

 
Sectional work            

Identify sections of the tree to be reduced, thinned or lifted and specify to what extent.

 
Selected Branches   

Identify individual branches that are to be removed or reduced and specify by how much.

 

 

It may be beneficial to include sketches or annotated photographs to indicate the proposed work.  The tree(s) must be identifiable from the plans or description.

 

All tree work must comply with British Standard 3998 Tree Works (2010)

 

For a tree work application for work on a tree covered by a Tree Preservation Order  (TPO) there MUST BE A REASON GIVEN for the work.

 

If any information is missing or not provided in the required format, your application will not be registered as valid.

 

The reason for the correct completion of theTree Work Application Form is to aid the new 'fast track' appeals system.  Should the Local Authority Arboricultural Officer refuse any of the works applied for, the applicant has the right to lodge an appeal with the Planning Inspectorate so that the application can be examined by an indipendent expert.  The appeal decision will henceforth be made on the basis of the material submitted with the original application made to the Local Authority, together with any third party comments received at the time and following a visit to the site by the appointed Inspector.  No other additional written information will be considered by the Inspector so it is extremely important that you submit all relevant information from the outset. 

 

Please follow this link for the latest Guidance for Appellants.

 

There are some exemptions from requiring permission, if you apply to remove a tree which you believe to be:

  • dead
  • dying
  • or dangerous

These categories are open to conjecture, and professional assessment must be sought to determine whether a tree can be classified as such.

 

If you are unclear as to what may be appropriate or acceptable works, it is advisable to employ the expertise of a qualified arboriculturalist or tree surgeon, who can then recommend what works are necessary for the tree.  A detailed and accurate application can save a lot of wasted time.

 

What happens once we receive your application

 

Once the application is registered the Borough Council has 6 weeks in which to make a decision in respect of trees in Conservation Areas, and 8 weeks in respect of trees protected by a TPO.  You will receive an acknowledgement letter confirming this.

 

In order to determine the outcome of the application, the Borough Council's Tree and Landscape Advisor may visit the site to inspect the tree in relation to the proposed works.

 

In some cases the Arboricultural Officer will have seen the site previously, perhaps at the request of the landowner or their tree surgeon.  If this is the case, a further site inspection may not be necessary.  The Arboricultural Officer then makes a report on the proposed works, and decides whether to approve or refuse the proposed works as detailed on the application.  

 

If consent is granted it is valid for a maximum of 2 years from the date of issue.
