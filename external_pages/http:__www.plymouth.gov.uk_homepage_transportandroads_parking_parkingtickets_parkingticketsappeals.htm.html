Appeal a Penalty Charge Notice (PCN)

If you receive a parking Penalty Charge Notice (PCN) that you feel is unfair, you can challenge/appeal it. For further information read our guidance policy below.

Penalty Charge Notice guidance policy [235KB]

All PCN appeals must be made in writing which can be done online or by post.

Online

Appeal your PCN online. You will need to enter the PCN number (starting with PL) and the registration of the vehicle. You can't appeal your PCN online until the day after (working day) the ticket has been issued.

You will also be able to view our evidence of the alleged contravention. There is video footage for PCNs issued by CCTV and photos for PCN's issued by one of our Civil Enforcement Officers.

By post

Write to Admail 3313, Plymouth, PL9 7BF.

Please make sure that you write your PCN reference number on all documentation. Your PCN number starts with 'PL' followed by 8 digits (which could include a letter). If you do not have this information to hand please include your vehicle's registration number.

Appeal stages

There are three stages to the challenge process:

  • Informal challenge
  • Formal representation
  • Appeal to the Traffic Penalty Tribunal.

The process which a Penalty Charge Notice (PCN) follows is set by law. A flow chart showing the route that a Penalty Charge Notice will follow until it's conclusion and enforcement information is available on the Patrol website.

Informal challenge

If you receive a PCN either on your vehicle, handed to the driver or for driving in a bus lane you can make an Informal Challenge up to 28 days after a PCN is issued. If you pay your PCN or make an informal challenge within the first 14 days from the date of issue, you will pay a discounted rate.

Formal representation

If you do not pay or challenge your PCN within the first 28 days after it is issued, we will issue a written reminder called a Notice to Owner, giving you an option to either pay the PCN or make a formal representation.

If a PCN is issued by CCTV, the PCN will be issued to the registered keeper of the vehicle by post who will have the option to either pay the PCN or make a formal representation.

Appeal to the Traffic Penalty Tribunal

If we reject your representation, you can make an appeal to an independent adjudicator at the Traffic Penalty Tribunal. For more information visit the Traffic Penalty Tribunal website.
