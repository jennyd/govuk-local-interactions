        Vehicle crossings (dropped kerbs)

        A vehicle crossing (often referred to as a dropped kerb) is a specially constructed section of pavement or verge to enable vehicular access to a property.

        If you require a dropped kerb for non-vehicular access (eg for a mobility scooter or a wheelchair) you will not need to go through this process. Contact us using our web form and we will get back to you with more information.

        The cost of constructing a vehicle crossing varies depending on the contractor you choose, and the specification of crossing required, but will probably be somewhere between £1,500 and £3,000. Our licence costs £250 in total, which covers our safety assessment, construction audit and administration costs.

        10 things you need to check before applying for a vehicle crossing or dropped kerb.

        1. Are you able to pay for the licence and construction costs? Construction pricing varies depending on the contractor you choose and the specification of crossing required, but will probably cost somewhere between £1,500 and £3,000. The licence costs £250 which will be taken in two payments: £85 before the first site visit and £165 after site approval.
        2. Are you the homeowner? We can only approve a vehicle crossing with the homeowner’s consent.
        3. Do you require planning permission? You probably will if:
          • you are on an A, B or C road
          • your property is a multi-dwelling
          • you are in a conservation area
          • your property is a listed building
          • you need to excavate, demolish, build or do any other engineering work to create the parking area and access
          • you will be changing over five square metres of your garden to hard surfacing
          • you will need to link to a surface water drainage system.
            For more information about planning permission please see the government Planning Portal website.
        4. Do you have enough room to fit a standard vehicle without overhanging the footway? A minimum of 4.8m in length and 3m width is recommended. Any gates must open in towards the property.
        5. Is your proposed site far enough away from the nearest junction? You need to be 15m from an A, B or C road or 10m from a minor road.
        6. Is there good visibility? Walls, hedges or other obstructions may mean that we are unable to approve the vehicle crossing.
        7. Are there any utility covers in the vicinity of the dropped kerb? If there are, you will need to contact the utility company to arrange for it to be removed. Utility companies usually charge for this.
        8. Are there any trees on the footway or verge in the vicinity? If a tree needs to be moved or removed it may cost around £600. If a tree is removed, you will have to pay for two new trees to be planted in an appropriate place.
        9. Is the site clear of street furniture such as road signs? It may be possible to move the street furniture, but it can cost up to £3,000.
        10. Is the site clear of any parking restrictions such as parking bays or bus stops? It may be possible to amend restrictions but the process can cost £3,000.
