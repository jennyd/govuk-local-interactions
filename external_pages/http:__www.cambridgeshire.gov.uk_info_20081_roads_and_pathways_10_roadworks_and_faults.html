Report a highways problem

This includes flooding, trees, public rights of way, road lines and most other issues on the public highway.

If you feel the issue presents a danger to the public, please call the police 101. 

Please be aware that we will not repair defects below our agreed intervention levels: 

If you are reporting a pothole or road damage on quieter or residential roads, we will not repair it if it is under 50mm deep (about 2 inches). On all other roads, it must be over 20mm (about 1 inch) deep before we will repair it. For footways, we will not repair it if it is less than 25mm (1 inch) high or low.

More information, including the full policy, can be found on our policy and standards page.

Track a reported highways fault

If you have already reported a highway fault problem, please enter your reference in the box below. (Please note that you cannot track street lighting faults through this).

Each report allows you to see its status. They are:

Recorded

The fault has been reported and is being investigated. Within 15 working days we aim to decide whether further action is necessary. We may need to undertake urgent or more routine programmed repairs, or refer the issue on to another organisation, e.g. a water provider company.

Where no further action is considered necessary, the person reporting the fault will be informed and the report will be closed.

Scheduled

We recognise that your reported fault needs addressing and the work has been scheduled to be undertaken at the earliest opportunity. Timescales for fixing the problem can vary depending on the type of work required.

The person who reported the fault will be kept uo-to-date with the work progress as much as possible.

Completed

The scheduled work has now been completed..

Referred

The fault reported has been investigated and it has been determined that Cambridgeshire County Council is not responsible for undertaking the repair work. Consequently, the reported problem has been referred on to the appropriate party for their consideration.

Closed

The reported problem has been fully investigated and subsequently closed, details of what action has been taken will be provided to the person reporting the fault. It may be closed because repairs are not considered necessary at present or will be added to more comprehensive programmed scheme work, or other actions that means the issue is no longer an urgent priority to us.

Damage and personal injury claims

If you feel that damage to your vehicle or personal injury has been caused by defects on the highway and you intend to make a claim please complete the Highway defects insurance claim form (pdf, 151Kb).
