Street Cleansing

Blaenau Gwent County Borough Council operates a borough wide street cleaning programme. This consists of:

  • 4 Zonal teams (Each team responsible for a different area of the borough)
  • 16 Litter Pickers
  • 3 Mechanical Sweepers
  • 2 Quad bikers for dog foul cleansing
  • 1 Dog Bin operative

Our street cleansing team work incredibly hard everyday, working as a team to keep our Borough looking clean and tidy. We as an authority are determined to keep our levels of cleanliness to a high standard and to continue improving on our cleanliness targets every year.


In recent years, Blaenau Gwent has been showing improvement after yearly inspections were carried out by Keep Wales Tidy. In 2012, Blaenau Gwent was the second most improved council in the whole country, with our score improving by 2.3 points compared to last years. New initiatives, such as employing enforcement officers, has helped to cut litter levels considerably.


Dog Fouling

The Council receives many complaints regarding dog fouling.  Dog mess is unhealthy, anti-social, unpleasant, unwanted and a risk to public health.
Dog fouling is associated with a number of diseases, the worst of which is a roundworm found in dogs known as Toxocara.  This can be picked up by young children and cause stomach upsets, sore throats, asthma and possibly blindness.


If you own a dog, please be responsible and always carry a poop scoop or suitable bag.  This can then be deposited in bins provided throughout the borough. FREE dog bags can be obtained from most council buildings and public libraries.


The Council recognises that dog fouling is a problem in Blaenau Gwent.  We are installing dog bins throughout the Borough, and have a rapid response unit for cleansing.  Irresponsible owners who allow their dogs to foul public land face being issued with a fixed penalty notice of £75 or even prosecuted.
