Disabled facilities grant

If you are disabled and are finding it difficult to cope in your home, you may be able to get adaptations carried out to make your home easier for you to use.

You can apply for a grant, if you or a member of your family who lives with you are disabled, a resident of Hounslow, and either an owner-occupier or a private tenant.

A disabled facilities grant helps towards the cost of making adaptations or providing facilities to help you continue to live independently in your home.  Adaptations, which may help you could include:

  • grab rails for he bathroom or entrance

  • a ramp

  • widening doors for wheelchair access

  • a stair lift

  • a level access shower

All disabled facilities grant applications are considered by the council's occupational therapy service or Hounslow Primary Care Trust, who will recommend what works and what would help you most.

A disabled facilities grant can only be given if it is considered to be reasonable and practicable considering the age and condition of your home.

The maximum grant which we can give is £30,000. If the costs of the adaptations you need are over this maximum limit, it will be subject to the council's Discretionary Repairs Grants policy.

For further information about this type of grant contact Private Sector Housing Unit on 020 8583 3876/3877

E-mail: privatesectorhousing.grants@hounslow.gov.uk

Getting advice

If you would like some advice about help in your home contact the Adult Access Team, who can arrange to put you in contact with the Occupational Therapy services: telephone  020 8583 3100 or email: adultsocialcare@hounslow.gov.uk.

Frequently Asked Questions -

Who can apply for a grant?

A disabled person, who is resident of Hounslow and either owns their home or is a private tenant.

What type of adaptation can be grant funded?

The following types of work, when recommended by the occupational therapist can be paid for by this grant provided they are necessary and appropriate:

  • adaptations to make it easier for you to get in and out of your home

  • helping you access your bedroom or providing a new bedroom

  • helping your access the principle family room in your home

  • creating a bathroom or helping you access your bathroom

  • providing a toilet or works to help you use your current toilet facilities

  • providing or helping you use washing facilities

  • improvements to your home to help you prepare and cook food (only if used by the disabled person)

  • providing or improving a heating system

  • making changes to make it easier for you to use the power, light or heating in your home

  • making your home safe.

Once the occupational therapist recommends what works would help you, a technical officer from Private Sector Housing Unit and the occupational therapist will visit you.  They will evaluate a cost effective scheme and tell you about the grant procedure.

How will the grant be calculated?

All applications are subject to a means test.  A means test will take into account the circumstances of the disabled person who the works will help and their spouse or partner, to see how much you will need to pay towards the cost of the works. A parent's application for their disabled child under the age of 19 and in full time education (if applicable) is not subject to means test.

To apply for the grant, contact us.
