Housing Grants

Help to adapt your home

If you’re disabled you may need to make changes to your home to make parts of it more accessible, and generally make life easier and more comfortable.  
This type of work might include making changes so that it’s easier and safer for you to:
  • Get in and out of the property including the garden.
  • Get in and out of a specific room (this may include the installation of a stairlift for example).
  • Having accessible bathing and toilet facilities.
  • Prepare and cook food.

Disabled Facilities Grant

Depending on your income and the amount of any savings you have, you might be able to get a Disabled Facilities Grant to help you make changes to your home.
A waiting list may apply and grants will be prioritised to the people who need them the most. Depending on your income, you may need to pay towards the cost of the work to the property.
A Disabled Facilities Grant will not affect any benefits that you’re getting.
Disabled children and young people may be eligible for a grant without their parents’ income being taken into account.
You will not get a grant if you start work on your property before the council approves your application.

Eligibility

You or someone living in your property must be disabled. Either you or the person you’re applying for must:
  • Own the property or be a tenant (this includes private rented accommodation or that owned by a social landlords).
  • Intend to live in the property for at least the next 5 years.
The council needs to be happy that the work is:
  • Necessary and appropriate to meet your needs.
  • Reasonable and practicable - depending on the age, layout and condition of your property.
You will be supported to find out:
  • What changes are needed to your property (an occupational therapist will look at your circumstances and recommend the type of changes needed, this will be required for the application).
  • The type of work that needs to be done.
  • The cost of the work.

How to apply

  • Contact the Grant Referral Co-ordinator at the Care Trust on telephone 01803 219700.
  • Ask your Doctor to make a referral for on your behalf.
As the grant is means tested you will need to be able to prove your income and your household savings and investments.

If you need help to apply

It’s important that anyone who thinks they might be entitled to a Disabled Facilities Grant has the chance to apply. When the Occupational Therapists visits they will explain how the process works.
We understand that you may need or want some help with this. As a result we work with a company called Aster Solutions. They will fill in the form with you and work with the Occupational Therapist to make sure the work that is done is what you need. They also arrange for a contractor to do the work and project manage it for you. This will form part of the grant so will not cost you anything, unless you have a contribution to make in general. You do not need to use this service, but it is there to help you if you wish.

Aster Solutions

Aster Solutions is a company that organises and co-ordinates the works required via the Disabled Facilities Grant in the Torbay area. If you’d like to find out more about the company you can visit their website. If you’d like to visit Aster Solutions you can find them at 7 Dartmouth Road, Paignton, TQ4 5AA, telephone 01803 557566. They have a showroom there where you can see what an adapted bathroom and other changes may look like.

Appealing an unsuccessful grant application

You can appeal a decision about your Disabled Facilities Grant if you’re unhappy with it via our Complaints Procedure.
If you appeal and you’re still not happy, you can complain to the Local Government Ombudsman .
