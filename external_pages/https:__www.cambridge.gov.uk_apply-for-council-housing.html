    Council and housing association properties

    You must register withtheHome-Link websiteto apply for council or housing association housing in Cambridge. If you can't complete an online form, call 01223 457917 for advice.

    Home-Linkis a choice based lettings (CBL) scheme.After you join, youcan see available homes and place bids for them.

    Propertiesusually go to the bidder who has been in the highest band for the longest time.

    Sometimes there is a Local Lettings Plan which can affect who is offered a property. You can find a full explanation of bandingon the Home-Link website.

    Problems logging in to Home-Link?

    • Go to thehelp section of the Home-Link websitefor a reminder of your log-in or to reset your password.
    • If you need help completing an application that you started previously, read Completing a partial application [PDF, 202kB].
    • Here's a guide to logging into your application without an email address: Log in with no email Address [PDF, 1.2MB]

    Have your circumstances changed?

    • You must let us know if you've had anychanges in your circumstances

    What you need to know before you first apply

    Register via the Home-Link website

    • The online form takes around 30-40 minutes to complete. You can save the application at any point andcomplete it later, even if you aren't on your own computer.
    • You musthave alocal connectionto Cambridge city.The leafletHomelink local connection [PDF, 48kB]explains how this works.
    • You must be eligible for housing in the UK. Visit the CIH Housing Rights website to find outwho is eligible.
    • You have 21 days to provide us with documents to prove your identity and circumstances or your application will be removed. The home-link_proofs_2016_0.pdf [PDF, 116kB]lists the documents you need to provide.
    • We need details of all addresses for everyone on your application for the past 5 years, you may wish to gather this information before you begin.
    • If you have any arrears (including housing benefit overpayments), you'll need to make (and stick to) a payment plan. Details are in the Housing Related Debt Policy [PDF, 198kB]
    • The Lettings Policy [PDF, 229kB] explains how we allocate housing.
    • The Home-Link Partnership Guide [PDF, 0.6MB] explains how Home-Link works across the sub-region.

    Home-Link forms

    • You may need to complete additional forms as part of your application:
    • If you have health issues that are affected by your housing, you need to complete a medical assessment form [PDF, 1.2MB].
    • If you share care of a child or children with someone else, you'll need to complete theChild access pro-forma [PDF, 207kB]
    • If you are self-employedyou need to complete the Home-Link_self-employment_form.pdf [PDF, 135kB]
    • If you disagree withyour banding, complete and bring in a Request for a banding review form [PDF, 27kB]
    • If you are barred from joining the register, you can request a review of the decision. Complete and bring in a Request for Statutory Review under Part 6 [PDF, 119kB].

    Arange of housing options

    There's a significant shortage of social housing in Cambridge, particularlyfor people below 60 years of age.

    Home-Linklooks ata range of housing options.The registration process begins witha Housing Options Assessment.

    • Home-Linkoffers youa personal action plan.
    • The action planshowssome of thehousing options available to you, including private renting, social housingand low-cost home ownership.

    The information on this page is alsoavailable as a leaflet:Registering for housing on Home-Link [PDF, 122kB].
