Getting help from adult social services

We offer information and support to adults who need some help to live as well as possible with an illness or disability.

This can include helping you to get some practical support with things like getting out of bed, washing, dressing, getting to work, cooking meals and caring for family members.

If you, or someone you know, is having difficulties with daily living tasks or getting out and about you can get in touch with us for advice.

We can tell you about services to support you to stay independent, safe and well.  We can also put you in touch with groups in your local community and other organisations that may be able to help you.  

This information and advice is offered free of charge, and is available to everyone, either over the telephone, by email or in person at one of our offices.

Contacting adult social services

You can telephone our Customer Access Team on (01226) 773300.

You can call us on this number if you're a new customer or if you're already receiving some services. 

If you are phoning us for the first time we'll ask you some questions about yourself to find out more about your situation.  We can give you information and advice over the telephone and can arrange for a social worker to visit you at home if you need some more help.

We'll be launching our new adult's portal soon, for you to start your assessment online by telling us about your situation and the things that you need some help with. 

If you already receive services and your situation has changed, or you want to speak with your social worker, we can either put you through to one of our district teams or ask your social worker to call you back.

Having an assessment

For those who are most in need of help, we can arrange to carry out an assessment.  This is a discussion with you to find out what kind of help and support you may need.  You can have someone such as a family member or friend with you, especially if the person is already helping out with your care.

Personal Budgets

If you've had an assessment and you qualify for some help from us, we will tell you how much money is available to meet your care and support needs. This is called your Personal Budget and you can read more about it, including the different ways of managing the money here.

Other services to support you

If you do not qualify for help from us to arrange and pay for your social care needs, we will tell you about services that can keep you safe and prevent your situation from becoming any worse. Such as:

• Telecare community alarms
• A piece of equipment or a minor adaptation to your property

We'll advise you about how much you'll need to pay towards the cost of these. 

There are also lots of services in your community that can give you some advice or support and some of this is offered for free. You can find out more in our eMarketplace directory of groups.

Paying for services

If you are entitled to help from us to arrange your care, it is likely that you will have to pay some money towards the cost of your services.  How much you'll need to contribute will depend on your financial situation. To work out what, if anything, you'll need to pay we will carry out a financial assessment of your circumstances.  

If you don't want to have a financial assessment for whatever reason, we will have to assume that you are going to make your own arrangements for your care.  We call this self funding and you can read more about it here. 

We will give you information and advice regardless of your financial circumstances.  It is your also your right to ask for an assessment of your needs, regardless of your ability to pay for services. 

Are you a carer?

If you're helping a family member or friend, because they are finding it difficult to mange on their own, then you are a 'carer'. You have a right to have an assessment of your own needs that is separate to the person you're caring for.  There are local support groups. forums and information websites specifically for you.  Read more here about services for carers. 

From April 2015 changes to the way care and support is provided in England as part of The Care Act mean that you may be able to get more help to carry on caring, as well as looking after your own wellbeing.   Please ask us about services for carers.
