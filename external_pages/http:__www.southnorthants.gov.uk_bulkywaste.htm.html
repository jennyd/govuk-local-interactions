Bulky Waste

We can collect items of free standing furniture and domestic white goods from households for a charge of £30 per collection. You can have up to 6 items collected on each collection.

Our crews collect on Wednesdays all items must be outside of the property (e.g. front garden, drive, back garden etc.) by 7.00am. The crew are unable to enter your property.

What can be collected?

  
  • Animal cage
  • Armchair
  • Bed including headboard
  • Bike
  • Bookcase
  • Cabinet
  • Carpet / Lino (one room = one item)
  • Chair
  • Chest of drawers
  • Computer
  • Cooker
  • Cupboard
  • Desk
  • Dining chair
  • Dishwasher
  • Dressing table
  • Freezer (empty & clean)
  • Fridge (empty & clean)
  • Fridge freezer (empty & clean)
  • Garden furniture
  • Gym equipment
  • Hi-fi
  • Ironing board
  • Mattress
  • Microwave
  • Mower (electric/push along)
  • Piano/organ
  • Pressure washer
  • Push chair/wheel chair
  • Shelf unit
  • Sideboard
  • Sofa
  • Stool
  • Strimmer
  • Swing/slide/trampoline
  • Table
  • Television
  • Tumble dryer
  • Underlay
  • Video recorder/DVD player
  • Wardrobe
  • Aga or range cookers
  • Bathroom suites
  • Boilers
  • Commercial fridge or freezer
  • Doors
  • Fence panels
  • Kitchen units/sinks
  • Petrol Garden Tools (mowers, strimmers etc.)
  • Pieces of wood, rubble or stone 
  • Radiators or storage heaters
  • Sheds
  • Water tanks
  • Any other item which is not furniture or a kitchen appliance 

For information on what to do with items we don't take, see below.

 

Book a Bulky Waste Collection

Phone: 01327 322 344 
Payment can be made over the phone by credit or debit card, but please note there will be a 1.8% surcharge on all credit card transactions to cover the additional charges incurred by the council. Payments by Debit Card are free.

Post: Complete the Bulky Waste Request Form (pdf) and return to us by post with a cheque for £30.

Online: Unfortunately we are unable to take Bulky Waste requests online, but we hope to have this service available soon.

What about things we don't collect?

You can take these things to one of the County Council Household Waste Recycling Centres or get a private contractor to collect them (Skipandbin.com is a useful comparison site). Always ensure that you choose a reputable company that will legally dispose of the items.

Most of the items we collect will go to landfill, but are your items too good to throw away? If so, why not:

  • Give them to a friend or neighbour
  • Take them to Farthinghoe Reuse and Recycling Centre (pdf)
    (restrictions apply  call the centre on 01295 713782)
  • Donate them to charity - The British Heart Foundation have a collection service and large furniture store in Northampton
  • Sell them in a local newspaper
  • Take them to one of the County Council Household Waste Recycling Centres
    (restrictions apply  call Northamptonshire County Council on 01604 236617)
  • Advertise your items by visiting Freegle, Freecycle or even StreetBank. They are all internet based networks which aim to find unwanted items a new home. You can list your item on the local site and see if someone wants it. Just Search each of the terms in a search engine.
