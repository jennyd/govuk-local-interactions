The following information provides details to questions frequently asked about the Disabled Facilities Grant (DFG).

What is a Disabled Facilities Grant?

If you or someone in your home is disabled you may qualify for help towards the cost of providing adaptations and facilities, so that you or the disabled person can continue to live in your home.

These grants are administered by local authorities on behalf of the Government. If you do not live in Woking borough, you should contact your own local authority.

The Department for Communities and Local Government (DCLG) publication Disabled Facilities Grant will give you more information on how the scheme works.

Back to top

Who can apply for a Disabled Facilities Grant?

You can apply for a grant if you are:

  • an owner or tenant of a property; or
  • a landlord on behalf of a disabled tenant.

The person who applies for the grant does not have to be the disabled person who needs the adaptations.

The person applying for the grant will be asked to sign a certificate stating that the disabled person intends to occupy the home being adapted for the next five years.

Whoever applies for the grant must produce a certificate of ownership or tenancy for the property the work is to take place in.

Back to top

What type of work is eligible for a Disabled Facilities Grant?

Disabled Facilities Grants are given for essential adaptations that give a disabled person better freedom of movement around and use of their home.

The DCLG publication Disabled Facilities Grant will give you more information on the type of work that can be carried out using a Disabled Facilities Grant.

When a grant is available but the cost of the work exceeds the maximum grant available (£30,000), it may be possible to access other sources of funding.

Does the Council have to give me a Disabled Facilities Grant?

Yes, if supported by an Occupational Therapist report confirming that the work is:

  • necessary
  • appropriate

and the Council are happy that the work is:

  • reasonable
  • practicable.

The Council can decline a grant application if it believes the scheme does not fit these criteria.

Back to top

How is the Disabled Facilities Grant calculated?

The grant is means tested and financial eligibility is determined by the combined income and capital (including shares and other forms of investment) of:

  • the disabled person, and
  • the disabled persons spouse or partner (if applicable).

A grant where the adaptations are for a disabled child is not means tested.

The means test will take into account the average weekly income and any savings above a set amount and will calculate the contribution that you would be expected to pay towards the cost of the work recommended by the Occupational Therapist.

If you would like to find out whether you may be eligible for financial assistance, please complete our Preliminary assessment form.

Back to top

How do I apply for a Disabled Facilities Grant?

Please contact the Homelink team at the Council's Civic Offices by telephone or email homelink@woking.gov.uk , providing as much detail as possible.

Grant funding is not available for work that has already commenced or been completed before the Council has formally approved your grant application.

Back to top

How will the Disabled Facilities Grant be paid?

In most cases the grant is usually paid directly to the contractor who carried out the work. It will only be paid when the Council is satisfied that the work has been satisfactorily completed in accordance with the grant approval and on receipt of an invoice, appropriate safety certificates, warranties and completion notices.

Back to top

Will I have to pay the grant back?

If you are in receipt of a Disabled Facilities Grant (DFG) over £5,000, a condition of the grant will require repayment of a proportion of the grant in certain circumstances.

In these cases, the Council will register a Local Land Charge against your property for the repayment. More information can be found on our information sheet.

Back to top

What other assistance is available?

For small scale work you should contact Homelink. It may be possible to access a loan or grant to help pay for such work.

If you have an enquiry regarding energy efficiency measures, for example insulation, boilers or solar panels, you could contact Action Surrey to see if they can assist.

More informationcan be found in the Housing Assistance Policy.
