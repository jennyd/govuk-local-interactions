Asbestos

Asbestos is a naturally occurring mineral that has been a popular building material since the 1950s. It has been widely used as an insulator (to keep in heat and keep out cold), has excellent fire protection properties and protects against corrosion

If your property is more than 15 years old, it's possible that it contains materials made from asbestos.

Asbestos becomes a health hazard when the asbestos fibres are released into the air and inhaled. Inhaled fibres can cause asbestosis, lung cancer and mecathelioma; a cancer of the inner lining of the chest or abdominal wall.

Common locations of asbestos are listed below but there may be others in your home - so if in doubt leave well alone and seek expert help.

The Health and Safety Executive have guidance and advice on how to deal with asbestos on their website.

Asbestos in residential properties

If the property you live in was built before 2000 then it is likely that asbestos is present in some parts of the building.

Don't panic if you think you have asbestos in your home - it's usually only a problem if it's disturbed or damaged. The general rule is to always leave asbestos alone.

Common locations of asbestos cement

  • garage or shed roofs

  • shed and garage wall panels

  • drain pipes

  • insulation board soffit, infill panels and partitions

  • bath panels

Privately owned or rented property

If you are the home owner, it is your responsibility to safely dispose of asbestos from your premises. If your home is rented, your landlord may have relevant information.

Always seek advice before thinking of removing asbestos. Do not attempt to remove asbestos lagging, spray coatings or large areas of insulation board by yourself as these materials can only be safely removed by a licensed contractor. The Asbestos Removal Contractors Association (ARCA) can provide more information on authorised contractors that can remove it.

Disposal of asbestos waste

Do not place asbestos waste into dustbins -  it will not be collected.  You can either dispose of it at a site licensed to accept it or arrange for authorised commercial operator to come and collect it.  You cannot dispose of asbestos waste at Space Waye Re-use and Recycling Site.

The City of London operates a hazardous waste collection service on behalf of London boroughs, providing it is wrapped and sealed in heavy duty plastic - please see its website for more details.

Residents can also take their waste to the West London Waste Site at Victoria Road, South Ruislip, HA4 0YS providing it is douible wrapped and sealed in heavy duty plastic.

If you intend to transport asbestos cement waste in your own vehicle, you do not need to be a registered carrier provided that it is your own waste.  However, you should take the following steps to ensure that asbestos does not contaminate your car:

  • Spray sheets of asbestos cement with water.

  • Double wrap or double bag the asbestos cement with heavy duty polythene.

  • Ensure the wrapping is secured with tape.

  • Make sure vehicle occupants do not have access to the asbestos waste.

For more information about disposing of hazardous waste, including Asbestos, please see our Disposing of Hazardous Waste page.

Council owned houses

If you live in a property owned and managed by the council and have any concerns about asbestos, you can contact the Hounslow Homes Customer Service Centre on 0800 085 65 75.

Visit their Asbestos page for further information.

Asbestos in commercial premises

Asbestos is the largest single cause of fatal disease and ill health caused by work in Great Britain. Therefore, asbestos in the workplace is subject to the legal requirements of the Health and Safety at Work, etc., Act 1974 and the Control of Asbestos Regulations 2012.

If you decide to have asbestos removed from your premises, this must, in general be carried out by a contractor licensed by the Health and Safety Executive. For most work, the licensed contractor will have to give the enforcing authority i.e. Health and Safety Executive or the local council, 14 days notice of the removal, although; in certain cases this requirement can be waived.

For more information, contact our Health & Safety (Commercial) team on
email: healthandsafety@hounslow.gov.uk or call 020 8583 5555.
