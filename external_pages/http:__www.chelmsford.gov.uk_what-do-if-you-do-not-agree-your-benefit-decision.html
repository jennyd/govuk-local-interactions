What to do if you do not agree with your benefit decision

    If you disagree with a decision about your Housing Benefit, or Council Tax Support, you can ask us to look at that decision again.

     

    Housing Benefit and Council Tax Support have different procedures about what to do if you do not agree with a decision. If you tell us you do not agree with your Housing Benefit decision we will not look at decisions about your Council Tax Support as well, you must tell us separately about that. Please note the our Council Tax Reduction Scheme will not change for the year April 2015 to March 2016.

     

    We cannot look at some things that are defined in law or Council Tax Support policy such as  the Local Housing Allowance rates for the area you live in, the amount that the government says you need to live on, whether you are of working age, or how much under occupancy charge must be applied to your rent. 

     

    Here are some of the things you can ask us to look at again:

     

    • Number of bedrooms you and your family need
    • Amount of earnings or other income used in the calculation
    • Refusal to backdate your housing benefit claim

     

     

    If you think our decision about your Housing Benefit is wrong you can

     

    • Ask us to explain the decision,
    • Ask us to look at the decision again (we call this a review or reconsideration)
    • Make an appeal in writing giving the reason why you disagree with our decision. 

     

    Your request for the above must be in writing and you must tell us why you think it is wrong and provide any supporting information and/or evidence. You must request a review within one month of getting your decision notification letter. If we do not get your request in writing within one month, then we may not be able to look at it again.

     

    If you have asked us to look again at a decision we have made we may change that decision either in full or in part,  we will write and tell you about the new decision.  If we cannot or will not change our decision we will write and tell you the reasons why.

     

    If you still do not agree with our decision after we have looked at it again, you can appeal in writing about a housing and/or Council Tax benefit decision giving your reasons in full within one month. If we cannot or will not change our decision, it will be referred to an independent appeals service.  

     

     

    If you think our decision about your Council Tax Support is wrong you can

     

    • Ask us to explain the decision,
    • Ask us to look at the decision again (we call this a review or reconsideration)

     

    Your request for the above must be in writing and you must tell us why you think it is wrong and provide any supporting information and/or evidence.

     

    If you have asked us to look again at a decision we have made we may change that decision either in full or in part, we will write and tell you about the new decision. If we cannot or will not change our decision we will write and tell you the reasons why.

     

    If you still do not agree with our decision after we have looked at it again, you can appeal in writing about a Council Tax Support decision giving your reasons in full within two months of our decision to the Valuation Tribunal. You must contact the Valuation Tribunal if you wish to appeal a decision about your Council Tax Support - we will not do this for you.

    Please send your written requests to:

     

     

    In Person: The enquiry counter is open between  Monday to Friday 8.45am to 4.45pm, at the address below. 10am on the last Wednesday of every month.

     

    In Person: Parkside Community Hub,  Melbourne Avenue, Chelmsford, Essex, CM1 2DX every Wednesday 9.30am to 2pm.

     

    In Person: The South Woodham Ferrers Customer Service Centre

     

     

    By Post: Chelmsford Council, Financial Services, Civic Centre, Duke Street, Chelmsford, CM1 1JE.

     

    Out hours service: From 5pm to 6.30pm Monday to Friday and 9am to 5.30pm, Saturday 9am to 5.30pm and Sunday 1pm to 4pm at Chelmsford Central Library.
