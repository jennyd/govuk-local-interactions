School transport

Your children may be able to get free transport to school, depending on how far the walk is.

All children between 5 and 16 qualify for free school transport if they go to their nearest suitable school and live at least:

  • 2 miles from the school if they’re under 8
  • 3 miles from the school if they’re 8 or older

If there’s no safe walking route, they must be given free transport, however far from school they live. Call the school transport team on 01925 443322 if you think that the walk to school isn’t safe.

Read the Home to School Transport Policy [pdf] for more information.

Families on low income

Your child may also be able to get free transport to school if you get the maximum Working Tax Credit, or if your child qualifies for free school meals and fits into one of the following categories:

  • aged 8 to 11 and the school's at least 2 miles away
  • aged 11 to 16 and the school's 2 to 6 miles away - as long as there aren't 3 or more suitable schools nearer to home
  • aged 11 to 16 and the school's 2 to 15 miles away - if it's their nearest school preferred on the grounds of religion or belief

Students 17 and over

We may make arrangements to subsidise transport for students who attend sixth form or college but only under exceptional circumstances, such as when the family has limited resources to get their child to school. Resources include:

  • lack of finances
  • no family or friends to take the child to school
  • no access to a vehicle 

If your child is not eligible for subsidised transport or you believe that they should receive additional support than the council would generally provide then you'll need to demonstrate their exceptional circumstances on your application form [pdf]. Please be aware that each application will be individually assessed by the SEN Transport Panel.

View the latest policy statement for student and learners in further education [pdf] for more information.

When and how to apply

Apply before 31 July as late applications may result in your pass being issued late or difficulties in arranging transport.

To apply for a school transport pass, you can:

  • Print off the school transport application form [pdf] complete it and send it back to us. 
  • Pick up a paper form from Contact Warrington, complete it and send it back to us. 

A passport size photograph is required for all new applications.  For more information, call the school transport team on 01925 443322.
