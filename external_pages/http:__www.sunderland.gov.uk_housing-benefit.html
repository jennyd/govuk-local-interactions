Housing benefit claims

  • facebook
  • twitter
  • google+
  • email

Jump to:

  • Related links

If you are on a low income you may receive support to help you pay your rent.

You can claim Housing Benefit if you or your partner pay rent for your property and are on a low income or claiming any of the following benefits:

  • Income Support
  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance
  • Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance
  • Guarantee part of Pension Credit

However, even if you are claiming one of the above benefits and have savings of more than £16,000, you are unlikely to be able to claim Housing Benefit.

You can check your benefit entitlement to see if you should claim (see 'Do it online').

You can claim Housing Benefit online. When completing the form it is best to have details of any savings or bank accounts and your National Insurance number to hand.

If you are a full time student, you will not get Housing Benefit unless you are disabled or have a child.

If you rent your home from a close relative, you may have to provide additional evidence that you have not contrived your tenancy agreement in order to take advantage of the benefit system.

If you are an asylum seeker, you cannot claim Housing Benefit. Find out more about the support available to asylum seekers.

If you rent your home from a private landlord, the amount of money you get will be based on the Local housing allowance (LHA). LHA depends on:

  • The number of rooms you need
  • The maximum rent allowed for properties in your area
  • Your age

If you are under 35, you may be able to get a higher rate of housing benefit if you are:

  • Aged under 22 and subject to a social services care order, or you were formerly provided with accommodation by social services
  • Your Housing Benefit, Income Support or Jobseeker's Allowance includes the Severe Disability Premium
  • Any non dependants living with you (for example, an older relative lives with you)
  • You require an extra room for a non resident carer
  • You are an ex-offender, aged over 25 and you are being managed through Multi-Agency Public Protection Arrangements (MAPPA)
  • If you are aged over 25 and have previously been homeless - to qualify you must have spent at least three months in a hostel or hostels for the rehabilitation or resettlement of homeless people

Even if you get the maximum amount of Housing Benefit, it may not necessarily cover all of your rent. If this is the case, you will have to make up the difference yourself or find less expensive accommodation. Our Housing advice pages could help you to find alternative accommodation.


What evidence do I have to provide?

Before we can award benefit for new or renewal claims you must provide evidence of all information you declare on your application form.

Where applicable, we will need to see evidence of:

  • Identity and National Insurance numbers
  • Income and earnings
  • Savings and capital
  • Household details
