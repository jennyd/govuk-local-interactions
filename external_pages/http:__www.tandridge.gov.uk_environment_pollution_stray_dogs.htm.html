Stray dogs

If you have lost a dog

Please fill in and submit the stray or lost dog reporting form or call Customer Services on 01883 722000 during office hours (8.30am to 5pm Monday to Thursday and 8.30am to 4.30pm Friday).

Found a stray dog during office hours?

The Council provides a service to collect stray dogs. As it is not a dog catching service, the dog must be tethered or otherwise confined so that the Dog Warden can collect it.

If you have found a stray dog, please fill in and submit the stray or lost dog reporting form or call Customer Services on 01883 722000 during office hours (8.30am to 5pm Monday to Thursday and 8.30am to 4.30pm Friday).

Found a stray dog outside office hours? 

We have to provide somewhere the public can take stray dogs out of normal office hours. Anyone who finds a stray dog should call the Council’s out of hours stray dog contractor on 07702509949 to arrange to meet them at Godstone Fire Station to hand the dog over. 

Alternatively you can look after the stray dog but you must report the find on the next normal working day.

The out of hours stray dog service operates.

  • Monday-Thursday 5pm–8.30am.
  • Friday 4.30pm to 8.30am Monday morning.
  • All hours on bank holidays.

The out of hours stray dog contractor's telephone number is not available during normal office hours.

What happens to the dogs we find?

The Council will make reasonable attempts to notify the owner that they have seven days from the time the dog was picked up to claim the dog and pay all costs. If the dog is claimed and the charges paid, the dog will be returned to the owner by arrangement with the Dog Warden during his working hours. If it is not claimed after seven days or the charges are unpaid, the Council may sell the dog, give it to a person who may care for it or to an establishment for the reception of stray dogs.

Charges include the collection and return of the dog, together with any kennelling, emergency veterinary treatment and other additional costs incurred.

Dog owners need to bear in mind that microchips can only be read with a special scanner which people finding a stray are unlikely to have. It is, therefore advisable to ensure that their dog wears a collar and tag with their contact telephone number. If micro-chipped, the contact details need to be updated when the owner moves house or the keeper changes.

Dog owners need to realise that the longer the time taken to repatriate their dog to them the higher the kennelling charges incurred, simply providing your dog with a collar and tag with an up-to-date telephone number can significantly reduce or possibly even eliminate this potential additional cost.

What is a stray dog?

A stray dog is defined as 'any unattended dog in a public place' and a public place means on the highway or any other place to which the public are entitled or permitted to have access.

Dangerous dogs

A dangerous dog is either a member of a breed that has been defined as a dangerous dog in legislation or it is a dog that is out of control and may have attacked someone causing physical injury. Dangerous dogs should be reported to the Police on 101.
