Allotments

Allotments are the responsibility of your town or parish council except in Weston-super-Mare where responsibility lies with the allotment club. For more information visit the Weston Allotment Club website.

Beaches and the coast

Find out information about beaches and the coast including: tide times, Knightstone harbour and slipway, animals on the beach, seafront rangers, seafront safety, beach cleansing and water quality.

Closed churchyards

We maintain churchyards that are no longer available for burials unless someone has bought a plot. This includes carrying out grounds maintenance, tree works for safety reasons, minor repairs to boundary walls and path surfaces, grass cutting from April to October on a three-weekly cycle, annual tree and shrub trims, and a five-year rolling programme of health and safety inspections of memorials. Grave tops are the responsibility of the grave owner but we will try to keep unattended graves tidy during the winter where resources allow.

Forests and woodland

We own and manage a large estate of woodlands across North Somerset and ensure its protection for you and its native wildlife, working closely with parish and town councils, local interest groups and the Forestry Commission.

Memorial benches and trees

You can donate a bench to commemorate a person or event at selected parks and open spaces. Benches are strong hardwood seats with cast iron ends and you can provide a plaque with wording of your choice which we fix to the seat. We offer our benches on a 10-year lease cycle.  There are two options, either a refurbished bench or a new bench; download our memorial bench information (pdf) for details on our lease agreement, costs and application form.  For more information download our memorial bench FAQs (pdf) or contact our streets and open spaces team. If you would like to donate a tree please visit our tree page for more details.

Nature reserves

There are 13 nature reserves in North Somerset for you to visit and enjoy and we actively encourage visits by schools and colleges for educational purposes. We provide one hectare of local nature reserve per 704 head of population, exceeding Natural England's recommendation of one hectare per 1,000 head of population. We manage the sites by working closely with town and parish councils, Natural England, local interest groups. For more information about your local nature reserve visit the wild spaces website. Download our local nature reserves service standards (pdf).

Portishead public art

The Portishead public art project is based around development of the Port Marine area which has a long history of industrial use as a nail factory, a phosphorous works and latterly a power station. The old dock has extensive views over the Bristol Channel to Wales and the Severn Bridges. Download the trail map (pdf) and information about the artists and their work (pdf) for a full illustrative list of the artists, their work and backgrounds and to follow the trail around Portishead.

Ranger service

Our rangers maintain lifebelts, parks and play areas and the seafront in Weston-super-Mare. They make sure that these important open spaces are safe for everyone to enjoy. Find out more about ranger service.

Service standards
