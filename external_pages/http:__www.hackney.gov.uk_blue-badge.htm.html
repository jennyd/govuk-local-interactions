Disabled parking permit - blue badge

The aim of blue badges is to help disabled people with severe mobility problems access goods and services by allowing them to park close to their destination. Applicants must have a permanent and substantial disability.

Renewals and applications have to be made online at gov.uk. You can also check to see if you are eligible and find out more about how to use a blue badge.

How do I know if I am eligible for a blue badge?

The way blue badges were issued in the past has changed. The criteria for blue badges have not changed, only the process of assessment has. If you do not have automatic entitlement, you will undergo a mobility assessment. We might ask for your permission to get more information from a health professional or ask you to be assessed by an independent health professional. 

Get more information about blue badge eligibility from the Department for Transport. 

Apply for or renew your blue badge

Apply for a blue badge

 

Applications can take six to eight weeks to process once we receive all the documents. You should submit your renewal application at least two months before your blue badge is due to expire.

Submit supporting information

Once you have applied, you need to submit the following supporting documents. If we do not receive the documents within ten working days of your application, it will be cancelled. The documents can be submitted in person or by post to Hackney Service Centre. Address is on the right of this page. 

  • Tracking reference number
  • proof of address dated within the last three months (e.g bank statement, utility bill, council tax bill)
  • copy of passport or driving licence
  • one passport size photograph
  • proof you receive the higher rate mobility component of disability living allowance dated within the last three months (if you receive it).

What if I don't have access to a computer or internet access? 

A member of Hackney staff can help you complete the application. You can visit the Hackney Service Centre for assistance. You may have to queue. Address and opening hours on the right of this page. You can also use a computer or wifi at any Hackney library free of charge.

Companion badges

We can issue companion badges to blue badge holders who live and park in Hackney. They are designed to protect eligible residents against blue badge theft.

Fraud and misuse

We take blue badge fraud and misuse very seriously.The badge is for the badge holder only; it is a criminal offence to let anyone else use it when you are not in the car.

Personal independence payment (PIP)

The government has changed the disability living allowance scheme (DLA)  to personal independence payments (PIP) for people aged 16 to 64.

From June 2013, anyone who applies for disability living allowance through the Department of Work and Pension (DWP), will now be assessed under the personal independence payment scheme. Anyone who scores more than eight points in the moving around category of the assessment will be eligible for a blue badge.

People with mental health problems, epilepsy, blindess and deafness are not assessed for their mobility. They will still be eligible for a freedom pass.
