Asbestos Service Guidance

Lincolnshire County Council will accept white asbestos waste by appointment only at its Household Waste Recycling Centres at Whisby near Lincoln and Boston from 25th November 2013. The council is offering the service for residents to dispose of small quantities of domestically produced, bonded, white (chrysotile) asbestos.

Please be advised that this service has specific restrictions in place, failure to adhere to these restrictions will result in refusal to accept your asbestos at the Household Waste Recycling Centre (HWRC).

Restrictions:

  • All deliveries must be pre booked via Lincolnshire County Council’s Customer Service Centre on 01522 782070
  • Only white (chrysolite) bonded asbestos will be accepted. Blue and brown asbestos will need to be handled by a licensed specialist. (Please refer to the Environment Agency for details)
  • This service only operates within the Boston and Whisby HWRC’s.
  • Asbestos will not be accepted at any other Lincolnshire County Council waste and recycling facility.
  • This service operates by appointment only on Thursdays 2pm to 4pm and Saturdays 10am to 12pm.
  • Asbestos will not be accepted at the HWRC at any other times or days.
  • There is a 20kg limit on your asbestos deposit and it must fit within the bag provided.
  • Lincolnshire County Council will provide two bags for double bagging asbestos waste. You may only use the bags provided.
  • Your asbestos must be double bagged; you cannot fill both bags individually.
  • You are only permitted to make 2 asbestos deposits a year per household.
  • Asbestos bags must be sealed with the supplied cable ties and have the supplied tag attached.
  • Once on site, you must make a member of the site team aware of your presence.
  • You will be required to handle the asbestos out of your vehicle and deposit it yourself into the appropriate enclosed skip (this will be indicated to you by the site operative). Under no circumstances are site staff permitted to do this for you.
  • Tags and bags are numbered and linked to your details; tags and bags are non-transferable to any other residence and therefore must only be used by you or someone in your household.

If you feel that you cannot adhere to the restrictions set out above, we advise you to seek a licensed specialist asbestos removal contractor to handle and dispose of your asbestos for you.

How to handle and bag up your asbestos:

It is important that you have properly assessed the risk. Asbestos can be exceptionally hazardous to you and your family’s long term health and therefore you must consider the following:

  • Bonded asbestos is most dangerous when it has been damaged, therefore if you have a damaged section/piece of asbestos or if your asbestos would need to be broken up to fit in the bags, we strongly advise you to seek a lisenced asbestos removal contractor. Do not break asbestos up yourself - as this will release harmful fibres into the atmosphere.
  • Asbestos fibres and particles can easily come loose and be breathed in by you, family members, pets or anyone in the surrounding area. Enclose the area as much as possible and restrict the number of people in the area. Remember to place children or pets in another room as far away as possible.
  • Put Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) on. We recommend that you use a disposable breathing mask, disposable gloves and a disposable overall with hood. Please refer to the Health and Safety Executive (HSE website address can be found at the bottom of this leaflet) for full details.
  • Dampen the surrounding area so that any fibres or particles that could come loose are more likely to stick to the damp surface.
  • Place your asbestos into the smaller of the bags, being careful not to damage or break up the asbestos.
  • Don’t overfill the bag, you will need room to place your first bag and PPE into the second bag and properly seal it. If the bag were to split we would not be able to accept it under any circumstances.
  • Tie up the bag and place it in the second bag.
  • Remove your PPE and place them into the second bag, on top of the first one.
  • Seal the second bag using the supplied tag.

When bringing your asbestos to the HWRC:

  • Only bring your asbestos to the site of your choice on your booked time and day as we will not accept asbestos at any other time or site.
  • We recommend you bring your asbestos in a vehicle that can fit under our 6 foot 6 inch barrier in order for you to go directly to the asbestos depositing area as this will lessen the risk that you might drop and split your asbestos bag.
  • When on site approach a member of staff as soon as possible and make them aware that you have a booked appointment to deposit asbestos.
  • A member of staff will be designated to attend the asbestos skip with you; never attempt to deposit asbestos alone.
  • You will be required to remove the appropriately bagged asbestos from your vehicle and place into the appropriate skip. Do not under any circumstances leave your asbestos anywhere other than in the correct enclosed asbestos skip.
  • Please do not ask any of the site team to help you remove or place your asbestos into the skip as they are not permitted to.

Further guidance on removing, handling and disposing of asbestos:

We strongly recommend you read through the Health and Safety Executive’s guidance and specific task sheets for dealing with asbestos. This information is easily accessible via the HSE’s website.

The information provided by the HSE details specifics on many aspects of handling asbestos.

If you have a specific query with regards to our own procedures for disposing of asbestos, or if you have a problem with your bag or tag, please contact our Customer Service Centre on; 01522 782 070 (open; 8am to 6pm Monday to Fridays).
