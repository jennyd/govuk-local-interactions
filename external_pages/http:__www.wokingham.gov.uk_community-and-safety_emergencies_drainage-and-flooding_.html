Flooding and drainage

Flooding

See:

  • Flood warnings on the Environment Agency website
  •  Read our sandbag information sheet (PDF document) 
  • Call the Floodline on 0345 988 11 88 for help
  • Check our Borough Alert page for updates

  • Report a blocked drain or flooding

  • Expand / Collapse
  • To report a blocked drain or sewer call Thames Water on 0800 316 9800   or visit the Thames Water website
  • To report flooding use our report problems with roads form

  • How to prepare for a flood

  • Expand / Collapse

Start preparing today before a flood happens: 

  • Check your insurance cover
  • Know how to turn off your gas, electricity and water mains supplies
  • Prepare a flood kit of essential items: water, blanket, torch etc
  • Know who to contact and how
  • Think about what you can move out now
  • Think about what you would want to move to safety during a flood

Visit the Environment Agency website for more advice on how to prepare for a flood. Also read the Thames Region Drainage Byelaws (PDF document.)

  • Sandbags

  • Expand / Collapse

We have only a limited supply of sandbags. We can only give sandbags to the homes at greatest risk.

You can buy sandbags from your local builders merchants.

  • Permission to work near water - "ordinary watercourse consent"

  • Expand / Collapse

Any work near a river, stream, ditch, drain or body of water may require consent from us. This is known as "ordinary watercourse consent". 

To find out more read:

  • Information about Ordinary Watercourse Consent (PDF document) 
  • Activities that do and don't need consent under Ordinary Watercourse Consent rules (PDF document) 

To apply for "ordinary watercourse consent":

  • Check Ordinary Watercourse Consent guidance notes (PDF document)
  • Complete our Ordinary Watercourse application form (PDF document) - include the relevant drawings and calculations and a cheque for £50 made payable to Wokingham Borough Council

Local Flood Risk Management Strategy

We are responsible for managing flood risk from local sources.

Our duties include:

  • Developing and using a Local flood Risk management Strategy
  • Assessing local flood risk
  • Setting objectives for managing flood risk
  • Measuring how well flood risk objectives have been achieved
  • Providing information on how we will work with other risk management authorities

For more information see the publications below:

  • Local Flood Risk Management Strategy (PDF document)
  • Strategic Environmental Assessment (PDF document)
  • Habitats Regulations Assessment (PDF document)
