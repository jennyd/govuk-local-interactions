Report a problem with a pavement

To report a problem with a pavement in Norwich that is a hazard to pedestrians, such as an uneven surface or potholes, please tell us about it using the online form.

                                          
Report a problem with a pavement

What to do if you have an accident on the highway

If you have had a tripping accident as a direct result of a dangerous defect in the road, pavement or verge in Norwich, please contact us.
 

Your route to understanding highway claims (74 Kb pdf, external link)

If you have had an accident within a commercial premises in Norwich, please email ehealth@norwich.gov.uk
 

Please note that our environmental health team does not deal with civil claims. 

Road and pavement inspections

As well as inspecting all roads and pavements in Norwich at least four times a year to monitor their condition, the council also carries out further safety inspections of: 
  • the city centre every four weeks
  • major radial and ring roads every month
  • highways near large schools every month.
The inspections involve locating potholes and other defects and anticipating where they are likely to form. Repairs are then arranged with our highway contractor, May Gurney..
