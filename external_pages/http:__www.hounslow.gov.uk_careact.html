Care Act

Care Act 2014

The Care Act 2014 came into force in April 2015, modernising the way care and support is provided in England and making it more consistent across the country.

Care and support is the term used to describe help for adults of all ages with things like washing, dressing, eating, getting out and about and keeping in touch with friends or family.

Many of us will need care and support at some point in our lives, and most people will pay at least something towards the cost of their care. Any decisions about your care and support will consider your wellbeing and what is important to you and your family, so you can stay healthy and remain independent for longer.

Everyone’s needs are different. They may be physical or emotional. You may find that the support you need could be met by something going on in your local community, for example services organised by local charities or other support networks. Whatever your level of need, Hounslow will be able to put you in touch with the right organisation to support your wellbeing and help you remain independent for longer.

If you are in need of care and support, email: adultsocialcare@hounslow.gov.uk or call 020 8583 3100.

Carers

In England,millions of people provide unpaid care or support to an adult family member or friend, either in their own home or somewhere else.

‘Caring’for someone covers lots of different things, like helping with their washing, dressing or eating, taking them to regular appointments or keeping them company when they feel lonely or anxious.

A carer’s assessment will look at the different ways that caring affects your life and work out how you can carry on doing the things that are important to you and your family. Your physical and emotional wellbeing will be at the heart of this assessment. As a result of the assessment, you may be eligible for support from the council, who will also offer you advice and guidance to help you with your caring responsibilities.

If you need a carer’s assessment, email: adultsocialcare@hounslow.gov.uk or call 020 8583 3100.

Delay to cap on care costs

The government has delayed the introduction of the cap on care costs to April 2020. The introduction of the proposed appeals system for care and support has also been delayed.

Information and Advice - CarePlace

CarePlace is Hounslow Council's health and social care online information and advice service. It can provide you with information and advice relating to your care and support needs, as well as signposting you to local care and support services in your area. The links below take you to CarePlace, where you will be asked a series of questions. Based on your answers, you will be directed to parts of the directory or Hounslow website supporting the areas you need help with.
