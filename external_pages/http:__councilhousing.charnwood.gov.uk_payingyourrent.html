Paying Your Council Housing Rent

Rent and Income Management

Charnwood Borough Council's Housing Income team offers help and advice on paying your rent, how to tackle rent arrears and how to get help with paying your rent.

There are several ways to pay your rent and you will have a rent calendar in the Your Place folder so you can keep track.  Rent calendars for each new year are available on request.

The team will provide quarterly rent statements which are easy to understand. Please get in touch if you want a statement at any other time.

It can also provide a specialist Tenancy Support Service to help you maximise your income and reduce rent arrears and refer to a more specialist debt advice organisation if you have lots of debts.

Rent Accounts

Your rent account is a record of debits (rent charged) and credits (payments made) made to your rent account. This page tells you how your rent and service charges are calculated and how you can pay your rent and how to get help paying your rent.

You will be sent a rent increase letter once a year. If you have any questions about this, please feel free to call us or read the frequently asked questions at the bottom of this page.

Rent Arrears

Our rent arrears page has full details of how we deal with rent arrears through repayment arrangements and actions taken to recover debt.

The Housing Income Team works in partnership with The Bridge Housing Advice Service, Money Advice and Clockwise Credit Union. The Bridge provides independent advice to housing tenants regarding action taken for rent arrears. If you would like more information regarding their services visit The Bridge.

Clockwise Credit Union provides an instant access bank account, savings accounts and low cost loans to all Charnwood residents subject to their terms and conditions. For more information regarding their services visit Clockwise Credit Union. 

The Money Advice Service (MA) provides advice and support on your finances.  They also run surgeries in our office - please contact us for details on 01509 634666.

If you need to contact the Housing Income Team to discuss your account you can call us on 01509 634666 or email information@charnwood.gov.uk. You can also visit our offices at Southfield Road, Loughborough between 9:00am and  5:00pm Monday to Thursday and 9:00am until 4:30pm Friday. If you call in without an appointment you may have to wait for an officer to become available.

Money Advice

Our Financial Inslusion Officer and  Tenancy Support team are able to assist tenants regarding benefits which they may be entitled to and assists with making claims. They are also able to help you manage your budget using our Money Management Self Help Guide.

Don't let your debts get the better of you. If you are struggling to make repayments on your loans and credit cards, the sooner you get debt help the better. The Consumer Credit Counselling Service is a national charity set up to help people in just your position and unlike many debt management companies, they don't charge for their services. If you think they could help - a good place to begin is at CCCS

Debt Remedy - free anonymous, professional advice online.

We also have some advice on dealing with payday loan lenders.

In 2013 the Welfare Reform Act introduced big changes to the way benefits are paid, including Housing Benefit. The leaflet below was produced to offer tenants help and advice. Your Housing Income Officer will be able to help you with any questions or concerns - please call 01509 634666.

Other Costs

Former Tenant Arrears

This is rent owed to Charnwood Borough Council after a tenant has moved out. Please see our Leaving a Property While in Arrears page. 

Recharges for Repairs

Tenants who have damaged their properties will be recharged for the cost of the repairs. These charges are recovered by the Housing Income Team. Please see our Recharges for Repairs web page for further information.

Garage Rent

Please see our garage rent page for more information.

Home Contents Insurance

Tenant can apply for affordable Home Contents Insurance which can be paid for with your rent weekly. The policy offers tenants accidental cover for a slightly higher weekly charge. Please see our Home Contents Insurance web page for more information.
