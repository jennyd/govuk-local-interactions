Together for Twos: Free early learning for two year olds

View pages in this section

If your child is two years old or going to be two in the next year, he or she may be eligible for a free early learning place in Royal Greenwich.

Families receiving income-based benefits

Your child may be eligible if your family receives any of the following benefits: 

  • income-based Job Seekers Allowance (JSA)
  • Income Support
  • Child Tax Credit and/or Working Tax Credit (provided your annual gross income does not exceed 16,190)
  • Disability Related Working Tax Credit
  • income-related Employment and Support Allowance
  • support under Part Six of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999
  • Pension Credit Guarantee and Child Tax Credit.

Children with additional needs

If your child has additional needs, he or she may also be eligible for free early learning from two years old.

Find out more about whether your child qualifies

If you think your child qualifies, please contact your local children's centre or the Families Information and Outreach Service to find out more.

Early learning places are free at the point of delivery. This means that parents will not be charged for these places.

Two year olds who meet the criteria become eligible to receive free early learning places the term after their second birthday.

The table below shows when children become eligible.

When eligible two-year-olds can start free early learning places
Date of birth When they become eligible
Between 1 April and 31 August 1 September following their second birthday

Between 1 January and 31 March 1 April following their second birthday

How it works

Free early learning places are available for eligible two year olds across Royal Greenwich.

  • Places can be up to 15 hours per week for 38 weeks of a year.
  • The hours can be taken flexibly over more weeks where providers can offer this, up to a maximum of 570 hours per year.

Questions you may have

  1. Will my child be safe? All the early learning places are with qualified staff who are fully trained in looking after children and have been police checked. They are inspected by Ofsted - the organisation that makes sure that schools, childminders and nurseries are safe and are providing a good experience for children.

    Only providers graded by Ofsted as good or outstanding are involved in offering free places for two year olds.
