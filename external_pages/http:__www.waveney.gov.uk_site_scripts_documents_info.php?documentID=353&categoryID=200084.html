General information

There is a set charge of £37.00 for the collection of up to three items of bulky household waste.

Where possible, please leave items whole. If they have to be dismantled, make sure they are tied or taped up and flat packed. 

"Bulky household waste" refers to household items which do not fit in the wheeled bin, such as:
  • free-standing items of furniture such as suites, beds or wardrobes.
  • fridges, cookers or washing machines
  • rolls of carpet (tied or taped up)

These are all things that you might be expected to take with you if you moved house.

Arranging a Collection

You can request a bulky household waste collection online or contact us on 01502 562111 to pay over the telephone.

Collections are made by one operative using a tail lift vehicle, so please ensure all items are placed where there is vehicular access, such as on your driveway, in an unlocked garage or front garden.

Please label the waste items and keep them well away from property you do not want disposed of.

Your items will then be collected as soon as possible after payment so have them ready the same day if possible. Our average collection time is about five working days (one week).

We cannot collect from inside buildings, flats, halls, stairwells, landings, or similar. If you are infirm or immobile please make allowance for this.

Types of bulky waste we cannot collect

Waveney District Council cannot collect the following waste under the Bulky Household Waste Service but will be pleased to quote to collect other items, including:

  • loose materials such as soil (even if bagged up)
  • garden waste or clearance
  • paving or concrete
  • construction waste such as sanitaryware, baths, brick rubble or timber
  • DIY waste such as kitchen units or other fixtures & fittings
  • storage heaters, radiators, water tanks & boilers etc
  • house clearances

For the removal of more than three items, or items which are not covered under the Bulky Waste scheme, please contact Waveney Norse on 01502 527100 to discuss a quotation.

Disposing of bulky items yourself

Apart from waste paint, you can dispose of your bulky household waste at a household waste site free of charge. Please read our details on how to dispose of waste paint if this is what you need.

If you have any trade waste to dispose of please contact Waveney Norse on 01502 527100 for assistance. By law you must dispose of your trade waste in the correct manner and we can advise you how.

You can also take most other types of waste to a household waste site, provided you produced the waste yourself and not a builder or other tradesperson. Staff at these sites may refuse to take waste if they believe it is trade waste.

Suffolk County Council run a site at Hadenham Road, South Lowestoft Industrial Estate and contractors operate sites at Anson Way, Beccles and Blyth Road, Southwold, on behalf of Suffolk County Council.

For further information on household waste sites, including opening times and facilities, please visit http://greensuffolk.onesuffolk.net/recycling/where/ or contact Suffolk County Council Waste Services on 0345 606 6067.
