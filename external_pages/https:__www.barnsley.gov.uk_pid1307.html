      Information and Advice

      Barnsley Council is reminding residents who may be affected by heavy rainfall and associated flooding to make sure they make the right preparations to protect their property and belongings.

      The onus is on the resident to prepare; it is only where there is an imminent risk of flooding that the council's emergency response teams can act, such as providing sand bags.

      Even in this situation the council's resources are limited and the response teams will prioritise attendance to the most needy location, therefore residents must appreciate it may be several hours before there is any response to what starts of as a non-urgent issue.

      Where sandbags are provided they are given to protect ingress to the habitable part of each property, note this would not include garages, sheds, greenhouses or gardens for example, these would remain vulnerable to flood damage.

      This is why the council encourages residents to make their own flood resilience plans, not only for where there is a risk of a river bursting its banks but also from flash flooding where drainage systems can get overwhelmed.

      To prepare in advance of an incident caused by adverse weather, Barnsley Council's advice is to:

      • Visit a builders' merchants which stocks traditional sandbags
      • Check out products available from The National Flood Forum's blue pages, which lists various flood defence products
      • Look at the flood advice on the councils website (this page)
      • Report highway drainage problems on 01226 773555
      • Report sewerage problems to Yorkshire Water on 0845 1242424
      • In areas of known river flooding there are flood wardens and flood stores officers will direct you to your flood warden on 01226 773555 (9am to 5pm Monday to Friday)
      • Get information from the Environment Agency's floodline on 0845 988 1188.

      Cllr Roy Miller, Cabinet Spokesperson, Development, Environment and Culture (Operations) said: "I ask residents not to put sole reliance on the council in times of adverse weather and to make their own preparations to protect their homes and contents. The results of flood damage can be devastating.
