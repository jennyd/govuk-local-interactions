The Care Act 2014

Care and support in England is changing for the better.

The Care Act 2014 is the most significant change to adult social care in over 60 years. It will modernise care and support in law so that the system is built around people’s needs and what they want to achieve in their lives. It will give:

  • individuals and carers more control over their care and support
  • clarification of what individuals and carers can expect from the care system

‘Care and support’ is the help some adults need to live their life as well as possible with any illness or disability they may have and to stay independent for longer. Many people will need care and support at some point in their lives. Family, friends, the council or other organisations may provide this care and support.

The Care Act is framing work taking place as part of Transforming Lives, a new strategic approach to social work and social care for adults in Cambridgeshire; and the development of older people's services, a programme of change activities set up to deliver Cambridgeshire’s Older People’s Strategy (pdf, 966kb).

Main features of the Care Act

Most elements of the Care Act will be implemented on 1 April 2015.

  • A change to the way people are assessed – so that decisions about the help they receive will consider their well-being, what is important to them and their family, and help to plan for the future.
  • New rights for carers and people who pay for their own care (called ‘self-funders’) to ask for an assessment of their needs and the council’s help to access services and support to meet their eligible needs.
  • Provision of information and advice to everyone who requires it, not just people using services.
  • The right to a deferred payment agreement – to give people peace of mind that they will not have to sell their home during their lifetime in order to pay for the cost of their care.
  • Establishment of a Adult Safeguarding Board.
  • Ensuring there is no interruption to a person’s care and support if they move to a different area.
