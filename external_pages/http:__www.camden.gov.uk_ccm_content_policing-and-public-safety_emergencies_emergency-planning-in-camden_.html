Emergency planning in Camden

Emergencies and disasters can happen at any time, from fires to flooding, from toxic leaks to explosions, and from aircraft crashes to severe weather.

It can mean the loss of basic services including water, electricity and gas, and telephones. It can mean damage or threat of damage to homes and businesses by flooding, fire or blast. And it can mean that evacuation from homes and businesses may be needed or, conversely, that everyone must remain indoors for an extended period of time.

Emergency planning is the general term for the work that the government, the emergency services, the health services and the Council all do in preparing plans and procedures for dealing with any emergency that might affect large numbers of people.

In 2004 the Government introduced a new piece of legislation, the Civil Contingencies Act, to improve the country's resilience. The objective of the Act is to enhance our ability to respond effectively to emergencies.  It covers the way organisations, such as local authorities and the police, plan and prepare for these events.

The emergency planning and business continuity team was set up with the aim of preparing, maintaining and co-ordinating a robust and effective response to emergencies in the borough. The team works to assess a range of risks at both a local and national level.  The risk assessment stage enables the team to plan for the potential consequences of an emergency.  Through this process of careful planning, the team aims to reduce the impact an incident may have on Camden’s businesses and communities.
