Homelessness

This section explains homelessness as set out under the Housing Act 1996, Part VII, as amended by the Homelessness Act 2002. When you present as homeless, a member of the Housing Options Team must consider the information set out below when investigating your homelessness claim.

You can contact a member of our Housing Options Team on 01352 703777

You are homeless if:
You have no accommodation in the UK, or elsewhere, that is available for your occupation and you have a legal right to occupy
You have accommodation, but cannot gain entry to it
You have accommodation but it is a moveable structure, i.e. caravan, houseboat
You have accommodation but it is not reasonable for you to occupy it

You are threatened with homeless if:
You are likely to become homeless in the next 28 days
Written confirmation will be necessary in some circumstances, i.e. notice from your landlord
Am I eligible for help?

You may not be eligible for housing assistance if:

You are not considered to be Habitually Resident in the UK
You are not a British Citizen and/or have full rights to remain in the UK because of your immigration status, i.e. student, visitor

What is priority need?

You may be a priority need if:
You are pregnant
You have at least one dependent child
You have become homeless as a result of an emergency, i.e. fire, flood other disaster
You are aged 16 or 17 years of age
You are aged 18 or 20 if you were looked after, accommodated or fostered or are at particular risk of sexual or financial exploitation
You are fleeing domestic violence
You are vulnerable due to old age, mental illness, handicap, physical disability or other special reason
You are a former member of the Armed Forces
You have been discharged from hospital or prison

If you are eligible, homeless or threatened with homelessness and in priority need, the council will have a duty to provide you with temporary accommodation whilst enquiries are made into your homeless application.

Temporary accommodation
Temporary accommodation will be arranged whilst Flintshire County Council are making their homeless enquiries or following their decision.

Many agencies work in conjunction with Flintshire County Council to provide suitable temporary accommodation and referrals will be made to ensure the minimum time spent in Bed and Breakfast accommodation.

Bed and Breakfast Accommodation

This facility will be accessed when there is no other suitable temporary accommodation available.

Family Hostels
Two facilities are available for homeless families in Flintshire. These units are situated in Holywell and Oakenholt.

Refuges
Working closely with Delyn and Deeside Womens Aid, accommodation can be accessed directly by women fleeing domestic violence. Housing Advisory Officers will refer clients to a refuge facility.

You can contact Womens Aid directly on 0808 2000 247

If you are a male and are fleeing domestic violence there are no refuges available in Flintshire, we would recommend however you contact the Male Domestic Abuse helpline on 0845 064 6800

Support for lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender (LGBT) people experiencing domestic violence is available by contacting Broken Rainbow on 08452 604460.

Please note, it is not usual practice to keep those persons in an area where violence has occurred.

Mother and Baby Unit

This facility is designated only for females who are pregnant or have a child aged less than 12 months.

Hostels for 16-24 year olds

Several hostels can be accessed via a referral process for young persons. Support is provided to enable those persons into independent living.

Move On Accommodation
With assistance from Clwyd Alyn Housing Association and Bod Hyfryd Developments Ltd, the Housing Options Team have a number of properties in the area for homeless families. This accommodation can be anywhere within the Flintshire area and the property can be made available for occupation for up to two years.

How long will I have to stay in temporary accommodation?

If you remain eligible, priority need and unintentionally homeless, our duty to provide you with accommodation lasts until suitable housing is found, this can be either with the Council, Housing Associations or in the private rented sector. If you feel that the temporary accommodation that has been offered is not suitable, you are able to request a review of the accommodation by putting your reasons in writing first.

Do I have a local connection?
If you have lived in Flintshire for 6 months out of the last 12 months or for 3 years out of the last 5 years, you may have a local connection; you will need to check this with the Housing Advice Officer.

Local connection is defined as:
You are or were in the past, normally resident in Flintshire and that residence was of your own choice
You are employed here, (i.e. you actually work in the area rather than the area is the site of your employers head office) for 12 months
Family associations (i.e. mother, father, adult children, siblings) who have lived in Flintshire for the last 5 years
Special circumstances (e.g. special medical or support services only available in Flintshire)
You are fleeing violence or threats of violence

If you have no local connection anywhere, the council to whom you have applied to will take responsibility.

If you have a local connection elsewhere in the UK, Flintshire County Council may refer your case to the other authority.

How do I know if I have made myself intentionally homeless?

There are a number of reasons why the Authority may say you have made yourself intentionally homeless.

Examples are:
You have sold your property when it was reasonable for you to remain in it
You gave up your tenancy without a good reason
You lost your accommodation through your own actions, e.g. not paying the rent or mortgage
What do I do if I am homeless tonight?

Staying with a friend or relative may be the best option for you if you have nowhere to stay tonight. You can contact the team the following day to discuss your housing options.

Alternatively, if it isn’t possible for you to stay with any relatives or friends and it is an emergency out of hours call, the council may be able to help you find a room in a bed and breakfast; the Housing Advice Officer will be notified and contact you the following day. For emergency accommodation, call 01352 763554.

Requesting a review of a decision

If you are advised of any of the following that apply to you then you can ask for a review on the decision. You will be asked to clearly state your reasons for the review in writing.

Not Priority Need
Not Homeless
No Local Connection
You are to be referred to another Local Authority
You are intentionally homeless
You have been offered temporary accommodation
Homeless duty has been discharged by way of a suitable offer of accommodation

You will need to make your request within 21 days from when you receive your decision letter (Section 184).

A senior officer will take account of all your reasons submitted by you or someone acting on your behalf. If you fail to provide further information, the senior officer will review the decision based on the relevant facts already to hand.

A decision will be reached normally within 8 weeks from the date of your request for a review.
