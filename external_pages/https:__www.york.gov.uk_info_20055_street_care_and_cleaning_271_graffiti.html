How can we help you today?

Graffiti

Graffiti is criminal damage, it spoils the local environment and encourages other forms of anti social behaviour. It can be costly to remove from private properties and from our property.

Use our eForm to report graffiti you find on public property. We aim to:

  • remove offensive graffiti in 24 hours
  • remove non offensive graffiti in 4 working days

Public property includes:

  • ancient monuments and some memorials
  • City walls and Bar Walls
  • council fences or walls/boundaries
  • some bridges
  • bollard
  • bus stops and sign posts
  • dog bins and litter bins
  • council houses or buildings
  • play equipment
  • street furniture, such as seats

Graffitti on private property

Street environment officers and enforcement officers work with private landowners to arrange removal from private residential properties or private commercial properties. There are no target times for removal of graffiti in these locations, as there is no legal requirement for us to remove it, except in exceptional circumstances.

You can pay for our graffiti removal service, tel: 01904 625751 for a free quotation or advice.

Anti graffiti kits

If you're the victim of graffiti damage, our street environment and enforcement teams can loan you an anti graffiti kit. We can visit, advise and loan the kit to help you remove the damage.

We also have 30 volunteers in the community who help local residents and businesses by loaning and training in the use of the kits. Some volunteers also give up their time to actively remove graffiti themselves in their neighbourhood.

If you are interested in becoming a graffiti kit holder please contact your Street Environment Officer via the Customer Contact Centre.

Tackling graffiti

We have drawn up a protocol with North Yorkshire Police explaining the work we will do together to tackle graffiti problems through:

  • prevention and 'target hardening'
  • detection of crime
  • removal
  • education and campaigning
  • engagement of local residents, community groups and businesses.

Officers around the city from various agencies work together to gather evidence of tags and add them to the website, making intelligence more effective for the police when they arrest someone responsible for these offences.

Dedicated removal

A dedicated two person team is currently being developed to provide a more rapid response to graffiti removal across the city. The work or the team will bring about better intelligence, quicker removal and a cleaner city for all who live, work and visit.

Target hardening

Street environment officers carry out regular ward audits and walkabouts, identifying and reporting graffiti damage. We give advice and support to any private property owners affected by graffiti including ways to prevent further damage such as:

  • removing the target
  • removing access
  • obstructing access with shrubs, fencing, etc
  • making removal easier, using anti graffiti paint
  • reporting the crime to the police

Education

The street Environment Officers will be carrying out school visits to educate and engage school pupils in why graffiti is wrong and bring about a 'name and shame' campaign against taggers.

Utility companies

We have set up a partnership agreement with Virgin Media (formerly NTL) to ensure prompt removal from their cabinets in York.

There are 1600 cabinets in the city , the partnership has included:

  • free continuous supply of paint from Virgin Media
  • financial and cleaning of cabinets by Neighbourhood Services
  • promotion of 'Adopt A Box' scheme.

Adopt a box

If you live near a Virgin Media box that is a target for graffiti damage, you can help combat the problem with us by painting your local box.

Helping us to respond quicker to graffiti will mean deterring those responsible and bring about greater community support and empowerment.

Your street environment officer will support you including regular networking events where you can meet other volunteers.
