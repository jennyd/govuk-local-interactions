Clinical waste collections

Listen

Clinical waste

We offer a weekly collection of clinical waste/sharp boxes on Thursdays. Call Streetscene before 12am on Wednesday to arrange a collection.

Items such as dressings, bandages, swabs etc, that are contaminated with a) materials known to cause disease in humans or other living organisms or b) medicines that contain a biologically active pharmaceutical agent, such as cytotoxic or cytostatic drugs.

These should not be disposed of in your general rubbish but be separated out and collected as part of clinical waste service.

To register contact the Streetscene Department.

Dressings, bandages etc that are not contaminated with such materials can be double wrapped and disposed of with your general rubbish.

Offensive hygiene waste

Items such as sanitary products, incontinence pads, stoma bags, catheter waste, home dialysis waste (empty saline or glucose IV bags and tubing), 'peg' or stomach feeding equipment.

Provided these are not contaminated with infectious materials and/or cytotoxic or cytostatic type drugs, they are not considered clinical waste and therefore can be double wrapped and disposed of with your general rubbish; they do not need to be collected separately.

Sharps

This is the general term for syringes, needles and other sharp instruments. Under no circumstances should these be disposed of with your general rubbish. Even if they are not contaminated or used they should be placed in special sharps boxes and collected separately.

Medicines

Take your unwanted and out-of-date medicines to your local pharmacy; do not put them in your normal rubbish bin.

Empty non contaminated packaging and containers for medicines and other medical products can be placed in household recycling bins. These may include cardboard boxes, paper instructions and clean plastic bottles.
