Alternate Week Collection System - Black, Burgundy and Green Bins

This page tells you all about the Alternate Week Collection System.

FAQs:

  • What is the Alternate Week Collection System?

  • When will my bins be emptied?

  • What CAN I put in my green bin?

  • What CAN'T I put in my green bin?

  • What CAN I put in my burgundy bin?

  • What CAN'T I put in my burgundy bin? 

  • How do I fit everything else in my Black Bin?

  • Why have the Alternate Week Collection System?

What is the Alternate Week Collection System?

We run an alternate week collection system to reduce the amount of waste going to landfill. This means that you will have a green 'wheelie' bin for all your 'green' waste (see below), a burgundy 'wheelie' bin for recyclable waste and a traditional black wheelie bin for everything else.

When will my bins be emptied?

We empty your black bin and burgundy and green bins on alternate weeks on your usual collection day. You will receive a calendar showing the dates each bin is collected towards the end of each year.

What CAN I put in my green bin?

  • Leaves, hedge and plant clippings

  • Twigs, branches and prunings

  • Grass cuttings, weeds and cut flowers- please ensure that any plants that are put in the bin have had all their excess soil removed.

  • Vegetarian Pet Waste - e.g. sawdust, shavings etc from rabbits, guinea pigs, hamsters
  • Leftover fruit, salad, vegetable waste, egg shells
  • Cooked food left - bread, pastries, pasta, meat, fish (raw and cooked - including bones), plate scrapings, tea bags and coffee grounds
  • Food can be placed in compostable paper liners manufactured to EN13432

What CAN'T I put in my green bin?

  • Cardboard boxes or cardboard packaging - these go in your burgundy bin
  • Wallpaper and wrapping paper
  • Waxed or laminated packaging, such as Tetrapak juice cartons - these can be go in your burgundy bin.
  • Stone, rubble, soil or turf
  • Dog or cat faeces/waste
  • Nappies (disposable or other)
  • Plastic bottles, plastic containers and packaging, glass bottles or jars, cans or tins, foil or foil trays, aerosols, or papers - these go in your burgundy bin
  • Plastic bags or plastic film, including carrier bags, all types of bin liners, including biodegradable or compostable, corn starch- or plant extract-based bags or liners.  Please note: the only bags accepted in the green bin are compostable paper liners manufactured to EN13432.
  • Polystyrene
  • Timber or plywood
  • Fire ash

If you put unacceptable materials, such as those listed above, in to your Green Bin this will contaminate your Green Bin and we will not be able to collect it.

When your green bin is emptied, the green waste collected is composted and is then used for the reinstatement of land. The compost is not available for sale. 

How do I fit everything else in my black bin?

  • Taking out all of your garden waste and putting it in your green bin should help reduce the content of your black bin

  • Use your burgundy bin for plastic bottles and containers, glass, cans, cardboard, envelopes and newspapers.

  • Take drinks cartons to the Tetrapak recycling banks located at the Household Waste Recycling Centres at Stonegravels or Buttermilk Lane.

Why have the alternate week collection system?

Since introducing green and burgundy bin collections the amount of waste collected in black bins has reduced. The Council is therefore balancing its bin collection resource to meet the changing demands of black, burgundy and green waste streams.

The alternate week collection means that your black bin will be emptied one week, and the green and burgundy bins will be emptied on the same day the next week.  This makes it easier for people to remember when there collection day is.  (Please note, residents collecting using the smaller 'rural' route vehicle might have a different collection day for the burgundy bin.)

The amount of waste our households recycle has increased significantly over recent years resulting in the amount of waste in black bins having reduced to 55% of what is was 10 years ago, when households only had one 240ltr wheeled (black) bin.  Households now have three 240ltr wheeled bins - black, green and burgundy - which are emptied on alternate weeks and it is with huge credit to our households that we are recycling 45% of our wastes.

If you are interested in further information about were your waste goes and what happens to the recycling, further information is available on Derbyshire County Council's website.

 

Remember that your black bin will be emptied on an alternative week basis so you will need to recycle in order to manage your waste effectively!

Use your burgundy and green bins - Reduce, Reuse and Recycle!
