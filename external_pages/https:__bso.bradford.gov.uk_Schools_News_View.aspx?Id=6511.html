Youth Opportunities Funding in Shipley - deadline 20 September

Young People in Shipley are being encouraged to come forward with great ideas that can be funded by Bradford Council’s Youth Opportunities Fund, which can provide a grant of up to £2,000 to develop ‘things to do’ for young people.

If you work with young people, are a young person yourself, or in a group of young people this is an opportunity to access resources that can help you.

The Fund aims to:

  • Give a voice and influence to young people, particularly those facing disadvantage, in relation to things they can do.
  • Change the way local authorities and their partners provide activities for young people, especially in deprived neighbourhoods, so they reflect what young people want.
  • Provide opportunities for young people to develop their confidence, knowledge, skills and abilities.
  • Increase young people’s engagement and participation in activities and enable them to gain recognition and accreditation for their involvement,
  • Offer young people the opportunity to develop projects that promote and increase their entrepreneurial skills.

Bids should be initiated and run by young people, and the proposed activities must be organised from within their local neighbourhood, and meets the needs of young people identified through the recent ward planning process. 

Bids should be supported by a bona-fida organisation and an experienced adult needs to be involved to ensure adequate support and deployment of any successful grant.

The deadlines for applications for this second round of funding for Shipley is 4.30pm on Friday, 20 September 2013.
