Please use this form to report anti-social behaviour.

Before completing the form below, please consider the following:

  • If the behaviour is criminal, violent or dangerous, then you should contact the police directly .
  • Please read our page on anti-social behaviour.

Please be aware that under current anti-social behaviour legislation we have no tools or powers to deal with any of the following issues that are listed below:

  • *No ball games* - We have no powers to prevent people playing in areas where there is a 'no ball games' sign.  These signs are purely advisory and for this reason we no longer erect 'no ball games' signs.  If permanent damage is being caused to property it should be reported to the police on 101.
  • *Civil disputes* - (including disputes over boundaries, rights of access, land ownership, building and development works). - You should try to speak with your neighbour wherever possible or contact your solicitor
  • *Illegal or nuisance parking or parking visitor bays* - The police can deal with obstruction only where a road, driveway or pavement is totally blocked.  Dial 101 at the time of the obstruction.
  • *Children playing in residential areas* - It is only anti-social behaviour if there is intimidation, verbal abuse or criminal damage to property.

When we receive the completed form below, we will send you a letter and log sheets to record further occurrences of this behaviour. Once you have completed and returned these log sheets, your complaints will be investigated by our staff.

Any information you provide will be kept confidential. Those committing the nuisance will not be given your details.
