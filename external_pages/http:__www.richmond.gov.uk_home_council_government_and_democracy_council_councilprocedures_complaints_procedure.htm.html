How to make a complaint

This guide outlines our procedures for most complaints.

Faults and missed collections

Some problems may need to be reported as a ‘fault’, such as:

  • Reporting a street fault: You should use this online form to report ‘infrastructure faults’ such as road markings, traffic signs and lights, street drainage, blocked gullies, pot holes, damaged paving, graffiti and street cleaning.
  • Reporting a missed collection: You should use this online form to report a missed waste and recycling collection. We also have a repeat missed collection form if we have missed your collection more than once.

We have separate complaints procedures for:

  • Parking tickets (Parking Charge Notices)
  • Planning application appeals
  • Housing benefit appeals and council tax decisions
  • School admission appeals
  • Special educational needs appeals
  • Children's social services
  • Adults' social services

More about our other complaint procedures

Stage 1: If you are unhappy with one of our services

There are two main stages to the complaints procedure. We will tell you at each stage who is dealing with your complaint and when you can expect to receive a response.

Please note, we will only consider complaints about matters that have occurred in the last 12 months, although in exceptional circumstances we may consider complaints outside of this period.

Find out who to make your complaint to

If you are not sure who to make your complaint to, find out from the Who to complain to section.

Make a complaint

Use the online complaint form below. This form should be used for all complaints (including social care ones).

Make a complaint

Alternatively you can:

  • tell a member of staff who works in the service you want to complain about;
  • email our contact centre on customer.services@richmond.gov.uk;
  • telephone our contact centre on 020 8891 1411;
  • fill in a complaint form if you have one of our leaflets(pdf, 453KB) and post it to us; the form at the back is pre-paid. These leaflets are available at the Civic Centre and all our main offices. Please note that if you download the form, you will need to put it in an addressed envelope and pay for postage yourself; or
  • visit one of our reception areas where a member of staff can help you to fill in a form.

What happens next

(This next section applies to all standard corporate complaints. Please note that social care complaints follow different processes with different response timescales. More information can be found under Adult social care complaints and Children’s social care complaints)

We will let you know we have received your complaint within two working days.

A manager will investigate your complaint and reply to you within 15 working days. If we cannot reply within this time, we will let you know.

If you are not happy with our response, tell us. We will listen to your concerns and, if appropriate, give you advice about how to take your complaint to the second stage.

Stage 2: If you are not happy with our response

If you are not satisfied with the manager's reply at stage 1, you can ask for your complaint to be escalated to stage 2.

Contact the person named at the end of your Stage 1 letter

You should contact the person named at the end of your stage 1 letter, either by phone, email or letter within 20 working days.

What happens next

  • We will let you know we have received your request for a stage 2 complaint investigation within two working days.
  • A senior manager (Investigating Officer) will investigate your complaint and write a letter for the Director of the service to consider, approve and sign.
  • A senior manager from a different service (Independent Person), not connected to the one you have complained about, will work alongside the Investigating Officer during the stage 2 investigation to ensure all aspects of the complaint are responded to and to provide an objective viewpoint.
  • We will tell you the names of both the Investigating Officer and the Independent Person.
  • The Director will aim to send you the stage 2 letter within 25 working days. If we cannot reply within this time, we will let you know why, and when you can expect to hear from us.

At the end of stage 2, if you are still dissatisfied, you can contact the Local Government Ombudsman.
