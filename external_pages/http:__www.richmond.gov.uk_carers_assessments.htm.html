Carers assessments

A carer's assessment is a discussion with you that will help us ensure that your needs are taken into account.

Self-appraisal

Take our simple self-appraisal to find out if you might be eligible for support in your role as a carer

Get started

Who we can help

We know how important your role as a carer is. If you provide care and support to an adult friend or family member, we may be able to help.

Find out if you are eligible for support.

How it works

Having a carer's assessment will help us understand the physical, emotional and practical impact that caring has on your life. As a carer, you have a right to have your needs considered when decisions are being made about support for the person you care for. This includes any education, training, work or leisure activities that you would like to take part in.

If you care for an adult, we can assess yourself and the person you care for at the same time. You can also choose to have your carer’s assessment done separately.

We will ask you what kind of support the person you care for needs, and what help you might need to provide it. We can discuss things such as financial worries, your own health, practical help you might need in the home and any concerns about future needs.

Download our Carers Assessment Guidance notes(pdf, 66KB)

What happens next

When the assessment is complete, we will decide whether your needs are eligible for support. We will write to you about our decision and give you reasons to explain what we have decided.

We will then explain the support options available to you which may include:

  • financial support and advice
  • arranging respite care for a short break
  • emergency planning
  • finding support groups and health services

Cost

There is no charge for information and advice or a carer's assessment. Services from other local voluntary organisations are also normally free.

Young carers

If you’re a young carer under the age of 18, please call our Children and Families’ service on 020 8891 7969.
