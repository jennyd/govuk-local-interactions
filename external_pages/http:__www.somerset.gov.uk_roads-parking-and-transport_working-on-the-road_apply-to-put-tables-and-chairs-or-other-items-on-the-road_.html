Apply to put objects on highway land

 

We review requests to place objects on the highway which will require a licence. These licences are also referred to as highway projection. These requests are received from District and Parish Councils, commercial organisations and members of the public and relate to, for example, notice boards, bus shelters, street cafes.

Apply now

You can also change a permit to place tables and chairs on the pavement or road.

We ensure that anything placed on the highway is in a safe location and does not inconvenience the public.Â 

Permission is required from us before items are placed on the highway to ensure that they do not cause an obstruction or interfere with visibility or verge maintenance.

These items include:

  • Tables and chairs placed on the pavement outside a cafe, restaurant or bar

  • Seating and benches

  • Notice boards

  • Plants and shrubs
  • Bus shelters (providing they are at a bus stop)

Who can use it?
We can receive requests from District and Parish Councils, commercial organisations and members of the public.

How do I use it?
Apply for permission by using the Apply now button above or by sending us your completed application form which can be found in the Information and resources section. When you apply for permission to place an object on highway land, we will inspect the proposed site to check it is safe and will not cause an obstruction. We will then ask our colleagues in Legal Services to start the formal notice procedure and draw up an appropriate licence.

When contacting us please provide the following information:

  • Your name, address, phone number and email
  • A site location planÂ 
  • Details of the object to be placed on the highwayÂ 

What happens next?
We will acknowledge your request within 10 working days and we will inform you of the outcome within 10 weeks.

Please note that the target period only begins on receipt of a fully completed application.
