Council tax

Council tax support

Find out if you may qualify for a discount on your council tax.

A full council tax bill is based on at least two adults (18 or over) living in a property. In some cases you may be eligible for a discount or reduction.

Single person discount

If you live on your own or the people you live with don't count as adults for council tax you'll get 25% off your bill.

To apply you need to fill in a  sole occupancy discount form [102kb].

Council tax reduction and second adult rebate

If you are on a low income or certain benefits, you may be eligible for council tax reduction. If one or more people who live with you are on a low income or benefits, you may be able to claim a reduction called second person rebate. - This is a form of council tax support that is available to council tax payers who do not have a partner, but share their home with someone on a low income who is not a paying lodger.
The rebate is not based on the councils tax payer's income, but that of the other adult in the property - they are known as the second adult.

The amount you get depends on the total gross income of the second adult. If there is more than one second adult in the property their gross income is added together to work out how much rebate you will get.  

Other discounts

Some adults do not have to pay council tax, so the bill for the property they are living in will be lower. To apply for this type of discount you need to download and complete the form that matches your circumstances:

  •  people over 18 who get Child Benefit [110kb]
  •  18 and 19-year-olds in full-time education [96kb]
  •  full time students [97kb],  student nurses [96kb] and  foreign language [97kb]
  •  apprentices [96kb]
  •  youth trainees [96kb]
  •  people who are severely mentally impaired [115kb]
  •  long-term residents of hospitals, residential care homes or nursing homes [112kb]
  • people staying in hostels or night shelters
  •  care workers [110kb]
  •  live-in carers [119kb] who look after someone who isn't their partner, spouse or child
  • members of visiting forces, armed forces and diplomats
  • members of religious communities
  •  people in prison [96kb] (except those in prison for non-payment of Council Tax)

If you already get single person (sole occupancy) discount you don't need to reapply every year. But you do need to let us know within 21 days if your circumstances change and you may no longer be eligible. If you don't you may get a penalty of £70 it it's the first time, or £240 if you've not notified us of a change before.

Physical disabilities discount

You can apply for physical disabilities discount if there's someone with a disability living in the property - even if the disabled person is not the one responsible for paying the council tax.

You'll need to show that one of the following applies:

  • a wheelchair is used at the property
  • there's an extra bathroom, kitchen or other room needed for the person with a disability

To apply you need to complete a physical disabilities discount form.

Empty and uninhabitable property discount

You can no longer get a council tax discount for property that is empty or uninhabitable. There are, however, certain situations when an empty property can be exempt from council tax.

If your property is empty for over two years you'll have to pay a premium charge of 150% council tax.

Second homes

You can no longer get a discount for second homes.

Caravans and mobile homes

You can't get a discount on caravans or mobile homes.

You'll continue to get any exemption though if there are planning restrictions that mean you can only live in your property at a certain time of year.

Housing provided by employers

You may be eligible for a 50% discount if you live in a property provided to you by your employer as part of your contract.

Appeals

You can appeal against our decision on your discount if you think it's wrong.

You must write to us and we will respond within two months and if we don't agree you can appeal to the Valuation Tribunal  (which must be received by them within two months of us making our decision).
