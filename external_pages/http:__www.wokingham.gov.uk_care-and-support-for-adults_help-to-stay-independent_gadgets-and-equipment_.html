Assistive technology - promoting independence

Assistive technology is any equipment, gadgets or apps that can support you to stay safe or independent in the community

    How can it support me?

A wide range of equipment is available to help. Examples include:

  • Making you aware that there is a fire or gas leakage (smoke alarm, gas detector)
  • Letting someone know if you’ve had a fall (fall detector)
  • Alerting someone if you're not feeling well (Pendant connected up to a call centre)
  • Locating vulnerable people in the community if they get lost (GPS tracking systems)

    Accessing the service

If you think you or someone you care for might benefit from this kind of support, you can talk to an adviser in our social care team. They will let you know if you are eligible for an assessment. If not, they will be able to advise you about local services and suppliers that you can access directly.

You can contact us on 0118 974 6000.

*Charges may apply

    More advice

Is there any technology out there that can help me stay safe, independent, or living in my own home longer? Who can advise me on what assistive technology is available and how it can meet my specific needs? The assistive technology service was set up to answer just these questions.

The service has a wide knowledge of what assistive technology is available, and more importantly, how it can be used to support people as effectively as possible. Individual assessments are arranged, where we listen to your concerns and advise you on what assistive technology will help meet your needs. We can install the equipment for you and review its effectiveness to make sure it’s right for you.

The service can also advise on, and provide, lifestyle monitoring technology. These monitoring systems help establish whether someone is able to live independently or if more support is needed. More information is available on request.
