Licensing Act 2003

Legislative changes regarding entertainment which came into force on 06 April 2015:
https://www.gov.uk/entertainment-licensing-changes-under-the-live-music-act

The Licensing Act 2003 has changed the existing laws about the type of licences needed. Under the new system:

A Premises Licence will be required if the premises falls into the above categories:

  • A Personal Licence will be needed by anyone who wants to allow the sale of alcohol as part of his or her business. These licences are valid for 10 years.
  • Every application for a premises licence where alcohol is being sold must include a Designated Premises Supervisor who is responsible for the day-to-day running of the premises. They must hold a personal licence.

Licence holders, Teignbridge District Council, the Police and other agencies must all work to promote the four Licensing Objectives as set out by the Act, which are:

  • Prevention of crime and disorder;
  • Public safety;
  • Prevention of public nuisance; and
  • Protection of children from harm
