Older Persons Freedom Pass

Do it online

  • Apply online for a Older Persons Freedom Pass

Older Freedom Pass Renewal

Older people across London whose Freedom Passes will expire on March 31 2016 will receive a letter between the middle of January and the beginning of February, explaining how to renew their pass.

Pass holders are being encouraged to renew online, which is a straightforward process and only takes a few minutes.

Anyone who is unable to renew online can return the form enclosed with their letter by post.

To renew your Older Persons Freedom Pass, you will need your letter with your unique reference number. Once you have received your letter, click here to renew your Freedom Pass.

Please note Freedom Passes cannot be renewed at the Post Office or at Redbridge Council offices.

Older Persons Freedom Pass

The Freedom Pass for older people is funded by your local borough council and gives free travel on most public transport in London 24 hours a day (after 9.30 on most National Rail services within the London Area), including all day at weekends and on public holidays. This pass may also be used to travel on local bus services in the rest of England under the terms of the Concessionary Bus Travel Act 2007.

Eligibility

You can apply for the Older Persons Freedom Pass if you meet the age criteria and your sole or principal residence is in the London Borough of Redbridge. To find out if you are  please visit the Freedom Pass website:

If you're a London resident, aged 60 or over, and not yet eligible for a Freedom Pass, you may be eligible for TfL's 60+ London Oyster Photocard. Find out more at www.tfl.gov.uk and search for 60 plus.

How to apply

You can apply for the Older Persons Freedom Pass online via the Freedom Pass website .

More information about what documents you will need when you apply online can be found on the Freedom Pass website.

If you are unable to apply online, you can pick up a paper application form, which you will need to complete and return in the post. Application forms can be picked up from any of the Borough libraries or the Council's One Stop Shop, Ilford.

You will need to provide proof of your identity, proof of your address and one recent colour passport-sized photograph to apply.

Lost, Stolen, Damaged or Faulty

Contact the Freedom Pass helpline: 0300 330 1433 (local call rate, open Monday - Sunday 8am to 8pm) or email infor@freedompass.org

Freedom Pass holders who lose or damage their passes will be charged £10 for replacements.

The Freedom Pass is paid for by your local council and the scheme is managed by London Councils on behalf of the London boroughs.
