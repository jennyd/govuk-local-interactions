Register to vote







Registering to vote

Registering to vote is straightforward and only takes a few minutes. 
 



1. Go to www.gov.uk/register-to-vote

2. Fill in your name, address, date of birth and the other details you are asked for. You'll also need your National Insurance number, which can be found on your National Insurance card, or in official paperwork such as payslips, letters about benefits or tax credits. 

3. Once we've checked your details, we'll send you a letter to confirm if you've been added to the electoral register.

Who can register to vote?

You can register to vote if you are:

  • 16 years old or over (although you can’t vote until you are 18); and,
  • a British or Commonwealth citizen or a citizen of the Irish Republic or other European Union state.

Individual Electoral Registration

The way we all register to vote changed in June 2014 to a new system called 'Individual Electoral Registration'. Previously the 'head of every household' was responsible for registering everyone who lived at their address, but now everyone is responsible for registering themselves.

You can register online at www.gov.uk/register-to-vote. You need to provide a few details to register - including your National Insurance number and date of birth. This makes the electoral register more secure.

For more details on the changes to electoral registration please go to www.gov.uk/yourvotematters

Confirm your details

Over the summer you will receive a household enquiry form asking you to check whether the information that appears on the electoral register for those living at your address is correct.

We do this every year to ensure that the electoral register is up to date and to identify any residents who are not registered so that they have the opportunity to do so.

Once you receive your form it’s easy to confirm your details:

If there are no changes to the information on the form you can respond quickly and easily, online at www.register-online.co.uk or by using our automated text or Freephone service. Details of how to use these and your unique code are printed on your form.

If there are changes to your details, simply amend the form, sign it and return it to us in the prepaid envelope, following the instructions on the form. Once we receive your amended form we will get in touch if we need you to take any further action. Remember to add the names of any 16 or 17-year olds so that they can register and vote as soon as they reach their 18th birthday. 

If you have recently moved or weren’t previously registered please add your names to the form and send it back to us and we’ll send an individual registration form to all the people in your household who are eligible to register. You can also register online at www.gov.uk/register-to-vote but you must still fill out the household enquiry form and send it back to us so that everyone in your house has the opportunity to register. 

The letter that comes with your household enquiry form will tell you what you need to do.

Important information about your personal details

Using information received from the public, we keep two registers - the electoral register and the open register (also known as the edited register).

The electoral register lists the names and addresses of everyone who is registered to vote in public elections. The register is used for electoral purposes, such as making sure only eligible people can vote. It is also used for limited purposes specified in law, such as:
 

  • detecting crime (e.g. fraud)
  • calling people for jury service
  • checking credit applications

The open register is an extract of the electoral register, but it is not used for elections. It can be bought by any person, company or organisation. For example, it is used by businesses and charities to confirm name and address details.

Your name and address will be included in the open register unless you ask for them to be removed. Removing your details from the open register does not affect your right to vote. 

To be removed from the open register, download and complete the  Opting out of the open register form (PDF 10KB)
