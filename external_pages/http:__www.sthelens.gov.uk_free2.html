Free Early Education Entitlement for 2 Year Olds

From September 2013 some two-year-olds in St.Helens will be entitled to a free place of up to 15 hours per week with a nursery, playgroup or childminder . This will depend upon your family circumstances.
 

Is my child eligible?

You can claim a free place for your child if you receive one or more of the following:

  • Income Support
  • Income-based Job Seekers Allowance
  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance
  • Support under Part VI of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999
  • The Guaranteed element of State Pension Credit
  • Child Tax Credit/ Working Tax Credits provisded you have an annual gross income of no more than £16,190 (a copy of the TC602 Award Notice will be required as proof of your entitlement).
  • The child has a current Statement of Special Educational Needs (SEN) or an Education, Health and Care Plan (EHCP).
  • The child attracts Disability Living Allowance (a copy of your child's Award Notice will be required as proof of their entitlement).
  • Looked after Children and children who have left care through adoption orders, residence orders or special guardianship (proof required either from your social worker or legal documentation) are also entitled to a free place.

What is available?

Your child would be able to benefit from 15 hours of free early education per week for 38 weeks of the year at a nursery or pre-school playgroup. This would start from the school term after your child turns two.

How will my child benefit?

Children like to explore the world around them, play with other children and have fun. Good early years education from a young age can help children to:

  • Become more confident
  • Play and join in with other children
  • Develop their language skills
  • Learn new things
  • Prepare for school

What happens when my child turns three?

When your child turns three, they will be able to take up a free place for 15 hours a week for 38 weeks of the year. If you would like your child to continue with the same childcare provider for their three year old funded place before they go on to start school, your childcare provider will try to ensure that this happens, and your child will then continue in a smooth transition from their two year old funded place into their three year old funded place.

I think my child is eligible. How can I apply?

Please check the criteria listed above carefully, to see if your child could be eligible for a funded place. If you think that you might be eligible, then you can apply online via the link below:

https://familyinfoservice.sthelens.gov.uk/Enrol/Website/

To apply online you need to register with an email address, before you can proceed with the application. If you experience problems using the above link using Internet Explorer then please either upgrade to IE9 or higher, or try using Google Chrome web browser.

Alternatively please contact us on 01744 676542 and we will confirm if your child is eligible and give you an immediate answer. In order for us to do this, we will need your National Insurance number, date of birth and the first three letters of your surname.  Once we confirm you are eligible, you will then be able to contact your preferred childcare setting to secure a place and confirm a start date for your child.
