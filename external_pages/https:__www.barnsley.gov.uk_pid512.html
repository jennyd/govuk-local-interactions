    Development or demolition in Conservation Areas

    Information about development or demolition within conservation areas.

    A conservation area is one that has special architectural or historic interest.

    Conservation areas are judged to be of such high quality that their character or appearance must be enhanced or preserved.

    Usually these areas are characterised by buildings with strong group value, created by consistent use of traditional forms and materials. Very often the areas include listed buildings, individually protected for their special historic or architectural interest. The high visual quality of these areas is often enhanced by mature trees and boundary walls.

    New development in a conservation area will be permitted only if it is considered to enhance or preserve the character and appearance of the area. Proposals for new development should be submitted as full, not outline applications. They must include a design statement explaining and justifying the proposals, and drawings showing the scheme in its context, such as a street scene elevation.

    In order to preserve the character of conservation areas there are strengthened controls, and these restrictions apply:

    Property Alterations: If you live in or run a business from a property in a conservation area you may need permission before building or making alterations such as cladding, inserting windows, installing satellite dishes and solar panels, adding conservatories or other extensions, laying paving or building walls. It is advisable to always contact us before making arrangements, or starting any work.

    Trees: If you are thinking of cutting down a tree or doing any pruning work you must notify us 6 weeks in advance. This is to give us time to assess the contribution the tree makes to the character of the conservation area and decide whether to make a Tree Preservation Order.

    Demolition or substantial demolition of a building in a conservation area will usually require permission.

    Any proposals for development or demolition in conservation areas (or development affecting a listed building) should be discussed with us, on (01226) 772576 or email: buildingconservation@barnsley.gov.uk.

    Further general information on Conservation Areas can be found on the English Heritage website.

    Pre Application Service

    The council operates a pre application service whereby customers can receive advice from officers before a formal application is made.

    Please note: Advice given by officers for pre-application enquiries does not constitute a formal response or our decision about future planning permissions.

    Views or opinions expressed are given in good faith, and to the best of ability, without prejudice to the formal consideration of the planning application. The application needs to undergo consultation with the public and interested parties which also be taken into account when the application is determined by us.

    Officers cannot give guarantees about the final decision that will be made on your planning or related applications. However, we will consider the advice note when making a decision on future planning-related applications. We will also consider changes in circumstances and information that could alter the original position.
