Housing with support

Self-contained accommodation with access to a range of support services.

This type of housing enables residents to live independently whilst having access to a Scheme Manager, a Mobile Warden and a range of other support services. Our schemes are usually reserved for people over 60 or those with other support needs such as a disability.

Scheme Managers and Mobile Wardens

The Scheme Manager's role to enable residents to live independently and to help them access any extra support they may need.  Scheme Managers do not provide care directly themselves, nor can they help with 'day to day' activities like shopping or housework.  However, they have excellent contacts with health and support organisations and are well placed to arrange help for residents.  The Scheme Managers also act as a vital link between residents and their Neighbourhood Housing Officer, who is based at either the Welwyn Garden City or Hatfield housing office.

A team of Mobile Wardens provide cover for all supported homes when the Scheme Managers are off duty and are available 24 hours a day, every day of the year via the emergency alarm system. 

Scheme options

There are four types of supported housing scheme:

  • Supported Schemes

These schemes have a Scheme Manager working on site during office hours.  Many have been built especially for older people and have extra facilities such as laundries, guest rooms and communal halls.

  • Supported/Neighbourhood Schemes

These schemes have a Scheme Manager working from within a group of flats/bungalows whilst also providing a service to homes outside the central group.  Some of these schemes also have communal facilities.

  • Neighbourhood Schemes

These schemes have a Scheme Manager either living in the neighbourhood or working from a scheme office.

  • FlexiCare Sheltered Housing at Chilton Green

This combines supported accommodation with a higher level of support.  The aim is to enable residents to live independently but with intensive home care and support, as needed.  This service is provided in partnership with Hertfordshire County Council's Adult Care Services.

All homes within our supported schemes are connected to our Lifeline emergency alarm system, which operates 24 hours a day, every day of the year. 

How to access supported housing

In order to access supported housing you will need to apply to join the Housing Needs Register (waiting list). Once you have registered for housing you will be able to view and bid for available properties within our supported housing schemes.

Please see our dedicated applying for a council or housing association home web page for more information about how to join the Housing Needs Register and to apply.
