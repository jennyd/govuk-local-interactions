Electoral Register

You can add your name to the register of electors at any time throughout the year.  You can only vote in elections if your name is on the Register of Electors at least 11 days before the date of the Poll.  The way in which you can register has changed and details can be found on the Electoral Registration page.

 

The Register of Electors is published on 1st December of each year and monthly revisions are published on the 1st working day of the month from January to September each year. The Register published on the 1st September is used for all elections between 1st September and 30th November. The registration process from January to September can take from 2 to 6 weeks depending on when an application is made.  Please see the list (pdf) for the current qualification dates and deadlines.

 

If you do not register then you cannot exercise your democratic right to vote in any election. It is also much harder to obtain credit if you are not on the Register as authorised credit reference agencies are allowed by law to purchase the register of electors to feed into their data-bases to carry out credit references etc.

Registration in Britain is compulsory, although voting is at the elector's discretion.

 

The Council is required to carry out an annual canvass each year between the 1st July and mid November.  A Household Enquiry Form (HEF) will be sent to each property during this time and the HEF must be responded to. Legal proceedings can be taken against householders who fail to return their HEF's and the maximum fine is £1,000. There are several options for responding to a HEF and these will be detailed on the form.

 

The Electoral Registration Office on 01572 722577 can deal with queries relating to previous years Registers for an elector. Third party enquiries cannot be dealt with under the Data Protection and Election legislation.

 

There are two types of Electoral Register, the Full Register and the Open Register. The full Revised Register contains the name and address of every elector, this is used for elections and can also be supplied to a limited number of government departments and credit reference agencies for a specific use that is laid down in the Representation of the People Regulations 2001. The Open Register is produced from the full register but omits the names and addresses of people who have asked for them to be excluded from that version of the register. The Open Register can be bought by anyone who asks for a copy, and may be used for any purpose.

 

If you are not on the published register, you can register online at www.gov.uk/register-to-vote.

 

You can check the full Electoral Register at our Customer Service Centre during office hours (appointments are advised). Libraries in the county have copies of the open register.

 

When we receive your completed application, we will add you to our list of pending applications. Any elector is then able to object to your name being added to the register. If this happens we will write to you.

 

If your application is not objected to, we will change your details on the first working day after the monthly claim period in which we received it (see list above). We will write to confirm that your application has been allowed.

 

If you have provided details of a previous address we will also tell the Electoral Registration Officer for that address that you have moved, so that your name can be removed from that register. People who live at two homes (including students) are allowed to register at both. If you do not want to be removed from your other address please explain why on your application.

 

There are special rules about names being added to the voters list during an election period. Because of these you may not be able to vote at that election if you are added during this time.

You can see more information and register Online by visiting the websites www.gov.uk/yourvotematters and www.gov.uk/register-to-vote or contact your local elections team on 01572 722577 and elections@rutland.gov.uk.
