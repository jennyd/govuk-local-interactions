Roadworks in Central Bedfordshire

Roadworks

We use Roadworks.org to give information about road works.

Roadworks.org has information from:

  • our council 
  • other Local Authorities
  • the Highways Agency
  • Transport for London
  • many utility companies

Type your postcode, town name or Local Authority name to view current and planned road works. You can set up roadworks alerts to be sent to your email address for any particular area of interest that you need.

Please note that the roadworks.org website only includes works where notice has been given to us and normally where the work is due to take longer than three days. The website does not include mobile (i.e. hedge cutting) or emergency road works.

Utilities work

Utility companies like British Gas and Anglian Water often need to fix problems with their pipes etc. under the road or pavements. Utility companies account for over 8,000 road openings a year across Central Bedfordshire. When these works are planned the utility company has to notify the council. Sometimes however the work is an emergency and it is not possible to notify the council prior to this.

The utility company is required to repair the road when they have finished. Repairs will have a minimum of a 2 year warranty. We do try to encourage them to do a first time permanent reinstatement but this is not always possible. The utility companies are allowed to use temporary repairs when the work is completed but they must come back and reinstate the road / pavement properly within six months

Central Bedfordshire Council is paid to inspect 30% of these works. We inspect 10% during the work, 10% within the first six months of the road being reinstated and 10% prior to the end of the two year warranty period.

We will also inspect if a resident reports a problem to us. You can report a problem online.

If the problem is serious and presents a danger to the public the council will contact the utility company and they should make it safe within two hours. 

All costs associated with making the road or pavement safe will be borne by the utility company. If the utility company doesn’t respond within a timeframe we agree with them, we will carry out the repairs and recharge the utility company. We can also impose fines when they stay longer on the road then they have told us.

Sometimes like us they have to close the road to do their work and they have to agree the road closure with us beforehand.

Applying for a street closure

Call 08453 656179 for an application form.

The request will then be processed and, if needed a diversion route will be specified. Any signing required will need to be supplied by the organisers.

You are advised to:

  • consider insurance to cover your event, although the council do not insist on this
  • seek permission from householders who are not involved in the party
  • provide signage (e.g. cones or barriers) to indicate that the road is closed

We want to support as many events as possible, however as the Highways Authority we do request as much notice of your event as you can possibly provide.
