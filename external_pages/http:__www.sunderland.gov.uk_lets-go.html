Youth support

Information about support and activities for young people.

  • Language and cultural support

    Information about interpretation and translation

  • Psychology, psychiatry or social work services

    Information about young people's mental health and wellbeing.

  • Student loan

    Government information on student loans

  • School mentors

  • Youth projects - volunteering

    Government information on volunteering for 14 to 19 year olds.

  • Young people - information and advice

    Information and advice for young people.

  • Supporting young people leaving care

    Information about the support and guidance we provide for young people leaving care

  • Youth leadership training

    Information about training for those working with young people.

  • Your options at 16+

    Useful information and advice on what you can do after leaving school

  • Connexions Sunderland

    Connexions is a confidential advice and support service for 13 to 19 year olds (and up to age 25 for young people with special needs).

  • Housing advice - young people

    Leaving home is something that most people think about at some stage in their lives. It's the time when you leave your family and strike out on your own and gain some independence.

  • Young carers

    A young carer is a young person aged between 4 and 18 who is looking after a family member with an illness, disability or alcohol/drug dependency. They may care alone or as part of a family.

  • Youth Drug and Alcohol Project

    YDAP offers specialist services for young people with drug or alcohol problems in Sunderland.

  • Keys Project

    The Keys Project secures appropriate accommodation and support for young people aged 16 and 17 who are homeless.

  • Supported Lodgings and Support 2 Independence

    These services provide accommodation and housing related support to young people and care leavers aged 16 to 21

  • Bump to Baby plus (B2b+)

    This project supports young parents and pregnant teenagers, providing advice on health, education, parenting and accessing further education or training opportunities.

  • Youth offending

    Provides intervention programmes to support the prevention of offending by children and young people
