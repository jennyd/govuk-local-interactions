Assistive technology explained

The term assistive technology refers device or system that allows an individualto live independently in their home.

Tap turner

Tap turners fit over existing taps to make them easier to turn on and off.

The long handle gives extra leverage to help people who have difficulty gripping conventional taps.

The tap turners are large and easier to see then conventional taps. On some models the red lever has a tactile top so that the hot and cold taps can be identified by touch.

The Carephone Home Safety Service

A Carephone is a special unit that plugs into a telephone line and can dial for help at the press of a button when someone needs it. People can also have a pendant to wear around their neck with a button to press for help if, for example, they have a fall or can't get to the phone for some other reason.

It calls the Carephone Home Safety Service and a trained member of our team will answer the call 24 hours a day, every day.

They will talk to you via your Carephone and if you need help they will take the most appropriate action, for example, contact a family member, neighbour, doctor or the emergency services.

This means you can use your Carephone for emergency and non-emergency situations.

Our specially trained staff will give you advice, contact your friends and relatives in emergencies, or simply give you the reassurance you need. When needed, we will contact the emergency services on your behalf.

Who can have this service

We help almost anyone who feels vulnerable or lives alone. Including:

  • people who feel isolated, particularly the elderly
  • anyone with a physical disability, learning disability or sensory impairment
  • people who have been affected by domestic violence
  • people who feel vulnerable and live alone, who want to feel safe and secure
  • carers who need a break and need peace of mind about the person they care for while they are away from the house.

How it works

We know that having equipment in your home can seem intrusive. That is why we want to give you the reassurance and information you need, to provide you with the right equipment.

A member of the carephone team will telephone you to arrange a suitable time to visit and will let you know when they are coming.

All our staff will show you identification. We will explain how the equipment works and discuss its suitability for you. An experienced fitter will call to install the equipment in your home.

In all cases you will need a piece of equipment called a Lifeline unit which is fitted in your home. You will need a telephone point close to an electric socket. If you do not have a telephone line, or the correct socket, we may be able to arrange to have one fitted for you.

How much it costs

The equipment that we supply is provided free of charge and there is no installation or maintenance fee. However depending upon your financial circumstances you may have to pay a monitoring fee.

A standard Carephone package includes smoke detector and carbon monoxide detector. The monitoring fee for this package is £4.20 per week.

The monitoring fee for a standard Carephone package which has been enhanced by one or more pieces of additional equipment is £4.45 per week

Detectors and sensors that connect to a Carephone

As well as your pendant there are many different types of sensors which can connect to your Carephone. They each perform different "jobs" and help people in different ways. These sensors are not provided to everyone, they are only provided to people who need that particular type of sensor.

Alert a local carer

Alert a local carer demonstration video

Providing reassurance for carers, this device ensures that if the person's sensors raise an alert the carer will be alerted immediately. This device makes the sensors alert a local carer first, and if they do not respond then the alert goes to the Carephone Home Safety Service. Or it can only alert a carer and not alert the Carephone Home Safety Service.

  • Alert a local carer video transcript

Bed occupancy sensor

Bed occupancy sensor video

Useful for people who fall out of bed or who leave their bed during the night and put their safety at risk

  • Bed occupancy sensor video transcript

Carbon monoxide detector

Automatically calls their carer

Carephone reminder messages

Carephone reminder messages video

A short reminder message can be recorded onto the Carephone. The message can be recorded by anyone, so it could be a family member, friend, or other voice that the person knows and is comfortable with.

It can be set to go off at any time of the day or night, up to 6 times per day. Each message can be different so that different messages can be played at different times of the day.

  • Carephone reminder messages transcript
  • Carephone reminder messages factsheet

Chair occupancy sensors

Chair occupancy sensors video

Useful for people who may fall or otherwise put themselves at risk when they get out of their chair.

  • Chair occupancy sensors transcript

Epilepsy sensor

Epilepsy sensor video

Suitable for certain types of epilepsy, this device detects when the person has an epileptic seizure and so allows a carer to take action urgently.

  • Epilepsy sensor video transcript

Flood detector

Flood detector video

If a person is at risk of flooding their property, for example if they leave taps running, this will alert when it detects water on the floor. Please also see the video and information for the magiplug.

  • Flood detector video transcript
  • Flood detector sensor factsheet

Fall detector

Fall detector video

The fall detector is a small piece of equipment that can be carried by a person. If that person has a fall it will automatically raise an alert to the Carephone Home Safety Service.

  • Fall detector video transcript
  • Fall detector factsheet

Gas detector

Gas detector video

A gas detector alerts the Carephone Home Safety service when there is gas leaking into a property, for example if a hob or fire is on but not lit.

  • Gas detector video transcript

Intruder alarm

An intruder alarm which has an easy to use push button pendant to turn the alarm on and off. It connects to the Carephone Home Safety Service when it alerts, so that Carephone Officers can take appropriate action

Movement sensor

Movement sensor video

Useful for people who may fall or otherwise put themselves at risk. If it can see movement it knows the person is safe. If it can't see movement it raises an alert.

  • Movement sensor video transcript

Property exit sensor

Property exit sensor video

The Property Exit Sensor helps to monitor the safety of people who may leave their homes when it isn't safe or appropriate for them to do so. It recognises when someone has left their home at an inappropriate time and calls for help.

  • Property exit sensor video transcript

Smoke detector

Detects smoke and automatically calls the Carephone Home Safety Service

Temperature extremes sensor

Temparture sensor video

The sensor monitors the temperature of the room. It alerts the Carephone Home Safety Service to potential fires by recognising when the temperature of a room either rises quickly or is very hot. Unlike smoke detectors it can be put into a kitchen

Other assistive technology available

GPS location devices

GPS location devices video

This is a GPS watch. It has a similar technology to Sat Navs. It is ideal for people who sometimes leave their home on their own or get lost easily due to memory problems.

  • GPS location devices video transcript

Medication dispenser

Medication dispenser video

A medication dispenser is a box that is set to automatically dispense tablets at the right time and in the right quantity. At the pre-programmed times, the dispenser rotates, an alarm signal is heard, and the correct dosage comes into view through an opening in the lid. The alarm will keep ringing until it senses that someone has taken the medication out of it.
