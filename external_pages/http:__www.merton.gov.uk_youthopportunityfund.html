Merton Youth Generator Fund

We are inviting a second round of applications from organisations who wish to bid for funding to deliver new projects within Merton.

Please note that the council intends that funding be allocated to support new ideas/projects. Repeat applications for projects which have been proposed in earlier rounds (whether funding was awarded or not) or for projects which are similar in nature will not be considered.

Funding for groups of young people aged 13-19 (up to 25 with learning disabilities). Â All applicants must live, be educated or be able to demonstrate a connection with Merton. Â You can apply for funding for equipment, materials, activities, projects, transport, instructors and trips.

All projects must take place after school and be open to anyone who would like to take part.

The aim of the fund is to help promote new and innovative projects, which will appeal to young people. We would also like the young people to be central to any decision making process on how the fund should be spent and therefore must be involved for the full life of the project including the activity.

Applications

The applications to the fund should be made by groups of young people with support from a youth group or organisation, for example: schools, churches, mosques, sports groups or residents associations. The role of the support organisation will be to encourage and support the young people, act as the banker to administer the fund and collate the appropriate documents needed.

All applications need to be submitted electronically together with supporting documentation on the

  • Merton Youth Generator Fund application form

The minimum funding allocation is Â£1,000 and maximum Â£3,000 per group.

The closing date for applications is 11th September 2015. All applicants will be asked to give a presentation on 17th September 2015 to a panel of young people.

Successful applicants support organisations need to provide the following documentation in relation to your organisation or confirmation that you have been awarded the London Youth Quality Kite Mark.

  • Safeguarding Children Policy and Child Protection Procedures
  • Safer Recruitment (Disclosure and Barring Service DBS)
  • Equality & Diversity or Equal Opportunities Policy
  • Health & Safety Policy
  • Latest audited accounts
  • Bank details
  • Written proof of provisional venue booking where project will run
  • In addition, please provide details of the Employers Liability and Public Liability insurance policies maintained by your organisation and copy of the cover note for the current year or other evidence that these policies are in force.

If you have previously supplied this documentation, you do not need to provide further copies unless they are 3 years old and need to be refreshed. Â Employers Liability and Public Liability need to be supplied on a yearly basis.

If you would like to discuss your application prior to submission please contact the Children and Young People's Participation Co-ordinator
