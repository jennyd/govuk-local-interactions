About the partnership

Manchester Community Safety Partnership brings together: the local Council; Police; Offender Services; the NHS; housing providers; and community and voluntary organisations to work together to tackle crime and disorder in Manchester.

The Partnership works closely with the following services to ensure Manchester is a safe place to live, work and enjoy:

  • Anti-social Behaviour Action Team (ASBAT)
  • Crown Prosecution Service (external site)
  • Domestic Abuse
  • Family Intervention Project
  • Greater Manchester Police (external site)
  • GM Fire and Rescue Service (external site)
  • Cheshire and Greater Manchester Community Rehabilitation Company (external site)
  • National Probation Service (external site)
  • Integrated Gang Management Unit (IGMU)
  • Manchester Safeguarding Children Board
  • Manchester Metropolitan University (external site)
  • Mediation Service
  • Police & Crime Commissioner (external site)
  • Public Health Manchester (external site)
  • Registered Social Landlords
  • Victim Support
  • Youth Offending Services (YOS)

Find out more about the work of the Manchester Community Safety Partnership by downloading the MCSP strategy documents.
