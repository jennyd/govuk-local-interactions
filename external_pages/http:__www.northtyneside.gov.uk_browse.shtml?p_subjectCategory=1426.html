Equipment and Adaptations to help you

For many people staying safe in their own home is an ongoing problem and accidents like falls can have serious consequences. These problems may be due to illness, sight or hearing loss, a physical or learning disability or just growing older and finding it harder to move around.

Our aim is to enable people to continue living independently in their own home for as long as possible.

This section will explain some of the helpful equipment and devices that are available as well as physical adaptations that can be made to your home, making it easier to live at home.

Equipment

Specially designed equipment can help you stay living independently in your own home. There are many types of equipment to help you carry out everyday tasks. If you would like to know more about the types of equipment available please click here.

Adaptations

Both minor and major adaptations are available. If minor adaptations aren't sufficient to meet your needs we will consider more major adaptations to assist you to stay living independently in your own home. If you would like to know more about the types of adaptations available please click here.

Telecare Assistive Technology

There are different types of telecare and assistive technology equipment to help people to continue to live in their own home and ensure that they get the help they need quickly in an emergency.

Telecare and assistive technology equipment is connected to our Care Call Response Centre, which provides friendly, helpful support - 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Find out more information about telecare, including community alarms.

Mobility support

If you have long term or permanent problems with mobility you may want to consider using mobility aids.

If you have mobility problems you should contact your GP surgery in the first instance. They will refer you for any mobility aids you need. For more information about mobility aids click here.
