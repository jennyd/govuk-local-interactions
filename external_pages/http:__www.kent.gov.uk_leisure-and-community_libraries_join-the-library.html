Join the library

Anyone who lives, works or studies in Kent can join a Kent library.

Joining the library is free and will give you access to:

  • more than 2 million books and more than 150,000 audio/visual items (for a small charge) in 99 libraries across Kent.
  • computers and the internet in all libraries
  • the online catalogue to renew or reserve items, or find a book
  • eBooks and audiobooks
  • our online reference library.

You can join online or at your nearest library. Our instant library account allows you to take out 3 items. To increase your borrowing allowance and access our full range of resources you will need to become a full member.

Becoming a full member 

To become a full member you will need to take proof of your address to a library. Once you're a full member you can borrow up to 30 items (including 12 DVDs or CDs) and will have access to our online resources including eBooks and audiobooks.

There are charges to borrow certain items (PDF, 186.7 KB) such as films and music. 

Your library card

When you join the library, you will get a library card and PIN which must only be used by you. If you sign up online, your library card and PIN will be issued on your first visit to the library. You will need the card and PIN to use the library and any online facilities associated with the library service.

People with disabilities or mental health problems

We offer an exempt card to people with disabilities or mental health problems. The exempt card allows you to:

  • borrow books for 3 weeks with no late fines 
  • borrow music CDs free of charge 
  • reserve books and audio books from the Kent libraries' catalogue free of charge 
  • use a library computer for 2 hours every day for free in any of our libraries (under 16s need parental permission to use the internet) with accessibility software to support planning, reading and writing
  • free black and white printing and photocopying (up to 20 pages), tinted paper is available if required.

We also have a range of specialist library services you may be interested in. For more information, speak to your local library. 

Forgotten PIN

If you have forgotten your library card PIN, you can request your PIN online or by telephone on 03000 41 31 31. The PIN will only be given to the library card holder or to the parent if the card holder is a child.
