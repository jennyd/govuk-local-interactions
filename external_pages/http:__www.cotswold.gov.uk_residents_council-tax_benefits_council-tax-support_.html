Council Tax support

Following changes in 2014, Councils are now responsible for creating and managing their local Council Tax support schemes. The new arrangementsmean that funding has been cut by 10%.

Council Tax support summary

  • If you are in not in the protected group the maximum amount of Council Tax support available is 91.5%
  • Customers with savings over 6,000 will no longer be eligible for Council Tax support
  • Claimants who are unemployed and then start work will be able to claim benefits for a further 13 weeks if they fulfil certain criteria

Exemptions

If you, or your partner, are of state pension age you are protected from the changes above.

You are also protected from some or all of the changes if you, your partner or any dependants are receiving any of the benefits listed below:

  • Disability premium
  • Enhanced disability premium
  • Severe disability premium
  • Disability premium for dependants
  • Disability earnings disregard
  • Council Tax disability band reduction
  • Employment support allowance (any rate)
  • War disablement pension
  • War widow's pension

How much Council Tax you could be expected to pay

The maximum amount of Council Tax supportis 91.5% of the household Council Tax bill. This means the remaining 8.5% of the bill will be payable by the householder.

For example: If you live in an average band D property in Cirencester your Council Tax bill will be around 1,452.88 per year. If you receive the maximum amount of council tax support you willneed to pay 8.5% of this bill around 123.49 over the year.*

*You may be entitled to other discounts, such as a single person discount, which could reduce your bill further.

We will send you your new Council Tax support entitlement and Council Tax bill in mid-March. If on receipt of this letter you think your bill is incorrect, please contact us.

Council tax is usually collected over 10 months (April January), although you can make payments over 12 months if you prefer, as long as this arrangement is set up in April. There are a number of ways to pay and the simplest is by direct debit.

  • More information about council tax payments

Apply for Council Tax support

To apply for Council Tax support, please complete and return the claim form:

  • Claim form for help with your rent and Council Tax

Please check you have provided the evidence required as your application cannot be processed without it. The evidence checklist is on page 23 of the form.

  • Evidence check list

Deciding applications

We aim to decide complete applications within 14 days. If we need further information to support your claim we will write to you. We will notify you once your claim has been assessed.

Back dating a claim

We will only consider back dating claims for Council Tax Support if good cause is shown. "Good cause" has been defined by a Social Security Commissioner as meaning "...some fact which, having regard to all the circumstances (including the claimant's state of health and the information which he had received and that which he might have obtained) would probably have caused a reasonable person of his age and experience to act (or fail to act) as the claimant did."
