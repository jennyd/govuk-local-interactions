How the adoption process works

Find out more about the adoption process

After you make an initial inquiry, you will be invited to attend one of our information sessions to find out more about the adoption process with the Royal Borough of Greenwich. 

After you attend the information session and you are happy that all your questions have been answered and you wish to pursue your interest in adoption further, you will need to complete a registration of interest (ROI) form.

Once we have received the registration of interest form, we will make contact and have a detailed telephone conversation with you. We will decide whether to accept or reject your application within five working days.

Stage one

This starts the formal evaluation process, where you will learn more about adoption. We will complete the necessary references and background checks.

This stage should be completed within two months, at the end of which you will be invited to an interview and a decision made on whether your application can will be able to proceed to stage two of the assessment process.

Stage two

This is the formal assessment phase of the process, and it should be completed within four months.

During this time the social worker gets to know you and your family and spends time helping you think about what strengths you could bring to adoptive parenting, in order to complete the home study report.

They will want to:

  • find out what type of parent(s) you are likely to be 
  • make sure your home meets health and safety requirements 
  • help you to prepare for the special job of being adoptive parent(s) 
  • plan your assessment 
  • organise your attendance at a preparation and assessment group, which is held over two days.

The whole process is focused on finding the right homes for the children who are currently in our care, so understanding the kinds of children you could support is very important.

We will be trying to establish that you and your partner have the resilience and emotional maturity to be a good parent, and that you have a good support network around you in friends and family.

Completion of assessment

Once the assessment process is complete, the social worker will gather all of the information together into a prospective adopter's report, which is what is taken to the adoption panel. You will be provided with a copy of this report and be able to comment on it before the panel.

The panel will make a recommendation of whether you're suitable to adopt to the adoption agency decision maker (AADM), who will make the final decision.

Matching for adoption

If you are considered suitable to adopt, there may be a wait before a child is matched to you. 

The crucial part of making sure an adoption is a success is making sure that the needs of each child are met. 

As part of this, we look at all aspects of a child's background, religion, language and culture. Your adoption social worker will help you identify a child or children who is/are a suitable match for you.

Your details will be shared with the member boroughs of the South London Adoption Consortium and also with the National Adoption Register, which is a register operating in England and Wales that has details of all available approved adopters and children who are waiting to be placed. 

Once the match has been considered by the panel and the adoption agency decision maker, there will a period of introduction before the child moves in with you.

Once the child has been living with you for at least ten weeks, you can then lodge your application to adopt the child.
