Assisted Gardening Scheme

The  Assisted Gardening Scheme is for elderly or disabled people.

To qualify tenants must be either:

  • 70 or over, or
  • physically incapable of doing their garden because of a disability (there is no age restriction for this). 

There must be no other household member that doesn’t fulfill the above criteria. People with relatives living nearby (within 2 mile vicinity) can apply but tenants without any close relatives will be given priority. 

The service tenants will receive two gardeners for up to one hour to work on the garden twice a year in April / May and September / October. The tenant can request help with planting, grass cutting, hedge cutting, strimming, chemical weeding hoeing and pruning (but not tree work) that can be done within the period of the visit.

For more details, please contact your Area Housing Office so a Neighbourhood Housing Officer can arrange to visit you and complete the application form.

Last updated: 26 February 2015
