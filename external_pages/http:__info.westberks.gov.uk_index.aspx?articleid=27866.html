Community Transport - for People with Restricted Mobility

Elderly or mobility-impaired residents of West Berkshire may find it difficult to use public or private transport. The voluntary sector provide a number of schemes to help.

Community Minibus Services

Community Minibus Schemes are mainly focused on helping the elderly and the mobility impaired, but they can be booked by other groups or organisations.  If space is available, individual bookings may also be taken.  The services are provided by volunteer organisations across West Berkshire with support from the council. 

The volunteer groups are committed to providing this service but are only able to help where drivers are available. 

A list of community transport service providers are listed in our  Getting There Guide [18Mb] and further details on minibus services can also be found through West Berkshire Handybuses.

Community Car Schemes

Community Car Schemes are run by volunteers, who use their own cars.  The schemes are there to help elderly and disabled people who are unable to use public transport and who don't have their own means of getting around.  

The schemes are not taxi services, so please plan ahead as they need at least 48 hours notice.  A small contribution is requested to cover the running costs of the car.  If you use a wheelchair, you'll need to discuss what assistance and support you need with the scheme operator before making your booking.

Further details can be found in our  Getting There Guide [18Mb] or through West Berkshire Volunteer Car Schemes.

If you are interested in becoming a volunteer driver, you can get details from your nearest volunteer bureau.
