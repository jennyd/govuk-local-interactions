Disabled Facilities Grants

    A Disabled Facilities Grant can pay for adaptations to be made to a disabled person’s home to make life easier for them. These changes can include:

     

    • Stair lifts to improve access to rooms and facilities
    • Widening doors and installing ramps
    • Adapting heating or lighting controls
    • Providing a graded floor shower area (wet room)


    The grant is means tested so we will look at your finances to work out if you would need to pay towards the cost of the works. The amount of grant available depends on the works that are required and your financial assessment. The maximum grant is £30,000 (for essential work only).

     

    How it works

     

    • Contact an Occupational Therapist from Essex County Council's Adult Social Care team. They will assess your needs and see if you need any adaptations.
    • If you need changes to your property, the Occupational Therapist will send a recommendation to the Council.
    • The Council will then work out if you are eligible for a grant.
    • If you are eligible, an officer will visit to assess the possible adaptation works. They will check that they can reasonably be achieved in your property. For major works, you may have to get planning permission.
    • If you want to go ahead, we will send out an application pack. You will need to get a minimum of two quotes for the works. Send your completed application and supporting documents to the Council.
    • Once you have submitted a valid application, the Council will assess it.
    • If it’s approved, we will write to you to let you know. You can then arrange for works to begin.
    • When the works are complete, we will carry out an inspection to check everything has been completed.
    • Once everything has been completed, we will pay your grant.

     

    Who can apply?

    • Disabled people who are tenants or owner-occupiers. The property must be your only or main residence.
    • Disabled people who live in a family member's property. The family member must own the property and it has to be your only or main residence. 
    • Parents of a disabled child.

     

     Am I eligible

     1. A disabled person is eligible for a grant if they receive any of the following benefits:

     

    • Housing benefit
    • Guaranteed pension credit
    • Income support
    • Job seekers allowance (income based)
    • Employment and support allowance (income based)
    • Child tax credit (with a family income below £15,460)
    • Working tax credit (with a family income below £15,460)

     

     2. If the disabled person is a child or young person under 19 they are eligible for a grant regardless of family income.

     

    3. If the disabled person does not receive any of the above benefits we will carry out a means test. We will assess their income and savings to work out how much grant they could get and if they will have to pay towards the cost of the works.

     

    Use of Home Improvement Agency

    Swan Care and Repair has been appointed as the Home Improvement Agency in Chelmsford to help disabled residents to adapt their homes. This service provides expert assistance in helping people to apply for grants and arranging works. They charge a fee which can be added to the grant. If you are not eligible for a grant, Swan can help you get the works done privately.

     

    If you are getting your own quotes for the changes, Swan can give you names of trusted contractors. This service is free. Contact Swan to find out more about how they can help.
