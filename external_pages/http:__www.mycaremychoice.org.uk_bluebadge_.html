Blue Badges

What is a Blue Badge?

This is a national scheme for people who have severe difficulty walking and who drive a vehicle regularly. It allows people to park legally near shops and offices and public buildings.

People who have difficulty in using both of their arms and cannot use parking meters easily, or those who are registered blind, can also apply for a Blue Badge.

The Blue Badge allows holders to park for free on single and double yellow lines on most streets for three hours. Blue Badge holders can use some pay and display car parks for free. Some people qualify automatically for a Blue Badge but others may need to have their mobility assessed.

Automatic applicants

You can get a Blue Badge if you:

  • Are in receipt of Disability Living at the higher rate for mobility;
  • Are registered blind;
  • Are in receipt of a War Pensions Mobility Supplement;
  • Have been awarded a lump sum benefit at tariffs 1-8 of the Armed Forces Compensation Scheme AND are verified as having a permanent, substantial disability that makes walking difficult or impossible.

Discretionary applicants

If you do not meet the automatic criteria listed above, you may still be eligible for a Blue Badge if you are more than two years old and:

  • You drive a vehicle on a regular basis but have difficulty using parking meters due to a disability that affects the use of your arms; or
  • Are unable to walk, or find walking difficult, due to a permanent disability.

In order to qualify under discretionary criteria, you may need too undergo an assessment by an independent mobility assessor who, the Government states, cannot be your own GP

What about children?

If your child is less than three years old, he or she could be eligible for a Blue Badge if one of the following conditions applies.

  • They need to have heavy or awkward medical apparatus with them at all times.
  • They need to be close to the car so that medical treatment can be given inside if needed, or they can be taken quickly to somewhere where treatment can be administered.

Is there a charge for a Blue Badge

Each Blue Badge costs £10.

How to apply

1.    Online 

The easiest way is to visit gov.uk. Here you can check your eligibility, apply for or renew your Blue Badge. You can also sign up to a reminder service to receive notifications of expiry and check the progress of your application.

Check eligibility and apply online at the Directgov website

2.     Appointment

If you meet the automatic criteria and would like support to complete an application you can attend a special session to apply in person and have your photo taken at the same time. These are held at Fred Perry House, Edward Street, Stockport SK1 3XE. You will need to make an appointment before attending one of the sessions. To make an appointment call 0161 474 3422.

If you do not meet the automatic criteria, but would like to make a discretionary application, contact the Blue Badge team on 0161 474 3422 to make an appointment with an advisor to discuss it over the phone.

If you are unsure, please call 0161 474 3422 or email blue.badges@stockport.gov.uk to discuss how best to apply.

What happens after you complete your application?

Once you complete your application, if you are eligible, you will receive a Blue Badge through the post, as well as information supplied by the Government. These will be sent to you from a central department, not from Stockport Council.

If you are refused a Blue Badge you will be sent details of the refusal and how to request a review of the decision.

Requesting a review of the decision

If your discretionary Blue Badge application is turned down following your mobility assessment, and you feel you meet the standards set out by the Department for Transport, you can request a review. To request a review of the decision you must:

  • Send a letter to Stockport Council within one month of the date on your refusal letter. You should send this letter to: BB Review Team, 4th Floor, Stopford House, Piccadilly, Stockport SK1 3XE 
  • Provide additional /medical supporting evidence which should be in addition to that made available during the application process, and which indicates the severity and permanent nature of your disability, and the impact that this has on your mobility.

The review

Once we have received your request, and the additional / medical supporting evidence, we will further review your case based on the eligibility criteria laid down by the Department for Transport, the information we have gathered during the application and assessment, and the new information you have provided. In normal circumstances you will be notified within one month of the outcome of your request
 
If your request is successful you will be notified by post. You may be asked for a passport-sized photo and payment so that your Blue Badge can be issued. If your request is unsuccessful you will be sent a letter outlining the reasons why.

What if I have a complaint?

You should contact the Blue Badge Team if you have any issues with how your application has been dealt with or if you feel you have been treated unfairly. We will provide information on how to present your complaint and the next steps you could take.
