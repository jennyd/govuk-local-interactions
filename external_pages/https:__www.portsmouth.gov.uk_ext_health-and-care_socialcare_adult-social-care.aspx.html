Adult social care

Pay for residential or non-residential care online

Pay now

At some point in our lives most of us will need help with an aspect of everyday living. This could be the result of an illness, a stay in hospital, a disability, or simply because we are getting older and need some support to carry on living independently. 

Adult social care helps almost 6,000 people, providing a wide range of services, as well as helping you to organise your own support.

  • Phone the adult social care helpdesk on 023 9268 0810

Other adult social care contacts   Open / Close

Contact us at the adult social care helpdesk in the first instance.

However, if you already have a link or keyworker in Portsmouth City Council's adult social care services, contact them first. They can help answer questions about your care to make sure you get the care and support you need.

To make a complaint, comment or compliment, email socialcare.comments@portsmouthcc.gov.uk or phone 023 9284 1172.

Let us know how we can put things right. We try to act quickly, fairly and to let you know how long it will take. Making a complaint will not affect your right to receive our services.  

Adult social care services   Open / Close

At the bottom of the page are documents giving information about the support and adult social care services in Portsmouth:

  • A-Z of services in two formats - shows the ranges of services available
  • frequently asked questions - in two formats, compiled with people who have used social care services, covers daily living, carers, getting around and money
  • jargonbuster
  • information on our performance in delivering adult social care.

Alternatively, visit the Social Care info Portsmouth website, which brings together information and advice about adult social care with a directory of local services. For example, you can search the Health and Wellbeing listings for support groups. 

Help with daily living   Open / Close

Our aim is to support people to live as independently as possible, and offer choice and control in the way that's achieved, whether this involves long-term care and support or short-term reablement. If you, or someone you care about, are struggling to live independently at home, the information below can help you consider your options. You may also wish to contact us for an assessment at Adult Social Care. 

Help with a specific condition   Open / Close

We support vulnerable adults with physical or learning disabilities, dementia and other problems associated with growing older, and those who have a loss of sight or hearing. Working with Portsmouth City Teaching PCT, we also arrange support for people who have mental health problems or issues with substance misuse. Follow the link for more information about specific conditions, or for:

  • disability information and advice, phone the Portsmouth DIAL group at the Portsmouth Disability Forum on 023 9282 4853
  • Portsmouth's one stop mental health resource, visit Thinking Ahead
  • help in speaking up about things that concern or worry you, there's SEAP, Portsmouth's free advocacy service. 

Help with money   Open / Close

Depending on your financial circumstances, you may be charged for the social care services you receive. After Adult Social Care have assessed your needs and agreed with you the best way to meet them, we will carry out a financial assessment to see how much you can afford to contribute.

We will also help you to claim any benefits you're entitled to, to maximise your income. If Adult Social Care pay for some or all of your services, you will be offered the option of self-directed support, which gives you more choice and flexibility over your services.

Have a look at our money advice pages, and Social Care info Portsmouth's money section.

Help develop adult social care   Open / Close

We are committed to involving users of our service and their carers to help develop and improve adult social care services.

If you would like to give your views on a wide range of adult social care related consultations, please contact us to get involved - email involvedsocialcare@portsmouthcc.gov.uk or phone 023 9268 8317.

Direct Payments   Open / Close

If you or someone you care for get help from social services, you can apply for direct payments. These let you choose and buy the services you need yourself, instead of getting them from your council.
