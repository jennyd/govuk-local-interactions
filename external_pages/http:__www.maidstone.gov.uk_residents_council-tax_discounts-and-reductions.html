Discounts, Reductions and Refunds

  • Single Person
  • Empty Homes
  • Students
  • Annexes
  • Second Homes
  • Disabled Person's
  • Applying
  • Refunds

A 25% discount is available if you live on your own, you are the only person over the age of 18 or if the other over 18’s are students.

Your Council Tax bill is based on at least two people living in a property. So if only one person lives in the property (as their main home), your Council Tax is reduced by 25%.

To apply you need to complete our Discount Application Form.

We're currently reviewing empty properties in the borough. You can update property records for your address using our online form. 

Update the Property Records

If a property is unfurnished and no one is living in it then you may not have to pay Council Tax for one month. The discount can only be given once and when it ends you will need to start paying again.  

From April 2014, properties that have been empty for more than two years and currently pay the full charge, will have an additional 50% premium added to their bill.

If you are a full-time student you are exempt from paying Council Tax. You will need to complete our application form. You will need to provide proof that you are a full time student. This is normally a student certificate that the place of education can give you.

When an annexe is the sole and main residence of someone who is related to the Council Tax payer of the main property, a 50% discount will be given.

If the annexe is not occupied, but is being used as part of the main house, there will be a 50% discount.

A 50% discount is available, in some cases, where a home has to be left empty, but furnished, because the owner is required to live elsewhere as a condition of their employment. The property they will be moving to must be supplied by the employer. The second home must be in England, Wales or Scotland and they must be liable for the council tax.

For example, a serving member of the armed forces who has to leave his home empty while he must live in military accommodation elsewhere in the country.

If your home has been adapted to meet the needs of a disabled person then you may be able to get a reduction.

You can get a reduction if your home has one or more of these facilities:

  • A room, other than a bathroom, a kitchen or toilet, which is mainly used by and required for meeting the needs of the disabled person
  • A second bathroom or kitchen required for meeting the needs of the disabled person
  • Sufficient floor space to permit the use of a wheelchair which is used indoors.

The discounted reduction you get will be equal to one band lower than the actual band of your home.

For example, if your property is in Band D, you will pay Band C.

To apply complete our application form.

If you think you are entitled to any of these discounts or reductions, complete our application form Discount Application Form.

If you want to apply for a discount based on severe mental impairment, download and complete the Severe Mental Impairment Discount Application Form. When you have completed it, post it to:

Council Tax
Maidstone House
King Street
Maidstone
Kent
ME15 6JQ

 

Use our on-line form to request a refund of your Council Tax.
