Dog fouling

Responsible dog owners clear up after their dogs and dispose of the waste either at home, or in one of the many waste bins provided throughout the district.

Failure to clear up after a dog in your control is a criminal offence for which a fixed penalty fine of £80 can be issued. The maximum fine that a court can impose for this offence on conviction is £1,000.

Always make sure you take something with you to pick up your dog's faeces and pay attention to what your dog is doing at all times - not having a doggy bag, or not noticing that the dog has fouled, is no excuse for not clearing up.

Any bin will do!

If there is not a dedicated dog waste bin available, dog waste can be placed in any litter bin. If there are no bins available, dog waste must be taken home and disposed of in your grey-lidded wheeled bin - leaving bagged dog waste behind is a littering offence for which fixed penalties of £80 can be issued.

How to report an incident or problem

If you see an incident of dog fouling, please report the details of the time, date and location of the incident together with any information that may help identify the culprit, including the registration number of any vehicle seen to be used to transport the dog. Any information provided will be treated in the strictest confidence.

If a public area is affected by dog fouling please let Suffolk Coastal Norse know and they will arrange clearance.

You can report any dog fouling problems or damage to dog waste bins to Suffolk Coastal Norse by calling 01394 444000 or email scs@ncsgrp.co.uk.

Dog restrictions on beaches

Seasonal dog restrictions apply to clearly signposted areas of beach at Felixstowe, Aldeburgh and Thorpeness between 1 May and 30 September each year. You must not allow your dog on the beach in these areas at these times. Any person who fails to remove their dog from one of these beaches whilst the ban is in force may be fined up to £500.
