Interested? Make an enquiry

If you would like to become a foster carer or find out more then call us now on 0300 300 8181 to speak to our Duty Officer or register your interest and we will call you back.

If you still want to go ahead, we will arrange a home visit to start the process.

If you apply to become a foster carer a social worker from the recruitment team will visit you in your home to talk about fostering and the process of checks and assessment which will take you towards approval as a foster carer.

The assessment gives us a full understanding of your strengths and experience; we need to be sure that we provide positive and safe homes for the children we are responsible for. Your assessment will then be considered by a panel of professionals. The time between your initial enquiry and approval as a foster carer by a Fostering Panel will be approximately 4-6 months.

Choosing to foster is a big decision that will change your life. You may feel that you need further information first before you can make a decision. In order to get more information you can give us a call, email us or fill in the enquiry form. If you contact us for more information we will be happy to give it to you without any obligation to apply.

Contact us

Call us on 0300 300 8181 or email us at fostering@centralbedfordshire.gov.uk.

Support for foster carers

There is a range of support available to foster carers. If you do decide to become a foster carer you will not be expected to 'go it alone'.

We feel we offer a service to our foster carers that meets their needs and the needs of our children. Our package of support includes:

  • support through the assessment process
  • an allocated social worker
  • clear and reliable guidance, including access to independent advice 
  • a mentor who is an experienced foster carer
  • training opportunities before and after approval
  • on call support outside of office hours
  • regular supervision
  • annual reviews of how fostering is for you and your family
  • financial support
  • monthly support groups
  • professional networks such as access to therapists and nurses

One of the main benefits to fostering for Central Bedfordshire is that we know our children. The services that work directly with the children, their families and the foster carers are all under one big umbrella and so can quickly, efficiently and accurately share the relevant information to those that need it. There is no middle agency to deal with. This makes better sense to us.

We also ensure that all foster carers are members of the Fostering Network, a national organisation which provides up to date information and an independent advice and mediation service. There is also the local Fostering Association run by our foster carers. The Association provides foster carers with support, social activities and advises senior managers on their views of foster carers. The Service also runs a mentoring scheme whereby experienced foster carers provide additional support for new carers.

Fees and allowances

Foster carers receive an allowance for the child and a fee, and those who care for children with complex and challenging needs could receive up to £650 per week.

There are three tiers within the fees scheme. We will provide you with a sheet that gives the full breakdown of what those tiers and fees are when you request an information pack.

What we are responsible for

Fostering in Central Bedfordshire is the responsibility of Central Bedfordshire Council. We are responsible for arranging foster care for children living in the Central Bedfordshire Council areas.

Children in Central Bedfordshire need foster care for a variety of reasons. Whatever the reason, children who require foster care will often be confused, anxious and in need of reassurance and support from the adult caring for them.

Some children will need temporary or short-term placements ranging from a few days to a few years. Some need permanent long-term foster care, so we need people like you who can care for a short while or see children through to adulthood.

Before you read any further, take a few minutes to watch our video about fostering on You Tube. This video features foster carers and young people who have been fostered talk about their experiences.

Foster Carer Charter

We have signed up to the Foster Carer Charter which is a Government sponsored initiative to recognise and underpin the valuable role foster carers play in caring for vulnerable children.

The Central Bedfordshire Foster Carer Charter (PDF 108KB) is the product of work undertaken by the Bedfordshire Foster Care Association and local foster carers with Central Bedfordshire Council and represents the aspirations of foster carers, the fostering service and children's social care services.
