Complaints and compliments

Listening to you

We are committed to providing a good-quality and fair service. We always aim to improve our services so we need to know where we are performing well, as well as where we can improve. We sometimes make mistakes and when this happens it is important that you let us know. This can help us to put things right for you and for everyone else who uses our services.

Give us a compliment

If you wish to compliment a service we have provided or the way we have treated you please let us know:

  • by completing the Your comments form
  • by telephone on 0161 253 5000
  • in person at any council office or to any member of staff.

We will give your compliments to the staff responsible.

Make a complaint

A complaint is a way of telling us that you are not happy with a particular service. It may be about the standard of service you have received, a delay in providing a service, poor communication, not consulting you or the way we have behaved.

We do not consider your first request for service to be a complaint. For example, when you first let us know about a street light not working, we do not consider this to be a complaint. Please use our Report a problem page to make a request for our services.

The services listed below have their own processes for dealing with complaints. Click on the service to find out more.

  • Adult Services complaints 
  • Childrens Social Care complaints 
  • Report a trading standards issue - When something goes wrong with the consumer goods that you have bought or when services are sub-standard
  • Standards Committee - for complaints about Councillors
  • Complaints about schools
  • Six Town Housing - Customer care and complaints
  • Six Town Housing - Anti-social behaviour

For a complaint about any other service provided by Bury Council, please view our how to make a formal complaint page. We will make sure that your complaint is passed to the right person.
