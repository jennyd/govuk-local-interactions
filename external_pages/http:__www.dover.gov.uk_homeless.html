Homelessness

We have a duty to provide people who are homeless or threatened with homelessness with advice and appropriate assistance.

If you are homeless or threatened with homelessness you should contact our Housing team straight away.

  • What to do if you are threatened with Eviction
  • What enquiries will be made?
  • The decision
  • The help that you will be entitled to
  • Protection of belongings and storage
  • Your right to a review

 

Rough Sleepers

Rough sleeping exists as the most serious form of homelessness and if you are concerned about a person sleeping rough please contact us:

Homelessness Strategy

The four East Kent authorities of Canterbury, Dover, Shepway and Thanet have developed a combined homelessness strategy.

  • East Kent Homelessness Prevention Strategy
  • East Kent Homelessness Prevention Strategy Action Plan
  • Youth Homelessness Strategy

 

Useful contacts

  • Citizens Advice Bureau
    The Cedars, 26 Victoria Road, Deal, Kent, 01304 374938
    Maison Dieu Gardens, Maison Dieu Road, Dover, Kent, 01304 202567

  • Shelter 

    The Shelter Helpline  ( 0808 800 4444) is available to all residents in Kent . This is a free service available everyday of the year from 8am to 8pm Mondays to Fridays and 8am to 5pm on Saturdays and Sundays. In addition they have Web based advice information, diagnostic tests and downloadable leaflets.

  • Local Government Ombudsmen
    Local Government Ombudsman, 10th floor, Millbank Tower, Millbank, London SW1P 4QP

  • Homeless UK website - holds information on services that can help homeless people and those at risk of homelessness. Includes details of hostels and day centres. 

  • The Mortgage Rescue Scheme is for people who are at risk of becoming homeless because they can't meet their mortgage repayments.
