Bulky waste

The council operates a collection service for 'bulky' household items. There is a charge for this service.

Bulky collections must be booked and paid for in advance. You can book in 1 to 3 items for £17 or 4 to 6 items for £34. We will collect a maximum of 6 items per booking. 

Bulky waste includes household waste that can't be fitted into black bags, or that weighs over 15kg. Small waste should be disposed of correctly either through our Kerbside collection or one of our Recycling sites you can also take reusable items direct to our Corner Shop.

How to use the service

To ensure your items are collected:

  • You need to book a collection by phone on 01792 635600 or by visiting the Contact Centre, when you book you will be allocated a collection day
  • When you book provide a full description of the items you want collected
  • Only put out the items you described when you booked the collection
  • Put the items out for collection between 7.00pm on the evening before your collection and 7.00am on the morning of your collection day
  • Try to keep items dry and don't leave items outside for a long time in all weathers. If you are leaving out an item that could absorb water (eg mattress or settee) then please cover them. Wet items may be too heavy to lift and will not be collected.

What you can put out

The types of items we will collect include:

  • Furniture
  • Televisions and large electrical goods
  • Carpets (less than 3.6 metres/12 ft)
  • White goods including washing machines and fridges
  • Doors
  • Bicycles

All the items collected are inspected and sorted for either reuse or recycling by the Swansea Waste Action team.

We will not collect the following items:

  • Clinical waste
  • Hazardous household waste
  • Trade waste
  • Items containing glass or mirrors
  • Fixtures such as radiators, bathroom suites etc

How to pay for your collection

Payment is to be made in advance either by phone on 01792 635600 or by visiting the Contact Centre at the Civic Centre. You can pay by credit or debit card or by cheque or cash presented at the Contact Centre. No payments can be made to the collection crew.  

If you are on Income Support, income related Job Seekers Allowance or guaranteed Pension Credit you can have a maximum of 9 items collected over 3 collections for free.

Further Information

The council operates five household waste recycling centres where you can take household waste for recycling or disposal, and garden waste for composting.

You can also find advice on how to recycle or dispose of specific items in Swansea Waste Forum's A-Z of WasteOpens new window.
