Demolition

 

In interest of safety of the general public, occupiers and adjacent building owners, most buildings to be demolished are subject to legal requirements, administered by the Councils Building Control team.

In most instances if you wish to demolish a building or part of a building, you are required to notify the Local Authority in writing, giving six weeks notice under Section 80 of the Building Act 1984 (application form can be found under the related forms section of the page, to the right hand side).

If the building is/or adjoins a dwelling the Town Planning Section also need to be notified. It should also be noted that if the demolition work relates to a "Listed Building" then a "Listed Building Consent" will also be required under the Town and Country Planning Act.

The demolition work should not start until:-

  • A notice under Section 81 of the Building Act has been received or
  • A period of six weeks has elapsed without a notice under Section 81  being received
    and if required:
    • Consent has been received from the Town Planning Section.

During the demolition process, in addition to the usual health and safety requirements, all conditions and works listed in the Section 81 Notice must be carried out. You have a right of appeal against any requirement, however, in these circumstances, you are advised to seek professional advice. 

Failure to give notice under Section 80 or failure to comply with a notice under Section 81 is an offense under the Act.

 You should also:-

  • send a letter to the neighbours before work starts, telling them about the work and what to expect. Give the neighbours a contact name and telephone number and keep them informed.
  • deal promptly with any complaints received on site
  • ensure that your heath and safety plan is fully adhered to
  • ensure that work which may involve noise is carried out at a reasonable time of day
  • ensure that the site is secured against unauthorised entry prior to leaving the site each evening
  • provide signs and telephone numbers so that you can be contacted in an emergency

Failure to deal effectively with any of the above problems could result in complaints from neighbours and result in possible prosecution from a number of agencies concerned with the demolition process.
