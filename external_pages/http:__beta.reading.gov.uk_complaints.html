Make a Complaint

We always aim to provide the best possible service but we know that, from time-to-time, things can go wrong.

If you are unhappy with the standard of service we provide or the way you were treated by a member of staff please let us know as soon as possible - the sooner you tell us there is a problem, the quicker we can work with you to resolve the situation.

Please note: If you are unhappy with a decision a service has made you may be able to use their appeals process to ask them to review their decision. You will usually find advice on the appeals process on your decision letter (or your Penalty Charge Notice in the case of parking or bus lane fines).

How do I complain?

The quickest and easiest way to let us know there is a problem is to talk to the staff working with you or their manager.

We believe most complaints can be resolved through an informal discussion, although we do ask staff to keep a record of the issues you raise and any actions agreed with you.

If you don't feel comfortable with this, or you have tried this route and are not happy with the outcome, you can contact the Customer Relations Team by: 

  • Completing our online Complaints and Comments Form 
  • Writing to our Customer Relations Team (Civic Offices, Bridge Street, Reading RG1 2LU)
  • Calling: 
    • 0118 937 2905 for complaints about Social Services or Special Educational Needs
    • 0118 937 3787 for complaints about all other services.

What happens next?

This depends on the service or issue to which your complaint relates.

Most Council services follow our Corporate Complaints Procedure but we have different procedures for complaints about:

  • Landlord Services provided to Council tenants and leaseholders
  • Adult Social Care Services
  • Children's Social Care Services or Special Educational Needs
  • A Councillor
  • A school.

What is the Corporate Complaints Procedure?

We have a two-stage process:

Stage 1:

We will confirm that we have received your complaint and let you know who will be investigating within five working days.

The Investigating Officer will review the case, talk to everyone involved and confirm the outcome of your complaint within 20 working days.

If you are dissatisfied with the investigation of your complaint you can ask us to review your complaint again - this is "Stage 2" of our complaints process.

Stage 2:

You must contact us within 14 days of receiving your letter saying why you are dissatisfied and the outcomes you want from a further investigation.

We will ask a more senior manager to carry out an independent review of your complaint - we will write to you within five working days to let you know who this person is.

They will ask you about your experience and record the details of your complaint in writing. They will also talk to other people involved in the case.

They will give their report and recommendations to the Head of Service for a decision.

The Head of Service will write to you to tell you their decision and send you a copy of the report. This should be within 30 days of receiving your letter.

This is the end of the Council's complaints procedure.

If you remain dissatisfied with the Council's investigation of your complaint you can escalate your complaint to the Local Government Ombudsman.

For more details see our:

  •  Comments and complaints about Council services [98kb]leaflet
  •  Corporate complaints procedure - stage two. [32kb]

What if my complaint is about "Landlord Services" for Council tenants and leaseholders?

Complaints about the "Landlord Services" we provide to our tenants and leaseholders (tenancy management, estate management, repairs, maintenance and improvements, rents and service charges) follow the corporate complaints procedure (above) but have a different escalation process following Stage 2.

If you are dissatisfied with the investigation of your complaint at Stage 2 you can either:

  • Wait eight weeks then refer your complaint to the Housing Ombudsman or
  • Refer your complaint to a designated person or tenants' panel who will carry out an independent review of your complaint and try to mediate an acceptable solution.

If this fails, you or the designated person can refer your complaint to the Housing Ombudsman Service.

For more details see our  Complaints About Landlord Services factsheet. [326kb]

What if my complaint is to do with my child's school?

Schools manage their own complaints - please complain directly to the Head Teacher or Chair of Governors in the first instance.

What if my complaint is about Children's Social Care or Special Educational Needs?

We follow a three-stage process to ensure your complaint is investigated thoroughly and fairly:

Stage 1:

We will talk to you about your complaint and try to agree a way forward outside the formal procedure. If we cannot then we aim to complete the investigation within 10 working days - we will let you know if it is going to take longer.

Stage 2:

If you are dissatisfied with the investigation you can contact the Customer Relations Team and ask us to look into your complaint at Stage 2. This will be investigated by an Investigating Officer and an Independent Person. 

They will look at the files, talk to everyone involved and write a report with recommendations for the Head of Service. They will decide what action to take. We aim to complete this stage within 25 days but can extend this up to 65 working days if we can't investigate fully within this time.

Stage 3:

If you are still dissatisfied you can ask for a review. A panel made up of three independent people will look at how your complaint has been handled. They will hear from everyone involved and write a report with recommendations about what should happen next (we will send you a copy).

The Director of Education, Adult and Children's Services will consider these recommendations and confirm their decision to you in writing within 20 days of the panel hearing.

If you are still dissatisfied you can refer your complaint to the Local Government Ombudsman.

You can find more details in our  complaints and comments about Children's Services [175kb] leaflet.

If you are a child or young person and need help to make a complaint contact the Customer Relations Team (0118 937 2905. We can refer you to an advocate who can support you to make a complain to the Council.)

What if my complaint is about Adult Social Care Services?

In September 2009, the Department of Health introduced a new complaint procedure to cover both Adult Social Care and Health Services. This meant a three-stage complaints procedure became a one-stage complaints procedure.  

When you contact the Customer Relations Team we will do our best to sort out the problem straight away.

If we can't do this we will agree a plan with you to set out how we will deal with your concerns and how long this is likely take. You can expect us to:

  • Listen to your concerns, investigate the situation fairly and respond to you in a professional way
  • Take your individual needs and wishes into account as well as those of other people involved in the situation
  • Keep you informed of our progress and let you know if there is likely to be a delay or if we need to change the way we are trying to resolve your complaint.

You can ask a friend or relative or someone with experience of representing other people's views (an advocate) to help you complain. If you need help finding an advocate please ask the Customer Relations Team or contact the Citizens Advice Bureau.

If you are dissatisfied with the way we have investigated your complaint you can meet with a senior manager to discuss your concerns. You also have the right to take your concerns to the Local Government Ombudsman.

For more information see our  complaints, comments and feedback about Adult Social Care [184kb] leaflet.

What if I want to complain about a Councillor?

All Councillors are required to follow the  Code of Conduct for Members [58kb] which sets out the standards they must meet when carrying out the business of the Council.

If you believe a Councillor has breached the code you can make a complaint using our online Councillors complaints form.

For information about how we deal with Councillor complaints see our  complaints About Councillors factsheet. [64kb]

Learning from complaints.

Your complaints are important to us because they help us help us improve.

Our annual complaints reports provide an overview of the complaints we receive and what we have put in place to help prevent the same mistakes happening again:

What does the Ombudsman do?

The Housing Ombudsmanand Local Government Ombudsmanoffer an independent, impartial service to investigate complaints that we haven't been able to resolve.

They will look at the way we have handled your complaint to make sure that we have applied our procedures and processes fairly. If they find that we are at fault they will recommend actions that we should take to make things right.

If they find we have followed our procedures fairly this will end your complaint.

The Ombudsman will not usually make any judgments about the fairness or effectiveness of our policies and procedures. If you believe a policy is unfair or needs reviewing you should talk to your Ward Councillor.
