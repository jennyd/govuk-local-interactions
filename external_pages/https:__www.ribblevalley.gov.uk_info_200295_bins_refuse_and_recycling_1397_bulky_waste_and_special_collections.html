Bulky Waste and Special Collections

Bulky household waste

Is your item in good condition or in working order?  Can it be reused?

If your furniture, furnishings (including selected white goods and electrical goods), are still in a good condition then why not think about donating them?

Recycling and re-using items in this way can help people in need who may not be able afford to buy brand new items.

Charities and not for profit community based organisations are always on the lookout for good quality household items that can be reused for emergency re-homing of vulnerable people or sold through their shops. 

Most even offer to collect large items for free!

For a list of organisations who collect items see Donating items to Charity and Community Organisations.

(For clothing and bric-a-brac see charity shops.)

How to book a special collection 

We offer a collection service for the disposal of large items of furniture and domestic appliances. There is a charge for this service.

Contact 01200 425111 to book a special collection. You will be given a price for the collection of the items and can pay directly over the phone when you book or visit the Council Offices and pay in person. We will not be able to collect you items until we have received payment.

There is a minimum charge of £13.05 for up to four items (each additional item will be charged at £3.25 per item)

Construction and Demolition (inert) Waste

From the 1 June 2015 , you will need an inert waste permit to deposit waste such as soil, rubble, plasterboard, bricks, tiles, concrete etc at one of Lancashire County Councils Household Waste Recycling Centres (HWRCs). For further information and prices please visit Recycling Sites and Tips.

Asbestos removal

The council does not remove asbestos and asbestos should never be put in your bin for collection.

For more information on what to do with asbestos see Lancashire County Council's disposal of asbestos. 

Householders can take small amounts of asbestos to the tip but it will only be accepted if wrapped in heavy duty polythene sheets. Asbestos wrapping materials are provided free of charge (in limited quantities) for householders and can be collected from the tip. Smaller pieces of asbestos should be double-bagged and securely sealed. Phone before visiting the tip with your asbestos.

  • Henthorn Household Waste Recycling Centre - 01200 42939

Chapel Hill, Longridge does not accept asbestos. 

Large amounts such as garages cannot be taken to the tip. A skip is required and it must be dismantled not broken up (Goggles, Masks, Gloves must be worn.)

Lancashire County Council operates 15 Household Waste Recycling Centres (HWRCs) throughout Lancashire where householders can recycle their rubbish. See the Lancashire County Council website for a list of all HWRCs.

The opening hours at all HWRC's are:

8.00am - 7.00pm 1 April - 30 September (7 days a week)
8.00am - 5.00pm 1 October - 31 March (7 days a week)

Closed only on Christmas Day, Boxing Day and New Years Day

Commercial vehicles

Certain commercial vehicles or trailers must obtain a permit from Lancashire County Council before visiting a HWRC.
