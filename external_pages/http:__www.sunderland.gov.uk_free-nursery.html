Free early education entitlement

Jump to:

  • Key contacts
  • External links

The free early education entitlement is available to all children aged three and four years old, and children become eligible the term after their third birthday.

In addition, many families across Sunderland can benefit from free childcare places for two year olds.

Families must be in receipt of one of the following benefits to be eligible for a place:

  • Income Support
  • Income-based Employment and Support Allowance
  • Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance
  • Guarantee element of State Pension Credit
  • Support under part VI of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999
  • Child Tax Credit (including Working Tax Credit) and have an income below £16,190
  • Have a child that receives Disability Living Allowance for children 
  • Current statement for Special educational needs or and Education Health care plan
  • Have a child that is in the care of the local authority
  • An adopted child

If eligible, children aged two can receive 15 hours a week during term time only or 12 hours for 48 weeks of the year. Children can take up their place in the term after their second birthday. Their place is available until they start their nursery education at age three.

All children aged three or four are entitled to up to 15 hours per week of free early learning and care over 38 weeks of the year.

This can be taken flexibly to meet individual requirements.


How to access free early education

To access free early education, please contact the childminder or day nursery of your choice.

Our Childcare directory includes all of the settings that are eligible to deliver the free early education entitlement.

All providers are required to deliver the Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) framework to support children's learning and development; making sure each child achieves the most that they can in the earliest years of their life.

The early education entitlement is a free, part-time place and no charges should be made in relation to the free entitlement.

Parents may choose to access additional childcare with their chosen provider. It is the responsibility of the provider to make sure that bills are set out clearly so parents can easily see what they have accessed in relation to the free entitlement and how any charges relate to these additional services.
