Mutual exchange

Due to the high demand for social housing, it may take some time for you to be offered a move. If you are an existing social housing tenant (i.e. a tenant of a local authority or housing association) you can exchange homes with another social housing tenant. Tewkesbury Borough Council has signed up to 'Homeswapper', an online service that allows social tenants to register their interest in swapping their property with another social tenant.

To begin the process of swapping homes with another social tenant, simply register with Homeswapper. 0nce your details have been registered, Homeswapper will notify you of any 'matches' - i.e. other members whose details match what you are looking for, and vice versa. Books containing details of all applicants looking for a swap are held in the reception area at the council offices and are renewed every month with new applicants that have joined the scheme.

In addition to receiving these matches, you will be able to search the system yourself to look for suitable properties. Homeswapper will also notify you, by text or email, of new matches from newly joining members.

You find a property on the register - what happens next?

In the first instance, you should contact the tenant of the property you are interested in and arrange an appointment to view the property. Remember that you are dealing with a stranger and so you should take normal personal safety precautions. There are a number of personal safety tips given on the Homeswapper website.

How do you proceed with the exchange?

When you find a suitable property you should contact your landlord to seek guidance on how to proceed and also to ask for their permission to exchange. Your landlord will not unreasonably withhold their permission but there are reasons why permission may not be given in certain circumstances. You will be advised in writing if permission is withheld and the reasons will be explained to you.

When a mutual exchange is completed, the tenancy is assigned from one tenant to another. The terms and conditions of that particular tenancy are also being transferred. Before exchanging with the tenant of a different landlord you should ensure you understand these terms and conditions.

If you proceed to exchange without the landlord's permission, you may be contravening your tenancy conditions. It is therefore advisable to seek independent housing advice from any of the organisations detailed before you complete the exchange.

Is Homeswapper free?

Homeswapper is free for tenants of landlords who have signed up to the Homeswapper scheme.  Currently, the landlords in Tewkesbury borough who are members of the Homeswapper scheme are:

· Fosseway Housing Association.

· Guinness Partnership.

· Knightstone Housing Association.

· Oxbode Housing Association.

· Severn Vale Housing Society.

· Westlea Housing Association.

However, landlords are joining Homeswapper all the time, so you should check the Homeswapper website to check whether your landlord is a member or not.

For further details of the Homeswapper scheme, please visit the Homeswapper website.
