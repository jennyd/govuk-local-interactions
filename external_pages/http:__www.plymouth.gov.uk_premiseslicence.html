Premises Licence

A premises licence is required where licensable activities, such as the sale of alcohol and regulated entertainment take place.

Applications for licensed premises have to be accompanied by a variety of documents which includes a scaled drawing, and an operating schedule. The operating schedule allows you to set out how you intend to satisfy the following four licensing objectives:

  • Prevention of crime and disorder
  • Prevention of public nuisance
  • Public safety
  • Protection of children from harm

To help you to complete your operating schedule we've come up with a pool of licensing conditions, from which you can choose model conditions which are relevant to your licensed premises.

Tacit Consent

This means that if we have not dealt with your application within a specified time then your application is deemed to have been granted or approved. Tacit consent will not apply in relation to any applications submitted under the Licensing Act 2003, as there is an overriding public interest in ensuring that the suitability of applicants is assessed before a licence is issued.
