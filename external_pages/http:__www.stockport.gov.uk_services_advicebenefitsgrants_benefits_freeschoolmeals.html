Free School Meals and Clothing Grants

Find out whether your child is eligible for free school meals or a clothing grant.

  • Free school meals
  • Clothing grant

Free school meals

You can get Free School Meals if you are responsible for a child (for example, you receive Child Benefit for them) who is still at school - including nursery children who attend full days and sixth form students - as long as you get one of these qualifying incomes: 

  • Income Support
  • income-based Jobseekers Allowance
  • income-related Employment and Support Allowance
  • Universal Credit
  • support under Part VI of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999
  • the guaranteed element of State Pension Credit
  • Child Tax Credit (provided you are not entitled to Working Tax Credit and have an annual gross income of no more than £16,190)
  • Working Tax Credit run-on paid for 4 weeks after you stop qualifying for Working Tax Credit
Universal Free school meals for all Key Stage One children at school

All reception, year 1 and year 2 pupils are entitled to receive a free meal at lunchtime. For the school year starting in September 2015 this benefits children born between September 2008 and August 2011.

If your child is in this age group but is also eligible for free school meals because you receive one of the qualifying benefits we still need you to apply for these as normal. This is because schools receive a 'pupil premium' payment (up to £1,300) for each eligible child which they can spend on resources which will benefit your child's education.

If you do not receive one of the qualifying incomes, but have a child in the eligible age group for Universal Infant Free School Meals, please contact your child's school for more information.

How to apply

If you are making a claim for Housing Benefit or Council Tax Support then you don’t need to make a separate claim for Free School Meals. You can request Free School Meals on the same form and we will assess your entitlement at the same time.

You can apply for Housing Benefit or Council Tax Support using our online application form.

If you do not need to claim Housing Benefit or Council Tax Support and only want to claim Free School Meals then you should complete the Free School Meals online claim form.

You should also make a claim online if you are already receiving Housing Benefit and Council Tax Support and one of your children starts full-time nursery or at a school maintained by the Local Authority.

You do not have to send in proof of your qualifying income as we will verify your claim with the Department for Work and Pensions.

Please note:

  • If your child is not attending a Stockport school, you must make your claim for Free School Meals with the Authority that is educating your child. 
  • Free schools meals are not usually available for children who go to independent (private) schools.
  • Schools cannot provide free meals to children until authorised by the Council.
  • Children who are 16 and over and have left school can't get free school meals but if they go to a further-education college you can apply to their college for free meals if you get a qualifying income.

Secondary-school clothing grants

Secondary-school clothing grants are: 

  • For children in Year 7 to Year 11 at secondary school 
  • Only paid once per year to help towards the cost of school clothing. We don't pay a second grant if a child changes secondary school during the year. 
  • Paid automatically if you are registered for Free School Meals

The grant for 2015/16 is £80 for a year 7 child and £20 for a year 8 to11 child.

Payment will be sent automatically from the Free School Meals records so there is no need for anyone to apply. However you will need to provide us with your bank details to enable a payment to be made.

If you have not provided these details to us please email us - you need to provide your full name and address, your sort code and bank account number.

Post Office Card Accounts (for receipt of pensions, benefits and tax credits)are not suitable for everyday banking and clothing grant payment cannot be made to these account. You will need at least a basic bank account if you wish to receive a clothing grant payment.

  • Please note we cannot make cash payments for clothing already purchased. 
  • You must have your own bank/building society account to receive a payment by bacs.
  • We can no longer issue a payment by cheque.
