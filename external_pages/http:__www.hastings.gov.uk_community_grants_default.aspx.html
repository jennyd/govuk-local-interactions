Youth Council

The Youth Council is an organisation working together to make a difference for young people in Hastings and every member of the Youth Council plays an important role in empowering the young people of Hastings.

Anyone can become a youth councillor, as long as you are aged between 13 and 25.  You don't need to have any experience or qualifications but it is important that you have opinions and are prepared to express them.

The Youth Council is supported by Hastings Borough Council in that they provide workers, office space and an annual budget.

The Youth Council hold a bi-monthly open meeting to discuss any issues, give a progress update of work and plan for the next two months work.

Recently the Youth Council have campaigned on:

  • Raise Mental Health awareness and Better services in Hastings 
  • Better sex education for LGBT in schools
  • Cheaper transport for Young people in Hastings and St Leonards
  • Continue to help develop and monitor Youth strategies  

This year, there are two ways in which you can be involved.  You can either choose to become a full member and commit to attending the weekly meetings or you can opt to join our database so that you are included in mail outs.  

Contact hyc@hastings.gov.uk for further information.

Register your details to receive Grant Application updates

We operate various grant schemes to support local organisations to improve the service to local residents, organise local events and generally help communities to help themselves.

Grants are available at different times throughout the year and this registration form will allow us to keep community and voluntary organisations and social enterprises informed as opportunities arise.

Once your registration has been approved, you'll receive regular updates and have access to the online grant applications as they become available - you must be a member of a community or voluntary group, or a social enterprise to register.

Community Partnership Fund 2015/16 to 2016/17

Community Partnership Funding enables local community and voluntary organisations to deliver services and projects on behalf of the Council to meet the needs of our most vulnerable residents.

The Council has agreed to a two-year programme of funding covering the financial years 2015 to 17. The funding available for each of the financial years is as follows:

  • 2015/16: £269,059 (subject to budget approval February 2015)
  • 2016/17: £250,494 (subject to budget approval February 2015)

Seniors Forum

Older People's Day 2015

Across East Sussex, throughout September and October, there's a whole programme of fun and informative events and activities going on in celebration of Older People's Day (officially 01 October).

Choose from afternoon teas and soup festivals, history talks, craft and hobbies, music, films, poetry, social gatherings, lots of fun fitness activities for all levels, lifestyle shows and more.

Many events are free – and all of them are very welcoming. If you have difficulty getting out and about, contact your local Community Transport service on 01273 517332.

Visit www.eastsussex.gov.uk for the full programme of events and further information.

About the Senior's Forum

The Hastings and St Leonards Seniors Forum (HSSF) was the first forum within East Sussex that was led by Seniors for Seniors.

Several special interest groups have since been formed that concentrate on issues of concern, they are Crime and Safety, Transport and Quality of Life.

The aim of the forum is to promote the welfare and interests of Seniors within the town of Hastings and St Leonards.  The group work in partnership and liaise with organisations both voluntary and statutory to make improvements for Seniors as well as setting up their own projects and initiatives.

The HSSF hold several open meetings each year to raise awareness and tackle issues of concern and also produce a quarterly newsletter.

Visit www.hssf.org.uk for further information.

Hastings and Rother Disability Forum

Hastings and Rother Disability Forum (HRDF) is a local community group run by disabled people for disabled people.  The group aim to promote the right of disabled people to achieve full integration and equality of opportunity in society.

The group work with many different organisations to raise awareness and represent the views of disabled people.  Forum members liaise closely with the local authorities on issues such as planning,transport and access to information.  The group also spend much time assisting other local voluntary groups, offering information and advice.

The group tackle areas of concern and campaign to make improvements throughout Hastings and Rother.

HRDF also hold regular open meetings and produce a quarterly newsletter.

Hastings and Rother Rainbow Alliance

Hastings Borough Council works closely with Hastings and Rother Rainbow Alliance (HRRA) to promote community cohesion and deliver the community cohesion action plan.

The Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender (LGBT) Research Forum aims at improving access to services and to establish how statutory agencies are meeting their responsibilities as part of the equalities agenda.

The council has also helped HRRA in a number of events, including the celebrations of LGBT people as active citizens and we continue to raise awareness of HRRA work and LGBT people history.

Black and Minority Ethnic Groups

The population of Hastings is becoming more diverse and multi-cultural. The Council support the Black and Minority Ethnic Groups (BME) and individuals and engage with this community and empower them to represent themselves. There are felt to be many ways of achieving this and the Council works with a variety of BME community groups and individuals to enable this to happen, where possible led by them and always on their terms.

Hastings Intercultural Organisation

Hastings Intercultural Organisation (HIO) aims to support and empower BME communities, also challenges prejudice and discrimination in all areas of members' lives.

Hastings Older People Group (Hope-G) is an organisation based in Hastings to represent the senior's BME group. There website is www.hope-g.org

Hastings Borough Council work very closely with HIO and Hope-g to enable members of the BME community have a voice in the town.


In the main, this work supports the development of BME groups in their own right, and also encourages the representation of BME groups and individuals within existing infrastructure; such as on the Youth Council, Seniors and Disability forums mentioned on these pages, the Community Network and other special interest groups that exist throughout Hastings.
