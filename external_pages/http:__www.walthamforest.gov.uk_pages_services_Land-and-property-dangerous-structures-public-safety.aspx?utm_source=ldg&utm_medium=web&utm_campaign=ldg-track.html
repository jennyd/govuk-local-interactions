Report a dangerous structure

Last updated 23 December 2014

On this page:

  • Reporting a dangerous structure
  • What happens next?

A dangerous building or structure is one that is likely to collapse or partially collapse, or from which materials such as timber, masonry or tiles may be about to fall.

Reporting a dangerous structure

If you consider the dangerous structure to be an immediate problem or hazard, phone Waltham Forest Direct on 020 8496 3000 providing us with: 

  • The location of the building or structure (please be as detailed as possible)
  • A description of the danger and any additional useful information
  • Your name and a contact telephone number or email

For buildings and structures that are defective, but do not pose a threat to passers-by and the general public you can complete our dangerous structure online form.

Alternatively you can email wfdirect@walthamforest.gov.uk, phone 020 8496 3000, or visit a Library Plus branch for advice on how to complete the form.

What happens next?

A dangerous structure surveyor will investigate the structure as soon as possible and not more than two hours after receiving such a report, and take steps to have any immediate danger removed.

If the structure is not considered to be immediately dangerous, we will contact the owner and may serve them with a notice ordering that they remove the danger within a specified time, normally 14 to 28 days.

If the owner fails to take action within the period of the notice we can commence court proceedings to get an Order to make the owner carry out any works required to make the structure safe. We can also instruct an emergency contractor to remove the immediate danger in default by the building owner, and we can recharge the cost of doing this emergency work back to the responsible owner.
