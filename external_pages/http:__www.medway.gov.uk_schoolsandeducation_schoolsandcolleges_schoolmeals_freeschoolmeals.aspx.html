Apply for free school meals

Free school meals for infants

All children attending school in Reception, year 1 and year 2 are entitled to receive a free school meal.

Even though your child will receive a school meal, if you are receiving any of the benefits listed below you should still complete an application form, as your child’s school will also be entitled to extra funding of £1,300 known as Pupil Premium.

Download and complete the application for free school meals (pdf 359KB) and send it to us using the freepost address: RSBA-CYBG-SZSG

Free school meals from Year 3

If you have children under 19 years old and still in full time education, they may be entitled to a free school meal if you receive either:

  • Employment and Support Allowance (income-based)
  • Income Support
  • Jobseeker’s Allowance (income-based)
  • The Guarantee element of State Pension Credit
  • Support under part VI of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999
  • Child Tax Credit

You will not be entitled to Free School Meals if you receive any element of Working Tax Credit or if your annual household income (as assessed by HMRC) is greater than £ 16,190.

Proof of your benefit will not need to be provided at this stage, however, you will be informed if any proof of benefit is required at a later date.

Download and complete the application for free school meals (pdf 359KB) and send it to us using the freepost address: RSBA-CYBG-SZSG

Change of circumstance

You must notifiy us if your child changes school (and all other circumstances remain the same), so we can ensure they still receive their free school meals.

You will need to reapply for free school meals if you change:

  • your name
  • your address
  • type of benefit
  • if you stop receiving benefit

If you do not reapply it could result in you having to pay for any free school meals your child may have received.
