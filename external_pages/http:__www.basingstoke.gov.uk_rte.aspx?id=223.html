Homelessness

We are committed to trying to help anyone who thinks they are homeless or are going to become homeless.

Where possible we will assist you to prevent this from happening. If we can’t help, we will tell you about a range housing options which will help you find a new home. However, there is high demand for housing in the borough, and it may not be possible to help you in the way that you would like. We can:

  • advise and assist you in resolving disputes with your current landlord to stop your eviction.
  • help to mediate with family or friends to help keep you in your current home
  • advise and assist you in accessing accommodation in the private sector
  • help you to apply for housing benefit
  • provide you with information on low cost home ownership options
  • advise you on hostel accommodation
  • arrange comprehensive debt advice, benefit information and support services
  • Help you adapt your current home to make it more suitable to your needs

Homelessness assessments

We have a legal responsibility to assist people who are losing their home or have no home at all. The Housing Act 1996 (as amended by the Housing Act 2002) sets out how we must deal with homelessness, and for whom we must find temporary accommodation. Unfortunately, this is NOT for everyone who is homeless. When dealing with an application, certain facts must be established. We need to know whether an applicant:

  • is eligible for help
  • is homeless, or threatened with homelessness, at the time of the application
  • would fall within a priority need category, as stated in the Act
  • is intentionally homeless
  • once the above are established, we may also enquire into whether you have a local connection with the borough, as stated in the Act.

Once all the above enquires have been made, we will inform you of our decision in writing. If you meet the above criteria, we will ensure that you have temporary accommodation. This accommodation is likely to be in the private sector, but the council can help you to secure such accommodation.

Are you aware of anyone sleeping outside within the borough?

StreetLink is a website that enables the public to alert us about people sleeping rough in this borough. This service offers you a means to act when you see someone sleeping rough and is the first step someone can take to ensure rough sleepers are connected to the local services and support available to them. You can report someone sleeping rough here so that we and other agencies can provide them with assistance.

Rough Sleeper Severe Weather Emergency Protocol 2014/15

Rough sleepers in Basingstoke and Deane can access emergency accommodation and support during periods of exceptionally cold weather through the Severe Weather Emergency Protocol (known as SWEP). Presently SWEP in Basingstoke and Deane is activated by a weather forecast predicting three consecutive nights, or more, of a temperature of zero degrees Celsius (0oC) or lower or lower or other significant adverse weather conditions.

If you are a rough sleeper or you come into contact with a rough sleeper, you can contact or signpost individuals to:

  • May Place House on 01256 840110 or at May Place Basingstoke Hampshire RG21 7NX

When SWEP has been activated or deactivated, relevant partner agencies will be notified.

Once you are aware that SWEP has been activated, if you are sleeping rough or an individual approaches you stating that they are rough sleeping, please direct them towards May Place House for an assessment as early as possible. Accommodation will not be offered at May Place House, but emergency bed spaces may be available at Mary Rose Court.

When SWEP is activated Local Connection and No Recourse to Public Funds criteria, which are usually applied within the borough, are relaxed. However, where appropriate, local services will endeavour to reconnect individuals to their district or country of origin that day, or as soon as possible thereafter.
