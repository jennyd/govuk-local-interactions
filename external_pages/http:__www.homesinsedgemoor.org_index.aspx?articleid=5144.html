What is the Right to Buy?

The Right to Buy scheme was introduced in 1980 and allows Council and Housing Association tenants in England buy their home at a discount.

How to apply

If you are interested in buying your council property and would like more information regarding your eligibility or the application process, please click on the official Government Right to Buy website link below:

Right to Buy

Help and Advice

For friendly, impartial advice, contact the Government's Right to Buy Agent service. Advisers are available Monday to Friday, 8am to 6pm.

  • Contact the Right to Buy Agent service on 0300 123 0913
  • To email them or request a call back, visit the official Government Right to Buy website.

Homes in Sedgemoor process the first stage of your application and will confirm whether or not you have the Right to Buy your home.

Homes in Sedgemoor have up to 4 weeks to reply to you, up to 8 weeks if you have been a tenant for less than 5 years.

Property valuations and sales

Please be aware that once your eligibility for the Right to Buy has been confirmed by Homes in Sedgemoor, your application is passed to your landlord, Sedgemoor District Council.

Please also be aware that once your right to buy has been admitted, we will only complete emergency repairs to the property from this date until the sale of the property or the cancellation of the Right to Buy.

Sedgemoor District Council will arrange to value the property and will send you an offer notice setting out the property value and discount, within 8 weeks for a house and 12 weeks for a leasehold property.

You have up to 12 weeks to accept your landlord's offer. You will need to get a survey, appoint a solicitor and arrange a mortgage or loan.

Once you are happy with Sedgemoor District Council's terms and have arranged to raise the money you can proceed and complete your purchase.

Paying back the Right to Buy discount

If you applied to purchase your home under the Right to Buy scheme and you sell within five years you will usually have to repay some or all of the discount. After five years, you can sell the property without repaying any of the discount you received.

Leasehold Flats

Subletting your flat

If you have bought a flat and intend to sublet your leasehold property you must contact your landlord, Sedgemoor District Council, to your register the sublet. There will be a fee payable to the council to register.

Please be aware:

  • Notifying the council of any sublets is a requirement under the terms of your lease.
  • Each time you sublet your leasehold property and there is a new tenancy, you are required to register this with the Council.
  • You will remain responsible for the payment of the service charges and ground rent for your flat.
  • If you intend to rent out your property and move to a different address, please inform the council of your new address to ensure you receive any correspondence regarding your property, which may require action.

To register to sublet your flat, contact 0845 408 2540 and ask for Sedgemoor District Council's Property Lawyer.

Selling your flat

If you are selling your flat the purchaser will require information about the lease terms, your flat and the block.

It is important to keep safely, all consultation papers, service charge invoices and miscellaneous correspondence concerning the management and maintenance of the property.

If you fail to divulge information that you are aware of to a prospective purchaser, you may be liable under the terms of your sale contract.

Under your lease, your solicitor must let Sedgemoor District Council have a copy of the legal documents transferring the lease within 28 days of sale. Until Sedgemoor District Council is notified in writing, demands for service charges and ground rent will still be sent to you after the sale.

If you are selling your leasehold flat, contact 0845 408 2540 and ask for Sedgemoor District Council's Property Lawyer.
