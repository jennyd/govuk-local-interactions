Motorcycles

We aim to increase the skills of motorcyclists by providing additional training opportunities, as well as educating other road users to the vulnerability of motorcyclists.

A lifelong learning approach to motorcycling should be adopted. Details of all of the training courses we support can be found at Motorcycling Matters training website.

We also run two annual events for motorcyclists:

  • Ride Free in April which starts the summer riding season
  • Winter Skills in October which provides advice to continue riding through the Winter. This year's Winter Skills Day is on Sunday, 6 October. For more details visit the Motorcycling Matters what's on section of our website

Further information of our support for motorcyclists, including how we are working in partnership with other agencies, can be found at Motorcycling Matters.

Highways - driving advice
Speed limits

Speed is a significant factor in about one third of road crashes in the UK. This is particularly so in urban areas, where speeding vehicles can adversely affect the quality of life of many communities.

Comprehensive information on the speed limits you would expect to come across on UK roads is given in The Highway Code.

If a road has street lighting on it with no speed limit signs the road has a 30mph limit. We are not permitted to place 30mph repeater signs on such roads. The street lighting should be sufficient evidence of 30mph limit.

RoSPA, the Royal Society for the Prevention of Accidents, has reviewed a number of studies carried out both in the UK and around the world. They found that traffic calming in a 20mph zone reduced the number of accidents causing injury by 15% on average.

We have introduced 20mph limits in Dunstable and Shefford, making them better environments for cyclists and pedestrians as well as reducing casualties.

Tackling speeding

The Council receives many requests from residents to help reduce speeding and rat running.  Only the Police can enforce speed limits and issue penalties for speeding.  The Council can however change speed limits and introduce traffic calming measures to reduce the speed drivers are able to travel.

Changing speed limits?
If you would like a speed limit to be lowered, raised or extended, please contact us at highways@centralbedfordshire.gov.uk. Your request will then be assessed. The police view on a change to a speed limit is vital and will be sought. Account should also be taken of the characteristics of the road, such as its alignment, the level of activity alongside the road, the accident record and the degree of severance caused to a community by the speed of vehicles.

If a change in the speed limit is agreed then a new Speed Limit Order has to be made. This statutory legal process takes approximately 9 months to complete.

Road humps
Speed control humps are a "slowing feature" - usually formed by a change of priority (traffic entering the system has to turn sharp left or right into the road, or has to "give way")

The shape of speed control humps is strictly regulated. They must be between 50mm and 100mm high, at least 2.75m long and extend over the full width of the road, except for a drainage channel at either end. They may have either flat tops or round tops.

75mm high hump is the most common. This is because it has been found to reduce traffic to around 22 mph. The 100mm high humps reduce speeds to 17mph. Flat topped humps can be of any length and are often known as "speed tables". These are sometimes used to reduce the impact on long vehicles such as buses.

Speed control humps can lead to complaints about increased noise and sometimes increased vibration from traffic. They have however been proved to reduce traffic speed.

Weight limits

Weight or width restrictions can be imposed for structural or for environmental reasons. Such restrictions prevent large vehicles from using inappropriate roads, routes and areas in order to:

  • reduce danger to pedestrians and other road users
  • prevent damage to buildings, roads and bridges
  • preserve the character, amenity and environment of an area
  • reduce and manage congestion on the roads

Some limits will include "except for access" clauses. These are needed to permit heavy goods vehicle access to local shops, businesses, residential properties for deliveries/removals etc.

Where a limit restricts the use of a road, a recommended alternative route will need to be considered as this can sometimes push a problem from one route or area to another.

Only the Police can enforce weight or width limits and issue penalties.

If you would like a weight limit to be changed, please contact us at highways@centralbedfordshire.gov.uk. Your request will then be assessed.

If a change in the weight limit is agreed then a new Traffic Regulation Order has to be made. This statutory legal process can take between 3 and 9 months to complete, depending on the complexity and how many objections are received.

Dangerous junctions
We keep an accident database which contains information on accidents that have caused and injury across Central Bedfordshire. This information is supplied to us by the Police. We constantly review this information to help identify where we need to make safety improvements.

Safety is a high priority for the public and we often receive phone calls and e-mail's telling us about locations of concern or that are considered dangerous. Central Bedfordshire Council will investigate these concerns and will seek ways in which safety can be improved.
