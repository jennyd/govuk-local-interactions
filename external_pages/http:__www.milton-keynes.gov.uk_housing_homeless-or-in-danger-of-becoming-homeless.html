Homeless or becoming homeless?

This page explains the help we offer if you are homeless or about to be homeless.
Severe Weather Emergency Protocol (SWEP) - We aim to get rough sleepers off the streets when the night time temperature is predicted to be zero degrees or below for three consecutive nights, by providing emergency accommodation. - Weather forecast for Milton Keynes

We offer advice about housing options via the Housing Options Self Help Tool. The help we offer will depend on your circumstances. 

We only have a duty to offer housing to households who are:

1.    homeless and

2.    eligible and

3.    in priority need and

4.    not intentionally homeless and

5.    have a local connection to Milton Keynes (or no connection to anywhere)

We will consider you to be homeless if you:

  • have no home here or abroad in which you have a legal right to live or
  • have a home but it is not reasonable for you to carry on living in it

You might not be eligible for assistance if:

  • You are a visitor to this country
  • You are a student from another country
  • You do not have valid permission to be in the country
  • You are seeking asylum but haven't had your decision yet
  • You have claimed asylum but the Home Office has refused asylum
  • You are not habitually resident in the UK or Ireland
  • The Home Office has said you no longer have a right to stay in the UK

You may have a priority need for housing if any of the following apply:

  • You are pregnant
  • You are 16 or 17 years old
  • Someone in your household is under 18 (or under 19 if in full-time education)
  • You are 18 to 21 years old and were in social services' care
  • You are homeless because of fire, flood, or a other disaster
  • You are aged 60 or over
  • You have a mental illness or disability which makes you vulnerable
  • You have a physical disability which makes you vulnerable
  • You have been in the UK's armed forces and this has made you vulnerable
  • You have been in prison or remanded in custody and this has made you vulnerable
  • You have left your home because of violence or threats and this has made you vulnerable
  • You are vulnerable for another reasons

If you lost your home as a result of something you have deliberately done, or failed to do, then we may consider you to be intentionally homeless. For example:

  • You were evicted because you did not pay your rent when you could have
  • You gave up your home when it was reasonable for you to carry on living there
  • You colluded with your landlord or other person to make yourself homeless to get housing through the council
  • You behaved in a criminal, unreasonable or anti-social way and lost your housing as a result

For the purposes of a homeless application, we will consider you to have a local connection to Milton Keynes if you:

  • Have lived here for 6 months out of the last 12 months, or 3 years of the last 5 years
  • Have close family who live here
  • Work in Milton Keynes
  • Have a local connection with another area, but someone on your application might experience domestic violence if they return there
  • Are not connected to any council in this country. For example, if you have recently returned from abroad or have been in the armed forces
