    Vehicle access kerbs

    Find out about the licence you'll need to create or change a vehicle access onto your property.

    If you want to apply for a licence to create or change a vehicle access please tell us and we'll send you an application form with a standard detailed drawing in about five working days.

    Is the road classified A, B or C?

    You'll need planning permission before you apply for your licence by calling our planning department on 01226 772600.

    When applying for your licence, please remember to tell us:

    • the current type of footway/verge, like tarmac or flags
    • the likely start date of the works
    • if it's to create a crossing to improve mobility for wheelchair users

    You can either:

    • pay us and we'll do the work
      we'll work out the costs of this when we've discussed your needs
    • employ a street works accredited private contractor to do the work - you'll need to pay for two highway inspections
      Please note: all private contractors are responsible for any third party insurance claims made as a result of works on the highway. We must see proof of their public liability insurance cover to the value of £10million.

    When you receive your application form you'll need to:

    • fill in the form
    • send the completed form back to us with a cheque for £51 (application fee only)
    • plus another cheque for £102 to cover two highway inspections if a private contractor is to do the work
      Please make all cheques payable to Barnsley MBC

    We'll send your licence in around five working days from the date we receive your completed form and payments.

    Important: It is an offence to carry out works in the highway without a licence, if you are found doing so we'll issue a licence at a cost of £225, this includes an extra £72 to cover administration costs. Legal action could also be taken.

    You can find legislation about vehicle access kerbs in the Highways Act 1980, Section 184
