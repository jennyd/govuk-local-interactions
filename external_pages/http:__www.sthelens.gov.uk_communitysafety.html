Community Safety

'Community safety' relates to your sense of security and personal safety in areas where you live, work or socialise. In St.Helens the council works together with a number of local agencies as part of a Community Safety Partnership (CSP) to reduce crime and disorder, together with the local community. The CSP includes agencies such as Merseyside Police, Merseyside Fire and Rescue Service, Housing Associations and community, private and voluntary sector organisations.

The CSP is better known as 'Safer St.Helens' and has been in place since 1999. Through effective partnership working and community engagement, we have seen positive reductions to the levels of crime and anti-social behaviour (ASB) in the area.

In the council, the Safer Communities Team is responsible for monitoring the performance of the CSP, which is done through the delivery of a local CSP Delivery Plan - with the main aim of keeping St.Helens a safe place to live, work and visit and reducing the fear of crime in our local communities.

To find out more about community safety, and the St.Helens CSP visit the Safer St.Helens website.

If you do have any concerns relating to community safety please contact the Safer Communities Team through our council contact centre on 01744 676789 or email us at contactcentre@sthelens.gov.uk
