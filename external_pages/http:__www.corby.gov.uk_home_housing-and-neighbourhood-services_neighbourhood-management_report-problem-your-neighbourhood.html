Report a Problem in your Neighbourhood

According to the Crime and Disorder Act 1998, anti social behaviour (ASB) is classed as:

Behaviour which is likely to cause harassment, alarm and distress to one or more persons not of the same household.

The term antisocial behaviour covers a wide range of selfish and unacceptable activity that can blight the quality of community life or the environment we live in. Terms such as 'nuisance', 'disorder' and 'harassment' are also used to describe some of this behaviour.

Anti social behaviour could also be:

  • Alcohol abuse (drunkenness, intimidation, street drinking);
  • Drug use, Drug dealing;
  • Littering, graffiti, fly-tipping or vandalism;
  • Neighbour disputes;
  • Noise, including general bad language and abuse;
  • Prostitution/sexual acts/kerb crawling
  • Youth nuisance problems;
  • Vehicle nuisance.
  • Dog fouling
  • Littering
  • Threatening behaviour
  • Verbal abuse
  • Alcohol related issues
  • Physical violence
  • Harassment
  • Intimidation
  • Vandalism

Reporting problems

If you have experienced any form of anti social behaviour it is important that you report it as soon as you can. You can do this by filling out the Anti-Social Behaviour Form. For any other issues or enquiries on your estate please send an email to neighbourhood.management@corby.gov.uk.

You may also wish to speak to your local Neighbourhood Team, your Housing Officer or your Ward Councillor to discuss the issues further.

Crime can be reported using the police emergency 999 and non-emergency 101 numbers.

In an emergency always call 999 - this is when a crime is happening, when someone suspected of a crime is nearby, or when someone is injured, being threatened or in danger.

You can also report crime anonymously to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or by filling out a form online by clicking here.
