Safety for Children

Road collisions are a leading cause of injury or death for school age children. Frequently children are being driven to school in a car because of parental fears about road safety and personal security. The Council is committed to improving child safety on the way to and from school for children using all modes of transport; however special emphasis is placed on walking and cycling modes.

The council has implemented several Road Safety initiatives and continues to roll out a planned programme of safety measures. These include Safe Routes to School, 20mph zones and various traffic calming projects. Education and training are also recognised as essential and all schools are given the opportunity to study aspects of travel awareness, which will integrate with other current Road Safety programmes. 

The School Crossing Patrol Service has been established in the County Borough to assist in the promotion of children’s school journey safety and to facilitate those journeys. 

The Council provides free transport to school for all pupils who qualify under its policy which is more generous than the national guidelines. 

Specific measures have been introduced to cater for the following age groups. 

Pre-school age

  • Parent and Child training sessions are provided on infant and child safety in cars by the Local Health Board
  • Get Across Road Safety booklets aimed at parents of children in the 0-6 age range
  • Sure Start/Early Years programmes are delivered through education and health and care services. Road Safety education forms an important part of that process and resources are provided to support the delivery of this message
  • The Children’s Traffic Club is published to parents of all pre-school children and resource kit boxes are made available to enable them to include road safety in their regular activities.

Primary School age

Kerbcraft is a national training project funded by the WAG in Wales. The money funds the employment of a coordinator to find and train local volunteers who will train small groups of five to seven year olds at the road side using the Kerbcraft model. The key skills are:

  • recognising safe versus dangerous roadside locations
  • crossing safely at parked cars
  • crossing safely near junctions

Additional measures include:

  • Get Across Road Safety booklets aimed at parents of children in the 0-6 and 7-10 age range
  • Cycle proficiency training is available to all primary schools. Cycle tests are arranged at the completion of the programme
  • Under Sevens Scheme is available to all infant classes including school based nursery and reception groups
  • Crucial Crew is a multi-agency safety initiative aimed at year 6 pupils. The main Road Safety message concentrates on the importance of wearing seatbelts.

Secondary School age

  • Making Choices is a teaching resource for teachers to use with children in their last year of primary school and first year of secondary school, who are starting to make more complex journeys alone
  • The D‘n’A scheme (Drugs ‘n’ Alcohol Awareness) is delivered as part of Gwent Police’s community safety education programme, and information is provided on how drugs and alcohol affect drivers and other road users
  • Traffic education courses using mopeds are undertaken by 14-16 year old pupils in the two of the secondary schools. The Tertiary sector is offered the same programme of initiatives as the secondary schools apart from the Making Choices unit
  • Megadrive is a pre-driving experience aimed at 16/17 year old students from Coleg Gwent Tertiary Education
  • Its purpose is to allow the participants to experience a number of motoring related aspects which includes safety, the consequences of crime, the value of first aid and driving abilities.
