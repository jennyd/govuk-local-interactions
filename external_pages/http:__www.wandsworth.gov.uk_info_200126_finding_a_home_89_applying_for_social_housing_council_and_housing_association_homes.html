Applying for social housing

What is social housing?

Social housing are homes let by the council and housing associations in the borough. Last year, we offered approximately 900 council and housing association homes for rent. There were mainly flats in high-rise blocks on estates.

Prospects of getting an offer

Your chances of being offered a council or housing association property in Wandsworth or elsewhere will depend whether you:

  • are eligible for social housing in England and
  • qualify for social housing in the area.

It also depends on the number of homes available in the area and priority awarded to your application.

You are unlikely to be offered social housing unless you are assessed as having a very high priority or a need for housing. To increase your chances of being housed you will need to be as flexible as possible about where you want to live and will need to consider other housing options.  

Applying to other local authorities

You can apply directly to any housing authority in the country. However, many local authorities offer housing to applicants who have resided in their borough for a set period of time (this is often referred to as having a local connection).

For example, in Wandsworth you must be currently living here and have done so for a minimum of two years before making an application for social housing.

Full details of eligibility and qualifying persons can be found in the allocations scheme. Also see the guide for new housing applicants and existing council tenants applying for housing and transfers. If you do not meet criteria set out in the allocation scheme, your application will not be registered (you will not go on a waiting list to be housed).

Housing associations

We work, and have nomination rights, with private registered providers of social housing (PRPs), also known as housing associations, who own or manage property in the borough. We are able to offer approximately 300 housing association homes for rent each year.

Resident Management Organisation properties

Some blocks or estates are managed by Resident Management Organisations (RMOs), which means that a resident group provides services to the block or estate. Nomination to these properties are through our usual allocations process. Residents who live in RMO properties are encouraged to participate to some extent. You can indicate on your application form whether you would like to be considered for these properties.

Buying a home

We also run various affordable home ownership schemes to assist with buying your first home. If you live or work in Wandsworth and are a council tenant or first time buyer, contact the home ownership team for information and advice on the various housing options available.
