Alcohol, Entertainment and Food

Page Content

Licence information and application guidance for people and businesses wishing to sell alcohol, provide entertainment and supply late night food and drink.  

Premises Licences 

Premises licences are issued by the Council's Licensing Service to individuals, businesses and organisations who wish to sell alcohol in Islington, put on certain types of entertainment (known as regulated entertainment) and/or supply hot food or drink between 11pm and 5am.  

Personal licences

Alcohol may only supplied at licensed premises by someone with a personal licence, or someone authorised by a personal licence holder.  Applications for personal licences are made to the Council where the applicant usually lives.  Islington residents need to apply to Islington's Licensing Service for a personal licence.   

You do not need a personal licence if alcohol is being supplied under the authority of a Temporary Event Notice or a Club Premises Certificate.

Temporary Event Notices

The Council also authorizes Temporary Event Notices (TENs) for small, short term events where licensable activities take place such as the sale of alcohol and regulated entertainment. 

Members' Clubs

Different rules generally apply to Members’ Club wishing to provide licensable activities. If you wish to provide licensable activities at a Members’ Club in Islington please contact the Licensing Service for further advice and guidance. 

licensing@islington.gov.uk or 020 7527 3031

Sex Establishments

Sex Establishment Licences are issued by the Council to venues providing adult themed entertainment, for example adult cinemas, adult book shops and table dancing clubs. 

Food Registration

Businesses that provide food and drink to the public need to register with Islington’s Environmental Health Service. This requirement applies to new businesses and new operators taking over existing businesses. 

Planning Permission

Premises used to sell alcohol or food need to have the correct planning permission in place. Land and buildings are put in to various planning categories, known as 'Use Classes', for example:

A1 - Shops, hairdressers, sandwich bars and internet cafes

A3 - Restaurants and cafes - for the sale of food and drink for consumption on the premises

A4 - Drinking establishments - pubs and wine bars (not night clubs)

A5 - Hot food takeaways - for the sale of hot food for consumption off the premises.

C1 - Hotels

D2 - Assembly and Leisure - Cinemas, music and concert halls, bingo and dance halls (but not night clubs)

Sui Generis - Theatres and nightclubs.

Any works carried out to buildings or structures used to sell food or drink will need to comply with minimum legal standards. These standards are designed to secure the health and safety of people using buildings and cover design, construction and demolition of buildings as well as the provision of services, fittings and equipment in buildings. For further information and advice please visit the Building Control pages.  

Street Trading

Any sales of goods taking place on the public highway will usually need to be covered by a street trading licence and can generally only take place in specific parts of Islington such as the markets. The Council has a policy of not issuing street trading licences to persons wishing to sell alcohol. There is a high demand for street trading licences to sell food, with a substantial waiting list.

Tables and Chairs

Businesses wishing to put items on the pavement and other parts of the public highway will usually need permission from the Council. The Council's Highway Service issue licences to businesses such as restaurants and cafes who wish to place tables and chairs, A-boards and other items on the outside pavement. No tables and chairs licence is needed if the items are placed within private outside areas.

Businesses selling alcohol to customers seated at outside tables and chairs need to ensure their premises licence allows this. Usually this is authorised by showing the outside drinking area on the plan attached the premises licence or the premises licence allowing alcohol to be sold for consumption "off" the premises.

Greenspaces

If you wish to sell alcohol or provide entertainment in one of the Council's parks or open spaces, then you will first need permission from the Council. 

Best Practice

There a number of best practice schemes and support networks operating in Islington for businesses involved in the sale of alcohol and providing entertainment.

Islington businesses with licenses to sell alcohol are strongly encouraged to join one of the borough’s pubwatch schemes. The aim of Pubwatch is to encourage a safe, secure social drinking environment in all licensed premises and help to reduce drink-related crime. 

Islington Council recognises that many operators of late night licensed premises are committed to operating their businesses in such a way as to minimise crime and disorder associated with alcohol consumption.  The Late Night Premises Best Practice Scheme has been devised to recognise those businesses who consistently demonstrate that they are working to the highest standards.  Successful applicants to the scheme are rewarded with a 30% reduction in the amount of Late Night Levy payable.
