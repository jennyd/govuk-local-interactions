Resident parking schemes and permits

Resident parking zones are established at Victoria Estate, Hardwick Estate and Stockton Town Centre West and Trinity Gardens.

Residents of each zone can buy a permit that allows parking at any time in any of the marked Residents parking bays within their zone.

  • View a guidance document about our residents parking scheme

A resident parking permit does not guarantee a parking space. Only vehicles displaying a permit can be parked during the hours of operation. 

Resident Parking Zone Hours of operation  Days of operation 
Victoria Estate 8am and 6pm  Monday to Saturday
Hardwick Estate 8am and 6pm All Week
 Stockton Town Centre 8am and 6pm Monday to Saturday
Trinity Gardens 9am and 5pm Monday to Friday

Permits are not transferable between zones. 

Residents and visitor permits cost £10 per year, business permits cost £50 per year and all zone permits costs £10.

 

How do I apply for my permit?

You must apply in person for a resident's parking permit at either Thornaby Customer Services Centre, Stockton Customer Services Centre or Billingham Customer Service Centre. However, you can contact our Customer Services Centre on 01642 528499 for advice in advance.

To get a resident's permit evidence must be produced to confirm that the applicant lives in the property (rent book, tenancy agreement). You must also produce documented proof that the vehicle is registered to that address (vehicle registration documents, insurance certificate). A permit will only be issued to the person who keeps and uses the vehicle.

In addition, residents may also purchase a visitor permit again proof of residency must be produced. This can be purchased whether the applicant has their own vehicle or not. This can be displayed in the visitor vehicle and on their departure, returned to the resident.

Residents within each zone are entitled to apply for a residents permit for each vehicle that is registered at that particular property. In addition residents can also request to purchase a visitor permit if necessary. However, if there are no vehicles registered to the property a maximum of 2 visitors' permits may be purchased.

If you have a business in the Residents Parking Zone and have a vehicle which is registered at the business address, you may apply for a business permit.

Blue Badge Holders are not permitted to park within the Residents bays, however are permitted to park on the single yellow lines for up to three hours displaying a valid badge and clock.  

 

How do I pay for a Parking Permit?

You can pay for a parking permit using any of the following:

  • Cash
  • Cheque or Postal Order - made payable to Stockton-on-Tees Borough Council
  • Credit or Debit Card (in person only)

You can make cheque or postal order payment by post or by calling into one of our Customer Service Centres.
