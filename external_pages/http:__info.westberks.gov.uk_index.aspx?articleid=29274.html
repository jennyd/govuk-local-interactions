Crime and Safety

Dealing with crime and helping to make sure residents are safe is a key role of a number of agencies, including Thames Valley Police, the Fire Service, Sovereign (and other housing associations), as well as health and probation services and West Berkshire Council.

In an emergency, if a crime is happening or you are in danger you should call Thames Valley Police on 999.  For all other reports call 101.  To report crime and information about crimes confidentially contact Crimestoppers.

The Safer Communities Partnership Team co-ordinate across a number of agencies to help prevent, respond to and decrease criminal, domestic abuse and anti-social behaviour offenses.

Anti-Social Behaviour
Reducing anti-social behaviour and repeat incidents (such as neighbour disputes)
Domestic Abuse
Help, advice and support for people affected by domestic abuse, through the "Reducing the Risk" website
