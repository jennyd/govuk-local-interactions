Severe winter weather advice

Winter weather - keep up to date

Winter weather brings with it rain, gales, ice and snow. This can disrupt council services, such as bin collections and force schools to close.

We want to keep you up to date when there are severe winter weather disruptions. We do this via our website, through social media (Twitter and Facebook) and using email alerts.

If there is anything, we have not included on this page, please email updates@centralbedfordshire.gov.uk with your feedback.

School closures

When winter weather strikes, you need to know early about school closures.

Our school closure system allows you to register to find out information via email directly from your school.

You can register on our school closure system to receive information for the school(s) you are interested in finding out about. You will receive an email giving the status (open or closed) and brief explanation of the issue.

This comes from Head Teachers, who update this information in the event of a forced closure due to an emergency.

Salting roads in cold weather

We salt when road temperatures drop below zero and when the roads are expected to be damp. Ice will not form on a dry road surface in dry air. We try to carry out salting either after the evening or before the morning peak traffic periods. We may work 24 hours around the clock if we have bad weather.

We use a computer system that collects weather and road information at different sites across Central Bedfordshire. The Met Office uses this weather information along with their other weather data to predict weather conditions and road surface temperatures. We then make our decision on whether or not to salt and at what time of the day or night.

Our Highways team do winter weather email alerts every day. Register now to find out when the gritters are out!

Check our main salting and gritting page for more information on the routes that we cover. You can also find out where are salt bins are and how to get salt bins filled.

Reporting highways problems this winter

We know winter weather can cause problems on the roads, such as potholes and damage to streetlights. We need to hear about these as soon as they happen. You can make a number of reports through our website, by telephone and email. Find our more information on our highways reporting pages.

Bin collections during severe winter weather

We try to maintain waste and recycling collections regardless of the weather, but where conditions are dangerous for our crews pulling waste bins and loading vehicles, we may need to suspend bin collections until conditions improve.

In the event of adverse weather conditions, we will make every effort to collect your kerbside waste. If we are not able to due to safety concerns, we will catch up as soon as possible. Residents are advised to make full use of their food waste collection (north of the region only) and recycling collection.

Garden waste and bulky waste collections will be suspended in adverse weather, to focus resources on kerbside collection of food, recycling and residual waste. Were street cleansing is suspended due to bad weather conditions, the vehicles and staff will be diverted to gritting.

You can keep up to date with changes to bin collections by registering for our email alerts.

Tidy tips – access during bad weather

We try to keep our tidy tips open regardless of the weather, but where conditions are dangerous for customers and staff, we may need to close our sites.

You can keep up to date with tidy tip closures by registering for our email alerts.

Flooding and severe gales

If there is deep standing water across the carriageway, we will respond as soon as possible. Other drainage issues are inspected within ten working days.

Find our more information on our highways reporting pages.

Flood alerts

If you live in an area that could be affected by flooding then you can get regular updates on any developing situations by signing up for the Environment Agency flood warnings, download the flood warning RSS feed, follow the Environment Agency on Twitter and / or Facebook page.

Winter driving advice

Our website has information and advice for driving in snow, ice and rain. Some of it may be obvious, but it is worth reading and being prepared at all times for bad weather.

Let’s face it, we live in England and know we can often have ‘four seasons in one day’, no matter what time of the year!
