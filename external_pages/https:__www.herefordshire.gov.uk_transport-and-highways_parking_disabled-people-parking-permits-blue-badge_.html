Blue Badge scheme

A Blue Badge Â - also known as a disabled parking badge - provides a range of parking and motoring concessions for people with severe mobility problems. It is designed to promote mobility and inclusion for the most severely disabled individuals.

Who can apply for a Blue Badge?

There are four types of Blue Badge available:

  • Eligible without further assessment
  • Eligible subject to further assessment
  • Child under 3
  • Organisation's Blue Badge

Eligible without further assessment

Some people automatically qualify for a badge without further assessment. Applicants must meet one of the following categories:

  • Registered blind
  • Receive the higher rate of the mobility component of the Disability Living Allowance except where the award is for a period of less than 12 months
  • Receive the mobility component of Personal Independence Payment (PIP) and you scored at least 8 points in relation to the 'moving around' activity in the PIP assessment. Find out more about PIP on the GOV.UK website
  • Receive a war pensioner's mobility supplement
  • Have received a lump sum benefit from the Armed Forces and Reserve Forces Compensation Scheme (within tariff levels 1-8) and have been certified as having a permanent and substantial disability which causes inability to walk or very considerable difficulty in walking

If you meet one of these criteria please either:

Apply online at GOV.UK

or

Download and complete the relevant section of the Blue Badge Individual Application form

Eligible subject to further assessment

There are some people who may qualify for a badge. An assessment will be required to confirm if you qualify. Applicants must meet one of the following categories:

  • Drives a vehicle regularly, have a severe congenital disability in both arms, and are unable to operate, or have considerable difficulty in operating, all or some types of parking meter
  • Is a child under the age of three suffering a medical condition requiring bulky medical equipment or immediate access to a vehicle for treatment
  • Is unable to walk or has considerable difficulty walking due to a permanent and substantial disability

If you meet one of these criteria please:

Apply online at GOV.UK

or

Download and complete the relevant section of the Blue Badge Individual Application form

If you are applying under the 'subject to further assessment' criteria ABLE2 Occupational Therapy Services who work in partnership with Herefordshire Council will contact you to assess eligibility.
Note: Whilst ABLE2 will confirm eligibility, Herefordshire Council will issue the Blue Badge.

Organisations

Organisations that have six or more people who qualify for a Blue Badge can apply for an organisation badge. If you meet one of these criteria please:

Complete the organisations application form

Children under 3

You may be eligible to apply for a Blue Badge if you are the parent of a child under 3 years old with a medical condition which means that the child either:

  • Must always be accompanied by bulky medical equipment which cannot be carried around without great difficulty
  • Needs to be kept near a vehicle at all times to get treatment for a condition when necessary

If your child meets one of these criteria please:

Apply online at GOV.UK

or

Complete the relevant section of the Blue Badge Individual Application form

How do I apply for or renew a Blue Badge?

A Blue Badge lasts for three years. To renew your badge you need to complete a new full application. You should do this a few weeks before it runs out so that there is sufficient time to process your application before it expires. Do not use the badge if it has expired or you may be fined.

To apply for or renew a Blue Badge, you can either:

Apply or renew online at GOV.UK

What do I do if my Blue Badge is lost or stolen?

If your badge is lost or stolen you must immediately report itÂ to the police. The police will issue you with an incident number.

Please complete and return a new application form to us together with proof of your address, proof of ID, one passport sized photograph and a cheque for Â£10. Please record the police incident number on the top of the application form.

Once we have received the completed application form aÂ new badge will be issued within five working days.

We are unable to re-issue a badge without a police incident number. Expired badges must be returned to Herefordshire Council.

How do I return a Blue Badge if it is no longer required?

You must return a Blue Badge to the issuing local authority if:

  • The badge has expired
  • Your medical condition or mobility improves and you are no longer eligible
  • A replacement badge has been issued for one that is lost or stolen and the original is found (the original badge must be returned)
  • The badge becomes damaged or faded and is illegible
  • The badge is no longer required, for example if you are confined to the house
  • The badge holder has died

If you have an organisational badge you must additionally return the badge if:

  • The organisation has ceased to exist
  • The organisation no longer cares for people who would qualify for a Blue Badge in their own right

Where can I park with a Blue Badge?

Disabled badge holders are entitled to three hours free parking in Herefordshire Council managed pay and display car parks if a valid badge and clock are displayed correctly.

Can I use a disabled person's parking permit issued outside the UK?

Badges issued in the EU

Herefordshire Council will recognise any disabled badge issued within the European Union (EU) without prior notification. The badge must conform to the recommended standardÂ set by the European Commission (98/376/EC) where the badge should display:

On its front:

  • The date of expiry of the badge
  • The distinguishing code for the issuing member state
  • 'Parking card for people with disabilities' written in both English and the language of the member state

On its reverse:

  • The holder's full name
  • The holder's signature
  • A photograph of the holder

Badges issued outside of the EU

Badges issued outside of the EU may be recognised by Herefordshire Council only with advanced notification of its use. To obtain permission prior to using your parking badge:
