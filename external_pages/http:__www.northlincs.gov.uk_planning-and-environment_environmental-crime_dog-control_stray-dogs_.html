Stray dogs

Dog tags and micro-chipping your dog

Micro-chipping is a permanent form of identification for your dog. A chip, about the size of a long grain of rice is injected under the dog's skin between the shoulder blades. The chip carries a unique number that allows the dog to be traced to its owner when scanned.

Your dog should also have a collar and tag, which is a legal requirement and a microchip doesn't replace the need for a collar and tag.

Read more essential information about dog tags and micro-chipping your dog [PDF, 31Kb].

How can I get my dog micro-chipped for free?

The Micro-chipping of Dogs (England) Regulations 2015 will come into force on 6 April 2016. This makes it compulsory for all dogs in England to be micro-chipped at eight weeks old.

The Dogs Trust run a micro-chipping scheme through vets, offering free micro-chipping to all dogs. For details of your nearest participating vet, visit the Chip My Dog website.

To make an appointment please call 0330 1230334.

What if I find a stray dog out of office hours?

If you find a stray dog out of office hours, please call Sangreat Kennels and then listen to the pre-recorded message with instructions of what you need to do.

What does Sangreat Kennels do with stray dogs and how can I get my dog back?

The kennels receive stray dogs from the Dog Warden, Police and members of the public 24 hours a day seven days a week.

Stray and lost dogs may be re-claimed from Sangreat Kennels during opening hours: Monday to Saturday 9am to 5pm and Sunday and Bank Holidays 1pm to 4pm.

Dog owners re-claiming their dog from Sangreat Kennels must prove ownership and pay our charges in full before they can take their dog home. Sangreat Kennels are not authorised to release any dog until all fees are paid in full.

Can I buy an unclaimed stray dog?

We have an excellent record of re-homing unclaimed stray dogs and always try to find dogs a new home. There are often dogs for sale at Sangreat Kennels, which cost £60. The price includes micro-chipping.

The breed, size and age of dogs can change daily - therefore we advise you to contact Sangreat Kennels to see if they have got what you are looking for. 

If you're looking to give an unclaimed stray dog a good home, please contact Sangreat Kennels. 

Please note - we reserve the right to refuse to sell any dog.
