We provide a collection service to domestic properties who require the disposal of infectious and non-infectious wastes.

We will collect infectious and non-infectious wastes from householders who are self-treating. Where wastes are being generated by a health care worker, district nurse or GP working in a private dwelling, the Primary Care Trust (PCT) must make their own arrangements for the appropriate disposal.

Non-infectious waste such as catheters, incontinence pads, nappies and stomach pouches are contained in yellow "tiger" bags. Infectious waste such as tissue, blood and equipment, dressings and swabs contaminated with blood and tissue are contained in orange bags. Both bags are provided on request.

Infectious and non-infectious wastes must not be placed in normal household waste containers or contaminated with household waste for normal refuse collection. They are collected separately as they are required to be sent to the appropriate licensed treatment facilities.

Collection Days

Newhaven, Lewes and surrounding areas

  • Infectious waste is collected on Tuesdays
  • Non-infectious waste is collected on Wednesdays

Peacehaven, Seaford, Telscombe Cliffs, East Saltdean and surrounding areas

  • Infectious waste is collected on Tuesdays
  • Non-infectious waste is collected on Thursdays

If you are unsure as to which area you fall in please contact Customer Services and they will be happy to advise you.

Arranging Collections

New Customers

We have a statutory duty to ensure that your waste is correctly described as a) infectious waste or b) non-infectious waste followed by disposal and treatment in an appropriate and legitimate manner.

When you make your first request - we will send you a letter by post (or email if desired) with a referral form which need to be filled in by your health professional e.g. health care worker, district nurse or GP. They are required to confirm the type of waste in question, sign and return the referral form to us within 30 calendar days from the date shown on the letter. On receipt of the referral form we will contact you to advise you of the collection arrangements. Once accepted, your collection will continue every week on the stated day for your area unless there are special arrangements in place at Public and Bank Holiday times.  These will be advertised on our web site.

If the referral form is not returned to us within 30 calendar days from the date shown on this letter we will assume that you no longer require our service.

If you require our service you can apply online or for more information you can contact Customer Services.

Replenishments

If you require more yellow "tiger" or orange bags click here to let us know and we will supply them on the day of collection. Please ensure that you place your name and address on the bag's imprinted label. This is a legal requirement and we will not be able to collect the bags without these details being completed.

You can receive email alerts on future bank holiday changes by signing up to our newsletter.
