Breakfast, After school and Holiday Clubs

  • Children, young people and families

If you are looking for a breakast, club, after school club or holiday playscheme, you can search online using our childcare directory or contact FYi for futher information.

Breakfast clubs

Breakfast clubs explained

What is a breakfast club?

Breakfast clubs are usually held in a school and often breakfast is available. Breakfast clubs can operate in a community hall/centre.

Age groups

4 - 16 years

Hours available

They operate during term time only and are generally open from 8am until the start of the school day.

Staff ratios

(staff:children)

1:8 for children aged 3 to 8 years.

Are they registered with Ofsted?

Breakfast clubs are generally not required to register with Ofsted as most operate for less than two hours a day.

Activities offered

Breakfast and activities before school starts, sometimes including quizzes and games.

Cost (average in Barnet)

£1.50 - £4 per session.

Can you pay with childcare vouchers or Working Tax Credits?

Yes

Can the offer the Free Entitlement to Early Education?

No

Why choose a breakfast club?

  • Some families where both parents work need a safe place for their children to go early in the morning.

  • Families who have a long journey to school may leave home too early for breakfast.

  • Breakfast clubs offer children an opportunity to socialise with others before school starts.

After school clubs

After school clubs explained

What is an after school club?

These can be held in a variety of venues including schools and community centres or halls.

Age groups

4 - 16 years

Hours available

They often serve several schools in the same area and are open from the end of the school day until 6 pm term time only.

Staff ratios

1:8 for children aged 3 to 8 years.

Are they registered with Ofsted?

If the after school club is run by the school they do not need a separate registration as they can use the schools registeration.

If an after school club operates for more than two hours a day and looks after children under the age of eight they are required to register with Ofsted, if they operate under two hours and/or look after children over the age of eight they are not required registration with Ofsted.

Activities offered

They offer a variety of activities including arts and crafts, sports or games. Some clubs will operate a school pickup service.

Cost (average in Barnet)

£6 - £8 per session

Can you pay with childcare vouchers or Working Tax Credits?

Yes

Can the offer the Free Entitlement to Early Education?

No

Why choose an after school club?

  • some families where both parents work need a safe place for their children to go after school

  • after school club within the school means that children do not have to travel and are familiar with the building and staff operating the service.

Holiday playschemes

Holiday playschemes explained

What is a holiday playscheme?

Holiday playschemes can take place in a variety of venues such as schools, community centres or halls.

Age groups

In general most holiday play schemes care for children from four years upwards.

Hours available

Most operate during the summer holidays varying from four to six weeks, while some operate during all school holidays and half terms.

Staff ratios

1:8 for children aged 3 to 8 years.

Are they registered with Ofsted?

They are only registered with Ofsted if they are open for more than two hours and take children below the age of eight.

Activities offered

Sports, arts and crafts and sometimes outings.

Cost (average in Barnet)

£112 per week

Can you pay with childcare vouchers or Working Tax Credits?

Yes

Can the offer the Free Entitlement to Early Education?

No

Why choose a holiday play scheme?

  • provide childcare for parents who are working

  • children can play and socialise in a safe and caring environment

  • opportunity for children to engage in a range of activities that are fun and age appropriate

  • many schemes will arrange visits and day trips.
