Asbestos

How do you rate this information / service?

What we do with your feedback

Asbestos is a naturally occurring silica material. It was widely used from the 1950s through to the mid 1980s in a range of products, including building materials, because of its strength and insulating properties. It is no longer used but can still be found in buildings and elsewhere.

If you think you've been exposed to asbestos fibres and it may affect your health, contact your GP for further advice. Please follow our guidance below to reduce any further risks of exposure.

Asbestos in the home - all homes

Many people have worries about asbestos but, undisturbed and in a good stable condition, it usually poses no risk to health. Asbestos only becomes a problem when it is damaged or disturbed and is able to release asbestos fibres which can be breathed in.

Asbestos in the home is most commonly found in the form of cement sheets which are mainly composed of cement but which include some fibres to give strength. Modern types of this boarding do not include asbestos, but asbestos fibres were used in the past. Cement sheet products are pale grey in colour and can be in corrugated or flat sheets. They are thin and brittle and have a clean edge when broken. Older garages may have asbestos-cement sheeting as roofing or cladding, and it was also used as guttering and rainwater pipes. Asbestos can also be found in other forms including insulation board, other wall boards, ceiling tiles, pipe lagging and as a constituent of some textured wall and ceiling finishes.

We strongly recommend that you do not cut, drill or otherwise disturb materials that you suspect may contain asbestos. If you need to carry out work to such materials we recommend that a sample of the material is analysed by a specialist contractor before any work is considered. The Council isn't able to provide that service but you should be able to find suitable contractors in the Yellow Pages phone-book or through an internet-search.

Rented properties

The same advice applies to asbestos in rented accommodation: it is only likely to be a problem if it is damaged or disturbed. If your landlord is carrying out works to the property they should ensure that asbestos has been considered and that any contractors take suitable precautions.

If you are concerned that asbestos in your home may have been damaged, you should report the problem to your landlord in the first instance. Our Private Sector Housing Team can, in certain circumstances, carry out an inspection to check on the condition of damaged asbestos and, following a risk-assessment, can take enforcement action under the Housing Act 2004 to ensure problems are put right.

Asbestos removal

Homes built between the 1930s and 1990s could contain asbestos. There are a number of different types of asbestos, most of which can only be safely handled by licensed contractors. The exception is asbestos-cement products which can be removed by homeowners as long as some simple precautions are followed. For more information on removing asbestos in your own home please refer to the guidance documents produced by the HSE: HSE Asbestos essentials 

BEWARE: You cannot accurately identify asbestos material by simply looking at it. The only way to know for sure whether a product contains asbestos and what type is to have it tested. If in doubt contact a licensed asbestos removal company.

For more advice and to get Asbestos removed please contact a licensed asbestos removal contractor. A list is available on the  Asbestos Removal Contractors Association website.

Disposal of asbestos-cement products

It is still possible for householders to dispose of small quantities of asbestos-cement material at Oxfordshire County Council's civic amenity site at Ardley, and a closed skip is provided at this site for those householders who carry out DIY maintenance to remove asbestos related products such as roofing sheets and rainwater fittings. Suitable precautions must be taken when removing this product

  • Clear the area surrounding the structure before starting work
  • Pre-wet the asbestos cement you are dealing with using water
  • Wear gloves and a disposable face mask suitable for working with asbestos
  • Remove the asbestos cement with as little breakage as possible - do not break it up unnecessarily
  • If you need tools to ease removal, use hand tools not power tools, which will reduce the amount of dust
  • Wrap and seal large pieces in heavy duty plastic sheeting which can be purchased at most DIY / hardware stores
  • Damp down any debris and place it in double plastic bags, together with smaller pieces of asbestos and your used face masks and gloves.

Oxfordshire County council - asbestos removal advice    provides more information regarding the disposal of asbestos at Ardley.

Please ensure that you contact Ardley on 01869 343459 prior to disposal in order to confirm that there will be space.

Asbestos in the ground

Occasionally, poor historical waste disposal activities have led to asbestos materials being buried. If you find materials you think may contain asbestos while digging on your property, stop digging immediately to prevent any possible further spreading of fibres, cover the hole, contact the Environmental Protection Team on 01295 221803, and ask a suitably qualified contractor to check whether the material does contain asbestos and advise on how to proceed safely, as necessary.

Fly-tipped asbestos

Our Environmental Services deal with fly-tipping issues. If you have cause to think that any fly-tipped material may contain asbestos you should contact them on 01295 221940

Asbestos in the work place

Employers are responsible for safety in the workplace. Any concerns should be referred to them initially. The Council and the Health & Safety Executive (HSE) share the enforcement responsibility for health and safety in the workplace. Each organisation is responsible for a different range of business types, which are summarised on the HSE local authority enforcement website page, and can provide advice about asbestos issues for those types of business. Contact  Public Protection Team on 01295 221672
