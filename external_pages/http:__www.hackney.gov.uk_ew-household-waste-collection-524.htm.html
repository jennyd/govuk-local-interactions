Household rubbish collections

We encourage everyone to cut down on rubbish in the first place - it helps save money and protects the environment.

Find out more about reducing waste.

Most waste can be recycled and should put in your green recycling sacks, blue bin for food waste or brown bin for garden waste. 

Please only throw away rubbish that cannot be recycled. 

Use our postcode finder to check your collection day.

Report a missed collection

 

Presenting your waste for collection

We do not supply or offer replacement bins or sacks for residents to store non-recyclable household waste.

  • We will collect your rubbish and recycling from the front of your property at street level.
  • Make sure sacks are not overfilled and are firmly tied.
  • Place your sacks or containers out for collection before 7am or during your time bands.
  • Make sure sacks and containers are clearly visible for collection. Our staff will not lift or carry bins or bags over stairwells or other obstacles.

Clinical waste collections

Hackney Council will collect clinical waste from residential properties, either on a regular basis or as a one off.

There is a charge for this service. You should arrange a referral by your GP or hospital for a collection either as a one off or regular collection.

Collection must be arranged for surgical waste, used syringes, drugs and pharmaceuticals - you must not put clinical waste in with your normal waste or recycling bins. Please note sanitary items are not classified as clinical waste.

To organise a collection, please contact us by emailing us at hygieneservicessupport@hackney.gov.uk or by calling  on 020 8356 5000.
