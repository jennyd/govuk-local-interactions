Who can register to vote?

You can’t vote in an election unless you have registered to do so. If you don’t register, you can’t vote - it’s as simple as that.

The following people are eligible to register to vote:

  • Anyone aged 16 or over (but you cannot vote until you are 18).
  • British or qualifying Commonwealth citizens (those citizens who have been granted entry to the UK (for a limited period only) and those that have an unrestricted right to live in the UK):
    Commonwealth member countries (external website)
  • Citizens of the Republic of Ireland or other European Union (EU) member states:
    GOV.uk - Countries in the EU and EEA (external website)

 

Special circumstances

You can also register to vote if you: 

  • have two or more homes in the UK
  • are a student
  • are in the armed forces
  • live overseas

 

Please note: There are certain qualifying criteria for who can vote at what elections?

How do I register to vote?

Register to vote deadline

The deadline to register to vote is 12 working days before the election. For the Police and Crime Commissioner election in May 2016 the deadline for new registrations is midnight on Wednesday 20 April 2015.

Online registration

The way we all register to vote has changed. You can now register to vote online and everyone from the age of 16 is responsible for registering themselves:

GOV.uk - Register to vote (external website) 

You will need:

  • your national insurance number (which can be found on your national insurance card, or in official paperwork such as payslips, or letters about benefits or tax credits)
  • Your name and address
  • Your date of birth 
     

If you are unable to register online, please contact the electoral services team.

A note about the new voting system

The way we all register to vote in the UK changed on 10 June 2014. The new system is called Individual Electoral Registration (IER). 

Under the old system the 'head of every household' could register everyone who lived at their address but now everyone is responsible for registering themselves.

To find out more visit the Your Vote Matters website:

Your Vote Matters (external website)

Can I register to vote anonymously?

If you’re concerned about your own safety or the safety of somebody you live with, you may be able to vote anonymously so your name doesn’t appear on the Register of Electors.

In order to register anonymously, you’ll need supporting evidence such as a court order or an injunction, or written support from a relevant organisation.

Please contact the electoral services team to register anonymously.

How does it work?

If you register anonymously, instead of your name and address appearing on the electoral register a code will be added to the end of the section of the register for your polling district. You should receive polling cards as usual which you will have to take with you on polling day.

How often do I need to re-register?

You only need to re-register if you move house or change your name.

Top of page

What do I do if I move house or change my name? 

If you move house or change your name you need to register your new address or name via the GOV.uk website:

GOV.uk - Register to vote (external website)  

Please note: You'll need to provide your date of birth and your national insurance number (which can be found on your national insurance card, or in official paperwork such as payslips, or letters about benefits or tax credits). 

If you are unable to change your details online, please contact the electoral services team.

Can I vote by post? 

You can vote by post if you live in the UK or abroad and are unable to get to your polling station on election day.

Deadline

Changes to existing postal votes or new postal vote applications must be received by electoral services 11 working days before an election. The deadline for receiving changes to existing postal votes or new postal vote applications for the Police and Crime Commissioner election in May 2016 is 5pm on Tuesday 19 April 2015.

You must be registered to vote before completing one of the following application forms.

I'd like to:

  • Apply for a UK postal vote (word 83kb opens in new window)


  • Apply for an overseas postal vote (word 45kb opens in new window)
  • Apply for an Armed Forces postal vote (pdf 68kb opens in new window) 


  • Apply for an anonymous postal vote (word 1mb opens in new window)

Read more about these special circumstances(overseas and armed forces electors). 

If you are unable to print these forms, please contact the electoral services team and we will post one out to you.

Do you have problems signing your name or reading and require a waiver?

If you have a postal vote and have a disability that prevents you from signing/signing in a consistent manner or are unable to read or write, you can apply for a waiver. This means that you will not need to sign your postal vote. 

Please complete the following form and send it to the address on the form:

Apply for a waiver postal vote (word 46kb opens in new window) 

If you are unable to print these forms, please contact the electoral services team and we will post one out to you.

Can someone else vote on my behalf (by proxy)?

You can nominate someone else to vote on your behalf at the polling station (for example due to disability or if you are away on holiday). This is called a ‘proxy vote’.

'By proxy' means that you are appointing an individual that you trust to vote on your behalf.

New proxy application deadline

For new proxy applications we must receive a printed and signed application form (return address included on form) six working days before an election. The deadline for receiving new postal vote applications for the Police and Crime Commissioner election in May 2016 is 5pm on Tuesday 26 April 2015.

Changes to or cancellation of existing proxy deadline

If you wish to change or cancel your existing proxy, we must receive a printed and signed application from (return address included on form) 11 working days before an election. The deadline for cancelling or changing your current proxy arrangements for the Police and Crime Commissioner election in May 2016 is 5pm on Tuesday 19 April 2015 (return address included on form).

You must check you are registered to vote before selecting one of the following application forms.

I'd like to:

  • Apply to vote on behalf of somebody else (proxy) (word 91kb opens in new window)

  • Apply to vote on behalf of somebody living overseas (overseas proxy) (word 69kb opens in new window)

  • Apply to vote on behalf of somebody in the Armed Forces (Armed Forces proxy) (pdf 134kb opens in new window)  

  • Apply to vote on behalf of someone who wishes to vote anonymously (anonymous proxy) (word 84kb opens in new window)

Read more about special circumstances(overseas and armed forces electors).  

If you are unable to print these forms, please  contact the electoral services team and we will post one out to you. 

Postal Proxy

If you have been assigned as someone’s ‘proxy’ you can apply to vote by post on their behalf, instead of going to the polling station.

Please note: Changes to existing postal proxies or new postal proxy applications must be received by electoral services 11 working days before an election. The deadline for receiving changes to existing postal proxies or new postal proxy applications for the Police and Crime Commissioner election in May 2016 is 5pm on Tuesday 19 April 2015.

I'd like to:

 

  • Apply for a postal vote on behalf of somebody else (postal proxy) (word 57kb opens in new window)

  • Apply for a postal vote on behalf of somebody living overseas (overseas postal proxy) (word 69kb opens in new window) 

 

If you are unable to print these forms, please contact the electoral services team and we will post one out to you.

Re-applying for a postal/proxy vote

You won’t have to re-apply, but if you would like to continue voting by post or via a proxy, we will need to get a current signature from you every five years. We will request this in writing.

If you don’t sign, complete and return this form to us within six weeks we will send you a letter to let you know your ability to vote by post or proxy will be removed. If this happens, and you wish to vote by post or proxy in the future, you will need to complete a new postal application.
