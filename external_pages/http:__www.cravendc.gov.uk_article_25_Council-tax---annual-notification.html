Council Tax levels 2015/16

Most properties are subject to Council Tax, which helps to pay for the cost of local services.

The money collected is used to help pay for services provided by :

  • Craven District Council
  • North Yorkshire County Council
  • Parish Councils
  • North Yorkshire Fire Authority
  • North Yorkshire Police Authority

There is one bill per property, whether it is a house, bungalow, flat, maisonette, mobile home or houseboat, and whether it is owned or rented.

The council tax for 2015/16 was set at Full Council on 24th February 2015 (see the news item Craven announces a further Council Tax freeze). Craven's council tax is frozen for a 5th year but other precepts have increased.

How is the council tax calculated for my property?

Every property is allocated a valuation band (letter from A to H), according to its market value at 1 April 1991.  Each band pays a different amount of council tax (A the lowest, to H the highest).  Some properties are exempt from council tax and some people will be eligible for discounts.  You can appeal to us if you think you are not liable to pay, or (in certain limited circumstances) against the banding to the local Valuation Officer.

Here  is a full list of amounts payable for Craven across all valuation bands and including parish precepts for 2015/2016  Council Tax amounts 2015/2016 [141kb]

How do I find out the valuation band for my property?

The valuation band can usually be found on your Council Tax bill. Alternatively visit the Valuation Office Agency website  where you can find out your banding level by entering the postcode of your property.

Who is liable to pay?

Usually the person who lives in the property will pay the Council Tax, providing it is their sole or main residence and they are over 18 years of age.

If more than one person lives in the property, then it is the person who comes first on the following list who will pay:

  • owner who lives in the property
  • tenant who lives in the property
  • any person who lives in the property
  • owner who does not live in the property

People who are joint owners or joint tenants will be jointly and severally liable for the bill. Married couples, and those living together as husband and wife, will also be jointly responsible for paying the bill whether or not they have an equal interest in the property.

Single person discount

If there is only one resident, a single person discount may be granted. A discount may also apply if all but one of the adult residents are not counted for the purposes of the council tax. See Council Tax - Discounts 

Exemptions and benefits

In some circumstances a property can be exempt from council tax altogether - see Council Tax - Exemptions.

If you are on a low income, you may be able to claim a Council Tax Reduction.

Change of address

If you are moving, please let us know as soon as possible by telephoning our Customer Services Department on 01756 700600. (Please note that calls may be recorded for training and monitoring purposes).  Alternatively you can download a form,  Council Tax Change of Address [236kb], complete it and return to Customer Services.
