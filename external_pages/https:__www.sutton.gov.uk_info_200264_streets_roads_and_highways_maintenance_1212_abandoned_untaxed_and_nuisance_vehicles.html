Abandoned, untaxed and nuisance vehicles - Report an abandoned vehicle

What is an abandoned vehicle?

It is an offence to use or keep on the public road a motor vehicle (registered in the UK) without a valid current vehicle licence (road tax). You may report such vehicles to the Council who can arrange for enforcement action by the DVLA, but only if the vehicle can be found by the DVLA during normal working hours and its tax has expired by over 28 days. In most cases the DVLA will clamp or impound such vehicles.

How to report an abandoned or untaxed vehicle to the Council

A vehicle abandoned or a motor vehicle untaxed (for over 28 days), on the public road should be reported to the Council.
  • Use the link on the side of this page to report a vehicle.
  • You can contact the council's call centre on 020 8770 5000.

Online reports may take longer to reach the Council as they are made via an external website. Customers should provide the vehicle's registration number, other relevant information and exact location, and are advised to provide their daytime contact details so that the Council can contact the customer if there is a problem in dealing with the vehicle.

If you would like to report an abandoned vehicle, please provide details using our online form

Report an abandoned vehicle here

The offence may also be reported to the DVLA. You can do this:

  • online - to report an unlicensed motor vehicle online to the DVLA, go to the DVLA web site, open the online services page, and then open Reporting of Unlicensed Vehicles on the Public Highway page.
  • by letter to the DVLA Vehicle Registration Office, Sidcup House, 12-18 Station Road, Sidcup, Kent DA15 7EQ, or
  • by telephone - DVLA Public Hot Line 08000 325 202

Abandoned vehicles on a council housing estate 

A vehicle abandoned or untaxed on part of a Council Housing Estate such as a garage or parking area, should be reported to:

  • Sutton Housing Partnership
  • Tel: 0800 195 5552
  • Email: customerservices@suttonhousingpartnership.org.uk

If the vehicle appears to be abandoned on privately owned land, the occupant of the property (or agent) should write to us to give permission for the removal of the abandoned vehicle, enclosing the investigation fee of Â£75 per vehicle.

What will the council do about an abandoned or untaxed vehicle?

The Council will assess vehicles by making enquiries with DVLA and Police, and by inspection if necessary. If the vehicle appears to the Council to be abandoned, a warning notice will be affixed to it.  If the vehicle is a danger to the public, it may be removed without notice. If the vehicle is safe, it may be left from 24 hours to 7 days before being removed in order to give the owner the opportunity to claim or remove the vehicle.

If the vehicle is on a public road and its vehicle licence has expired by 28 days but it does not appear to be abandoned, the Council will arrange for the DVLA to clamp or impound the vehicle if possible.

Our Commitment to You (performance standards)

We will assess nuisance vehicles within 24 working hours. We will request enforcement action by the DVLA against untaxed vehicles within 24 working hours. We will remove dangerous vehicles from roads within 24 working hours of assessment. We will remove other vehicles assessed as abandoned within 24 working hours of expiry of the period of notice served at assessment (notice period is from 1 to 7 days).
