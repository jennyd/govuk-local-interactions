I want to adopt

 

 

 

Adults in Slough can adopt local children and young people.

 

What is adoption?

 

Adoption is a lifetime commitment to raise a child who cannot be brought up within their birth family.

 

Adopters become the legal guardians of children, assuming the right to make decisions about the child and their upbringing (known as parental responsibility). Once adopted, a child has no legal connection to their birth family.

 

Many adoptions today involve some contact with birth family members (often in the form of an exchange of letters once or twice a year).

 

Can I adopt?

 

There is no such thing as a typical adopter. Just like the children we support, all adults are individuals, with varying circumstances and experiences. It is important we find different types of adoptive families to meet the different needs of the children we support.

 

Children often do best in families that reflect, or can positively promote, their customs and values. We therefore welcome enquiries from people from all backgrounds.

 

Who can adopt:

 

  • Individuals or couples

  • People of any ethnicity, religion and cultural background

  • People with a disability

  • Parents, or those without children

 

Who can’t adopt:

 

  • People under 21 years old

  • Those not legally resident in the UK, or who have not been so for at least 12 months.

  • UK citizens living abroad

  • Individuals with a criminal conviction or caution for offences against children, or for serious sexual offences

 

We consider single applicants and couples, who are married, in a civil partnership or co-habiting.

 

We consider applicants with or without children. We recommend a two year age gap between an adopted child and any existing children in the home. We recommend any childless couples trying to conceive have concluded fertility investigations and/or treatment prior to their application to adopt.

 

Applicants need to be at least 21 years of age but there is no upper age limit as long as applicants are in good health. Children under 5 years of age are not placed with applicants who smoke.
