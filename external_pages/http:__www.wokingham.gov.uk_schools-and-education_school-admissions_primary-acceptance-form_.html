Infant and primary school place acceptance form

Use the online form below to accept or decline the offer of an infant or primary F2 Reception place for September 2015:

Please complete this form by 30 April 2015. Failure to do so may result in your child's school place being withdrawn.

Schools and education - Infant or primary school acceptance
It is the duty of your home local authority to ensure that your child received an education.  If you are making alternative arrangements for your child you must inform us.
To ensure your child has a school place for September 2015, it is recommended that you accept this offer. If you are appealing for an alternative school, the appeal process will not be affected by accepting this school place offer.
Please discuss further with the Headteacher if you are accepting on a part-time basis and later wish to change to full-time or to discuss what part-time options may be available.

Deferring (or delaying) your child's school place

Deferring means delaying your child’s entry to the school and remaining eligible for the 15 hours free entitlement at an early years setting.

A parent may defer their child’s school place until the child reaches statutory school age provided the child starts in the same school year.

Your child must attend school full-time the term after your child’s fifth birthday (statutory school age).

Information for parents of children born between 1 April and 31 August 2011

The term after your child's 5th birthday is September 2016. If you decide to defer the offer of the school place until September 2016, the school place will be removed as this is a new school year. If this is your intention, please read the following statement, so that you understand the consequences.

I understand that should I decide to defer the offer of a school place for my child until the term after my child's 5th birthday (September 2016) that I will need to apply again for a year one place for September 2016. I note that, by deferring to statutory school age in September 2016, that a place may not be available at my preferred school/s.
I have read and understood the statement above.

Waiting lists:  

If you ranked school(s) higher than your allocated school and a place becomes available, the current offer will be withdrawn automatically (and allocated to the next child on the waiting list if applicable) and the higher ranked school place will be allocated, provided your child is on the waiting list. Waiting list information will be available from 30 April 2015.

Please indicate below whether you wish your child to remain on the waiting list of any higher ranked preferred schools. If this is not completed; it will be assumed that you wish to do so. It is your responsibility to ensure that we are kept up to date on whether your child is to remain on a waiting list, but we will send periodic reminders.
