Large item collection

Herefordshire Council encourages the reuse of furniture and electrical items. There are a number of organisations across the county who will collect good condition furniture and unwanted electrical items from you. For more information please visit the Reuse page on the Let's Waste Less website.

Who can use the large item collection service?

This service is available to Herefordshire households only.

For commercial waste, visit the Trade waste page.

If you would like to dispose of the large item yourself, you can do this at one of our household recycling centres.

How much does a large item collection cost?

The collection costs £20 for up to three items. Additional items cost £5 an item.

How do I arrange a large item collection?

To arrange a large item collection, please contact us at:

  • Telephone: 01432 260051
  • Visit one of our customer service centres

When booking the service you should list the items to be collected and notify us of any alterations 24 hours before collection.

Please note: items not listed are not collected.

A contact number MUST BE supplied when making the booking. We will make collection based on the information given. If collection is not successful due to incorrect information and a return visit is necessary a further charge may be made.

When will the large item collection take place?

Collections will be made the week after the booking is made. You will be given the collection day at time of booking. All collections take place between 9am and 4pm.

What large items DO you collect?

Free standing bulky domestic items or appliances which exceed 25 kilograms in weight or which do not fit or cannot be fitted into a domestic bin fall under this collection service. As a general guide items that are eligible for collection under this description are those which a householder would reasonably expect to take with them when moving house.  

A maximum of two people should be able to lift each item. This list is only intended to provide you with an idea of the types of items we can collect. It is not exhaustive. 

  • Armchair 
  • Bed base and headboard (1 item) 
  • Bicycle 
  • Bedside table 
  • Cabinet (free standing) 
  • Carpet (per roll, must be able to be lifted by 2 people) 
  • Chairs (each chair is one item) 
  • Chest of drawers 
  • Children's swing or climbing frame (must be dismantled)
  • Corner sofa (each section is 1 item) 
  • Cooker - gas/electric (must be disconnected from mains) 
  • Cupboard (free standing) 
  • Dishwasher 
  • Dressing table and chair 
  • DVD/video player 
  • Fridge / freezer (maximum of 3 per annum) No commercial fridges/freezers accepted
  • Lawnmower (no sit-on mowers)
  • Ottoman box 
  • Mattress 
  • Microwave 
  • PC tower 
  • PC monitor 
  • Sofa 
  • Stereo and 2 speakers 
  • Sun lounger 
  • Table 
  • Tumble dryer 
  • Television 
  • Underlay (per roll, must be able to be lifted by 2 people) 
  • Washing machine 
  • Wardrobe (free standing)

What large items do you NOT collect?

  • Asbestos
  • Bathroom suites
  • Clinical waste
  • Doors
  • Domestic refuse (black bag waste)
  • Fencing
  • Fluorescent lighting tubes
  • Grass or garden cuttings
  • Hazardous waste
  • Kitchen units or worktops
  • Liquids
  • Paint
  • Rubble
  • Sheds
  • Shower trays or shower screens
  • Sinks
  • Soil/stones
  • Windows

Please note: this list is not exhaustive and we have the final decision on whether we will collect an item or not.

What happens to the large items you collect?

Items collected through the large item collection service, which are suitable for reuse, are kept by the Full House charity and made available to those in need who have very limited resources. 

Where do I leave my large items for collection?

All items to be collected must be listed and should be placed in an accessible position, at the front of your property where possible. We cannot make collections from inside a property.  

Unfortunately we are unable to collect from upper floor flats or balconies. All items must be presented for collection at ground level.

Requests for collection from within your garage/shed/porch is at your own risk. Herefordshire Council and its contractors will not be held liable for any loss, theft, accident or damage caused as a result of the collection process.

How do I report a missed large item collection?

You should have been advised of the collection date when booking the collection. We collect large items between 9am and 4pm.

To report a missed collection, please provide your name, address and the details about the items left out for collection and where they were placed, to us at:

  • Telephone: 01432 260051
  • Online: Contact us form
  • Visit one of our customer service centres

Are there any local organisations that collect large items for free?
