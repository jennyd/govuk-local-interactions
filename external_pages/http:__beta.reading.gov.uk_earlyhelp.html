Help for Children & Families

We try to help children and families as early as possible to prevent a problem or tackle it before it gets worse.

If you think a child is in danger of harm, there is advice on our child safety and welfare page page.

Questions

Close allOpen all

I'm struggling with my child's behaviour - what can I do?

Our Early Help Services can help with:

  • Challenging behaviour at home or at school
  • Establishing consistent routines and boundaries
  • Understanding and managing challenging behaviour (mainly teenagers)
  • Improving relationships with your child.

If you feel you need support in these areas please complete our Early Help contact form.

 

I have a disabled child - what support can we get?

Our Disability Information Servicedetails the support available to children with disabilities or learning difficulties and their families.

I need childcare.

Go to our Childcare area.

What are children's centres and where is my nearest?

Children's centres provide services for families with children under five in your local area. Activities vary between centres and you can find out more, including links to their websites, on the Reading Services Guide.

If you live within Reading Borough, your address will have its own designated children's centre but you don't have to use that one if it is not the most convenient or doesn't provide the activities you're interested in. Search for your local children's centre.

What parenting courses are available in Reading?

Depending on the issue you have, we can offer the following courses to Reading parents:

  • Triple P
  • Webster Stratton
  • Time Out for Special Needs

To find out more, see the   parenting courses FAQ leaflet. [63kb] If you are interested in this support, complete an  Early Help contact form and we will contact you to discuss your case.

Your local  children's centres may also run parenting sessions.

My child is struggling to achieve at school - what should I do?

Speak first to the school to discuss your concerns. Your school will have a special needs co-ordinator (SENCO) who can advise on the best way to support your child. Find out more about what to do on our Reading Services Guide.

I'm struggling to get my child to attend school - what should I do?

Discuss this first with your school but if you need further advice and support, contact our Children's Action Teams.

A young person I know has a problem with drugs/alcohol.

Sourceis our drug and alcohol service for young people in Reading and can also help family members affected by a young person's substance misuse.

My child is in trouble with the police, how can you help?

Our Youth Offending Team works with children and young people to address the causes and help them understand the consequences of their behaviour to prevent re-offending.

We will get involved if:

  • Your child is charged with an offence and sent to the youth court - we will be at court to support you and work with your child on any sentence he/she receives
  • Your child receives a Conditional Youth Caution from police - we will get in touch to explain how we can work with your child and support you
  • Your child receives a Youth Caution or Restorative Disposal - we may contact you to discuss how we might help.

Please note that we give priority to children aged 10 to 12, children looked after by the local authority and children who have been violent towards their parents.

Even if the police take no formal action against your child, you can ask for help by speaking to the officer dealing with their case. The officer can submit a form (a YOT 1) to us outlining your concerns and what help you are asking for, and the Youth Offending Service will get in touch.

If your child is getting into trouble locally in your community but not being formally dealt with by the police and you need further help, contact our Children's Action Teams.

What clubs, services and activities are there for children, young people and families?

The Family Information Service Directorylists the activities and support available for families with children up to the age of 19 (or 25 for young people with special needs).

We also have specialist Youth Areaon the directory.

For families with children under five, take a look at our Children's Centres information.

My child does a lot to help at home because someone in the family is ill or has a disability

We run a Young Carers Projectwhich can offer support and access to other services.

I'm worried about my child's emotional wellbeing or mental health.

If you're worried about your child's sleeping and eating habits, levels of stress or if s/he appears withdrawn and unsociable or has talked about harming themselves, the professionals in our Children's Action Teams may be able to help.  Please contact us using a Children's Action Team enquiry form.

Find other local counselling services.

My child needs someone to talk to.

Time to Talkis a free confidential counselling service for young people in Reading aged 11 to 19 (and up to 25 for young people with additional needs).

We are also launching a new support service - Horse Assisted Education - for 5-19 year olds who need extra help with how they feel about themselves, their friendships, relationships and future. For more details, contact our Young Carers Service.

More counselling and support services are listed in our Reading Services Guide.

There is domestic abuse in my household

See our Domestic Abuse page to find out about information, advice and support available locally and nationally.

Our Children's Action Teams may be able to help. Fill out our enquiry form to raise an issue.

 

There is another issue within my family - where can I get help?

 Contact our Children's Action Teams who can give you help and advice.
