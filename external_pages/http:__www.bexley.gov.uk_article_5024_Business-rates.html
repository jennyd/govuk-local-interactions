Business rates

This is the national rate payable by businesses and is also known as the National Non-Domestic Rates (NNDR).

Business rates contribute to the cost of local services. Your business rate bill is based on the rateable value of the property and the multiplier.

Each year the Government sets the multiplier in line with the retail prices index (RPI). In 2015-16 this has again been capped at 2%.

2014/15 Multipliers:

  • Non-domestic rating multiplier - 48.2p (0.482)
  • Small business non-domestic rating multiplier - 47.1p (0.471)

2015/16 Multipliers:

  • Non-domestic rate multiplier -  49.3p (0.493)
  • Small business non-domestic rating multiplier - 48.0p (0.480)

In addition, from April 2010 businesses with a rateable value over £55,000 are required to pay the Crossrail Business Rate Supplement.

Business Rates 2015-16

The Chancellor in his Autumn Statement delivered on the 3 December 2014 made some announcements in respect of Business Rates for the financial year commencing on the 1 April 2015. These can be summarised as follows:

  • The Small Business Rate Relief will be retained at the higher level for a further year until 31 March 2016
  • The Retail Rate Relief introduced from the 1 April 2014 at a maximum of £1,000 has been increased to a maximum of £1,500 for the financial year 2015/16. Broadly this relief applies to shops, pubs and restaurants with a rateable value below £50,000
  • To extend the existing transitional relief scheme for two years for properties with a rateable value up to and including £50,000. As a result of this measure small properties with a rateable value of less than £25,500 that would otherwise face bill increases above 15% and medium sized properties with a rateable value of £50,000 or less that would otherwise face bill increases above 25% will benefit.

Paying by instalments

You will be able to choose to spread your payments over 12 months instead of the standard 10 from the 2014/15 year. If you wish to do this please let us know as soon as you receive your new bill by emailing bexley.nndr@secure.capita.co.uk

Business Improvement District

A Business Improvement District (BID) has now been created for Bexleyheath town centre, to improve the perception and image of the town, improve accessibility and transport, and ensure it is clean, attractive, safe and secure. A business levy is collected by the Council and paid to the Bexleyheath Business Partnership to fund the BID.
