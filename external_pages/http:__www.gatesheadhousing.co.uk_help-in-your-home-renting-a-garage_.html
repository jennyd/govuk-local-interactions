Renting a garage

Last updated on November 17, 2015, by The Gateshead Housing Company & filed under Help in your home - garages.

What will it cost?
Garage rents start from £5.28 per week for existing Gateshead Council tenants and £8.91 for other residents.

How do I apply for a garage?
You can apply for a garage by contacting any housing office.

You will be issued with an application pack, which provides all the information you will need including:

  • An application form.
  • A step-by-step guide
  • A garage estate list and the contact details of the nearest housing office.

How do I ensure the information contained in my application is up to date?
If your circumstances change you can contact your housing office and they will update your application.

The Gateshead Housing Company also carries out a review of all applications on a yearly basis to ensure our records are correct.

If you do not respond to the yearly review your application will be cancelled.

What proof of ID do I need to provide when I sign up for a garage?
If you decide to rent the garage you will need to sign a tenancy agreement and will be asked to provide the following forms of ID:

  • A passport showing that you are a British Citizen
  • Home Office letter confirming that you have a current right of residence in the UK
  • A new UK photocard driving license.

Or any two of the following

  • Benefit payment book
  • Original birth certificate
  • Paper driving license
  • Marriage certificate
  • Divorce/annulment papers
  • Credit cards
  • Bank statement from within last two months
  • EC or EEA identity card
  • Medical card
  • Life assurance or insurance policies
  • National insurance card
  • UK residence permit
  • Utility bill
  • Wage slips from current employer (dated within last two months)
  • Certificate of employment in HM Forces or Merchant Navy.

You will also be asked to provide a copy of the car’s vehicle registration document showing that you or a member of your household owns a car that is registered to your home address.

How do I pay rent for homes and garages?
The rent is required to be paid in advance and there are a number of ways to pay. They are:

Online
Pay and check your balance 24 hours a day.

Direct Debit
This is the simplest and safest way: choose from four preferred payment dates. To start paying this way complete a Direct Debit mandate and return it to us by post or via any housing office.

Standing order
Print out and fill in this form then post it or take it in person to your bank or building society.

At our payment offices
Pay with a debit card, postal order or cash at payment offices, including Gateshead Civic Centre.

Debit card
Pay with a debit card at any payment office or Gateshead Civic Centre.

Phone
Pay with your debit card by phone, 24 hours a day, by calling 0800 052 3455. It’s automated, safe, fast and easy.

Post Office
Use your rent payment card at any UK Post Office.

PayPoint
Take your payment card and cash to anywhere you see the PayPoint sign, including newsagents, supermarkets, convenience stores and garages.

  • Payment options – in and around Birtley, Low Fell, Springwell and Wrekenton
  • Payment options – in and around Gateshead town centre and Sheriff Hill
  • Payment options – in and around Felling, Leam Lane and Old Fold
  • Payment options – in and around Dunston, Teams and Whickham
  • Payment options – in and around Blaydon, Highfield and Ryton
  • Or find your nearest PayPoint outlet.

Post
Cheques or postal orders can be sent to any payment offices. Please do not send cash by post.

Salary deduction
If you work for The Gateshead Housing Company or Gateshead Council, your rent can be taken directly out of your salary.

What can the garage be used for/what can’t the garage be used for?
Garages are to be used for the storage of motor vehicles owned by the tenant or members of the tenant’s household.

However, if we have empty garages that nobody wants to use for this purpose we sometimes allow people to use them for storage purposes (provided that written permission has been given and that the materials aren’t flammable or dangerous in any other way)

If I purchase my council home what will happen to my garage?
You will be able to carry on renting your council garage, however your rent will be increased to either the private residents’ rate or leaseholders rate (see above).

Can I buy a council garage?
Yes, if the garage you rent is within the curtilage of your home and you have applied to purchase your home (through the Right To Buy scheme) you may be able to purchase the garage along with their home.

You won’t be able to buy if the garage you rent is in a block.

Can I swap my garage with another garage tenant?
No. You are not allowed to swap your garage. If your circumstances have changed and you wish to rent a different garage that may be nearer your home, you will need to complete an ‘Application for a Garage’ form available from your housing office.

How do I raise a repair?
If you have a problem with your garage, please contact the HomeRepairs service on 0800 408 6008.

How do I end my garage tenancy?
You can end your garage tenancy by informing us in writing or by completing a Notice of Termination form.  If the tenant has died and you are acting on their behalf you will need to supply a copy of their death certificate.

How much notice must I give to end my garage tenancy?
At least one week.

Please ask your housing office for a ‘Notice of Termination of Tenancy of a Garage’ form. Once we receive your notice we will send you a termination letter confirming when your garage tenancy will end and the balance on your rent account.

For further information please contact your housing office.
