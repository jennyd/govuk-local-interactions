Dropped kerbs

There are two types of dropped kerb applications that we process at Telford & Wrekin Council these are:

  • pedestrian dropped kerbs
  • vehicular access dropped kerbs.

Both of these applications are processed by different teams within the council. To ensure your application is dealt with by the correct team, please ensure you fill in the correct dropped kerb application form depending on your request. For more help in understanding what type of dropped kerb you require, please read the information below.

Pedestrian dropped kerb

A pedestrian dropped kerb is where we lower the kerb to make it easier for people with pushchairs, wheelchairs or trolleys to cross the road at certain points.

We tend to programme the work in batches this ensures that the work is cost effective. Therefore we undertake the work at approximately four month intervals although in urgent cases it is possible to get the work done sooner. We are unable to reliably assess each claimant in respect of their personal disabilities, therefore, we asses each request on a first come, first served basis.

Vehicular access dropped kerb

A vehicular access dropped kerb is a crossing across a public footpath or verge which allows your vehicle to gain access to your property from the highway.
