How to report an abandoned vehicle

Suffolk Coastal Norse carries out the removal of abandoned vehicles on behalf of the Council.

If you think a vehicle has been abandoned you can report it to Suffolk Coastal Norse, by calling 01394 444000 or emailing scs@ncsgrp.co.uk - giving the vehicle details and location.

If you think a vehicle has been involved in a crime you should contact Suffolk Police on 01473 613500 or 101 or Crime Stoppers on 0800 555 111.

Suffolk Coastal Norse work closely with the police, the Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA) and credit checking agencies to tackle the problem of abandoned vehicles and the DVLA are informed of any vehicles found on the highway without a valid tax disc.

Vehicle owners are liable for the cost of removal, storage and disposal and will only be able to claim back their vehicle, if it has not already been destroyed or disposed of, after paying all fees due. It is an offence to abandon a vehicle and we can impose a £200 fixed penalty fine on the person that has abandoned the vehicle or they may also face prosecution. Over 90% of confirmed abandoned vehicles are destroyed once we have removed them.  

Scrapping your vehicle

Responsible vehicle owners scrap their vehicles at an authorised treatment facility. Only an authorised treatment facility can legally scrap a vehicle and they will make sure it's dismantled in an environmentally friendly way.

They should give you a Certificate of Destruction straight away if they agree to take your vehicle. They will also tell the DVLA that you are no longer responsible for the vehicle. You should keep the certificate as proof that the vehicle has been destroyed and that you are no longer responsible for it.

You can find your nearest authorised treatment facility on the GOV.UK website.
