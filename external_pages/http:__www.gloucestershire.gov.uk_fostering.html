Thinking about fostering?

Call us on 01242 532654

Or fill in this short form and we'll call you back later. Our duty team is available Monday-Friday 9am - 5pm.

Call us today if you:

  • Are over 21 years old
  • Have a spare room in your home
  • Have no criminal convictions against children
  • Have no birth children under the age of two living in your home
  • Have experience of looking after/working with children
  • Have time to care 

We are particularly keen to hear from people who want to foster teenagers, sibling groups and disabled children.

Click on our frequently asked questions to see if you could foster with us.

 Frequently asked questions [965kb]

Already fostering?

Transferring to us is simple and you'll see the difference the moment you join us.

Fostering is a way to make a real positive difference to a child's life providing children and young people with a stable and loving home and the support and encouragement they need to flourish and do well. It is a challenging job, but many carers find it incredibly worthwhile and rewarding and it offers a new career path with the opportunity to undergo training and learn new skills.  

There is good support available to our foster carers as well as a skills and maintenance allowance to help care for a child. Foster carers with the county council receive over £300 a week to foster a teenager and considerably more if they have the skills to care for our more challenging young people.

To meet the diverse needs of the children and young people who need foster homes in the county we are seeking to recruit fosters carers from all backgrounds and sections of the community. There is no upper age limit to foster and you can be married, single, living with a partner (for over two years), a home owner or renting a property, employed or unemployed (although the age of the child will determine the hours you are able to work).

There are many different types of fostering, from short term placements and short break care for children with disabilities to providing a long-term or even permanent home - we can help you decide which is right for you.

Please note our priority at the moment is to recruit carers who would be willing to look at fostering a broader age group of children between the ages of 0-13 unless you would consider fostering sibling groups or children with disabilities. We are also very much in need of people willing to consider fostering teenagers.

I'm interested, what happens next?

For further information on becoming a foster carer, either fill in the on-line enquiry form below and someone will call you back to discuss the process in more detail or contact our Fostering Recruitment Team on 01242 532654 for an initial chat.
