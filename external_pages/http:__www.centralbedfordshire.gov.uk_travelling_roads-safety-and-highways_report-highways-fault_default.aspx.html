Reporting highways problems

Report a problem

You can report a range of problems on our roads or pavements online. You can register for an online account and report the problem – this way you will get updates on progress of your report by email and on your account. You’ll also be able to log straight into your account next time you want to report a problem. Alternatively you can report an issue anonymously.

Want to register or already registered?

You can report the issues below by register for an account or logging in to your account:

  • potholes
  • faulty street lights
  • dead animals 
  • damaged pavements / footpaths 
  • traffic lights 
  • drainage or flooding problems 
  • overgrown trees and hedges 
  • obstructions in the road 
  • grass cutting issues
  • road sign or other street furniture
  • another problem on the road or pavement

Registration is easy and only takes a few minutes, all you need is an email account that you can access.

Enter your email address and choose a password. You will then receive an account activation email. Please open the email and click on the link to confirm your email address. If you cannot find the email please check your spam or junk folders. You will then be able to complete your registration with your address details.

If you are a new customer you can watch this short video, which shows you how to register step by step.

If you registered for an account before 30 October, 2014 all you need to do is change your password. Again, we have created a short video to explain how to change your password.

Register now for your online account.

Hints and tips

Whenever you report a problem to us please tell us the town or village name (i.e. Ampthill, Biggleswade, Dunstable, Flitwick, Houghton Regis, Leighton Buzzard, Leighton-Linslade and Sandy), the street the problem is on and any other location details which will help us find the problem.
Whilst the council is responsible for most of the roads in Central Bedfordshire there are some privately owned roads and some roads in new housing estates might be still owned by the developer. The Highways Agency is responsible for the A1, M1, A5 and A421. You can report a problem on these roads via the Highways Agency website.

You can also tell us about a problem on the road or pavement using our mobile app.

Emergencies

If the matter is urgent then please call our Highways Helpdesk on 0300 300 8049. This number is answered out of office hours for emergencies.

What happens after you have reported a problem?

The first thing we will do is inspect the problem. Our response times and process is different for different highways issues.

Potholes

We will inspect the problem within 5 working days to assess the severity of the pothole. 

We will repair potholes which we determine to be dangerous (i.e. could cause severe injury or damage) within 5 working days of inspection.  Generally, potholes of more than approximately 2 inches / 5cm deep and 6 inches / 15cm in diameter are considered as dangerous. Smaller potholes may not be repaired but monitored to see if they become more severe.

If there are less dangerous potholes within 10m of the dangerous one we will repair all of them at the same time to help reduce the rate at which our roads deteriorate, make the most effective use of the crew’s time and cut down on repeat visits. 

Sometimes we will need to use a temporary repair to make the road safe until we can make a permanent repair.

You can read more about how we deal with potholes in our factsheet (PDF 276KB).

Street lights

We will inspect the problem within ten working days to identify the exact fault and repair it if possible at that time. Emergencies, such as street lights being struck by a vehicle or a door missing leaving exposed wiring, will be inspected and made safe within two hours. Please call us on 0300 300 8049 if it is an emergency.

There will be times when the street light cannot be repaired on the first visit.

Reasons for this could range from needing specialist access equipment or complicated traffic management to protect our workforce, to full replacements of the lantern. It could also mean we need the electricity company to fix a problem where the supply cable is faulty. In that case, the electricity company timescales can be anything up to 28 days or longer in some cases.

If the time clock is faulty the street light will stay on during the day time. If it is possible, we will fit a photoelectric cell to correct this.

If it is not possible to repair the streetlight due to the age and condition of it, we may include it in our programme for upgrading the street lights (link to street light plans). 

You can read more about our street lights in our factsheet (PDF 243KB).

Obstructions and dead animals in the road

The first thing we will do is inspect the problem. We treat obstructions on the road as a priority and will usually inspect within two hours and clear the problem as soon as possible.

Obstructions on pavements will be inspected within five working days.

If the obstruction is dumped rubbish (fly tipping) we will move the rubbish to the side of the road or pavement but we will not clear it. Our Environmental Protection Team will come and look through the rubbish to see if there is anything in there that can be used to identify the person who dumped it (e.g. a household bill) before removing it.

We will pick up larger wild animals that have recently been killed on the road that could be a road safety hazard. This includes badgers, foxes or larger wild animals. We will not collect smaller wild animals which do not cause a traffic hazard.

We will always collect a domestic pet such as a cat or dog. We will take the animal back to our depot where we check for a microchip. We will then freeze the animal for around a minimum of two weeks in case the owner comes forward. We appreciate that losing a domestic pet is difficult for owners. If you need to contact us, please call 0300 300 8049.

Traffic lights

We treat these problems as a priority and will inspect immediately and fix the problem as soon as possible.

Our traffic light engineers have an early warning system which alerts them if all of the lights at a junction stop working so that we can respond quickly.

Pavements

We will inspect the problem as soon as we can, we will try our utmost to do this within 5 working days.

If you report a hole or a trip hazard the speed of repair is based on the severity of the defect and where it is in the pavement. Generally, trip hazards of over 20mm or holes deeper than this and more than 100mm across we will treat these as urgent and will make them safe or repair within 24 hours of our inspection.

Other minor trips and potholes will be monitored.

Road signs and other street furniture

We will inspect the problem within ten working days. If the problem is dangerous, for example a sign overhanging in to the road, then we will attend within two hours and make the situation safe.

There may be occasions when the item of street furniture is not our responsibility. If it is not then we will contact the owner on your behalf, for example utility companies are responsible for their own surface cabinets on the public highway.

Flooding and blocked gullies

If there is deep standing water across the carriageway we will respond as soon as possible. Other drainage issues are inspected within ten working days.

Grass cutting

We will inspect the problem as soon as possible and see how we can resolve it within our programme of grass cutting.

Overgrown trees and hedges

Trees and hedges that we maintain are those which are growing within the roadside verge.

Unless there is a serious risk to safety we avoid cutting hedges between March and September so we don’t disturb nesting birds.

Where an overhanging tree, hedge or shrub which does not belong to us but has been identified as an obstruction to road users, we will ask the owner in writing to cut back the vegetation. If after 28 days this has not been done then we will do it ourselves and invoice the owner full costs plus an administration fee.
