Deposit your dream in North East Lincolnshire’s youth bank

Funding available for young people in the borough

Thousands of young people across North East Lincolnshire are being challenged to turn a dream project into reality with the help of Government funds designed to provide teenagers with safe places to go and positive things to do.

A total of £256,000 from the Youth Opportunity Fund (YOF) and Youth Capital Fund (YCF) has been allocated to North East Lincolnshire Council from April and bids are now being invited from 13-19 year-olds for the first slice of the 2010/2011 funds.

Young people have until mid-May to research their idea, get it down on paper and make a ‘business case’ for a grant. First and foremost they will need to show how their idea would be good for them, other young people and benefit the community.

Steve Kay, Head of Youth Services for North East Lincolnshire Council, said, “These funds have made, and are making, a huge difference to the lives of many young people in North East Lincolnshire. The more that we can enable and empower young people to contribute to achieving positive change for themselves and their broader community, the better for both them and their communities.”

YOF and YCF are part of the Government’s Aiming High for Young People strategy which aims to ensure there are safe places to go and interesting things to do for young people, at times when they and communities say they most want and need them.

Over the past year 81 projects in North East Lincolnshire have been funded through the grants, including drama, dance, gymnastics, sports and outdoor activities, music, multi-media and IT.

Courses have been held to help educate young people about drugs and alcohol, mental health, stress, sexual relationships and first aid as well as funding residential trips and days out, promoting active lifestyles and helping teenagers meet personal challenges. Funding gave Immingham’s young people the opportunity to undertake various rock band night events in order to raise funds for their required skatepark.

Shannon Dixon, 16, who helped win a small grant for the Clee Young Carers’ group, said: “We all want to learn more. In our case it was learning new recipes, finding out about healthy eating and gaining new skills. But without this funding from YOF, we couldn’t have done these things.”

Steve Kay said: “All in all, the funds have promoted and encouraged healthy lifestyles; how to stay safe, enjoy and achieve, have fun and be recognised. It’s about making a positive contribution to help the community and reaching their full potential as young people of North East Lincolnshire.

“Over 40 young people made the decisions on last year’s funding. Projects do have several hurdles to clear – not least in demonstrating how use of the Aiming High money will have a positive impact on the lives of young people, and their neighbourhood. There has been such a range of innovation and creativity from young people, it promises an exciting year ahead.”

YOF and YCF provide grants of between £50 and £40,000 for young people and decisions about how to award the money are made by young people from around the region. Those wanting to bid can get help from an adult supporter but the bid and project must be planned and led by young people themselves.

For more information and an application pack, go to www.youthbank.org.uk or email darren.sheeran@nelincs.gov.uk – or talk to your local youth project workers.
