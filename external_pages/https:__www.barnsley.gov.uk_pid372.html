    Abandoned Vehicles

    An abandoned vehicle is a vehicle which is in an open area or on a highway which does not appear to have a known owner.

    It is not simply a vehicle which is a nuisance, is untaxed or is obstructing or dangerously parked on the public highway. A taxed vehicle may still be abandoned.

    The decision whether or not a vehicle is judged to be abandoned rests with the authorised officers of the council.

    You can use our online form toÂ report an abandoned vehicle

    You may contact the police if the vehicle is causing an obstruction or is dangerous, or contact the Highway department if a car is causing a nuisance by being parked on a grass verge.

    .

    Procedure

    When you make a report, an officer will check their records to see if the vehicle is already being dealt with, or has been dealt with before. This presumes that a correct location, description and registration mark (often not possible if vehicle is burnt out) is supplied by the complainant. If on council land or highway and burnt out, an order will be placed for its immediate removal.

    In other cases, a visit will be made and the vehicle inspected. If a decision is made that the vehicle is abandoned, an order is placed for its removal, after which it is taken away. The officer will also note damage, any visible property in it, and so on. Enquiries into whether or not a vehicle is abandoned may take a little time. If the vehicle is fit only for destruction, it will then be destroyed. In other cases where the vehicle may have some value, attempts are made to ascertain the owner and a seven day notice served on them, requesting that they arrange to collect it from the storage compound (there is a charge for this). After this time, however, it can be disposed of or destroyed, if not collected.

    If the vehicle is abandoned on private land, the land occupier must be given a 15 day notice before it can be removed (waiver available) and can only be removed if the occupier does not object within this time.

    The officer will advise the complainant of the action taken (if it is not immediately obvious that the vehicle has been removed), explaining why it cannot be moved, if that is the case.

    On return to the office, once the vehicle has been removed, the officer will complete other forms which will be sent to the Driver & Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA), Hire Purchase Information and the police.

    In certain circumstances, the person who has abandoned a vehicle will face legal action. This can include the issuing of a fixed penalty notice to them, and/or prosecution.

    If you own a car that you wish to get rid of, the team can arrange to remove it and have it destroyed at no cost to you. All you need to do is phone up, complete a form that will be sent to you, and produce the vehicle's log book upon collection. The contractor will normally contact you to make arrangements to remove the vehicle within three working days of the completed form being received back and checked by the officer.

    Charges/fees

    Offenders found guilty of the offence of abandoning a motor vehicle can face a fixed penalty of Â£200 and / or a fine of up to Â£2500.

    You can pay by debit or credit card.

    How the customer can help

    When you contact the service, please have the following information on hand:

    • Precise location of vehicle
    • Make and model
    • Colour
    • Registration number (if known)
    • Is the vehicle taxed? (if known)
    • How long it has been there?
    • Possible owner details of the vehicle
    • Any damage to the vehicle, or other information that makes you suspect the vehicle is abandoned

    To voluntarily surrender your vehicle for destruction, there is a Voluntary Vehicle Surrender Form to complete. Alternatively, contact the service to arrange for one to be sent to you.

    Legislation

    • Refuse Disposal (Amenity) Act 1978

    • Removal and Disposal of Vehicles Regulations 1986 (as amended 1993)
    • Removal, Storage and Disposal of Vehicles (Prescribed Sums and Charges) Regulations 1989
    • Environmental Protection Act 1990
    • Clean Neighbourhoods and Environment Act 2005

    Response times/next steps

    Once a decision has been made that the vehicle is abandoned, the council endeavours to remove it as soon as possible.

    If the vehicle is on private land, however, we must serve a 15 day notice on the land occupier before removal (waiver available), and must have their permission to remove it.

    If it would be unreasonably expensive to remove a vehicle, it will not be moved by the council.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Where can I get rid of my old car?

    At present, you can contact the service, as above, if you live in Barnsley. The service can arrange for their contractor to remove your vehicle for disposal, from your property, at no cost to you.

    Alternatively, look in the Yellow Pages under "Car & Commercial Vehicle Dismantlers". Only sites licenced to accept "End of Life Vehicles" will be able to accept your vehicle. You could also contact the Environment Agency for advice on where you could take your car, on 08708 506506.

    How much will it cost?

    If the council's contractor arranges to take away your unwanted vehicle, there is no cost to you. Disposing of it yourself, it depends on current market forces. However, any charge will be much less than the Â£2,500 you could be fined if it is abandoned.
