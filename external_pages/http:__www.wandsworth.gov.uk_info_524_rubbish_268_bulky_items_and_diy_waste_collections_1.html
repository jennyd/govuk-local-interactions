Bulky waste and DIY waste collections

Re-use collection service

Donate your bulky items to the re-use service if they are in a good, usable condition. We have partnered with the Resco, a re-use service, to refurbish the items and pass them on to somebody else.

What you can donate

  • Furniture: including sofas, tables, chests of drawers and cupboards
  • Large appliances and white goods: including fridges, washing machines, cookers
  • Small electricals: almost anything with a plug including TVs (flat screen only), audio and computer equipment, kettles, toasters
  • Bicycles
  • Children's equipment: including prams, cots, toys
  • Garden tools and equipment: including lawn mowers, forks, spades.
  • Beds (but not mattresses) 
  • Textiles: curtains, carpets, bed linen, clothes
  • Books

Items must:

  • be in a good condition cosmetically (but do not have to be in full working order).
  • have the Fire Safety label attached on foam filled furnishings.

Do not donate items which are:

  • stained
  • ripped, or
  • torn.

If your items include re-usable and non-reusable items, only choose the re-use service if most of them are re-usable.

How to book a re-use collection

  • Book online or telephone 020 3405 4859.

Collection details

  • Collections will normally be made within three to four days of booking.
  • Collection staff carry ID and will arrive in clearly marked vans.
  • The re-use service will collect items from inside your property to keep them in good condition and avoid rain damage.
  • Items can be collected from upstairs provided that there is a lift if the item is on the third floor or above.   

What happens next

Once collected, items are refurbished if necessary and checked for safety at the Re:Work repair workshop in Smugglers Way, Wandsworth.

They are then sold from local re-use centres and charity shops at affordable prices. This re-use service is not-for-profit.

Find out where your nearest re-use store is.

About the re-use service

Benefits of using the re-use service for suitable items include:

  • Providing affordable furniture, appliances and household items to improve the living conditions of others.
  • Creating local employment, skills training and volunteering opportunities.
  • Reducing waste disposal related costs and environmental impacts.

Collection of non-reusable bulky items

If your most of the items you want collected are not suitable for re-use, book a bulky waste collection.
