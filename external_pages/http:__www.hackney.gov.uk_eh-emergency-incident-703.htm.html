Emergencies 

If you need to contact us in an emergency call 020 8356 3000 or see contact us for specific service telephone numbers.

Our out of hours contact telephone number 020 8356 2300.   

For emergency planning and business continuity management, please visit emergency planning.

For the police, fire and ambulance services call 999 and advise the emergency services of the incident.

As a local authority it is our role to support the emergency services in the case of a major incident.

Environmental health will be involved in directly advising the emergency services on the public health aspects of an emergency and as part of the Council's coordinated response to any such incident.  

What is a major incident/emergency? 

  • Natural disasters such as floods and storms
  • Pollution i.e. spillages, radioactive substances, toxic gases
  • War or terrorism
  • Any incident which is a threat to the welfare of the community

For information on London and national emergencies, please visit:    

  • Metropolitan Police
  • London Fire Service
  • Preparing for emergencies
  • London Prepared

The Council has an emergency plan (PDF, 1MB) in order to deliver services during a major incident. The plan contains details of actions to be taken following a request for assistance by the emergency services or the Council.

Hazchem emergencies

The Council provides general information, advice and links on hazardous chemicals and emergency procedures for incidents involving hazardous chemicals.

The Civil Contingencies Secretariat provides links to government and non-government sources on a wide variety of emergencies and crises that can affect the UK, plus emergency planning guidance and government information.

The National Chemical Emergency Centre plays a key role in national arrangements for responding to chemical incidents. It provides a 24-hour national advice service to the public emergency services on dealing with chemical incidents and is a central part of the Chemical Industries Associations.

Emergency Action Codes (commonly called Hazchem Codes) are designed to assist the emergency services in the first few minutes of dealing with a hazardous goods distribution incident. Further information on Emergency Action Codes is available from the National Chemical Emergency Centre.
