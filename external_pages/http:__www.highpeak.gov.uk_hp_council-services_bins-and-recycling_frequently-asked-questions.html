Frequently asked questions

  1. Why were my bins not emptied?

    There are several reasons why a bin may not be emptied.

    • The bin may contain the wrong materials. Please click here to ensure you only put acceptable items in your bins. We are unable to empty brown or green lidded bins that contain incorrect materials. If you have a yellow sticker on your bin it should advise you of any incorrect items. Please remove the contamination and present your bin on the next scheduled collection day or contact us within 48 hours to arrange a return visit.
    • The bin may have been overflowing. Make sure your bin lids are fully closed. No extra waste will be collected.
    • The bin may have been overweight. If you or we cannot move your bin the vehicle will be unable to lift it. Please reduce the weight by removing some of the waste.
    • The bin may not have been out on time. Bins should be presented by 6am on the scheduled collection day.  
    • The vehicle may have gone to be emptied.
    • In some cases we collect opposite sides of busy roads at different times of the day.
    • Access may have been difficult. Sometimes we encounter parked cars or road works blocking the road. In the winter bad weather can make it difficult or dangerous for us to operate. Please click here to check our collection update.
    • ​If none of the above apply - we may have missed your bin.  
      Please report it to us within 48 hours of the scheduled collection day and we will return within 5 working days.
  2. Can I have a commercial waste bin with a lid that locks?

    Our metal 660, 1100 and 1280 litre commercial waste containers are fitted with locks. Smaller plastic bins do not have locks. Please do not drill holes in the bin to fit your own locks.

  3. Is it possible to have an extra collection in addition to my normal commercial waste collection?

    In most cases this will be possible. Please contact the Commercial Waste Officer with your request. The additional charge will depend on the size of the bin(s) you have.

  4. I own holiday cottages, do I have to pay for a rubbish and recycling collection?

    Yes.    Waste from holiday cottages is classed as commercial waste, which is a chargeable collection service.  Rubbish collection is not included in your business rates.
    The exact charge will depend on the size of containers you choose to have.
    Please contact our Commercial Waste Officer for details.

  5. Can I have a larger container if I have under estimated the amount of waste my business produces?

    Yes. Please contact us.
    We will amend your contract and deliver the different container. Your payments will be amended to reflect the new price.

  6. How soon can you provide me with commercial waste containers?

    Delivery would normally be within 7 working days but we cannot start to collect your rubbish or recycling until we have received your completed commercial waste agreement and Duty of Care documents.

  7. Do you collect materials for recycling from businesses?

    Yes.  Please contact our Commercial Waste Officer for details on recycling your business waste.

  8. Is it possible to have more than one collection of commercial waste per week?

    Please contact us to discuss collection options.

  9. Do I have to fill in the Duty of Care Document?

    Yes.  You must complete a new Duty of Care and Contract document each year. It is an offence under the Environmental Protection Act 1990 to not have a completed Duty of Care for your business waste. You must keep copies of your Duty of Care Waste Transfer Notes for two years.

  10. What is special waste?

    Examples of special waste are: asbestos, acids, oils, paint, car batteries, solvents etc. Special waste is hazardous and can only be disposed of by someone who is specifically licensed to do so. Do not put hazardous waste in your commercial waste bin.

  11. Can I put oil in my commercial waste bin?

    No. Please do not put hazardous or liquid waste (including oil) in your commercial waste bin. There are private companies who operate special collection services for used cooking oil.

  12. Can I still use my home compost bin?

    Home composting is still the best way to deal with organic waste.  Use your green lidded bin for extra garden material and the food scraps such as meat, bread and dairy items you may not want to include in your home compost bin.
    Click here for information on home composting.

  13. When are my bins emptied?

    Your bins are emptied on an alternate week cycle. In most instances your green lidded bin, brown bin, green box and red bag are emptied one week and your black bin the following week.

    Each property has a specific collection day. We deliver an information leaflet & calendar of collection dates to every household each June. You can also check your collection day online.

  14. How can I comment on the rubbish and recycling service?

    We are grateful for comments on the services that we provide. Please use the contact waste collection form to pass on your comments.

  15. I have moved into a new property - do I have to pay for three bins?

    No. Please contact waste collection to arrange for delivery.

  16. My bin has gone missing, or, is damaged and needs repairing, what can I do?

    Please request a new bin or recycling container using the form in our do it online section.

  17. How do I report a missed collection?

    To report a missed collection please complete the report a bin collection problem form.

  18. I am unable to present my bins for collection, can you help?

    We offer an assisted collection if you are unable to present your bins on collection day due to illness or mobility problems. Please contact us to apply for this service. Once your application is approved we will collect and return your bins to a specified location on your property.

  19. What goes in my bins?

    We provide a set of containers for you to separate your waste for recycling, composting and disposal.  

    • A brown bin for mixed dry recycling.
    • A green lidded bin for garden plant waste and food scraps.
    • A green box for glass.
    • A red bag for textiles and shoes.
    • A black bin for all other non recyclable rubbish.

    Click here to see what goes in each container.

  20. What do I do with extra material for recycling?

    If you have additional material for recycling please leave it in cardboard box or carrier bag with untied handles next to you brown bin. If you regularly have extra recycling you could request a larger recycling bin.

  21. How can I get a green box for glass?

    Request a new bin or recycling container from our do it online section.

  22. What happens if I don't want to recycle?

    We would prefer you to recycle and compost as much as possible, if you do not want to use the green-lidded bin, brown bin and kerbside box this is fine.
    You will still only receive a fortnightly collection of your black bin (or sacks) and will not be entitled to present extra rubbish.

  23. What happens if I put incorrect items in my green lidded bin?

    If non acceptable items such as plastic or cardbpard are found in your bin it will not be emptied. A sticker will be placed on the bin to tell you what is wrong and how to arrange for the bin to be emptied.

  24. Can I put food waste in my green lidded bin?

    Yes, the green bin is for food waste and garden waste. :

  25. What happens to the material from the green lidded bin?

    The material is made into compost at an in-vessel composting facility in Buxton.
