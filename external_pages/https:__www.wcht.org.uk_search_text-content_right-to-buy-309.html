Right to Buy

The Right to Buy is based on a discount system. Only secure tenants who were transferred from Watford Borough Council to the Trust qualify. (If your tenancy began after September 2007, you do not qualify but may have the Right to Acquire.)

You must also meet one of the following rules

  • Have been a public sector tenant for at least five years.
  • Live in a self-contained house or fl at that is your only main home.

You will not be able to claim the Right to Buy if you are:

  • An undischarged bankrupt, or have a bankruptcy petition pending
  • The subject of a possession order served by the court at your landlord’s request.
  • Subject to formal Creditors’ Agreement made under the Insolvency Acts.
  • Living in housing that was specially designed for people with disabilities.
  • Living in housing that was specially built for elderly people and is let to people aged 60 years plus.
  • If your home is going to be demolished.

How to apply for the Right to Buy

First, contact Watford Community Housing Trust to get a Right to Buy application form. You should fill in this form, sign and date it and return it to the Trust. If you are joint tenants the other tenant(s) must also sign the form.

To request an apllication form please call 01923 209010 

What happens next?

The Trust will check your application details and let you know:

  • If you are entitled to buy your home under the Right to Buy.
  • What the purchase price of the home you currently rent will be, based on an open market valuation.

Price discount

The discount you are entitled to is set by the Government, the maximum discount you can receive is £75,000.

The letter providing you these details is called the Offer Notice.

The discount can depend on the value of this property. If you previously received a discount to help you buy a home, this will be taken off your Preserved Right to Buy discount.

How long will it all take?

You will be advised if you have the Right to Buy within 4 to 8 weeks of applying, depending on the checks that need to be made about your application. It will take a further 8 to 12 weeks after you have been advised if you have the Preserved Right to Buy to tell you the purchase price of your home.

Making the purchase

Having been informed of the price, if you want to go ahead, you must tell the Trust you intend to buy within 12 weeks.

You should also consider:

  • You will need to appoint a solicitor to act on your behalf.
  • If you require a survey to be carried out, you will need to arrange this at your own expense.
  • If you need a mortgage, you will need to contact an approved lender – bank, building society or mortgage broker.
  • You must complete the purchase within three months of receiving your Offer Notice. If you don’t do this, the Trust will serve up to two notices giving you a reasonable time to complete. If you still fail to do this, your Preserved Right to Buy application will be withdrawn.
