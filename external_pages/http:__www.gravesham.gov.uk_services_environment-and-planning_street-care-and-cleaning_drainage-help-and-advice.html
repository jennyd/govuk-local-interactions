Drainage - help and advice

Important changes to the ownership of private sewers

From 1st October 2011, due to new government legislation, local water authorities in England and Wales adopted the majority of private sewers and lateral drains, taking responsibility for their maintenance.

This transfer of ownership happened automatically on 1st October therefore occupiers and owners did not have to do anything to accommodate the transfer.

The responsibility for maintenance, clearing blockages etc for private sewers shared by more than one property belongs to your local water authority, as does the responsibility for lateral drains (i.e. drains which carry waste from a single property but which lie outside the curtilage of that property).

Private drains that serve only one property and lie within the curtilage of that property are still the responsibility of the private owner, as are cesspits and septic tanks.

Please note that a block of flats is considered as a single property for this purpose.

In the example above property A is responsible for maintaining their drain up to their curtilage. They however are not responsible for the section of drain within the curtilage of property B (Lateral Drain coloured blue). That responsibility is with the local water authority. Property B, C and D are still responsible for their drains up to the point they connect with another drain and become a sewer. From that point on the responsibility for maintaining the sewer lies with the local water authority.

The local water authority for Gravesham's area is Southern Water Services Limited. Further information on the hand over can be found on their website at Southern Water - Changes to sewer ownership

All underground drainage complaints that do not involve cesspits or septic tanks should be made to Southern Water Services who will investigate responsibility and inform the council if necessary. The contact telephone number for Southern Water Services is 0845 278 0845. If you are advised by Southern Water Services that they are not responsible for the affected drainage than you can contact Gravesham Council direct to discuss. The telephone number for environmental health admin is 01474 33 73 34.

Please be aware that the council is not the regulator for local water authorities. If you are unhappy with the service provided by Southern Water you can make a complaint to the Water Services Regulation Authority (OFWAT). Their telephone number is 01216 44 7500. Further information can be obtained from their website at OFWAT. Alternatively you can contact the Consumer Council for Water on telephone number 0121 345 1000 or 0845 039 2837. Enquires can also be made to enquires@ccwater.org.uk

Frequently asked questions

Will Gravesham Borough Council clear/repair blocked or defective drains?

Gravesham Borough Council is not responsible for clearing or repairing drainage defects to private or commercial premises. The Council is only responsible for clearing blockages and repairing drainage defects to the private drains offor its own properties e.g. Council housing.

Within the area of Gravesham, Southern Water Services are responsible for the maintenance of the mains public sewer and shared sewers. If you wish to inform them of a problem with their sewer you can contact them on telephone number 0845 278 0845 or via their website Southern Water.

It is the responsibility of the occupier and/or owner of private premises to remedy any defects within the private drainage which serves only their property and falls within their curtilage. Cesspools and septic tanks which do not fall within their curtilage would still be their responsibility to maintain.

It is strongly recommended that you first call your buildings insurance provider before you employ a contractor yourself as they may organise the clearance and work for you.

If you wish to employ a contractor yourself sources such as the Yellow Pages and Thomson Local advertise drainage contractors. It is recommended that if you use a drainage contractor you check their charges before authorising them to do the work.

Council tenants should contact their Housing Officer on telephone number 01474 33 70 00. Private tenants should contact their landlord.

There is a blockage/defect within the private sewer serving my property and my neighbours, who is responsible for remedying it?

From 1st October 2011 shared private sewers within England and Wales were adopted by the local water authorities, therefore you should contact them to resolve the matter. Within Gravesham Borough the local water authority is Southern Water Services.

Local water authorities hold enforcement powers under section 111 of the Water Industry Act 1991 which can be used against parties they believe have discharged any matter into the public sewers which may interfere with the free flow of waste water.

I Am Affected by a Drainage Problem But Do Not Know If I Am Responsible for Fixing the Problem, Can You Help?

Whilst we do hold some plans which indicate the layout of Southern Water Services main sewer lines Gravesham Borough Council do not hold plans for private drainage, or private drainage which was adopted by Southern Water Services from 1st October 2011

You are advised to contact Southern Water Services and request that they investigate.

If they discover that the defective sewer has been adopted by them then they will be responsible for remedying the fault. If they advise you that the drain is not their responsibility then you are advised to either contact Council Housing or your housing warden if the drain serves a council house, or contact the council's Environmental Protection Team if the drainage serves a private property.

I believe Southern Water Services is responsible for remedying the drainage problem, and I am dissatisfied with their Service, who do I make a complaint to?

The regulatory authority for Southern Water Services is OFWAT. You should inform them of your complaint directly as our service does not hold legal powers to enforce Southern Water Services to carry out works. Before doing so it is advisable to send your complaint to Southern Water Services in writing, retaining a copy for your own records.

The telephone number for OFWAT is 0121 625 1300 their website can be found at OFWAT.

Alternatively you can contact the Consumer Council for Water on telephone number 0121 345 1000 or 0845 039 2837. Enquires can also be made to enquires@ccwater.org.uk

How Can I Reduce the Possibility of Future Blockages?

Do not flush disposable items such as nappies, sanitary towels, condoms, baby wipes, incontinence pads etc down the toilet. They should be disposed of with your normal refuse, double bagging them if necessary to avoid odour problems. If you need a medical waste collection for certain items please request this from Waste Management Services on 01474 33 70 00.

Do not dispose of food scraps down the kitchen sink.

Do not dispose of cooking fat down any sink or drain, instead allow it to solidify and then dispose of with your normal refuse. Fat solidifying within drains is a common cause of blockages during the winter months and can build up over years.

Do not over use soap powder when using your washing machine.

Keep your drain grates free from leaves and moss, and regularly wash down with hot water and disinfectant.

Do not put chemicals down your drain other than those specifically for use in kitchens, bathrooms and drains.

Keep objects such as bottles and toys away from where they may fall into your toilet.

Can I Contact You For Advice?

The council are happy to offer advice and if appropriate visit to help assess the problem. Please note that if the problem is with drainage owned by Southern Water Services then any assistance we offer will be at our discretion.

Please contact the appropriate telephone number below to place your service request:

If you are an owner occupier please call Environmental Protection on telephone no: 01474 33 73 34.

If you are a tenant of a privately rented property please call Private Sector Housing on telephone no: 01474 33 74 66.

If you are a council tenant please call your Housing Officer or Housing Repairs on telephone no: 01474 33 70 00.

I have a Foul Drain Odour in my Bathroom/Kitchen - Can you Help Me?

The council does not investigate such odours in side a privately owned property as this is the responsibility of the owner/occupier to investigate and remedy the source of the odour. If however you are a tenant of a privately rented house it may well be the responsibility of your landlord to sort the matter out and as such you should contact them in the first instance.

If you are unsuccessful in getting your private landlord to investigate and remedy the situation you can contact the Private Sector Housing team for advice on the number given above. If you are a council housing tenant you need to contact either your Housing Officer or Housing Repairs for advice.
