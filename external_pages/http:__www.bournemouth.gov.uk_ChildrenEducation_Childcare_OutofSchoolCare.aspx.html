Out of School Care

Out-of-school clubs are for school-aged children. They are run outside of school hours and include breakfast clubs, after-school clubs, holiday clubs and play schemes.

Activities are planned to support children’s play, so they can have fun and relax with their friends. Some out-of-school clubs offer children the choice to do some educational activities, such as completing homework.

Ofsted will randomly inspect 10% of out-of-school settings which are registered on the Childcare Register each year.

Out-of-school clubs are staffed with playworkers. At least 50% of the staff in an out-of-school club must hold a valid Level 3 formal childcare and/or playwork qualification.

Benefits of out of school care

  • Children can relax and have fun with their friends
  • Out-of-school clubs can help support the needs of working parents
  • Children learn through play
  • Some clubs offer support to complete homework
  • They provide a safe and secure environment
  • Holiday clubs offer peace of mind during holidays when finding childcare could be difficult 

Times

Out-of-school clubs are run in out-of-school hours.

There are three different types of out-of-school clubs as follows:

  • Breakfast clubs open in the morning before school, usually from 8am
  • After-school clubs open in the afternoons, usually between 3pm and 6pm
  • Holiday clubs and play schemes open during school holidays, usually between 8am and 6pm

Did you know? Some parents arrange for children to be taken to, or collected from, their school or out of school club by a childminder.

Costs

Out-of-school clubs set their charges according to the service that they provide. Within Bournemouth, out-of-school clubs charge (on average) the following:

Breakfast clubs charge on average £2.30 per session
After-school clubs charge on average £7.50 per session
Holiday clubs charge on average £23 per day

If you work and pay for childcare, you may be able to get tax credits or childcare vouchers to help with the cost.

Other children

Most out-of-school clubs offer places for between 10 and 40 children.

The child-to-staff ratio must be a minimum of:

  • One adult to eight children for children under eight
  • One adult to 10 children for children aged between eight and 11

For more information on out of school clubs in Bournemouth and for help finding childcare, please contact the Children's Information Service.
