Fostering

All age ranges of children need fostering, from babies to teenagers and young mothers.  Some may be children on their own and others may be with brothers and sisters who need to stay together.  

Children come from a wide range of different backgrounds and some may have special requirements.  For example they may have particular dietary, cultural or religious needs.

Some children may have learning difficulties or other special needs. Foster children want and need the same experiences as any other child.  The foster carer's role is to bring warmth, stability and continuity of care to these children’s lives.

Have you considered fostering as a career?

Foster carers needed!

Hounslow has more than 320 children and young people in care, who, for a variety of reasons, are unable to live with their own family.

We are currently looking for foster carers for children and young people who are in our care. We would particularly welcome applications from those who are interested in fostering teenagers and sibling groups. Our looked after children and young people come from a diverse range of ethnic, cultural and religious backgrounds and we are looking to recruit foster carers who can meet the needs of our looked after children and young people.

Advice and support is always available for carers, 24 hours a day, 365 days a year and a generous weekly allowance is paid to meet the needs of each child placed. Hounslow’s allowances are higher than the recommended national rate for foster carers and are paid up to £816 per fortnight, per child.

If you have a spare room and you are interested in finding out more about fostering please call us on 0800 731 8558 for a friendly chat, or come along to one of our fostering information sessions.
