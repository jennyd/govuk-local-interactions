Clean Streets

Stockport Council are committed to making sure that the Borough remains clean and attractive for both visitors and residents.

Street cleansing services cover over 600 miles of public highway ensuring Stockport’s streets are maintained to standards laid out under the Environmental Protection Act 1990.

Street cleansing is the removal of grit, mud, litter, grease, debris and leaves from the highways. It is the standard of cleanliness rather than the frequency of the cleansing that is important. Our sweeping and cleansing programme concentrates on those areas which need attention most based on four grades of cleanliness.

  • Cleanliness Grades
  • Graffiti
  • Fly Tipping
  • Litter
  • Dog Fouling
  • Fly posting

The four grades of cleanliness are detailed below;

  1. No litter or refuse / detritus
  2. Predominantly free of litter / detritus and refuse apart from some small items.
  3. Widespread distribution of litter / detritus and refuse with minor accumulations.
  4. Heavily littered / heavy detritus with significant accumulations.

It is expected that the Council should, through monitoring and the appropriate use of available resources, keep land clear of litter and refuse so that it does not fall below a grade B.

During the leafing season, the normal street cleansing schedule is suspended so that leaf fall can be prioritised. The cleaning crews remain in an area until all the leaves are cleared, before moving on to the next area. This pattern is repeated throughout the autumn months until the borough is clear of leaves.

Where dangerous leaves (leaves that are beginning to rot and become a slip hazard) are reported the aim will be to attend the location within 1 working day to assess the danger and cleared within ½ a working day if deemed to be dangerous.

To report litter, problems with litter bins, dog fouling, broken glass, leaves or dead animals on the highway use our street cleansing online form located under "Report it" section.

Spillages on the highway that could cause an immediate hazard to road users need to be reported to as a priority using the details located on the right hand side of the page under "contact us".

Graffiti

Graffiti damages the appearance of our streets and public places, with areas affected looking neglected and uninviting.

Graffiti is an illegal practice; it spoils both public and private property and can be very costly to remove.

Graffiti is classed as anything which is written, scratched, painted or sprayed on the surface of any property. It ranges from the simple act of tagging a location with initials through to elaborate paintings.

The Council will remove graffiti from privately owned domestic buildings, public buildings, street furniture or monuments.

Other items such as telephone boxes, bus shelters and electricity boxes are the responsibility of the owner or company that has placed them there.

To report graffiti or fly posting issues use our online forms located under "Report it" section.

Fly tipping

Fly-tipping is the illegal deposit of rubbish on land that is not licensed to receive it. Fly-tipped waste can be found anywhere, such as roadsides, in lay-bys or on private land.

Fly-tipping can be dangerous, can be hazardous to the public and can also pollute surrounding land and waterways, damage wildlife and ecosystems and costs the council tax payer significant amounts of money to clear away.

In Stockport we deal with around 3000 incidents of fly-tipping each year and we urge residents to report fly-tipping to us so we can keep Stockport clean and green you can do this by visiting the report it section of the council website.

There are many different ways you can dispose of unwanted household goods. To find out more visit our waste and recycling webpages.

Fly-tipping is illegal. Anyone caught fly-tipping waste is committing a serious criminal offence. Fly-tipping fines are up to £50,000 and/or 12 months' imprisonment in a Magistrates Court.

If the case goes to the Crown Court fines are unlimited and a sentence of up to 5 years in prison can be awarded.

To report fly tipping issues use our online forms located under "Report it" section.

Litter

Few people admit to dropping litter but it's a problem that everyone can see. All litter is unsightly and makes our local areas look untidy and uncared for.

Any type of litter takes a long time to disappear naturally. According to Keep Britain Tidy the estimated timespan for items to degrade is:

  • Orange peel / banana skin / apple cores – up to 2 years
  • Plastic bags – 10 to 20 years
  • Aluminium cans / nappies – 80 to 100 years
  • Plastic bottles – indefinitely

There is a £75 fixed penalty for people who are witnessed dropping litter. Failure to pay will result in prosecution leading to a fine of up to £2500.

To report litter or problems with litter bins use our street cleansing online form located under "Report it" section.

Dog fouling

Some dog faeces contains a parasite which, if ingested, can lead to the onset of the disease Toxocariasis in humans.

Children are more susceptible to this disease which, in the worst case scenarios, can lead to blindness.

On average, the dog warden service receives 600-700 complaints about dog fouling every year.

It is a criminal offence for the person in charge of a dog to allow it to foul on designated land within Stockport and fail to remove and dispose of the faeces appropriately. This law applies even if a dog is out alone.

If anyone is witnessed committing a dog fouling offence, they could be liable to an on the spot fine of £50 or a fine of up to £1000 on summary conviction.

For more information, visit the dog fouling page.

Fly posting

Fly-posting is putting up unauthorised posters and stickers (usually adverts) in public places. Fly posting damages the appearance of our streets and public places.

Areas afflicted can appear to be neglected, which in turn, can discourage others from taking pride in their surroundings. Fly-posting can sometimes cause hazards, especially when it is on or near road junctions and obstructs the view of on-coming traffic.

Fly-posting is illegal. The Council will remove fly-posting that is put up on street furniture. Enforcement will be considered in line with our enforcement policies.

To report fly posting issues use our online forms located under "Report it" section.
