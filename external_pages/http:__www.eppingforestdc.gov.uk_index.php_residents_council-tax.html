Information about your Council Tax bill 2015/16

Epping Forest District Council collects your Council Tax and then shares this with Essex County Council, the Essex Police and Crime Commissioner, the Essex Fire and Rescue Service and the Parish and Town Councils. You will find links from this page to the information that will explain how your Council Tax bill has been calculated and how the money you pay is spent on local services.

 

Information about your Council Tax bill 2015/16 (pdf 371KB)

On average across the district, for every £1 of Council Tax you pay the money is shared in the following way

  • Essex County Council 72%
  • Essex Police 10%
  • Essex Fire and Rescue Service 4%
  • Epping Forest District Council 10%
  • Town and Parish Councils 4%

You can see the information from Essex County Council, Essex Police and Crime Commissioner and Essex Fire Authority by clicking on the following links:

  • Essex County Council
  • Essex Police and Crime Commissioner
  • Essex Fire and Rescue Service

+ - How much do you have to pay? Click to collapse

We work out how much Council Tax we must collect by adding together the money each of the four authorities needs. This year, the total Council Tax we have to collect is £74,187,656 not including town and parish councils. If for example, your home is in band D, the average Council Tax for this year will be £1,449.09 plus the amount you pay to your local parish council, which is shown on your Council Tax bill.
