Drain & Sewer blockages

My drain or sewer is blocked, what should I do?

Public Sewers
If the problem is on the public sewer (this is a shared pipe serving properties built prior to October 1937 or a shared pipe adopted by the water company) you will need to contact Severn Trent Water (STW) on 0800 7834444 who should arrange a clearance or repair (at no cost to you).

Private Sewers
If the problem is on a private sewer (a shared pipe serving properties built after September 1937 and not adopted by the water company) it is the responsibility of the owners or occupiers of those premises, which drain into it.

In the event of the appropriate owners or occupiers being unable or unwilling to arrange clearance of a blockage on a private sewer you should contact the Environmental Health Section.

After investigation to determine the properties affected by the blockage a Statutory Notice will be served on the owner or occupier of each property requiring them to clear the blockage (usually within a period of 48 hours). If the private sewer is still blocked after this time then an officer of the Environmental Health Section will arrange for a contractor to clear the sewer and the costs will be apportioned between those owners or occupiers on whom statutory notices were served.

Private Drains
If the drain is blocked, or otherwise defective, you are responsible for clearing it or arranging for clearance by a contractor. If you are unable or unwilling to deal with the problem then the Environmental Health Section can deal with it in a similar manner to a blocked private sewer as detailed above and you will be solely responsible for the costs incurred.

Pitch Fibre Pipes
Some private drains and sewers in this area are of pitch fibre construction. These pipes are liable to deform over the years. This reduces the cross section making blockages more likely. If blockages become too regular, the normal remedy is to replace the problem section of pipe.

Bag it and bin it campaign
The Bag It & Bin It campaign was instigated following discussions between South West Water, the Marine Conservation Society (MCS), Surfers against Sewage and other organisations concerned about the occurrence of Sewage Related Debris (SRD) in the water environment.
