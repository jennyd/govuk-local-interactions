Large or bulky household waste

How to dispose of large or bulky items such as furniture and fridges.

Sell it or give it away

If the item is in working order you could donate it to charity, give it away or sell it and make yourself some cash.

Reuse and recycling centre

You can take bulky household items, including fridges and freezers, to the reuse and recycling centre. Most of this waste is recycled.

Ask your retailer

If you buy a new item which is delivered to your house, some retailers will take away your old item.

Our free bulky waste collection

You may have to wait up to five weeks between booking a collection and the collection taking place.

Conditions

Our free bulky waste service has a number of conditions:

  1. Only available to residents who are up to date with their council tax
  2. Eligible residents can book a collection of up to five items, once every three months
  3. We will only collect the items as notified by the residents booking the collection. Anything else left out with the booked items will be left.
  4. All bulky waste items to be left out by 6am on the day of collection. If this does not happen, the collection may be missed and the waste crews will not be able to go back to collect it later that day.
  5. All bulky waste items to be left in the front garden for collection. If your home has no front garden, you need to tell us so we can prioritise your collection for earlier in the day.
  6. You must not leave items on public space such as the pavement or road. If you do, you risk having to pay a hefty fine which could amount to thousands of pounds. You are also at risk of being taken to court and prosecuted for the fly tipping offence.
  7. Examples of what we collect: electrical appliances*, TVs, monitors, furniture, rolls of carpet, bagged household waste (must be bagged or tied and light enough for one person to lift safely)
  8. We will not collect:
  • Hazardous waste
  • fence panels, soil, rubble, construction and demolition waste (You will need to hire a private waste contractor to dispose of this waste. You have a duty of care to ensure that they are an authorised waste carrier. They need to provide you with a waste transfer note.)
