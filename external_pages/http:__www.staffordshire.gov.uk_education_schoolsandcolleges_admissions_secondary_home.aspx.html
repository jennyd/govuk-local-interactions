Admission to Secondary and High School

Important dates for applications for September 2016

 

07 September 2015

Applications opened for all Staffordshire residents. 

Please see the Secondary School Open Evening timetable and Secondary Information for Parents Booklet. 

31 October 2015
 Closing date.  This has now passed.  However, you can make a late application by completing a paper form.
Spring Term 2016

Applications are processed and information is shared between neighbouring local authorities.

01 March 2016

 Offer day.  Online applicants will receive an email.  Others will have their offer posted 2nd class on this date. 

If your child is currently in Year 9, s/he is eligible to apply for a place at a University Technical College (UTC) for September 2016. 

In Staffordshire, there is one UTC currently operating which offer places to pupils from Year 10 onwards (JCB Academy at Rocester). The Burton and South Derbyshire UTC is due to open in September 2016. There may be other UTCs operating in neighbouring local authorities.  These UTCs deliver practical learning combined with academic study.

Staffordshire also has an Academy which offers places from Year 9 onwards - The Rural Enterprise Academy.

See below for more details of how to apply:

  • The JCB Academy - applications closed on 31 January 2016. You can make a late application by completing a paper form.
  • The Rural Enterprise Academy - applications closed on 31 January 2016. You can make a late application by completing the relevant form on the Academy's website.
  • Burton and South Derbyshire UTC - applications closed on 01 February 2016.  Please view their website for details on how to make a late application.
  • West Midlands Construction UTC - applications close on 31 October 2015.  You can make a late application by completing a paper form.

Important points to remember

You must apply by 31 October 2015 even if you have an elder child attending your preferred school.

You are strongly advised to make more than one preference.  Failure to do so could have repercussions in terms of the distance your child is required to travel to school and you will not gain an advantage by only listing one.

Be realistic about the schools you apply for.  It is recommended that you include your catchment school as one of your preferences, as otherwise you will not be considered for a place.

All applications are considered at the same time.  Priority is not given to those who put the school as a first preference, or on a 'first come first served' basis. 

Wherever possible, we will aim to offer a place at the most preferred school.  Last year 94% of Staffordshire children received an offer of their first preference and 98% got one of their top 3.

Understand the admissions criteria for each of your preferred schools, this will enable you to assess what order of priority will be given for each school.

You may be required to provide additional information or submit a supplementary form if you are applying for an Academy, Trust or Voluntary Aided school.  Please see below.

It is your responsibility to provide any supportive information e.g. baptismal certificate or medical evidence.  The Local Authority will not seek to obtain this information on your behalf.
