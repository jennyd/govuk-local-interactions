Pay Your Council Tax

Thinking of contacting us about your Council Tax or Housing Benefit? ...No need!

If you register for MySouthend you can have access to your Council Tax bills, make payments, view your account, check your housing benefit entitlement, payment dates and notifications, and now tell us about changes using our new MySouthend forms, all from your secure online account.

To find out more, see the About MySouthend page.

To register, or to sign in to your MySouthend account Click here.  


Pay Your Council Tax Online or set up a Direct Debit

When you set up your Council Tax account and, at the start of each financial year, you will be sent the dates that your instalments are to be paid and the amounts you have to pay.

Usually the monthly instalments will start from 1st April until 1st January to clear your charges for the year. But you can ask to have your instalments spread over 12 months from 1st April to 1st March.

If you are struggling to pay your instalments on time, we suggest that you call us on 01702 215001.

This is because early contact could stop us having to take action because you haven't paid.

There are many ways to pay your Council Tax, please choose from one of the methods below.

Online or on the Phone

You can pay online or on the phone using a credit or debit card. Credit card payments incur a 1.5% surcharge.

We accept:

  • Mastercard
  • Visa (not Visa Electron)
  • Switch/Maestro

To pay online please use our online payment system.

To pay over the phone call the 24 hour automated payment line on 08702 406650.

Direct Debit

If you pay by direct debit, each month we will automatically claim the instalment from your bank account.

Paying this way you can if you prefer choose to spread your payments from April until March and you can pick one of either the 1st, 10th, 20th or 25th as your monthly payment date.

You can set up a direct debit by:

  • using the online direct debit form or
  • printing a Direct Debit form and completing and returning it to the Council.

If the payment amount or date changes we will tell you 14 working days before your payment is meant to come out of your account.

If an error is made by us or your Bank/Building Society, you are guaranteed a full and immediate refund from your branch of the amount paid.

You can cancel a direct debit at any time by writing to your Bank or Building Society. Please also send a copy of the letter to us.

At the Bank

You are likely to be charged an extra fee for paying at the bank.

To pay at the bank you need to quote your Council Tax account number, putting the letter 'T' in front of it.

You will also need the Council's bank details.

  • Sort Code: 20-79-73
  • Account No: 20145904
  • Account Name: General

You will also need to tell the bank the instalment amount.

AllPay Payment Card

If you don't have a bank account and cannot pay by any of the previous methods, you can ask for an AllPay card.

With the AllPay card you can pay at anywhere that has a PAYPOINT sign and at all Post Offices.

To find your nearest outlet visit the AllPay website.

This card remains valid unless you move address or you get a new Council Tax account number.

If you would like an AllPay card or you have lost your card, please contact us using our Council Tax Query Form.

Cheque

Send a cheque (made payable to Southend Council) along with a letter quoting your full name, address and account number to:

Southend-on-Sea Borough Council
PO Box 21
Civic Centre
Southend-on-Sea
Essex
SS2 6AN

Standing Orders

You can also pay by setting up a standing order. To set up a standing order, with every payment you need to quote your Council Tax account number putting the letter 'T' in front of it.

You also need the Council's bank details.

  • Sort Code: 20-79-73
  • Account No: 20145904
  • Account Name: General

With a standing order it is your responsibility to make sure that the correct amount is paid and that you pay your instalments on time.

Standing order payments can take up to 7 days to reach us after leaving your bank account.

Salary Deductions (SBC Employees Only)

If you live in Southend and work for us you can pay your Council Tax through salary deductions.

To set up salary deductions, simply complete the Agreement To Deduct Council Tax Payments From Salary online form

Once it has been submitted your Council Tax will be collected from your salary from the next available pay date through to the following March.
