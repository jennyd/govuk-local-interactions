Pests and infestation

If you have problems with rats, mice, wasps or ants you can arrange a pest control visit. We aim to deal with your request within five working days, or 24 hours for removal of rats.

Request a pest control visit

Please use the online form via the button below to request an appointment.

Appointments are available on the following days in the areas listed by post code:

  • Monday – L1, L2, L6, L7, L16, L17, L25, L27
  • Tuesday – L3, L4, L5, L18, L19, L20, L24
  • Wednesday – L8, L12, L13, L28
  • Thursday – L9, L10, L11, L14, L15

We must receive your request by 1pm on the day before we visit our area. 

Arrange a pest control visit

Urgent work

Friday is left for urgent work. If you think your job is urgent, please make sure your request is made by 1pm on Thursday.

Examples of urgent work include rats in a property that are causing distress.

Do I have to pay?

  • Wasps and ants nests - £62 for a single treatment, or £92 for more than one nest. Payment is taken on booking. If we come out to you and a treatment is not needed (or a wasps' nest turns out to be a bees' nest - see information on Honeybees below) we will refund the fee, minus a £20 call out charge. Please note we do not remove dead wasp nests.
  • Treatment of rats and mice - free for domestic properties in Liverpool.

Commercial service for landlords and businesses

We provide a commercial pest control service for landlords and businesses. Please email us at pest.control@liverpool.gov.uk to arrange an appointment for us to carry out a survey and give you a quotation for the work.

Honeybees: Contrary to popular belief bees aren't protected and can be treated, however they are endangered so we'd always recommend exploring all other avenues before considering eradication.

For more information please visit The British Pest Control Association website.

Urban foxes: We are not responsible for the removal of urban foxes. Please see the RSPCA website for useful information on how to deal with foxes in your garden.

Bats: We do not deal with bats. For information, help and advice about bats please contact the Bats Conservation helpline on 0845 1300 228 or visit the Bats Conservation Trust website.
