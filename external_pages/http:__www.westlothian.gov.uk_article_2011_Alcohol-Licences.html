Alcohol Licences

If you wish to sell alcohol on a premises then you will need a licence.

All alcohol licences for premises within West Lothian are issued by West Lothian Licensing Board.

The sale of alcohol is controlled by law. There are a range of initial alcohol licences depending upon your requirements. 

Information regarding the different types of alcohol licences and applications can be found using the menu on this page.

Each local government area must have a Licensing Board. West Lothian Licensing Board consists of 10 Board members.

  • A Convener - elected at the first meeting after the council election and holds office until the next election, and;
  • Nine ordinary members.

West Lothian Licensing Boards Festive Policy

Following the review of the Board's festive policy and having considered the responses received during the consultation, the Board would advise that at the meeting on 14 August 2015 the undernoted policy was agreed which will apply to the festive period December 2015 to January 2016 only. 

The Board decided that a further review of the festive policy will be undertaken next year with a view to agreeing a policy to cover the 2016/2019 period. Public consultation will be undertaken early next year and further information on this consultation process will be published on this page. 

Licence holders are encouraged to apply as early as possible for extended hours over the festive period. The Board's policy is that applications must be made at least 35 days prior to the event. However, you should be aware that due to the statutory timescales for processing applications very little notice of the outcome can be given if applications are not submitted until November.  In order that you are able to plan your festive events the Board would encourage you to make your applications as soon as possible and by no later than October if you want to have at least a month's notice of the outcome of your application. 

You can access a copy of the Boards  amended Policy Statement [377kb] within the 'related documents' section of this page.

West Lothian Licensing Board Statement of Licensing Policy - Section 7 Overprovision

The Licensing Board in conjunction with the Licensing Forum began an evidence and information gathering exercise in May 2014 as stated in section 7 (4) of their policy. The timescales for this stage were that it would be complete by October 2014 with further consultation taking place with stakeholders after this date if appropriate. The Licensing Forum requested that the Board extend the period for them to complete the exercise until July 2015. The Board have agreed to this request. Further updates will be provided after this date.

Complaints regarding the operation of licensed premises

If you wish to complain about the operation of a licensed premises you should initially make your complaint to

  • West Lothian's Licensing Standards Officers by emailing LSO@westlothian.gov.uk or telephoning 01506 281632 (please leave a message if your call is not answered) or 
  • Police Scotland by telephoning 101 or emailing lothianscotborderslicensingwest@scotland.pnn.police.uk

Should you not be satisfied with the outcome of your complaint you can make an application  to the Board for a premises licence review. More information can be found in this document entitled  'Requesting a Premises Licence Review' [46kb].

Local Licensing Forum (LLF)

The Forum is independent from the Licensing Board but at the same time they share the common goal of having the best interest of the local community at the heart of decision making.

The role of the Forum is to keep under review the operation of the new Licensing Act in the Forum area and to review the relevant Licensing Board functions. It is also their role to give advice and make recommendations to the Board in relation to those matters as the Forum considers appropriate. This will be mainly at a policy level as the Forum cannot comment on individual cases.
