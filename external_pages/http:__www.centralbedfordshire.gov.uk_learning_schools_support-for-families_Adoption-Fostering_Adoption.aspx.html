Adoption

We welcome everyone who has an interest in adopting and offer a telephone enquiry service from 9am to 5pm Monday to Thursday and from 9am to 4pm on Friday. 

You can reach our Duty Social Worker on 0300 300 8090. If you would like us to contact you please email us at adoption@centralbedfordshire.gov.uk with your contact details.

In order to adopt you will need to be over 21 years and be a safe person (see can I adopt). Our children need families who can:

  • consider adopting siblings
  • can consider children up to 8 years old
  • will celebrate and support their heritage

You don't have to be a British Citizen to adopt a child, but:

  • You (or your partner, if you're a couple) must have a fixed and permanent home in the UK, Channel Islands or the Isle of Man
  • You (and your partner, if you're a couple) must have lived in the UK for at least one year before you begin the application process
Come to an information session

If you'd like a chat with us to find out more about adoption, we hold regular information sessions.  We'll be able to answer any questions you have to help you decide if it's for you. 

Give us a call on 0300 300 8090 to find out more and book your place.  You can also register your interest in adoption and request a call back by using our online form under the do it online section of this page.

Post adoption support

There may be times throughout your child's life when you would find it helpful to talk to someone outside of the family about issues related to adoption. You may for example want some ideas about how to discuss adoption with your child, or how to react to and cope with your child's feelings about his or her adoption or you may be concerned about contact arrangements.

We provide post adoption support to all the children we place. If a child is living in Central Bedfordshire and it has been three years since their Adoption Order has been granted we will also offer support.

Our Support Service can offer:

  • the chance to meet up with other adopters and adoptive families
  • groups and workshops to help explain adoption to your children
  • discussion and counselling on any aspect of adoption
  • advice and help in accessing specialist services for you or your child
  • an assessment of your adoption support needs at any time until your child reaches the age of 18

By law all adopted children are entitled to an assessment for their support needs and we will provide this on request. Whilst we will always seek to support any assessed needs an adopted child may have we cannot guarantee this. A health need for example would require support from health services rather than our own.

The Adoption Team will be happy to talk to you to discuss your individual support needs.  For further information and to talk to our Duty Social Worker contact us on 0300 300 8090 or email us at adoption@centralbedfordshire.gov.uk.

I am an adopted person

If you have been adopted and are aged 18 or over, Central Bedfordshire's Adoption Team can provide you with support and counselling. 

Our social workers are there to help you and to give you an opportunity to discuss what adoption has meant for you. This may be the first opportunity you have had to discuss your adoption with anybody who is not closely involved, such as siblings and adoptive parents.

If you want to trace your birth family, we will help you and give you the opportunity to discuss your expectations and fears. Research has show that for adopted people finding out about their origins can have a profound effect. 

Many adopted people simply want information from their records, others wish to go further and actively search for their birth families.

If you would like to discuss your situation with a social worker from the Adoption Team, please give us a call on 0300 300 8090 from 9am to 5pm on Monday to Thursday and 9am to 4pm on Friday.

I am the birth relative of someone who has been adopted

If you are a close birth relative or an adult (aged 18 or over) who was adopted as a child you may like the opportunity to discuss your feelings about this with a member of the Adoption Team. 

Under some circumstances it may be possible for us to trace that person on your behalf in order to find out if they would like to have any form of contact with you.

If you would like to learn more about this service, please ring to talk to a social worker from the Adoption Team.

My child is being placed for adoption

Some birth parents may have had their child removed by the Local Authority because of concerns about the child's welfare. 

They may then decide themselves to have their child adopted. In other cases, it is the Local Authority that will decide it is best for the child, and you may not be happy with the decision.

Birth parents are able to have access to a support worker, independent of their child's social worker, as soon as the Local Authority definitely decide that adoption is to be their proposed plan for the child.

Birth parents are entitled to counselling, advice and information about adoption and related issues.  This includes an explanation of the adoption process so that you know what is going to happen and what it means for you. You will be able to talk through any worries you have about adoption and your views about the plan.

Central Bedfordshire Adoption Team can help you:

  • access independent advice and support
  • maintain letterbox contact with a child who has been adopted

If you would like further information, please ring the Adoption Team on 0300 300 8090.

Adopting your partner's child

If you wish to adopt your partner's child/children from a previous marriage or relationship so that you become the legal parent of the child/children and share parental responsibility for them, this describes adoption by a step parent.

Adoption is not necessarily appropriate in all step parent situations. The Local Authority has a duty to investigate the circumstances and prepare a report to court of their findings. This is because once adopted the child's legal links with their absent birth parent and wider family will be broken.

If you would like to talk to someone who can give you advice based on your family's circumstances, please call the Adoption Team.

Inter Country adoption

Adopting a child from a country outside of the UK is called Inter Country Adoption.  Central Bedfordshire Council has an agreement with PACT (Parents and Children Together) to advise enquirers about adopting a child from abroad.  PACT may go on to complete the assessment of prospective adopters who wish to adopt from overseas.

Should you require further information on Inter Country Adoption or wish to take forward an application to become an approved adopter for a child from another country please contact PACT directly on 0300 456 4800.

Alternatively you can email icduty@pactcharity.org or visit pactcharity.org.

What is a Special Guardianship Order?

The Adoption and Children Act 2002 introduced a completely new order, Special Guardianship, and this came into effect in December 2005.

It is an order made in court for the applicants to care for the child and have day to day parental responsibility for that child. The parents are still legally the child's parents but their parental responsibility is limited. The purpose of the order is to give the child the security of a permanent home until the age of 18 and beyond.

The Central Bedfordshire Fostering Team is responsible for offering ongoing support to families where there is a a Special Guardianship order in force and the child is under the age of 18. This could, for example be in the form of supporting ongoing contact with birth parents.

If you would like further information about this, please call and speak to a member of the team by calling 0300 300 8090 or by email at fostering@centralbedfordshire.gov.uk.
