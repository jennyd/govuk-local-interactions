Equipment to help you at home

  • Adult social care

If you or someone you care for is unable to carry out everyday tasks such as bathing, washing or dressing without support, there is a range of specially designed equipment available to help you live independently in your own home.

Types of equipment available

  • equipment to help you get in and out of bed safely such as bed levers, pillow lifts, mattress lifters
  • specialist chairs
  • grab rails, raised toilet seats, commodes
  • hearing impairment equipment such as amplified doorbells and phones
  • visual impairment equipment such as talking clocks, large-button phones
  • standing and mobile hoists
  • household items such as kitchen equipment
  • bath boards, seats
  • telecare equipment such as heat detectors, voice alert, flood detectors

Equipment to help you at home can be purchased independently, or with funding from the council if you are eligible.  See our online directory for details of local equipment providers.

How to get equipment in Barnet 

To get equipment from us you will have to have an assessment of your needs to see if you qualify for support from us.

To find out if you qualify for equipment and support from us, speak to your allocated occupational therapist or social worker who will carry out an occupational therapy assessment. If you do not have allocated occupational therapist or social worker, contact Social Care Direct Tel 020 8359 5000.

How to get equipment

You can get equipment from us in two ways:

1. Prescriptions

If your equipment costs less than £100 you will be given a prescription, which you can take to one of the local accredited equipment suppliers who will be able to provide the equipment free of charge. Details of participating equipment suppliers can be found in our directory.

The supplier will have trained staff who will be able to demonstrate the equipment and give any information, help or advice that you need.

If you decide that you would like the item you have been prescribed in a different brand or colour, you will need to pay any difference in cost. You will own the equipment that has been prescribed to you.

2. Medequip

If your equipment costs more than £100, we will arrange for the equipment to be delivered to you through Medequip.

Unlike equipment issued to you through prescriptions, equipment issued to you through Medequip is on a loan basis and therefore must be returned once you have no further use of it. 

What to do when you have finished with the equipment

If you no longer need equipment you have been prescribed you can recycle it at  the Civic Amenity Recycling Centre.

If you have been provided with equipment  through Medequip you must return it. To arrange a collection for equipment, please call Medequip, Tel: 020 8438 2920.
