Safer Routes to School

Last updated: 21/11/2014

Since 1997, Cornwall Council has been working with schools to encourage sustainable travel, initially, through the Safer Routes to School programme but more recently through the development of School Travel Plans (STP). These schemes provide a whole school community approach to travel awareness, road safety, sustainable transport and healthy travel options.

A school travel plan is an action plan of measures to encourage walking and cycling but also seeks to find infrastructure solutions to transport and safety problems on and around the school site, in addition to the journey to and from school. As part of the development of an STP, information about the journey to school is collected from pupils and parents and used to assist our traffic engineers in providing the best possible environment for safe walking and cycling.

Requests for infrastructure improvements such as new crossing facilities, traffic calming, a speed limit reduction or street lighting can be made through a school’s travel plan. A highways engineer assesses the requests based on their feasibility, cost and effectiveness.
