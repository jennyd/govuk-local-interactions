Housing advice

If you are experiencing problems with your accommodation, don’t wait till the last minute to look for help.  

The Housing Needs department can offer advice and assistance with your housing problem but it is best to get as much help as you can. There are many charities and organisations that can help with advice, assistance and advocacy.

The Citizens Advice Bureau can provide you with useful information to help you consider your options.

There are other organisations that can also help - see the links on the right for more information.

The laws concerning housing and welfare rights are very complicated. When you seek advice, you should take any documentation that is relevant to your case; for example, your original tenancy agreement, court papers, correspondence from your landlord, housing benefit letters. Even if you do not have any papers, you should still get advice.

You can also contact our housing service for advice, by completing our online housing enquiry form (using the link at the bottom of this page).  We can give advice about a range of appropriate housing options to help you to keep your current accommodation or find a new home. However, there is high demand for housing in the borough and it may not be possible to help you in the way that you would like.

We can visit where you are staying to discuss your options and will do our best to keep you in your current home. If we can't, we will try to help you find privately rented accommodation to meet your needs. In extreme situations only, and as a last resort, you may be temporarily put into nightly paid accommodation, or supported lodgings if you are a vulnerable young person. This accommodation is likely to be outside the borough due of the shortage of accommodation within the borough. You will be expected to pay with the help of housing benefit, if applicable.  

You should seek housing advice if you are worried about losing your home because of:

  • Relationship breakdown

  • Domestic violence

  • Friends or relatives want you to move out

  • Possession proceedings - what to do if you receive a Notice to Quit, Court Summons, Court Order, Bailiffs Warrant

  • Being forced to sell your home

  • Needing support to stay independent

  • Needing help with adapting your current home and make it more suitable to your needs

  • Custody support - for those going into prison on remand

  • Mental health and housing issues

We can also provide you with assistance in the following areas:

Problems with your landlord including:

  • Repair problems

  • Privacy - landlord coming into your home uninvited

  • Rent problems - rent books, rent disputes, rent increases etc

  • Landlord refusing to accept rent

  • Overcrowding and shared facilities

  • Nothing in writing? - Your rights if you have no written tenancy agreement

  • Tenancy deposit legislation

  • Landlord not paying the mortgage on your home

Specific accommodation

  • Housing for the elderly like extra care housing

  • Private rented housing (finding accommodation, types of tenancies)

  • Council and housing association rented housing

  • Shared housing projects for single people

  • Affordable housing schemes

  • Paying for new accommodation

  • Need a rent deposit  

Money problems and housing

  • Rent arrears

  • Mortgage arrears

  • Service charges

  • Welfare benefits and other ways to increase your income

  • Housing benefit claims including fast-tracking of Housing Benefit

Landlords: problems with your tenants

  • Tenancy advice and information

  • Rent arrears

  • Damage to your property

  • Legal procedures for gaining possession

  • Access to your property for inspection and repairs

  • Tenancy deposit legislation - your obligations

We can give you advice about council and housing association rented accommodation but there is a severe shortage of these types of housing so we will also advise you on how to find private accommodation or about improving conditions in your present home.

Rent a room scheme

If you own your home you may wish to rent a spare room to a lodger to increase your income and help you pay your bills. Government rules allow you to earn up to £4,250 a year tax free from renting a furnished spare room.

If you rent from a housing association or private landlord you will need to ask for their permission before taking in a lodger to make sure that your tenancy agreement allows you to do it.

Supporting People Service

Supporting People services can provide support to a vulnerable person to make the place they live feel like home. This can involve helping someone to find somewhere to live or preventing someone from losing their home if there is a danger of that happening.

They provide housing-related support which can include support with setting up a home, developing life and domestic skills, managing finances and benefit claims, gaining access to other services and community organizations, establishing social contacts and activities, establishing safety and security, monitoring health and well being and support with a range of other housing-related matters that can help someone live independently.

In Hounslow there are over 80 different Supporting People services, provided by more than 28 different organizations and some have accommodation with the support they provide. If you think this may help you, call  020 8583 3842. Lines are open weekdays between 9am and 5pm, or come to the Civic Centre and ask to see an adviser, Monday to Friday, 9am - 4.15pm. The service is ‘first-come first-served’. You may have to wait to be seen at busy times.
