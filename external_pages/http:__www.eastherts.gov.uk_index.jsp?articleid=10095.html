Allotments in East Herts

An allotment is a plot of land on which you can grow fruit, vegetable, plants and flowers. Having an allotment is a rewarding pastime, with the benefits of regular exercise in the fresh air and the enjoyment of eating fresh fruit and vegetables you have grown.

East Herts Council manages three allotment sites in Hertford; West Street, Norwood Close and Windsor Drive.  The Council operates a waiting list for these sites, for more information please contact Environmental Services on 01279 655261 or email operations.admin@eastherts.gov.uk 

The cost of renting an allotment varies depending on it's size; the standard size of an allotment is 5poles or approximately 125sqm.  East Herts Council has rules for the allotments it manages and has guidance on erecting sheds and greenhouses on its allotments - click here for more information on  Allotment Rules [24kb] and  Allotment Sheds and Greenhouses [12kb] 

How do I get an allotment?

There are allotment sites in the main towns of East Herts which are managed by the Town Councils.  Many of the allotments sites have waiting lists, for more information on where the allotment sites are and how to get one please contact the relevant Town Council directly.

Bishop's Stortford

Bishop's Stortford Town Council has nine allotment sites.  Please contact them directly for more information: Bishop's Stortford - Town Council

Hertford

Hertford Town Council has six allotment sites.  Please contact them directly for more information: Hertford Town Council - Allotments

Sawbridgeworth

Sawbridgeworth Town Council has four allotment sites.  Please contact them directly for more information:  Sawbridgeworth Town Council

Ware

Ware Town Council has two allotment sites. Please contact them directly for more information: Ware Town Council

Buntingford

There are two allotment sites in Buntingford - please view  Buntingford Town Council - sports clubs and associations for contact details.
