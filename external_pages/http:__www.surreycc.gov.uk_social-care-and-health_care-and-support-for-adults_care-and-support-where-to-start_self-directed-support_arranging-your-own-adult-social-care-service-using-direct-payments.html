Who can have Direct Payments?

Do you have a Support or Care Plan? Are you eligible to receive social care support? If so, rather than the council arranging your services, you could arrange your services yourself, through Direct Payments. Direct Payment regulations changed in November 2009 to extend the scheme to a wider range of people. We can make a Direct Payment to most people who need support, including:

  • people who lack the capacity to give consent themselves
  • people with mental health illnesses who are subject to mental health and certain criminal justice legislation
  • people who have been assessed as needing community care or residential or nursing services*;
  • disabled people (including people with Mental Health needs) aged 16 and over, including those with short as well as longer term needs;
  • carers over 16 for 'carers services',
  • parents of disabled children, for children's services;

*Surrey is a trailblazer site and it is now possible to offer direct payments to residents of residential and nursing homes.

Once assessed as being eligible to receive council support and Direct Payment, a person must either:

  • be able to legally consent to receiving the payment from the council;
  • be over 16 years of age;
  • be able to manage the money to meet their needs. This maybe on their own or with someone's help, as long as the recipient is in control of how the money is spent.

or, if lacking mental capacity as defined by the 2005 Mental Capacity Act:

  • be over 18 years of age;
  • have someone who will at all times act in the individual's best interest - this will be referred to as a "suitable person" and it may be someone who has Lasting Power of Attorney or may have been appointed a deputy for the individual by the Court of Protection, certain close relatives or friends involved in the provision of care
  • the "suitable person" will manage the money to meet the individual's needs. This maybe on their own or with someone's help.

Who cannot have Direct Payments?

A small number of people may not be eligible to receive a Direct Payment. For example, people who are subject to some mental health or criminal justice orders.

How Direct Payments can help you?

Direct Payments can give you more flexibility and choice, by making it possible for support to be tailored around your individual needs.

Direct Payments allows you to be a part of the decision making about how your support needs will be met, by whom and at what time. You will be given the opportunity to employ your own support staff, which means they will be accountable to you rather than Surrey County Council.

Why would you want Direct Payments?

Direct Payments allow someone to make more of the decisions that affect their life. They give more flexibility and choice, by enabling support to be more tailored around individual needs.

If a Direct Payment is decided upon as the best way to deliver identified needs, there is more individual decision making about how those support needs will be met, by whom and at what time. The individual is at the centre, and has more control, of the support planning. This gives the opportunity to employ someone's own support staff, which means they will be more accountable to the individual. Or, there could be a contract with a local agency, in which case the agency will be answerable to the individual and not to Surrey County Council.

What to do if you are interested in Direct Payments?

If you are interested in Direct Payments and are not currently receiving social care support from care support from us, please call us on 0300 200 1005 for more information.

If you are interested in Direct Payments and are currently receiving social care support from us, please contact your care coordinator, social care practitioner or social worker for more information.

Alternatively, you can get more information from Surrey Independent Living Council (SILC):

Tel:  01483 458111

Fax: 01483 459976

Web:  www.surreyilc.org.uk

Please also see Direct Payments - useful links for further help and advice.

What responsibilities will you have when you receive Direct Payments?

If you receive a Direct Payment you will be responsible for managing and accounting for how you spend the money. The money received as a Direct Payment is given only to pay for support or services to meet assessed social care needs. For this reason, records will need to be kept which will help to ensure that the Direct Payment is working well.

An agreement needs to be signed between Surrey County Council and the person receiving the money. The agreement outlines the conditions that need to be followed. For example, records must be kept on how the money is spent, money can only be spent to achieve the agreed social care outcomes, and so on.

Will you have to make a financial contribution towards your Direct Payments?

You may be asked to contribute towards your social care support. Any charge that you may have to pay will be based on your personal financial circumstances. Carers are not charged for services.

For information about future changes to social care services, please see Self Directed Support.
