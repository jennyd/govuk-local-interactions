We provide bins for the disposal of litter and dog mess in public places which are emptied on a regular basis. Town and Parish Councils also provide bins they are responsible for.

The Environmental Protection Act of 1990 states that if a person drops, throws, deposits, or leaves anything that causes defacement in a public place, they are committing a littering offence. So always try to use a litter bin or take it home.

Dog Fouling

Incidents of dog fouling not cleared up afterwards can be reported. If possible, take a note of the date, time, location and details of the dog owner (if known).

It is an offence not to clear up after your dog when it has fouled an area to which the public have access. We are unable to get involved with incidents on private property and we can only offer advice and suggest you seek legal representation if the problem persists.

If someone is allowing a dog to foul outside your house we may be able to help, as long as it is not the frontage of your property.

  • Report dog fouling

Dog Bins

The dog bins are for the disposal of dog faeces from parks or on the street. If no bin is readily available it should be taken home and disposed of in a normal and hygienic manner. Should one of our bins be full, you can report it to us for emptying.

  • Report a fault with a Dog Bin

 

Litter Bins

Please do not use the litter bin to dispose of your household waste directly from your property. It can cause the litter bin to overfill very quickly which can be detrimental to the surrounding area. If one of our bins is full, you can report it to us for emptying.

  • Report a fault with a Litter Bin
