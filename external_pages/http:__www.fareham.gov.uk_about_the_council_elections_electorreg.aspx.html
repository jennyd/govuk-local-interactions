Register of Electors

You can register online at www.gov.uk/register-to-vote 

Edited/Open Register

One of the important changes as part of Individual Electoral Registration (IER) to note is that the Edited Register, which is available for purchase by third parties, is now called the Open Register.

Some residents have found the wording on the confirmation letters confusing. The explanation about the difference between the open and electoral registers is set out by law.

Being on the Register

We are not allowed to assume that people do not want to be on the Open Register and we can commit them only if they specifically ask us to do so.

Anyone who chose not to appear on the Edited/Open Register when it was published in February 2014, will not appear on the Open Register. The law still requires us to maintain and sell an Open Register.

If you received a letter confirming you are registered under the new system you do not need to do anything. This letter will tell you whether your details are on the Open Register or not. If you want to change your opt out arrangements, please email elections@fareham.gov.uk.

 

What is the Register of Electors

The Register of Electors is a list of all those residents in the Borough who are eligible to vote in various elections. It also contains a list of overseas voters whose last registration to vote was at an address within Fareham Borough, Service personnel who have registered under a service declaration and can include the names of homeless persons if they can claim some local connection. If you are not on the Register of Electors, you cannot vote.

More and more the Register of Electors is being used to confirm an individual's address, especially if they are applying for credit such as a mortgage, personal loan or even a mobile telephone. If you are not registered you will probably be refused credit as well as losing your right to have your say via the ballot box.

Between the summer of 2013 and December 2015 the UK electoral system will undergo substantial change as we move from a system of household registration to individual elector registration. The information on this web site will change a number of times during that period. Check back for updates.

New registration arrangements and verification of applicants' identities

From 10 June a new way to register was introduced which is called "Individual Electoral Registration".  You can now register online at www.gov.uk/register-to-vote  but you need a few more details to register, including your date of birth and national insurance number.  This will ensure the electoral register is more secure and less likely to be used for fraudulent activity.  If you don't have access to the internet, you can ring the elections office on 01329 824588 and we will help you to register.  You can also register using the internet in the Civic Offices.

If you are already registered to vote at your address, you will receive a letter between 1 – 10 August 2014.  This will tell you whether you need to take action to join the new register.  Most people who are currently registered will be added to the new register automatically and you don't need to do anything.  Around 15% of electors will not be added automatically and the letter will tell you what to do to re-register.   It is important that you respond to the letter if it asks you to take action as you won't be able to vote if you are not registered.

Registering to vote is free and you should not be charged for this service.  Please visit www.gov.uk/register-to-vote  to submit your application for registration.

The register is held in two formats – the electoral register and the open register. The electoral register contains the details of all electors and is the version used in elections and also for preventing and detecting crime and for checking applications for credit. The open register is an extract of the electoral register and contains the names and addresses of persons who have made no objection to their names being sold on to commercial and other organisations.

The government is introducing a new system for registering to vote called Individual Electoral Registration or IER.
 

Frequently Asked Questions you may have regarding IER

Why are changes being made to the current electoral registration process?

The process of getting onto the electoral register is changing from a property-based system (where one person in the household completes the form on behalf of everyone living in the property) to one where every individual must register themselves.  This change is being introduced nationally by the Government to help deter elector fraud and so that people are able to take individual responsibility for their own vote.  The aim is to improve the integrity of the system and engagement with each potential voter.

How will IER differ to the current way of registering to vote?

With the new individual registration form replacing the out-dated household application form people will have more control over registration and be able to take ownership of the process. IER will bring electoral registration into the 21st century with the introduction of online registration for everyone who is eligible.

Will registering under IER be complicated?

No, registering to vote under the new system will be straightforward. With the additional option of online registration, the process will be greatly improved.

When is IER being introduced?

The transition to IER will take place from 10 June.  Residents will receive a letter between 1 – 10 August 2014.  If you do not receive a letter during this time, please let us know by contacting the elections team on 01329 824588 or elections@fareham.gov.uk

What will happen if I am already registered?

The Council is now required by law to cross-check its electoral data with records held at the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP).  Where there is a perfect match we will write to advise you that you are simply being transferred across to the new individual register and you need do nothing further.  However, if there is not a match with DWP records, we will write to you to advise you of this but also include an 'invitation to register' form, which you will need to complete and return.  We anticipate around 15% of electors will need to complete such forms.

If that's the case, what information will I need to provide on the invitation?

Each person who does not have a match with DWP records will be asked to give identifying information including full name, national insurance number and date of birth. This will allow each person's application to be verified before they are added to the register. If you cannot provide a national insurance number, there is an exceptions process that can be followed.

What happens after 2014?

From 2015 onwards, we will conduct a full canvass in a similar way to the current arrangements; that is, we will canvass all properties in the Borough each year.  However, this will be a much larger form called a Household Enquiry Form.  If the information we get back on a form does not match our records we will send out an invitation to register form to each of the affected electors in that property.  As before, when completing the invitation, the new legislation requires individuals to provide their national insurance number and date of birth, so that this can again be checked against DWP records.

Where can I find more information on IER?

Please visit www.gov.uk/yourvotematters 

 

For further help and advice please contact Election Services at Civic Offices, Civic Way, Fareham, PO16 7AZ or telephone 01329 236100 or e-mail elections@fareham.gov.uk.
