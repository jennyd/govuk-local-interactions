Investing in Stockport Consultations

Information, Advice and Guidance Consultation

The Council has to reduce the amount it spends on Information Advice and Guidance (IAG) by £250K in 2016/17 and a further £250K in 2017/18.

Our proposal aims to limit the impact of the reduction on the most vulnerable households.

The Council is proposing to introduce a Core Offer which sets out the different ways in which Information Advice and Guidance will be delivered.

If you wish to make any comments in relation to this policy, please complete the online survey by 22nd February 2016.

For more information, visit the Information, Advice and Guidance Consultation page.

Current Council Consultations

Parent/carer survey for young people with Autism and Asperger's

As parents and carers, we all want our children to grow up and live fulfilling lives. We support our children the best way we can to reach their potential and take those first steps into adulthood.

A young person with additional needs is likely to need more support than others. For young people with Autism and Asperger’s, the world can be a confusing place.  This means that when they are old enough to want to live independent lives, they will need support to tackle some of life’s pressures.

Stockport Council are currently exploring how young people and their families can be best supported as they move from childhood into adulthood, and so that they can prepare for independence wherever possible.

We are looking for feedback from 2 groups of parent/carers:
•    For those with young people aged 14-17, we would like to know what would best help you to support your child as they prepare for adulthood.

•    For those who are still supporting young adults aged 18-25, what have your experiences of services been like and what could have made that journey better for you?

This is really important as it will help to shape and improve services.  The aim is to make sure that less young adults find themselves in crisis once they have made that leap into independence.

This survey has been developed in partnership with parent/carer groups in Stockport.

Please base your answers on your thoughts and your family’s experience.  All findings will be presented so that no one can be personally identified from what they say. Everything you say will remain anonymous and will be treated confidentially.

Young people will be consulted separately.

This survey should take around 5-10 minutes to complete.

School Admission Arrangements for 2017/18

The Local Authority are proposing changes to admission arrangements affecting maintained and academy schools in the Borough, in addition the LA are proposing school term and holiday dates for 2017/18.  For more information visit our consultation web page.

Improvements to cemeteries and churchyards

The Friends of Stockport Cemeteries (FOSC) and the Council have been working together since 2004 to establish improvements in all cemeteries and closed churchyards in Stockport.

Please help us to identify further improvements by completing our Cemeteries and Closed Churchyards online questionnaire.
