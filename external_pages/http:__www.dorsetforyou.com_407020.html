Adoption process

Listen

Information about the route you will take if you decide to adopt a child in Dorset.

The process for adopting a child in the UK is prescribed by law.  For full information about this process, please read our Adoption Information Pack (pdf, 1Mb) (opens in a new window).

Register your enquiry and discuss adoption further

Dorset County Council currently does not have waiting lists for prospective adopters wishing to be assessed, which means that the entire process from enquiry to approval usually takes under a year. Having said that, adopting a child is a major commitment and we understand that prospective adopters need to go at their own pace, to make sure that adoption is right for them.

The process is as follows:

  • Initial enquiry to the Adoption and Kinship team, a detailed information pack will then be sent to you

  • You will then be asked to complete and return a Request for Further Information form which will be enclosed within your pack

  • You will receive an invitation to an information evening

  • There will be a home visit by an Adoption and Kinship social worker

  • If you meet the criteria you will be able to make an adoption application

  • You will then be invited to attend a preparation course

  • Criminal Records Bureau checks and medicals and personal references will be taken up

  • Completion of the preparation course

  • Assessment interviews will be carried out

  • The assessment report will then be shared with you and presented to the Adoption Panel

  • The agency will then make a decision based on the Panel's recommendation

  • If all goes well you will then be approved as prospective adopters and will await a suitable 'match' with a child or children to be placed with you

You can also see our Adoption - Draft Statement of Purpose 2015 (pdf, 72kb) (opens in a new window).
