Grants Access Point (GAP)

Voluntary and community groups must register with the Grants Access Point (GAP) every three years.

Once registered, you can apply to funding schemes from any council department.

  • Grant Access Point (GAP) Information

We can:

  • help you develop the information you need to register.
  • advise you about funding opportunities that are appropriate to your group's needs.

Contact the Voluntary and Community Sector (VCS) Link Team:

    01484 414824
    funding@kirklees.gov.uk

Community funding and grant schemes

  • Kirklees 4 Funding

    Kirklees 4 Funding is a free to use funding information website which gives charities, community groups, social enterprises, and businesses access to a comprehensive database of funding opportunities including government, lottery, EU, trusts and other available funding in Kirklees.

  • Community Grants

    Grants of up to £300, are awarded to groups or individuals. To be eligible your project has to show a clear benefit for people living on and around KNH estates and the communities that KNH manage.

  • Grant Aid Policy for Investment in Voluntary and Community Organisations

    Information on how the Council will give out grants to the Voluntary and Community Sector (VCS), who to and for what purpose.

Care and support

  • Grants for childcare providers

    Grants to support start up, quality and sustainability of childcare settings.

  • Funding for health and social care projects

    Grants and contracts from £500 to £50,000 per year for health and social care projects in Kirklees.

Arts and activities

  • Arts in the Neighbourhood

    A small grant to help community and voluntary groups set up arts and cultural projects and events by contributing towards the costs.

  • Young people's activities funding opportunities

    Funding from the Young People's Service to run activities for 8-19 year olds in the school holidays.

Education

  • Dewsbury Endowed Schools Foundation Trust

    An Education Trust which was set up in to promote the education, including social and physical training, of persons between the ages of 11 and 25.

  • Huddersfield Education Trust

    The Huddersfield Education Trust is a charity which promotes the education of young people under 23 years of age.

  • Environment Grants Scheme
