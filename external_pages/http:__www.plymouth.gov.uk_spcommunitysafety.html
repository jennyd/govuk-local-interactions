Community safety

Safer Plymouth Partnership (The statutory Community Safety Partnership) operates within a statutory framework with responsible authorities expected to work with other local agencies and organisations to develop and implement strategies to tackle crime, disorder, misuse of drugs and other substances, anti-social behaviour in Plymouth.

Safer Plymouth Partnership is responsible for overseeing the delivery of Safer Plymouth Partnership Plan and priorities arising from annual Partnership Strategic Assessments (local and Peninsula) to ensure effective action and partnership collaboration to address crime and community safety issues in Plymouth.

Members that make up Safer Plymouth Partnership are representatives of the Plymouth City Council, Devon and Cornwall Police, NHS Clinical Commissioning Group, Devon and Somerset Fire & Rescue, Devon and Cornwall Probation Trust and The Police and Crime Commissioner.

Safer Plymouth Partnership has recently reviewed its representation and broadened this to include community and voluntary sector representation and has co-opted Members from the following organisations:

  • Plymouth University
  • Plymouth University Students Union
  • Zebra Collective
  • Neighbourhood Watch
  • Victim Support
  • Two 'open seats' for where Safer Plymouth Partnership identifies additional value from attendance of representative(s) with skills, knowledge or experience of a particular topic it can invite them to attend meetings for specific items or time-limited periods

The role of the co-optees on Safer Plymouth Partnership is to be a community 'voice' and ensure a wider community perspective is taken account of in our work. They should feel empowered to make suggestions for how communities can be more involved and assist Safer Plymouth Partnership with its work. They will be required to promote our work and provide meaningful feedback to communities about what Safer Plymouth is doing to tackle crime and disorder.

Some of the key requirements of Safer Plymouth Partnership include:

  • Ensuring compliance with legislation requiring Community Safety Partnerships to meet (as a minimum) and work to the principles of Meeting National Standards (Hallmarks) of:
    • Empowered and effective leadership
    • Intelligence led business processes
    • Effective and responsive delivery of structures
    • Engaged communities
    • Visible and constructive accountability
  • Ensuring effective sharing of information
  • Development, publication and delivery of a three year partnership plan, revised annually
  • Ensuring annual strategic assessments are undertaken to assist in producing or revising the partnership plan
  • Ensuring communities are consulted about crime and disorder issues
  • Ensuring the implementation of a strategy to reduce reoffending
  • Ensuring cooperation with the Police and Crime Commissioner (P&CC) to tackle crime and disorder, having regard for the objectives in the Police and Crime Plan, responding to any request from the P&CC for a report
