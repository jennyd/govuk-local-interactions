Day Care Services (Milton Keynes Council)

Milton Keynes Council have three Day Care Centres offering older people with dementia the opportunity to continue to be a part of the community, whilst providing them with a safe and stimulating environment in a day care setting. Each day at the centres is different, the staff will offer activities acccording to the interests and needs of  the people attending. The centres also provide respite for carers, assisting them to care for relatives and friends, for as long as they are able and wish to do so.

Assistance with personal care is available, according to assessed needs. Appointments with outside agencies such as District Nurses, Social Care Services and Advocacy can also be held on site. Drinks and snacks are available during the day and a hot meal can be provided (Meals are subject to an additional charge).

Transport can be provided to and from the centre in a specially adapted minibus which has a tail lift to aid those in wheelchairs or those who are physically frail and who are unable to manage steps safely. The driver always has an escort and both have received first aid training. There is a mobile phone on the minibus to aid communication throughout the journey and the number for this will be provided to clients and their carers.

To attend one of these centres you will need to  be assessed by Adult Social Care, see Help from Adult Social Care 

There is a charge for these services; however  a financial assessment will be carried out to identify if you will have to make a contribution for these services, for more information  see Adult Social Care Funded Support
