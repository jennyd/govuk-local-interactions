Nuisance

What is a Nuisance?

Nuisance is defined as "an unlawful interference with a person's use or enjoyment of land or of some right over, or in connection with it".

This interference could be caused by a range of things, from barking dogs to security lights, noisy parties to bonfires, DIY to dirty houses. A guide to statutory nuisance (PDF, 213 Kb) explains nuisance further. 

Nuisance takes into account the frequency of occurrence, volume or intensity, timing, locality and duration of an event as well as the motive and sensitivity of the complainant.  

How Can I Complain About a Nuisance?

We would always recommend that you talk to the person causing the problem first to try to resolve the situation amicably. Remember, whatever happens, you will still need to live next door to each other.

If you have tried to resolve this matter informally and been unsuccessful, you can report the matter to us and one of our Officers will take such steps as are necessary to investigate the situation further.

Where we are satisfied that a nuisance exists we will take measures to ensure that the nuisance is stopped.    

You can phone us on 01539 733333, or report problems online using our forms: 

Noise Complaint

Smell Complaint

Smoke Complaint 

If you are a tenant or you are aware that the property where the nuisance is coming from is owned by a landlord then you should also contact that landlord and explain the problem you are experiencing. Both private and social landlords can evict tenants for anti-social behaviour, such as causing nuisance. 

We can't deal with noise from low flying jets: contact the RAF Liaison Officer at Penrith (01768 891391).  General aircraft noise is dealt with by the Civil Aviation Authority.

Nuisance and Planning 

The Environmental Protection Group is regularly consulted on any planning applications which may have the potential to cause nuisance.

If you have concerns regarding nuisance and proposed development then you should contact and report the matter to the relevant Planning Authority.

If you are a developer and need to submit environmental information showing that your development will not cause nuisance as part of your application, we would recommended that you speak to us prior to submitting a planning application in order to ensure that the correct information is submitted with your application.  

We have a leaflet giving advice on the information required to be submitted with an application for a ventilation system.

Nuisance and Licensing

Similarly, the Group is consulted on Licensing applications for pubs and clubs, outdoor events and other public entertainment.  Conditions will be requested, if required, in order to prevent noise nuisance being caused by the event or operation of the premises.
