Registering to vote

Registering to vote has never been as easy

View pages in this section

If you are a British, Commonwealth or EU citizen aged 17 and over you can now go online to register to vote.

Register to vote at Gov.UK

In order to vote in the May 2016 Mayoral elections, you will need to apply to register as an elector before Monday 18 April 2016.

Verifying your identity

When you apply you will need your National Insurance number to hand if you have one. We will then verify your identity using that number, your date of birth and your name. If you have not been given a national insurance number or don't have one for some other reason, just state that on the application and we will write to you to explain how you can prove your identity using other documents.

Individual electoral registration

Electoral registration applications are made individually. Each resident must complete their own application and agree to the statement of truth during the application.

If a person has a disability, they may be assisted to complete the application as long as they have the mental capacity to understand and agree to the statement of truth in the application.

What happens next?

If you give your email address in the application, you will receive an email acknowledgment from the government online form. The Royal Borough of Greenwich will:

  • receive your application
  • send a letter to you to acknowledge receipt or to tell you we have added you to the register.

This can take a few weeks due to legal deadlines. We may request more information or evidence of your identity. We will not be able to continue with your application until we have received an adequate reply.
