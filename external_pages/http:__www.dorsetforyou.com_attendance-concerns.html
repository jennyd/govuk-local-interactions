Support for school attendance concerns

Listen

Legal responsibility for getting children to school lies with parents and carers however in cases of persistent non-attendance the responsibility for parental prosecution remains with the Local Authority.

With the realigning of services into locality based Early Intervention teams, non-attendance at school will be part of a wider safeguarding and well being of children agenda. In the light of these changes the role of the Education Social Worker has been reviewed.

The result of this is that from 1 April 2012:

  • Education Social Workers have become Locality Social Workers and will work with children, young people and families on a range of issues including attendance

  • The responsibility for auditing registers lies with individual schools who will need to ensure that absences are recorded correctly and appropriate action with regard to attendance is undertaken
  • Fast Track (pdf, 38kb) (opens in a new window) has been used successfully by a number of schools to address attendance issues

How does a school get support?

  • Initially a school should undertake appropriate action such at contacting parents, undertaking face to face meetings and consideration of the underlying issues. It may be that the Common Assessment Framework (CAF) procedure is appropriate if a multi agency response is required. Full guidance on school attendance - good practice and procedures (pdf, 141kb) (opens in a new window) is available. It is important to complete a child missing education referral form (word, 266kb) (opens in a new window) and send it to the included address.

  • A request for involvement through a pre-assessment CAF checklist (pdf, 139kb) (opens in a new window) is the correct way to access support from the Locality Team and this should be sent to their generic email address

  • Concerns may be raised at a Team Around a School meeting

Schools may find the following resources useful:

  • Pupils may respond to attendance certificate a (pdf, 39kb) (opens in a new window) or attendance certificate b (pdf, 163kb) (opens in a new window)
  • A class could work together to achieve recognition as  Attendance Champions (pdf, 66kb) (opens in a new window)
  • Traffic Lights (pdf, 83kb) (opens in a new window) , Postcard (pdf, 48kb) (opens in a new window)  and Effects of Absence (pdf, 16kb) (opens in a new window) documents provide information to parents and carers
  • A sample latecomers letter (word, 22kb) (opens in a new window) is provided to advise parents and carers

Cross Border Issues

These are yet to be finalised and we are endeavouring to agree protocols where home and school geography differ. Dorset continues to be responsible for prosecution of parents where there are issues of non attendance of a Dorset school pupil.

Support for Children and Young People with Medical Needs

There is specific guidance available for professionals Ensuring a good education for children who cannot attend schools because of health needs [113kb]  in Dorset. There are plans to provide updated Government guidance early in 2013.
