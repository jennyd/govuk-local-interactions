Dog fouling

Report dog fouling

Report it online, by calling the Council's Customer Contact Centre on 0191 427 7000 or by text to 07786 200 802.

Report dog fouling

Dog fouling is a big concern, not just because of the mess it causes, but because it can also be a health risk.

What are we doing?

The Council is launching measures to take action against irresponsible dog owners and to raise awareness around the issue of dog fouling.

To take action against irresponsible dog owners and to raise awareness of dog fouling, the Council have:

  • Installed new bins - but, did you know you can use any public bin to dispose of your poop-scoop bag?
  • Trained more people on the street - to issue on the spot fines, talk to people and hand out poop-scoop bags
  • Arranged dog awareness events - with the Dogs Trust, including, free microchipping and basic health checks
  • Put up'we're watching you' posters - to let people know dog fouling will not be tolerated

Did you know...

  • Around 100 cases of toxocariasis are diagnosed each year in the UK
  • Local authorities in the UK receive approximately 226 000 complaints about dog fouling every year
  • The cost to British taxpayers of clearing up after dogs is around £2.3 million per year
  • It is an offence for anyone in charge of a dog on public land to fail to remove dog faeces deposited by that dog

Report it

If you see someone who doesn't clean up after their dog, Report It.

What are the health risks of dog fouling?

Dog fouling is a serious health risk and we need to act on it.

Win vouchers with Pets at Home

South Tyneside Council and South Tyneside Homes will be rewarding people for picking up after their dogs.
