Clinical, Asbestos and hazardous waste

Clinical waste, such as blood and syringes, may be dangerous to any person coming into contact with it unless it is made safe. This means that it must not be collected with other waste as it may be hazardous or infectious. Find out how to dispose of Clinical Waste, as well as Asbestos and other hazardous items.

Clinical Waste

Clinical Waste is classed as materials that pose a threat to humans including human or animal tissue, blood or other bodily fluids, excretions, drugs or other pharmaceutical products, swaps or dressings, syringes, needles or other sharp instruments.

The council is able to collect Clinical Waste for a fee, depending on the type of clinical waste, amount and number of collections. Each companies requirements will need to be looked at individually.

Asbestos and other hazardous waste

Hazardous waste includes common household products that could be harmful to human health or to the environment, such as bleach, paint stripper, pesticides, engine oil, electrical items and gas bottles. Some types of hazardous waste can be disposed of at the Household waste recycling centres. For more information please see Cambridgeshire County Council's information page on Hazardous waste.

Cement Bonded Asbestos Disposal Service

In partnership with Cambridgeshire County Council, a drop off disposal service for cement bonded asbestos from households is available by appointment. You can dispose of the following types of household cement bonded asbestos using the free service:

  • Asbestos cement wall and ceiling cladding
  • Asbestos down pipes and gutters
  • Asbestos cement flues
  • Asbestos promenade tiles (used in walkways
  • Asbestos cement roofs

To order special Asbestos Disposal sacks, please call our contact centre on 01354 654321. You can pick the sacks up from our offices on Melbourne Avenue. This is by appointment only, which is between 2-4pm every Tuesday and Thursday. We can dispose of your filled Asbestos sacks (and any unused ones you may have) within 28 days of you initially picking up from us. For more information please read Asbestos disposal for householders.

Please note: no other asbestos containing materials can be disposed of using this free service.

For more detailed information on asbestos including where it is likely to be found and how to deal with it, you can also read this leaflet published by Department of the Environment: DETR leaflet- asbestos in the home.
