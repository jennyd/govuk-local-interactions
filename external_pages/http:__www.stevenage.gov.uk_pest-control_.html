Pest control

The service is provided for pests considered to be a risk to public health or safety including rats, house mice, cockroaches and bedbugs.

What type of pests does pest control treat?

Our priority is to treat pests which may carry or transmit diseases i.e rats, mice, bedbugs and cockroaches. If we have the capacity, we may also carry out treatments for other pests such as wasps, fleas, squirrels and ants.

Does the Council carry out treatments to destroy / remove bees?

Bees are beneficial to the environment and the Council does not, therefore, normally treat bees’ nests. The attached information sheet gives advice on what to do if you are troubled by bees or see a swarm of bees. 

How long will I have to wait for a pest control appointment?

Pest control visits are allocated on a priority basis. We will try to visit within 2 working days in the case of rats, mice, bedbugs and cockroaches. In other cases we will try to visit within 4 working days but it may be longer at peak times.

How much does the pest control service cost?

There are charges for our pest control service, see ‘fees and charges’ or alternatively contact us for more information.

  • Fees and Charges

Who can use the service?

Anyone who lives in Stevenage is entitled to use the Council’s Pest Control Service.

What will we do?

If we arrange an appointment, we will specify whether the visit will be in the morning or afternoon, whichever is most convenient for you.

Before any treatment, we will give you safety information, in writing, about the proposed treatment and will answer any questions you may have.

Our trained and experienced pest control officers will eradicate infestations of pests which are considered to be a risk to public health.

We will use pesticides which are considered by experts to be the least harmful available to both humans and the environment.

We will, where practical, visit as often as necessary to eliminate the infestation, within the charge quoted.

Help us to help you

Check your garden regularly for signs of vermin, especially near sheds, compost heaps and overgrown areas. If you have cats or dogs they should be treated for fleas using preparations available from pet shops or vets.

When you make an appointment

Check when you will be available over the next few days before you ring us or use the online form

Make sure you are at home for the appointment, otherwise we may have to impose an extra charge

Give us your full address and give us clear directions about how to find you

If possible, describe the type of pest which you have and the extent of the infestation

Tell us if you have tried any other treatments – this is important.
Before our pest control officer arrives

If you are eligible for the reduced charge because you are claiming benefit, please have proof that you are receiving that benefit available for the office to examine.
