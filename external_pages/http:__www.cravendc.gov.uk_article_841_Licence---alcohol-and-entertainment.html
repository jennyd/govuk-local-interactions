Alcohol and Entertainment Licence

Premise Licences, Personal Licences and Temporary Events

  • Personal Licence

    A Personal Licence authorises the holder to supply alcohol or authorises the supply of alcohol in accordance with a Premises Licence.

  • Licensing Policy

    Statement of Licensing Policy

  • Premises Licence

    A premises licence authorises the premises in question to carry out licensable activities. Almost any business that carries on one or more licensable activities will need a premises licence, including takeaways and late night cafes.

  • Temporary Event Notice

    A Temporary Event Notice (TEN) is a notification to the licensing authority that an individual intends to carry on licensable activities.

  • Latest Licensing News

  • Licensing Act 2003 Public Register

    You can view Licensing Act 2003 Public Registers by downloading the documents provided below.

  • Alcohol consumption in designated public places orders

    Designated Public Place Orders (DPPOs) give police officers discretionary powers to require a person to stop drinking and confiscate alcohol or containers of alcohol in public places. DPPOs are implemented by local councils in order to address alcohol-related crime and disorder in public places.

  • Licensing Act 2003 fees

    The Licensing Act 2003 ("The Act") provides for fees to be payable to the licensing authority in respect of the discharge of their functions.
