  • Rough Sleepers

    We do not want to see anyone sleeping on the streets in Kirklees. Our No Second Night Out Service works to get people off the streets, for anyone we verify is sleeping rough we will initially offer temporary accommodation. They will then be supported to move to more appropriate accommodation with support if necessary. If you’re concerned about someone sleeping rough, you can report it to the Housing Solutions Service on 01484 221350 or via Street Link. We will ensure they are connected our No Second Night Out Service.

What it means to be homeless

You are homeless if you have no accommodation available in the UK or elsewhere to occupy, such as

  • a property you own.
  • a property you are a tenant of.
  • living with family or friends.
  • a property you have the right to occupy by virtue of a court order.

You are considered homeless if you have accommodation but

  • cannot secure entry to it.
  • it is a moveable structure designed to live in (caravan, mobile home) and there is nowhere to securely locate it.
  • you are living in a situation which is obviously unreasonable for you to continue to endure.

You are by law threatened with homelessness if

  • it is likely that you will become homeless within 28 days.

You have a priority need if

  • you have a dependent child living with you under 16 or under 19 if in full time education.
  • you or a person who normally lives with you is pregnant.
  • you or any person living with you is vulnerable because of old age, mental illness, handicap or physical disability or another special reason.
  • you become homeless due to a flood, fire or another disaster or emergency.
  • you are 16 or 17 years old and not in the care of social services.
  • you are under 21 years old and have been in some kind of care or institution that has resulted in you being less able to fend for yourself.

Making a Homeless Application

How do I make a Homelessness Application?

You can call into Kirklees Housing Solutions offices in either Dewsbury or Huddersfield and speak to us in person. You can also contact Housing Solutions on 01484 221350 to make an appointment. This will enable us to ensure that we give you the best possible advice having listened to your individual circumstances. You may also contact us via letter or email. If you are homeless in an emergency and it is outside of our opening hours, we operate an emergency service which you can access by telephoning 01484 414933.

How long will it take to get a decision?

We aim to make all decisions as quickly as possible. Government guidelines suggest 33 working days for a final decision to be reached, but we are often able to make a decision on your application much more quickly than this.

What if you have no duty to me?

The Local Authority has a legal duty to provide anyone in the district with housing advice and no matter what the result of your formal homelessness application we will ensure that you are provided with high-quality, free housing advice and assistance.

What if I disagree with the decision?

If you disagree with a decision we have made on your homelessness application you are entitled to request a review. The review is carried out by an independent officer who is senior to the person who made the decision.

What if I am not in Kirklees?

If you are from out of the area, we recommend that in the first instance you approach your local council’s housing service who will be able to advise you.
