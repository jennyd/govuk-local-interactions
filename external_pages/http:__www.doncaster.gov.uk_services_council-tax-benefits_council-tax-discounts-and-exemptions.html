Council Tax discounts

Usually, the full Council Tax charge assumes two adults (aged 18 and over) are living in your property but in some circumstances you may be entitled to a reduction.

The following is a list of circumstances where you might be entitled to a reduction.

Single occupier discount

If you are the only adult resident living in the property, you may be entitled to a 25% reduction of your Council Tax.

Reductions for people with disabilities

A reduction in Council Tax can be allowed for a property which is the home of a disabled person. There is no age limit and the disabled person does not need to be the taxpayer, but must suffer from a 'permanent and substantial' disability.

One of the following conditions must apply:
  • there is a room, other than a bathroom or kitchen, used mainly to meet the needs of the disabled person
  • there is a second bathroom or kitchen which is required to meet the needs of the disabled person
  • the disabled person needs to use a wheelchair in the property, and there is enough floor space to allow this

If we give you a reduction, we will charge your council tax at one band lower than your actual valuation band. If you are already in band A, you will get a reduction equal to 1/9th of the band D charge for your area.

Military & diplomatic reasons

You may also be entitled to a discount if you are:

  • a member (or dependant) of international headquarters and defence organisations
  • a member (or dependant) of a visiting force
  • a foreign diplomat

Please contact us below if you think this applies to you.

Local discounts

Circumstance

Details

Empty properties, second homes and properties that are uninhabitable

No discount is allowed for these property types and full Council Tax is charged from day one.

Empty property premium

If your property has been empty and unoccupied for two years, a 50 percent premium will be added to the usual Council Tax charge. This means the level of charge will be 150 percent after two years have passed.

Section 13a discretionary discounts

If you wish to request a discretionary reduction on the grounds of financial hardship, please apply in writing providing details of your income and expenditure. Discounts can only be awarded if hardship is proven and to grant a discount would be in the interests of other Council Tax payers.

If you have any questions about local discounts you can contact us.

How to apply

Should you wish to apply for a discount please print the appropriate form, complete and post it, together with any proof detailed on the form, to:
