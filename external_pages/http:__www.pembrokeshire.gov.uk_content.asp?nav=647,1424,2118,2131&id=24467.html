Choosing a Day Nursery

Day nurseries provide registered early education and childcare for young children from birth to the age of 5. Most are open from Monday to Friday; generally from 8am until 6pm and normally for 51 weeks of the year.

They offer a caring, safe, stimulating environment either as full day care or part time care for babies and pre-school children to fit around your family requirements

Some may also provide care before and after school and in the holidays for children aged 3 - 11 years.

All day nurseries must be registered and inspected by the Care and Social Services Inspectorate Wales (CSSIW).

There are different types of day nurseries with different ways of operating, so look around for one that suits you and the needs of your child.

Plus points: 

  • Opportunities for children to learn and play with friends
  • Usually geared to the needs of children with working parents
  • Trained, experienced staff
  • Stimulating environment for children to develop at their own pace

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know that my child will be safe and secure?

You know your child better than anyone; visit all the day nurseries in your area and choose the one you feel will be the most suitable for your child.

Day nurseries must be registered with the Care and Social Services Inspectorate Wales (CSSIW). This means that Criminal Record Bureau (CRB) checks are carried out to make sure that all employees are suitable to look after children. Staff must be qualified and be involved in ongoing training.

A check on the nursery premises is carried out to make sure the building is safe and suitable for children. After the nursery is registered, an annual inspection is carried out to make sure the nursery is continuing to provide a safe and suitable service.

There should be a set number of staff to work with the children. Staff ratios should increase the younger the children. Here are the guidelines day nurseries should adhere to:

1:3      0-2 years

1:4      2-3 years

1:8      3-5 years

You can ask to see the nursery's registration certificate and you may ask to see a copy of the latest CSSIW inspection report highlighting the quality of nursery education and care and the appropriateness of equipment and facilities.

You can download a copy of the last inspection report of any nursery here:

What age range are the children in a nursery?

Nurseries are for children aged 0 - 5 years. Nurseries should have separate rooms for babies and toddlers as well as for children aged 3 - 5 years. Children are usually divided into small groups based on their age.

When are nurseries open?

Most nurseries open at about 8am and close around 6pm. Most nurseries are open all year round except for public holidays and the week between Christmas and New Year.

What about part-time childcare?

Many nurseries are happy to offer you part-time places. Often these are organised into morning and afternoon sessions.

How much does a nursery cost?

Costs vary and you can expect to pay approximately £185.00 per child per week for a full time place. Some nurseries charge an hourly rate dependent on the age of the child, whilst others charge a full day or half day rate. How do I find a day nursery?

If you feel a nursery would suit not only you, but also your child's needs, you must now find the most suitable nursery to meet your requirements. Pembrokeshire Family Information Service (FIS) can help you by providing you with details / costs of nurseries in your area. Call 01437 770014 for up-to-date information or search online for childcare facilities in your area.

Questions you may wish to ask

  • May I see a copy of your registration certificate and last inspection report?
  • Are meals provided?
  • Can the nursery cater for special diets?
  • What will the charge be? What does this include e.g. nappies, meals, holidays etc.
  • What security systems do you have in place?
  • What will my child do all day?

Issues to consider

 Visit the nursery and consider the following:

  • Is there a safe outdoor play area?
  • Is the interior bright, attractive, clean, well decorated, warm and light?
  • Is the equipment good quality, clean, safe and appropriate?
  • Are the staff friendly and approachable?
  • Look at the children in the nursery: do they appear happy and well occupied?
  • What is the interaction like between staff and children?
