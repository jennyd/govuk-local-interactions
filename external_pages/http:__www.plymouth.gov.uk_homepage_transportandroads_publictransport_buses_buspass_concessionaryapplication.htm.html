Apply for a bus pass

You must apply in person at the 1st Stop shop, 71 New George Street or at one of the libraries listed below:

  • Central Library
  • Crownhill
  • Devonport
  • Efford
  • Ernesettle
  • Estover
  • Laira
  • North Prospect
  • Peverell
  • Plympton
  • Plymstock
  • St Budeaux
  • Southway
  • Stoke
  • Tothill
  • West Park

Sorry but we don't at the moment have an online application system.

Applications can be made at any time during normal 1st Stop and library opening hours.

You will be required to provide two items of authentication - proof of residency and proof of eligibility (one from section 1 and one from section 2 below).

Please note: You will not be required to provide a photograph because this will be taken at the time of application.

Once registered, your new pass will be produced and sent directly to your home address. You should expect to receive it within 10 days.

If you are of pensionable age you can apply for your pass up to 10 days before the date on which you become eligible (see document below for details).

Section 1

Residency

All applicants must prove that their sole or principal residence is in Plymouth. This can be one of the following items with the applicant's name and address printed on it:

  • Council tax bill
  • Utility bill
  • Driving licence
  • Rental agreement
  • Bank statement
  • NHS medical card
  • Letter from the Department of Work and Pensions

If you cannot provide any of the above a letter from your landlord confirming residency plus proof of the landlord's name on a council tax bill can be accepted.

Eligible persons who live in more than one residence in the UK should apply for a concessionary bus pass only through the council in whose area they live for most of the time.

Foreign nationals whose sole or principal residence is in Plymouth would be eligible. Persons whose sole or principal residence is outside England are not eligible, whether or not they hold a British passport.

In respect of boat dwellers a mooring licence for a site in Plymouth would be acceptable proof of residency.

Section 2

Eligibility - Age passes

The date of eligibility for a concessionary bus pass for both men and women is linked to the age at which a woman becomes eligible for her state pension. See the document below to check the date on which you will become eligible for your bus pass.

State Pension age timetables [276KB]

The same dates are applicable to both men and women.

All applicants must provide proof of their age in the form of one of the following original documents:

  • Birth certificate
  • Passport
  • Driving licence
  • Letter from the Department of Work and Pensions confirming state retirement pension
  • Retirement pension card
  • NHS medical card bearing name and date of birth
  • Printed prescription bearing name and date of birth

Eligibility - Disability passes

As specified in the Department for Transport guidance disabilities which qualify someone for a bus pass are those which are considered permament, or which have lasted at least 12 months and are ongoing, or which are likely to last 12 months. Such disbailities should have a substantial effect on a persons ability to carry out normal day to day activities.

All applicants must provide proof of their eligibility. See the document below to check which original documents will be accepted. Letters used to support an application must be no older than 12 months. Please note that we no longer accept letters from GPs as an acceptable form of evidence.

If you think you may be eligible for a bus pass, but are unable to provide a document from the list of approved documents, we may be able to offer you an independent medical assessment. Forms and further information can be obtained by calling the Public Transport team on 01752 668000.
