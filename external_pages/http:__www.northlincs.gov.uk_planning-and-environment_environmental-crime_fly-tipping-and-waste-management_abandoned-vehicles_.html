Abandoned Vehicles

An abandoned vehicle is not only blot on the landscape, it can be a danger to people and animals, and often attracts vandals and arsonists.

Abandoned vehicles are often:

  • left in the same location for long periods

  • neglected, in poor condition or vandalised

  • leaking toxic fluids, such as oil and coolant etc.

Report an abandoned vehicle

Once it's been reported what happens next?

We will investigate the vehicle within 24 hours of it being reported. Once we have determined that there is no local registered keeper, we may remove a vehicle and dispose of it via recycling.

Anyone found to have abandoned a vehicle may be issued with a £200 fixed penalty notice or prosecuted.

How can I pay a Fixed Penalty Notice?

To pay a Fixed Penalty Notice, please use our online form: 

Pay a fixed Penalty Notice

How do I report an untaxed vehicle?

Untaxed vehicles should be reported directly to the Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA) using their online form

Report an untaxed vehicle to the DVLA

Alternatively contact the DVLA directly   08000 325 202
