Starting Primary School

When should I apply?

The online application system will be open from 2 September 2015.

When is the closing date for applications?

The closing date is 15th January 2016. 

What happens if I send my application in after this date?

Applications submitted after the closing date will only be considered alongside those who applied on time, when they are received before the allocation procedures begin. However, as this date cannot be predicted, parents have no guarantee that any application received after the closing date will be included. Your child, therefore, may not be offered a place at one of your preferred school(s).

What if I want to change my preferences after the closing date?

Your application will be treated as late so you are advised to speak to the Admissions Team before you put anything in writing.

Which schools can I include on my application form?

You can name three schools in order of preference, these can be schools in or outside the St Helens borough.

What about transport?

Obtain details of St Helens school transport policy

Should I look at the oversubscription criteria for a school before I name it as a preference?

We strongly advise you to read the oversubscription criteria for your preferred school to enable you to decide what priority your child would have for a place and also check whether the school has been oversubscribed in previous years. You can view the oversubscription criteria used for St Helens primary schools in the Primary Information for Parents Booklet 2016-17.

Where can I get information about my preferred school?

You can attend the school’s open evening or visit the Directgov website, where if you search for your preferred school you should be able to find details on its performance, Ofsted information and a link to the school’s website.

Will my child be allocated a place at my preferred school?

This will depend upon the number of applications and the number of places available. If there are fewer applicants than there are places available in a school, all applicants will be offered a place.

What happens if there are more applications than available places at a school?

The admissions authority (either the LA or the governing body if it is a voluntary aided (faith) school) will apply its oversubscription criteria in order to decide which children should be offered a place.

When will I find out the result of my application?

You will be notified in writing of the result of your application on 18th April 2016.

What can I do if I do not get a place at my preferred school?

You have the right to appeal to an independent appeals Panel get more details about appealing.

Will my child’s name be kept on a waiting list?

If you are refused a place at a school because there were more applicants than available places, your child’s name will be kept on a waiting list. If a place becomes available, it will be offered to the applicant who is ranked highest on the list in accordance with the published oversubscription criteria.

Important

Priority is not given on the waiting list on the basis of the date upon which an application form was received. This means that a child can be moved down the list if a late application is received for another child who has higher priority to a place based on the oversubscription criteria for the school. We will keep waiting lists for all St Helens schools until 18th December 2016. If a parent still wishes to be considered for a place after the waiting list has ceased, it will be necessary for them to re-apply as a mid year transfer. Please check the waiting list arrangements for schools outside the borough with the local authority in which the school is situated.

Where can I get advice and help with completing my form?

If you need any help or advice on the application process you can contact the School Admissions Team. Or if you are struggling to complete your application form because you have difficulty understanding the information that would help you make a decision on which school would be best for your child you can contact the Choice Advice Service on 01744 673377.

Where can I find more information on the admission process?

The Primary Education Information for Parents booklet has been compiled by the Local Authority to help give parents a greater understanding of the admissions process you can download a copy here.
