About The Blue Bin Scheme

View the app advert

Tameside's new smartphone and iPad app

Download our free app to check your refuse collection dates, set up an alert to remind you which bins to put out for collection and report any missed bins.

Download our free bin app from Apple Store or Google Play Store

Find out your blue bin collection dates using the Bin Collection Dates Form

If you have not yet received a bin but would like one, please use this form to order a blue bin, or phone the customer contact centre on 0161 342 8355.

Assistant Executive Director Ian Saxon said "Our scheme is without doubt one of the best in the country and it is thanks to every householder who has taken and used a blue bin for recycling their waste paper that we are in this position".

We as a council, are committed to reducing the amount of waste we send to landfill sites and this is one initiative that shows we mean business.

"With council's becoming more responsible for the amount of waste they dispose of, recycling becomes an increasingly important aspect of waste management".

What can be put in your blue bin?

  • Newspapers
  • Cardboard
  • Cardboard cartons
  • Magazines
  • Junk mail
  • Blue telephone directories
  • Envelopes (except windowed envelopes)
  • Catalogues
  • Letters and general paper
  • Yellow pages

What cannot be put in your blue bin?

  • Plastic Wrappers
  • General Household Rubbish

The above items will contaminate the load and cannot be processed as part of our scheme.  Please use this simple guide and help us maintain the Tameside scheme as the best in the country.

If you know of any abandoned or unused blue bins, please contact us - other people can make use of them and help the borough recycle.
