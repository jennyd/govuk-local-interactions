Elective Home Education (EHE)

EDUCATING YOUR CHILD AT HOME

Access to education is a fundamental right for every child and the responsibility for a child’s education rests with their parents/carers. The law clearly states that parents/carers are responsible for ensuring that their child (or children) receives a full-time education suitable to his or her age, aptitude and ability and to any special educational needs (SEN) he or she may have, by regular attendance at a school or otherwise.

Most people do choose to send their children to school to be educated. However, you are legally entitled to educate your child at home. In England, education is compulsory, but school is not.

If you are considering elective home education please think carefully about your decision. EHE is a great responsibility which demands a lot of time, energy and commitment.

A decision to educate at home should not be made solely to resolve a particular problem in school. If you have a difficulty or disagreement with the school, please discuss the issue with the head teacher in the first instance, or write to the school governors.

It is important to plan for your child’s learning before making a final decision and also look at potential costs involved as there is no funding available for those who choose to educate their child at home.

It is also important to keep your options open as your child’s needs change at different ages and stages. The School Admissions Team is always happy to assist you with applying for a school place.

If you would like to talk to one of our Elective Home Education Consultants about your decision to home educate or you would like any further support and advice about any information on this site please use the following link to contact us.

If you decide to home educate and your child is registered at a Suffolk school you will need to send a letter to the head teacher informing them that you wish to educate your child at home (a sample deregistration letter can be found on the Education Otherwise website).  The school will pass on this information to the Elective Home Education office who will then contact you to ask for some basic information.

If your child has never attended school, you do not need to tell anyone, but we do ask you to send details to us, so we know that you are making arrangements for your child’s education.  Please also be aware that:

  • Where a child is registered at a school as a result of a school attendance order parents must ask the local authority to revoke the order when they plan to electively home educate their child.
  • Where a child is placed at a special school under arrangements made by the local authority, the local authority has to consent to the pupil being electively home educated. In such cases the parent must request permission from the local authority before the child's name can be deleted from the school roll.
  • Children in the care of Suffolk County Council (Children Looked After) should attend maintained educational provision and will not be considered for EHE.
