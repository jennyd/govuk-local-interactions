Disabled Facilities Grant

If you are a disabled Test Valley resident and you are experiencing difficulties in your home then grant-aid may be available to assist you to make your home more suitable for your needs.

  • Do you find that getting in and out of a bath is a problem?
  • Do you find climbing the stairs in the house is a problem?
  • If you are a wheelchair user, do you find getting in and out of the house a problem?

These are typical situations where Test Valley Borough Council has been able to assist residents in the past.

If you feel that your home could be adapted to make life easier for you then please read on.

How do I get started?

Grants are available to assist home owners, private tenants and Housing Association tenants.

The first step is to contact:

Social Services Direct
Occupational Therapy
Glen House
Glen Road
Swanwick.
SO31 7HD
Tel: 0845 6004555 (calls are charged at the local rate) or
Email: otdirect@hants.gov.uk

Social Services Direct will take from you all the relevant information about your particular circumstances and then, if appropriate, they will refer your case to your local Social Services office.

This is an important first step. Test Valley Borough Council could not consider an enquiry for grant-aid until Social Services Direct has been contacted.

What happens next?

When your case has been received by your local Social Services office, arrangements will be made for an Occupational Therapist to visit you in your home. The Occupational Therapist will carefully assess your current difficulties and will discuss with you the options that are available to assist you.

If the required works are relatively small scale (e.g. provision of a suitable handrail to assist client to climb steps at the front of the house) then that may be organised directly by Social Services.

If more major works are required (e.g. the installation of a stairlift or the replacement of a bath with a level access shower) then it is likely that the Occupational Therapist will refer your case to Test Valley Borough Council (Private Sector Housing Team) with a recommendation that grant-aid be made available to assist with the cost of the works.

Who decides whether I am eligible for grant-aid?

Grant-aid is only available for the works recommended by the Occupational Therapist.
If you are in receipt of a means-tested benefit such as Income Support, Guarantee Credit (This is a form of Pension Credit) or means-tested Job-Seekers Allowance then grant-aid is likely to be available to fully fund the cost of the works.

Grant-aid is still available to those persons who do not receive one of the above benefits. If you are not in receipt of one of those benefits then it will be necessary to carry out a test of your financial resources to determine whether grant-aid is likely to be available. The test of financial resources would also indicate whether or not you would be likely to have to make a contribution towards the cost of the recommended works.

What sort of information is needed for the test?

A financial information form would be sent to you. The form would ask for information about your financial circumstances. The information supplied by you is strictly confidential and would not be shared with anyone else. The information supplied in the form would be used to assess whether you would be eligible for grant-aid and, if you were likely to be eligible, it would indicate whether the works would be wholly or partly funded by the grant.

Is help available to assist me to complete these forms?

We would be pleased to assist. Whether you are a tenant or a home owner, we are usually able to arrange for someone to visit you in your home.

I have some savings – will I still qualify for grant-aid?

The first £6000 of any savings is ignored. The fact that you may have savings above £6000 does not disqualify you from grant-aid.

What would happen if I am not eligible for grant-aid?

If grant-aid is not available then it may still be possible to find other funding that could assist you to afford the cost of the recommended works. Test Valley Borough Council works closely with a local Home Improvement Agency (Test Valley Home Improvement Agency) to assist Test Valley residents to carry out much needed home improvements. The Test Valley Home Improvement Agency would assist you to try and find alternative funding for the works.

Is grant-aid available to assist with adapting my home for my disabled child?

Since 31st December 2005, it is no longer necessary for the parents of disabled children to undergo the test of financial resources. If the scheme of works is considered to be appropriate then it is likely that grant-aid would be available to fully fund the cost of the works.

Is there a limit to the amount of grant that is available?

The Disabled Facilities Grant has a limit of £25000. If the costs of the proposed scheme exceed that limit then it is possible for a Disabled Facilities Loan to be made available to fund the difference.

Disabled Facilities Grants/ Disabled Facilities Loans – what is the difference?

A Disabled Facilities Grant would not have to be repaid in the event of the applicant moving house (whether it is a home owner selling their home or tenant choosing to move to another rented property).

A Disabled Facilities Loan would have to be repaid when the house is next sold. The Loan is interest free.

I have been told that I am eligible for grant-aid – what happens next?

You will be visited by an officer from the Council’s Private Sector Housing Team. The aim of the visit is to prepare a schedule of the works that can be considered for grant-aid. The schedule would be used to obtain estimates of the cost of the proposed adaptations.

Please note that the works are not carried out by Test Valley Borough Council. It would be necessary for you to employ a private contractor to carry out the work on your behalf. We would be available to advise and assist where possible. The Test Valley Home Improvement Agency may be able to assist you to obtain the necessary estimates.

The schedule of grant works, application forms etc will then be sent to you. Help would be available to assist you to make your application for grant.

I have made my application for grant-aid and it has been approved – what happens next?

It would be necessary for you to contact your contractor to discuss and agree a start date for the works. Again, help would be available to assist where required.

The above notes aim to provide basic information about the grants and loans that are available. If you would like more information then please do not hesitate to contact us.

For further information please contact the:

Private Sector Housing team
Tel 01264 368000
Email envhealth@testvalley.gov.uk
