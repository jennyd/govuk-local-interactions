Service details for Homelessness

Responsibility: Housing Options

If you are single or a couple aged between 16-24 with no dependant children then further assistance is available at Taunton Association for the Homeless youth hub at the Old Vic café in East Reach, Taunton. A housing officer will be available there between 1pm and 4.30pm Monday to Friday or you can contact them on 01823 271326.

If you are over 25, have dependant children or are pregnant you will need to be seen by a Housing Opitions Officer at The Deane House.

About this service

Rough Sleeping

What is rough sleeping?
It is estimated that between 150 and 200 individuals sleep rough in Taunton Deane each year.
The definition of rough sleeping is when someone is bedded down in the open air (such as on the streets, in tents, doorways, parks, bus shelters or encampments). People in buildings or other places not designed for habitation (such as stairwells, barns, sheds, car parks, cars, derelict boats, stations, or "bashes").

How to know if someone is at risk of rough sleeping?

If someone is:
• under a valid notice with no scope to negotiate with landlord
• formally asked to leave by family and friends and given a date to leave by
• has had previous episodes of rough sleeping
• sofa surfing: ie can only stay on a night by night basis and has no financial means to secure accommodation

Street Outreach Service

To help make contact and offer support to all of these people, Taunton Association for the Homeless run an Outreach Service for Rough Sleepers.  The Outreach workers will try and find and contact anyone sleeping rough - wherever they are in Taunton Deane.

You can report a rough sleeper to the Outreach Team by phoning: 0800 9177765

Anyone rough sleeping can see the Outreach Team at one of their drop-in sessions:
