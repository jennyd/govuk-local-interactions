Bereavement advice and support

Last updated: 11/12/2015

Child Bereavement UK (CBUK)

Child Bereavement UK (CBUK) supports families and educates professionals when a baby or child of any age dies or is dying, or when a child is facing bereavement. Every year we train almost 7000 professionals, helping them to better understand and meet the needs of grieving families.

In the UK, when a baby or child dies, or a child is bereaved, many of those affected are unable to access good quality support which meets their individual needs. Child Bereavement UK believes all families should have the support they need to rebuild their lives when a child grieves or when a child dies. Our aim is to make sure they do.

For more information or to support the work of the charity Email: enquiries@childbereavementuk.org or call 01494 568900 Support and Information line 0800 02 888 40 

Hope Again

Hope Again is Cruse Bereavement Cares website for young people. Cruse is a national charity that provides support, advice and information to children, young people and adults when someone close to them dies. We also work to enhance society’s care of bereaved people.

Cruse Bereavement Care

Cruse Bereavement Care exists to promote the well-being of bereaved people and to enable anyone bereaved by death to understand their grief and cope with their loss. The organisation provides counselling and support. It offers information, advice, education and training services.

Lasting Post

The purpose of Lastingpost is to help people address the very difficult subject of death. In particular the ‘Planning ahead’ section of the website helps people to set down privately the instructions and wishes they want in place for use after their death. The benefit of Planning Ahead is that these wishes can be stored privately and securely online. The ‘When death occurs’ section then helps the deceased’s family with practical advice including arranging the funeral, sorting out probate and financial matters. The website also provides a place to notify friends and family of the death. These notices can be accompanied by a personal obituary and a memorial book. There is also an extensive section on bereavement. Lasting Post is completely free.

Bereavement Guide

For more information about  the organisations who can offer support, please see our Bereavement Guide.
