College Transport for 16-19 Year Olds

Last updated: 23/10/2015

Cornwall Council provides subsidised transport for students to access Post 16 education at a school Sixth Form, Further Education establishment, Local Authority commissioned learning provider or apprenticeship provider for students aged between sixteen and nineteen (or to the age of twenty-five for students with statements of special educational needs).

Support under Cornwall Council’s Post 16 subsidised transport scheme may be in one of the following ways:

  • Subsidised bus or train pass
  • Subsidised specialist transport - e.g. taxi
  • In exceptional circumstances, via a fuel allowance

Transport support will be provided for standard academic terms only and at the start and end of a standard college/sixth form day (e.g. 9am - 4pm, and only one standard day per establishment).  Access to courses outside of these times will have to be discussed directly with the establishments attended.

Link transport to and from a subsidised transport route will only be provided where the student’s home address is more than five miles from a pick-up/drop-off point. The distance between the subsidised transport route and home address will be calculated along the nearest available route which might include footpaths, other pathways and bridleways as well as roads.

Similar travel schemes are operated by some individual schools and colleges and students (including those ineligible for the Council scheme) may choose to apply for transport under those schemes instead of via Cornwall Council (see Section 10 for more details).
