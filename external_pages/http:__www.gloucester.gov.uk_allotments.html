Allotments

To find your nearest allotment, input your postcode or address & click the "Search" button, then scroll down to "Community and Living" on the next page.

 

Allotment locations & number of plots at sites

Gloucester currently has 10 sites across the city:

  • Cotteswold Road, GL4 6RG (6 small plots)
  • Dean's Way, St Oswald's Road, GL1 2SF (6 small plots)
  • Estcourt Close, GL1 3LT (132 full plots)
  • Estcourt Park, Escourt Road, GL1 3LW (62 plots of mixed sizes)
  • Hawthorns & Tredworth Fields, Hartland Road, GL1 4RU (52 plots of mixed sizes for Hawthorns; 23 plots of mixed sizes & 1 large community plot for Tredworth Fields)
  • Hempsted Cross, St Swithuns Road, GL2 5LH (2 large plots)
  • Innsworth Allotments, Innsworth Lane, GL2 0DF (94 plots of mixed sizes)
  • Robert Raikes Avenue, GL4 0HE (31 large plots)
  • Saintbridge Allotments, Cheyney Close, GL4 4PR (37 starter plots, 147 full plots & 3 large community plots)
  • White City, Bibury Road, GL4 6TU (26 large plots)
  • Willow Way Allotments, Off Willow Avenue, GL4 4NZ (10 small plots)

Quedgeley Allotments:

  • Goosebay Drive, GL2 2GZ (the site includes a variety of plot sizes including raised bed plots for individuals and groups with mobility problems)

For all enquiries related to allotments at Quedgeley, including the waiting list, contact Quedgeley Parish Council.

Please note that there is a waiting list for the majority of allotment sites, although some waiting lists are shorter than others. There are currently plots available at Innsworth Lane & Saintbridge.

If you would like to register your interest in an allotment or would like more information, please contact heretohelp@gloucester.gov.uk

How do I rent an allotment?

Call the City Council on 01452 396396 between 8:30am-5pm Monday-Friday, or email heretohelp@gloucester.gov.uk for further information.

The rules and responsibilities for allotment sites can be found here:

  • Allotment Rules and Responsibilities - Saintbridge
  • Allotment Rules and Responsibilities - All other sites

How much does it cost?

In 2016, the plot charge will stay the same as the 2015 rate at 16.144 pence per square metre.

Please note that there is a charge of £20 to set up a new tenancy. This is a one-off fee and you will be billed with your first invoice.

The following outlines the general costs & concession rates:

  • Standard half plot - (126 square metres): £20.34 per annum (Over 65 years /Housing Benefit/Council Tax Support/ Disabled: £12.20 per annum)
  • Standard full plot - (253 square metres): £40.84 per annum (Over 65 years /Housing Benefit/Council Tax Support/ Disabled: £24.50 per annum).
  • Disabled concessions are based on certain criteria which will be clarified at the start of the tenancy.
  • All concessions are applicable to one plot per person only.

From 2016, the allotment tenancy year will alter from previous years which have run from 1st January to 31st December. It will subsequently run from 1st November (2016) to 31st October.

Allotment Structure Applications

If you're a new/existing allotment tenant and you wish to erect a structure (shed, greenhouse, poly tunnel, etc.), you need permission from the City Council to do so.

If you wish to erect a structure on your allotment, you can download the Allotments Structure Form which you will need to fill out and send to:

Allotment Strategy for Gloucester

On the 10th of December 2014, the City Council's Cabinet adopted the Allotment Strategy for Gloucester. The Strategy provides a framework in which allotments can develop and thrive both now and into the future.
