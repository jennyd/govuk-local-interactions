Garden waste in Weymouth and Portland

Listen

Garden waste is not accepted in the green wheelie bin for household residual waste or the brown bin for weekly food waste and cardboard.

Garden waste can be:

  • Composted at home - This is the most environmentally friendly way to deal with your garden waste, as you do not need to transport it anywhere, and you will also reap the benefits of the lovely compost you produce.  Why not purchase a compost bin to help you get started composting at home?  Please feel free to contact us on 01305 838000 for more advice.
  • Taken to your local household recycling centre - Where it will be collected in large containers and transported to a central composting plant and turned into a soil improver.

Council garden waste sacks 

You can also purchase garden waste sacks for collection with your brown bin. These are currently £1.20 per sack.  

Please note: council garden waste sacks will no longer be sold after 30 September 2014.

The new garden waste service will began on Monday 13 October 2014. Residents that purchased sacks for the current service could use them until Friday 10 October 2014.

 

The sacks are made from compostable paper and are not reusable, and need to be placed alongside your brown bin on your usual collection day.  

They will be taken for composting with the brown bin material and made into soil improver.  Please note that sacks without the council logo will not be collected.  

Council garden waste sacks can be purchased in the following locations:

  • Portland Town Council Offices, Fortuneswell, Portland, DT5 1LW (open Monday-Friday, 10am-2pm)  
  • Londis Stores, Westham Road, Weymouth, DT4 8NU (open seven days a week, 7am-10pm)
  • Londis Stores, Abbotsbury Road, Weymouth, DT4 0LX (open seven days a week, 6am-10pm)
  • Goulds Garden Centre, Littlemoor Road, Weymouth, DT3 6AD (open seven days a week, 9am-5.30pm Monday-Friday, 10.30am-4.30pm on Sunday)

There is no limit to the number of garden waste sacks that you can put out for collection.
