Consultation

The people, groups, stakeholders and communities of Cannock Chase are what make the district unique, and as a Council we are committed to not only listening to and responding to what our residents say; but also involving residents in shaping and improving our services.

On this page you will find information about our residents panel: Your Community, Your Voice, current Cannock Chase Council consultations, results of past consultations and information and links to external consultations that affect the District.

Your Community, Your Voice

Have you got something to say about your local area? Come and join Your Community, Your Voice for the opportunity to have your voice heard.

What is Your Community, Your Voice? Your Community, Your Voice is a panel of local residents that want to share their views on the area they live in. Your Community, Your Voice gives residents the opportunity to have their say about living and working in the district.

How does Your Community, Your Voice work? Time to time members will be asked to take part in surveys and focus groups. These could range from the Council's service areas to a specific subject.

Why have Your Community, Your Voice? By consulting with our residents we can be sure that we are serving those whose opinions count. Using the information we receive from Your Community, Your Voice and other consultations can help us shape our service areas and priorities. To join Your Community, Your Voice click here.

External Consultations

Grow Up Great - WLCT Community Wellbeing Arts Team

The Grow Up Great projects is a new scheme which aims to involve targeted families with early years children who have barriers to living a healthier lifestyle. To do this a mapping exercise is taking place of all the provisions happening across Cannock Chase District. An online survey is available for parents/carers of early years children living in the district to complete. By taking part you will have the opportunity to win a £50 Boots gift voucher.

To take part follow the link

https://www.surveymonkey.com/r/growupgreat

The survey closes on Sunday 6th December

South Staffordshire Network for Mental Health - Crisis Services

Have you used crisis services in the last 12-24 months? SSNMH are writing a report about crisis resolution and home treatment teams and would like to talk to you over the telephone about the service received or invite you to a focus group. SSNMH would like to know how you accessed the teams, how you felt about the support that you had from the teams, were you given a plan when you were discharged? Did you feel empowered, engaged and supported? Your views REALLY matter and will be fed back to the Trust and the teams themselves so please get in touch and have your say! SSNMH are particularly interested in whether you felt that your ethnicity, gender or sexuality had any effect on the service you received, directly or indirectly. If you would like to be involved email tracey.hackett@ssnmentalhealth.co.uk or call the office on 01543 301139 and leave your name and number for Tracey.

This will finish by the end of January 2016

Staffordshire County Council - Extending Free Childcare for Parents

From 2017 it is expected that the majority of working parents of three and four year olds will be eligible for 30 hours of free childcare per week.

In order to help assess the current situation in Staffordshire the County Council would be grateful if you as a parent or prospective parent could complete a survey. They need to make sure there is enough childcare available in the places and at the times parents need it. They are also interested in parent's views about quality childcare and how you choose childcare for your children.
