Assessing your needs

Bridgend County Borough council has received a request from yourself, your family, your carer, a friend, or health professional such as a Doctor or Nurse because they are concerned about how you are managing.

What happens after your request for help?

A Care Manager will make an appointment with you at a time that is convenient for you and your carer or family. The Care Manager could be a Social Worker, Community Care Worker, or a Community Nurse working in an adult services assessment team.

We will visit you and carry out an assessment to decide the risks to your independence and the level of support you, your family or your career may need.

What is an assessment?

An assessment is a way of working out your needs and how you can be best helped.

We will give you information about services and organisations that may be available to help meet your needs.

Preparing for you assessment

You or your carer or family may want to prepare for the assessment visit by writing down anything you want to talk through with us, such as –

  • What is difficult for you?
  • What kind of help are you having at the moment?
  • Are some days easier than others?
  • If so why do you think this is?

We know that there are some things which can be difficult or stressful to talk about but please do not be too embarrassed. We will understand your feelings and respect them.

If you have a carer (a relative or friend who provides you with unpaid help), we will want to talk to them about what help they are able and willing to give.

Carers who provide significant care can also ask for a separate assessment of their own needs.  Ask us for more information on this.

If your first language is not English, or if you use sign language, we can arrange for an interpreter. Please let us know what you need

With your permission, we may need to get information from other people, such as your Doctor.

When the assessment has been completed the worker will discuss with you and your carer or family how best to meet your needs.

Everything that is discussed and agreed or that we may have disagreed with, during the assessment will be written down on the assessment form.

For some people we will just provide equipment and/or advice and we will not need to do anything more

Do I need to do anything now?

No.  As soon as a worker is available they will contact you to make an appointment.
