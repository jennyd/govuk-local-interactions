Our Housing Options Officers offer housing advice and assistance to people who would like to secure suitable and affordable accommodation within the private sector or through the Choice Based Lettings Scheme

The Housing Options Officers are able to discuss the options available and assess whether certain funds can be used to prevent homelessness and secure accommodation in the private sector. In addition to general housing advice, the Housing Options Officers can offer basic debt advice, advocacy and can attend court, representing tenants facing eviction and owner-occupiers dealing with mortgage/secured loan repossessions. You can also find useful advice and information about housing on the Government website.

Advice and assistance is available to tenants, landlords, lettings agents, owner occupiers, parents, young people and agencies to try and prevent homelessness wherever possible. We have strong links with organisations and services such as the Citizens’ Advice Bureau, social services and a number of supported housing organisations in order to provide joined-up working and continually look at ways of providing a smoother service to all our customers

Please contact your local Housing Options Team at the earliest opportunity if you are in any difficulty with your accommodation or just need some advice. The earlier you address your problems or concerns, the easier it is for us to try and solve them.

Wiltshire Council’s Housing Options teams offer free advice and assistance to people at risk of losing their home or who are experiencing housing problems. Our offices are open from 8.30am - 5.30pm, Monday to Friday:

If you would like to visit your local office it would be helpful if you could bring along the following documentation:

  • ID
  • Proof of income
  • Child benefit / Tax credit information
  • National Insurance number

Additional documentation may include;

  • Tenancy Agreement
  • Eviction Notice (e.g. s21 Notice)
  • Court Orders
  • Bailiff Warrant
  • Letters from mortgage lender
  • Mortgage statements
  • Any supporting medical evidence such as GP letter and confirmation of medication.
