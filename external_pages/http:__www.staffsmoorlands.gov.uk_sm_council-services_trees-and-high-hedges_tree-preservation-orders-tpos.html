Tree Preservation Orders (TPOs)

A Tree Preservation Order (TPO) is a legal document made, administered and enforced by ourselves as the Local Planning Authority (LPA), to protect specified trees and woodlands with public amenity value.

You are advised when considering carrying out work on trees to consult us to determine if the trees are protected.

If the trees are protected by a Tree Preservation Order (TPO), consent will normally be required for pruning or felling.  An application must be made by completing the standard application form, stating your reasons for the application and giving details of the proposed work.  Supporting technical information may also be required as follows:

  • If the reason for the application relates to the condition of the tree - for example due to the presence of pests, diseases, fungi or structural defects affecting the safety of the tree - written evidence from an appropriate arboricultural professional must be submitted in support of the application.
  • If the reason for the application relates to suspected structual damage caused by the tree, a report from a structural engineer/surveyor together with technical advice from other experts such as root and soil analysis must normally be submitted in support of the application.

Please note that we are unable to consider applications which are not completed on the standard form and/or are not supported by relevant professional reports where necessary.

The TPO application form and guidance notes for its completion can be downloaded below.  An application can also be made electronically through the Planning Portal.
