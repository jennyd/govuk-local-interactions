Getting a break

Page Content

Breaks from caring, as well as other forms of support, can be a huge help to carers. It can make the difference between being able to carry on and having to give up.

As a carer, there are different ways you can take a break. The kind of break that will suit you will depend on your own needs and circumstances, as well as those of the person you look after.

Here are some of the ways carers can get a break from their caring role. 

Carers personal budget

Following an assessment, some carers may be eligible for their own personal budget. . 

Carers can use their personal budgets to pay towards a variety of activities which will give them a break from their caring role and to achieve an improved level of health and well-being.

More about carers assessments

Flexible breaks fund - Islington Carers Hub

Carers who aren't able to access any social services support e.g. a personal budget, can access the flexible breaks fund. The fund is administered by the Islington Carers Hub and carers can access up to £200 for an individual and up to £600 for a family. There is also a specific breaks fund for palliative patients. Find out more about the flexible breaks fund and the eligibility criteria on the Carers Hub website

Service users personal budget - Respite Services 

We provide respite services for service users (such as residential care, day-services and sitting services) which allow carers to have a break and to help support them in their role. 

Service users, in discussion with their carer, can choose how to use their personal budget to meet respite needs. For example:

  • Arranging a personal assistant to take the service user out for the evening so the carer can have a break
  •  Arranging a personal assistant to support the service user at home when the carer is away on holiday
  • Asking the council to arrange a residential care stay when the carer is away on holiday
      A financial assessment will be completed to see if the service user can contribute towards the cost of their care services.

    Shared Lives Short Breaks

    For some people living at home with their families, Shared Lives Short Breaks is another way of getting a break. The service user will stay at the Shared Lives carers home so that the family carer can have a day or a few days break.

    Flexible breaks fund for parents or carers of children under 17 -Children and Families

    The Flexible Breaks Fund is for people who provide unpaid care or support to an Islington resident aged 17 or under. It could be a child, relative, friend or neighbour  who can't mange without help because of physical, mental illness or disability and additional needs including social, emotional or behavioural.
