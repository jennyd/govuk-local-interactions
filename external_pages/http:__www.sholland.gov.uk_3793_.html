Blocked Drains and Septic Tank Clearance

The Council operates a free drain clearance and septic tank clearing service to Council tenants or property owners who pay their sewage charge to South Holland District Council.

Drain and sewer responsibility can be confusing so here are some guidelines to help.

South Holland District Council own and maintain several sewage treatment works around the district and any problems with these, or the drains to them, should be reported directly to us on 01775 761161.  You will know if we treat the waste from your home as you will pay sewage rates to us rather than Anglian Water.

There are many Council owned properties in the District that have Septic tanks or cesspools servicing them, the emptying and maintenance of these and the drains to them are the Council's responsibility.  Please call us on 01775 761161 if yours needs emptying or you have blocked drains.  

Council owned properties connected to the main public sewer will pay sewage rates to Anglian Water.  The Council is responsible for pipes that are situated within your property boundary (such as beneath your house, garden and driveway), and which take away the flows from your property up to the point where they connect to pipes that are shared with another property, if you have any problems with these drains please contact us on 01775 761161.  All shared drainage pipe work leading to the public sewer is the responsibility of Anglian Water, as is the main public sewer in the road.  In many cases you will not be able to tell where the blockage is, however if your neighbours are affected as well, it is more likely to be a blockage in the shared or main drains which are Anglian Water's responsibility, you can contact them on 03457 145145.

For more information about Anglian Water sewer ownership please see their website Anglian Water Sewers

Please be aware that plugholes and toilet bowls are not the place to dispose of fat, oil, grease, nappies or baby wipes.  If we put things like fat, oil and grease (FOG) down our sinks, and sanitary waste and wipes (known as 'uncrushable's) down the loo, it can cause sewer pipes and pumps to block leading to sewer flooding in homes and gardens and sewage spills into local rivers and waterways.

Frequently asked questions

I have got blocked drains.  What should I do?

Have a look at the guidelines above to find out who is responsible for your drains, if they are Council maintained or if you are unsure call 01775 761161 or for Anglian Water call 03457 145145.

How long do I have to wait for the Council to clear my drain?

Our target response times are:

Up to 4 hours for drains to be unblocked.

Up to 48 hours for septic tanks / cesspools to be emptied.

However please be aware that, we will not generally clear drains or empty tanks in hours of darkness as the work can be noisy and also for health and safety reasons.

The road is flooded, who is responsible?

Lincolnshire County Council is responsible for maintenance to roadways in most cases and therefore the drainage of them.  Please contact your local Highways Department on 01522 782070 for more information or you can report a fault online Lincolnshire County Council Highways
