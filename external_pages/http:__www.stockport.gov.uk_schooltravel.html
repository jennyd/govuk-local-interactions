Travel to School

Stockport is a relatively compact district and is generally well served by public transport.

The area offers many commercially operated bus services, which are supplemented with extra school services where necessary by Transport for Greater Manchester (TfGM). Stockport Council actively promotes environmentally friendly and safe walking routes to school as part of its Council Plan.

Concessionary Travel

Children under 16 years of age who obtain an igo pass may travel on all bus services for a concessionary fare.

You can find further details about concessionary fares on the TfGM Website.

Free School Travel

Some children qualify for free travel passes. You can apply for a Free School Bus Pass for September 2016 using the online form below. Please ensure that you read the Free School Transport Policy below.

You can also view the guidance document  which contains information on how to complete the online form.

Free School Transport Policy 2015-16 (PDF 162kb)

Free School Travel Online Form Guidance (PDF 319kb)

Free School Travel Online Form

To apply for a Free School Bus pass for use during this term, please email admissions.support@stockport.gov.uk

Replacement Free School Travel Pass - if your child has lost their free school travel pass you can obtain a replacement from TfGM.

Yellow School Bus - TfGM

To apply for a Yellow School Bus Pass you need to already have an igo pass.

Apply for an igo pass

Once you have an 'igo pass' you can apply for a Yellow School Bus Pass.  The application form will ask you for your preferred Yellow School Bus Route.  Please direct any queries to TfGM.

Apply for a Yellow School Bus pass

Please remember you cannot use megarider tickets on your Yellow School Bus.

Sustainable School Travel Strategy

Local Authorities are required to promote the use of sustainable modes of travel and transport and to publish a Sustainable School Travel Strategy. You can find more information about the strategy in the document below.

Sustainable School Travel Strategy (PDF 297kb)

The strategy sets out what has been achieved to encourage more children and young people to use sustainable modes of travel to school such as walking, cycling or public transport and also the extended right to free transport to pupils from low income backgrounds. 

The document also sets out what work will be undertaken in the years to follow in order to meet the requirements of the Department for Education – Home-to-school travel and transport statutory guidance.
