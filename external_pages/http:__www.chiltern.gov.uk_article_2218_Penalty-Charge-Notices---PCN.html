Penalty Charge Notices - PCN

If you do not buy and clearly display a ticket for the correct amount or if you ignore the parking regulations (lines and signs), you will be issued with a Penalty Charge Notice.

Our policy for the enforcement and cancellation of Penalty Charge Notices (PCNs) is available to download below.

Whilst processing PCNs, the registered keeper details of vehicles may be requested from the DVLA - Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency.

What to do if you receive a PCN

Don't ignore a PCN, it won't go away. Follow the payment instructions on the PCN. There is a discount for paying within 14 days of issue.

Pay a PCN (parking ticket)

You can pay a PCN online using our secure site or the automated telephone payment line 08453052148. This call will cost you 3 pence per minute plus your phone company's access charge. NB should you wish to challenge the PCN and appeal, do not pay.

Appeals

If you are issued with a PCN, you have the right to appeal against it. Find out how to Appeal against a Penalty Charge Notice

Parking regulations


For the safety of people, protection of vehicles and general use of on and off street parking, all car park and road users are asked to park according to set regulations under the Traffic Management Act 2004. You can view the full text of the Act on the Office of Public Sector Information website using the link below.
