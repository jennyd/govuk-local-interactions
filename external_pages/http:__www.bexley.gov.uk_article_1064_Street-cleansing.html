Street cleansing

The Council currently has a term contract with Serco Local Government Ltd to undertake street cleansing throughout the borough.

There are approximately 1,800 roads within the borough which are covered by this contract.

The service is divided into the following principle categories: shop fronts, borough roads, car parks and emergency response cover. This page also has information about drug related litter and leaf clearance.

In addition to the above, the London Borough of Bexley is also responsible for the sweeping of the sections of the A2 and A20 within the borough.

All categories with the exception of the emergency response are carried out to agreed schedules.

To find out when your road is swept, please contact Customer Services or Works Direct.

Shop fronts

All 96 shop fronts are visited daily at regular intervals, with the exception of Welling, Erith and Sidcup which are manned Monday to Saturday between 7am to 6pm. Bexleyheath is also manned Monday to Saturday but between 7am to 8pm and on a Sunday between 10am to 4pm. Staff deployed in the four major shopping areas are the traditional "man with a broom and barrow".

Borough roads

Borough roads are swept on a three weekly cycle, Monday to Friday.

There are two teams, each consisting of three operatives. One operative cleans the carriageways using a Ravo 5002 mechanical sweeper, whilst the remaining two operatives sweep the footpaths with the aid of an Applied 424 ride-on sweeping machine.

All roads are scheduled to be cleaned at least once during the three week cycle.

In addition to the sweeping of the residential roads, a total of 85 of the major roads within the borough are swept by a large mechanical sweeper during the night.

Due to heavy traffic flows, dual carriageway principal roads receive an bi-annual deep cleanse requiring lane closures.

Car parks

All 22 car parks within the borough are swept twice weekly (Monday to Friday) and are all swept on a Sunday by a mechanical sweeper.

Emergency response crew

There are two crews available to respond to emergencies (Monday to Friday) such as road accidents, small spillages, syringes and drug related litter, and dead animals etc. When not responding to emergencies, they are undertaking cleansing operations, including sweeping inland footpaths.

Leaf clearance

Leaf clearance is an annual task undertaken (depending on time of leaf fall) between October and December. Additional resources are used and the level of staffing and machinery will vary upon conditions.
