Housing options and advice

Advice on your housing options including how to apply for a council or housing association home and information for private tenants and landlords. Links to our dedicated website for advice on your housing needs - RBC Living and Homes.

  • Joining the housing register

    We have an independent allocations policy and we no longer offer properties to applicants without a local connection to Runnymede. We reward working households as we recognise their community contribution and we work towards managing applicants expectations. Housing demand outweighs the supply in Runnymede and it is important that applicants realise that social housing is not the only route open to them. We will always provide applicants with full housing options. There are links on this page to help you with those choices. Please login using your LIN number above.

  • Need advice on your housing options?

    Our aim is to prevent you losing your accommodation or to help you find a new home that best meets your needs. We expect you to have tried to resolve your own housing problems first and to be able to tell us what action you have taken. The earlier we know about your problem, the more we can do to help.

  • Homeless young people (16 and 17 years old)

    Surrey County Council's Youth Support Service (YSS) has a scheme to prevent homelessness in young people of 16 and 17.

  • Hostel and night shelter accommodation

    We do not have a legal duty to provide crisis accommodation for everyone who finds themselves homeless.

  • Domestic abuse

    Domestic abuse can come in many forms. It is rarely a one-off incident and it is not only about being physically or sexually abused. You may be subject to more subtle attacks such as isolation, psychological games and harassment. If you are in immediate danger dial 999.

  • Extra Care housing

    Extra Care Housing is also known as 'assisted living' or 'housing with care'. It allows you to live independently with the added security of access to 24-hour support services.

  • Independent Retirement Living

    Independent Retirement Living accommodation is somewhere you can live independently with the added advantage of support if you need it.

  • Private rented accommodation - Landlords

    We are always looking to work with landlords within the borough and out of the area to find rental properties of all sizes which we can offer to people in need of accommodation.

  • Private rented accommodation - Tenants

    Housing in the Runnymede area is in great demand. With over 1,100 applicants on our housing register and, on average, only 150 properties available a year, the demand cannot be met through social housing alone.

  • Runnymede Rentstart

    Runnymede Rentstart provides help to homeless couples and single people who need support getting privately rented accommodation. They may be able to provide a deposit bond to a landlord to secure accommodation for you. Runnymede Rentstart is operated by Runnymede Citizens Advice Bureau (CAB).

  • How we allocate social housing

    We are required to have an Allocations Policy which details how we allocate social housing within Runnymede.
