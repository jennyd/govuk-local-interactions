This page contains information relating to the North East Lincolnshire Car Parks, variety of parking permits and season tickets, Civil Parking Enforcement, residents' parking schemes, verge and footway parking in some locations within the Borough.

Here you can find information on Car Parks' charges and locations, residents' permits, season tickets and Civil Parking Enforcement via the attached downloads and links. The Parking Strategy is also available for download on the right hand side of this page.

Penalty Charge Notice

If you have been issued with a Penalty Charge Notice (PCN) you can either pay it or challenge it.

  • Pay your PCN
  • Challenge your PCN

Important: if you do nothing, the charge will increase.

For more information, visit www.patrol-uk.info or Know your parking rights

Residents' Parking schemes

Residents can find information about where residential parking schemes exist and identify the cost and number of permits allowed per household. For further advice and information please follow the link on the right hand side of this page.

Car Park locations

North East Lincolnshire Council has joined the Park Mark – Safer Parking Scheme and now in the fifth year has 22 car parks with the Park Mark award.

You can view the locations of the Boroughs' Car Parks on our Interactive map or in the downloadable 'List of all Car Parks' on the right hand side of this page.

Civil Parking Enforcement 

North East Lincolnshire Council has adopted the powers under the Traffic Management Act 2004 to enable Civil Parking Enforcement (CPE) as of the 5th July 2010. To read about these changes and for further advice and information please follow the link on the right hand side of this page. 

Verge and footway parking

A Traffic Regulation Order is now in place at New Waltham, Waltham and Immingham. North East Lincolnshire Council's Civil Enforcement Officers are enforcing verge and footway parking in these locations. For further advice and information please follow the link on the right hand side of this page.

Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA) news:

From 1st October 2014 the paper tax disc will no longer be issued and required to be displayed on a vehicle windscreen. DVLA holds a digital record of taxed and untaxed vehicles and a paper disc is therefore no longer necessary as proof that vehicle tax has been paid. To drive or keep a vehicle on the road you will still need to get vehicle tax and DVLA will still send you a renewal reminder when your vehicle tax is due to expire. This applies to all types of vehicles including those that are exempt from payment of vehicle tax.

From 1st October 2014, when you buy a vehicle, the vehicle tax will no longer be transferred with the vehicle. You will need to get new vehicle tax before you can use the vehicle. If you sell a vehicle after 1st October 2014 and you have notified DVLA, you will automatically get a refund for any full calendar months left on the vehicle tax.

For further information please visit the DVLA Website or follow the link: https://www.gov.uk/government/news/vehicle-tax-changes
