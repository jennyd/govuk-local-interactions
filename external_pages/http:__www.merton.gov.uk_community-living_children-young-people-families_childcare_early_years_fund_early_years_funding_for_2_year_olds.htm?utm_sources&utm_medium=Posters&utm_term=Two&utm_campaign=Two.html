Early education and childcare for two-year-olds

In Merton, around 40% of children are entitled to free part-time early education and childcare at the age of two.

What is available?

A free early education and childcare place for 570 hours over the course of a year. Free places are available at approved day nurseries, preschools and childminders in Merton. See when your child would be able to take up their place:

Child's date of birth Will be able to take up a place from
1 January - 31 March 1 April
1 April - 31 August 1 September
1 September - 31 December 1 January

Places are usually available on weekdays for 38 weeks of the year. The free hours are offered as 3 hours a day every day, 5 hours a day over 3 days or as a total of 15 hours between 7am and 7pm. Some providers offer places for more than 38 weeks a year with fewer free hours each week.

Can I get a free place for my child?

Your child may be able to get a place if you:

  • Receive certain non-working benefits, such as income support, income-based jobseekerâs allowance or support through Part 6 of the Immigration and Asylum Act (a National Asylum Support Service (NASS) reference number); or
  • Receive Working Tax Credit and have an annual household income of less than Â£16,190 (before tax)

Find out if you can claim a place by following the three steps below:

  1. Visit the My Early Learning Website
  2. Enter your details into the Online Eligibility Checker
  3. The checker will tell you if you are eligible and provide you with a unique Reference Number for you to use to claim your child's free place

Places are also available if:

  • Your child has a current Statement of Special Educational Needs (SEN) or an Education, Health and Care Plan (EHCP); or
  • Your child receives Disability Living Allowance (DLA); or
  • You have adopted your child or you have a special guardianship order or residence order to care for them.
  • A child is looked after by the Local Authority, for example, they are in foster care

If you meet any of these three criteria, you will need to have paper proof of this to claim your child's free place.

I am eligible for a free place for my child. What shall I do next?

Approved nurseries, preschools and childminders who offer early education places, are registered with the Directory of Providers of Free early Education Places for 2-year-olds.

To find providers in the Directory, simply :

  • Visit the Family Services Directory - www.merton.gov.uk/fsd
  • Select the childcare and education category
  • Search for your postcode
  • Filter the results to select those which offer funded places for 2-year-olds.

The results will be all Merton providers of free (funded) places for 2-year-olds in order of distance from your home.

You can find further information about each childcare provider by clicking on the links in the directory, including their contact details and Ofsted grade.

Contact the provider or providers that interest you to arrange a visit to see what they offer and find out if they have a place available for your child.

The provider you choose will process your application for you

Once you have chosen a provider who has a place available for your child, give them your Reference Number. The provider will check that the number is valid using the My Early Learning Website.

Families with children in receipt of DLA, who have a Statement of SEN or an EHCP or who are adopted will not have a reference number and so must provide paper proof of eligibility to the provider they choose.

The provider will ask all eligible families for proof of identity and ask them to sign a Parental Agreement Form which includes details of the free place.

How will early education help my child?

Enjoying good quality early education at the age of two has been found to give children a good start in life, making them more likely to enjoy and do well at school.

For example, children can:

  • Develop their speech and language
  • Make friends and play with other children
  • Try out new and exciting activities
  • Learn new skills, such as how to put their coats on by themselves

What happens when my child turns three?

They will be able to take up a free place for 15 hours a week for 38 weeks a year in a school nursery class or with an approved childcare provider. Your child may be able to stay where they have been when they were two. For information on how to apply for a nursery place, visit the nursery admissions webpage

I'm worried about my child going to preschool

Some parents feel that their toddlers are too young to start preschool at the age of two. The Early Learning Together programme is a friendly, gentle way to introduce your child to their preschool before they start their free Early Learning place.

With Early Learning Together, you stay with your child at preschool. You can play and explore together and get an understanding of exactly what their day will be like at preschool.

To find out more about the Early Learning Together programme, please do contact us.

How do I make a complaint?

If you are unhappy that your child may not be receiving their free entitlement in the way that it is published on this website, please see the complaints procedure (0.1MB) for more information.

To start a formal review, you will need to complete a Stage Two Form (80kB) (as specified in the complaints procedure above).
