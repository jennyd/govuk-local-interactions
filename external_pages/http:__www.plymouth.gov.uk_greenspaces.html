Parks, nature and green spaces

Did you know that over a third of Plymouth is green space? That includes parks, allotments, nature reserves, cemeteries and those neighbourhood greens at the end of your road.

These are important places for people to relax, enjoy nature, take children to play or for sport or recreation. They are essential for the health and well-being of the city.

We look after and develop 950 hectares of these open spaces within the city, working to provide a better cleaner, safer, greener environment and quality of life for the residents of Plymouth. Best of all our parks and open spaces are free to access and use!
