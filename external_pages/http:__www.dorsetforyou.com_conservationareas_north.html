Conservation areas - North Dorset

Listen

North Dorset currently has 47 Conservation Areas and over 2500 listed buildings which have been designated to cover the most historically and architecturally important and interesting parts of the District's towns and villages.

Find out more information on the conservation areas by downloading the relevant leaflet. As part of its statutory duties North Dorset District Council is currently undertaking an on-going review of the District's Conservation Areas.

The quality and interest of an area will be assessed by examining the following matters:

  • Historic layout of boundaries and routes
  • Characteristic materials and forms
  • Mix of uses
  • Contribution of recent developments
  • Hard and soft landscaping

Conservation area designation recognises the individual importance and interrelationship of all these factors. The Wyke Conservation Area Appraisal (pdf, 5Mb) (opens in a new window) and  Tarrant Valley Conservation Area Appraisal (pdf, 1009kb) (opens in a new window) are supporting documents that helped us to add new conservation areas to our current List of Conservation Areas (pdf, 17kb) (opens in a new window).

Kington Magna conservation area consultation

We have commenced consultation on a review of the Kington Magna conservation area. A draft appraisal has been produced following discussions with the Parish Council and the document was launched at a public consultation event held on Friday and Saturday 14 and 15 June. The consultation period will run until the 2 August. Any comments should be made to the council's Environment and Community Services at environment@north-dorset.gov.uk or to Sally Fisher at the address shown below.

New conservation area appraisal

We have recently been working with Milton Abbas Parish Council on a review of the Milton Abbas Conservation Area. We now have an adopted appraisal of the Milton Abbas Conservation Area (pdf, 5Mb) (opens in a new window)  The process of adoption and consultation also identified the desire and need for an Article 4 direction which controls certain types of development which are normally permitted without the need for planning permission within The Street. Article 4 Direction and plan (pdf, 1Mb) (opens in a new window)
