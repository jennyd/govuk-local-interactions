Paying for my support with a direct payment

If you meet the eligibility criteria to receive funding for your care and support, or support if you are a carer, you can choose to pay for services directly using money paid to you by us.

A direct payment is one way of using your personal budget. 

  • Visit our personal budgets page to find out more

    Advantages of direct payments

You decide who provides you with care and support and how and when you receive your care or support.

    Can I get a direct payment?

You can receive a direct payment if:

  • You are eligible to receive care services from us
  • You receive funding from us
  • You are willing and able to manage your own care and support as well as your finances (we will give you help and support with all the arrangements)

    I'm interested! What happens next?

  • If you want to talk to someone about what’s involved, telephone the direct payments officer on 0118 974 6809
  • If you want to go ahead, you need to talk to your care manager or contact the community services duty team, on 0118 974 6000
  • We will help with setting up a direct payment, provide information and advice and ongoing support
  • We will regularly review with you how well this is going. You may choose to stop a direct payment at any time

    What can I use direct payments for?

The money is for you to arrange the services (including equipment) which will meet your care and support needs. These will have been worked out in your assessment. You can employ someone directly or arrange for your care to be provided by an agency.

    How much money will I get?

We will talk to you about what support and assistance you may need in your everyday life - this is called an assessment. Following the assessment of what support you need, we will calculate how much money you will receive as your direct payment. 

    Do I need to make a contribution?

Most services are subject to means testing. This means that our staff will discuss your financial circumstances with you as part of a financial assessment. We will also make sure you receive all the benefits you may be entitled to. The financial assessment will show whether you need to make a contribution towards paying for your support.

This contribution is the same whether we arrange the services for you or whether you receive a direct payment.

    How will the direct payment be made?

The money will be paid monthly into a bank account of your choice. You will need to set up a bank account specifically for your direct payment.

    Can someone else manage my direct payment for me?

Yes, if you want you can nominate someone else to manage your direct payment. It is best if this person is involved throughout the care planning process. This will help give them a clear understanding of what is involved. This person is known as a nominated person. An authorised person is someone who agrees to manage a direct payment for a person who lacks capacity.

    How will my direct payments be monitored?

At the start we will monitor direct payments monthly to make sure that there are no problems. We will then monitor quarterly to check that everything is being done properly. We will need to see a range of evidence, such as bank statements, timesheets, receipts, employers insurance and any other relevant document.

    What can't I use direct payments for?

  • You may not use direct payments to pay a spouse, partner or close relative living in the same household for the care they provide, unless that person is someone who you have specifically recruited to be a live-in employee (other than in exceptional circumstances, which we may agree with you)
  • You cannot use direct payments to pay for permanent residential accommodation
