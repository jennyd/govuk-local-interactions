Appealing against a penalty charge notice
Level of charges

There are two levels of penalty charges, higher and lower, dependant on how serious the parking contravention is.

Examples of higher level

  • on street - parking on double yellow lines
  • off street - parking in a permit bay without clearly displaying a valid permit

Examples of lower level

  • on street - parking for longer than you are allowed
  • off street - parking after the amount of time you have paid for has expired

View the full list of contraventions higher and lower contraventions (PDF 21KB) for on and off street parking.

The charges for these different levels, plus other charges if you do not pay a parking fine, are as follows:

Higher level penalty charge: £70 (£35 - if paid within 14 days from the date of the PCN)

Lower level penalty: £50  (£25 - if paid within 14 days from the date of the PCN)

Court registration fees:View the fee schedule at legislation.gov.uk

Bailiff fees:View the bailiff fee schedule


These charges are set by the Secretary of State for Transport. Two different bands of charges were made available and councils were able to select which band they thought would encourage drivers to obey the parking rules. Central Bedfordshire Council chose band two. The charges set out in both bands can be seen below:

Band 1 - Higher level penalty charge - £60, lower level penalty charge - £40.
Band 2 - Higher level penalty charge - £70, lower level penalty charge - £50.

Avoid getting a penalty charge

Do

  • always check road markings and time plates adjacent to where you have parked. Even if you have parked in that place before you should always check the markings and signs as the restrictions may have changed or the parking bay may be suspended. Time plates are only relevant to the side of the road they are on
  • have the correct money for the pay and display machine
  • check the times when parking is allowed
  • check that your pay and display ticket, residents permit is properly displayed
  • check when parking in a bay, the wheels of your vehicle must all be within the markings of one parking bay

Don't

  • leave your vehicle to go and get change for a pay and display machine
  • leave more than one ticket on display, always remove expired ones. Pay and display tickets are not transferable
  • ignore a PCN if you receive one. It will not go away

Disabled badge holders

Blue disabled badges should be displayed on the dashboard so the date of expiry, issuing authority and serial number are clearly visible from outside of the vehicle.

The time clock must be set at the time of arrival, even if there is no time limit for disabled parking at the location.

There are various restrictions on which a disabled badge does not allow parking.
