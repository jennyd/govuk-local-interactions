Street naming and numbering

Applying for a new postal address.

Scarborough Borough Council has a number of responsibilities for street naming and property numbering, including:

  • assigning street names to new and existing streets
  • assigning property numbers to new and existing properties
  • assigning property name to new and existing properties
  • maintaining the definitive record (Local Land and Property Gazetteer) of all street names and property addresses.

The appropriate naming and numbering of streets and buildings is vitally important to ensure that properties can be located quickly and efficiently by emergency and postal services in order to prevent occupiers experiencing problems.

Applications should be made by individuals or developers who are:

  • building new houses, commercial or industrial premises,
  • undertaking conversions of existing residential, commercial or industrial premises which will result in the creation of new properties or premises,
  • wishing to add/change a property name or address, or
  • wishing to confirm the correct address of a property.

Applications for new addresses should be submitted as soon as possible after permission for the proposal has been granted. This is important, as utility companies are often reluctant to install services where an official postal address has not been allocated.

Paper applications for naming and numbering can be made by completing the forms below. A site location and a layout plan should be attached with the form. The completed form should be sent  to Planning Services, Scarborough Borough Council, Town Hall, St Nicholas Street, Scarborough, YO11 2HG or email to planning.services@scarborough.gov.uk
