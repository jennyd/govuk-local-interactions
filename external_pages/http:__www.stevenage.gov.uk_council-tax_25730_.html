Council tax discount

Discounts

The basic Council Tax bill assumes that two adults reside in a dwelling. If there are more than two resident adults the bill is not increased. However, if there are less than two adults the bill is reduced on the following basis:

  • If there is one resident adult, 25% discount is given.

Certain groups of people may help the liable person to qualify for a discount.

The people in these groups are said to be “disregarded for discount purposes”. These groups are:

Disregarded persons

If you wish to apply for a discount for any of the following categories please download an application form

  • Full-time students and student nurses at colleges - please obtain a student certificate from your place of study for all students in the property. Please send the certificates onto the address shown on the right.
  • Patients who are being looked after in care homes;
  • Care workers working on low pay for, or introduced by, charities or local authorities;
  • People caring for someone with a disability who is not a spouse, partner or child under 18 in receipt of certain qualifying benefits; 
  • People staying in certain hostels or night shelters - please contact us for details of how to apply.
  • Members of visiting forces and certain international institutions;
  • Members of religious communities (monks and nuns);
  • People in prison (except those in prison for non-payment of Council Tax or a Fine);
  • Patients resident in hospital;
  • People who are severely mentally impaired;
  • 18 and 19 year olds, who are at, or just left school and still in receipt of child benefit

If a dwelling is wholly occupied by disregarded persons, a 50% discount is given.

If a dwelling is occupied by disregarded persons and one further adult who is not disregarded, a 25% discount is given.

If a dwelling is occupied by disregarded persons and at least two other adults, who are not disregarded, no discount is given.

If you have been given a discount on your bill the council will periodically send you a form to confirm whether it still applies. If you do not return the form your discount may be cancelled.

If your bill shows that a discount has been given, you must tell the council of any changes that affect your entitlement, within 21 days of the change.

If there are any changes to your circumstances that could affect your entitlement please download a form, available via the links on the right and return to us as soon as possible.
