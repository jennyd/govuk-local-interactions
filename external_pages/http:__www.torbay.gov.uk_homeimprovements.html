Home Improvements and Adaptations

Improvements and Adapations to a Registered Provider's Property

Tenants of registered providers have what is known as a 'secure tenancy'. This means as a tenant you have the right to carry out improvements to your home, such as fitting a new bathroom suite, replacing kitchen units or building a new fireplace, provided that you first get permission from the registered provider.  To apply for permission to carry out your own alterations, contact them and provide full details of what you propose to do.
Each application is considered separately and permission is usually given, subject to any conditions that may be appropriate. They will only refuse permission if the work would cause them expense, make it difficult to let the property in the future or give rise to complaints from other residents.
If permission is not given they will write to you, giving you the reasons. If you are not satisfied with the decision you have the right to appeal. Appeals should be sent to the registered provider in the first instance, and then to the County Court if you are still not satisfied.  You also have the right to appeal if you think that the conditions under which permission is given are unreasonable.
You must not start any improvement work until written permission has been given by the registered provider.

Modernisations Schemes for a Registered Provider's Property

This type of improvement is the most extensive type of improvement carried out by the registered providers and is carried out throughout the year.  These properties have usually been identified as needing modernisation works to bring them up to modern day standards. Extensive consultation is carried out with tenants before work commences.  The work carried out will generally include:
  • A rewire of the property
  • New upvc windows
  • New external doors
  • New kitchen units
  • New bathroom fittings
  • Energy efficiency measures
In addition the tenant can choose work up to a certain monetary value. This could include flooring in the kitchen or bathroom; internal doors; or structural alterations to the layout of the property.  For further information on modernisation schemes contact your registered providers.

Adaptations for Disabled People

The Council works in partnership with Aster Living to provide adaptations to suit the needs of disabled people or people with mobility problems. These can include providing a rail, lever taps, ramp, shower or stairlift.
For further information on housing adaptions for disabled people contact your occupational health therapist or GP.
