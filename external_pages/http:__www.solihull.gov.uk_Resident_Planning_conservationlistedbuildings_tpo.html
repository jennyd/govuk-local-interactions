Protected trees in Solihull

To conserve the unique natural environment of Solihull, many of our trees are protected by Tree Preservation Orders (TPOs). Any works to trees within a Conservation Area also requires consent from the Council before going ahead.

Tree Preservation Order consent

If you wish to undertake felling or any form of pruning to any protected tree you need to submit a treeworks application form.

To find out if a tree in your area is subject to a TPO please call 0121 704 8008 or contact us by email at planning@solihull.gov.uk.

To request a copy of a TPO please call 0121 704 6013 or email landcharges@solihull.gov.uk.

To find out if your home is in a Conservation Area, you can check your postcode on our online maps. Simply select ‘Conservation Area’ from the list of options on the left hand side of the screen when you have found your home.

Local Validation for tree works consent

In addition to the application for consent to tree works, a structural damage report may be required. This must come from a professional arboriculturalist or structural surveyor. All works must be clearly accounted for and quantified. For guidance, please refer to Trees in Relation to Construction and see our Local Validation Checklist.

What are TPOs?

Designated by us, we issue TPOs to protect any tree adding value to its local environment. A TPO can apply to a single tree, a group of trees or a woodland.

To request protection for a tree in your area please call 0121 704 8008 or contact us by email at planning@solihull.gov.uk.

We'll need to be satisfied that there's a reasonable degree of public benefit before serving a TPO.

Report damage to a protected tree

A criminal offence, wilful destruction of a TPO tree without consent carries a maximum fine of £20,000. We urge anyone with information to let us know immediately about the risk of damage to, destruction of or damage to a TPO Tree.

Report damage to a protected tree

Are high hedges covered under protection orders?

High hedges are not subject to TPOs. If you're having issues with a high hedge there are steps you can take, but, as a last resort, you can request High Hedge intervention from us.

Report a dead or dangerous tree

You can report a dead or dangerous tree by calling 0121 704 8000.

We can only attend to trees on Council owned land.
