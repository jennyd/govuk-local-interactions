Children in entertainment

Many children enjoy taking part in performances and their parents enjoy supporting them.

However, there are laws to protect all children who take part in a performance from birth until they reach statutory school leaving age.

These laws exist to to ensure that their experience is enjoyable, safe and rewarding.

  • Chaperones for children in entertainment

    All children under the statutory school leaving age involved in any performance will always need to be accompanied by a registered chaperone, if they are not with their parents.

  • Child performance licence

    There are strict laws and regulations governing children who take part in performances.

  • Child modelling

    Sunderland City Council aims to issue full licences for each performance or 'shoot' as they are required.

  • Exemption for unlicensed performances

    Exemptions where a Child Performance Licence is not required - Regulation 37(3)(a)

  • Does the child require a licence?

    Information about when (or not) a child performance licence is required.

  • Permitted hours

    Maximum number of days and hours a child is permitted to perform without a break.

    Information about schools and learning including support and intervention for schools, safeguarding in schools, and special educational needs.
