Noise nuisances

In the first instance we recommend that you talk directly to the person who is causing the noise. Mediation can also be very useful.

You can also take your own action following our simple guidelines. Please see Factsheet 26 - Noise Nuisance - Taking your own legal action in the documents section.

Or you can contact us info@preston.gov.uk or 01772 906907.

We also operate an out of hours noise reporting service between the hours of:

  • 7.00pm - 1.00am Sunday - Thursday
  • 7.00pm - 2.00am Friday - Saturday

Out of hours number: 01772 794728.

What we will do

Environmental Health staff can investigate noise nuisance on your behalf and if necessary take action against persons causing the nuisance.

Domestic noise

For domestic noise such as from loud music see Factsheet 27 - Loud music.

Barking dogs

We deal with all aspects of noise nuisance and this includes noise caused by barking dogs.

If you are concerned about the noise caused by a neighbour's dog then you can either:

  • Speak directly to your neighbour and explain your concerns. You may find they are unaware of the problem and will co-operate to resolve the situation;
  • Alternatively, you can contact us and we will deal with your complaint info@preston.gov.uk 01772 906907

Noise from pubs and clubs

Many pubs, restaurants and clubs in the city play music for their customers, and some of them are licensed for entertainment. We will deal with noise problems from such premises either as a noise nuisance or as a breach of license conditions.

Car and intruder alarms

In the case of car alarms, we try to find the owner, but failing that we serve a notice and arrange for a contractor to disable the alarm. For intruder alarms, we also serve a notice but before we can enter the premises to disable the alarm we must obtain a magistrates' warrant, and this adds to the time taken to deal with the problem.

If you have an intruder alarm installed, you are required by law to:

  • ensure that it meets BS 4737 for the installation, operation and maintenance of the alarm
  • ensure the alarm has a 20 minute cut-off device fitted
  • notify the council about the installation. The alarm company will give you the appropriate form to notify the police of two key holders. If not, you can contact us for the forms - info@preston.gov.uk, 01772 906907.

Construction site noise

Noise from demolition or construction operations is inevitable, but does not usually last for a long period. Our approach with this type of noise is to try to minimise the disturbance by restricting the hours of working and ensuring the quietest methods are used. The hours that are normally permitted for operations which cause noise audible at the site boundary are 8.00am - 6.00pm on Monday to Friday, and 8.00am - 1.00pm on Saturday and no time on a Sunday or Bank Holidays.

There can sometimes be exceptions to these hours when special circumstances demand that work is done at other times. For example, work on railway tracks or stations can only be done when there are no trains; if heavy plant needs to be brought on site the police sometimes insist on it being done on a Sunday to reduce interference with traffic. In such cases, we encourage contractors to notify local residents in advance.

General

We do not have powers to deal with aircraft, railway and road traffic noise or general noise in the street from people shouting etc.

Housing providers have additional powers to deal with noise problems that arise from rented accommodation. 

Where older houses have been converted into flats, the lack of proper sound insulation between flats often gives rise to intrusive noise. Residents in these types of premises may have to be more tolerant of 'living' noise i.e. domestic cleaning, voices, doors closing. 

Keeping records of the noise nuisance

In majority of instances if you contact Environmental Health to request that they investigate your complaint, you will be asked to complete records to support your allegations. On this page is a downloadable Noise Nuisance Diary Sheet which you can fill out ready for us for a speedier response. There are also additional diary sheets available if you run out of space.  Additional information on completing a record sheet can be found on Factsheet 25 - Completing a Nuisance diary sheet.
