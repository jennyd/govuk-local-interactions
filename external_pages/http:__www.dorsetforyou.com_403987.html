Older people - supported housing

Listen

Information about sheltered housing, extra care housing and the Dorset Accessible Homes Service.

Older People's Housing Plan 2013- 2018

The Older People's Housing Plan 2013 - 2018 (pdf, 1Mb) (opens in a new window) sets the strategic direction for the development of accommodation based services for older people.

The plan is complemented by an Action Plan (pdf, 73kb) (opens in a new window) which is monitored by the Older Peoples Housing Strategy Group (OPHSG).

Sheltered housing

Sheltered Housing is ideal for older people who are largely independent, but need some help and the security of having someone close at hand.  Most sheltered housing schemes consist of 20 to 40 self-contained flats or bungalows, often with communal areas, such as a lounge and garden and organised social activities.  They usually have a scheme manager who may live on site and an alarm system to provide 24-hour emergency help.

A review of housing support services provided for older people began in July 2014. These services include the sheltered housing services currently provided to over 5,000 households at an annual cost of about £1.5m. The outcome of the review will be agreement on the range of services to be provided from October 2016.

During early 2015 there was widespread engagement with sheltered housing residents.  The outcome of this engagement was a  housing support options report (pdf, 237kb) (opens in a new window) and a housing support options summary (pdf, 1Mb) (opens in a new window).

Extra care housing

Extra care housing is a development of sheltered housing and aims to be an alternative to residential care. It is designed with the needs of frailer older people in mind. Both care and support is available on site 24 hours a day and a midday meal is available.

Following widespread consultation with partners, including older people, housing authorities and housing associations, the council published a new  Extra Care Housing Strategy (pdf, 1Mb) (opens in a new window) in November 2014.

There are extra care housing schemes in Blandford, Christchurch, Portland and Weymouth.  Subject to planning permission, a new scheme is currently being developed in Poundbury, Dorchester.

Consultation on a review of charging for extra care services is taking place during the summer 2015.

For further information contact Robin James, Service Development Officer

Dorset Accessible Homes Service

The Dorset Accessible Homes Service is locally based within offices in Dorchester and Blandford Forum. It works on behalf of Dorset County Council and is available to anyone in the Dorset County Council area.

As well as providing practical help with home adaptations, repairs and improvements, the service offers free advice, support and information on:

  • checking whether you are entitled to any financial help
  • your housing options
  • deciding on home adaptations that might help you
  • carrying out minor repairs
  • making your home more energy efficient.

For more information, please see our  My life my care (opens in a new window) website.

Housing strategy groups

The following groups oversee the development of strategy in this area:

  • Older Peoples Housing Strategy Group
  • Home Improvement Agency Strategy and Contract Monitoring Group

For information about any of these strategy groups please contact Robin James, Service Development Officer.
