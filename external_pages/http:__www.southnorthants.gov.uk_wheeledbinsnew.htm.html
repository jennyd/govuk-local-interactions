Wheeled Bins

The recycling and waste scheme in South Northamptonshire provides most households with three wheeled bins:

  • a black one for normal rubbish
  • a blue one for all recycling, and 
  • a green one for compostable garden waste. 

You also have two silver caddies for food waste collection.

If you have lots of garden waste you can have an extra green bin for £30 a year. Please give us a call to arrange this.

If you regularly have lots of recycling then you can have an extra blue bin for a one off cost of £30. Please give us a call and we will deliver one.

Please download our quick reference guide (gif format) to check what goes in each bin.

Blue Recycling Bins

  • Try to keep your bin clean. 
  • Please put your recycling loose in the bin, not in plastic bags. 
  • Don't put anything in your bin that cannot be recycled.
  • Squash large plastic bottles and cardboard so that you can fit more in your bin.


What to put in the blue bin:

 


Mixed Plastics

Foil


Cartons (Tetrapaks)


Cans & Tins


Cardboard


Newspapers, Magazines & Paper (inc. shredded paper and wrapping paper)


Glass Bottles & Jars


Aerosols

 

On Top of Your Bin

Batteries - You can recycle your batteries in a small bag on top of your blue bin. Our crews will collect them and keep them separate on the vehicle, and then recycle them.

Small Electrical items - You can recycle these in a carrier bag in top of your blue bin now. Any electrical items from the home can be be put in a carrier bag and left on top of the blue bin to be recycled.
 


Sweet and crisp wrappers
Cling film
Childrens' toys
Carrier bags
Other non-recyclable waste (e.g. disposable nappies, dog or cat waste).


CD / DVD cases
Polystyrene
Pyrex glass
Sheet glass
Kitchen waste (e.g. meat, vegetable peelings) - these should go in your food caddy.

 

Food Waste Caddies

You should have two food caddies (one outdoor and one indoor) to use for your food waste collection. Please visit our food waste collection webpage to find out how to use the service. 

Green Bins

  • Try to keep your bin clean. 
  • Don't put any of the waste in plastic bags. 
  • Don't put anything in your bin that cannot be composted.
  • Don't put cardboard in your green bin.

What to put in the green bin:

 

Garden rubbish - Grass cuttings, small branches, hedge clippings, weeds, prunings
Other rubbish (e.g. pet straw, untreated sawdust and wood shavings, cut flowers, cold ash)

 

Kitchen waste (e.g. meat, plastic bags, cooking fat and oil, food leftovers and peelings) - these should go into your food caddy
Cardboard - please put in your blue bin
Yellow Pages - please put in your blue bin
Garden waste (e.g. soil, plant pots, stones, rubble)
Other waste (e.g. recycling, general rubbish)

Black Bins

Your black bin is for all other non-recyclable household waste. 

  • Wrap waste in bags before putting it in your bin. Old carrier bags are excellent for this.
  • Try to keep your bin clean.
  • Do not overload your bin or make too heavy

Heavy materials such as large amounts of soil, stones, rubble or car parts
Electrical appliances (please recycle on top of your blue bin)
Liquids such as oil or paint
Scrap metal, asbestos or chemicals 

 

If there is a piece of rubbish you want to recycle and it's not listed here, please check the A-Z of Waste on the Recycle for Northamptonshire website. 

Bin sizes

  • View bin size guide

If you wish to request a larger or smaller bin of any colour, please contact us. Smaller bins are available free of charge. Larger bins are available free of charge under certain circumstances (e.g. for a large family), or can be provided for an annual fee.
