Hazardous waste

How residents can dispose of hazardous waste such as asbestos, paint, batteries and chemicals.

Businesses, please see Dispose of Hazardous Waste: Advice for Businesses (GOV.UK website)

Asbestos

The City of London can collect a limited amount of asbestos for free: see Hazardous waste collection (City of London website). This service is subsidised by Merton Council.

There are a number of different types of asbestos. If you are not sure what type of asbestos you have, you should call a specialist contractor. For example, we will not accept blue asbestos, which is particularly hazardous.

Asbestos of the type commonly found in garage and shed roofs, which is usually found in corrugated sheets and is grey in colour, is the cement-bound variety. You can take cement-bound asbestos to the Reuse and Recycling Centre, Garth Road, Morden for safe disposal. Please contact the site before you visit on 020 8337 8998 to ensure that they have space in their container to accept it.

You should:

  • Take care to try not to break up the asbestos sheet
  • Dampen the sheet with water if it appears to be dusty
  • Double wrap the sheets in heavy duty plastic if large or double bag smaller bits
  • On entering the site inform a member of staff that you are bringing in asbestos, they will show you were to put it.
  • Take either a driving licence or recent council tax bill with you as proof of address. Only asbestos from residential properties in the borough will be accepted. We do not accept any from commercial premises, please contact a specialist contractor.

To make an asbestos enquiry online use the street scene enquiry form.

Car batteries

You can dispose of car batteries at the Reuse and recycling centre, Garth Road, Morden.

Car oil

You can dispose of used car oil at the Reuse and recycling centre, Garth Road, Morden. The oil will be recycled.

Chemicals

The City of London can collect a limited amount of chemicals for free: see Hazardous waste collection (City of London website). This service is subsidised by Merton Council.

Alternatively, call the Reuse and recycling centre, Garth Road, Morden to see if we will take them: 020 8337 8998 Or call the Environment Agency for advice on 0800 807 060 (incident hotline) or 08708 506 506 (general enquiries) for safe disposal of pesticides, poisons and other hazardous waste.

Fluorescent tubes and energy saving bulbs

Low-energy light bulbs need to be recycled as they are classed as hazardous waste. They contain minute quantities of mercury, normally no more than 4mg per bulb which in larger quantities could pose a risk to the environment, and therefore must be recycled sustainably. These Â along with fluorescent tubes can be taken to the Reuse and recycling centre, Garth Road, Morden.

Gas bottles

Where possible please try to return these to the supplier. You may dispose of small numbers of gas bottles at the Reuse and recycling centre, Garth Road, Morden. We accept LPG gas bottles such as "Calor" or "Flow-gas".

Paint

The City of London can collect a limited amount of paint for free: see Hazardous waste collection (City of London website). This service is subsidised by Merton Council.
