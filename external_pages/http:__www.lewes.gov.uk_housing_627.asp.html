Homes which offer support for older residents to assist them to live independently.

Sheltered housing is a group of unfurnished flats, studio apartments and bungalows designed for the needs of older people. The aim is to provide comfortable, convenient and secure accommodation whilst encouraging residents to maintain full independence.

The accommodation is for people of retirement age. There is no upper age limit. Residents may need to continue receiving the support they already get from relatives and friends.

All homes have an intercom connected to an alarm system where assistance is available in an emergency. Properties are usually let on secure tenancies but tenants do not have the âRight to Buyâ.

Sheltered schemes often contain facilities such as a communal lounge and laundry room. However each studio apartment, flat or bungalow is self contained with its own entrance for residents to come and go as they wish.

In addition all homes have a smoke detector. Some residents may qualify for visiting services such as home care, community nurse, chiropody or meals on wheels via Social Services or their GP. Many of our residents live independent lives but are reassured by the background service of the emergency alarm and visiting Scheme Manager service. The accommodation is centrally heated, double glazed and insulated. It has social, recreational and leisure activities all in a safe environment.

Heating and lighting costs are included in the rental of properties in most purpose built blocks of sheltered flats, so residents do not have to worry about separate fuel accounts. Charges are made within the rent for the communal alarm system and, where applicable, for the cleaning of communal areas and the Scheme Manager service.
In bungalows, residents are responsible for their own heating and lighting costs. A service charge still forms part of the rent for communal facilities of the alarm system and Scheme Manager service.

Dogs and cats are allowed in bungalows. Dogs are not allowed in flats and cats are only allowed on the ground floor.

Bungalows and Flats

We have a mixture of sheltered flats and bungalows throughout the District. However, it is worth bearing in mind that whilst residents receive the same services of a visiting Scheme Manager in both types of accommodation, some people may find living in a bungalow slightly more isolated than living in one of our flats within a sheltered scheme.
In the flats all the facilities are to hand without going outside. It is short walk to the communal lounge and laundry room and it is easy to meet up with other residents whilst using the facilities or at an event such as a lunch or film night.

In contrast at bungalow sites, facilities such as laundry room and residentâs lounge are sited in the vicinity and residents need to leave their home to walk outside to the communal facilities which can sometimes be a few streets away.

Many of our bungalows have access to garden areas and tenants are responsible for the upkeep of individual plots. This can be challenging for some people and is worth bearing this in mind when considering your options. 

Scheme Managers will offer events and chances to meet others in both types of accommodation, but it is important to also think about your mobility and your interest in social activities when bidding for sheltered properties. 

Some schemes have limited storage facilities for mobility scooters and you are advised to check availability before purchasing or considering a move to a new home. See separate section on Mobility Scooters.

Eligibility

To apply complete a housing application form. This will then be assessed. Each organisation has its own criteria for acceptance of housing applications and most operate a waiting list procedure.

You do not have to have a set number of points in order to be housed. A property is allocated to the person with the greatest housing need at the time it becomes available. Access times can be relatively short.
