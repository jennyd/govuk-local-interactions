Post-16 transport support

Listen

Arrangements to assist post 16-students with the daily travel to and from school or college of further education.

If you are attending a college of further education, Dorset Local Authority's support arrangements are:

  • In approved cases, students following a full-time further education course aged between 16 and 25 and with particular medical or learning difficulties qualify for free transport to their nearest designated/appropriate college; some additional criteria may also apply
  • To provide a grant to each of the seven local and nearby colleges to help towards transport costs of those students living five miles or more from the nearest/designated college, including those whose family (or the student) receives income support, job seeker's allowance (income-based) or receives income up to a certain level (15,860 as of 2011) and receives child tax credit and/or working tax credit.

With the exception of the above groups of students, colleges have funded the development of their own arrangements for transporting students into their college and helping students with the cost. Many colleges use public transport supplemented by contract vehicles. Further enquiries should be made to the college concerned.

For further information please see our post 16 transport policy and GOV.UK further information on 16-19 transport subsidies (opens in a new window).

Other sources of support

Students aged 16-19 may be eligible for funding if their circumstance requires them to live in accommodation near to their place of education. For example due to where they live, or to attend a  course which isn't available close by. This is available from the national residential support scheme (opens in a new window) provided by Skills Funding Agency (opens in a new window). You should contact your school or college for more information about the accommodation grant.

If you are attending a college of further education, you may be eligible to apply for support towards transport cost, depending on whether you meet the criteria. This is provided by the Discretionary Support fund and is administered by your college. To find out if you qualify, and for more information contact your college.
