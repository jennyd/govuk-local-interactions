What is a Carer Assessment and how can it help me?

A Carer Assessment is your chance to tell us about the things that could make caring easier for you.

Through the Carer Assessment we will be able to discuss the different types of services that may be available to support you.

This is not an assessment of how well you are caring. It looks at the support you might need to help you continue in your caring role, and to look at the things you think are important to your life and wellbeing.

The focus of the assessment is on needs rather than identifying the amount of money required. However your assessment could result in you having financial support.

We will assess the information you provide to see if any support can be provided that will help to meet your needs.

If you are eligible for funded support from Adult Social Care, you will be provided with a Support Plan which may include a Personal Budget, paid as aDirect Payment.

Will I have to pay for a Carer Assessment?

No, you will not have to pay for your assessment.

How will the council decide what help I can receive?

We will use National Carer Eligibility Criteria to determine who will receive support from Adult Social Care.

Carers who do not meet eligibility criteria will still be provided with information and advice and signposted to services which may help their situation. This might also include a small amount of funding to provide short break activities or equipment.

You may complete the Carers Pack[pdf - 250kb] and assessment tool yourself, or with support from Voluntary Sector Services, or someone from Social Care.

How much might I get through a Direct payment?

The amount you will receive will depend upon the assessment of your needs. Each person's needs are looked at individually.

What can the Carers Support Fund or Direct Payment be used for?

That depends on what you feel would be of most benefit to you and what might help you in your caring role. Here are some examples:

  • relaxation therapy sessions at a local health centre
  • help with housework or gardening
  • a laundry or ironing service so that you have time to relax
  • respite care to enable you to have a break
  • a break away from home

Sometimes, services provided to the person you care for can help you too, such as breaks at a day centre or respite care.

Is there anything I can't use the Carers Support Fund or Direct Payment for?

You can't use your payment to pay for Adult Social Care services that should be provided to the person you care for.

They will need to be assessed for services in their own right. You can't use your carers Direct Payment to buy services or equipment which should be paid for by the Health Service.

You can't use your carers Direct Payment to pay normal household bills.

Will a Direct Payment affect my benefits?

No. Direct Payments are one way of responding to what you need as a carer. They are not classed as income and cannot be taken into account when you are assessed for any welfare benefits.

We can help you find out what benefits you are entitled to.

How will my Direct Payment be paid to me?

We would like to pay this directly into your bank account and will ask for this information within the Carers Pack[pdf - 250kb].

If you are unable to receive the payment this way, we may be able to arrange for a cheque to be sent in the post.

Carer eligibility criteria


In April 2015, a new National Eligibility Criteria was introduced, to ensure all Carers who meet the criteria are provided with support from Adults Social Care. All Carers who are assessed as eligible for support from Adult Social Care will be offered advice and guidance on the best services to meet their needs, which will outlined in a Support Plan to explain how those needs will be met. A Personal Budget may be provided for some services which require funding.

This applies to Carers who provide necessary care, or are intending to provide care, so that hospital discharge and reablement procedures should take account of Carers and ensure they are involved in discharge and support planning.

Find out more about the carer eligibility criteria

Can I get help from anyone else?

There are voluntary organisations and groups in South Tyneside who offer help to carers on things such as:

  • information and advice
  • support groups
  • counselling
  • befriending
  • advocacy (supporting carers at formal and informal meetings)
  • social events
  • sitting service
  • newsletters/forums

What do I do if my situation changes?

We know that your situation can change at any time. We would like you to call us if you have problems or if you are no longer able, or willing, to carry out your caring tasks.
