Community Development

The purpose of Community Development is to build strong, active, resilient and sustainable communities based on mutual respect, social justice, equality and inclusion.

What we do

There are five key values that underpin all Community Development practice:

  • Equality and Anti-discrimination
  • Social Justice
  • Collective Action
  • Community Empowerment
  • Working and Learning Together

It is based on the notion that the solutions to local problems can be found inside the community.  Therefore, the aim is to build skills and confidence and support individuals and communities to identify and articulate their needs and then develop their own solutions.

There are numerous barriers which can prevent people from participating in many aspects of life, including education, finance, health or being a member of a minority group.  Community Development aims to work with all members of a community to remove the barriers that prevent them from participating.

Cultivating social capital and building a community's confidence to work with organisations and statutory authorities helps to change relationships between ordinary people and those in positions on power.  This results in the creation of active citizens who are involved in democratic process.

Community Development is a long term process.  It must be recognised that working with communities involves investing large amounts of time building trust, raising self-esteem and overcoming barriers to participation.

Why do we need Community Development?

Now, perhaps more than ever before, the importance of Community Development work is being recognised.  As Central Government continues to promote Localism there is an increasing emphasis on community control.  Increasingly communities are being encouraged to deal directly with statutory authorities and organisations.  Whilst this should be encouraged, it must also to be remembered that not all communities are equal in their capability and capacity to do this.   Community Development is essential to ensure that everyone's voice can be heard.

The current economic climate and resulting reduction in public spending brings with it the significant danger that deprived communities will decline further.  Communities need to be supported and informed to help increase their resilience and strength. 

Communities are also becoming increasingly diverse.  Community Development work is used to redress imbalance and ensure that all sections of the community are heard and considered equally. 

How does Community Development work?

Community Development Officers lead, facilitate and support a variety of work, from small scale social groups through to large, multi-agency strategic projects. Overall all projects aim to:

  • Build trust between officers and the community
  • Create opportunities for people to develop their skills and realise their potential
  • Reach out to those excluded or disadvantaged
  • Create community organisations who can represent the community and develop solutions to local issues
  • Promote dialogue between communities and agencies

 
Community Development in Taunton Deane

The Community Development Team at Taunton Deane Borough Council brings together staff with a wide range of expertise and backgrounds.  As facilitators of community development we undertake a wide variety of work.  Our team is part of Taunton Deane Borough Council's Housing Service and we work across Taunton Deane in areas where we have housing stock. 

We are heavily involved in the delivery of the Priority Areas Strategy and the Housing Revenue Account 30 Year Business Plan.  For this reason we spend most of our time working in the more deprived areas of Taunton Deane, specifically North Taunton, East Taunton, Wellington and some of the more rural areas. 

The themes of our projects can vary widely dependent of the needs of the community with which we are working.  We work with a wide variety of partners, both internally and externally.

Currently our team priorities are to support the development of communities in order to:

  • Support education and skills development
  • Remove barriers to employment
  • Promote and achieve healthier lifestyles
  • Improve the look and feel of the area
