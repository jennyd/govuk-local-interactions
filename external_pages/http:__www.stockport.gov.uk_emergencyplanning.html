Civil Resilience

This page contains useful links and information on what to do before and after an incident occurs.

There are lots of emergencies that affect the UK every year, but fortunately in Stockport and Greater Manchester as a whole, they don’t happen often.

Whilst we can’t prevent emergencies happening, we can plan ahead to help reduce the impact.

Flooding

Following the December 2015 floods which affected many parts of Greater Manchester, two helpful leaflets below provide guidance on planning for flooding before it happens, what to do during a flood, and recovering and cleaning up after a flood.

You can also find some useful flooding fact sheets on the Greater Manchester Prepared website.

Flooding - Advice for the public (PDF 1,404Kb)

Flooding - How to clean up your house safely (PDF 191Kb)

Weather Preparation

The Met Office has produced a series of information guides outlining the practical steps you can take to support your local community to prepare for severe weather. For further information, visit the Met Office website.

You can find information about the Council’s gritting operation by visiting our gritting web pages. You can also find more helpful information about driving in winter weather conditions on the Greater Manchester Prepared website.

Many people don't know who to contact in the event of a power cut. You can find more information about this and how to prepare by reading the Power cuts - get ready for winter document.

Winter Wellbeing

Get Ready for Winter

Get ready for winter campaign is a partnership to encourage individuals, families and communities to think about winter preparations to help them stay warm, healthy and safe. The Met Office is working the Council and other partners including the voluntary sector to offer a range of advice.

Flu Vaccinations

Flu is a highly infectious illness that spreads rapidly through the coughs and sneezes of people who are carrying the virus. Make sure you get your flu jab if you are aged 65 and over, are pregnant, have a weakened immune system, a serious medical condition or are a carer for someone. Young children aged two, three and four are also recommended to be vaccinated against flu. Your local GP can offer these for free. Find out more on our flu vaccination web page.

Public Health England

Public Health England has published a winter 2015 update of its Keep Warm Keep Well leaflet which provides useful advice for the over 60s, low-income families, and people living with a disability. Age UK and NHS has published Top tips to keep warm keep well which outlines the things that people you can do to help themselves stay healthy during cold weather. You can view and download these documents below.
