Learning and Achievement (L&A)

 

The Learning and Achievement Service (L&A) provides: 

  • Support for children and young people with special educational needs.
  • Support for children and young people who have difficulty accessing education.
  • Support for excluded children and young people and those at risk of exclusion. 

We work effectively together with carers, families and young people, providing for all of their educational and social needs.

We provide a range of support for access to education through Somerset's 'Graduated Response', in order to meet the needs of our children and young people.

You can find out more about L&A on the Somerset Learning Platform website

The website contains information about the following services and support:

  • Autism and Communication;
  • Pupil Behaviour and Attendance;
  • Children's Centres;
  • Children in Care, Electively Home Educated children, Traveller/Gypsy/Roma children, Adopted children and children in Shelters or Refuges;
  • Educational Psychology;
  • Special Educational Needs Learning Support Services;
  • Pupil Referral Units and other Alternative Provision;
  • Social Emotional Behavioural Support Service;
  • Sensory, Physical and Medical Support to enable pupils to access education;
  • Youth Services 

Somerset Children and Young People's Compact

The Somerset Children and Young People’s Compact, established in 2011, is an exciting collaboration between schools and the Local Authority. 

At the heart of the Compact is an unequivocal commitment to joint decision making and engagement with all schools and early years settings in Somerset.

It is a significant partnership involving schools, early years settings, school governors and the Local Authority, and works to help collectively shape services for the benefit of children and young people throughout our County. It is the main body for consulting and directing Somerset’s educational policy and service provision.

You can find more information about the Somerset Children and Young People’s Compact on the Somerset Learning Platform website.

Somerset Parent Partnership

The Somerset Parent Partnership Service offers independent information, advice, guidance and support for parents and carers of children with special or additional educational needs. You can find out more about this service at the Somerset Parent Partnership website.
