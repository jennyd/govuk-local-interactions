Suggestions and compliments

If you have a suggestion about how we could do things differently to improve service delivery, please let us know. It is also important that we know if you are satisfied with the quality of service provided and therefore we welcome compliments.

Please feel free to share your views with our staff in person, in writing or by using our complaints, suggestions and compliments form.

Back To Top

Complaints

If you feel we are doing something wrong or have given you a poor service we need to know about it so that we can deal with it as soon as possible. 

Tell us about it first
The quickest way of sorting things out is to contact us to give us the chance to put things right. If the matter needs further investigation you will be given a staff name and contact number and you will be contacted within 20 working days to inform you of the outcome or report progress.

If you are unhappy with our response you can make a formal complaint through our complaints procedure.

Making a formal complaint
Please remember that the more information you are able to give us, the easier it will be to investigate your complaint.

Stage one
The first thing to do is to contact the section responsible by telephone, letter, email, in person or complete the Complaints, Suggestions and Compliments form. If you prefer a member of staff will write down the complaint for you.

Stage two
At Stage 2 your complaint will be investigated by the appropriate Corporate Director.

Stage three
If you have been through Stages 1&2 and are still dissatisfied you can request that the complaint be passed to the Chief Executive to arrange for an independent senior officer to carry out an investigation.

Our promise to you

The level of service you will receive during the investigation of your complaint at Stages one - three is set out below:

  • Your complaint will be acknowledged within 3 working days. 
  • Our target is to give you a full response within 20 working days or let you know why it is not possible to achieve this together with a new target date. 
  • You will not be treated adversely as a result of making a complaint. 
  • Confidentiality - The information you provide is protected under the Data Protection Act 1998. In order to process this information your details may need to be passed to a relevant third party (the identity of a person making a complaint will only be made known to those who consider the complaint and not revealed to any other person or made public by the Council), if you do not want this information passing on, please let us know. Anonymous complaints will be investigated as far as possible in accordance with the set procedure. 
  • Where we are at fault we will do our best to put things right.
  • The Complaints, Suggestions and Compliments procedure is operated in accordance with the Council's Equalities Policy

Not satisfied with the outcome?

If at the end of the our investigation you are not satisfied with the outcome you can contact:

The Local Government Ombudsman
The Ombudsman provides an impartial and confidential service free of charge. The Ombudsman investigates allegations of maladministration causing injustice to the person who has complained - but you will be asked if your complaint could be settled locally, so you must try the Councils procedure first.

The contact details for the Ombudsman are:0845 602 1983 or write to The Local Government Ombudsman, The Oaks, No.2, Westwood Way, Westwood Business Park, Coventry CV4 8JB or visit www.lgo.org.uk.

Complaints about councillors

Changing how we deal with complaints
Our Standards Committee considers any complaints that a councillor may have breached the Code of Conduct.

What this means to you
If you want to complain about the conduct of a district councillor, or a councillor from one of our parish, town or city councils, you should send it to: The Standards Assessment Sub-Committee, c/o The Monitoring Officer
Lichfield District Council, District Council House, Frog Lane, Lichfield WS13 6YU

What type of complaint will the committee handle? 
The committee will only deal with complaints that relate to a breach of the Member's Code of Conduct. Read the Code of Conduct online.

If you feel you would like to make a complaint to the Standards Assessment Sub-Committee it must be about why you think a member has not followed the Code of Conduct.
