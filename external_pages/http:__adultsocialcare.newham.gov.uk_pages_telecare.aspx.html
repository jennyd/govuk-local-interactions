Assistive Technology and Telecare

Page Content

What is assistive technology and Telecare?

Assistive Technologies are equipment to help you do everyday activities that are difficult or have become difficult. They support you to stay safe and independent in your own home for as long as possible.

Assistive technologies include:

  • grab rails
  • raised toilet seats
  • flashing door alert
  • speaking clocks
  • personal alarms

The Audit commission, 2004 defines Assistive Technology as:

“Any item, piece of equipment, product or system that is used to increase, maintain or improve the functional capabilities and independence of people with cognitive, physical and communication difficulties”.


Telecare

Telecareis a part of Assistive Technologies. It is a range of sensor aids that offers remote support and care such as a telephone. Telecare can let others know when you need help in your home and can stop you needing more intensive support.

Telecare include personal alarms with a push button worn as a pendant around the neck or wrist.

Other sensors can now help manage the risks involved in living independently by monitoring certain changes. For example:

At home aids or equipment to detect:

  • smoke
  • raised or lower temperature

Personal careaids or equipment

  • to prevent falls (Click here for more information about falls prevention
  • unsupervised mobility for people who wander
  • for people with epilepsy or incontinence

You can also find equipment to help you in and outside your home. For example, if you have a memory problem that begins to affect everyday living and carers find it hard to care for you.


How does it work?

Personal alarms and other telecare equipment raise alerts by sending a signal through a unit called a life-line in Newham, plugged into your telephone line.

The more advanced aids use the internet, global positioning satellite (GPS) and mobile systems to report information.


How is telecare and assistive technology monitored?

Most telecare is monitored by Newham Network Telecare Service (NNW). NNW is the London Borough of Newham's in house Telecare service provider. The specialist monitoring service receives alerts from telecare equipment and will take appropriate action to support customers as needed. This may include contacting a friend or relative or calling emergency services.

They can also provide a personal response service i.e. a call centre officer coming to your home when an alert is raised. A response service is useful to customers who do not have a local key holder. Newham Network's monitoring and emergency response service is avaliable 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, every day of the year (including Bank Holidays).

Stand alone monitoring means that the alert call goes direct to a local carer. The carer needs to be available at any time of day or night to take the appropriate action required. In supported housing this person may be a member of staff.


Requesting Telecare Equipment

To find out about the service and make a referral contact:

Newham Network Telecare Service

Waddington Road
Stratford, E15 1QW
Tel: 0208 534 8626
Email: newhamnetworksupportservice@newham.gov.uk

Personal alarms are arranged and provided by Newham Network. If a need for additional sensors or equipment is identified, these will be provided by the Adult Social Care team, working in partnership with Newham Network.

For further information, about the available equipment, please contact IEDA on 020 8430 2000, selecting option2 on the menu.


How much does it cost?

There is no charge for standard telecare equipment (such as a personal alarm and extra sensor such as a smoke alarm) provided by us. However, charges do apply for monitoring services.​​
