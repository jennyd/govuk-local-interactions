Street lighting


LED Lighting Project

Energy-efficient 'smart' street lighting has been installed in Caldmore, Walsall as part of a £250,000 scheme aimed at reducing the council's carbon footprint.

View the Walsall LED Lighting project in partnership with Walsall Public Lighting Ltd

How do I report faults and problems?

The Council’s public lighting assets such as street lights and illuminated street signs are maintained under contract by Walsall Public lighting Ltd whose subcontractors are Amey LG. Please use the report form on Amey Highways website to report problems with such items. The more information you can give us the better, such as, for faulty lights, is the light out altogether, going on/off, burning all day, intermittent? If possible please include the number of the column. If you cannot see this, please state the exact location or adjacent house number.

For out of hours emergencies involving street lights or illuminated street signs, call Amey on 0800 389 1732).

How is the public lighting service set up?

The council is working in partnership with Walsall Public lighting Ltd and Amey LG  to maintain the street lighting stock, in addition to standardising the lighting in the borough over a period of 26 years, starting from April 2002 within the PFI (Private Finance Initiative) contract. The Council monitors the project to ascertain that the service is delivered as set out in the contract.

What does the street lighting PFI service provide for the public?

The service provides:

  • Improving road safety
  • Reduction of fear of crime
  • Regular maintenance of the street lighting
  • Periodic lamp change and cleaning

Bloxwich street lighting - residents survey November 2012

This invest to save initiative has seen old lanterns in an area of Bloxwich replaced with new LED lighting technology. Approximately 590 lanterns have been replaced under the scheme which started in April 2012. A state of the art central computer system enables lights to be controlled and energy consumption levels monitored.

      

Left; old sodium lights gave off an orange glow. Right; New LED lights give off a more natural white light.

Bloxwich West was selected as the first area in the borough to pilot the project as it had the largest concentration of old street lighting in the borough being over 25 years old.  In April 2012 a questionnaire was circulated to residents to ascertain the views of the public on the lighting pilot scheme. 481 people responded, a 23% response rate.

The LED lights have now been in operation for over 6 months and in early November 2012 a second follow up survey was sent to residents to find out their thoughts on the new lighting. 591 households responded, a 29% response rate.

In both the April and November surveys, questionnaires were posted to 2,000 households in the areas where the lighting was changed, (view list of the affected streets) The questionnaire asked questions about the new lighting and travel in the local area after dark. A full written report of results from the April and November surveys is now available here (972KB PDF), with Appendix 3 (60KB PDF) as a separate download.

What can I expect from the service?

We will ensure that:

  • All the street lights are maintained as necessary
  • The street lights are cleaned on a regular basis
  • Regular electrical and structural inspections are carried out on all the street lights
  • Faults that are reported are attended to as quickly as possible to provide little disruption to the service.
  • Fly posting and graffiti are removed within 24 hours of the receipt of information.

What do you expect from me?

  • To report any faults on the free phone number
  • To report any vandalism on the street lights
  • To report fly posting and graffiti

What are customers care standards?

  • We will handle your request promptly
  • We will provide an effective service that meets the latest standards
  • We will be fair, courteous, helpful, flexible and responsive in the delivery of the service regardless of your ethnicity, gender or any disability
  • We will respect your viewpoint, when providing either feedback or complaint
  • We will provide all information you require to the best of our ability
  • We will fully protect your privacy, and information about you will be treated as confidential

How can I comment on the service?

You can comment on the service:

  • Via the "Tell Us" forms available at the council
  • Online using the contact us form
  • via the Amey highways report form
  • by telephoning 0800 389 1732
