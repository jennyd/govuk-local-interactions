Pest control

Contact Response Services for professional advice from the experts.

Rats and wasps found indoors are dealt with on the same day, and most requests are dealt with within 24 hours.

All Pest Control Officers have a level 2 certificate issued by the Royal Society of Health/British Pest Control Association.

Pest Control does not get rid of foxes (but see below for information about fox problems).

Bookings

Contact Response Services on 0161 770 2244 (Option 1).

Payment

You must pay for pest control treatments before they begin.

Payment will be taken when you make a booking (by credit or debit card).

If you cannot pay over the phone, you can pay cash or cheque by visiting Response Services (cheques are payable to 'Oldham Council').

Foxes

In the UK it is illegal to poison, gas, asphyxiate, maim, stab, impale, drown or club foxes. Most forms of snaring are prohibited as well. But there are ways of deterring foxes from entering your property.

The best time of year to deter foxes is during the late summer and autumn.

You can try the following:

  • Remove potential food sources
  • Keep rubbish in your wheeled bin, and don’t leave bagged rubbish outside
  • Tidy up an overgrown garden, and remove piles of grass clippings
  • Consider removing earthworms from your garden (some products may be available from DIY or garden centres)
  • Fill gaps under garden sheds – foxes like to shelter under sheds, and can squeeze under a 10cm gap
  • Only leave bird food in bird food dispensers
  • Repair any damaged air bricks (but don’t block them up – your house needs ventilation)

If none of that works you could use a chemical animal deterrent (such as Renardine and Scoot) available from garden centres.

Or try a cat scent neutraliser such as Cat Off, which reduces the territorial marker smells from urine and faeces.
