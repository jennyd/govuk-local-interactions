New Claims for Council Tax Support

Popular Task for New Council Tax Support Claims

 

 Make a Claim for Council Tax Support and/or Housing Benefit 

You can claim Council Tax Support if you:

  • receive income-based Jobseekers' Allowance
  • Employment Support Allowance
  • Income Support
  • Pension Credit or
  • have a low income

If you are Pension Age and receive the guaranteed element of Pension Credit, you will get the maximum amount of Council Tax Support allowable, although the income of any other adults living with may be taken into account.

 

If you are Working Age, receive income-based Jobseekers' Allowance, Income Support and are in one of the Protected Groups, you will get the maximum amount of Council Tax Support  allowable, although the income of any other adults living with may be taken into account

 

You can find out more about Jobseekers' Allowance and Income Support on the Department for Works and Pensions website.

 

For more information on Pension Credit, visit The Pension Service website.

 

If you do not get income-based Jobseeker's Allowance, Income Support, the guaranteed element of Pension Credit, the amount of Council Tax Support you get depends on:

  • how many people live with you,
  • your personal circumstances,
  • how much money you have coming in and
  • how much you have in savings.

Will I qualify?

 

You can complete our Council Tax Support Calculator to find out what your entitlement to Council Tax Support  would be.

 

To obtain an accurate determination, you should complete a Online Claim form

 

You cannot get Council Tax Support if:

 

If you or your partner are Pension Age and have over £16,000 savings between you with the exception of some people who receive the guarantee element of Pension Credit.

 

If you and your partner are Working Age and have over £6,000 savings unless you are in one of the following Protected Groups

  • You have at least one child under the age of 5 in your household
  • You receive the Disability Premium or Child Disability Premium when we calculate your weekly income allowance
  • You receive the Support Component of Employment Support Allowance
  • You receive or have underlying entitlement to Carer’s Allowance

If you and your partner are Working Age and in one of the Protected Groups and have over £16,000 savings.

 

Council Tax Support may be restricted if:

 

Other people over the age of 18 live with you, as they are expected to contribute towards the payment of the council tax (even if they don't).

 

Second Adult Rebate

 

If you are Pension Age or Working Age and in one of the Protected Groups you may be able to have your bill reduced, regardless of your own circumstances (including earnings and savings) because other people who live with you are on benefit or a low income. In such cases you should make a claim for Second Adult Rebate.

 

You cannot claim Second Adult Rebate if you are married or have a partner.

 

Appeals and Complaints

 

If you are refused Council Tax Support and you think the decision is wrong, or you think the amount of Council Tax Support has been calculated incorrectly, you can ask for the decision to be looked at again, or you can appeal. 

 

You should do this within one month of the date the decision is notified to you.

 

If you are not happy with the service you have received from the Council Tax Support section, you can complain. You can do this whether or not you are also challenging the entitlement decision. 

 

For further details regarding customer complaints see the Revenues Customer Care Standards or print and complete a Revenues Services Complaints Form (Word Doc).
