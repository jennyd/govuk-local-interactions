Settlement, nationality and citizenship

This section of our website provides information about:

  • settlement and using our settlement checking service
  • using our nationality checking service when applying for British nationality.
  • citizenship ceremonies for successful applicants for British nationality.

General advice about indefinite leave to remain in the UK or applying for British nationality

For advice and guidance on all aspects of applying for indefinite leave to stay in the UK or British citizenship, you can visit Gov.uk - nationality and citizenship. You'll also find some additional nationality and citizenship resources at the bottom of this page.

Settlement checking service

The settlement checking service is for people who are applying for settlement in the UK on form SET (M) as the husband, wife, civil partner or unmarried/same-sex partner of a British citizen or someone who is settled here. The service provides you with the opportunity to have your documents and application form checked when applying for settlement in the UK.

Nationality checking service

The nationality checking service is open to both Hackney residents and non-residents. It means that:

  • we check that your application is completed accurately and supported with the correct documents.
  • we certify and copy your documents so that you can keep the originals.

Joint nationality and passport checking service

We also offer a joint nationality and passport checking service for nationality checking service applicants who wish to apply for a British passort at the same time.

Citizenship ceremonies

If your application to become a British citizen is successful, and you are 18 or over, you are required by law to attend a citizenship cermony. 

Find out more about our citizenship ceremonies.

Nationality and citizenship resources

For more information about settlement, nationality and citizenship, you can visit

  • Gov.uk - nationality and citizenship
  • the UK Visas and Immigration, which is responsible for applications for British nationality, permission to stay in Britain, citizenship and asylum.
  • Gov.uk advice for Asylum seekers.
