Construction Waste

The amount of construction waste and rubble finding its way into our household amenity and recycling centres (HARC’s) has steadily increased in recent years.  This type of waste is not classified as household waste; therefore, it does not have to be accepted at HARC’s.  The cost of handling the increasing volumes of construction waste has become very expensive.

However, it is recognised that minor house repairs and improvements might generate small amounts of construction waste.  Therefore, each of the 3 HARC’s will accept small amounts of the material.  As a guide the amount of construction waste that will be accepted should be no more than what can be carried in two 75 litre compost bags.  Repeat visits of similar quantities of this material will not be permitted.

There are containers for small amounts of asbestos at Tythegston and Brymenyn. The asbestos should be double wrapped in thick polythene sheeting and fully sealed with strong tape. However, you will need to obtain a tipping permit, for which there may be a charge, to dispose of this type of waste as explained below.

Tipping permits

If you are taking waste to a Household Recycling Centre in Bridgend County Borough using a van or trailer then you will need to register your vehicle before your visit. This is required in order to control the deposit of non- household waste. Please note vans and trailers are not permitted access to the Brynmenyn site.

 The types of vehicles that require prior registration are:

  • Van up to transit size
  • Trailers between 5ft and 6ft 6in (1.5m to 2m) in length
  • 4x4 SUV pick-ups (open or closed back)

Larger vehicles or trailers are not permitted to use the Household Recycling Centres. If you are using a car or a trailer under 5ft in length then you DO NOT need to register your vehicle prior to your visit.

To register your van or trailer:

Please contact the council by telephoning 01656 643426 48 working hours before you wish to visit one of the Centres. You will need to give the following details –

  • Name and address
  • List of items that you are taking
  • Date and nominated centre that you wish to use
  • Registration number

Under certain circumstances assessments may be required and you will be advised if an assessment is required over the phone. If you are wishing to dispose of asbestos then you will require an assessment and a charge may apply.

Please note that plasterboard and rubble are classed as construction waste and are limited to two manageable bags only. If more than two bags are generated at home alternative methods of disposal should be arranged by the householder i.e. via a skip company.
