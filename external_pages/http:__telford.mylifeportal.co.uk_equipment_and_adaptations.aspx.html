Equipment and adaptations

You may benefit from making some adaptations to your home or having equipment which helps you to live independently. These may include:

  • equipment to help you maintain independence;
  • minor adaptations such as grab rails, stair rails;
  • major adaptations such as levelling your access, showering facilities.

You may need some equipment to help you with a sensory impairment.

The Independent living centre may also be able to provide you with some equipment that can support your independence. Please view examples of the sort of equipment you can get.

You can help avoid injury and illness by making sure you reduce your risk of falling.

Telecare, or assistive technology, as it is sometimes called, involves a range of different equipment. Examples of this is are community alarms or sensors which can tell if you have had a fall or raise the alarm if there is a risk of household dangers like a flood or fire.

You can view more information in our Assistive equipment fact sheet and Telecare (community alarm) fact sheet.

You can contact us to find out if you are eligible for a care act assessment.
