Garden Maintenance

Where tenants have a garden included in their tenancy, it is a requirement that this is maintained to a reasonable standard. Not to do so will place the tenants in breach of their tenancy conditions leading to action being taken by the Council.

It is appreciated that some tenants are not able to maintain the garden themselves, due either to a physical impairment or age.

Do I Qualify?

The service is available to elderly and/or physically impaired tenants, who do not have any family members living either with them or within a reasonable travelling distance and who could maintain the garden for them.

How Do I Apply?

To apply for the Garden Maintenance Service you should fill in an application form, which is available for collection from any of the Council's offices. We can send you one if you ask us to.

What Happens Next?

When we receive your application one of our officers will visit you to discuss your request and check that you qualify for the service. If you are eligible the officer will look at your garden to see what work is needed.

We will then write to you to confirm that your garden has been added to the contract for this service and your details will be sent to the Council's contractor (usually the Council's own workforce) with an instruction to include you in the next series of maintenance visits.

If, however, the officer feels that you do not qualify for the service you will be advised of this and this will be confirmed in writing. The letter you will receive will also tell you why it is felt you do not qualify and that you can ask for this decision to be reviewed by the Tenancy & Estates Manager.

It should be noted that when the Council agrees to maintain a garden it clears all borders and shrub bed areas, which it then levels ready for grass seed to be applied. This makes it easier to maintain the garden and keeps costs down, thus allowing the Council to include more properties in the service.

Should you wish to have a small border this will need to be next to the walls of your home. If you do this please ensure that the top of the soil is at least one brick below your damp proof course.

Please note that all garden ornaments, seats, etc. will need to be removed from the areas that the Council's contractors are required to maintain. If when the contractors visit to carry out the gardening work items are still present and have to be moved, although the contractors will exercise reasonable care when moving such items, neither they nor the Council will accept any liability whatsoever for any damage caused while they are being moved.

What is included?

  • The Garden Maintenance Service provides a basic service to keep your garden in a clean and tidy condition.
  • It includes the mowing of all grassed areas and the cutting of hedges to the top and one or both sides, dependent on the Council's responsibilities.
  • Hedges are cut twice a year and the grass is cut on average eleven times a year, depending on the growing season and weather.
  • As part of the service the Council will also clear the garden paths and treat these for any weeds.
  • If you have created a border next to the walls of your property you will have to maintain this yourself.

Other Information

As mentioned above, once your application is approved your address is added to the garden maintenance contract ready for the next series of visits. The first visit is usually carried out within 2-3 weeks*, subject to the weather.

*Where a garden is badly overgrown and needs clearing before regular maintenance can begin the Council employs a specialist contractor to carry this out. In this instance there will be a delay before the first maintenance visit is made.
