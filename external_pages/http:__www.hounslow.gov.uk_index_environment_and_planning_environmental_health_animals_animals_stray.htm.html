Stray dogs

We are required by law to receive stray dogs that are found within our boundaries. We used to share this responsibility with the police but have had sole responsibility since 2006.

If you find a stray dog

The law requires that you do one of two things:

  • Return the dog to its owner (if known), or

  • Report the dog to the local council for the area in which it was found

Reporting a stray dog

  • If you report a stray dog to us during normal working hours then we will arrange to come and collect it as soon as is practical. You may also bring stray dogs to us at the address given below

  • If you report a stray dog to us on a weekend or public holiday between 9am and 5pm then a duty officer will contact you to discuss the best way of admitting the dog to our care

  • There is no service to receive stray dogs outside of these hours

Please note that we will generally only attend to calls where the dog has been captured or restrained.

You will need to provide your own name, address and telephone number, the date and time that the dog was found, and the location where the dog was found.

Claiming a dog

If you think that your dog has been collected by us then you need to do the following as soon as is practical:

  • Telephone us to confirm that we have your dog

  • Confirm the costs payable prior to getting your dog back (see next section)

  • Arrange to visit the dog pound to pay all fees and costs due and to collect your dog. This is best done by appointment to ensure that one of our officers will be present to return your dog to you. Remember to bring a lead with you

Please note that dogs can only be claimed between 9am and 5 pm and by appointment only on weekends or public holidays.

Costs of claiming a dog

Owners are legally required to pay £25 plus reasonable costs that are incurred by us. All costs must be settled before the dog can be returned to its owner. We charge an additional £10 per night that the dog is with us plus the costs of any veterinary fees that we may incur. However, owners of dogs that are repeatedly found straying may be charged more. If this is the second time that your dog is claimed then you may be charged £40 plus overnight and veterinary costs. For the third and any subsequent claim you may be charged £55 plus overnight and veterinary costs. This more accurately reflects the costs that we incur in dealing with any stray dog.

If you are unable to pay this then please talk to us. If you are in receipt of at least one low-income related benefit then we might come to an appropriate arrangement. However, if you leave the dog with us for an unreasonable length of time, or if your dog is repeatedly allowed to stray, then we may require you to fully comply with the law and pay all costs advised before we will return your dog to you.

Reporting lost dogs

If you lose your dog then please tell us as soon as you can. In addition to your contact details we will need, the breed of dog, colour, size, sex, distinguishing marks, and any collar details. If your dog has been micro-chipped then we will also need to know if any of the registered contact details have changed. We will also need to know the date and approximate time that the dog was lost and the location that it was lost from.

What happens to unclaimed stray dogs?

Unclaimed dogs are held at our own pound for seven clear days, or until space becomes available at Battersea Dogs and Cats Home. If dogs are moved within the first seven clear day period then Battersea will hold the dog for reclaim on our behalf for the remainder of this period.

Following the expiry of this deadline, the law allows us to dispose of dogs by various means including by gift or sale to an individual or rescue centre, or even by destruction. The previous owner of a stray dog may lose any claim to it after this period.

At Hounslow, dogs are generally held until they can be accommodated at Battersea Dogs and Cats Home or other rescue organisation. We also occasionally re-home dogs directly.
