Council Tax Support scheme

Council Tax Support (CTS) is a local welfare benefit which helps working age people on a low income pay their council tax.

There are two types of Council Tax Support:

  • Main Council Tax Support is for people who are on a low income
  • Second Adult Rebate is for people who may not have a partner but who share their home with someone who is over 18, is on a low income and does not pay rent.

Council Tax Support is means tested. This means that we have to take into account your income, savings and investments when deciding whether you are entitled.

The scheme does not apply to retirement age pensioners who continue to receive the same level of support they would have received had the Council Tax Benefit scheme continued.

Contact us to make a claim for Council Tax Support.

If you have previously claimed Council Tax Benefit

From 1 April 2013 the national Council Tax Benefit (CTB) scheme was abolished. All councils including Bromley have replaced it with their own local scheme called Council Tax Support (CTS).

How will the changes affect you?

We adopted a scheme from the 1 April 2013 which meant that all working age claimants have to make payments towards their council tax.

For 2014/15 and 2015/16 we are providing support of up to 81% of the claimant's council tax liability. Therefore, all working age residents will be required to pay a minimum of 19% of their liability and those currently receiving partial assistance will see their contribution increase by that sum.

Any support granted was shown in your council tax demand for 2015/16. Below is an example of how much you may have to pay for a band D property:

Band D council tax full amount £ 1,325.14. You will pay £ 251.78 (19%) towards this, which when paid in 12 monthly instalments will be £ 20.98 per month. For the same band with a single person discount it will be £ 15.74 per month.

Your views  

We asked  for your views  as to whether the minimum working-age contribution for 2016/17 should remain at 19% of the residents council tax liability, increased to 25% or increased to 30%.

The consultation closed on 18 October 2015. Thank you to all those who responded. The responses to the consultation are included in the Council Tax Support Consultation Report and will be reported to councillors on 2 December 2015, as part of the decision making process.
