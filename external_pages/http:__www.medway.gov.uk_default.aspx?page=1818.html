Join a library

Online access for 28 days before you collect your library card in person

Online reference library

Includes reference works like newspapers, magazines and family history databases

It is free to join a library. Find out how to get started or if you have a Medway library account log in to it online here.

Like our Facebook page or follow us on Twitter to find out about the latest events and join in with discussions.

Medway Libraries offer events for all ages from parents and babies to social groups for older people.

It is free to borrow books for up to three weeks at a time but fees and charges apply to some services.

Find out what to read next or where to meet and discuss books with other readers.

Search our catalogue or check out this week's fiction bestsellers.

 

Children and young people

Every Medway library has areas for children and young people including books, activities and more.  Use these pages to  discover what your local library can offer for children, young people, parents, carers, teachers and early years professionals.

 

Computers and online resources

Public computers and computing courses are available free in all Medway Libraries. 

Online resources are available free; some from the comfort of your own home. Great for general interest, homework and study.

 

Films and TV series

Discover the latest films and TV Series, recent price reductions and our sound and vision loyalty card scheme.

 

 

Specialist library services

Information for people with health conditions and carers including details about reading groups, dyslexia friendly books, the home library service, services for the hard of hearing, postal service for the blind and partially sighted and open access resources for care homes and certain community locations.

Healthy living

Visit our healthy living page to see how we can help you enjoy a healthier life.

Information about health walks, specialist book collections for good mental and physical health and more.

 

Creative Medway

If you are a writer, an actor, a poet, a musician, a games maker, a photographer, a dancer, a film maker, someone who works with traditional and modern crafts or something else entirely, visit Creative Medway to find out how Medway can help you reach your creative goals or to join one of our craft groups.

Jobseeking and business support

A range of resources for jobseekers. 

Are you interested in starting your own business?

Links to external business and jobseeker information can be found in library links.

 

Are you researching local or family history?

Discover Medway's truly amazing history at Medway Archives and Local Studies Centre  For anyone interested in researching the history, geography and people of Medway. 

The centre houses special collections on Charles Dickens, Chatham Dockyard and Medway’s rich naval history.

Volunteering opportunities

Discover volunteering opportunities with Medway Libraries including the Home Library Service and Friends of Medway Libraries.
