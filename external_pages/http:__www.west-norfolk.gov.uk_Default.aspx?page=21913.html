Asbestos Advice

Asbestos has become the single greatest cause of work related death in the UK.  However, the risk from asbestos often depends on the type of asbestos and what condition it's in.  This page explains what you can do about asbestos in your home or in the workplace.

 

  • Advice for Businesses Factsheet (101KB)
  • Asbestos Advice for Domestic Properties (254KB)

 

Asbestos in your home. What to do with it.

The Health and Safety Executive have produced a useful guide highlighting where you can find asbestos in domestic properties. The Borough Council's factsheet explains what you can do about asbestos in your home or garden.

                                                                         

Asbestos licensed and non licensed removal works

For more information on whether works require a licensed contractor or fall into the category of notifiable non-licensed work or non licensed work the Health and Safety Executive (HSE) website provides much more information.

 

Asbestos on Domestic Private Land

 

Asbestos on private land is the responsibility of the land owner.

 

If you have concerns of damaged asbestos that is on private land which the public maybe exposed too, please contact the Community Safety Neighbourhood Nuisance (CSNN) team on 01553 616200.

 

The team can visit the site and if they suspect the material contains asbestos they can require the land owner to dispose of the asbestos safely. 

 

Further information

If you still have some unanswered questions, you may wish to consult the Health and Safety Executive's website which provides more advice and resources. 

 

Contact Details

If you would like any further information then please email: healthandsafety@west-norfolk.gov.uk or alternatively you can telephone 01553 616200
