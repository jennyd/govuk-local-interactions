Emergency Planning

What is Emergency Planning?

Emergencies are happening somewhere in the country almost every minute of every day. The emergency services (police, fire, ambulance and other organisations) deal with them quickly and efficiently. These incidents can include traffic accidents, fires, medical emergencies and other serious incidents. The local authorities do not necessarily activate their emergency plans for these incidents.

It’s when a more disastrous event takes place that is beyond the capacity of the emergency services to deal with unaided, or we have a specific interest or contribution to make that the district/ borough special plans are put into action. These plans are designed to support the emergency services in their difficult task.

What is an emergency?

An emergency is an event or situation which threatens serious damage to human welfare or the environment, or war or terrorism which threatens national security.

The Northamptonshire Community Risk Register details risks for North Northamptonshire and who will lead the response during an emergency situation.

What is the council’s role in emergency situations?

Three local authorities (Kettering, Corby and East Northants) work together as the North Northamptonshire Safety & Resilience Partnership (NNSRP) to provide a health and safety, emergency planning and business continuity service.

The NNSRP has an emergency plan which covers many eventualities.

We have a significant part to play in the various stages of an emergency - from the initial response through to the recovery process which we may lead.

We will work with partners to resolve accommodation and welfare needs for the people involved and communities affected, with priority being given to the vulnerable. We may also be involved in investigations and enforcement, as well as providing advice relating to structures and the environment.

Northamptonshire County Council also has plans for dealing with emergencies which they may lead. Find out more at www.northamptonshire.gov.uk/emergencies

What should I do in the event of an emergency?

To report an emergency, call 999.

For the latest information and advice on what to do when an emergency happens:

  • Tune into local radio or television
  • Check your local emergency services and council website
  • Check your local emergency services and council social media channels

www.northamptonshire.gov.uk/emergencies has a section on how to prepare for emergencies so everyone in your house or community knows what to do.

If you have a question about emergency planning in North Northamptonshire, email safetyresilienceteam@kettering.gov.uk or call 01536 534489
