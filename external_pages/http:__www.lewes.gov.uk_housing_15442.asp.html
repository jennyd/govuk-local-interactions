Existing tenants can exchange homes with another tenant from Lewes or another social landlord. Permission from both landlords is required.

A mutual exchange is a swap of properties between two social housing tenants. Both parties move on the same day. You can be a tenant from the Lewes district, another council or a Housing Association.

To apply you must have a secure tenancy. This means you have completed an introductory tenancy period if appropriate. It is illegal to offer money to another tenant to induce them to move.

Some tenants who mutually exchange to smaller properties in our district may be eligible for cash incentives.

Your rights when you exchange your tenancy

You should check what your tenancy rights will be in a new home before you agree to a tenancy exchange. You may get different or fewer rights with your new tenancy. Your Housing Officer can provide you with advice.

Finding a new home

The Council uses Exchange Locata to provide a mutual exchange facility. It lists other tenants wanting to move. There is no charge to advertise. You may also wish to advertise in local shops or magazines in the area you want to move to.

Once agreed, both parties are required to complete an application form. If the person you swap with has a different landlord you should ask them to supply their landlord consent form.

We will aim to give you a decision within six weeks, subject to references being received from other housing providers. We will arrange for you to be visited by your Area Housing Officer and Area Maintenance Officer.

They will check your home has no unauthorised alterations and if repairs are needed. We may refuse the mutual exchange if the property is in a poor condition.

We will then advise you if there is anything which you need to do to put the property back to a reasonable standard.  If the work is not completed before you exchange you will be charged the cost of putting it right.

References

If a new prospective tenant is not an existing Lewes District Council tenant, we will seek references from their landlord. We will need to know their family size, if there are any outstanding arrears, if there is any history of anti-social behaviour.  We can only refuse a mutual exchange application on certain specified grounds.

  • There is a court order for possession or a suspended possession order
  • The landlord has started possession proceedings
  • The property is too large or not reasonably suited to the incoming tenant's needs
  • The property is a service tenancy â this means the tenant occupies it for the better performance of their duties, such as Scheme Managers or caretakers
  • The property is designed for a disabled person and the incoming tenant is not disabled
  • The property is sheltered and the prospective tenant does not require this service

Reasons for refusal will always be given. The only appeal is by way of judicial review through the courts on a point of law.
