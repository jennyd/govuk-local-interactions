About the Licensing Act 2003 and Licensing policy 2011-2014

Anyone wishing to provide certain types of entertainment, supply alcohol or who are involved with club premises needs to apply for:
  • either the relevant premises certificate
  • and/or a personal licence.

Premises Licence

If you wish to provide late-night refreshments and regulated entertainment, and sell or supply alcohol, you need to apply for a licence from Kirklees Council, Licencing Department

Applicants must 18 years or older. You will need to apply for a premises licence if:-

  • You do not currently have a premises licence which related to one of the licensable activities; or
  • You already sell or supply alcohol and / or provide regulated entertainment and want to change how you currently operate

You will need to make your application 2 months prior to the starting of trading or the variation of trading

  • If you require a temporary authorisation for licensable activities you can apply for a Temporary Events Notice

Club Premises Licence

A club premises certificate authorises a members' club to carry out licensable activities which are:-

  • sale or supply of alcohol to members or guests
  • provision of regulated entertainment

If you carry out a licensable club activity without a club premises certificate (or premises licence) you will be breaking the law.

If you do not presently have a club premise certificate which relates to one of the licensable activities, you will need to make application at least two calendar months from the time you make your application to the time you wish to start trading

For a new Licence or where you are requesting variation, you must advertise by displaying a notice for a period of no less than 28 consecutive days starting on the day after the day on which the application was given to the relevant licensing authority

Requesting a review of a premises licence

At any stage following the grant of a premise licence or club premises certificate, an application can be made to the licensing authority for a review of a licence or certificate where problems are occurring.

The application must be on grounds relating to one or more of the licensing objectives:

  • Prevention of crime and disorder
  • Prevention of public nuisance
  • Public safety
  • Protection of children from harm.

Personal Licence

If you wish to sell or supply alcohol, you will need to hold a personal licence. To apply for a personal licence you need to contact Kirklees Council, Licensing Department.

Hypnotism

Hypnotism


  • Application for consent for an exhibition, demonstration or performance of hypnotism at a place licensed for regulated entertainment

Consent to perform Hypnotism

Hypnotism for the purpose of this application includes hypnotism, mesmerism and any similar act or process which produces or is intended to produce in any person any form of induced sleep or trance in which the susceptibility of the mind of that person to suggestion or direction is increased or intended to be increased, but does not include hypnotism, mesmerism or any such similar act or process which is self -induced.

  • No such exhibition, demonstration or performance must take place without the written consent of Kirklees Council
  • You must supply information regarding the last three performances you have undertaken, the name of the Venue, the Date, Description of the act and the telephone number of the venue.

You must make statements to show that:-

  • You have never been refused or had withdrawn consent for hypnotism by any licensing authority.
  • That you have never been convicted of an offence
  • That you have never been convicted of an offence involving the breach of a condition, regulation or prohibiting the giving of an exhibition, demonstration or performance

There is no cost for this licence.

Sex shop and Cinema Licence

If you wish to run a sex shop - ie any premises selling sex toys, books or videos, or run a venue where explicit films are shown to members of the public, you will need a licence from Kirklees Council, Licencing Department An applicant:-

  • Must be at least 18 years old
  • Must not be disqualified from holding a licence
  • Must have been resident in the UK at least six months immediately before the application or, if a body corporate, must be incorporated in the UK
  • Must not have been refused the grant or renewal of a licence for the premises in question within the last 12 months unless the refusal has been reversed on appeal
