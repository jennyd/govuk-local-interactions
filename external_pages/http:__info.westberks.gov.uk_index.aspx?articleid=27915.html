Transport Service Changes or Disruption

Planned public transport changes and travel disruption information

Travel Disruption and Bad Weather

If you're planning to travel by public transport during periods of disruption or bad weather, you should check with the bus or train company you'll be using to make sure they're running.  

The district's major transport operators are:

  • First Great Western - 08457 000 125 - they operate passenger train services
  • GoRide - 0330 330 8489 - they're also on Facebook
  • Newbury & District - 01635 33855
  • Reading Buses - 0118 959 4000 -  they're also on Facebook and Twitter
  • Stagecoach - 0845 121 0190
  • Thames Travel - 01865 785 400 - they're also on Facebook
  • Thamesdown Transport - 01793 428 428

The Traveline public transport journey planning service can also help you during travel disruption and bad weather -  the Traveline South East website has regularly updated information, or you can telephone them on 0871 200 22 33, before you set off.

Changes to Bus Services

Changes to services 6 and 107

With effect from Monday 4 January 2016, there will be changes to 'Connect' bus services 6 and 107, operated by Newbury and District bus company.  The changes are as follows:

On service 6, the first morning journey on weekdays will operate ten minutes earlier, now departing at 06:40 from Newbury Bus Station and running ten minutes earlier throughout its route through Chieveley, Beedon, Harwell Business Campus, the Ilsleys, Hampstead Norreys and Shaw back to Newbury and the schools.  The reason for this change is to reduce the impact of delays caused by other traffic coming back into and through Newbury.   

On service 107, the first morning journey on weekdays will start five minutes earlier, departing at 07:05 from Newbury Bus Station and will run five minutes earlier throughout the route via Leckhampstead, Chaddleworth, Brightwalton, Peasemore, Chieveley and Donnington back to Newbury and the schools.  At Donnington, the 107 bus will turn from Oxford Road into Grove Road and then serve the Speen Estate.  It will not serve the Donnington Square bus stop.  Customers from Donnington Square will need to board at a new bus stop in Grove Road.       

Withdrawal of late evening services N1, N2 and N4

Owing to the need for savings, the late evening services N1, N2 and N4 in Newbury and Thatcham, operated by Newbury and District, will be withdrawn after end of service on 31 December 2015. 

For more information please contact West Berkshire Council's Transport Services Team.

Rail Travel - Reading Station Scheme and Network Improvements

Network Rail are improving the rail approaches to Reading Station.   The works are programmed to continue through into 2016.  In addition, the rail lines through the Thames and Kennet Valley regions are being electrified.   These major schemes are likely to cause delays and there may be changes to train services and links with other public transport at times.  Details of the Reading Station works can be obtained by visiting Network Rail's Reading Station webpage and changes to rail services during this period will be publicised by First Great Western.
