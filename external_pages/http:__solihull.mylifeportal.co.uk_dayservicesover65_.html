Day services for people aged 65 or over

There are a variety of day services and opportunities in Solihull for you if you are aged 65 and over.

Some day services are provided in leisure services available to everybody in the borough. Others are designed specifically to ensure that you can live the life you want in the way you want.

If you have assessed care and support needs, the day services in Solihull can provide you with that extra personal care, practical attention and emotional support that you need.

Day services in Solihull for older people

There are a variety of activities in and around Solihull for everyone. Whatever you are interested in, whatever your abilities, skills or mobility you should be able to find activities close to you and ideal for you in the Solihull Directory.

If you would prefer to speak with someone directly, you can visit the Community Advice Hubs, presented and managed by Age UK Solihull in partnership with other organisations such as Alzheimer’s Society and Health Exchange, in:

  • North Solihull at Chelmsley Wood Library and is open Monday to Friday from 10.00am to 2.00pm
  • South Solihull at Solihull Connect Theatre Square and is open Monday to Friday from 10.00am to 4.00pm

You can also visit your local library.

Private and not for profit day care centres in Solihull

There are a number of private and not for profit day care centres in Solihull which are not funded by the Council. The Solihull Directory may have a number of organisations for you to consider.

Day care centres in Solihull

There are a number of day care centres in Solihull for older people, older people with dementia and people with physical and sensory disabilities. 

These day centres operate throughout the borough. It is a service available to those people needing help with personal and practical car to stay in your own home for as long as possible.

Members of staff at the day care centres are professionally trained to deliver the most appropriate level of care to meet specific needs. The staff provide reablement and enablement work, including helping people with issues such as:

  • taking medication
  • personal care and hygiene 
  • getting out and about during the day

The centres are located within the community and provide help and support with arranging health appointments such as optical, chiropody, dental care. There are number of varied activities from physical exercise to days out too. To ensure your day services work for you, you are encouraged to get involved in selecting which activities would be most suited, enjoyable or relevant and helpful to you.

A number of activities are offered through the day, including the chance to socialise and experience:

  • handicraft, craft activities and hobbies
  • sing-a-longs and music groups
  • gardening in the centres’ own gardens
  • learning and developing skills such as cooking , gardening, housework and laundry to help you stay active and enjoy an independent lifestyle
  • gentle physical activities such as going for walks and exercise sessions to improve mobility
  • food and clothes shopping
  • trips to nearby entertainment venues
  • visiting places of interest

For more information you can contact Solihull Connect on 0121 704 8004.

Day services for people with assessed care and support needs

If you are over 65, have care and support needs and live in the borough you may be entitled to day care services following a care and support needs assessment.

Solihull day care services can also offer carers a break from their day to day responsibilities.

To find out more you can:

  • learn about and request a needs assessment or carer’s assessment
  • find out about financial assessments and paying for care 
  • visit the Information and Advice Hubs in Chelmsley Wood and Solihull for advice and help to complete your own assessment

The cost of day care services

All care has some cost but how much you will have to contribute will depend on the results of your financial assessment.

If you have to make a financial contribution, there are a number of ways you can get help and support when paying for your care.

You can also take a look at the costs of social care in Solihull.
