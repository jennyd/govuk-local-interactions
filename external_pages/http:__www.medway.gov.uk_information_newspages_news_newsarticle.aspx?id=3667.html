Royal wedding street party deadline extended to Friday, 8 April

Date: 30/03/2011           Category: Highways Traffic and Parking

The deadline for residents to submit their royal wedding street party applications has today been extended to Friday, 8 April.

Medway Council has extended the deadline so that more people in Medway can arrange a party to celebrate the royal wedding as interest in the big day continues to grow.

Applications would normally have to be in six weeks ahead of an event, but this has been cut back to three so that neighbours have more time to rally together and start party planning.

The council has received a total of seven applications to date and is helping to cut the cost of parties by providing all necessary road closure signs and cones free of charge and reimbursing the cost of public liability insurance.

Earlier this month, the council also announced plans to extend road closures until 9pm on Friday, 29 April, so that people can party for longer.

Medway Council’s Director of Regeneration, Community and Culture, Robin Cooper, said: “Interest in the royal wedding is continuing to grow and we want to make sure anyone who wants to hold a street party doesn’t miss out.

“Holding a street party is a great opportunity to enjoy this momentous occasion with friends, family and neighours. As always, we’re happy to provide residents with plenty of help and advice about organising a party and I'd encourage anyone who's interested to get in touch with us and find out more."

To celebrate the marriage of Prince William and Kate Middleton by holding a street party, residents will need to contact the council to apply for a road closure.

Arrangements need to be made in advance to ensure the correct signs are being used, to make sure it is safe to close the road and that alternative routes are available for traffic. The council also needs to ensure that the emergency services continue to have access to all routes and are aware of any planned closures.

Residents interested in organising a street party are advised the contact the council as soon as possible. Site visits can also be carried out on request so that organisers can get advice in person.

To apply for a road closure, contact Medway Council’s traffic department on 01634 333333 or email traffic.management@medway.gov.uk for an application form.
