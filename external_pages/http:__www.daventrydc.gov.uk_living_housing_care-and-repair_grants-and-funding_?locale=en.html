Care & Repair: Grants and funding

Care & Repair Daventry District can help you access any grants and funding support that is available to help pay for the cost of any work that is required on your home.  


One of our friendly caseworkers will visit you to discuss your needs and go through the eligibility criteria for each available grant.


They can help you fill in the forms and will contact you to tell you whether you have been successful or not.


You can find out more about the grants that are available and the sort of work they can be used to fund below:


Home Repairs Assistance Grants


Home Repairs Assistance Grants (HRAs) can provide up to £5,000 to fund a range of work including the replacement or repair of central heating boilers and radiators, new security measures, replacement windows and doors, roof repairs, and much more.


Who can apply?

To apply for a HRA grant you must be a homeowner over the age of 18 and be living in the property as your main residence.


You must also be in receipt of an income-related benefit such as:


  • Council Tax Reduction (not single occupancy reduction).
  • Income Support
  • Income-based Job Seekers Allowance
  • Pension Credit (Guarantee Credit)
  • Tax Credits (Income-related)
  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance.

  

If you are unsure whether you are receiving a qualifying means-tested benefit, please contact us by emailing careandrepair@daventrydc.gov.uk or phoning 01327 871100.


What type of work is considered?

The grants covers a wide range of essential repairs, adaptations and improvements including:

 

  • Repairs to leaking roofs and guttering
  • New door and window locks
  • Replacements for draughty and rotten windows and doors
  • Central heating repairs and installation
  • Essential minor repair works
  • Plus many more

  

Top-up grants available

For work costing more than £5,000, eligible homeowners can also access a further £5,000 in the form of an interest-free loan, repayable only when the property is sold.


Find out if you're eligible for a Home Repairs Assistance Grant by:


  • Filling in our online referral form 
  • Emailing careandrepair@daventrydc.gov.uk
  • Phoning us on 01327 871100

  


Download one of our Home Repairs Assistance Grant leaflets to find out more

Disabled Facilities Grants


Disabled Facilities Grants (DFGs) can provide up to £30,000 to help people with a disability pay for a range of necessary improvements and adaptations to their home. The amount awarded is also subject to confirmation of the assessed needs of the disabled person by a suitably qualified professional.


Who can apply?

To apply for a DFG you or someone living in the property must be disabled. A disabled person under the age of 18 is entitled to  the maximum £30,000, but if you are 18 or over the amount you can apply for will depend on your income and savings.


You are eligible to apply for a DFG if you receive any of the following:


  • Disability Living Allowance
  • Personal Independent Payment
  • Council Tax Reduction (not single occupancy reduction)
  • Income support
  • Income-based Job Seekers Allowance
  • Guarantee Pension Credit

 

If you do not receive any of the above you may still be eligible. Email careandrepair@daventrydc.gov.uk or contact us on 01327 871100 to find out.


What type of work is considered?


The grants can fund a wide range of projects including:


  • Widening doors
  • Installing ramps
  • Stairlifts or hoists
  • Level access showers
  • Ground floor bathroom or bedroom adaptations
  • Driveway and parking solutions
  • Plus many more.

 

Find out if you're eligible for a Disabled Facilities Grant by:


  • Filling in our online referral form 
  • Emailing careandrepair@daventrydc.gov.uk
  • Phoning us on 01327 871100


Download one of our Disabled Facilities Grant leaflets to find out more

Care & Repair Hardship Fund


Grants of up to £250 in any 12 month period are available to vulnerable people facing severe financial hardship, to help them to live independently and safely.


Who can apply?

You can apply for a Care & Repair Hardship Fund if the following apply:


  • The repairs(s) required will: resolve or alleviate a health condition; improve personal safety; prevent weather ingress to the home.
  • You are unable to carry out the repair(s) yourself and have no family or friends who can help you.
  • You are in receipt of a means-tested benefit.

 

What does the fund cover?


  • Minor plumbing or heating repairs such as new taps or washers and repairs to leaking radiators
  • Painting and decorating following building work
  • Minor electrical checks and repairs to things such as faulty plug sockets and broken light fittings
  • Minor repairs to roofs, draughty doors, and windows
  • Security measures such as repairs to doors and windows/locks

  

Find out if you're eligible for the Care and Repair Hardship Fund by:


  • Filling in our online referral form 
  • Emailing careandrepair@daventrydc.gov.uk
  • Phoning us on 01327 871100
