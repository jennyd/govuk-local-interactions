Carrying out work on trees

Requesting work on trees the council owns

You can request work is carried out on the trees we own.

Read our policies for work on council owned trees.

Protected trees

We protect specific trees and woodland from deliberate damage and destruction. They are legally protected by a Tree Preservation Order (TPO).

You can find out if a tree is protected by searching property records.

Carrying out work on trees in conservation areas

Anyone proposing to cut down or carry out work on trees in a conservation area is required to give the council 6 weeks' prior notice  - this is legally known as a 'section 211 notice'. 

Tell us about proposed works to a tree in a conservation area.

We can then consider whether a Tree Preservation Order (TPO) should be made for the tree. There are some exceptions to this rule including:

  • Work carried out by, or on behalf of, the District Council
  • Work on a tree with a diameter not exceeding 75mm, or 100mm if cutting down trees to improve the growth of other trees such as thinning operation

We will deal with a Section 211 notice in one of 3 ways:

  1. Make a TPO if justified - the proposed works to the tree would then have to be the subject of a formal application under the TPO
  2. Decide not to make a TPO and allow the 6 week period to expire, at which point the proposed work may go ahead as long as it is carried out within 2 years from the date of the notice
  3. Decide not to make a TPO and inform the applicant that the work can go ahead
