Childcare

You can choose from a range of childcare options available in Herefordshire, including holiday and after school care.

Once you have decided which type of care is likely to suit you best, you can search the Family Information Directory to see what's available in your area or ring the family information service on 01432 261681. You can also call us to find out more about other options such as nannies and crèches.

Difficulty finding childcare in your area

If you have searched the directory and been unable to find suitable childcare, please complete our online form to help us improve childcare provision in your area.

We have a duty to provide enough childcare places to meet demand. If you are having difficulty finding a suitable childcare place, please let us know using our online form. We will use this information to work with childcare providers to help us develop and expand the available places and options.

Completing the form will not provide you with a childcare place. If you not been able to find a place using the Family Information Directory, please contact us on 01432 261681 or email: jstephens@herefordshire.gov.uk

Financial help and free childcare for families with children

Free childcare

Most childcare providers charge families for the care they provide. However, every three and four year old is entitled to receive 570 hours of free childcare a year. Now, some two year olds are also eligible to receive free childcare. 

The rules around free childcare entitlement can be confusing for parents and we understand you may have lots of questions about how the funding works.

It is important you know what to expect from your childcare provider whether it is a nursery, playgroup or childminder.

  • Find out about free childcare and education for three and four year olds

Child tax credit

The child tax credit is extra money for all families with children. You don't have to be working to claim the child tax credit, but you have to be responsible for at least one dependent child or young person. Contact the Inland Revenue for more information.

Working tax credit

You could be able to claim extra cash through the working tax credit if you or your partner work 16 hours or more a week. If you are paying for childcare, you could also be eligible to claim up to 70 per cent of those childcare costs back. For further information contact the Inland Revenue.

Help from employers

Some employers provide childcare help to employees. That could be childcare allowances, vouchers or subsidised childcare services. It's always worth asking if your employer has plans to help with childcare. See more about getting help from your employers at the GOV.UK website.

Extra help for lone parents

Lone parents can get some help with childcare costs while they train for work or search for work - contact your local Jobcentre Plus for more information or visit GOV.UK.

Extra help for parents in education

Care to Learn helps teenage parents under the age of 19. It supports parents who wish to continue with their education or training by providing information, advice and funding for childcare.

The Learner Support Fund helps further education students aged 16 to 18 with a variety of costs including childcare.

Parents studying an NHS funded professional training course will be able to claim the NHS Childcare Allowance which can help parents claim up to 85 per cent of their childcare costs. Find out more at the NHS website.

What are childminders?

A childminder is someone who is paid to look after children under the age of 12 in domestic premises - not the child's home - for more than two hours a day. All childminders are registered with Ofsted.

Many childminders offer flexible childcare arrangements. Childminders can be a good option for those who want their child to be looked after in a home environment or for those who want their children of different ages to be cared for together. Parents or carers negotiate the childminder's rate of pay directly with the childminder.

What are out of school clubs and holiday clubs?

Breakfast clubs and after school clubs offer supervised play and care for school aged children before and after the school day.

Some clubs operate during school holidays. Most will offer age-appropriate activities for the children, as well as homework support. Many of these clubs are registered with Ofsted.

What are day nurseries and playschools?

Day nurseries provide care and education for children from six weeks to five years old, but the age range covered varies from nursery to nursery.

Nurseries can be run as private businesses, voluntary organisations or community day nurseries set up by a local group. All day nurseries are registered with Ofsted.

What nursery classes are available in local authority schools?

Sometimes called maintained nurseries, local authority nursery classes are funded by the government through Herefordshire Council. They provide Early Years Foundation Stage education for children between the ages of 3 and 5 for 15 hours per week. Our nursery classes are attached to a maintained primary school. Some classes may be part of a unit where the children in the maintained nursery and the school's reception class share the same space.

Maintained nurseries follow the Early Years Foundation Stage Framework and are subject to inspection by Ofsted alongside the school inspection.  Places at some maintained nurseries are in great demand, and it is advisable for parents to register their child well in advance of their third birthday.

You can download more information on the Early Years Foundation stage from the Foundation Years website (Word document).

Staff and organisation

Local authority nurseries must employ a relevantly qualified teacher and support staff.  A nursery's catchment area is larger than that of the attached primary school and therefore, a place in a nursery is not a guarantee of an automatic place in the attached infant or primary school.

What are parent and toddler groups?

Parents and toddler groups provide an excellent opportunity for parents and children to socialise. They do not have to be registered with Ofsted because parents and carers stay with the children. This also means that children can attend from any age.
