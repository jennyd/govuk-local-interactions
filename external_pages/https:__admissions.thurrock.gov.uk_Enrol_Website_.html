Apply for a school place

Parents and carers living in Thurrock can apply to :-

·                     Start school (Reception) in September 2016

·                     Transfer from Infant to Junior School (Year 3) in September 2016

·                     Transfer to Secondary School (Year 7) in September 2016

·                     Start at a Studio school (Year 10) in September 2016

·                     Move to a different school between September 2015 and July 2016 (In-year admission)

Don't apply online if you've already sent us a paper application.

Creating an admissions account

You will need to create an account to make an admissions application. The 'Create Account' link is only available during open admissions periods, either on the right side of the screen or from a menu button in the top corner.

You will need a new account even if you have applied online in previous years.

When you have created your account you can add your children and school preferences. Use the 'Login' link on this page to return to your account at any time.

You should read the admission arrangements for your preferred schools before applying. You can find these in our admissions booklets, linked on the right of this page.

Late applications

We process late applications only after we have processed all the on-time applications. If you have already applied online you will not be able to make another application.

Contact us if you feel you need to make another application, or to send evidence that your application was late due to exceptional circumstances.

Use of your information

We normally process applications on the basis of the child's home address at the time of application and offer.

The home address is the address where the child is a permanent resident, or where the child is ordinarily resident. This is usually the address of the parent, or carer with parental responsibility. The adult with whom the child is ordinarily resident would receive the child benefit for the child.

We ask you to include your council tax reference number so we can check the address on your application against information we have in our council tax records.

Data Protection Act Statement

The purpose for collecting personal details on the application is to allow the Council’s Admissions Team to process your Application. The details provided may also be used in the future to ascertain your levels of satisfaction with the service provided. Once the form has been completed the information will be stored in hard copy and on the Admissions Team’s electronic database. Access to the information will be on a need-to-know basis and will be archived until 6 years after the child’s 18th birthday (before being securely disposed of). The Council may share information about you in certain circumstances where the law allows. This could include sharing with other authorities and schools in order to process your admission application; or with external statutory bodies for the purposes of child protection or criminal investigations. Any sharing will always be done fairly and securely.
