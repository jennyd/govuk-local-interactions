Provision of Entertainment and the Sale of Alcohol or Hot Food

The Licensing Act 2003 transferred responsibility for control of sale of alcohol, the holding of public entertainment, theatres, cinemas, and provision of late night refreshments between the hours of 11.00pm and 5.00am to local authorities.

Licence summary

To provide late-night refreshments and regulated entertainment, and sell alcohol, you need a licence from East Herts Council.

Eligibility Criteria

Any of the following may apply for a premises licence:

  • anyone who uses carries on a business in the premises to which the application relates
  • a recognised club
  • a charity
  • a health service body
  • a person who is registered under the Care Standards Act 2000 in relation to an independent hospital
  • a chief police officer of a force in England and Wales
  • anyone discharging a statutory or function under Her Majesty's prerogative
  • a person from an educational institute
  • any other permitted person

Applicants must not be under 18 years of age.

Licensing Act 2003 explained

Application Evaluation Process

Applications must be sent to the Licensing Authority for the area that the premises are located in.

Applications must be in a specific format, whether by hardcopy or online, and be accompanied by any required fee (details of the fees can be found at the bottom of this page). There are several parts to an application for a premises licence including an operating schedule (where you explain how you will promote the Licensing Objectives), a plan of the premises (showing the extent of the premises to be licensed) and a form of consent from the premises supervisor (for applications requesting the sale of alcohol).

An applicant will need to detail the following items:

  • what they want to do (which licensable activities they want to be allowed)
  • the times when the activities will take place
  • where the activities will take place (this should be shown on the plans which are required to accompany the application)
  • any other times when the premises will be open to the public (any seasonal variations for Christmas, New Year's Eve, etc.)
  • whether the licence will be time limited (if not the licence will last forever unless it lapses or is revoked or surrendered, an annual fee will apply)
  • details of the designated premises supervisor (if the sale of alcohol has been requested you must nominate a personal licence holder to fulfil this role)
  • if alcohol is to be sold whether it is for consumption on or off the premises or both
  • any steps that are proposed to be taken to promote the licensing objectives
  • any other supporting information the applicant considers may help people to understand the application

Some applications require the applicant to advertise their application (both at the premises and in the newspaper). It may also be a requirement that notice of the application is given to other persons or responsible bodies, e.g. Planning, Police or Fire and Rescue etc. A list of the responsible authorities for East Herts can be found by clicking on the link below for 'Reviews of premises licences'. Some applications can be made online via GOV.UK and in these circumstances it is generally the Licensing Authority that has to circulate the application on the behalf of the applicant.

Some applications are open to consultation (either with the public, responsible authorities or both). Where objections are received (known as representations under the Licensing Act 2003) it is likely that a Licensing Sub-Committee hearing will be held to decide the application at which all parties will be invited to have their say. If a hearing is held there are a number of options open to the committee, these include:

  • granting the licence as requested.
  • adding additional conditions to help address the concerns raised.
  • exclude particular licensable activities or amend requested hours.
  • reject/refuse the application entirely.

The Licensing Authority will notify all parties of the date and time of any hearing and of its decision once the hearing is complete. Holding a hearing is an expensive and time consuming process and the Licensing Act 2003 encourages communication between parties to try and find solutions to concerns. The Licensing Authority will actively try to mediate between parties to try and find solutions that all parties are happy with.

There are a variety of other types of applications and notices that may be made/given which are mostly granted under delegated authority (which means that straight forward decisions are made by Council Officers) but where there are concerns these may also require a decision by a Licensing Sub-Committee.

Will Tacit Consent Apply?

Yes. This means that you will be able to act as though your application is granted if you have not heard from East Herts Council by the end of the target completion period.

Failed Application Redress

Please contact East Herts Council in the first instance.

If an application for a licence is refused the failed applicant can appeal.

Appeals are made to a Magistrates' court within 21 days of notice of the decision.

Licence Holder Redress

Please contact East Herts Council in the first instance.

If an application is made by the chief police officer, as detailed below, and interim steps are taken by us you may make representations. A hearing must be held within 48 hours of your representations.

A licence holder may appeal against any conditions attached to a licence, a decision to reject a variation application, a decision to reject a transfer application or a decision to exclude an activity or person as premises supervisor.

Appeals are made to a Magistrates' court within 21 days of notice of the decision.

Consumer Complaint

An interested party or responsible authority may apply to the licensing authority to review the premises licence. A hearing will be held by East Herts Council as the licensing authority.

Appeals are made to a Magistrates' court within 21 days of notice of the decision.

Other Redress

The chief police officer for the police area where the premises are located can apply to East Herts Council for a review of the licence if the premises are licensed to sell alcohol by retail and a senior officer has given a certificate that they are of the opinion that the premises are associated with either serious crime or disorder or both. A hearing will be held and the licence holder and other interested parties may make representations.

A chief police officer can give a notice to East Herts Council if they believe that the transfer of a licence to another, under a variation application could undermine crime prevention objectives. Such a notice must be given within 14 days of receiving notification of the application.

An interested party or responsible body may make representations in relation to a licence application or request East Herts Council to review a licence.

An interested party or responsible authority may apply to East Herts Council to review the premises licence. A hearing will be held by us, as licensing authority.

A chief police officer may make representations to the licensing authority for a review of the licence if the premises are licensed to sell alcohol by retail and a senior member of the force has given a certificate to state that in their opinion the premises are associated with serious crime, disorder or both.

An interested party or relevant authority who made relevant representations may appeal against the granting of a licence or against any condition, variation, licensable activity or premises supervisor decisions.

Appeals are made to a Magistrates' court within 21 days of notice of the decision.

  • The Licensing Act 2003
    Under the Licensing Act 2003, any premises or place where licensable activities will take place will need to be covered by a licence or notice. Licence holders will be responsible for the prevention of crime and disorder, public safety, the prevention of public nuisance and the protection of children from harm
  • Licensing Policy
    The Policy outlines how East Herts intends to balance both the positive aspects (such as increasing the leisure industry, community provision and enhancing the attractiveness of the area), whilst controlling the negative impacts (such as noise, anti-social behaviour and crime and disorder).
  • Temporary Event Notices (TEN)
    You are encouraged to consult with East Herts Council and the Police before you serve your TEN.
  • Alcohol Licence Application Forms
    Forms relating to premises licences, personal licences, consents, declarations and reviews, as well as guidance notes can be found here.
  • Gaming Machines
    Premises holding a Premises Licence that authorise the supply of alcohol under the Licensing Act 2003, may serve a notice on the Licensing Authority to provide up to two category C or D gaming machines.
  • Reviews of Premises Licences
    This guidance is for anyone living or working in the vicinity of licensed premises, and that wish to request a review of that premises licence by the Licensing Sub-Committee.
  • Guidance Notes to Applicants on advertising requirements
    This guidance is for applicants for new premises licences and club premises certificates. It specifies the information that must go in newspaper advertisements, and in notices displayed on the premises during the application period.
