Rubbish and recycling top tips

Here are our top tips to help you deal with all your rubbish and recycling and how to use our waste collection schemes to their full potential.

Please note: we only deal with bins in the Preston area. If you live outside this area, you can find your local council through Directgov: Find your local authority.

1. Sort your waste correctly ready for collection

Use your 'Simply Recycling' refuse and recycling guide (from the documents section) or our What do I put in my recycling containers page to help you sort your waste correctly.

2. Make sure your bins and boxes are out for collection by 7.00am on your collection day

We may not always collect your containers at the same time every week, so please make sure your bins and boxes are out by 7.00am to make sure you don't miss a collection.

Use the about my area tool to find your bin collection day.

3. Put your house number on all your bins and boxes

Make sure you clearly mark all your bins and boxes with your house number so that we can return them to the right house and to avoid them being stolen or vandalised.

4. Bring your bins and boxes back on to your property as soon as possible after collection

Bins left on the street outside of collection periods are exposed to crime, arson, fly-tipping, litter and vermin.  If your bin is continually left on the street outside of collection periods we may be forced to repossess it and you will be charged for its return. 

Any loose waste left on the street as a result of not having a bin will be treated as an offence and may lead to prosecution. 

Your bins and boxes should be presented no earlier than 6.00pm on the day before collection and should be returned to your property as soon as possible after collection.

5. Ask yourself "Do I really need my recycling bin emptying this fortnight?" 

If your area has recycling bins you may not have enough recyclables to fill your bin every fortnight so ask yourself do you really need to put it out for collection.

6. Don't bag your recycling in your bins and boxes

Please do not use carrier bags as this is classed as contamination and may result in us not being able to empty your containers.

7. Do not place any extra bags of rubbish out with your grey bin

We will only collect rubbish contained within your grey bin so please do not place extra bags of waste out for collection as this will be classed as fly-tipping and may lead to further investigations by our enforcement officers. 

If you continually have extra bags of waste and are fully participating in our recycling schemes you may be eligible for a larger bin. For more information see do you need a bigger bin?

8. Request extra boxes if you have too much material for one box

If you have too much material for one box email neighbourhood services, you can place extra recyclables out for collection in untied carrier bags or request a new recycling box.

9. Recycle even more of your waste by using the Household Waste Recycling Centres (HWRC)

You can recycle batteries, wood, rigid plastics, hardcore, beverage cartons and much much more at your local HWRC which will help to reduce the waste in your grey bin even further.

10. Remember you can now recycle all your cooked and uncooked food waste

If you live in a kitchen waste area use your green buckets to recycle all your cooked and uncooked food waste and place it out for collection every week. 

If you live in a brown bin area remember you can also recycle all your food waste in your brown bin mixed with your garden waste. Use newspaper to wrap your food waste in or you can purchase biodegradable bags from local supermarkets.

11. Book a skip through our Neighbourhood Services if you are having a clear out or doing some DIY etc...

Neighbourhood Services operate a fast, competitive skip hire service with prices based on the location and skip size.  For more information please see skip hire.

12. Book a bulky waste collection for any large household items that you need removing

For just £15.00 for 3 items and £5.50 per item thereafter you can pass on your unwanted items for reuse and recycling. For more information see bulky waste collection.
