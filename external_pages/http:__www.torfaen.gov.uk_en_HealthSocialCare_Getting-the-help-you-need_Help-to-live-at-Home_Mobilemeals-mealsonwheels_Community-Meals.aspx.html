Community Meals

Torfaen provides a community meals service for people living in their own homes who are unable to prepare their own meals. Although many of those who use the service are older people, the service is open to anyone who is unable to prepare a meal because of frailty, illness or disability, subject to our eligibility criteria.

The community meals service can be arranged for varying lengths of time. Some people use the service for a short time after coming out of hospital or while their carer is away; others use the service on a long-term basis.

Hot meals

We deliver a hot meal and a pudding to those who are eligible between 11.30am and 2.00pm, seven days a week. The current cost is £4.50 per meal. There is a weekly menu to choose from with a choice between four meals and four puddings every day.

Payment can be made by cash/cheque to the driver.  Alternatively we can arrange to invoice you monthly or a monthly direct debit can be set up.

You do not have to have a meal delivered every day – the service can be arranged for however many days it is needed.

Frozen meals

We also deliver frozen meals for people who require some assistance but are able to heat the meals themselves or have carers who do it for them. You can choose your meals from Menu Selector – a catalogue with photographs of individual meals. The minimum order for frozen meals is for seven meals.

Our delivery day is a Friday but can be on a weekly, fortnightly or monthly basis to suit the customer. The service is provided by Wiltshire Farm Foods. Payments for frozen meals should be made on delivery by cash or cheque. Frozen meals currently cost £2.70 for a main course and dessert. 

If you would like to know more about community meals, ask your care manager, or if you are not currently receiving social services, telephone 01495 762200.

Frequently Asked Questions

How are hot meals delivered?

Hot meals are delivered by our friendly team of drivers who are trained to put your welfare and safety first. Every driver carries an identification card with his/herphotograph clearly displayed for your peace of mind. 

Hot meals are usually delivered between 11.30 am – 2.00 pm, seven days a week (though you do not need to have a meal every day). 

A regular delivery time may be established, but allowances should be made for adverse weather conditions, heavy traffic and temporary drivers having to learn a new route.

Getting the best from meals on wheels

Hot meals should be eaten when you receive them. We do not recommend that any part of the meal be retained for later in the day, or for consumption the following day. Enjoy your food whilst it is fresh and hot!

How are frozen meals delivered?

All frozen meals are delivered on a Friday, however you can arrange a weekly, fortnightly or monthly delivery to suit your needs.

Payments for frozen meals should be made on delivery by cash or cheque. Frozen meals currently cost £2.70 for a main course and dessert.

The minimum order for frozen meals is for seven meals. The service is provided by Wiltshire Farm Foods.

If I am on a special diet will I be able to use the service?

In addition to our wide range of hot meals, we also cater for the following special diets:

  • Diabetic
  • Low fat
  • Soft diet
  • Low sodium
  • Vegetarian
  • Gluten free
  • Reducing diet

What does a meal on wheels cost?

The charge for meals is reviewed annually. The current cost is £4.50 per meal for a hot meal and £2.70 for a frozen meal (main course and pudding for both).

How will I pay for my meals?

There are a number of ways to pay:

Hot meals:

  • The driver will collect your payment each time your meal is delivered
  • The driver will collect your payment weekly in advance or in arrears
  • We can arrange to invoice you monthly or a monthly direct debit can be set up

Frozen meals:

Payments for frozen meals should be paid on delivery by cash or cheque. 

What happens if I need to cancel?

It is very important that we know if you are not going to be at home when your meal is delivered.

Our drivers will let us know if you do not answer the door and we may need to call someone to check that you are safe and well.

If we are unable to speak to a relative or neighbour and we are very concerned about you, it occasionally may be necessary to inform the police who may need to gain entry to your home, to check that you are not ill and unable to call for help.

If you wish to cancel a delivery of meals either short or long term, e.g. perhaps if you are going into hospital, enjoying a day trip or going on holiday, please contact us to let us know.

Luncheon clubs

If you are able to get out, there are a number of luncheon clubs in Torfaen which provide a hot meal for around £4.00 for a two or three course meal. 

Contact 01495 766373 for more information.

Comments and Complaints

If you are unhappy about the quality or content of your meal or any aspect of the service we offer, you should contact the community meals manager on the number or address below, or you may contact your local customer care centre.

We will be only too pleased to answer any queries you may have about the service.
