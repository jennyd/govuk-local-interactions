Report a noise problem

Noise Control

What is domestic noise nuisance?

It is difficult to give a definition of noise nuisance - we all make noise at some time whether at home or at work. This may include shouting to others, watching TV, playing music, having a party or barbeque, using DIY equipment, dogs barking, and when generally going about our daily lives.

However, excessive and inconsiderate noise is unacceptable when it affects the quality of other people's lives.

I am suffering from noise nuisance what should I do?

If you are suffering from noise nuisance, try talking to the source of the noise. Many problems can be dealt with in a neighbourly way, as the source may not even be aware they are causing a problem.

If talking to the source of the noise (perhaps noisy neighbours) is not possible because you feel frightened or intimidated, you can report it to us online:

Report noise nuisance

Alternatively, you can contact us by phone or email.

If you do submit a complaint, we aim to contact you within two working days, either by letter, telephone or by visiting you at home.

How can we help with noise nuisance?

Before we can take action, we need to be sure that the noise constitutes a statutory nuisance. Put simply, we have to prove that the noise is harmful to health or is causing an unreasonable and regular disturbance to you.

If you make a complaint, we may send you some noise monitoring diary sheets and we may send a letter to the alleged source telling them that a complaint has been made and an investigation is being undertaken. Often the noise nuisance is resolved at this stage.

If the noise nuisance continues, we may install some digital noise-monitoring equipment at your property. If a statutory nuisance is recorded, we may invite the source into our offices so they can listen to the sound they are making played back in 'real time’. We may investigate further by sending some officers to witness the noise nuisance at first hand - we may then issue an Abatement Notice.

Dependent on the circumstances, and where an Abatement Notice is in force, we may provide a 24 hour, seven days a week noise nuisance service. Where our officers will respond with the sole purpose of enforcing the requirements of any notice.

If a breach of an Abatement Notice is witnessed by an officer, at any time, the matter may be placed before Scunthorpe Magistrate's Court as a criminal prosecution. Additionally, at the time of the breach, a warrant will be sought and any noise making equipment at the property that is capable of causing the nuisance, may be seized.

What can we do about commercial noise?

We often have to deal with noise from commercial premises in the same way as domestic premises. However, in some cases, commercial premises may be covered by other pieces of legislation, which may also help control noise. For example, a premises licence or planning permission.

However, in the case of construction sites, noise is usually expected. A restriction on working hours may be a part of the planning permission.

To report commercial noise, you can report it to us online:

Report commercial noise nuisance

Alternatively, you can contact us by phone or email.

Aircraft noise

The council does not deal with aircraft noise. If departing or landing aircraft is causing you a nuisance, please contact the Civil Aviation Authority:
