Free childcare for two year olds

Birmingham City Council offers an early education entitlement for two year old children for some families in the city.

Entitlement

Eligible two year olds will be entitled for up to 15 hours per week for 38 weeks of the year. Your child can take their free childcare place following the school term after their second birthday.

This must be taken over a minimum of two days from a childcare provider that is approved to provide the early education entitlement for two year olds. The childcare provider must also be registered with Ofsted.

Criteria

To be eligible families will need to access certain benefits or the children are looked after by the local authority (please refer to the attachment below 'which two year old children are eligible').

You can check if your child is eligible or contact the Children's Information and Advice Service Telephone: 0121 303 1888.

If your financial circumstances change your child does not lose their free childcare place.


Eligibility dates

A child born between If meeting the agreed criteria will access a place from
1st January – 31st March (inclusive) The start of the Summer term (April) following their second birthday, until they access early education entitlement for three and four years olds
1st April – 31st August (inclusive) The start of the Autumn term (September) following their second birthday , until they access early education entitlement for three and four years olds
1st September – 31st December (inclusive) The start of the Spring term (January) following their second birthday, until they access early education entitlement for three and four years olds


To find out what childcare options are available or for more information on the eligibility criteria contact the Children's Information and Advice Service Telephone: 0121 303 1888.
