Building Control Guidance Note No.5 - Dangerous Structures

What do I do if I see a dangerous structure? A Reporting Procedure

Wrexham County Borough Council has the responsibility for dealing with dangerous buildings and /or structures. Buildings may become gradually dangerous due to old age, deterioration, inclement weather, settlement, vandalism or by more dramatic causes such as fire, explosion or impact by vehicles.

The legislation used by the Council to deal with dangerous structures is contained within the provisions of the Building Act 1984, and enables the Building Control Section to make a building safe and alleviate any potential danger to members of the public.

Buildings can become dangerous at any time of day and to deal with this the Building Control Section responds to numerous requests to attend site by the Police, Fire Service and members of the public.

When the Building Control Surveyor arrives on site, they will examine the extent and nature of the danger and will consider the action to be taken to ensure everyone's safety. This is determined by assessing the nature of the danger and risk to people. Immediate action can be requested of a contractor to either remove or make safe the dangerous building or structure.

In less serious cases the Building Control Surveyor would have the premises secured to prevent unwanted access. They would then contact the owner to discuss how he/she could rectify the situation. If the owner does not remove the danger, action would be taken in the Magistrates Court leading to the owner(s) being ordered to carry out the necessary work and be fully responsible for all the costs incurred. The Authority would execute the works should the owner be unwilling to carry out the works.

If you notice a dangerous structure or notice a potential danger to members of the public contact Building Control Section tel: 01978 292050

Adopted October 2002.
