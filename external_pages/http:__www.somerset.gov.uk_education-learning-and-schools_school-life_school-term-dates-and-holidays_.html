School term dates and holidays

 
Somerset County Council publishes school term dates and holiday information.  Academies, free schools, foundation schools and voluntary-aided schools have the freedom to set their own term dates; SCC consults on and publishes term dates and holidays for community and voluntary controlled schools. Parents are strongly advised to check the agreed term dates with their children's school(s). Suggested dates for the current year and the following two academic years is shown in the table below.

INSET days
These are training and development days for school staff and for maintained schools there are five per academic year.

Each school will determine when their own INSET days take place and will inform parents directly. Usually they are taken at the beginning or end of an existing school holiday, but they can be arranged for any day of the advertised school year.

For information about school closures, restricted opening or other special arrangements, please see the Somerset Learning Platform.

Each year a total of 195 school days are identified and maintained schools are able to choose 5 INSET days which best suit them. In other words, pupils attend maintained school for 190 days each year. It is the responsibility of each school to notify its parents and staff which dates have been identified for INSET.

Absence from school during term time
To do well, children and young people need to attend school. There are 14 complete weeks holiday in a school year and Somerset County Council and the Government believe that family holidays should be taken during these school holiday periods so that children's education is not disrupted.

Parents and carers may request leave during term time and the decision whether these requests are authorised rests with the child's head teacher. Requests are usually only authorised where there are exceptional circumstances preventing the holiday being taken outside of term time.

For more information see the GOV.UK School attendance and absence web page.
