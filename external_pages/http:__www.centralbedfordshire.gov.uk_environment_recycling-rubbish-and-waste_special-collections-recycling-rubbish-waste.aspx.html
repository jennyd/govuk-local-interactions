Bulky waste collections

Bulky waste collection

A bulky household waste collection service is a collection service for larger household items that need to be disposed of. Types of waste collected include beds, wardrobes, washing machines, TVs, tables etc.

The removal of fridges and freezers is a separate service (see removal of fridges and freezers)

Our service allows for the removal of up to 12 items only. Items that have more than once piece, such as a bed with a divan base, mattress and headboard, would be counted as separate items.

There is a charge of £52.50 (£26.25 concessionary fee if you claim housing benefit or council tax benefit) for this service and collections are made by arrangement only. To book a bulky waste collection call 0300 300 8302.

Could your item be re-used?

If you think the item could be re-used by someone else why not pass it on to a local charity organisation such as Emmaus or Preen (a local enterprise that helps local people with low-priced furniture, appliances and household goods) or offer the items via Recycle.co.uk and  Freegle (an online re-use group for local people to give items away and get things for free)

If your items are unsuitable for re-use, you can take them to your nearest tidy tip for disposal.

What can / cannot be collected?

If you think the items could be re-used by someone else why not pass it on to a charity shop?

Or contact Preen (a local enterprise that helps local people with low-priced furniture, appliances and household goods) or offer the items via Freegle (an online re-use group for local people to give items away and get things for free).

What can be collected - up to 12 items at any one time

  • bed - bed base, cot, duvet, headboard, mattress or sofa bed
  • bins - old metal or plastic bins, including old compost bins
  • children's play equipment - this needs to be dismantled beforehand e.g. large toys or outdoor trampoline
  • carpet or rug
  • computer - base unit, monitor, printer or scanner
  • compost bin - metal or plastic
  • electrical items - general appliance, DVD player, games console, lamp, power tool, music centre, portable heater, sewing machine, speaker, sunbed, television, vacuum cleaner or video player
  • furniture - curtain pole / rail, desk, free standing, garden, sofa, table, television stand or wardrobe 
  • kitchen items - cooker, dishwasher, microwave, tumble dryer or washing machine
  • outdoor - dog kennel, rabbit hutch or wheelbarrow
  • other items - exercise machine*, pram / pushchair, shower screen (plastic) suitcase, typewriter or wheelchair

* Please check that this item can be easily lifted and is not an excessive weight

Fridge / freezer collection - This service allows for the removal of 1 item only (a combined fridge/freezer is accepted as 1 item) charged at £41.50. More than one item will cost £41.50 (incl. VAT) for each additional item. Please ensure any fridge or freezer is empty before collection.

If you are in receipt of housing benefit or council tax benefit, the standard fee is £20.75 (a 50% reduction of the normal fee).

What cannot be collected

  • asbestos and chemicals - visit our asbestos removal and disposal (Trading Standards Approve) page for guidance
  • builders' waste e.g. radiators, boilers, gas / oil heaters or fires
  • car parts including tyres, batteries and gas bottles
  • DIY waste including bricks, broken concrete, soil, stones and loose rubble
  • domestic waste including bags / boxes of waste, paint or paint tins  
  • garden refuse including garden sheds, fencing or greenhouses
  • household items including cardboard, garage doors, pianos (or similar very heavy objects)
  • large items including glass / mirrors and old windows

Thinking of hiring a skip?

In order to place a builder's skip on the highway, a permit for each location is required from Central Bedfordshire Highways. Advice on skip permits is available on our licensing page.

How much does it cost?

There are two types of collections:

  1. Bulky waste collection - up to 12 items at one time
  2. Fridge / freezer collection - this service allows for the removal of 1 item only (a combined fridge/freezer is accepted as 1 item) charged at £41.50. More than one item will cost £41.50 (including VAT) for each additional item. Please ensure any fridge or freezer is empty before collection.

Where do we collect your items from?

Items must be placed at ground floor level, outside your property by 7am on the agreed day of collection. It is not possible for the collectors to enter the property.

How long does it take to book a collection?

Once it has been scheduled in our collection team will call to confirm the collection date. This is normally within 7 days of the request being made.

Concessionary fee explained

If you are in receipt of housing benefit or council tax benefit, the standard fee is a 50% reduction of the normal fee.

Arrange a collection

To book and pay for your bulky household waste collection, please call us on 0300 300 8302.

We accept payment by debit or credit card, cheque, or postal order. If paying by cheque, please allow 10 working days for your cheque to clear when arranging your collection date.

When and how will it be collected?

Once it has been scheduled in we will call to confirm the collection date. This is normally within seven working days of the request being made.

Fridges and freezers - removal

This service allows for the removal of 1 item only (a combined fridge/freezer is accepted as 1 item) charged at £41.50. More than one item will cost £41.50 (incl. VAT) for each additional item. Please ensure any fridge or freezer is empty before collection.

If you are in receipt of housing benefit or council tax benefit, the standard fee is £20.75 (a 50% reduction of the normal fee).

Leaving out items for collection

Items must be placed outside, be clearly visible from the main road and accessible from the kerbside by 7am on the day of collection - the collection crew are not permitted to enter any property to collect items under any circumstances so your items must be outside.

Clinical waste collection

Central Bedfordshire Council can arrange to collect clinical waste from households that need it. This is a separate collection and will need to be arranged on an individual basis dependant on the needs of the resident. This service is free of charge.

If we have snow or icy conditions this winter, keep checking our website for details of bin collections in case this weather disrupts normal arrangements.

What is clinical waste?

Clinical waste is any waste which consists wholly or partly of:

  • human tissue
  • blood
  • other body fluid
  • drugs
  • other pharmaceutical products
  • swabs or dressings
  • syringes, needles or other sharp instruments

If you or someone in your household needs a clinical waste collection that is not dealt with by your healthcare visitor / carer, please contact us and we will make arrangements for a member of the waste team to provide advice.
