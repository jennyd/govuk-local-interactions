Councillors

The Council is run by Councillors who are elected by local residents in the ward they represent.  See our Councillors poster [843kb] for more information.

Questions

Close allOpen all

How are Councillors elected and what is the Council's political composition?

Councillors are elected for four years. We hold "elections by thirds". This means that one ward Councillor is elected each year - we don't hold elections on the fourth year.

We have 46 councillors representing 16 wards (three per ward except Mapledurham which has one).

Who are the Leader and Lead Councillors?

The Council appoints a leader who has overall responsibility for leadership of the Council.

The Leader is assisted by a Deputy Leader and by Lead Councillors, who are each responsible for a different area of the Council's Services. Councillors are also appointed to chair the Council's Committees.

The Leader of the Council is Jo Lovelock (Labour). 

Use this link to see a full list of Lead Councillors and Committee Chairs.

What do Councillors do?

Councillors have a variety of responsibilities which include:

  • Representing the interests of their ward constituents and wider community in the decision making processes of the Council
  • Making decisions at Council and Committee meetings
  • Advocating for ward constituents to help them in their dealings with the Council
  • Supporting local organisations
  • Campaigning on local issues
  • Developing links with all parts of the community.

You can find more details in the Council's Constitution.

See How we Make Decisions for more information about our decision making processes.

How do I contact a Councillor?

You can:

  • Search for your Ward Councillor by postcode to find their contact information
  • See our Ward Surgery Calendar for details of your local ward surgeries
  • See our Leaders and Chairs page to find a Councillor with a specific responsibility.

Or contact Councillor Services for advice.

Do Councillors get paid?

Councillors don't receive a salary or wage, but they are entitled to allowances and expenses to cover some of the costs of carrying out their public duties.

The Independent Remuneration Panel recommends a Scheme of Allowances for Councillors which includes:

  • A basic allowance paid to all Councillors and
  • Special responsibility allowances (for the leader and deputy leader of the Council, opposition group leaders, lead councillors and committee chairs)
  • Claims for travel, subsistence and dependent carer costs.

What is the "Code of Conduct" for Members?

The  Code of Conduct [58kb]sets out the standards conduct we expect Councillors to follow when carrying out Council or ward business. It doesn't apply to their personal lives. The Code also applies to any co-opted members of Council Committees.

The Code includes a requirement for Councillors to declare any interests that could influence any decisions they make. You can find a "Declaration of Interests" attached to each Councillor's profile page - see Councillors by Ward.

How do I make a complaint about a Councillor?

If you believe a Councillor has breached the Code of Conduct you can complain to the Council's Monitoring Officer using our online Councillor complaint form.

The Monitoring Officer will acknowledge your complaint and consider whether your complaint provides evidence to show the Councillor has breached the  Code of Conduct [58kb] while carrying out the business of the Council. He may:

  • Reject your complaint
  • Explore your complaint informally with you
  • Refer your complaint to the Councillor's political group for investigation under the their disciplinary procedure.

See our factsheet for an  overview of the Councillors complaints process. [64kb]
