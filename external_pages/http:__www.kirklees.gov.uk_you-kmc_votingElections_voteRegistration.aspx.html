Registering and voting links

Don't forget to respond to your Register of Electors 2015-16 Household Enquiry Form

If you haven’t replied, you will be receiving a reminder shortly, so please respond as soon as possible. The form tells you who is currently registered at your address.

You do need to respond to the form, even if there are no changes to the details. The information you provide will enable us to send a separate individual form to all the people in your household who are eligible and need to register.

You can provide the information requested online instead of posting the paper form to us. You will need the security codes printed on the form use this service. www.registerbyinternet.com/kirklees

Those who are eligible can go on line to register at Register to vote.

Individual Electoral Registration (IER) means that every individual is personally responsible for being on the register. Most people have automatically moved over to the new register by a process of matching names and addresses with government records, but some people may need to register again – for example if they’ve moved address.

  • Register to vote

    • Apply to register to vote
    • Its simple and easy to apply online.
    • You need your name, address, national insurance number and date of birth.

  • Open register form and information

    • There are two types of register, the electoral register and the open register.
    • You can opt in or out of the open register at any time.
    • You don't need to re-apply, just use our form.

Why you should register to vote

  • If your name is not on the register you will not be able to vote.
  • So that you can have a say in issues that affect you.
  • If you are not on the register you might be refused a loan, mobile phone contract or even be turned down for a bank account.

Who can apply to vote

  • Someone 16 or older ( although you cannot vote until you are 18) and a British Citizen, or
  • An Irish, qualifying Commonwealth, or European Union Citizen living in the UK.
  • Students can register at both their term time address and their home address.

Do you need to apply?

Register now if:

  • You are not registered already, or
  • You have recently moved house or changed your name, or
  • We have told you that your electoral details do not match other records

How to check that your name is on the register

You can check at any time of the year by contacting Electoral Services:

  • Contact Electoral Services

You can also visit these locations to check the register.

  • Huddersfield Town Hall
  • Dewsbury Customer Service Centre
  • Batley Library and Information Centre
  • Cleckheaton Customer Service Centre
  • Holmfirth Library and Information Centre

How to register if you move house

  • If you move house complete the voter registration form.
    • Register to vote
  • You can also contact
    • Contact Electoral Services
  • Your name will normally appear as an amendment to the register in 2-6 weeks.
  • We will also notify your old Electoral Registration Office if you have moved from another local authority.

How to register as an overseas elector

  • This is anyone living overseas who has been registered as an elector in the UK during the last 15 years.
  • Overseas electors cannot vote in local government elections.
