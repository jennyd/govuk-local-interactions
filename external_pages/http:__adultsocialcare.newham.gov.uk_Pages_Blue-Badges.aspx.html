Blue Badges

Page Content

What is a Blue Badge?

The Blue Badge scheme is for people with severe mobility problems who find it difficult to use public transport. It allows Blue Badge holders to park close to where they need to go.

You can use the badge when you are driving or are a passenger in a car. Other people such as your friends or relatives can't use the badge unless you are travelling with them.


Do I qualify for a Blue Badge?

You will qualify if you meet one of the following criteria:

  • are receiving Disability Living Allowance (Higher Rate Mobility. From June 2013 this will replaced by Personal Independence Payment (PIP).
  • are receiving a War Pension Mobility Supplement
  • are a registered blind person
  • use a motor vehicle that is supplied by a government health department

You may also qualify if you:

  • have severe disability in both upper limbs
  • drive a motor vehicle regularly but cannot turn the steering wheel even if that wheel is fitted with a turning knob
  • have a permanent and substantial disability which causes you to be unable to walk or very considerable difficulty in walking.

Children

Children under the age of 3 may be eligible for a badge if:

  • because of their condition, they must always be accompanied by bulky medical equipment
  • because of their condition, they must always stay near a motor vehicle so they can be treated in the vehicle or taken to a place where they can be treated

Restrictions

You can use the badge throughout the UK, the European Union and some other European countries.

You can not use the Blue Badge for:

  • off street car parks
  • private road
  • most airports

You should always make sure to check parking restrictions.


How to apply for a Blue Badge

You can apply online through GOV.UK


Supporting Information

You will need to provide a passport photograph and the means to pay the £10 fee (credit or debit card only)

Other documents that you may need include:

  • proof of address such as, a current council tax or utility bill, or bank statement
  • current photo identification such as, a passport or driving license
  • up to date supporting evidence such as, a letter of confirmation of Disability Living Allowance (Higher Rate Mobility) / PIP.
  • If you need an up to date copy of this letter telephone 08457 123 456.

Renewing your Blue Badge

If you are renewing your Blue Badge please make sure you apply at least four weeks before your badge expires. This will give us enough time to action your application and send your Blue Badge out to you by second class post. It is the blue badge holders responsibility to be aware of the expiry date of their badge.


What happens next?

Once we get your application, we will assess your eligibility and tell you the result. We will charge you £10 for your blue badge if your application is successful.


Appealing or making a complaint about a blue badge decision

If you want to appeal or make a complaint about a decision not to give you a blue badge, you have the right to ask for an appeal within one month of us telling you of our decision

You will need to write to:

Transport and Concessionary Travel Team
East Ham Customer Service Centre and Library
328 Barking Road
East Ham
London, E6 2RT

If you wish to make a complaint please click on the link below:

Make a complaint



If the Blue Badge is not right for you:

The Blue Badge may not be the right answer to your mobility issue. If so, you may wish to look into the following options:

  • London Taxicard Scheme - subsidised door-to-door transport for people who have serious mobility impairment and difficulty in using public transport.
  • Dial-a-ride- free door to door minibus scheme run by TfL for people with mobility problems
  • Freedom Pass - Free use of public transport for people with mobility problems.

Replacing Lost and Stolen Badges

If your Blue Badge is lost or stolen, you must get a police reference number before you can apply for a replacement. To apply for a replacement badge use the same process as if applying for a new badge.

Find out more from the section on How to Apply for a Blue Badge.

You should put the police reference number in your application. This is to make sure that your old badge is cancelled and help prevent misuse of it. We will charge £10 for all replacement badges.
