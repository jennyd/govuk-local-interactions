Are you being disturbed by noise?

Most people experience a noise problem at one time or another, whether it be from a commercial or industrial premises, or the house next door.

Noise problems can often be resolved simply by a polite, but nevertheless frank, approach to the person/s responsible for the noise, asking for their cooperation.

Good neighbourly relations will often involve a degree of 'live and let live' and the occasional noisy party, loud stereo or barking dog can to be tolerated.

Persistent disturbances, which by timing and duration can cause real inconvenience and anxiety arise when you have discussed the matter with the individuals concerned but the noise continues.

This noise could amount to a 'Statutory Nuisance' (a serious prolonged noise problem) and you may need to contact the council to investigate.

The term 'Statutory Nuisance' is not legally defined but there is an extensive body of case law to support it.  We would require a Nuisance Diary to be completed which will form the basis of any case we investigate.

Each case has to be decided upon its merits - what may be a nuisance in one area may not be in another.

The assessment by our Enforcement Officers has to take into account the circumstances which would be tolerated by an ordinary reasonable person.

If you, or the neighbour creating the noise are tenants of a housing association such as AmicusHorizon or Orbit, you should contact them first.  Housing Associations have their own procedures for dealing with noise complaints and they may be able to resolve the situation for you.

What can you do?

  1. Discuss the noise problem with the person responsible for it and make them aware of your concerns
  2. Speak to other neighbours affected and get them to support your claim if possible
  3. Keep detailed and accurate records of the noise incidents on the diary sheets sent to you
  4. Be prepared to make a formal statement and to attend court if required
  5. Be patient. The Council will try to resolve the complaint as quickly as possible but in some cases it can be a lengthy process, particularly if the noise is intermittent

What Hastings Borough Council can do

Following the receipt of a completed Noise Diary, a council officer will contact you within 5 working days to discuss your case.

They may visit the person responsible for the noise to advise them of the complaint and remedial action needed to prevent further noise nuisance.

If a visit to the person responsible is not made, we may write to them explaining the problem.

You will not be identified as the source of the complaint without your consent and in many cases, receiving this letter will be sufficient to solve the matter.

What happens if the noise continues?

If the noise continues to be unacceptable, we may install sound level monitoring equipment in your premises to obtain evidence to support the complaint.

We can also carry out personal visits to your home.

Statutory Nuisance

If the noise is considered a Statutory Nuisance, we can serve an Abatement Notice which is a legal document requiring the noise to be kept within acceptable levels.

We can also prosecute if evidence shows that the Abatement Notice has been breached. Currently the fines are up to £5,000 (£20,000 for commercial premises).

We can seize noise making equipment when a Statutory Nuisance has occurred in breach of the Abatement Notice.

Is it worth reporting noise nuisance?

Yes, it can be. If you are able to demonstrate that despite having adopted a reasonable approach from the outset, you have a genuine cause for concern, this will help us to pursue your case. Please contact us sooner rather than later with your completed diary. The quicker the Enforcement team are involved, the quicker they may be able to help resolve the problem. The following document contains guidance on filling in your Noise Nuisance diary and additional diary sheets:

  • Noise Nuisance diary guidance and additional diary sheets 

Most people take measures to reduce their noise levels when they are made aware of the problem. It's only in extreme cases that the Council needs to take formal action.

As an alternative, you can make a direct approach to the Magistrates Court if you feel that you are a victim of a noise nuisance. You can take this course of action if the Enforcement team can't gather sufficient evidence to proceed. The following guidance may help you if you decide to take this course of action:

  • Private Legal Action for Nuisance: advice for complainants

Court officers will also be able to explain the procedure and course of action open to you and the evidence needed to support your case.

If the Court is satisfied that a nuisance exists, or is likely to be repeated, an order direct can be made, compelling the person responsible to reduce or stop the nuisance or carry out works to avoid it in the future.
