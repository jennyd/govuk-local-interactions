    Citizenship ceremonies

    Page Content 1

    Every adult who is granted naturalisation or registration as a British citizen takes a citizenship oath and a pledge of commitment at a citizenship ceremony.

    If the Home Office approves your application for British citizenship you can make an enquiry with us to book a ceremony.

    • Make a ceremony booking enquiry

    Citizenship ceremonies celebrate the significance of becoming a British citizen and welcome new citizens into the community. Successful applicants for British citizenship must attend a ceremony to take an oath or affirmation of allegiance to the Crown and pledge their loyalty to the United Kingdom.

    Citizenship ceremonies are conducted by registration officers. Group ceremonies normally take place in the Civic Suite which is next to the Town Hall, but we can offer individual ceremonies at the Register Office for an additional fee.

    The format for citizenship ceremonies

    Ceremonies begin with a speech of welcome by the superintendent registrar. The ceremony also includes a welcome speech by a local dignitary, who gives information about the local community and the history of the borough.

    Taking the citizenship oath and pledge is a legal requirement and the key moment at which citizenship is conferred. New citizens are then called up one at a time to receive their certificate and information pack.

    The nationality certificate is presented by a local dignitary on behalf of the Home Secretary and can subsequently be used to support passport applications and to prove the individual is a British citizen.

    The ceremony will end with a closing speech and the playing of the national anthem. Afterwards there may be an informal celebration with light refreshments, when your guests can take photographs.
