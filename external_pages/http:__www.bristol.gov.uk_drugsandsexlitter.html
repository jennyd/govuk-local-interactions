Street care and cleaning

Report graffiti, fly-tipping, fly-posting, abandoned vehicles, dog mess, dead animals, overflowing bins, blocked drains and other street issues.

Information given on forms is strictly confidental.

Abandoned vehicles

Report an abandoned vehicle
We operate a service for the removal of abandoned vehicles from the highway.
Abandoned vehicles - more information

Cycle paths

We clear leaves from cycle paths. To report a problem with leaves on cycle paths, contact us at bristolparks@bristol.gov.uk and 0117 922 3719 with information on where the problem is.

Dead animal removal

Report a dead animal on the street cleansing form

We remove dead animals or birds from roads, pavements, public spaces or private gardens.

Remember

  • We're unable to remove dead animals or birds that are inaccessible such as roofs and guttering. We try to check all animals found for identification (including microchips).
  • If you request a dead pet to be removed there may be a charge for this service.

Dog and animal fouling (dog mess)

Report dog or animal mess on the street cleansing form

We have a responsibility to clear dog mess from the public highway as part of normal street cleansing.

Remember

  • It is an offence to not clear up after your dog
  • You can be fined £80

Call

Dog fouling - call 0117 922 2100 or report to the Dog Warden Service
Full dog bins - email bristolparks@bristol.gov.uk or call 0117 922 3719
Stray dogs - Dog Warden Service - 0117 922 2500

Drug and sex related litter

Report drug and sex litter on the street cleansing form

We remove needles, condoms and other litter of this type from private land, the highway, car parks and council-owned land provided access is available.

Call

Urgent weekend calls - Emergency Control Centre on 0117 922 2050

Fly-posting

Report fly-posting 

Remember

  • You can be fined £75.
  • Legal notices can be served on the promoting company or owner of the venue to remove the advertisements.

Fly-tipping (illegal dumping)

Report fly-tipping (illegal dumping)

We clear fly-tipping from the highway and council owned land generally within two working days, unless specialist arrangements are required for removal/disposal.

We do not clear fly-tipping from private land although we can take a report to pass to our enforcement team for investigation. Call 0117 922 2100.

Any information you can obtain is helpful

  • vehicle registration and description
  • description of the people seen fly-tipping
  • photographic evidence

Remember

Convictions for flytipping can carry a fine of up to £50,000 and/or up to five years imprisonment.

Graffiti

Report graffiti

Remember

If you are the owner of the property, an indemnity form needs to be completed. We cannot carry out any work until this has been received.

Grass cutting

Report grass that needs cutting.

We cut the grass on land we own to ensure safe use of the city's roads and pavements.

Litter and litter bins

Report dropped litter or overflowing litter bin on the street cleansing form

Remember

  • Litter includes all fast food litter, smoking related litter, such as cigarette ends and discarded chewing gum.
  • You can get an on the spot fine of £75.

Litter from businesses

If your staff or customers drop litter on or outside your premises you could be issued with a Litter Clearing Notice. Failure to comply can lead to a maximum fine of £2,500.

Street cleaning

Report a street that needs cleaning

You can report these for us to clean up:

  • blood
  • fuel, oil and chemicals
  • glass - broken or not
  • human excrement or vomit
  • leaves or blossom
  • paint - wet or dry
  • weeds

You an also report these - there's also more information about them on this page

  • dead animals
  • dog fouling
  • litter and litter bins overflowing
  • sex and drugs litter

Bristol roads are cleaned at least once every three weeks.
