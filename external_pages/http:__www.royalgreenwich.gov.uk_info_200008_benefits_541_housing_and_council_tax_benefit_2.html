Housing Benefit and Council Tax Support

Housing Benefit and Council Tax Support appeals

View pages in this section

If you disagree with a decision made on your claim for Housing Benefit or Council Tax Support you have the right to:

  • Ask us to explain the decision verbally or in writing
  • Ask us to review the decision
  • Appeal to an independent tribunal

You must make any request in writing within one calendar month of  the date on the letter informing you of the decision. You must also make it clear what part of the decision you disagree with.

Your signature

  • A legal requirement for an appeal is that it must be signed by the person making the appeal. This also applies to any documents you send to us.
  • Even if someone is acting on your behalf with your permission, you must sign any document or letter they provide to us.
  • This also means that you can only appeal by email, if you send a scanned document with your signature on it

How to appeal

You can challenge any decision we make on your Housing Benefit or Council Tax Support claim at any time if you think that the entitlement is wrong or the amount awarded to you is wrong. To do this, write in to request that we look at the decision again and give details of why you think it is incorrect.

There are two steps involved in the appeal process:

  1. Explanation

    You may ask for a written or a verbal statement detailing our reasons for the decision. You can also request a full breakdown of how your Housing Benefit or Council Tax Support was calculated.

  2. Review

    You must tell us why you think the decision is wrong and provide any extra details you think may be relevant. Your case will then be looked at by a separate team who will notify you of whether the original decision has been changed. If we cannot change the original decision:

    • If your appeal concerns Housing Benefit we will prepare a submission of your case and forward it to HM Courts and Tribunals Service (you will be advised of this in writing)
    • If you are challenging a decision on Council Tax Support you have the right of appeal to to an independent body called the Valuation Tribunal.

HM Courts and Tribunals Service (for appeals relating to Housing Benefit)

When your papers are received by HM Courts and Tribunals Service you will be sent an information pack acknowledging receipt of your appeal and providing you with full information about the process.

You will have an option of attending a tribunal ('oral hearing') or having your case heard without you ('paper hearing').

The Valuation Tribunal (for appeals relating to Council Tax Support)

If you still disagree with our decision on Council Tax Support, or you have not received a response within two months of your challenge, you can appeal to the Valuation Tribunal.

Please note: You may not agree with the Council Tax Support Scheme that Royal Borough of Greenwich has set up but please be aware that you can only appeal to the Valuation Tribunal against a decision regarding Council Tax Support and not against the scheme itself.
