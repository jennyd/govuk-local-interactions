Finding a school, communication equipment and transport for a child with SEND

Schools for a child with a statement of special educational needs / Education, Health and Care Plan (EHCP) or a disability (SEND)

You may wish to refer to our Local Offer for further information.

Please Note: This page refers to Statements of SEN (before 1 September 2014) and Education, Health and Care Plans (EHCPs) (after 1 September 2014). All existing statements of SEN will continue to have legal status beyond September 2014 until they are converted to new EHCPs.

Children with SEND are educated in local mainstream schools in Surrey whenever possible, and have access to the National Curriculum wherever possible.

Our Information for schools admissions – children with special educational needs booklet provides advice for you when choosing a school for your child. It also outlines the admissions process for:

  • primary and secondary education 
  • special provision centres
  • special schools admissions.

Our SEND administration team is responsible for placing pupils with statements of SEND  or EHCP. The team's contact details are in our 'Information for schools admissions – children with special educational needs' booklet.

Surrey Parent Partnership provides advice on planning for your child's move from primary to secondary school.

Expressing a preference for a school

If your child has a statement or an EHCP, you can express a preference for a school you wish your child to attend and we will consider it. Please bear in mind that only children whose needs cannot be met in a supported mainstream school are eligible to attend special schools.

If your child has SEND but no statement or Education, Health and Care Plan (EHCP)

If your child has SEND but no statement or EHCP, the Information for schools admissions – children with special educational needs booklet has useful information about how to select a school for your child. You may also want to speak with Surrey Parent Partnership for independent advice.

Moving to Surrey

If you are moving to Surrey with a child who has a special educational needs statement or an EHCP, you should speak to the relevant SEND team before you move. Their contact phone numbers are:

  • North-east SEND management 01737 737940
  • North-west SEND management 01483 518110
  • South-east SEND management 01737 737990
  • South-west SEND management 01483 517890

When you move, your child's statement or EHCP (along with all other documents relevant to your child's SEND, for example your child's individual education plan) will be transferred from the local education authority where you live to us. We then become responsible for making sure your child receives the support outlined in the statement / EHCP. We will have six weeks to decide whether to review the statement / EHCP and/or make any new assessment.

It may be that the move means your child is now too far away from the school named in the statement/EHCP to attend it; we will need to name a new school but we may need your child to go to a school of our choice temporarily, to allow enough time for you to visit schools and discuss options. We will always consult with you over changes to your child's statement or EHCP and you can express a preference for a school.

If your child has SEND but no statement/ EHCP , you should contact our admissions team.

Finding a school in Surrey

If you are looking for a school in Surrey there are several resources to support you:

  • our searchable directory of Surrey maintained schools and academies including specialist schools and specialist centres
  • a list of independent schools and non-maintained special schools across the country - these schools are registered with the Department for Education and charge fees for placements for children with SEND.

Communication support and specialist IT equipment

If your child is finding it hard to communicate by speech alone, the school (with your permission) may refer them for an augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) assessment with your child's speech and language therapist. AAC covers a huge range of techniques, which support or replace spoken communication. These include gesture, signing, symbols, word boards, communications boards and books as well as voice output communications aids (VOCA).

Specialist advisory teachers and a specialist ICT team make assessment referrals, supported by the school's special educational needs coordinator (SENCO).

The specialist team can:

  • assess and review your child's AAC and ICT needs
  • provide information, advice and support to professionals working with your child
  • monitor your child's use of their ICT or AAC equipment
  • organise countywide training for specialist software packages.

Transport for a child with SEND

If your child has a statement of SEND or an EHCP, we will provide home to school transport if they meet the distance criteria and are attending the nearest appropriate place of education. This is arranged as part of the placement process. Further details of the transport we provide for children with SEND are in our Home to school transport policy – special educational needs 2011.

For pre-school children with SEND, if your child has been given a statement / EHCP or SEND assessment placement at a special school or specialist nursery, we will provide transport if they:

  • need transport for health and safety reasons
  • live more than two miles from the placement we name.

If your child does not have a statement of SEND / EHCP, there is advice on the transport available and other schemes to help children in Surrey get to school quickly and safely.

*Surrey County Council is currently updating the way we provide home to school or college travel support for children and young people with either an education, health and care plan or a special educational needs statement.

To help us do this, we'd like council staff, children, young people, parents and carers to take part in an online consultation. Please share this travel assistance policy page with anyone you know who may be interested in taking part.
