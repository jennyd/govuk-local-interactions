Gritting & cold weather advice

Daily weather update for Middlesbrough and the planned gritting action as well as useful advice for coping with winter

This page will be regularly updated this winter with details of any disruption to Middlesbrough's roads due to bad weather.

Information can be found below on where and when the gritters will be in action, a link to the latest weather forecast, opening times and closures of venues and winter driving tips. If any school closures occur this will also be added here along with other useful information to help you cope with the winter weather.

For live updates follow us on twitter @mbrocouncil and look out for #mbrowinter.

Latest update - Friday 5th February

Cloudy this afternoon with drizzle merging into more persistent rain later.

Heavy rain this evening will tend to move away towards the middle of the night. After midnight it will become mostly dry with variable amounts of cloud and some clear periods. Turning chillier.

Cloud will thicken during the early morning tomorrow with rain spreading in from the south. This rain will turn heavier and more persistent in the afternoon.

Remaining wet into Saturday evening.

Gritting action not needed

 

School closures

There are currently no school closures in Middlesbrough due to the weather

 

Key stats for winter 2015/16

  • First grit this season - 20th November 2015
  • Number of grit runs completed - 25
  • Distance covered per grit (five routes) - 180 miles
  • Total time to grit all routes - 2 hours
  • Number of gritters - 7
  • Amount of grit - 3,200 tonnes with 2,000 tonnes on back order, further stocks available if necessary - 20/11/2015
  • Amount of grit used last year - 1,300 tonnes
 

Gritting routes map

Middlesbrough has five priority routes totalling 180 miles, details of which routes are due to be gritted will be listed within the latest update above. - View the gritting routes map
