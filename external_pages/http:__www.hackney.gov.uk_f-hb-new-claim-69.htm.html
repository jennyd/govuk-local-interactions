You can claim housing benefit and council tax reduction if you are on a low income.

Benefit claim FAQs and queries

Read our FAQs about benefit claims or submit a query:

Benefit claim questions

 

If you need support applying online please call us on 020 8356 3399. We can help you to complete the online form.

If you have claimed income support, job seekers allowance (income based), pension credit - guarantee credit or employment support allowance (income related), you may already have completed housing benefit forms with your application for these benefits. However, you will still need to make a separate claim for council tax reduction directly to us.

If you receive income support, job seekers allowance (income based), or employment support allowance (income related), you may get your full eligible rent paid, and 85% of your council tax liability. If you receive pension credit - guarantee credit, you may get your full eligible rent paid, and upto 100% of your council tax. You may receive a smaller amount if you have non dependants living with you.

If you do not get income support, job seekers allowance (income based), pension credit - guarantee credit or employment support allowance (income related), the amount of housing benefit and council tax reduction you get depends on your personal circumstances, how many people live with you, how much money you have coming in and how much you have in savings.

If you are of working age council tax reduction will not cover the first 15% of your council tax liability regardless of your income or circumstance.

If you apply for housing benefit or council tax reduction we can share information collected as part of your application with other council departments, other local authorities, the Department for Work and Pensions and, in some circumstances, your landlord and other organisations. For more details, read our privacy notice (PDF, 24KB).
