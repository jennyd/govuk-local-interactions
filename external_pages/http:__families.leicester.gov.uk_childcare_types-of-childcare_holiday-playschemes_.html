Holiday Playschemes

What are holiday playschemes?

Holiday playschemes provide fun and activities for children during the holidays. Some are all day, some are for only a few hours.

 

What's the difference between holiday playschemes and normal childcare?

Holiday playschemes usually only run for a few hours during the day. If you are working, you probably need full day childcare which can be offered by childminders and day nurseries (some day nurseries look after children up to 8 years old in the holidays).

 

If you would like a list of childminders or day nurseries in your area, you can either carry out your own search or contact your local Children's Centre for support. 

 

Planning ahead

If you would like to plan ahead for up and coming holidays, then the following playscheme is scheduled to run every school holiday. Download a copy here.

 

How can children with special needs access holiday playschemes?

Under Ofsted rules, registered playschemes must make sure that all reasonable steps are taken to ensure children with disabilities have access to the premises and are not treated less favourably than other children.

 

TAP (The Access Point) can provide support to enable children with disabilities to access mainstream holiday playschemes.  They can be contacted by phone on 0116 294 6056/0116 2946065 or email at tap@leicester.gov.uk

 

Do holiday playschemes have to be registered?

Holiday play schemes can be registered with Ofsted and inspected each year, provided they are caring for children eight years of age and under, for more than two hours every day. 

 

If they run for under 2 hours they do not have to be registered.  This is something to consider if you are thinking of claiming to help with the cost of childcare.

 

Help with the cost of childcare during the holidays

You can get help with the cost of childcare through the holidays in the same way you would get help throughout the year, as long as the childcare is registered with Ofsted. You will need to check with the playscheme that they are registered with Ofsted before you use them.

 

Remember the holidays when you're calculating the cost of childcare

If you are claiming help towards the cost of childcare, remember to include the costs of childcare in holidays when you are making your calculations.

 

Remember, the cost of childcare in the holidays may be higher than the cost during term time as you may be using childcare all day rather than a few hours before or after the school day. It's important that you include this in your yearly calculations.

 

Futher information on costs

If you would like further information about claiming help towards costs of childcare in the holidays, contact HMRC on 0345 300 3900.

 

Questions to ask

Many of the playschemes will be registered with Ofsted. Before you send your child to a holiday playscheme, it's important to check it out first. If you would like some ideas about what questions to ask, or what to look for when you visit, click here for more information.
