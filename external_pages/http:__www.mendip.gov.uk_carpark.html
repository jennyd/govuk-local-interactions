Car Parks

Mendip District Council operates 44 car parks throughout the district. Of these seven are free, three are for residents only, three are for business use only and the remainder are pay & display. Charges and operating times vary between car parks.

Parking bays may be hired from Mendip District Council.

Mendip District Council has no responsibility for Aster Ltd car parks or private car parks.

Enforcement of parking regulations (on and off street) is the responsibility of Somerset County Council and their service provider NSL.

Penalty Charge Notices

Any Penalty Charge Notice (issued on or after 11th June 2012) is the responsibility of Somerset County Council.

Details of how to pay the PCN are available on the reverse of the ticket. The telephone number for any PCN payments or queries is 0333 999 8889. PCN and general enquires can be sent to somerset@parkingenquiry.co.uk or to Somerset County Council, PO Box 167, Sheffield, S98 1JN. For further information visit the Somerset County Council website.

Disabled Parking

Due to the increasing number of Blue Badge holders, designated spaces tend to fill up quickly, therefore, Blue Badge holders may park free of charge in any marked bay (except Greenbank Pool, Residents Only & Service Yard areas). All major car parks have designated disabled bays. Please see the tariff boards in the car parks for any time restrictions and additional requirements that apply.

  • The Blue Badge holder must display the badge in a prominent position at the front of the vehicle showing the expiry date.
  • Failure to comply with this regulation may result in a Penalty Charge Notice being issued.

When using car parks with a time restriction on them, Blue Badge holders are required to display the clock part of their badge as well. Please see tariff board in each car park for specific instructions.

The renewals and issuing of Blue Badges for the Mendip area is carried out by Somerset County Council Social Services.

Caravans and Motor Homes

There is no specific parking allocation within Mendip for caravans and motor homes, with the exception of Clarks Village (see below).

Caravans and motor-homes may park in all major car parks but payment must be made for the number of spaces occupied. (The 1525 kg weight limit primarily applies to commercial vehicles only.)

Caravans and motor homes may not use coach or bus bays.

Caravan and motor-home parking is available at Clarks Village. This parking is located in the car park off Southleaze Road. Customers wishing to use this facility should follow the signs from Southleaze car park.

Adverse Weather  

The Council will take reasonable action to ensure Council owned car parks are safe to use, dependent upon resources available. Priority will be given to car park entrances and access routes used by a high number of pedestrians. Depending on the scale and severity of the weather event, some low priority areas may not be cleared for several days. Clearance of any car park will not start until the highway is clear to allow vehicular access. Drivers are responsible for the safe movement of their vehicles and should drive with care when using the car parks during icy or snowy conditions. The Council does not carry out precautionary salting of car parks. As with minor roads and pavements, pedestrians and drivers need to make their own assessment as to whether a car park is safe to use during snowy or icy weather. In the event of any gritting being undertaken priority will initially be given to the following car parks:

  • Cattle Market Car Park, Frome
  • St Johns Car Park, Glastonbury
  • Commercial Road Car Park, Shepton Mallet
  • Northside Car park, Street
  • Union Street Car Park, Wells
