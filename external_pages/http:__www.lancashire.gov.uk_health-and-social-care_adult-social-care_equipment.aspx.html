Equipment

If you are struggling with sight loss, hearing or both (even though you wear glasses/hearing aids) or if you have difficulty with everyday tasks such as:

  • getting in to and out of your property
  • getting up and down stairs
  • getting in and out of the bath
  • making your own meals or even carrying a drink

You may wish to find out about equipment or adaptations to help you in your home

If you have a district nurse or therapists involved in your care they can provide advice and may be able to offer some equipment to help you.

If you need more than just basic equipment you may need to consider Telecare services. Telecare provides support and reassurance to you, family and carers. It uses wireless sensors monitored by a call centre 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. It provides an early warning that a person:

  • has fallen and is unable to call for help
  • has a tendency to wander away from the house
  • has a medical condition which can lead to seizures or blackouts

Telecare can also give an early warning if there is:

  • a flood from the bath or sink
  • a build up of smoke or gas within the home
  • extremes of temperature
  • a requirement for medication reminders

You can buy your own equipment or Telecare without coming to us. For more advice contact:

  • equipment providers - you can find them on our safe trader website (external link)
  • Telecare providers
  • home improvement agencies
  • AskSARA is an online self-assessment that asks you questions about your needs and then suggests equipment and products that will help in solving any problems you might have. AskSARA is extremely useful if you are not sure what equipment is available or which items you should choose. The questions and product guidance are written by Occupational Therapists from the Disabled Living Foundation, so you can be sure it is expert advice.

More information about 'equipment' options

If you think you, a family member or friend needs adult social care support please see 'getting our support' information.

You may be contacted by the Occupational Therapy service who can advise on services that help people remain in their own homes for as long as they want to.

If you are eligible, we can recommend equipment and provide you with a prescription to obtain simple equipment from local retailers (external link) across Lancashire. This gives you more choice and control over the type of equipment you can choose and where you get it from.

What we don't provide

Lancashire County Council is not responsible for services that the NHS is required to provide. For example; wheelchairs, beds and walking aids.

If your needs are mostly health related the NHS is responsible for supporting you.

For more information see 'NHS Choices What is NHS continuing healthcare' (External link).
