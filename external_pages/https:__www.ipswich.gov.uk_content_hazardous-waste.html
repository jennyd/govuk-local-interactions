Disposing of hazardous chemicals, paint and asbestos

You are here

You are here: Home » Waste and recycling » Household waste » Disposing of hazardous chemicals, paint and asbestos

Hazardous waste includes:

  • DIY products e.g. varnishes, paint stripper and some wood treatments.
  • Flammable liquids e.g. white spirit, paraffin and wood dyes.
  • Garden chemicals/pesticides e.g. weed killers and insect killers.
  • Poisons e.g. rat and mouse poisons
  • Household cleaning products and chemicals e.g. toilet cleaners, drain cleaners and bleach.
  • Motoring products e.g. antifreeze and brake/clutch fluid.

If you need to dispose of the above chemicals you can apply online using the button below, or email us a list of what you have for disposal, for example the number of tins or bottles and the type of waste they contain.

The cost for collection is £39.80 which is payable at the time of booking. If you are on "means tested benefit" (pension credit, Income Support or Job Seekers Allowance, income based) you may be entitled to a free collection. Call us to check.

Collection will take place within 4-6 weeks.

 

Disposing of paint

If you have less than five nearly empty tins of paint they can be disposed of in your black bin as long as the following action has been taken:

  • If the paint is emulsion, you must add some sand to the tin until the paint has been soaked up.
  • If the paint is gloss, it needs to be left out until the paint has gone hard.

Once this has been done you can place the tins into bags in your black bin.

To dispose of new or full tins of paint safely apply online using the button below, or email us a list of what you have for disposal, such as the number of tins and the type of waste they contain.

The cost for collection is £39.80 which is payable at the time of booking. If you are on "means tested benefit" (pension credit, Income Support or Job Seekers Allowance, income based) you may be entitled to a free collection. Call us to check.

Collection will take place within 4-6 weeks.

Paint cannot be disposed of at any of the Household Waste Recycling Centres in Ipswich.

Asbestos waste

We will arrange for the collection of domestic asbestos waste from Ipswich households. This service is only for small items of waste e.g. a water tank; ironing board, up to 3 roofing sheets or a length of downpipe.

The cost for collection is £72.60 which is payable at the time of booking. If you are on "means-tested benefit" (pension credit, Income Support or Job Seekers Allowance, income based) you may be entitled to a free collection. Call us to check.

Following DEFRA guidelines, dismantled garages, sheds, outhouses etc. are classed as construction waste and are liable for additional collection and disposal charges.  Ipswich Borough Council's approved contractors Enviroco are able to provide a quote to clear this type of waste.  Please contact Enviroco direct on 01493 856722 or email info@enviroco.co.uk.
