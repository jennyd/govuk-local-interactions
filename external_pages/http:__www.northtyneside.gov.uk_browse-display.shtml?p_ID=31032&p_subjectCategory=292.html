Noise pollution

Noise is normally thought of as unwanted sound. The sound could be too loud, too intrusive, occurring at the wrong time or because it is sudden and unexpected.

Excessive noise can make life a misery and is a source of irritation and stress. It can affect the quality of their life and peaceful enjoyment of their homes.

Some noise will always be audible especially for those living in flats or semi-detached properties as no house is totally soundproof. However you may be disturbed because the neighbours are acting unreasonably or because the insulation between the properties is poor.

It is recommended to try and approach your neighbour about the noise as they may not be aware that they are causing such a disturbance and to discuss a compromise. A leaflet on neighbourhood noise is available on related documents below.

Dog Barking

Dog barking can also cause a annoyance if they bark at unreasonable times or are barking and howling for prolonged periods of time. Defra have provided a free advice leaflet here for dog owners to help them to resolve dog baring issues.

Burglar Alarm

Burglar Alarms if falsely sounding are an annoyance to your neighbours. It is recommended that burglar alarms are serviced regularly. Burglar alarms can malfunction and if this occus on holiday it may be necessary for the council to serve a statutory notice and silence the alarm. It is recommended that residents take up a free registration scheme for your alarms providing telephone numbers and contact names of keyholders.

For further information on domestic noise solutions or to register your burglar alarm please refer to leaflets below or contact on 6436100.

Construction Sites

Noise from constuction sites is unavoidable however all builders and contractors should show consideration to their neighbours. As a general rule, where residential occupiers are affected by noise or vibration,it is expected that works of demolition and construction shall be carried out during normal working hours:-

08:00 to 18:00 hours Monday to Friday; and

08:00 to 14:00 hours on Saturdays; and

no working on Sundays or Bank Holidays.

These times may not always apply particularly with regard to work on the railway line where they need to provide a public transport service or for health and safety reasons or in case of emergency.

If it is found that a construction site is causing a disturbance beyond these hours or is not taking reasonable steps to minimise the noise the council can serve a section 60 Control of Pollution Notice. This notices requires the developer to have regard to the British Standard for noise and vibration control BS5228 to ensure they follow best practical means.

Alternatively the developer may prior to demolition or construction submit a prior consent form for the work. A copy of the form is available for download and must be submitted to the pollution team in Environmental Health.
