Contact us

Adult Social Services is changing, we will no longer be operating through the Access team. Telford & Wrekin Council has commissioned a new service called My Choice and this will be starting from the 9th November 2015.

My Choice is an information, advice and advocacy service for adults who live in Telford.

If you are unable to find what you are looking for on MyLife or feel you need some more support to access information, advice or advocacy you can contact My Choice.

You can contact My Choice on 01952 459 252 the service is open from Monday to Friday from 9am till 5pm.

My Choice provide information and advice on:

  • Access to carers services
  • Welfare benefits for carers and cared for
  • Community care
  • Service to promote and support independence
  • Budgeting and paying for care
  • Personal budgets
  • Supporting and coping strategies for dealing with long term conditions

There are also sources of information and advice from Council services and other organisations available at Southwater One. Click here to view the list of services at Southwater One and opening times.

Adult Safeguarding Team

If you are worried about a vulnerable person being abused or neglected by others, please see our Safe from Harm page for information on how to report your concerns.

Emergency Duty Team

The Emergency Duty Team (EDT) provides an emergency social work service for urgent situations which arise out of normal office hours and which cannot be left with an appropriate degree of safety until the next normal working day.

The number to call is 01952 676500.

The service operates Monday to Thursday 5pm to 9am and from 5pm Friday through to 9am Monday. Also during Bank and Public Holidays.

Sensory Impairment

If you have a sensory impairment you can Text (SMS) 07797 873 866.

If you can not hear or speak on the phone you may find NGTS.org.uk helpful.

Drug & Alcohol Recovery Services

You can contact the Drug & Alcohol Recovery Service (DARS) on 01952 381 777 for all enquiries relating to any type of alcohol or drug abuse. Please see the DARS information pages for further information on available services.

Mental Health

To access Mental Health services you will need to contact your GP.

If you are experiencing a Mental Health Crisis you can contact either Crisis Resolution / Home Treatment on 01952 632 452 who provide crisis support during the day and night or you can contact the out of hours Crisis helpline on 0300 124 0364 this service is available Sunday to Thursday from 6pm till 2am and Friday to Saturday from 6pm till 6am.

Carers Emergency Response Care Service

If you are a carer a new service has been locally developed, which provides peace of mind to family Carers when a crisis or emergency occurs, this is known as the 'Emergency Response Carers Service (ERCS)'. View more information about the ERCS.

Missing or out of contact client – Police

In all missing persons cases it is advised that you first contact the police and file a Missing Persons report, listen to the police advice given and act accordingly.

Adults and children who go missing from social services care invariably place themselves, and often others, at risk. Every "missing" episode should, therefore, attract proper attention from the professionals involved with the child and those professionals, in turn, must work together to make sure a consistent and joined up message is given to the client on their return.

The police are frequent partners of social services in managing "missing" episodes and it is, therefore, important that staff in both agencies work together.

Contact the police by telephone:

Non-emergency – 101

Emergency - 999
