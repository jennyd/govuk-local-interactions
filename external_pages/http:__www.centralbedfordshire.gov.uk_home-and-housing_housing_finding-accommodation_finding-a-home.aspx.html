Choice Based Lettings

Choice Based Lettings

With the introduction of the Choice Based Letting Scheme, customers now have a say in which property the council will offer you. Customers who register on the scheme are given access to a database of social housing and select the property they would like the council to offer to them. 

Choice Based Lettings scheme

The key elements of this scheme address customer choice, balance housing needs and provide transparency about the selection and allocation of all social housing. 

Local councils and housing associations work closely in terms of building and allocating properties.  

Central Bedfordshire's partners in this sub regional choice based lettings scheme are Aragon Housing Association, BPHA, Luton Borough Council, and Bedford Borough Council. 

Bidding for a property

In Bedfordshire the scheme includes advertising all the vacant properties that become available.  All applicants waiting for housing can “bid” or express an interest in a property.  The person with the highest priority for that size property and waiting the longest will be the person who it is allocated to.

Each of the main housing providers will also accept bids for a small proportion of their vacancies from applicants who live in other parts of Bedfordshire.  

The winning bid

Feedback is given on who successfully bid on previously advertised vacancies, stating what priority the successful applicant had, and how long they had been waiting.  

Housing allocations - rehousing decision appeal

Under the Choice Based Letting Scheme offers are normally made to customers at their own request. However in some cases the council may decide to make an offer to a customer and where they refuse the offer the council may prevent them from making further bids.  Under these circumstances a customer may choose to appeal.    

In cases where the customer receives an offer following a homeless application they have a statutory right to request a review under s202 of the Housing Act 1996 as amended. This is a particular concern when the refusal of the offer could lead to the council ending its duty to make any further offers.

Introductory Tenancies

Central Bedfordshire Council now offers Introductory Tenancies for new council tenants.

What is an Introductory Tenancy?

An Introductory Tenancy is a 12 month probationary tenancy given to new tenants.

How does it work?

For the first 12 months of any new tenancy, the tenant will be on probation. If successful the tenancy automatically reverts to being a Secure Tenancy. If within the first 12 months any issues such as Anti-Social Behaviour arise, then the Council can decide not to offer a Secure Tenancy and can either take legal action or extend the probation period by a further 6 months.

What support will be offered?

Estates Officers will monitor new tenants and intervene if there is a breach of tenancy and will offer support and advice to avoid further incidents.

Who can offer support?

Estates Officers, Tenancy Enforcement Officer, Tenant Liaison Officers, Police, Social Services, Gateway Housing Support or any other agency that can provide a positive input to the process of ensuring a successful outcome for the tenant.

Who will it affect?

Introductory Tenancies will only be given to new tenants from an agreed date if they are approved for use.

What will be the benefits to new and existing tenants/residents?

Introductory Tenancies benefit residents by allowing the Council the opportunity to deal with issues of Anti-Social Behaviour perpetrated by new tenants faster and to intervene earlier in order to resolve breaches of tenancy.



Housing Associations

Housing associations provide homes for people in housing need. They don't make a profit - any extra money goes back into providing services, repairing homes or building new houses.

What type of housing do they provide?

Housing associations provide low-cost housing - also known as affordable housing. There are several types of affordable housing available. The most common locally are homes for rent, and homes for shared ownership (shared ownership is where you part buy, part rent a home).

Why does the council work with housing associations?

Not all people can afford to buy a home. Through our housing strategy, we aim to get homes built to a high standard, in the places they are needed, at prices that people can afford. There are several ways that we can help to do this by:

How can I apply for a new housing association home?

Usually, all new homes are let to people nominated by the council. So if you want to apply for a brand new home, you should contact our Rehousing Team.

How can I apply for other housing association homes?

When someone moves out of a housing association home, things are slightly different. We can only nominate a certain amount of customers for housing association vacancies. (Usually for every two vacancies, we can nominate one person.)

Do housing association tenants have the same rights as council tenants?

Housing associations provide "assured tenancies" to their tenants (this is a contract between the tenant and the housing association).The rights are broadly similar to those of a council tenant, but there is no Right to Buy on housing association homes.

Our current housing association partners are:

  • Aragon Housing Association

  • Bedfordshire Pilgrims Housing Association
  • Chiltern Hundreds Housing Association

We work with our partners wherever possible, because they are committed to helping us achieve key targets in our housing strategy.

We set targets for new housing association homes in Central Bedfordshire, based on housing needs surveys and other information. We want to make sure that affordable housing is available for people who need it.

Homeless

Can I stay where I am?

Bearing in mind the shortage of affordable housing in Central Bedfordshire, it is important to consider whether moving is actually going to benefit your circumstances. In some cases it may not be possible to stay where you are, and you should definitely seek urgent advice if you do not feel safe at your current home.

If you would like to stay at your current property but need help with adaptations or security measures, we may be able to help.

If you have affordability issues or are having disagreements with another household member, we can offer mediation with your landlord or refer you to a debt specialist.

If you are currently living with parents or family and have been asked to leave it may be possible to talk with your family to extend your stay while you look for your own accommodation.

For more information, please click on the link for the Maintaining Existing Accommodation leaflet (PDF 269KB) 

Finding a home in the private rental market

Central Bedfordshire Council's Lets Rent housing option is for people who are either threatened with homelessness or in need of housing.
It allows families or individuals to have a choice of good quality, well managed, private sector accommodation in the local area. For landlords, Lets Rent has been designed to maximise rental income and protect long term residential property investment

Assistance with deposit or rent in advance

People who are on benefits and cannot afford to pay rent in advance or a rental deposit can apply for a loan from the Department of Work and Pensions (DWP) website. The DWP will consider applications and depending on the applicant's circumstances may make a loan. Repayment is usually by a fixed amount deducted from weekly benefit payments.

If you need help applying for a loan, you can get advice from the Housing Options Team.

Local Housing Allowance (formerly Housing Benefit)

Local Housing Allowance and council tax benefit help people to pay their rent and council tax if their income is low. Anyone who has to pay council tax, rent or both for their home may be entitled to claim these benefits. You do not have to be out of work to make a claim. If you are in danger of losing your tenancy because you have fallen behind with the rent or are not complying with another condition of your tenancy we can arrange for you to be given help to manage your tenancy better which may avoid you losing your home.

Homelessness Review


The Homelessness Act 2002 places a legal duty on local authorities to undertake a comprehensive review of homelessness in their area and produce a Homelessness Strategy every 5 years based on the findings of the review.

Central Bedfordshire Council undertook this review in Autumn 2014. Housing Policy page

Want to make your own home secure from a perpetrator

The Sanctuary Scheme provides security measures to allow survivors of domestic violence to remain in their home where they choose to do so, where safety can be guaranteed and the violent partner no longer lives within the home. It is available across all tenures (private property and social housing) where the landlord has given permission for the work to be carried out. It consists of additional security to any main entrance doors to the accommodation and locks to any vulnerable windows. Wherever possible we provide a safe room in the home secured with a solid core door and additional locks. This service is only provided where it is the clear choice of the survivor.
The Sanctuary Scheme is designed to enable survivors to stay in their own homes where this is possible and desired, rather than being forced to move and disrupting education, employment and family networks. These problems can be exacerbated by survivors of domestic abuse frequently being forced to move on numerous occasions

For more information, please use the following link to open The Sanctuary Scheme leaflet. (PDF 283KB)

Want to buy your own property and need some help?

Low cost shared ownership exists to help people who cannot afford to buy a home outright to buy just part of their home, if they qualify under the council's shared ownership policy. Having brought a part-share in a home, the applicant then pays rent to a housing association for the remainder of the property. If they wish, the applicant may at a later date wish to buy more of the property, even buying it outright.
Applicants are responsible for paying for the valuation and legal fees. They are totally responsible for the maintenance and upkeep of the property

For more information, please use the following link to open the Shared Ownership Scheme Shared Ownership Scheme leaflet (PDF 273KB)

Joining the Council's Housing Register

In most cases this is no longer seen as a realistic housing option, as it can take years before families can be rehoused into social housing. Unless you have medical, social or welfare grounds for additional priority, it is unlikely that you will be rehoused through this route at all.


The exception to this is for applicants who are aged over 50 or 60 years old. This is because there are more elderly and sheltered housing units available in comparison to other general needs housing.

To join the register either call us on  0300 300 8302 email customers@centralbedfordshire.gov.uk We will help you to complete an application to the Council's Housing Register if you have not done so. If you are already registered on the list you may be entitled to additional priority as a result of your changed circumstances

Frequently Asked Questions

Below is a quick reference guide to some of the more commonly asked questions regarding leasehold properties:

Q: How can I report and / or chase a repair? 
A: By ringing the freephone number 0800 074 0263 and selecting the appropriate option.

Q: How can I make a payment?
A: Click on the "Do it online" tab above and select "Sundry", "Sundry Invoices" and have your invoice number to hand. Alternatively, you can ring 0300 300 8030 and select option 5.

Q: When can I expect my next bill?
A: Bills are raised quarterly, in advance, so you should receive them in March, June, September and December.

Q: Why have I received five bills this year when I have previously only received four?
A: Following a review conducted on the leasehold service, with a view to easing understanding, it was decided that supplementary bills (or the "fifth bill") would be raised for any shortfall from the previous year, from 2013/14 onwards. Previously, this shortfall was added to the October and January quarters of the year.

Q: What is a Section 20 Notice?
A: A Section 20 Notice is a legal requirement that CBC must issue to affected leaseholders in the event that works totalling more than £250.00 per leaseholder, or £100.00 if it is a long-term contract (over 12 ?months), are required to the block of flats in which your property is situated.

Q: Can I extend my lease?
A: Yes, at a cost. You will need to contact our Legal Department for details.

Q: What is the "management fee" portion of my service charge?
A: The management fee is a calculation of officer time and administration spent working on leasehold issues. This will include the Leasehold Officer, Surveyors, Maintenance Inspectors, Neighbourhood Estate Officers etc.

Q: When is my block of flats due to be decorated?
A: External decorations are done on a cyclical programme: Timber components every 7 years and masonry every 10 years.

Q: Water has leaked into my flat from the flat above and caused damage to my property. What can I do?
A: If the leak is from a communal or shared pipe, or from the flat of a CBC tenant, it will be CBC’s responsibility to rectify the fault but you must contact your insurers to register a claim for any damages. If the leak is from something solely servicing the flat above and they are also leaseholders, it is their responsibility to correct the fault and you must contact your insurers to register a claim for damages.

Leasehold Valuation Tribunal

First Tier Tribunal (formerly Leasehold Valuation Tribunal or “LVT”) can be used to resolve disputes between leaseholders and landlords.  

For further information go to Lease Advice.
