Concessionary Travel Pass

Eligible older adults and disabled adults are entitled to free off-peak travel on local buses anywhere in England.

This bus pass can be used anywhere in England between 9.30am and 11pm, Monday to Friday and all day at weekends and on Bank Holidays.

In the West Midlands you can also travel free on Ring and Ride and train and Metro services from 9.30am until the end of daytime services. These benefits are provided by Centro except for Ring and Ride which is run by the charity West Midlands Special Needs Transport Limited.

If you regularly want to travel before 9.30am on weekdays in the West Midlands area you can buy a ticket for use before 9.30am. For current prices please ring the Centro ticketing team on 0845 303 6760 or follow the link to their website: www.networkwestmidlands.com

Who is an eligible older person?

If you live in England, you'll be entitled to a bus pass giving free off-peak travel on local buses when you reach the 'eligible age'. If you were born after 5 April 1950, the age you become eligible is tied to changes in the State Pension age for women. This affects both men and women.

If you were born before 6 April 1950

You're eligible for an older person's bus pass from your 60th birthday, if you were born before 6 April 1950.

Women born after 5 April 1950

If you're a woman born after 5 April 1950, you'll become eligible for an older person's bus pass when you reach pensionable age.

Men born after 5 April 1950

If you're a man born after 5 April 1950, you'll become eligible when you reach the pensionable age of a woman born on the same day. To check if youare eligible follow the link to National West Midlands.

Who is an eligible disabled person?

if you live permanently in the West Midlands county and meet certain criteria you may be entitled to a free travel pass from Centro. Centro can issue passes to people who qualify under any of the seven categories of disability defined by the Transport Act 2000. The qualifying conditions of the seven categories are very specific and your disability must be long term. The categories are:

  • Registered blind or partially sighted
  • Severely or profoundly deaf
  • Without speech
  • Disability or injury which has a substantial and adverse effect on ability to walk
  • Loss of use of both arms or without arms
  • Learning disability
  • Would be refused a licence to drive a motor vehicle, or have had a licence withdrawn because of uncontrolled epilepsy, severe mental disorder, liable to sudden attacks of giddiness or fainting, established dementia conditions
