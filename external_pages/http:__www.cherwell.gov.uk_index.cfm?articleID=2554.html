Water companies are responsible for the maintenance and repair of private sewers.

How do the changes affect me?

Prior to 1 October you and your neighbours would have be collectively responsible for maintenance and repair of private sewers. We had an active role in organising unblocking and repairing of private sewers because it had been difficult, in practice, for groups of owners to make the necessary arrangements, but our role has always been one of enforcement and we have sought to recover our costs where possible.

Some of these private sewers have given repeated problems and needed regular unblocking. As a result you may have become used to phoning us when there are problems. We will no longer be responsible.

The water companies will have written to you to notify you of the changes. 

Who should I contact

Problems with blocked sewers should now be referred to the your water company. Details below:

  • Thames Water Telephone 0845 920 0800
  • Severn Trent 0800 783 4444
  • Anglian Water 0845 714 514.

Water companies will pay for future repair and maintenance for which you were previously responsible.

You will continue to be responsible for the maintenance and repair of you own drains, that is, for all the pipes on your own land which serve only your property. We do not provide a service for this work.

If your private sewer connects to a cesspool or septic tank rather than a public sewer or your private sewer connects to a private sewer by means of a private pumping station you may still be responsible. We will be responsible for enforcement if required.

Practical definitions:

Drain - A pipe serving property on land in separate ownership, effectively a pipe serving a single home.

Sewer - A pipe serving property on more than one plot of land, effectively a pipe serving more than one home.
