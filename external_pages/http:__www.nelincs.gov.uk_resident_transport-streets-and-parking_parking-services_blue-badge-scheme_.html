The Blue Badge (Disabled Parking) Scheme provides a national arrangement of parking concessions for those people who have a permanent or substantial disability to allow them to park close to their destination, when travelling independently as either a driver or passenger. The Blue Badge allows the vehicle in which the badge holder travels to park in designated areas.

1) Apply online for a blue badge on the Gov site - https://www.gov.uk/apply-blue-badge

2) Pay online for a blue badge ...
Only make this payment, after submitting an application, receiving notification that your badge has been approved and having received the new badge reference number -www.nelincs.gov.uk/pay-it-blue-badge - the cost will be £10.00.

If you already have a blue badge and it has expired, it is no longer valid and must not be used. It is your responsibility to reapply for a badge as a reminder letter will not be sent. You should allow a minimum of 4 weeks from the time the application and all required evidence has been received.

There is more information about the Blue Badge scheme available on Directgov website.

This authority is under a duty to protect the public funds it administers, and to this end may use the information you have provided on your application form for the prevention and detection of fraud. It may also share this information with other bodies responsible for auditing or administering public funds for these purposes. For further information, see our privacy notice or contact Internal Audit on 01472 323903.

A permanent and substantial disability is one that is likely to last for all of your life. Eligibility is not determined on the basis of a particular diagnosis or condition. It is the effect of the permanent disability on your ability to walk that is important. Medical conditions such as asthma, autism, psychological/behavioural problems, Crohn’s disease/incontinent conditions and Myalgic Encephalomyelitis (M.E.) are not in themselves a qualification for a badge. People with these conditions may be eligible for a badge under this criterion, but only if they are unable to walk or have very considerable difficulty in walking, in addition to their condition.

Some people may also be eligible for a badge if they drive a motor vehicle regularly, have a severe disability in both arms and are unable to operate, or have considerable difficulty operating, all or some types of parking meter.

To qualify for a badge under this criterion you will need to show that you meet all of the following:

  • that you drive a vehicle regularly
  • that you have a severe disability in both arms;
  • and that you are unable to operate, or have considerable difficulty operating, all or some types of on-street parking equipment

Most drivers with disabilities in both arms drive a vehicle with adapted steering.

Only drivers with the most severe disabilities in both their arms will be eligible under this criterion. It does not apply to people who, for example, have difficulty carrying parcels or shopping or to people who travel solely as a passenger.

How do I apply for a Blue Badge?

There are two ways to apply for a Blue Badge:

  • You can apply online at https://www.gov.uk/apply-blue-badge
  • You can download the Blue Badge Application Form from the right hand side of this page

Please note: If you already have a blue badge and it has expired, it is no longer valid and must not be used. It is your responsibility to reapply for a badge as reminder letter will not be sent. You should allow a minimum of 4 weeks from the time the application and all required evidence has been received.

Completed application forms can be returned by post to:

Blue Badge Administration
Municipal Offices
Town Hall Square
Grimsby
DN31 1HU

  • Please ensure that sufficient postage is paid

Badges are valid for 3 years or until the Higher Rate Mobility Component of the Disability Living Allowance or Personal Independence Payment expires, if this is less than 3 years.

Important: please refer to the next section entitled “What evidence do I need to apply?”

What evidence do I need to apply?

In support of your Blue Badge application, you must provide the following:

  • A coloured passport sized photograph of yourself - taken within the last three months. This can be taken using a digital camera or mobile phone which you can print and enclose with your application

The following evidence should be provided as photocopies only:

  • Evidence of your residency in North East Lincolnshire – by indicating on the application form, you can give your consent for us to check our Council Tax database or the Electoral Roll. Alternatively, please provide a copy of either your Valid Driving Licence, Council Tax bill, Letter from the Department for Works and Pensions, or Housing Benefit Notification dated within the last 12 months
  • If you are in receipt of the Higher Rate ‘Mobility’ Component of the Disability Living Allowance, or in receipt of Personal Independence Payment (PIP) and receive 8 points or more for ‘Moving around’ you must provide a letter from the Department for Work and Pensions dated within the last 12 months, which includes the page with your name and address and the page which shows your point score in the moving around category. This will also act as proof of your residency
  • Proof of your Identity – either a copy of your Birth Certificate, Marriage/Divorce Certificate, Passport or Valid Driving Licence

Please note: your application will be delayed if you do not provide the above information when submitting your application form.

How can I pay for a Blue Badge?

Once your Blue Badge has been approved, we will contact you with your unique Badge Number giving details of how to make payment. The cost will be £10.00.

Dependent upon the information provided on your application we will contact you in one of the following ways:

  • If an email address has been provided, we will email you
  • If a mobile phone number has been provided (either your own or a relative’s) we will send a text message
  • If neither of the above have been provided we will write to you

Once the payment has been received your badge will be ordered and dispatched directly from the printers to your address by Royal Mail second class post. We would anticipate this taking between 5 and 10 working days from the order date.

If after this period of time your badge has not been received then please contact us by email  or contact the Call Centre on 01472 313131 in order that we may investigate.

Can I apply for a child under 3 years of age?

A parent of a child under the age of three may apply for a Blue Badge, in the child’s name if their child has:

A medical condition which means that they must always be accompanied by bulky medical equipment which cannot be carried around with the child without great difficulty

or

A medical condition which means that they need to be kept near a vehicle at all times either for treatment or for transportation to a location where treatment can be performed.

What happens if my Blue Badge is lost or stolen?

In both instances a fee of £10.00 is payable. Please see the section above entitled “How do I pay for a Blue Badge.”

Stolen

If your Blue Badge is stolen you will need to contact the police who will provide a crime reference number. The next step is to complete a Lost/Stolen Blue Badge Application Form  or by downloading the form available on the right hand side of this page. Please remember to include the necessary evidence when returning this form to us for processing.

Lost

If your Blue Badge is lost you will need to contact the Police who will provide a lost property number. The next step is to complete a Lost/Stolen Blue Badge Application Form  or by downloading the form available on the right hand side of this page. Please remember to include the necessary evidence when returning this form to us for processing.

A new photograph is not required for lost or stolen badges

Completed application forms can be returned by post to:

Blue Badge Administration
Municipal Offices

  • Please ensure that sufficient postage is paid

Is a badge being misused?
If you have a Blue Badge, it must only be used when you are the driver of a vehicle or a passenger in it and the journey is for your benefit.

You should not let others use the badge on your behalf without yourself being present, even if they are making a trip on your behalf.

It is an offence to misuse a Blue Badge and the maximum fine if someone is convicted is £1000.00 plus any additional penalty for the related parking offence.

If you think someone is misusing a Blue Badge you can report it in writing to:
