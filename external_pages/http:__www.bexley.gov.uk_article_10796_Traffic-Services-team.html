Traffic Services team

The Traffic Services team are responsible for general traffic schemes and traffic management across the Borough.

This includes the development of minor traffic improvements, on-street waiting restrictions (i.e. single and double yellow lines), on-street parking bays (i.e. pay and display, disabled persons' parking bays, limited waiting bays, footway parking bays), pedestrian refuges, zebra crossings, controlled parking zones, speed indication devices (SID) and requests for road signs and road markings.

There are 16 Controlled Parking Zones (CPZs) in the Borough. Of these, 3 CPZs (Bexleyheath, Welling and Sidcup) are located around town centres and operate 'all day' Monday to Saturday. The other 12 CPZs are mainly located around railway stations and operate for periods of between one and two hours, Monday to Friday. These schemes protect local residents and businesses from commuter parking with the provision of resident bays and business bays, although some free bays provide locations where residents or their visitors can park, without the need for a permit. Short stay bays are also provided at specific points for local shops and businesses.

The Traffic Services team can also deal with CPZ requests. More information can be found under Controlled Parking Zones.

The development of major traffic schemes in the borough is carried out by the Traffic Engineering team, which includes junction redesigns, public realm improvements and safety schemes. Further information in relation to the work of this team can be found at Traffic engineering.

Information relating to traffic schemes being developed and/or installed by the either the Traffic Services or Traffic Engineering teams can be found at Traffic projects.

Other traffic and road safety roles in Bexley

Many elements of traffic-related duties in the borough are carried out by other Council teams or partner organisations. These include:

  • Yellow Line and Parking Enforcement - The enforcement of parking on yellow lines, on and off-street public parking bays and Council car parks is managed by the Council's Customer Services Department. More detailed information can be found at Parking or contact Parking Services
  • Road works/Street works - The Council carries out road works for repairs/changes to footways and roads, however, most are carried out by the utility companies. (i.e. for gas, water, telecom supplies) The Council's Public Realm Management Department is responsible for permitting these works to take place. A list of the main Council road works and main utility works can be found at Road Watch
  • Road and footway repairs - The repairing of public roads, footways and footpaths is carried out by the Council's Public Realm Management Department. Further information can be found at Road and footway maintenance or via email worksdirect@bexley.gov.uk
  • Road safety - Further information on our road safety initiatives (child and adult cycle training, school crossing patrols (lollipops), School Travel Planning, road safety education through lessons and in-school theatre shows) and/or to view Bexley's Road Safety Plan can be found at Road safety or email  roadsafety@bexley.gov.uk
  • Traffic lights/signals -These are owned and managed by Transport for London (TfL). Any faults to traffic signals in Bexley should be reported direct to TfL on 0845 606 1005 or online
  • Bus stops/shelters/routes - These are managed and operated by Transport for London's (TfL) London Buses. Any comments regarding these aspects, including cleaning and vandalism to bus shelters, should be reported direct to 0845 300 7000 or online
  • A2 and A20 - These two primary roads across the borough form part of the Transport for London Route Network (TLRN) and are fully managed by agents for Transport for London. Any comments regarding these two roads should be reported directly to TfL on 0845 7222 5600 or online
  • Blue Badges - The Traffic Services team look after the introduction of disabled persons' parking bays which may be used by drivers with a blue badge. Applications for blue badges or renewing existing badges is administered by Inspire Community Trust. They can be contacted on 020 3045 5100 or e-mail information@inspirecommunitytrust.org.
