Building Regulations

The Building Regulations are made under powers provided in the Building Act 1984, the current edition of which are the Building Regulations 2010.

They exist to ensure the health and safety of people in and around all buildings including domestic, commercial and industrial units, and also provide for energy conservation, and access to and use of buildings.  These national standards cover all aspects of construction, including foundations, damp-proofing, the overall stability of the building, insulation, drainage, energy conservation, ventilation heating, fire protection and means of escape in case of fire.  They also ensure that adequate access and facilities for people with disabilities are provided to buildings.

Approved Documents

The Building Regulations set out broad, functional requirements which are supported by a set of Approved Documents.  These documents are published by the Department of Communities and Local Government (DCLG) and are available to provide guidance on meeting the current Building Regulations. The Approved Documents are available on the  Planning Portal website..

Part A  Structural Safety - Provides guidance on the design of buildings to ensure that they are constructed and/or altered so that they are structurally safe and robust, and also so as not to impair the structural stability of other buildings.

Part B  Fire Safety - All fire precaution measures that are necessary to provide safety from fire that will safeguard building occupants, persons in the vicinity of buildings and Fire Fighters are covered by this part.

Part C  Site Preparation and Resistance to Contaminants and Moisture - This part deals with weather and water tightness of buildings, subsoil drainage, site preparation and measures to deal with contaminated land, Radon, Methane and all other site related hazardous and dangerous substances.

Part D  Toxic Substances - Controls against the use of certain toxic materials being used in buildings.

Part E  Resistance to Sound - Requirements for sound insulation between dwellings, both new dwellings and conversion of buildings to form dwellings.  Also covered are the requirements for sound reduction methods between rooms for residential purposes, designated rooms in dwellings and acoustic conditions for common areas in flats and schools.

Part F  Ventilation - Standards for ventilation and air quality requirements for all buildings.

Part G  Hygiene - Sets out standards for the provision of sanitary and washing facilities, bathrooms and hot water provision.  It also covers safety requirements in respect of unvented hot water systems.

Part H  Drainage and Waste Disposal - Addresses the requirement for the provision of drainage systems and also deals with pollution prevention and sewage infrastructure issues.

Part J  Heat producing appliances - The construction, installation and use of boilers, chimneys, flues, hearths and fuel storage installations are covered by the regulations under this part.

Part K  Protection from Falling - Sets out standards for the safety of stairways, ramps, windows and guarding.

Part L  Conservation of Fuel and Power - Relates to all building types and controls amongst other items, the insulation of building elements, the allowable area of windows and openings, air permeability of the structure, the heating efficiency of boilers and the insulation and controls for heating appliances and systems.

Part M  Access and Facilities for Disabled People - Sets out requirements to ensure ease of access to and circulation within all buildings, together with requirements for facilities for disabled people.

Part N  Glazing Safety in relation to Impact, Opening and Cleaning

Covers the use of safety glazing and suitable awareness requirements to avoid impact hazard.  In addition, it sets out safety requirements for the use, operation and cleaning of windows.

Part P  Electrical Safety - Sets out safety requirements which cover electrical installations in domestic situations.
