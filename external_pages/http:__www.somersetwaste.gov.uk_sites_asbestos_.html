Asbestos disposal

Asbestos can give off harmful dust particles and fibres, so needs to be handled with care. Cement bonded, sheet asbestos and asbestos containing materials (ACMs) can be taken to some recycling sites for disposal, but must be securely double bagged or wrapped in plastic and sealed when taken for disposal.

Safety Advice
Use gloves and masks when handling asbestos and, where possible, do not break, drill, scrub or saw the asbestos.  Large sheets should not be broken up. Damp down the working area with water to limit airborne particles and remaining fibres should be cleared up with a damp cloth, which should be included with the asbestos for disposal.

Double bag or wrap and seal all asbestos in heavy duty plastic, such as rubble bags closed with gaffer or duct tape, prior to taking to a recycling site for disposal.

Where to take it
There are seven recycling centres in Somerset which will accept asbestos and asbestos containing materials from your own home: Bridgwater (Saltlands), Frome, Highbridge, Minehead, Street, Taunton (Priorswood) and Yeovil.

Please telephone in advance of your visit to ensure they have the space to take your waste as they have limited hazardous waste storage.  There is a disposal limit for asbestos which roughly equates to 16 sheets of 6ft x 2ft or 16 sheets of 2.5m x 0.6m. For vinyl tiles the limit is 8 rubble sacks.

On arrival let one of the site staff know you have asbestos, they will then direct you to the correct container.  Staff at the site are not able to assist with unloading of this material.  Place the asbestos (complete with bags, wrapping and cleaning cloths) in the container.

If you are unable to take bonded asbestos or a storage heater to one of the designated recycling sites, you can arrange for an asbestos collection from your home for a fee.

Households items containing asbestos

The seven designated recycling centres accept cement bonded/sheet asbestos, storage heaters (which can contain asbestos) and other asbestos containing materials. Storage heaters, should not be broken up and also need to be double bagged or wrapped and sealed when taken for disposal.

The list below is not exhaustive but includes:

•    Vinyl (Marley) floor tiles and textured (Aertex) ceiling tiles
•    Insulation materials such as airing-cupboard door, fire doors and oven doors
•    Roofing materials, soffits, gutters and drains
•    Iron rests on old-fashion ironing boards and fire blankets
•    Asbestos insulation board, fire surrounds and domestic flues
•    Cement products and cold water cisterns

Further information on asbestos in the home

For information on managing all types of asbestos and where it may be found in the home see www.direct.gov.uk. To find a contractor to remove asbestos use the search facility on the website of the Asbestos Removal Contractors Association. The asbestos section of the Health and Saftey Executive website contains more helpful information and Somerset County Council’s Scientific Services can also provide advice. The Asbestos Information Centre provides further advice on identifying storage heaters which may contain asbestos.


Asbestos from commercial or trade sources

We are unable to arrange collection of commercial or trade asbestos. If you have commercial asbestos or a large amount for disposal, find a contractor at the Asbestos Removal Contractors Association or arrange with a commercial waste management company (a list of registered carriers can be obtained from the Environment Agency).
