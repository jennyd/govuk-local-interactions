Direct payments

The majority of people who are eligible for support to meet assessed social care needs have a right to receive direct payments. This means we give you the money to arrange and buy the care/support you need that fits around how you live your life. You decide how your needs will be met, by whom and at a time that suits you. You can choose to have all or part of your support as direct payments.

Where a person lacks mental capacity to consent to receiving direct payments we can appoint a 'Suitable Person' to receive the direct payments on their behalf.  

Support available if you choose direct payments

We have worked with our partners Salvere to provide professional support to help you manage your direct payments, including:

  • advice on buying services from an agency or an organisation
  • information, advice and support to become an employer
  • assistance with staff recruitment, contracts and interviews
  • financial management and budgeting advice
  • on-going advice with personnel issues

Some people are not able to have direct payments and we will discuss options with you at this time. We can provide you with more information about this, to help you make the decision that is right for you.

Please note: direct payments are not income or a benefit and therefore will not affect any benefits you receive.

If you think adult social care can help support you, a family member or friend please get in touch. You can telephone us on 0300 123 6720.
