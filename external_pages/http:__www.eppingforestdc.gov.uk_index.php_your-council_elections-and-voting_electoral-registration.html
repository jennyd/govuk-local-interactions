Electoral registration

If you're not registered, you can't vote! Sweeping changes to the way we register to vote have been introduced across the UK. The new system called Individual Electoral Registration (IER).

Click here to register online

At present, one person in every household is responsible for registering everyone else who lives at that address. With the introduction of IER, each person will be required to register to vote individually, rather than by household.

How is the new system different?

  • You can now register online at www.gov.uk/register-to-vote
  • Everyone is responsible for registering themselves. Under the old system the ‘head of every household’ could register everyone who lived at their address
  • You need to provide a few more details to register – including your national insurance number and date of birth. This makes the electoral register more secure
  • Anyone moving house or changing their details will need to register under the new system

How do I register under the new system?

  1. Go to www.gov.uk/register-to-vote
  2. Fill in your name, address, date of birth and a few other details. You’ll also need your national insurance number, which can be found on your national insurance card, or in official paperwork such as payslips, or letters about benefits or tax credits
  3. Look out for a confirmation to say you’re registered

If you have access to a computer please register online – registering online is free, quick, easy and secure.
