Pupil premium & free school meals - An easier, private way to apply that saves money for you and increases fund for the school

More in "Pupil premium & free school meals"

  • An easier, private way to apply that saves money for you and increases fund for the school
  • Eligibility & how to apply
  • What happens once an application has been submitted

Applying for a free school meal and for the Pupil Premium is now easy and more confidential. The application process is managed by our housing benefit service. They take the application so that you don't have to approach the school directly and means that you keep your details private.

As from September 2014 there will be no charge for Infant School meals. However, it is important that parents of infant pupils who may be in receipt of qualifying benefits register their entitlement for Free School Meals in order to attract the Pupil Premium for your child's school.

If your child is in junior or senior school they may qualify for a free school meal and the pupil premium if they meet the required criteria. Please refer to the related pages Eligibility and How to Apply.

Anyone eligible for a free school meal and who registers their eligibility by simply completing this application form will save themselves hundreds of pounds per year and your child will benefit from a healthy hot school meal.

They will also help raise money for the school following a government commitment to pay schools for each pupil registered through the pupil premium. Your child does not need to take the meal but the school still gets the extra funding if you register!

The Pupil Premium funding is paid to the school directly for them to decide how best to use this resource. Ofsted inspections ensure the schools use the funding appropriately.

The funding can be used in many ways. Examples of how your school may use the funding include:

  • Introducing reading and homework clubs
  • Summer schools to help children with transition to secondary school
  • Subsidising the cost of school trips or uniform
  • Paying for additional staffing
  • Providing small group tuition
  • Providing a wide range of extra-curricular activities
  • Purchasing new technology
  • Providing mentoring programmes
  • Improving resources such as books, computers, sports equipment.

If your child is still attending the same school and you have had no changes in your financial circumstances, there is no need to contact us for the new term. If your child has moved schools and/or you have had a change in your financial circumstances, please email fsm@sutton.gov.uk  or call 020 8770 6953.

For further information on the extra benefits from free school meals, please visit the free school meals section on the gov.uk website and for information on the pupil premium please visit the pupil premium section on the gov.uk website.
