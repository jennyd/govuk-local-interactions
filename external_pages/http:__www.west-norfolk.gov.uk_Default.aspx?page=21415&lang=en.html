New and Replacement Bins

Popular Tasks for Waste & Recycling

 

 Request a New or Additional Bin

 

 Register your Recycling Bin

Order a new bin

 

If your bin is damaged, lost or stolen and you need a replacement please call us on 01553 616200.  Depending on the individual circumstances there maybe a charge for any replacements provided.  Our dedicated waste advisers will inform you of the associated costs (if applicable) when you order your new bin(s).

 

To help prevent the loss of your bin, paint your house number/name and postcode on the front of it.  You can also register your recycling bin details with us.  If we then find your bin we will return it to you and if you have purchased a replacement, we will refund your money to you.

 

Additional bins

 

If you would like to request an extra bin please read the information below and then complete the new or additional bin eForm.

 

General waste (black bin)

 

Only waste that is safely contained within the bin will be collected. Most residents find that their black bin is sufficient to hold their waste. However, if you find that you have excess waste please place it in a black sack and seal it with a red tag, purchased from the council for £1.50 (available from your nearest council information centre).

 

Sacks sealed in this way should be put out for collection alongside your bin on your scheduled collection day. One tag must be used per extra sack of rubbish. Only sacks sealed with the council’s red tags will be collected.  Alternatively, extra waste can be taken to one of Norfolk County Council’s household recycling centres.

 

Under some circumstance (for example if you have a particularly large family) you may be eligible for an additional black bin. To make a request, which will be assessed by officers, please complete the new or additional bin eForm.

 

If , after we have assessed your application, you are not deemed to be eligible for an extra black bin, we can work with you to try to reduce the volume of your waste.

 

Putting your rubbish in small kitchen bin liners or old supermarket carrier bags rather than large black sacks can help to make the most of the space in your bin. Larger bags can fill up with excess air causing them to get jammed inside the bin and using up valuable space.

 

Recycling bins

 

In most circumstances, additional green recycling bins are available free of charge. Our dedicated waste advisers will inform you of the associated costs (if applicable) when you order your new bin(s).
