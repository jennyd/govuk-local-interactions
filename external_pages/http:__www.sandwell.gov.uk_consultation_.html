Consultations

We have a duty to involve our customers in decisions that affect them and the area they live, work or study in.

As well as local people and service users, our customers include local businesses, voluntary groups, and visitors to the area, amongst others.

Current consultations

    Sandwell Metropolitan Borough Council is currently consulting on a revision of its Statement of Community Involvement (SCI), which was originally adopted in 2007.

    The SCI sets out how the Borough Council will involve the community and stakeholders in the preparation and review of planning policy for the borough and in decision making on planning applications. The Council is carrying out this review because of changes to the planning system in recent years.

    Comments are invited on this consultation for a six-week period, from Monday 1st February to Friday 11th March 2016. Paper copies of the document can also be viewed at the following locations:

    • Sandwell Council House, Planning reception, Oldbury, B69 3DE
    • All main public libraries in Sandwell Metropolitan Borough (Please check with the particular library for opening times)

    Representations must be made in writing (including electronically) and be received by 5pm on Friday 11th March 2016 and can be submitted as follows:

    • Using the online form
    • By e-mail to ldf_planning@sandwell.gov.uk (using the response form on the website)
    • By post to Sandwell Council House, Strategic Planning Policy, Oldbury B69 3DE (using a printed response form)

    We look forward to receiving any comments you may have.

    You can view a full list of current consultations on our consultation database.

    Statutory consultations

    Statutory consultations go on from time to time. These range from proposed road closures to changes in licensing laws.

    Sandwell Metropolitan Borough Council is currently consulting on a revision of its Hot Food Takeaway (HFT) Supplementary Planning Document (SPD) which was originally adopted in 2012.  The consultation will run for a six-week period, from Monday 1st February to Friday 11th March 2016.
