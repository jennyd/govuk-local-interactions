Emergency Planning

The role of the Borough Council in Emergencies

The council's role in an emergency is to:

  • Respond to emergencies within the borough boundary in conjunction with other members of the Northamptonshire Local Resilience Forum (LRF).
  • Plan to ensure that the people and environment of Wellingborough are protected.
  • Exercise our plans to ensure a prompt, coordinated and adequate response to an incident or emergency.
  • Liaise with other Category one Responders  to ensure an integrated response to an incident or emergency.
  • Provide aid to neighbouring district / borough councils when requested.
  • Restore services affected by the emergency for which the council is usually responsible.

The Northamptonshire LRF

The council is a member of the Northamptonshire Local Resilience Forum (LRF) together with the Police, Fire Brigade, Ambulance, NHS, local authorities and Environment Agency who are designated Category One Responders.   The LRF ensures that the key requirements of the Civil Contingencies Act are implemented across relevant agencies in Northamptonshire and addresses civil protection issues. The LRF produces the Northamptonshire Community Risk Register which shows the outcome of risk assessments for the likely causes of incidents or emergencies in Northamptonshire and enables it to decide our priorities and identify the further actions required, including contingency planning.

Utilities companies, transport companies, and the Health and Safety Executive are all Category 2 responders. These organizations support local and regional emergency plans.

Emergencies can happen at any time. Most can be dealt with by the emergency services (Police; Fire and Rescue; Ambulance).

On rare occasions an incident or emergency can be so big or have such a widespread impact that other organisations need to be involved in the response; for example, in the event of:

  • wide-spread flooding or flash flooding;
  • major transport accidents;
  • animal health diseases; and
  • major fires.

Further details can be found in The Borough Council of Wellingborough Emergency Plan and the Northamptonshire Community Risk Register.
