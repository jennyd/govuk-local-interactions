Plans, Policies and Performance

Find out more about how we run the Council and the services we deliver in your community.

  • Your Views 2016 - Draft Business Plan and Draft Budget Consultation

    Fenland District Council is consulting on its Draft Business Plan and Draft Budget. We would welcome your views by Monday 8 February 2016.

  • Draft Business Plan 2016-2019

    Fenland District Council's Draft Business Plan and Draft Budget sets out how we propose to deliver Council services, deliver financial savings and work in partnership over the next 3 years.

  • Draft Budget

    We would like your views on our draft Medium Term Financial Strategy, General Fund Budget 2016/17 and Capital Programme 2016-19. To complete the consultation, visit www.fenland.gov.uk/yourviews2016

  • How would you save £1.8 million?

    The Council must save £1.8 million over the next three years. We asked for your views on our future spending priorities and the changes we should make.

  • Corporate Plan

    Our Corporate Plan 2015-18 is a vision for the future of Fenland. It explains how our priorities support the overarching aim of improving quality of life in Fenland through addressing the social, environmental and economic needs for residents to live happy and fulfilled lives.

  • Annual Report 2015

    Find out how the Council has worked with residents, partners and community groups to deliver some great projects over the past year.

  • Fenland Strategic Partnership

    The Fenland Strategic Partnership (FSP) brings together all of the local agencies and organisations that are dedicated to improving the district and making life even better for Fenland residents and businesses.

  • Information Sharing

    The Cambridgeshire Information Sharing Framework has been developed by a number of public organisations within the county. It is series of documents which cover our obligations under the Data Protection Act around sharing information.

  • Internet service standards

    Information about our Internet service standards.

  • Cookies Policy

    Our website uses cookies. By using our website and agreeing to this policy, you consent to our use of cookies in accordance with the terms of this policy.

  • Equality and Diversity

    The council is committed to ensuring that everyone is able to access our services and all users and employees all receive the same level of service delivery.

  • Regulation of Investigatory Powers (RIPA) Policy

    RIPA is the legislation that governs how public bodies undertake covert surveillance in the detection and prevention of crime. Each public body that uses RIPA must have a policy.

  • Corporate Debt Policy

    This document is our Corporate Debt Policy. It tells you how we collect debts owed to us, the stages we go through to do this and how to contact us to get advice.

  • Health and Wellbeing Strategy

    How we're working with partners to tackle health inequalities in Fenland.
