Crime and safety

We're committed to making the borough a safer place in which to live or work. Hackney still has some of the best crime reduction figures in London. Crime has been reduced by 11% in Hackney over the last five years, and by 30% in the last ten years, with 12,000 fewer victims of crime than ten years ago.

Preventing burglaries

From October a uniformed police officer will visiting approximately 600 homes in the Leabridge area.

If you live in or around the area approximately highlighted on the map, a police officer will visit your house to carry out a crime prevention survey and advise you on making your home safer. They'll also give you window locks, light-timer switches and door frame reinforcers to make your home more secure if they think you need to.

 

We'll also have a community safety stall in a nearby street to discuss local issues and offer crime prevention advice, and you can contact your local Safer Neighbourhood Team if you want to talk about an issue. 

Recent crime statistics show the number of burglaries has fallen, but some homes are seen as easy targets by burglars, for example because they have bushes or trees that hide the windows, or ladders and other tools laying around in the garden. Police officers are patrolling certain areas, targeting known offenders and, alongside Council officers, providing crime prevention advice for victims and their neighbours.

The officers will be visiting the surrounding area and other areas in the coming months. The project has been funded through the Safer Neighbourhood Board with money from the Mayor of London Office for Policing and Crime. Older residents and vulnerable residents will have security upgrades carried out by the Hackney Homes Handy Man scheme. To find out more call 020 8356 3170.

What are we doing to combat crime and antisocial behaviour?

Our community safety plan 2011-14 (PDF, 216KB) outlines our priorities and how we will deal with them. We refresh it annually to take account of any new or emerging issues. Community safety isn't just about managing the risks of crime - it refers to all actions that can cause harm to communities, including transport related incidents and other environmental and pollution factors that impact on public wellbeing. This means we work with other agencies, as part of a community safety partnership.

Our main strategic priorities are:

  • youth crime, gangs, perception of youth disorder and youth engagement
  • alcohol related crime and disorder, licensing and safer socialising
  • domestic violence, rape and sexual assault
  • nuisance neighbours and domestic noise
  • substance misuse, treatment and drug dealing

Read our community safety priorities (PDF, 84KB) to find out about the activities we are doing to tackle each priority. 

Have your say on crime 

We're interested in hearing residents' views on crime and safety in Hackney. If you'd like to contribute your opinion or have any concerns about crime in the borough, please contact us.
