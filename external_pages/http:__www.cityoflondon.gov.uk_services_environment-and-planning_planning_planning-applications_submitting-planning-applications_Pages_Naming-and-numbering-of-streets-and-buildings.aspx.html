Naming and numbering of streets and buildings

Consistent naming and numbering is important for the public, postal services and the emergency services to locate properties quickly and efficiently.

The London Buildings Act (Amendment) Act 1939 requires authorisation for the naming and numbering of all buildings and streets in the City.

An application for authorisation must be made to the City of London Corporation. It should be made as early as possible as naming and numbering is required for land registration, allocation of postcodes (see below) and marketing the property.

Please provide the following information:

  • Contact details for the applicant and site owner (including confirmation of permission by the property owner to make an application).
  • An explanation of the proposal, including the present address details. If the application comprises a street name or a building name, an explanation into the origin of the name must be included.
  • Details of the relevant Planning Permission (where appropriate).
  • A site plan showing the location of each entrance at street level.

For more information, see the Advice Note and Application Form:

Street Naming and Numbering Advice Note (2MB)

Application Form for Street Naming and Numbering (160KB)  

Display of Address

All properties have a legal obligation to display the correct street number (and, where applicable, building name). This is to ensure that properties can be quickly and easily located by the Public, Emergency Services and other service providers such as Royal Mail and Utilities. Detailed information on this can be found in section 3 of the Street Naming and Numbering Advice Note (2MB).

Of particular concern in the City is the number of shops not adequately displaying the appropriate name and number. Further information and guidance on display of address can be found in this leaflet:

Retail Premises: Are you displaying your street number? (1.83MB)
