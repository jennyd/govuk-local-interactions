Burials and cremations

A funeral director will usually contact us and make arrangements for buying a grave. You can make your own arrangements by contacting us and we will help you to make an informed choice from the options available.

Burials can take place on any week day - except on bank holidays. Please talk to your funeral director to discuss your specific needs.

Our adopted Cemetery regulations govern how the cemeteries are managed and what is permitted in relation to grave spaces within them.

Cemeteries in Harborough

There are 6 cemeteries in Harborough:

  • Market Harborough
  • Great Bowden
  • Great Easton
  • Saddington
  • Foxton
  • Blaston - closed for 'new' graves 

View the location of cemeteries on a map.

    Some cemeteries in the district have both consecrated and unconsecrated grounds:

    • Consecrated ground is blessed according to the rites of the Church of England
    • Unconsecrated ground has not been blessed and includes plots for other religious denominations (such as Roman Catholic) - the ground may be blessed at the time of interment by the minister officiating

    Un-purchased Graves

    An un-purchased grave, sometimes known as a public or common grave, is where the exclusive right of burial has not been purchased so the council has total control of the grave and will decide who is buried in the grave.

    Purchased Graves

    A purchased grave is where the right of burial is bought for a set period of time. The owner of the right of burial can decide who will be buried in the grave, and memorials are allowed.

    The Exclusive Right of Burial is a form of lease that may be purchased for any of the grave spaces available in any of the cemeteries we manage. It grants the holder of the deed exclusive rights to the plot for 100 years. Residents of Harborough can purchase the exclusive right of burial for a grave space. View the cost of the exclusive right of burial for a grave space.

    Memorials

    Memorials and any form of monument can only be placed on the grave space with our permission. This permission will only be granted where a grave space has an Exclusive Right of Burial recorded against it.

    Alterations to any existing monument or memorial must be carried out with the permission of the council. This will include alteration of an existing inscription or the cutting of an additional inscription.

    Removal of any monument or memorial must also be carried out with the permission of the council.

    Request permission for a memorial, or to change or remove a memorial.
