Discounts & exemptions

Do you think you may be entitled to a discount, or that your property is exempt from Council Tax?
 
Council Tax Reduction
If you have a low income or are in receipt of benefits you may be entitled to Council Tax Reduction. Complete a Council Tax Reduction application form.
 
Single person discount
If you live on your own you are entitled to a 25% discount off your Council Tax bill. Complete a Single Person discount application form or find out further information about single person discount.
 
Student exemption
We will need evidence of your student status. Currently Solent University and Southampton University provide the council with a list of their students. (You will be included on these lists providing you agreed for the Universities to share your data at the time you enrolled). Students from other establishments or not on the lists provided to the council will need to provide a copy of their student certificate. Complete a Student Exemption application form or find out further information about a student exemption.
 
Empty properties
Council Tax charges on empty properties, including second homes and properties owned by charities. Find out more information about Council Tax charges on empty properties.
 
Deceased discount or exemption
We understand the difficulties relatives and representatives’ experience and the arrangements that need to be made when someone has died. There are certain things you need to tell us when a Council Tax payer dies, both about the person who has died and the property they occupied. Complete a deceased discount or exemption application form or find out further information for relatives and representatives.
 
Severely mentally impaired discount or exemption
People who are severely mentally impaired are exempt from Council Tax. Download and complete a severely mentally impaired discount or exemption application form or find out further information about the severely mentally impaired discount or exemption.
 
Patients in hospital or care homes
This exemption or discount may apply when the owner/tenant is registered permanently into a hospital or care home and does not intend to return to the household. Temporary stays in hospital or care homes do not qualify for this exemption or discount. Complete a patient in hospital or care home discount or exemption application form.
 
Disabled reduction
If your property has been adapted to meet the needs of a disabled person who lives with you, we may be able to reduce the bill by putting your property into a lower Council Tax band. Download and complete a disabled reduction application form or find out further information about disabled reduction.
 
Special discount for people who are receiving care in their own home     
In some circumstances, we do not count a live in carer when adding up the number of people in a property. If just you and your carer live in your home, you may be able to get a carers discount to reduce your bill by 25%. Find out more about discounts for those receiving care in their home home.
 
Prisoner discount or exemption
This discount or exemption may apply if someone in the household is or was detained in prison for any other reason than non-payment of Council Tax or fine. Complete a prisoner discount or exemption application form for find out further information about prisoner discount or exemption.
 
Apprentices discount
If you or someone who lives with you is an apprentice, you may be entitled to a 25% discount off your Council Tax. Download and complete an apprentices discount application form.
 
Members of visiting forces, international organisations or diplomats
You will not be counted for council tax if you are a member of a visiting force, an international organisation or a diplomat. Find out more information about members of visiting forces, international organisations or diplomats.
 
Members of certain religious communities (inc. monks and nuns)
Apply using the Religious Communities information form.
 
Annexes discount or exemption
A 50% discount will be available for annexes if it used by the occupier of the main building or a member of his or her family. Find out further information about annexes discounts or exemptions.
 
Removal of discount or exemption
If you are currently receiving a discount or exemption on your Council Tax bill and it no longer applies, it is your responsibility to notify the council. Complete a removal of discount or exemption form.

Removal of Single Person discount
If you are currently receiving a single person discount on your Council Tax bill and it no longer applies, it is your responsibility to notify the council. Complete a removal of single person discount form.

Request Refund
If you would like to request a refund, complete a request refund form.
