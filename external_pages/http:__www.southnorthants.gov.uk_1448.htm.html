Consultations

We see effective consultation as a key part of our programme to provide services which meet your needs, both now and in the future.

Our aim is to generate information, insights and learning and the pages in this section bring together the consultation work that is currently in progress as well information that we have collected in the past.

Throughout the year we will seek your views in a number of areas including:

  • the direction of the council
  • the councilÂ’s budget and council tax
  • major decisions about council policy
  • issues affecting the quality of life in the area
  • issues around service design, delivery and satisfaction levels
  • raising issues that are of importance to you

We will also tell you the results of that work and what we have done in response.

Throughout the year, we seek the views or residents, business owners, visitors and other people with an interest in the district on a range of subjects.

Council Tax Reduction Scheme Consultation
Description

Each year the Council is required to formally adopt a scheme by 31st January to commence on the 1st April of that year. Councils across the Country are developing proposals for the 2016/2017 scheme.

South Northamptonshire Council is currently considering options for funding their Council Tax Reduction scheme for 2016-2017 taking into account the reduction in central government funding and also the need to protect some of the most vulnerable residents in the district.

Everyone liable to pay Council Tax pays for the Council Tax Reduction Scheme through their Council Tax payments. Therefore, it is important to get the views of all Council Tax payers regardless of whether you receive Council Tax Reduction or not.

Any changes to the scheme will directly affect working-age households receiving Council Tax Reduction now or in the future.

Our current Council Tax Reduction Scheme broadly follows the principles and rules that applied to Council Tax Benefit and has been largely unchanged since its introduction on 1 April 2013. A copy of the CTRS Regulations can be found on ourÂ Council Tax Reduction Scheme FAQ's page.Â Council Tax discounts and exemptions have also remained the same.

Central Government set the scheme for pension-age applicants and they will not be not be directly affected by any changes. For Council Tax Reduction, we treat you as being of pension age if you or your partner is old enough to receive Pension Credit. From April 2016, anyone born before 5th April 1953 will be considered to be of Pension Age.Â 

The consultation will be from 15th September 2015 to 5th October 2015.

Bradden Conservation Area
Description

The preservation and enhancement of the Â‘special characterÂ’ at Bradden
Public re-consultation

South Northamptonshire Council (SNC) is consulting residents on the review of the Bradden Conservation Area boundary and responses should be returned by Friday 27th February.

Designed to preserve and enhance the special character of the area they cover, conservation areas provide stricter guidelines for planning permissions and may also prevent the demolition of buildings which contribute to the areaÂ’s character. Revising the existing conservation area boundaries ensures that the special character and appearance of the area will be preserved and enhanced.

Bradden Conservation Area was originally designated in 1991. In 2014 the conservation area boundary was reviewed again. The proposed boundary excludes two fields to the north-west of the conservation area of no archaeological value, the rationalisation of plots that have changed since the initial designation and the inclusion of modern development to the rear of Water Lane.

South Northamptonshire was recently rated the third best place to live in the UK. It is important to do all we can to ensure that this area remains a great place to live and visit. The Bradden Conservation Area is not intended to prevent change but to help ensure the important historical aspects of its character are conserved and respected.Â 

Any comments and views of local residents are an important part of this process. These comments should be directed to Liz Knight, Assistant Conservation Officer before the deadline.

The draft plans are available to view at www.southnorthants.gov.uk/688.htm or at South Northamptonshire CouncilÂ’s office in Towcester.

Comments and views should be submitted to the heritage team in writing to liz.knight@southnorthants.gov.uk or by post to Liz Knight, Assistant Conservation Officer, South Northamptonshire Council, Springfields, Towcester, NN12 6AE before 5pm Friday 27th February.
