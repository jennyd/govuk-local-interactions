Garden waste

Garden waste includes grass cuttings, hedge prunings, twigs, leaves and seasonal garden fruit (but not fruit scraps from the kitchen or animal bedding).

Residents can sign up to our chargeable fortnightly garden waste collection service, or take garden waste to our Reuse and Recycling Centre, or compost garden waste at home.

You can join the scheme at any time during the 12 month period- July 2015 to June 2016. There is also a reduced rate for pensioners and those in receipt of qualifying state benefits joining in the first part of the year. Payment is due in advance.

The final date for renewal of the service will be 1st August 2015. If we do not receive a renewal notification and payment we will reclaim the garden waste bin.

Sign up to the service

Please read the Garden Waste Terms and Conditions 2015/16 first. These can be found in the Garden waste collection service in the leaflet section below.

Then ring our waste helpline 020 8274 4902 to subscribe.

What happens next

  • You will receive a 240 litre wheeled brown bin to contain your garden waste. Paper sacks with a 75 litre capacity are also available for residents with little or no storage space at the front of their property.
  • When your container is delivered you will receive a letter containing details of your first collection day and a leaflet fully explaining the service.
  • Your garden waste will be collected once a fortnight (excluding two collections over Christmas). Your current collection year will run until June 2016
  • After collection the waste is turned into compost.

Collection days for garden waste

Collections are fortnightly and are usually on the same day as your rubbish and recycling collection.
