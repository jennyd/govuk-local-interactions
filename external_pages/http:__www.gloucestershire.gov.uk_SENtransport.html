Support available to enable young people with SEND to attend school or college

Children and young people with special educational needs may get help with travel to school or college but it is not an automatic right. An assessment is made of the child's needs and individual circumstances.

A door-to-door service is provided only in specific circumstances. Children may have to travel to their nearest pick up point.

Travel assistance may include

  • bus passes
  • cars & taxis
  • mileage paid to parent / personal transport budget
  • minibus

You will need to complete an application form and meet the criteria in order to qualify for help and we will ask for quite a lot of information because:

  • we need to make sure you are eligible for travel assistance
  • if we can offer help, we need to be sure that the arrangements are suitable to meet your child's needs

If you need help with completing the form you can ask staff at the school or college or your health visitor. You can also contact us or the SENDIASS Gloucestershire  for advice.

If we can't help we will let you know and tell you how you can appeal against our decision.

Read the guidance, policies and download the application forms below.

Children 4-16 years

If you think you are eligible for assistance with travel please read the  Guidance and frequently asked questions. [166kb] and the  policy [147kb] before applying.

Click here to apply online for SEN 4-16 travel assistance

You can also apply by post using this form:  Home to school travel assistance application form for children aged 4 - 16 with learning difficulties and/or disabilities [202kb] but this will take longer to process.

Young people 16 years and over

If you are applying for travel assistance for travel to school or college please read the post 16 travel policy and download and complete the application form, direct debit proforma and mandate forms below. 

  •  Home to school travel assistance application form for students aged 16+ with learning difficulties and/or disabilities [226kb]
  •  2015-16 post 16 home to school/college transport policy for students with learning difficulties and/or disabilities [427kb]

Setting up a direct debit

Please complete both forms and send them to the address on the mandate. Direct debit forms must be posted to us as your bank will require an original signature.

  •  Direct debit proforma for Post 16 SEN transport 2015-16 [310kb]
  •  Direct debit mandate for Post 16 SEN transport 2015-16 [105kb]

Mileage reimbursement claims forms

If you have applied for mileage reimbursement and the application has been approved, you can download and print  mileage reimbursement claims form below.

Completing a claim form

Step 1: Read the  instructions for completing the claim form [11kb]

Step 2: See our  example of a completed form [16kb]

Step 3: Select and download a form from the list on the top right of this page.

Step 4: Complete the form for the month you are claiming for and post it to the address in the instructions.

If you are unable to download and print these forms, we can send them to you by post. For further information please telephone 01452 425334

Refusal of transport

If you do not meet the entitlement criteria set out in our policy your application for travel assistance will be refused. If, after being refused, you feel that you have extenuating personal circumstances you should request an application form for your case to be considered by a Senior Officer Review Panel.  To request a form for a Senior Officer Review, please contact the SEN Travel Enablement Team on 01452 426770 

On this form you should clearly demonstrate that our policies do not adequately deal with your circumstances or that your circumstances warrant a decision outside the policy.

If your application is refused by senior officers you can request a formal appeal by elected members against their decision and you will be advised of this process in writing.
