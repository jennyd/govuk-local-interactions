Apply for a Blue Badge Parking Permit

Disabled parking badges are issued to people who have a permanent and substantial physical disability which significantly affects their mobility.

Can I get a Blue Badge?

Before you apply we advise you to read:

  • can I get a Blue Badge?

The leaflet provides a handy guide on whether or not you qualify for a Blue Badge.

Eligibility criteria

There are 2 types of eligibility:

Eligibility without assessment

If you meet 1 of the following criteria you are entitled to a Blue Badge:

  • receive the higher rate mobility component of disability living allowance (DLA)
  • receive a Personal Independence Payment (PIP) and score between 8 and 12 points on the "Moving Around" descriptors within the Mobility component
  • are registered blind (severely sight impaired)
  • are in receipt of a War Pensioner's Mobility Supplement
  • receive entitlement to the Armed Forces Compensation Scheme (AFCS)

Eligibility following assessment

If you meet 1 of the following criteria you may be eligible for a Blue Badge:

  • you have a permanent and substantial disability which means you cannot walk, or have very considerable difficulty in walking
  • you have a severe disability in both arms which may stop you being able to use parking meters and pay and display machines
  • you have a child under 3 suffering a medical condition requiring immediate access to a vehicle so that they can be treated or be taken quickly to a place they can be treated

If you’re applying on the basis of eligibility following assessment you may have to attend a clinical mobility assessment with an Occupational Therapist.

Apply or renew

The most convenient way to apply for or renew a Blue Badge is to do so online. 

Alternatively you can download an application form. For help with completing the form, please have a look at our guidance notes.

If you have any questions contact us by email or call 0121 704 6000.

Alternatively, pop in to any of our Connect Walk-In Centres and we’ll be happy to help you.

What if my Blue Badge is lost or stolen?

If your Blue Badge is lost or stolen please contact us on 0121 704 6000.

If your Blue Badge has been stolen you must also contact the police to obtain a crime reference number on 101.

There is a £10 re-application cost for lost or stolen Blue Badges
