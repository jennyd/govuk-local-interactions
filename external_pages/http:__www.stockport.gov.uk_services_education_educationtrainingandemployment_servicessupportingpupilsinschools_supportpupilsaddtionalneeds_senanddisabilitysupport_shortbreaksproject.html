Short Breaks Project

The Short Breaks project has been developed to help children and parents to avoid a problem turning into a real crisis.

Some of us are lucky and can access support from a friend or relative which can help to avoid a problem turning into a real crisis. 

However, we are not all as fortunate as this and can be left struggling on our own to cope and usually at a time when we have little energy to do this.

The Short Breaks Project has been developed to help children and parents who find themselves in this situation.

Who are we?

  • The Short Breaks team consists of one manager, three coordinators and a team of registered foster carers and child minders.
  • We are based within the Stockport Families First Team of Stockport Children's Service.

Who can access the service?

  • We offer support to all children under the age of 18 years, who have been assessed as needing Short Breaks support. Support may be required because the family is going through a particularly stressful time and it is considered that the child and their family may benefit from Short Breaks involvement.

What do we offer?

  • The Short Breaks Project offers flexible overnight or day support to children and young people.
  • Short Breaks placements can be offered to families who may be experiencing stresses and need additional support for a wide range of reasons.
  • Short Breaks can be a regular or occasional arrangement depending on the needs of the family. 
  • Carers will aim to involve children in activities relevant to their needs.
  • Short Breaks offers both the child and the family a chance to develop relationships and activities independent of each other. 
  • There is no cost to families for this service

What are our aims?

  • We aim to work in partnership with families and carers and other agencies. We will only become involved with the family's permission and where the child agrees to the placement.
  • We are committed to providing training and resources to carers to ensure they can meet the needs of all children.
  • We aim to offer timely support to families before they reach crisis.
  • We aim to develop good communication between all parties, to ensure ideas and approaches are shared, providing consistency for the child and their family.
  • We aim to maintain support to families to provide consistency if difficulties are likely to be long term.
  • We aim to support families to develop and build on their own strengths.

How can you access the service?

Short Breaks take referrals directly from Social Workers who will provide some background information about the family.  The referral is then considered by the team and, if appropriate, a suitable carer who can provide the required support will be matched with the child and family.
