Fly-tipping

Fly-tipping is the illegal disposal of waste and it is a criminal offence. Anyone caught fly-tipping could face a fine of up to £50,000 (unlimited if the case goes to the Crown Court) or up to five years in prison. The Council is able to use CCTV to catch fly-tippers.

You can use the online form below to report incidents of fly-tipping or littering in the Suffolk Coastal district:

  • Fly-tipping and littering reporting form

Alternatively, you can call Suffolk Coastal Norse on 01394 444000 or email scs@ncsgrp.co.uk.

Details like the name and address of culprit if known, registration number of any vehicle involved, and the time, date and place of the incident would be helpful. All information supplied will be treated in strict confidence.

  • Guide to reporting and tackling fly-tipping in Suffolk

Suffolk Coastal Norse will arrange clearance from public land and will investigate all incidents. They can also provide a clearance service to private landowners for a cost based fee.

Whilst Suffolk Coastal Norse will not clear waste fly-tipped on private land free of charge, if you are affected by fly-tipping on your land, we would urge you to still report all incidents to us as it may be possible for the incident to be investigated.  If someone is charged and convicted of fly-tipping waste on private land, they may be ordered by the courts to pay the costs incurred in clearing the waste that they dumped, including costs incurred by the landowner - but we can only investigate incidents that are reported to us.

'Tip-Off' - stop fly-tipping in Suffolk campaign

As part of the 'Tip-Off' - stop fly-tipping in Suffolk campaign, the Suffolk Waste Partnership has produced 'Tripping up the Tippers', a video showing householders that they have a duty of care to ensure that their waste is disposed of legally. The video urges residents to check that anyone offering to take their waste away is legitimate and not a rogue trader looking to make easy money by fly-tipping waste.

A warning poster and leaflet have been created, and are available for download. The warning poster is available in two designs, and can be freely used by anyone affected by fly-tipping on their own property to act as a deterrent.

Produced by the National Fly-Tipping Prevention Group the Framework provides useful practical advice on how to prevent and tackle the problem of fly-tipping and includes a handy checklist for use by councils and local partnerships.
