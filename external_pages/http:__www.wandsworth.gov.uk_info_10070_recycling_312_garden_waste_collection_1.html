Garden waste

Home compost it

Compost as much of your garden waste at home. Home composting:

  • avoids disposal costs
  • gives you a free supply of peat-free compost
  • reduces the environmental impacts of your waste.

More about home composting from our recycling A-Z.

Some garden waste such as soil, bricks, rubble and old fence panels can't be composted. Take small quantities of these free-of-charge to the Household Waste and Recycling Centre (HWRC) in Smugglers Way, Wandsworth.

Or have it collected

  • If you can, please compost garden waste at home or take it to a local tip or HWRC. 
  • Up to five bags of garden waste can be collected free of charge each week - put it in tied refuse sacks and/or dustbins along with other domestic rubbish for collection. Please note that where crews find more than five sacks of garden waste, they have been instructed to leave the excess bags uncollected unless a chargeable collection has been booked and paid for.
  • Garden waste suitable for collection includes grass cuttings, leaves and twigs, weeds, plants, flowers and hedge trimmings. Please note that the garden waste we collect gets mixed with general rubbish in the dustcart, so it cannot be composted. Instead, it's delivered to an energy-from-waste incinerator.
  • Do not put garden waste in clear recycling sacks.

Do not include

  • Anything too big to fit in an ordinary dustbin or refuse sack. This can only be collected on request and for a charge using the bulky waste collection service.
  • Do not leave soil or rubble out for collection.
  • Garden waste produced by paid gardeners: it is their waste and they should make appropriate arrangements to get rid of it.
  • Japanese knotweed.

Japanese Knotweed

Do not include any Japanese Knotweed in with your waste for collection or take it to local tips - you would be breaking laws controlling how this is dealt with and could be prosecuted.  Japanese Knotweed is harmful to the environment,  extremely invasive and is difficult to kill.  Further advice about Japanese Knotweed from the GOV.UK website and the Environment Agency.

If you do need Japanese knotweed waste collected there will be a substantial charge. Please contact a member of the Invasive Non Native Species Association (INNSA) or  contact us for a quote.

Find out more about Japanese Knotweed and how to tackle it in your garden, or visit the Royal Horticultural Society website.
