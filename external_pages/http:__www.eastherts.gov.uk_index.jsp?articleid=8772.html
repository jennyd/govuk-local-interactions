Housing Benefit

Housing Benefit helps people pay their rent. Local Housing Allowance is a new way of working out claims for housing benefit from tenants renting accommodation from a private landlord

Local Housing Allowance

Local Housing Allowance applies where you are renting from a private landlord. The 4 bedroom allowance is the maximum Local Housing Allowance payable.

The allowance is based on the lowest 3 out of 10 private rents. This will mean that only about a third of properties available on the market will be affordable to people claiming benefits.

If you are using an extra bedroom for a non-resident carer you may be able to get this taken into account in your local housing allowance rate. The conditions are that you or your partner have a medical need for overnight care; that you have an additional bedroom; that your carer sleeps in that bedroom when they provide overnight care but they normally live elsewhere.

Find out more about:

  • How to claim Housing Benefit
    Anyone who has to pay rent for their home can claim. It does not matter whether your landlord is a housing association or a private individual or company, you can still claim.
  • Overpaid Housing Benefit
    Paying back Overpaid Housing Benefit
  • What happens if my circumstances change?
    We need to know any change of circumstance within 4 weeks of the change.
  • Local Housing Allowance
    Local Housing Allowance (LHA) arrangements are a way of working out Housing Benefit for people who rent from a private landlord.
  • Single And Under 35? Changes To Housing Benefit
    From January 2012, anyone under 35 is only able to claim housing benefit on a shared accommodation rate to help with their rent (rather than being able to claim for self-contained one-bedroom accommodation).
  • How to Claim Backdated Housing Benefit
    Housing Benefit is normally awarded from the Monday following the day your claim is received by the Benefit Service at East Herts District Council.
