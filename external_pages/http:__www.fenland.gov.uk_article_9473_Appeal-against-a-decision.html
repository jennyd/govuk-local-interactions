Appeal against a decision

You can appeal against a decision that the Council has made regarding Housing Benefit and Council Tax

You can appeal against a decision made about a claim for benefit. This includes the amount of benefit payable, the rent eligible for benefit and the calculations of a claimants income. It also includes the calculation and a recovery of an overpayment.

If you are not happy with our decision, you can contact us and ask for more information about it. We will give you an explanation which can sometimes be done over the phone. Not all decisions can be appealed against - if this is the case we will let you know when you contact us.

Who can appeal?

You can only appeal if you are a "person affected", which is:

  • the Benefit claimant
  • someone acting on behalf of the claimant who is appointed by the Courts
  • someone who the Council agrees is appointed to act on behalf of the claimant
  • A landlord (only in cases relating to who the Benefit should be paid to)
  • An agent (only in cases relating to who the Benefit should be paid to)
  • Any person who overpayment is to be recovered from.

It is only the benefit claimant who can ask us to change our decision about how we have calculated their entitlement. The landlord or agent can only ask us to change a decision about whether payment should be made to them, and whether the decision to recover an overpayment from them has been correctly made. If the Council reduces a tenant's benefit to recover an overpayment in respect of a previous address, the current landlord cannot appeal against this.

What if I'm still not happy with your decision?

If you're still unhappy, you can appeal or request a revision of our decision. You must write to us within one calendar month within one calendar month of the date on the notification letter. In exceptional circumstances, we will extend the time limit for requesting a decision to be revised. You must write to us giving reasons for not asking for a revision at the appropriate time. The Council cannot consider a later requests for a revision if the request is made 13 months after the decision notice was first issued.

After reconsidering, we will let you know in writing if the decision has changed or if it is staying the same. We may ask for more information from you before making a final decision - this must be given within one month of the request.

You can ask us to provide a written Statement of Reasons - this explains how we reached our decision and does not affect your appeal rights. The time taken for us to provide the statement will not count towards the time limit for asking for a revision or sending an appeal to the Tribunal.

Appeal Tribunals

You can ask the Appeals Tribunal to reconsider our decision. The request must be in writing and received by the Council within one month of the date on the decision notification letter. You have one month from the date that you were given the outcome of your revision request to ask for your case to be considered by the Appeals Tribunal. There are special circumstances where the one month rule will be waived - the Appeals Service will decide this. Appeals cannot be accepted as 'late' for any reason if they are received more than 13 months after the date on the revision letter.

Appeal Tribunals are held locally. The Tribunals Service will write to you to tell you the date, time and place of their hearing. You will be asked if you want to attend, or if you want the Tribunal to consider your case without you there. Mostly, the Tribunal will consist of one panel member who is a legally qualified person. A financially qualified person will be there too if complicated financial matters are being discussed. The Clerk to the Tribunal and the Council's representative may also be there.

If either the Council or you thinks the decision of the Appeal Tribunal is wrong in law, they can appeal to the Social Security Commissioners.
