How to pay your council tax bill

When making payment please allow time for your payment to reach your account by the due date. Working days are Monday to Friday, excluding bank holidays. Details are shown on your bill of the date when we need to receive payment by.

There are a number of ways in which you can pay your bill:

  • Online account - sign up for a Salford customer account.
  • Direct Debit - fill in our online form to set up a Direct Debit. You can amend a Direct Debit via your Salford customer account, you will need to set up access to your council tax in the account. You can download a setup form from our online forms page, you can complete and return the one on your bill or alternatively contact us on 0161 909 6502.
  • Credit or debit card - you can pay by credit or debit card via your Salford customer account, online in complete confidence using our secure server pay your council tax bill online. This service can also be used to pay a number of other council bills in addition to council tax. You can also use our automated payment service on 0161 793 3325. Please note transaction charges of 1.4% apply to credit card payments.
  • From your bank account - we recommend you allow three working days but check with your bank exactly how long it will take for the payment to reach us. The details you will need to set up a standing order or to make payment from your bank account are as follows:
    • Bank account number: 93073130
    • Sort code: 20-54-58
    • Bank account name: Salford City Council
    • Your council tax reference number, which can be found on your bill or via your Salford customer account
  • Internet banking - you can use your own internet banking facilities to pay us. Please make sure that you quote the correct reference/account number.
  • Payzone and PayPoint - you can make a payment at any Payzone and any PayPoint outlet by presenting your council tax bill with your payment to the retailer. This service is free.
  • At a Post Office - payments may be made by debit card, cash or cheque (payable to Post Office limited) at any Post Office. This service is free. Please present your council tax bill to the cashier along with your payment - the bill contains a barcode that is used to record your payment.
  • Cheque by post - cheques should be made payable to 'Salford City Council'. Please ensure that your council tax account reference number is quoted on the back of your cheque. Your cheque should be posted to the Strategic Director of Customer and Support Services, Salford City Council, Unity House, Chorley Road, Swinton, Salford, M27 5AW.
  • Payment from overseas - Payments can be made from an overseas bank account direct to Salford city council's bank account. The information you need to do this is shown below:
    • Bank account number: 93073130
    • Sort code: 20-54-58
    • Bank account name: Salford City Council General Fund
    • Bank Address: Barclays Bank, The Gateway Office, Anchorage Quay, Salford, M50 3XE
    • Swift code: BARCGB22
    • IBAN Number: GB66 BARC 2054 5893 0731 30
    • Your council tax reference number, which can be found on your bill or via your Salford customer account

When making payment by Payzone at a Post Office or by PayPoint, please make your payment at least five days before the payment is due, so it will be credited to your account in time.

Do not send cash payments through the post, if you wish to make a cash payment use Payzone, Post Office or PayPoint outlets.

Instalments

The bill shows the date and frequency of the payments you need to make. It is important that you ensure your council tax payments reach us before or on the due date to prevent recovery action being taken against you. If you wish to pay on a different date please use your Salford customer account or please contact us to change your instalments. You can choose to pay your bill annually, monthly, fortnightly or weekly.

Why have a Salford customer account?

See all your details from Salford City Council in one place by registering for an online account.

View details about your council tax account and make payments, report a problem, get contact details for your local councillors and MP and find out when your bins will be emptied. The online account also links to 'Your Salford' where you can find out where your nearest schools, libraries and leisure centres are.

If you're looking for a new home to rent in the city and you're currently a Salford Home Search member, you'll be able to place your expressions of interest without having to login again. If you're not a Salford Home Search member you'll be able to search for available properties and have some of your registration form pre-filled should you decide to register.

With a Salford customer account, you can now access free Wi-Fi in the city's Gateway centres and Broughton Hub. You just need to use your Salford customer account username and password on your phone, tablet or laptop when connecting to the Salford City Council network in any of those locations.

When will the new instalments take effect?

Whenever you contact us, we will, if we can, spread your payments over the remaining months available and issue you with revised payment details. We may not be able to spread your payments if the account has been subject to certain recovery action.

It is important that you ensure your council tax payments reaches us before or on the due date to prevent recovery action being taken against you.

Direct debit is the council's preferred payment method as it helps reduce administration and costs. You are able to choose from four direct debit dates of 1, 5, 15 and 25 of each month. If you decide to pay by direct debit, there are other advantages too:

  • You do not have to complete a new direct debit request each year
  • You will not have to remember to make your payments
  • You will not have to pay the postage to send us a payment
  • You will not have to queue up in the Post Office or local shop
  • You are covered by the direct debit guarantee scheme offered by your bank

If you wish to pay by direct debit, you can change to this method using your Salford customer account, or call us on 0161 909 6502.

Check your balance

You can check your council tax balance online.

Payment difficulties

If you are experiencing difficulty in paying your council tax bill, please contact us as soon as possible. Do not wait for a reminder letter to arrive before contacting us.

We are here to help you, particularly when circumstances are difficult to overcome on your own. Please call us on 0161 909 6502 or use our council tax enquiry form for further guidance.

General advice

If you need any further advice or guidance about paying your council tax bill or to apply for a discount or exemption please see our council tax forms page.

Our business rates pages give details of payment methods available if you pay business rates.
