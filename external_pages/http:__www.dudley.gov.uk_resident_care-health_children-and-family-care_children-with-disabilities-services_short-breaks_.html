Short breaks

Families who care for a child with severe disabilities and/or complex health conditions  who meet the Children with disabilities services criteria may be entitled to receive short breaks.   A short break can allow the parent or carer of the child or young person to have time away from the caring role and allow the child or young person to participate in different activities and gain new and positive experiences.

 

What is a short break?

  • ‘…short breaks provide opportunities for disabled and other vulnerable children and young people to spend time away from their primary carer(s).
    Taken from “Shared care network all kinds of short breaks “ (2004)

A short break can be anything from a daytime/part daytime, evening activity or overnight stay and can be directly provided by the local authority or purchased via direct payments.  This would be following an assessment of the child's needs and only regulated child provisions can be used for overnight care/short breaks.    

There are different types of short breaks, which include;

Care at home - a sitting service, which provide someone to sit with or ‘mind’ your child, in your home environment.  This service may be provided by a number of different providers such as: Kids Orchard.

Day care away from home – these may include nurseries, play groups, leisure groups, and classes for pre-school children.  There may also be out of school and weekend clubs and/or school holidays play schemes.  Out of school play schemes in Dudley are organised by a number of different providers including; Kids Orchard and Barnardos.

Residential short breaks - these would be with regulated child providers such as; residential homes, special units in hospitals and hospices which cater for overnight stays (a special criteria would apply for these types of short breaks)  . Within Dudley local authority these include Barnardos, Tipton road residential unit and Acorns.

Accessing short breaks within Dudley

Some families, who are in need of whole packages of care, including short breaks, can access the short break services by a referral to the children with disabilities team and this can be made via any agency or by self-referral.  A key worker will be allocated to the family who will complete an ‘Initial Assessment’.   The need for a whole package of care, including short breaks would be considered at a resource allocation panel, where the families needs and the reasons for a short break would be discussed, a decision will then be made on the package of care required including what short breaks can be offered to the family.

Other families may need less involvement from the children with disabilities team and may just want to receive short breaks.  These families can request a short break service by completing a self-referral form.   Help in completing the self-referral form can be given by a professional who already has involvement with the family, by the Duty officer or Short Breaks Co-ordinator based at the children with disabilities services.

Purchasing short break services using direct payments.

Some families may prefer to purchase their own short break services and this can be done via direct payments.

Families who are already in receipt of direct payments may be able to purchase the short break services they have been assessed for directly.

  • For further information contact Children with disabilities services
