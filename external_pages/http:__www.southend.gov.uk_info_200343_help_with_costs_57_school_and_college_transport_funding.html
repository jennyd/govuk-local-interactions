School and College Transport Assistance

Please read the following carefully before making any application

Primary and Secondary Schools

You qualify for travel assistance to primary or secondary school if your home address is in the Borough of Southend and one of the following applies to you:

  • You are a primary school student, you applied to your nearest and next nearest schools for a place and we were not able to provide a school place within two walking miles of your home address.
  • You are a secondary school student, you applied to your nearest and next nearest schools for a place and we were not able to provide a school place within three walking miles of your home address.
  • You are a secondary school student, attend a selective school in Southend which is at least two walking miles from your home address and you qualify for Free School Meals or your parent/carer is in receipt of the maximum level of Working Tax Credit.
  • You are a secondary school student, attend one of the three closest secondary schools between two to six miles of your home address and you qualify for Free School Meals or your parent/carer is in receipt of the maximum level of Working Tax Credit.
  • You are a primary or secondary school student, attend a Denominational (Faith) school on the grounds of religious belief within two to fifteen miles of your home address and you qualify for Free School Meals or your parent/carer is in receipt of the maximum level of Working Tax Credit.
  • You are a secondary school student studying GCSEs, move home during year 10 or 11 and attend a school at least three walking miles from your new home address.

Sixth Forms and Colleges

You qualify for travel assistance to a publicly funded sixth form or college in the Southend, Rochford and Castle Point areas if all of the following six points apply to you:

  1. You meet the criteria for Free School Meals or your parent/carer is in receipt of the maximum level of Working Tax Credit or you have a Statement of Special Educational Needs or you have an Education, Health and Care Plan or you have a Learning Difficulty Assessment (and these do not exclude transport).
  2. Your home address is in the Borough of Southend.
  3. Your home address is over three miles from the sixth form or college.
  4. You are under 19 years old on 1st September 2015 (or were under 19 years old on 1st September 2014 if you are commencing the second year of a course)*
  5. You are on a full-time course (a minimum of 450 hours of guided learning spread evenly over 36 weeks of the academic year).
  6. You attend the nearest publicly-funded sixth form or college offering the course to be studied. If you study a multi-subject course such as A levels you must attend the nearest sixth form or college offering 50% or more of those subjects.

You qualify for travel assistance to a publicly funded sixth form or college outside the Southend, Rochford and Castle Point areas if either:

  • Points 1 to 6 above for places within Southend/Rochford/Castle Point apply to you or
  • Points 2 to 6 above apply to you and your parent/carer makes a contribution of £560 towards the cost of transport by 31st March 2016.

* If you have an active Statement of Special Educational Needs or Education, Health and Care Plan, and have been identified as needing transport support, travel assistance may continue until the age of 25. 

How to apply

Please read the Primary and Secondary School Transport Guidance Notes 2015-16 or Post 16 College and Sixth Form Transport Guidance Notes 2015-16 before completing the relevant online application.

  • Fill in the relevant online form found to the left of this page. For Denominational (Faith) Schools, you will then be provided with a denominational signature form, which your celebrant will be required to complete.
  • Online applications can take up to ten working days to process, but during July to September may take longer. For transport assistance to be issued by 1st September an application should be submitted by 22nd July. Incomplete applications cannot be processed
  • Decisions are advised by email - please ensure you check your junk/spam box also. If you are approved for assistance with transport, the email will explain what that will be. Please read it carefully.  If you are not approved, the email will explain how you can appeal should you wish to 

Please be advised that as of this year and going forward we will be making a direct payment to parent/carer for the amount of the appropriate bus pass for your child.

Upon confirmation that your child is eligible for a bus pass, we will email you with the following details:

  • which pass has been agreed
  • the annual amount of the cost of the pass
  • where the pass can be purchased from

We will also attach a form that you will need to complete and email back to us, with your preferred bank account details so that payment can be made to enable you to purchase the bus pass.

Please note that if it is deemed a rail pass is to be issued we will continue to purchase and supply these, details will be given in a confirmation email.

Children and young people with an Education, Health and Care Plan or a Statement of Special Educational Needs should contact their SEN Case Officer to discuss their transport needs or request a Getting To School form.
