Stray dogs

If you find a stray dog, we cannot collect it if it is roaming, so if it is safe and practical, catch the dog.

We are able to arrange for our kennels to collect the dog between Monday - Friday 9am to 4pm. If you do catch a stray you should either:

  • Return it to the owner if the dog carries identification that enables you to contact them
  • Contact the the Public Protection service at the Council who will provide you with the details of our kennels so you can take the dog there or arrange for them to collect it

If the owner can be located the dog will be returned to them, otherwise the dog will be detained at the Council's kennels and kept for a period of seven clear days, after which time the dog becomes the property of the Council and may be re-homed, passed to a rescue society or destroyed.

A dog would only be destroyed if it could not be re-homed due to illness or behavioural problems. A fee of £100 is payable before an owner may reclaim a stray dog. However, if the dog carries identification* and it is the first time we have picked up this particular dog the fee will be £50.

*Identification means: the dog is microchipped or has a collar and tag.

If you have found a stray dog and wish to keep it yourself, this may be possible but you must follow the correct procedure. You will need to inform the kennels of your wish and they will advise you of the procedure.
