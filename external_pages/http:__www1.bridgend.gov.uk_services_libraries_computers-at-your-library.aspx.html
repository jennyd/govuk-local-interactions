Computers @ Your Library

Computer access is available FREE of charge @ Your Library

  • If you are already a library member, you need only complete a simple form to start using the computers (if you are under 16 you will need a parent's signature).
  • If you are not yet a library member, please enquire about joining at your local library or click here to join online. Membership is free to all.
 Â 

Each local library has the following facilities:

  • Fast Internet access
  • E-mail
  • Microsoft Office software (word processing, spreadsheets etc.)
  • Scanners and printers
  • Downloading to CD and USB drive
  • FREE WIFI is available at all libraries for those who want to use their own devices. Please note that the use of Wi-Fi requires a free one-time registration.

In addition, an extensive programme is underway to make thousands of historical photographs, documents and texts available on every computer terminal and over the Internet.

If you have never used a computer before, don't worry. When possible staff will assist inexperienced users in getting started on the Internet. Regrettably, staff cannot offer extensive instruction in the use of computers or software but will try to answer specific questions about the Internet and will offer suggestions for effective searching. However Bridgend Library and Information Service does not guarantee that suitably-trained staff will be available at all times to assist users

Please contact your local library or Library Headquarters (Tel: 01656 754800) if you have any questions about using the computers at your library.
