Young people's activities funding opportunities

Integrated Youth Support Service (IYSS.Projects@kirklees.gov.uk) Last updated - June 2015
On this page
  • Young people's holiday activity programme,
  • Weekend programme,
  • Play in the Community Programme,
  • Small grants programme

Young people's holiday activity programme

The young people's holiday activity programme is a programme of fun informal activities for 8-19 year olds in Kirklees, and up to 25 years old for disabled young people. The activities are run in the school holidays by Integrated Youth Support Service in partnership with the voluntary and community sector. Activities range from arts and crafts, environmental projects, sport and recreation and community cohesion projects.

Programme funding

The programme is managed by Kirklees Council's Integrated Youth Support Service. The programme covers both end-of-term and half-term holidays for all young people in Kirklees. Applicants can apply for the full cost of delivering activities.

Weekend programme

The weekend programme is a programme of fun informal activities for 8-19 year olds in Kirklees, and up to 25 years old for disabled young people.

Activities offer young people who live in Kirklees opportunities to engage in positive developmental activities as a diversion from crime and anti social behaviour.

The activities are run at the weekend by Integrated Youth Support Service in partnership with the Voluntary and Community Sector. Activities range from Arts and Crafts, Environmental Projects, Sport and Recreation and Community Cohesion Projects.

Programme funding

The programme is managed by Kirklees Council's Integrated Youth Support Service. The programme covers both end-of-term and half-term holidays for all young people in Kirklees. Applicants can apply for the full cost of delivering activities.

Play in the Community Programme

Kirklees Neighbourhood Housing (KNH) has allocated funding to invest in regular term time junior work programmes on estates with an identified anti-social behaviour risk. In addition KNH is committed to encouraging and supporting local led community development and believes that by combining junior work with community outreach that long-term sustainable outcomes are achievable. Engaging and involving parents and other local volunteers in leading play activities and developing additional activities should provide a sustainable link to the commissioned provision.

Programme funding

The programme will be managed in partnership with Kirklees Council's Integrated Youth Support Services. We are inviting Voluntary and Community Sector organisations with experience of delivering in the areas mentioned below to apply to deliver part of the programme and would particularly welcome applications from organisations who can offer diverse programmes in young people's own communities.

Application dates and documents

The commission may comprise a consultation period and an activity programme. Consultation should take place in November and December with activity programmes beginning in January running through to October.

Small grants programme

One off project costs of up to £500 where the project diverts young people from anti social or criminal behaviour. The small grants programme aims to promote or improve activity programmes, particularly at the weekend, which offer young people who live in Kirklees opportunities to engage in positive developmental activities as a diversion from crime and anti social behaviour. Projects should be additional activities that complement other sessions taking place in Kirklees. Activities range from Arts and Crafts, Environmental Projects, Sport and Recreation and Community Cohesion Projects. Young people benefiting should be 8-19 or up to 25 for disabled young people.

Programme funding

The programme is managed by Kirklees Council's Integrated Youth Support Service; we run activities in partnership with the Voluntary and Community Sector.

The programme aims to cover Friday, Saturday and Sunday nights for all young people in Kirklees. Applicants can apply for the one off costs of delivering the project.

Application dates and documents 2015-16
