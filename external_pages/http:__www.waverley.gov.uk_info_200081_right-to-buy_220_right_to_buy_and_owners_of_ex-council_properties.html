Buying your council home

Council tenants usually have the right to buy their homes.

 You can apply to buy your council home if:

  • it is your principal and primary residence
  • it is self-contained
  • you are a secure tenant
  • you have had a public sector landlord (e.g. a council, housing association or NHS trust) for five years - it does not have to be five years in a row.

You will need to think about a few things before making this big financial decision. So take your time to consider whether it is the right choice for you. You will be taking on new responsibilities as owner including the maintenance and repair of your home. Please see Your right to buy your home website to check your eligibility for Right to Buy.

The Money Advice Service provides helpful financial advice and a Mortgage Payment Calculator can help you work out how much you will be paying should you decide to take on a mortgage.

Apply to buy your council home 

To apply to buy your Council home, please contact the Homeownership Officer on 01483523030 or 01483 523163 for a Right to Buy pack.
