Blue badge parking permits for disabled people

The blue badge scheme helps disabled people with severe mobility problems to access goods and services by allowing them to park close to their destination.

Apply or renew a blue badge on GOV.UK

After completing your application on GOV.UK, you will need to upload one passport-sized photograph and email all relevant documentation to servicedeliveryteam@northyorks.gov.uk including evidence of your disability. You must also include your BBIS tracking reference (this can be found in your GOV.UK confirmation email).

  • It costs £10 for a blue badge (including replacements and renewals). Payment can be made by calling 01609 536997. If your application is unsuccessful your payment will be refunded.
  • It can take up to eight weeks to process your badge from receiving your application.

If you prefer you can get a blue badge application pack at one of our local libraries (apart from community libraries).

If you wish to know more about blue badges or organisational badges, download the Department for Transport's blue badge scheme: rights and responsibilities in England document.

Lost or stolen blue badge

You should report stolen badges to North Yorkshire Police. After reporting a stolen badge to the police, or even if you have simply lost it, tell us your badge has been lost or stolen as soon as possible. You may request a replacement badge by emailing servicedeliveryteam@northyorks.gov.uk.

Residential disabled parking bays

In certain circumstances, on-street parking bays can be created in residential areas for disabled drivers who are blue badge holders and who don't have access to off street parking, such as a drive or garage.

Find out more, including how to make an application, on our residential disabled parking bays page.
