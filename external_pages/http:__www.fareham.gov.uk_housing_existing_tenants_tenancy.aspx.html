Tenancy services

The section covers the following areas:

What does tenancy management cover?

  • Setting up new tenancies and terminating existing tenancies (including garage tenancies). This includes change of tenancy, making joint tenancy applications, dissolving a joint tenancy and succession of tenancy
  • Enforcement of tenancy conditions, including dealing with anti-social behaviour carried out by our tenants, their families or their visitors
  • Rent payment enquiries including giving advice and taking payments by phone
  • Tenant participation – we run a forum so that tenants and leaseholders can give us their views about where they are living
  • Supported tenancies - providing housing to support elderly people and sub-letting properties to organisations providing support for people with independent living
  • Managing different tenancy types.
  • Mutual Exchange – where two or more tenants decide they want to swap homes
  • Providing information and advice on the right to buy your Council property and administering the scheme
  • Help with garden maintenance  - a service for elderly and disabled tenants
  • Help with internal decorations – a service for elderly and disabled tenants.

What does estate management cover?

  • Abandoned vehicles - Advice on reporting vehicles abandoned on housing land
  • Block cleaning - General and window cleaning at Council flats/maisonettes.
  • Grounds maintenance - Maintaining housing land, including grass cutting
  • Estate improvements - Improving estates so that they are safe places to live
  • Block captain regime – To gain feedback on works carried out on estates
  • Refuse disposal - Dealing with items dumped on housing land or within blocks

 What is sheltered housing?

If you are of retirement age and have support needs, you may have thought about sheltered accommodation. This is a range of unfurnished accommodation suitable for people aged over 60 years with a support need. It aims to provide independent living conditions with support available if it is needed in an emergency. We have more than 650 units of sheltered accommodation throughout the Borough. These are divided into categories depending on levels of service and your support needs:

  • Mobile sheltered housing officer service with a weekly visit
  • Sheltered housing officer visit/support five days a week
  • Sheltered housing officer visit seven days a week
  • Sheltered housing officer visit seven days a week - enhanced care & support.

Communal facilities are available at some of the schemes. All accommodation has alarms fitted, linked to a central control. Calls for assistance are covered 24 hours a day, every day throughout the year.

For more information, contact the sheltered housing team leader on 01489 557511 or the mobile sheltered housing office on 01329 282318. You may also find information provided by Age UK useful at www.ageuk.org.uk 

 What are Leasehold Properties?

Leasehold properties are flats or maisonettes which former Council tenants have bought under the right to buy scheme. Leaseholders own their property and are responsible for maintaining it. They have certain rights and responsibilities and also have to contribute towards the cost of maintaining communal areas and the exterior of the building. There are more than 400 leasehold properties in the Borough.

Who should you contact?

Our tenancy services manager oversees the work of the division and can be contacted on 01329 236100. If you have an enquiry about tenancy, estate or rent matters, contact the area housing estates officer on 01329 236100 or email housing@fareham.gov.uk. You can also call into the Civic Offices and ask to see the duty officer. If you wish to see a particular officer we may ask you to book an appointment by phone or at the Civic Offices.

Since April 2012 the Homes and Communities Agency (HCA)  has been the regulatory body for all housing organisations including local authority housing. We try to be as helpful as possible in providing Council services. If you have a comment, compliment or complaint or if you are dissatisfied with the way in which you have been treated, please talk to a member of our staff.
