West Dorset district councillors

Listen

West Dorset District Council has 42 councillors who are elected by the public every four years. They represent 24 different wards of West Dorset.

The next district council election will take place in May 2019. See previous election results for West Dorset District Council.

Become a councillor in West Dorset

The next elections for West Dorset District Council take place in May 2019.

Political composition

Following the 2015 elections, the political composition of West Dorset District Council is:

  • 30 Conservative
  • 12 Liberal Democrat
  • The Conservative party is the controlling political party of the council

There are 24 wards across West Dorset.  The interactive map of District Councillors (opens in a new window) can be used to find your local councillor and other council facilities and services.

Each councillor is a member of Full Council and may also sit on additional Committees - West Dorset District Council.

  • West Dorset Councillor complaints

    West Dorset District Council has a duty under the Localism Act 2011 to promote and maintain high standards of conduct by its councillors and co-opted councillors. The council has a process for investigating complaints made about individual councillors under West Dorset District Council's code of conduct.
