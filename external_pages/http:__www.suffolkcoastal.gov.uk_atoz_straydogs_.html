Stray, lost and dangerous dogs

Stray dogs

The following advice applies if you find a stray dog within the Suffolk Coastal District Council area. If the dog was found in another council’s area, please contact that council for advice.

During office hours - please contact Suffolk Coastal Norse on 01394 444000 to report that you have found the dog and for further advice. It is helpful if you can have the following information ready:

  • Description of dog, including breed (if known), size and colour.
  • Details of any collar and tag.
  • When and where the dog was found.

Please note that the Council does not have an obligation to collect the dog from you, although we will do so during office hours if at all possible.

Suffolk Coastal Norse office hours are 8.45 am to 5.15 pm Monday to Thursday and 8.45 am to 4.45 pm on Friday.

Outside office hours - if you find a stray dog, please check whether it has a collar with an identifying tag. If there is no collar or tag, a veterinary practice may be able to scan the dog to determine whether it has a microchip which will identify the owner. If you are able to identify the owner, please contact the owner and ask them to collect the dog from you.

If you do not know who the owner of the dog is, please consider whether you can hold the dog until the next working day, but please call our out of hours number 01502 527133 to log that you have found the dog, in case the owner has reported it to us as missing. Alternatively, you may take the dog to the Council’s designated kennels up to 9pm on any day. In order to leave a dog at the kennels, you will be required to show valid ID containing your name and address otherwise the dog will not be accepted.

Lost dogs

If you have lost your dog, please contact Suffolk Coastal Norse on 01394 444000 or email scs@ncsgrp.co.uk and they will check their records to see whether it has been reported as found, or collected and taken to the kennels.

For dogs that have been taken to the kennels, before a dog can be released to the owner, all fees and charges must be paid in full to Suffolk Coastal Norse Limited. These fees and charges are made up of a statutory charge plus administrative and kennelling charges, based on a rate for each day (or part day) the dog is in our custody. When the payment has been made, the owner will receive a certificate of release enabling the collection of the dog from the Council’s designated kennels.

Veterinary fees and any other treatment charges incurred are payable at cost price in addition to all other fees.

1 day - £25 release fee plus £61.90 kennel and administration costs – total fee £86.90

2 days - £25 release fee plus £85 kennel and administration costs – total fee £110

3 days - £25 release fee plus £107 kennel and administration costs - total fee £132

4 days - £25 release fee plus £123.50 kennel and administration costs - total fee £148.50

5 days - £25 release fee plus £140 kennel and administration costs - total fee £165

6 days - £25 release fee plus £156.50 kennel and administration costs - total fee £181.50

7 days - £25 release fee plus £173 kennel and administration costs - total fee £198

8 days - £25 release fee plus £189.50 kennel and administration costs - total fee £214.50

and thereafter a further £20 per day that the dog is kept.

Where 2 dogs from the same household have been collected together the charge for the second dog on day 1 is reduced by £35.

Dogs will be kept for a maximum of seven full days, after seizure or service of a statutory notice, whichever is the latest. Unclaimed dogs are usually offered for re-homing. However, the Council retains the right to have unclaimed dogs destroyed.

Dangerous dogs

If a dog is dangerously out of control, and/or injures someone, it may be possible to take action under the Dangerous Dogs Act 1991. In an emergency, please contact the police by dialling 999, otherwise please contact the Incident Control Room on 01473 613500.

For incidents of a dog attacking a dog or other animal, please contact the dog warden service for further advice by calling 01394 444000 during office hours.
