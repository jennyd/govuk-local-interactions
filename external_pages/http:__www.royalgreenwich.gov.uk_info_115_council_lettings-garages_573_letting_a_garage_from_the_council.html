Letting a garage from the Royal Borough of Greenwich

Garages to let

Do you need a garage? There are a limited number of council-owned garages available to rent in Royal Greenwich.

What does it cost?

Garage rents must be paid by monthly direct debit. 

Prices are graded A to C according to the standard of the garage and its location.

How do I apply?

Download a garage application form, complete it and return it to the Royal Borough of Greenwich's Tenancy Support team. You can also request a form by contacting the Tenancy Support team. 

For more information on council-owned garages, contact Tenancy Support.

Download a garage rental form

What happens next?

You will be put on a waiting list. Garages are allocated according to priority and then by the date of the application. Priority groups are described in the table below.

Descriptions of priority groups for council garage allocations
Priority group Description
Group A A household where any member of the household is disabled and the residents are council tenants or leaseholders who pay a service charge to the Royal Borough of Greenwich
Group B A current garage tenant whose garage is being closed down
Group C A household that is suffering car damage due to racial or homophobic harassment and the allocation of a garage is recommended by the multi-agency panel
Group D Members of a household that are council tenants or leaseholders who pay a service charge
Group E Other residents of Royal Greenwich
Group F Applicants from outside Royal Greenwich
Group G Requests for second garages from council tenants or leaseholders who pay a service charge to the Royal Borough of Greenwich

Please note: These facilities are not available to Royal Borough of Greenwich staff for employment related parking.

Arrears

If you are a tenant with rent arrears or a leaseholder with service charge arrears, your name will not go onto the garage waiting list until your account is clear.

If you rent a garage from us and your account falls into arrears, we may repossess the garage.

Terminating your garage rental

If you wish to terminate your garage rental, you must contact the Royal Borough of Greenwich and give us at least a week's notice. The notice period must end on a Monday.

Please phone the Tenancy Support team to discuss this.

  • You will need to come in to one of the Royal Borough's service centres (The Woolwich Centre or The Eltham Centre) to fill out a Garage Termination form and return your garage keys.
  • Keys should be returned by 12 noon on or before the date at which the notice period comes to an end, to avoid the Â£100 charge for a lock change.
  • You should ensure that when keys are returned that the garage is clear of any belongings as you may be charged for the clearance of any items that remain past the expiration of your notice.

In the event that you are served with a Notice to Quit due to non-payment you will also need to follow the same termination procedure.

Garage termination procedures are the same for tenants of Royal Greenwich and non- tenants.
