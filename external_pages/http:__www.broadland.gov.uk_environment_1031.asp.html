does the council offer hazardous waste collections?

Yes - Broadland can collect small amounts of household hazardous waste from residential properties. From April 2014 Broadland is offering a chargeable hazardous waste collection service at £30 per collection.

We can collect hazardous household chemicals that should not be place in your green rubbish bins such as pesticides, herbicides and weedkiller.

Our contractor will collect the waste on a specified day, they will identify it, pack the waste in a suitable container and then transport it to a licensed disposal site.

To avoid this charge, you could see if your left over chemicals can be used responsibly by someone else.

Norfolk County Council run hazardous waste amnesty days at the household waste and recycling centres in the autumn months to enable the public to dispose of hazardous waste for free.

Small amounts of left over paint can be left to dry in the tin or turned out onto an old cloth to dry before being placed in your green rubbish bin.

how do I order a collection?

To order a collection contact the Contracts Team providing a list of the items with a description and an approximate quantity.

Wherever possible if the chemical you have is re-usable such as weed killer or petrol please use it or pass it on to someone who can before contacting the Council. 

Payment can be made using a debit/credit card. We require payment before the collection can take place.

back to top

what items are classed as hazardous waste?

'Hazardous waste' can be defined as any material discarded by a household which puts human health or the environment at risk due to its chemical or biological nature.

The following are examples of hazardous household products that you might have at home.

Household products:

  • White spirit
  • Paint brush cleaners
  • Hazardous paint products
  • Descalers
  • Stain removers
  • Drain cleaners
  • Household batteries
  • Fluorescent light tubes / lamps
  • Glues
  • Photographic chemicals
  • Oven cleaners
  • Swimming pool cleaners

Garden products:

  • Pesticides
  • Insecticides
  • Lawn treatments
  • Fungicides
  • Weed killers
  • Wood preservatives

Car maintenance products:

  • Antifreeze
  • Brake fluid
  • Lubricants
  • Degreasers

back to top

how should I handle hazardous household waste?

  • Use the product carefully and safely
  • Always leave materials in their original containers
  • Store in a cool, dry place, out of reach of children or pets
  • Never pour hazardous liquids down the sink or drain
  • Never mix chemicals together

back to top

which types of hazardous waste are not accepted and what can
I do with them?

  • Liquid fuels: including petrol, diesel and paraffin 
    • Re-use where possible (e.g. petrol driven lawnmowers or motoring / military societies)
    • Some motor repair centres may take it too
  • Commercial hazardous waste: contact the Environment Agency on 0870 850 6506
  • Ammunition, explosives or marine flares: contact your local police station
  • Medicines: take back to your local dispensing chemist
  • Fireworks: contact your local fire and rescue service for advice on local points where they can be taken

further information

Please be aware that Norfolk County Council household waste and recycling centres do no accept hazardous waste. From April 2014 they will no longer be accepting paint at their sites. For further information see the leaflet in the document section.

Norfolk County Council run Hazardous Waste Amnesty events in the autumn months at their recycling sites to enable the public to dispose of hazardous waste for free. Please see the Norfolk County Council link below to find out more about any future events.

Small amounts of left over paint can be left to dry in the tin or turned out onto an old cloth to dry before being placed in your green rubbish bin.
