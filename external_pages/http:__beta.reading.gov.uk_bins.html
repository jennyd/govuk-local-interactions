Rubbish, Recycling & Garden Waste Collections

Most households in Reading have a grey wheelie bin for general waste and a red wheelie bin for certain recyclables. These are collected fortnightly on alternate weeks. You can recycle even more materials at the tip or at a local recycling point. RecycleNow holds information on where you can find a recycle bank.

We encourage you to try and reduce the amount of rubbish you produce - it helps save money and the environment. Read our tips on reducing waste.

We also offer fortnightly collections of garden waste from households that have our green garden waste bins or bags.

Enter the postcode for your property in our search to find your bin day.

Where and when should I put my bin out for collection?

Please put your bin, box or bag on the kerb outside your property before 7am on the morning of collection but no earlier than 6pm the night before.  Please remove it as soon as possible after collection.

Please make sure that the bin is not too heavy and the lid is down.

If you do not put your bin out in time for collection, we will not collect it and you will either have to wait until the next collection date or take it to the tip.

Enter the postcode for your property in our search to find your bin day.

What should I do if my bin has been missed?

Please first check that it:

  • Was the correct bin on the correct day
  • Was put out before 7am
  • Wasn't too heavy or left with the lid open
  • Didn't contain the wrong kind of waste
  • Is after 4pm on collection day.

If you think we've missed your bin even though you have put it out correctly, use our report a missed bin form to tell us. You must do this by 5pm on the working day following your scheduled collection day.

If our crew hasn't reported your bin as not presented or containing the wrong items, we will return to empty it before the next scheduled collection day for that bin.

If severe weather disrupts collections, we will keep you up-to-date on this website, via our Twitter account or local media.

I have more rubbish or recycling than I can fit into one bin.

General waste

For most households one standard grey bin is enough when used alongside recycling bins or boxes. We will only empty one grey bin of rubbish from your property in each collection and won't take any extra bags or rubbish.

In some circumstances, for larger households, we might approve a larger grey bin. Please call us to provide more information.

If you have an item which is too large to fit in your bin, you can either take it to the tip or arrange a bulky waste collection with us.

Recycling

If you have a cardboard box too large to fit into your red bin or box, please flatten it and put it to the side of your bin and we will take it at the next collection.

If you regularly have more recycling than you can fit into your container, you can order a new one from us free of charge..

Green Bin

We will only collect green waste in our bin or bags.  If you regularly have more waste than will fit in your green container, you can order a new green bin from us.

Is there anything that won't be collected?

As part of our regular household waste collection, we won't collect waste from businesses, hazardous waste or items that are too big for your bin. See our Special Waste Collections and Business Waste pages for more information.

How do I get a garden waste bin or bag and what do they cost?

We will only collect your garden waste if you have one of our green garden waste bins or bags. These containers are specifically designed for the storage and collection of garden waste and keep it in good condition for composting. See what you can put in your green bin.

Containers cost:

  • 240 litre wheeled bin - £35.82
  • 90 litre waste bag - 11.26

Order a Green Bin now and we will deliver it in 15 to 20 working days.

Our green waste collections are fortnightly, except for a short period around Christmas. Enter your postcode in our search to find your bin day.

If you have a gardener or other contractor working on your property, they should not use your green bin as this waste is treated as business waste.

If you don't want to buy one of our containers, you can take your garden waste to the tip yourself. Alternatively, try home composting.

What should I do if my bin has gone missing or I don't have one?

If you don't have a bin or it has gone missing or got damaged, you will need to order a replacement from us.

The charges are:

  • 41.20 for a new or replacement standard grey general rubbish bin (we do not allow residents to buy additional grey bins). Contact us to order one.
  • 61.80 for a replacement large grey general rubbish bin (must be authorised by us - contact us with details)
  • 35.82 for a new, additional or replacement green garden waste bin. Order a Green Bin.
  • 11.26 for a new, additional or replacement garden waste sack
  • No charge for new or additional red recycling bins/boxes. Order a new recycling container.

We will deliver it to you in 15 to 20 working days.

If our bin crews report that your bin was lost or damaged during collection, we will replace it free of charge.

Can I take my bin/box with me when I move?

If you move, please leave your grey and red bins at your old property.

If you have a green bin or bag and are moving to another property in the Borough, you can take it with you but please contact us to tell us your new details so we know you need a collection.

What are the collection arrangements for flats?

Recycling and General Waste

Large blocks of flats will have communal bins for recycling and general waste. Recycling bins are emptied fortnightly but general waste collections may be more frequent in some circumstances. We are committed to maintaining weekly collections of general waste bins in 11,000 households with existing weekly collections.

You can help to keep your communal bin area tidy and make sure we can empty the bins by putting your rubbish and recycling in the correct bins, not leaving rubbish on the floor and not parking in front of the bin area on the collection day.  We will not clear any rubbish or recycling which is not contained in the bins or empty bins that contain the wrong items.

You may find our  leaflet explaining recycling from your flat [1Mb] helpful.

Green Waste

We do not provide large communal bins for green waste.  If you live in a flat and have your own garden, you can Order a green bin from us and we will collect it fortnightly (except for a short period around Christmas). Gardeners or other contractors working at the property should not use it as it is classed as business waste.

I have difficulty moving my bin - what should I do?

If you are unable to move your bin due to age, illness or disability and there is no one to help you at your property, please contact us to arrange an assisted collection.

How can I keep my bins clean and free from pests?

Give the inside of your bin a wash every now and then. If you can't manage this yourself, there are companies who will clean it for you.

To avoid smells and reduce the risk of flies and maggots:

  • Wrap food waste and double-bag cat and dog faeces before putting into your bin
  • Keep the lid of your bin closed
  • Try to store your bin in the shade to prevent it from getting hot
  • Smear the lid of your bin with essential oils such as citronella to deter flies (do not use if you are pregnant)
  • Don't leave food lying open in your home as flies will lay their eggs on it
  • If you have maggots in your bin, kill them using boiling water or insecticide sprays or powders.

My street does not have wheeled bins - how is my waste collected?

Some roads - if they are too narrow, have small front gardens, or are in the town centre - have different collection arrangements, such as rubbish bags, recycling boxes or communal bins.

What can I do with glass, textiles and other recyclables that the council doesn't collect?

You can take glass, foil, textiles, cartons and other recyclables that we don't collect to the tip or to one of our recycling centres around Reading. Find them on the Recycle Now bank locator.

Find out more about what you can re-use or recycle in our  A to Z of how to reduce, reuse and recycle. [105kb]

Why can't I put other plastic items into my recycling bin?

We only collect plastic bottles because they can be recycled easily and there is only a limited market in the UK for other types of plastic. Margarine tubs, yoghurt pots, meat trays and plastic bags are all made from different types of plastic which are difficult for us to separate and process once collected.

What happens to my recycling after it has been collected?

After collection, our crews take your recycling to the materials recycling facility (MRF) at Smallmead Tip in South Reading. There it is sorted and sent to reprocessors to be made into new products. For more information go to our  A to Z of how to reduce, reuse and recycle. [105kb]

See also the re3 Annual Report.
