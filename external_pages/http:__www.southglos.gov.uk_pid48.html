Child performances

Children in entertainment

Many children enjoy performing, whether on stage, films, advertising or on television and parents enjoy supporting them. However, laws exist which are designed to protect children’s welfare and prevent them from being exploited.

The local authority is responsible for issuing performance licences and must be satisfied that:

  • the child is fit to take part in the performance or activity
  • proper provision is made to secure the child’s health and kind treatment
  • proper provision is made to ensure that the child’s education will not suffer
  • the child will be chaperoned at all times (this can be the child’s own parent or a registered chaperone hired by the applicant)
  • the conditions of the licence will be observed

Children who perform in television, film, on stage, who work as models, or who take part in paid or professional sport, will require a licence issued by their local authority if:

  • they are receiving payment; and/or
  • absence from school is required; and/or
  • ahey perform on more than 4 days within a 6 month period

The law applies to all children from birth until completion of compulsory school age. A child is compulsory school age until the last Friday in June of the school year in which they turn sixteen.

Next steps

It is essential that your child obtains a licence before the performance takes place, therefore we ask for the application to be sent to us at least 21 days before the show/performance is due to start. Applications received with less than 21 days notice may be refused (Please note that we need to be in receipt of all paperwork in order to commence the processing of a performance licence).

If school absence is required, the local authority, before granting a licence, require a letter from the headteacher of the child’s school to ensure that the child’s education will not suffer should a licence be granted. Therefore, a licence will not be issued without the school’s permission.

You can download the Performance Licence application form and return with the necessary documents to:

Child Performance Licensing, CAH Business Support, Department for Children Adults & Health, PO Box 298, Civic Centre, High Street, Kingswood, Bristol BS15 0DQ. Email: CAHBusinessSupportBMR@southglos.gov.uk
