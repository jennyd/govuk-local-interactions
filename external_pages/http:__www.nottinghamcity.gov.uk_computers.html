All Nottingham City Libraries have free computer facilities, with access to the internet*, scanning, printing and Microsoft Office applications.

All computers have an accessible USB drive and allow you to save your work on to our computer server. You can then access this work from any library computer.

As a library member you can

  • Have 60 minutes of free computer time per week
  • Have a maximum of 10 hours per week (600 minutes) of computer time on any Nottingham City Library public computer
  • Book a maximum of 3 sessions up to 15 days in advance
  • Book a maximum of 60 minutes per session. If additional time is required this can be booked via library staff

*Pay day loan company websites:  Nottingham City Council believes that pay day loans are detrimental to the health and economic wellbeing of Nottingham's citizens. The Council is supporting: stricter controls over pay day loan companies and premises; stricter regulation of advertising for pay day loan companies; and the promotion of credit unions which provide a safer loan alternative to vulnerable citizens. As a result all pay day loan company websites will be blocked and inaccessible on all Nottingham City Council computers, including public computers in libraries from Thursday 19th September 2013. For information on alternative and advice on financial services visit the Welfare Rights Service.

How to Use a Library Computer

  • To use a computer you must be a library member
  • To join the library please click here
  • If you are under the age of 18 you will need written permission from a parent or carer
  • When using a library computer you must also agree to our computer access policy

Book a Library Computer

  • Once you have joined the library you will receive your library membership card and PIN number
  • You will need both your membership card and PIN number when booking and logging on to a computer
  • You can book a computer at your local library or online via our Netloan booking website

Computer Learning 

We offer free computer learning sessions across all Nottingham City Libraries

  • One2One sessions - One to One tutoring covering computer basics, including an introduction to the internet and email
  • Group sessions - Advanced computer sessions covering a variety of topics, including social networking and online shopping
