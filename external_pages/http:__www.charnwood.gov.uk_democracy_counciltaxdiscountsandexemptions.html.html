Council Tax Discounts

Council Tax is based on 2 or more adults (i.e. aged 18 or over) being resident in a property. If only one adult is resident, there may be a 25% discount.

If we receive a completed application form and proof of their status, certain people may be 'disregarded' when calculating discount.  These people include full-time students, full time student teachers, school leavers aged 18 and 19, student nurses, apprentices, youth trainees, severely mentally impaired people, some carers, prisoners, members of religious communities, patients who are resident in homes and hospitals, diplomats and members of visiting forces. If you think somebody in your household could be disregarded please complete the relevant claim form (available on our Council Tax Forms page). If all but one adult resident is disregarded, a 25% discount may be awarded. If all the adult residents are disregarded, a 50% discount may be awarded.  If all the disregarded residents are students then the property will be exempt from Council Tax.

If a property is unoccupied and unfurnished it may be awarded a 100% discount for a maximum of one month. 

If a property is unoccupied, unfurnished and is undergoing major structural alterations or repairs a discount of 50% may be awarded for a maximum of one year. 

If a property is unoccupied and furnished it will not be awarded a discount.

If a property is unoccupied and furnished because the  liable person lives elsewhere in a property provided by their employer in order to carry out his or her job, a 50% discount may still be awarded in respect of the unoccupied property.

If an annexe is occupied by a relative of the person liable to pay Council Tax in respect of the main dwelling it adjoins, a discount of 50% may be awarded.

If an annexe is unoccupied but used by the people living in the main dwelling as part or their main residence, a 50% discount may be awarded.

Discounts and exemptions will only be considered upon receipt of appropriate documentary evidence of entitlement. You may also be required to complete an application form.  You must continue to pay your bill as requested while we consider your application. If a reduction is granted a revised bill will be issued to you taking into account payments received. If there is a credit balance on your account after a reduction has been applied, this will be carried forward against your future liability or refunded to you on request.

If a discount or exemption has been applied to your account you must tell us within 21 days of any change in circumstances affecting your entitlement

Some properties may be eligible for exemption and more information can be found on our Exemptions Page. This includes advising when an unoccupied property becomes furnished and/or occupied or sold, when there is a change to the number of occupants in the property which affects a discount or when an occupier of the property turns 18 or ceases to qualify for disregarded status.

You may apply online for a Council Tax Sole Occupant Discount or Student Disregard Status.

For other discounts please view our Council Tax Forms to down load an appropriate form. The form should be completed in full and sent to Charnwood Borough Council, Council Tax Department, PO Box 184, Erith DA8 9EU.  You may contact the Council Tax office for further details or advice.

For details of current Council Tax charges visit Council Tax Banding Rates.
