Parks and green spaces

  • Allotments

    Tending an allotment is a great way of growing healthy vegetables, fruit and herbs at a competitive price.

  • Boston Woods

    Established 10 years ago, Boston Woods Trust now has 100 acres of woodland, parkland and meadow open for public access with good quality all-weather paths for walking and cycling.

  • Central Park

    Established in the Victorian times Central Park is in the heart of the town centre.

  • Country parks and open spaces

    We maintain a diverse range of green open spaces that are accessible for all to enjoy.

  • Crematorium/cemetery maintenance

    We are responsible for maintaining the grounds of the Boston cemetery and crematorium and also Fosdyke cemetery.

  • Floral sponsorship

    How would you and your business like to help us achieve a Gold Award in the Royal Horticultural Society Britain in Bloom campaign?

  • Football pitches

    We currently maintain eight football pitches which are hired by many local football teams.

  • Unauthorised use of football pitches

    We are experiencing issues with unauthorised use of Boston Borough Council football fields at Rosebery Avenue and Garfits Lane.

  • Grounds maintenance

    We currently undertake a wide variety of maintenance jobs throughout the borough. We strive to make Boston a more colourful attractive place to live, work and visit.

  • Havenside Country Park and Local Nature Reserve

    Havenside is a great place to experience Lincolnshire's wildlife.

  • Play areas

    We are responsible for managing 10 play grounds within the borough.

  • The Wash

    The Wash, shared between Lincolnshire and Norfolk, is one of the UK's most important estuaries providing a winter feeding ground for over 300,000 visiting water birds.

  • Tree and hedge planting

    Only 3% of Lincolnshire is covered by woodland, well below the national average for England of 8%.

  • Tree maintenance

    We are responsible for the management of trees which are on council-owned land.

  • Wildlife conservation

    The Boston area presents a wealth of opportunities to view wildlife, ranging from barn owls and bats to common seals and oystercatchers.

  • Witham Way Country Park and Local Nature Reserve

    Situated approximately one mile from the town centre, the park offers a great setting for both wildlife and people!
