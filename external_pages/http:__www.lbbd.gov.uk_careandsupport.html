Care and support is changing for the better in Barking and Dagenham

From April 2015, important changes to Adult Social Care came into force which could affect the way care is provided to you either now or in the future.

The Care Act 2014 represents the biggest change to adult social care in over 60 years. The Act, which is being introduced in two phases from April, reforms the law relating to the care and support of adults and their carers.

The main focus of the Care Act is to promote wellbeing and prevent or delay people needing social care services by putting people at the heart of the system.

The Act will encourage greater integration of adult social care and health services, create new national eligibility criteria for care and support, give carers the same legal rights as the people they care for and set a cap on the maximum amount individuals will have to pay towards the cost of their care.

For details of all the changes in the Care Act, please refer to the Department of Health’s factsheets on the Care Act, which provide an overview and information on the duties and powers of Local Authorities.

The Department of Health has also produced an easy read version – Making sure the Care Act works.
