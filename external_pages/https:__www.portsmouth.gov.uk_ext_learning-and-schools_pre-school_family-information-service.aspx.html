Family information service

Information, advice and guidance for families 

The family information service (previously Portsmouth Chat) provides tailored information, advice and guidance for families with children from pre-birth to 19, and those working with families.

Our Online Service Directory has now closed and is due to be replaced with a brand new Family Service Directory. You'll be able to access our new directory from this page when it's ready.

In the interim - please contact Family Information Service for details of local childcare and services for families.

  • phone 023 9268 8830
  • email fis@portsmouthcc.gov.uk
  • social media facebook.com/portsmouthchat

Professionals, please complete and submit our referral form, or simply call us to discuss the needs of the family you're working with.

 

Childcare      Open / Close

Choosing a childminder, nursery or other childcare provider can be one of the most important decisions you have to make as a parent.

The process can often pose questions - what childcare should I choose? What happens if my child doesn't settle? Will I get any financial help? Where are the nurseries or childminders close to my home? What will my children be learning? Don't panic - we're here to help. We can help you to understand the jargon, access financial support to pay for childcare, or brokerage to help you obtain a place.

  • all childcare we list is Ofsted registered - to find out more or to view a childcare provider's inspection report visit the Ofsted website
  • our introduction to childcare guide will help you get started
  • find out more about free childcare for two-year-olds - who is eligible and how to apply

Schools, children's centres, education, leisure, benefits and other family services    Open / Close

The family information service can advise on all areas of family support, including those provided by the council. Follow the links below, or contact us for further information on:

  • school admissions - key dates, catchment areas, how to apply, making a school preference, understanding school reports
  • leisure opportunities - children's centres, libraries, community centres and other local groups and clubs
  • rights for working parents - maternity/paternity leave, flexible working options, taking time off for dependants
  • financial entitlements - accessing family benefits, such as funding for two-year-olds, early years education for three and four year-olds, completing claim forms, estimating tax credits
  • family and parenting support - help to make life as a parent or carer run smoothly. Whether you're a new parent, lone parent, new to the area, or simply in need of some advice, we're here to lend a hand

Parents and carers     Open / Close

Search the directory for information, advice, and support on a variety of topics as well as things to do in the local area for you and your family.

If you need support or advice on things that may be worrying you, such as your health, debt, career, parenting, relationships or anything you feel you need to talk through with someone, you can find services in Portsmouth who are there to help you.

We can inform you of your legal rights on a range of subjects, such as housing, advocacy and special needs/disabilities. We can also give general advice on the law or specialised areas and have more in-depth information for separated parents on subjects such as residency, contact and maintenance.

In addition we can assist with rights for working parents - for example, flexible working requests, time off for dependants and maternity/ paternity leave.

Young people aged 13 - 19     Open / Close

 

There are loads of ideas of things to do and places to go in and around Portsmouth, such as youth and sports clubs, music and drama groups.

If you need support or advice on things that may be worrying you, such as relationships, bullying or anything you feel you need to talk through with someone, there are services in Portsmouth who are here to help you.

Please contact Family Information Service for details of local services for available.

Children and families with disabilities     Open / Close

Please visit Portsmouth Local Offer for information on services and support available to children and families with disabilities and Special Educational Needs (SEN).

Customer feedback and complaints    Open / Close

We would like to hear from you if you have a suggestion about how we can improve our service or if you are happy with any part of the service you have received. You can do this using our online feedback form, by email or letter, in person, on the telephone - see our contact details above.

If you have any concerns or issues relating to the childcare provider you are using please ensure you first follow your childcare provider's published complaints procedure. It is important that you try to resolve any issues directly with the provider. If you are unable to resolve your concern or issue:

  • Where the concern relates to the care a registered provider is offering and the welfare of the children attending you must contact the regulator, Ofsted. Ofsted can be contacted by telephone 0300 123 1231 or email enquiries@ofsted.gov.uk
  • Where the concern relates to the way a provider offers free early education funding please ensure you first follow your childcare provider's complaints procedure. It is important that you try to resolve any issues directly with the provider. If you are unable to resolve your concerns please contact us by phone or email as above, or use our online feedback form, directing your message to the 'Early Support Team'. We try to resolve any concerns about funding directly with the parent and childcare provider.
