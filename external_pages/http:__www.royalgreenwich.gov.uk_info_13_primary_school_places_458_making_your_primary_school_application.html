How to apply for a primary school place

Applying for a place in the reception class

View pages in this section

If your child is due to start primary school in September 2016, you will have needed to apply for a place between 1 September 2015 and 15 January 2016.

How to apply

You can apply for schools in any area, but if you live in the Royal Borough of Greenwich you need to make your application through us.  

The easiest way to apply is through the eAdmissions portal - it is quick, easy and secure - and it is the fastest way to hear the results. 

If you would rather complete a paper form, you must complete the Royal Borough of Greenwich's common application form.  

If you live in another borough, you need to apply through your home authority even if you wish to apply for a place in a Royal Greenwich school. 

Applying for a place at a junior school

Some boroughs have infant and junior schools. If you live in Royal Greenwich and your child attends Year 2 in an infant school in another area, you will need to apply through us for a Year 3 place in a junior school.

Apply for a primary school place now (eAdmissions website)

Supplementary information forms for voluntary aided schools

You will also need to complete a supplementary information form if you are applying for a place at:

  • a voluntary aided school as a faith entrant
  • or at St Mary Magdalene, regardless of faith.

This must be returned to the school by the same date as the deadline for the main application form.

You can find out more about what type of school a school is by looking at its entry in the primary school admissions booklet.

Download supplementary information forms for September 2016 primary school admission

Split site schools

Please note that there are additional arrangements for schools with split sites. Please refer to the admissions booklet for details on the schools you are interested in.

Decide on your preferred schools

Primary school admissions booklet 2016 to 2017

Before deciding on your preferences, you need to consider each school's admissions policies and previous years' application rates to see whether your child is likely to be offered a place. 

Apply for up to six schools

You can apply for up to six primary schools, listing them in order of preference.

Your preference schools can be in Royal Greenwich or in other areas, but they can't be independent (fee paying) schools.

You do not have to list six schools, but the more schools you list, the better your chances of being offered a place at one of them.  

Please do not list the same school more than once. Your child can only be considered once for each school so any duplicates will be deleted.

Be realistic about your preferences

Although it is up to you to decide on your preference schools, your preferences do need to be realistic. We recommend that you do not 'waste' a preference by naming a school at which your child is unlikely to be offered a place.  

As any preference may be offered, you should not name a school you do not wish your child to attend.

While there is no guarantee that your child will be offered a place at any of your preference schools, we recommend that you include schools nearest to your home address. If you do not, and it is not possible to offer you one of your preferred schools, your child may be allocated a school much further away.

Please make sure that you have ranked your preferences in the order you genuinely want them to be considered.  Any changes you wish to make to your preferences after the closing date will only be considered once all applications, including those submitted late, have been processed.

Siblings already attending a school

If you have an older child or children already attending the school, you must make this clear on your application.

Medical or social reasons for preferences

In most cases, a child's medical or social needs can be met in any mainstream school.

However, if you feel that there is an acute medical or social reason why your child should be offered a particular school, you need to state this in your application.   

In your application you will need to:

  1. Give reasons why your child should attend a particular school.
  2. State the consequences for your child if they had to attend an alternative school. 
  3. Provide supporting documentation from the relevant professional, such as a qualified medical practitioner.

Please bear in mind that childcare arrangements do not fall under the category of an acute medical or social need.

For information on affordable child care provision available in Royal Greenwich, such as breakfast and after-school clubs and childminders contact our Families Information Service.

Please also note that we do not normally consider moderate learning difficulties such as delayed speech and language or dyslexia as being an acute medical or social need.

We can only consider a request for a place in a school's Designated Specialist Provision (DSP) if it is supported by our Special Educational Needs Monitoring and Assessment Service.

Get help with your application

The Families Information Service offers support to parents and carers who may need support with the school admissions process.

The service is available to everyone and provides: 

  • a wide range of information on local primary schools
  • support in making informed and realistic preferences
  • help in completing the relevant forms
  • information about the appeals process. 

If you have questions about which schools will best meet your child's academic and developmental needs, or you require support in making your application, please contact the Families Information Service.
