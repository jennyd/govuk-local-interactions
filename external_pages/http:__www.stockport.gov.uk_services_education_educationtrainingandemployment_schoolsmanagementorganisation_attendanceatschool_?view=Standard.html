Attendance at School

One of the functions of Services for Young People is to promote and enforce regular school attendance.

This is achieved through monitoring each child’s attendance and providing support and advice to schools, the pupils and their families.

Young People's Workers are based in secondary schools but also visit all primary schools regularly to check registers, talk to teachers and receive referrals about young people. They also undertake home visits to speak to parents  and carers whose child is not attending school regularly.

Punctuality is also an area of concern, as late arrival at school has a disruptive effect on pupils' education.  It can effect a child's attendance record as arrival after the close of register results in an unauthorised absence being recorded against the pupil.

The school leaving date for pupils who reach their 16th birthday between 1st September 2011 and 31st August 2012 is the last Friday in June 2012, young people are not allowed by law to enter full-time employment until after that date. 

With effect from summer 2013, however, all young people will be expected to stay in some form of learning until they are 17 and up to age 18 by 2015.  More information on Raising the Participation Age.
