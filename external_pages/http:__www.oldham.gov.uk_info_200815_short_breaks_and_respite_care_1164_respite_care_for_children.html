Respite care for children

Respite care provides a series of short term placements with the same carer.

The length and timing of the arrangements can vary according to the specific needs of the child and their family.

Problems at home 

For children to be placed within the scheme there has often been some family crisis or problem for which they need some support or assistance (for example a family illness, the parents finding the child's behaviour very difficult to handle or the parent is not coping).

Contact the Children's Assessment Team on 0161 770 3790 to find out about assessments. 

Disabled children

The Oldham Family Link Scheme provides short term breaks for children and young people with disabilities, by linking them with a carer who looks after them for short periods of time in their own home, on a regular basis. This can range from an overnight or weekend stay, to an occasional full week.

The children who need short breaks may be autistic, have a physical disability, a learning difficulty or sensory impairment.

You have to be assessed as needing support, so if you think you and your family could benefit from Oldham Family Link contact the Social Work Team on 0161 484 1240.

Paying for respite care

There is a charge for staying in a care home for a period of respite.

You will need to have a financial assessment to determine the rate you will be charged.
