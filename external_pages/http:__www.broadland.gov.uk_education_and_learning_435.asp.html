tots2teens school holiday scheme

Listen

Broadland District Council's Tots2Teens run holiday activities for children aged five years and upwards during all school holidays. Activities are held throughout the Broadland District in village halls, schools and community centres from Reedham to Reepham and in the fringe parishes of Hellesdon and Thorpe St Andrew.

To be eligible, children must live or go to school in Broadland. Families can register free of charge to receive our holiday information and are required to complete and sign a parent consent form.

A variety of activities are organised including fun zones, sport zones, art zones, teen zones and wildlife zones.

what to do next...

  • Download the latest brochure for the February half-term holidays 
  • Book online - you will need your username and password 
  • You will need to register your details before any bookings can be made, please contact the Tots2Teens team using the details below
  • If you are a member and have misplaced your username or password, please contact the Tots2Teens team using the details below                  
  • When booking online please ensure that you press 'finish' on the 'payment successful' screen, this will then take you to a 'booking confirmed' screen where you will need to press 'finish' again to confirm your booking
  • If you navigate away from these pages before the booking is confirmed, although the payment will be made, a place will not be booked on the relevant activity
  • If you are a school or organisation in Broadland and would like to work with us or would like to find out how you can use your pupil premium funds with us, please contact the Tots2Teens team using the details below

further information

Fun Zones run from 9.30am - 3.30pm and are a mixture of arts and crafts activities and games for children aged 5 - 12 years. Each Fun Zone involves a different specialised workshop.

Art Zones consist of specialised art based workshops such as canvas painting, learning to draw, jewellery making and cookery.

Sports Zones offer a chance to try new sports and build on current skills. Sessions, led by qualified coaches, are offered in sports such as badminton, tennis, archery and golf. A number of sports follow governing body award schemes.

Teens Zones consist of specialised activities for those aged 10+. Activities include crime scene investigation, radio broadcasting and computer skills.

Through partnership working we are able to offer additional sessions such as wildlife zones, subsidised theatre tickets, dance workshops and additional sports events.

Children's University

Young people are able to collect stamps towards their Children's University learning passports at some of our sessions. Look out for the logo in our current brochure to find out at which activities stamps can be gained. Learning passports can currently be obtained from all libraries and some schools in Norfolk. There are over 125 activity providers set up across Norfolk where young people can take in activities outside of school time and earn stamps. Young people can graduate once they have reached certain milestones of learning hours.

Visit the Children's University website for more information.
