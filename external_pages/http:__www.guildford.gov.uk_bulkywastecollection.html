Bulky waste collections

Guildford Borough Council is now working in partnership with the Surrey Reuse Network to collect bulky waste from domestic properties within the Borough.

This service will be provided by The Surrey Reuse Network and their partner The Furniture Helpline. Their aim is to increase the amount of reuse and recycling from the bulky waste that is collected.

To arrange a collection

  • Call 01420 488 783 and our receptionist will arrange the collection
  • Email bulkygbc@surreyreusenetwork.org.uk providing your name and contact telephone number, we will contact you to arrange the collection.

All collections are normally completed within seven days of the receipt of your payment.

Those on low income, unemployment and housing benefit may be entitled to a discount.

Do I need to be home when the collection is made?

No, but we would prefer to collect from inside your home as we are able to reuse or recycle more from bulky waste that is kept dry. If this is inconvenient, we ask you to cover the items concerned and we will collect them from the pre-arranged location.

We start collecting at 9.30am, allowing the householder time to complete the essentials, such as the school run.

Collections of more than five items

If you need more than five items collected we would arrange to visit your home to assess the collection and would then provide you with a quote based on an hourly rate of £84. There will be a minimum charge of £84 and the charge is based upon the time required to remove the waste including the travelling charge and if appropriate, a disposal charge.

Items that cannot be collected

There are a number of items that cannot be collected and you will need to contact a private registered waste carrier to have these removed. These include: asbestos, builder's rubble, fluorescent tubes (even in a sun bed), garden waste, oil tanks, car batteries, various liquids including pots of paint, sheet glass, sheet plate glass, full size snooker/billiard/pool tables, large organs and pianos.

Cast iron baths count as one item but must be broken up before collection.

Petrol mowers must be emptied of fuel and oil.

Ride on mowers and mobility buggies will be assessed first.
