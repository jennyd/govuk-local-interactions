Council garages and store sheds

Applying

Anyone can apply to rent a council garage, storeshed, motor cycle unit or bicycle locker. Priority is given to estate residents. In areas of low demand we also let garages for the purpose of general storage.

  • How to rent a garage
  • How to rent a storeshed
  • How to rent a motorcycle unit
  • How to rent a bike locker

When you receive an offer

If you receive an offer you must let us know whether it is suitable within a specific time (see below). You may be refused an offer if you have any type of arrears with the council. Discretionary offers may be made on a case by case basis in special circumstamces.

Charges

Rent varies and you will be advised of it when you receive an offer. You may be charged VAT. 

You can pay rent and view your statement on housing online. 

I am moving

If you move address you must notify the non-residential section as you will continue to be charged rent until you give formal notice.

If you are no longer a council tenant or leaseholder you will be required to vacate the garage, storeshed, unit or locker and clear it of contents. For more information ask the non-residential team.

Repossession

The council reserves the right to repossess where:

  • there are arrears, including property rent or service charge arrears
  • it is not being used for the agreed purpose
  • it has been sub-let or passed to a third party
