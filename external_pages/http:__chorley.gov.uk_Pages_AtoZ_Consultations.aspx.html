Consultations

Main Content
We are keen to ensure that our services are tailored specifically to the needs of our customers - and to do that, we need to know exactly what you want.

We carry out consultations with customers throughout the year - most notably when we're planning the budget and want to know your views on where we should be allocating resources.

Our teams often carry out consultations to help improve the way services are delivered. You can view the consultations online.

Accordian Content
  • Budget Consultation 2016/17

    Have your say on the council budget for 2016/17

    Our Executive (the Leader and Cabinet) is seeking the opinions of residents, partners, staff and councillors on the proposed budget for 2016/17.

    The consultation asks where you would like to see the council invest money for the coming year. The survey should take 2 to 5 minutes to complete and will run until the 14 February 2016.

    It’s important that you have your say as the outcomes of the survey will be shared with all Councillors before any final decisions are made regarding the setting of the budget at the Full Council meeting on Tuesday 1 March 2016.

    If you would like to have your say please complete the online survey in the ‘Do it online’ section. Paper copies are available on request.

  • Council Tax Local Discounts & Exemption Policy

    From 1 April 2013 local authorities have had the discretion to adopt a policy charging a council tax premium of up to 50% for long term empty properties that have been unoccupied and substantially unfurnished for more than 24 months. However, Following consultation in 2013 Chorley Council made the decision to charge an additional 25% premium in these circumstances. The Council is now proposing to increase the premium to 50%, resulting in a council tax liability of 150%. However, if properties are actively being marketed for sale or rent in line with average prices within the local area, there would be a six month period of grace before the charge would be levied.

    The number of empty properties can have a negative impact on local communities. The change to the premium will support the policy decisions to encourage the occupation of empty properties within the borough. The Empty Residential Properties Policy is available to download.

    We’d like to hear your views. Please complete the Council Tax Local Discounts & Exemption Policy survey by Sunday 3 January 2016 for your comments to be considered.

  • Knowley Brow Play Area Consultation

    Chorley Council are proposing to improve Knowley Brow Play Area. The site has been identified in the Play Area strategy as low quality but high value.

    We want to hear from you about where you think money would be best spent by filling in this questionnaire. The budget won't stretch to a full scale revamp, we will have to keep the existing equipment, install a few new pieces of equipment and improve the access, and general environment.

    To create the best open space possible we need your ideas and local knowledge. Please complete our online survey.

    The Consultation will close on Monday 8 February 2016
