Register to vote

 

Why you should register to vote

  • If your name is not on the register you will not be able to vote
  • So that you can have a say in issues that affect you
  • If you are not on the register you might be refused a loan, mobile phone contract or even be turned down for a bank account

Who can register?

  • Anyone aged 16 or over (but you cannot vote until you are 18)
  • British or qualifying Commonwealth citizens. This means Commonwealth citizens who have leave to remain in the UK or do not need such leave
  • Citizens of the Republic of Ireland or other European Union (EU) member states

What you will need

  • Your name
  • Your address
  • Your date of birth
  • Your National Insurance number (find your National Insurance number)

Individual Electoral Registration (IER)

Before June 2014, the head of a household was responsible for registering everyone at their address.

Each person must now register themselves using a new, more secure system, called Individual Electoral Registration.

Anyone who is aged 16 or over needs to register; you become eligible to vote when you reach your eighteenth birthday.

Some residents may receive two similar letters about electoral registration. Anyone who has recently registered or advised the Electoral Services office of any changes at their address does not need to do anything further.
