Care assessment

Anyone over the age of 18, in need of care or support, is entitled to ask for an adult social care assessment. There is no charge made for this assessment. This also applies to young people under 18 who are transferring their care and support from children's to adult services.

If the level of your unmet needs meet the national eligibility criteria, the council can help arrange support to meet your needs. This might range from a couple of hours a week home-help to support you to live independently in your own home, to organising residential care where that is the best option.

Find out about the eligibility criteria

How can I get a care assessment for myself or someone I care for?

Please contact the Advice and Referral Team, Adult’s Wellbeing:

  • Telephone: 01432 260101
  • Email:ASCAdviceandReferralTeam@herefordshire.gcsx.gov.uk
  • Fax: 01432 261666

Can the council help me arrange care even if I am not eligible for financial support?

The purpose of a care assessment is to establish the support and care needs you may have and to agree a support plan for how best these can be provided. This service is available to everyone and can provide you with a very useful planning document with outline costs for the various services you may require.

If you may be eligible for financial help with paying for these services, we can complete a financial assessment as well as a care assessment.

But even if you are not eligible for financial assistance, we can work with you to help you organise and secure the services you need.

What happens in the care assessment?

The purpose of an assessment is to establish the care and support needs you may have and better identify what support is required to meet your needs. The assessment will also be used to check if you are eligible for assistance using the national eligibility criteria.

A social care worker will come to visit you and your carer or family member. They will go through the assessment with you and talk to you about your needs and work with you to find out what services and support will best help you to live a more independent life. Alternatively, you may choose to complete the assessment yourself, with the help from a family member, friend or carer, with support from the council as required.

With your permission, we may need to share information about you with other agencies that become involved with your care such as your doctor or district nurse.

Timescale

We aim to complete assessments within 28 days. In an emergency we aim to start the assessment and provide help within 24 hours.

What happens after the assessment?

We will check that you agree with the content of the assessment and then use the assessment to check if you are eligible for assistance from the council. This will be done using the national eligibility criteria as set out by the Care Act 2014 in the Care and Support (Eligibility Criteria) Regulations 2014.

We will then share a copy of the assessment and eligibility decision with you in writing and agree the next steps, which may include preparation of a support plan if you have eligible unmet needs.
