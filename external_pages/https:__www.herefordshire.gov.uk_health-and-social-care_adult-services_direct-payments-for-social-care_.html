Direct payments for social care

If you or someone you care for has been assessed by social care services as needing care and support services you may be able to get direct payments.

We can make direct payments to:

  • Elderly people who need community care services
  • Carers aged 16 and over
  • People with parental responsibility for a disabled child
  • Disabled parents for children's services
  • Disabled people aged 16 or over (with short or long term needs)

What can direct payments be used for?

You can use direct payments to:

  • Employ your own staff to help with your personal care needs and running your home
  • Buy services from a social care agency
  • Maintain your relationships and social activities
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle
  • Arrange short breaks
  • Get equipment for your home

People who have savings and capital above £23,250 are not eligible for financial assistance towards non-residential care services from Herefordshire Council. 

Support and advice in managing direct payments

If you have been offered a personal budget by Herefordshire Council and are considering taking a direct payment to manage the budget there are several sources of support and help available to you:

  • Factsheets - available to download or print from this page or to request from your care worker. Read through these to see if direct payments will suit you, or for help with a specific topic
  • Direct Payment Advisors - available to support you in setting up or deciding whether to take direct payments
  • The council's approved providers for direct payment support - organisations which can provide services to help you manage your direct payments

Support with finding out about and setting up direct payments

The council’s Direct Payment Advisors can provide you with some help and support, free of charge, such as:

  • Advice about the costs of services and day activities available to you
  • Advice on setting up a direct payment bank account
  • Setting up personal contributions into your direct payment account
  • Information about using the council's approved direct payment support providers and what they can offer you
  • Helping you understand financial accounting requirements and associated paperwork

If you change your mind

If you decide direct payments are not working for you, we can also:

  • Advise on closing a direct payment account, including assisting your family or a suitable person to pay all outstanding liabilities
  • Help you or a suitable person find information on employment law and redundancy
  • Advise and support you completing final accounts to close the direct payment account

Managing your direct payments

There are a number of organisations in Herefordshire that can provide help and information, support and advice about employment law when using direct payments.

This includes help with all the legal aspects of employing a personal assistant such as: information, support and advice about employing and recruiting staff and advice and support around self-employment and duties of being an employer.

View the council's approved providers for direct payments support

You do not have to use a council-approved provider. Details and information about other providers are available from the external websites on this page.
