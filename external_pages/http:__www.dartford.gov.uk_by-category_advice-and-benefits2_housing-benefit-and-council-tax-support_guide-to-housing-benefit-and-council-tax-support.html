Guide to Housing Benefit and Council Tax Support

Housing Benefit

You can usually claim Housing Benefit if you pay rent to a social or a private landlord. If you rent privately what you receive will usually depend on the Local Housing Allowance (LHA). For more information on Housing Benefit and LHA please see the Housing Benefit pages on the GOV.UK website.

Housing Benefit can't be paid for heating, hot water, energy or food - if you need help, use the Benefits Adviser on the GOV website to see what else you might be entitled to.

 

Council Tax Support

From 1 April 2013, Council Tax Benefit was replaced by a localised Council Tax Support scheme which is shown as a discount on your Council Tax rather than as a benefit.

The local scheme adopted by this Council will calculate your support in the same way as Council Tax Benefit did, and then, if you are working age, reduce your entitlement by a percentage.  For 2013/14 the percentage reduction was 8.5%, and for 2014/15, 2015/16 and 2016/17 the percentage reduction is 18.5%.

For claimants of pension-credit age the Government have prescribed the rules we must use to calculate Council Tax Support.

Housing Benefit/Council Tax Support eligibility

If you are on a low income, you may be entitled to Housing Benefit and/or Council Tax Support. Depending on your income and circumstances, benefits and support can be awarded for up to 100 per cent of your Council Tax and rent. To find out if you qualify for Housing Benefit, see the Benefits Adviser on the GOV website.


Discretionary Housing Payments

These are extra payments made at our discretion to help people on Housing Benefit who are in serious financial difficulty. They are free-standing payments and can only be made to help with housing costs. The amount of money we have for these payments is subject to an annual cash limit.

For more information, see our leaflet Discretionary Housing Payments - Extra help towards your rent PDF, 967.17 KB.

Please note that you cannot receive a Discretionary Housing Payment to help with Council Tax.


Guide to receiving Housing Benefit/Council Tax Support

We will tell you how much you will receive, how you get it and the date your benefit/support will start. This will normally be the Monday after you apply. In some cases we can pay benefit/support before the date you apply.

The law and the local scheme say that we can backdate a claim for up to six months for working age customers, from the date of your written request. All requests for backdating must be in writing.

See our leaflet Can my claim be backdated? PDF, 965.98 KB for more information, which includes a form.


How Housing Benefit/Council Tax Support is paid/received

We will tell you how you will get your entitlement once your application has been assessed. 

If you rent from the Council your Housing Benefit will be credited to your rent account.

If you rent privately but still wish payments to go to your landlord you should read our Safeguard Policy PDF, 93.08 KB for further information.

We will usually need you or someone on your behalf to complete a Request for payments of Local Housing Allowance to be paid to the landlord or third party form PDF, 191.95 KB. This will need to be fully completed and detail why you feel that you will have difficulties managing your rent and you should provide evidence to support this. Once we have received your completed form we will write to you with our decision.

Completion of this form does not guarantee that payments of Housing Benefit will go directly to your landlord; each application is assessed on its own individual merits.

If you rent from a housing association you will usually be able to choose if you want your Housing Benefit paid to your landlord or yourself.

Benefit must be paid into a bank account or building society account. Your local Citizens Advice Bureau provide advice and information about how to open a bank account.

Information is also available at www.moneyadviceservice.org.uk.

Council Tax Support will be shown as a discount on your Council Tax Bill.


Paying rent

As a tenant you are responsible for paying your rent to your landlord. If you do not pay the rent your landlord may apply to us to have your Housing Benefit paid to them, or take other action to recover their money.

If you are in rent arrears your landlord may be able to go to the courts and ask that you be evicted from your home.


Calculating Housing Benefit/Council Tax Support if you return to work

Contact us with details of your expected earnings and other income and savings and we should be able to give you an estimate of how much Housing Benefit and/or Council Tax Support you would get.


Financial help if you return to work

Extra financial help may be available. If you go back to work and your Income Support, Jobseeker's Allowance (IB) or Employment and Support Allowance (IR) ends, you may be entitled to an extended payment of your Housing Benefit and/or an extended award of Council Tax Support for up to four weeks.

You must have been receiving Income Support or Jobseeker's Allowance for 26 weeks before starting work, and the work must be expected to last for at least five weeks.

Some people who have been receiving Incapacity Benefit and/or Severe Disablement Allowance may also be entitled to an extended payment and/or an extended award.

When you tell us you have started work, we will check to see whether you are entitled to such a payment and/or award and advise you of the outcome.


Childcare costs

In certain circumstances we take childcare costs into account, but you must use an approved childcare provider. Contact us for details.


If you are away from home

You must tell us if you are going to be away from your home. We need to know the date you are leaving, the date you expect to return and why you will be away.

In some cases we can continue to give you Housing Benefit and/or Council Tax Support. In most cases you must not intend to be away for more than 13 weeks.


If you disagree with our decision

If you disagree with our decision about your application for Housing Benefit you can ask us to:

  • explain our decision
  • look at our decision again - this is called a reconsideration or
  • pass your case to an independent tribunal - this is called an appeal.

If you disagree with our decision about your application for Council Tax Support you can ask us to look at our decision again. Your application will be looked at again by Benefit Services and then may be referred to the Valuation Tribunal if you remain dissatisfied with the outcome.

For more information see our leaflet What do I do if I disagree with your decision?  PDF, 985.62 KB

You cannot appeal against the Local Housing Allowance rates for the area you are living in, because these statutory rates are set by the Rent Service, an independent body separate from us.

You cannot appeal against the Local Scheme itself, except by judicial review, only the award of support in your particular circumstances.
