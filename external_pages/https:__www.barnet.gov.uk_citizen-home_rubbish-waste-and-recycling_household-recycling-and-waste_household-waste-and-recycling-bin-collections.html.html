Your waste and recycling bins

Depending on the kind of property you live in, you will have a different selection of bins. This page is for residents who live in houses, converted flats in houses and small blocks of flats with five or less properties.

Find out more information on recycling if you live in a flat block with 6 or more properties.

Blue bin for mixed recycling

Your 240 litre blue bin is for mixed recycling and is collected weekly. You do not need to sort the items.

Mixed recycling:

  • cardboard
  • cartons
  • food tins and drinks cans
  • household plastic packaging
  • mixed glass, bottles and jars
  • mixed paper, including loose shredded paper

What you shouldn't put in your blue bin

  • batteries: please place household batteries in a clear bag on top of the lid of your blue recycling bin on your collection day
  • textiles: please do not place clothes and textiles in your blue recycling bin. Textiles can be taken to the Summers Lane Reuse and Recycling Centre, charity shops or textile recycling banks. If you cannot recycle your textiles in any of these ways please contact us using the details below.
  • electrical items: You should not place electrical items in your blue bin. These items can be recycled at the Summers Lane Reuse and Recycling Centre. Small electrical items can also be recycled at various electrical recycling banks across the borough or you can arrange a free collection by contacting ReStore Community Projects on 020 3142 8506.
  • clinical waste: please do not put any clinical waste items such as syringes, needles or other sharp items in your recycling bin as these are extremely dangerous in the manual processing of materials. These items cannot be recycled. If you need to arrange a clinical waste collection please click here.
  • wood: please do not place this in your blue bin. Wood can be recycled at the Summers Lane Reuse and Recycling Centre.
  • food waste: please place food waste in your brown bin for recycling
  • nappies, polystyrene and crisp packets: these items cannot be recycled and should not be placed in your blue bin. Please dispose of these in your refuse bin.

Download an A-Z list of what can be recycled and where. If an item is not listed, unfortunately it cannot be recycled.

Additional Blue Bin
If you need an additional blue recycling bin you can request one free of charge by using the bin order form or by contacting our Street Based services team using the contact details below.

Clear Sack Recycling Service
If you cannot physically accommodate a blue bin on your property, we will provide a clear sack recycling service. We will deliver batches of recycling sacks two times a year and you can put the same items in your sack as in a blue bin. If you are unsure if this applies to you or need to order more sacks please contact our Street Based services team using the contact details below.

Brown bins for food waste

You have a 7 litre food waste caddy for your kitchen and a 23 litre food waste bin. Your caddy is for you to collect unwanted food and leftovers in your kitchen. You can empty this into your larger food waste bin that is kept outside. The brown bin has a lockable lid.

We will collect your brown food waste bin weekly.

All cooked and uncooked food, including:

  • bread, pasta, and rice
  • dairy
  • fish
  • fruit and veg
  • meat and bones
  • tea and coffee grounds

Closing and locking your brown bin

  1. empty kitchen caddy: take bag from caddy and tie it up
  2. open outdoor food waste bin by moving handle to rear of the bin
  3. put tied bag in
  4. close and lock food waste bin by closing lid and pulling handle to upright position

Compostable liners
Compostable liners are widely available at most supermarkets, many hardware stores, and through online suppliers. We can only accept liners with the seedling logo, as these will break down during the composting process.  Here are some of our recommended suppliers:

  • Alina for Barnet
  • Caddy Liners Direct
  • Gardening Delights
  • All Green

If you do not wish to use liners you can place your food waste loose into your brown bin for collection.

Brown bin collections during warm weather

Follow these tips to minimise any problems while using the food waste recycling collection service during warm weather:

  • Place any food waste to be recycled immediately into your food waste caddy in your kitchen and empty this daily into your external brown food waste bin.
  • Clear out all food that requires recycling from your fridge the night before collection day
  • Try to keep the food waste bin out of direct sunshine in really hot weather
  • Keep the bin lid locked at all times by placing the handle in the upright position
  • If you are using compostable liners, please note that we can only accept liners with the seedling logo
  • Some essential oils, such as citronella and tea tree oil are natural insect repellents. Try smearing your bin lid with one of these oils if flies are a problem but please do not use if you are pregnant
  • If your food waste caddy or bin needs cleaning, you can rinse it with soap and water or a mild disinfectant.

Green bin for garden waste

Barnet offer an optional service for garden waste. If you are signed up to this service, you will be provided a green wheeled bin which should only be used for garden waste. Your green bin is collected fortnightly.

Garden waste, including:

  • grass cuttings
  • hedge trimmings and tree prunings
  • branches up to 20cm in diameter (8") and under 60cm (24") in length
  • flowers leaves and weeds

The majority of the organic waste collected in Barnet is garden waste. Collecting garden waste with food waste together is costly as it has to be collected weekly and processed in an indoor facility due to food waste treatment.

For many residents with a green bin they will have a Saturday collection. You can check your green bin collection day using our postcode lookup.

Black bin for general waste

All items you cannot recycle should go in your black refuse bin. Your black bin is collected weekly.

  • Un-recyclable rubbish
  • Plastic film / wrapping
  • Polystyrene
  • Nappies and pet waste

Please note that energy saving light bulbs contain small amounts of mercury and must be treated as hazardous waste. These should not be put in your blue or black bins but should be taken to the Summers Lane Reuse and Recycling Centre for safe disposal.

Green bin collections during cold weather

During very cold weather the contents of green bins can become frozen and stuck inside the bin – preventing the contents from being fully emptied by your collection crew. For health and safety reasons, crews cannot manually remove any items which are stuck in the bins. We will empty the contents on your next scheduled collection. Where possible, the council will endeavour to provide an interim collection of those bins that could not be fully emptied.

If you would like to request an interim collection due to frozen contents in your bin please contact the Street Based services team (telephone: 020 8359 4600, email sbs@barnet.gov.uk).
