Disabled Facilities Grants

What are Disabled Facilities Grants?

The Council offers two types of Disabled Facility Grant (DFGs).

Mandatory Disabled Facility Grants (DFGs) are grants paid by the Council to help towards the cost of adapting your homes to enable a disabled person to continue to live there. A grant can be paid when the Council considers that the changes are necessary to meet your needs, and that the work is reasonable and practical. Mandatory Disabled Facilities Grants have national rules laid down by central Government under the Housing Grants, Construction and Regeneration Act 1996 (as amended) that local councils are required to follow.

Discretionary DFGs are given under the Council's Private Sector Housing Financial Assistance and Grants Policy to help with moving to alternative, more suitable, accommodation if the current home cannot be adapted.

Who is eligible for a DFG?

You can apply if you, or someone living in your property, is registered or registerable as disabled, and:

  • you, or the person on whose behalf you are applying, are either the owner or tenant (including licensees) of the property
  • you can certify that you, or the person on whose behalf you are applying, intend to occupy the property as your/their main residence throughout the grant period of five years
  • The property is within Elmbridge

The applicant must be over the age of 18 so will not necessarily be the disabled person - for example, a parent or guardian can apply for adaptations for the benefit of a disabled child. A landlord may also apply on behalf of a disabled tenant.

Mandatory Discretionary Disabled Facilities Grants

What types of works will a DFG pay for?

Adaptations and provision of facilities that meet one or more of the following criteria:

  • to make it easier to get into and out of the home - for example, by building a ramp to the front door
  • to make the home safe for the disabled person and other occupants
  • to provide access to, and within, the main family room, bedroom, kitchen, bathroom or garden
  • to provide suitable washing facilities - such as a level access shower
  • to improve or provide a heating system, or to adapt heating or lighting controls
  • to improve access and movement around the home for a disabled carer


The above list provides an example of the types of work which a mandatory DFG may pay for, but please ask the |Private Sector Housing Team if you would like to know whether any other work is likely to qualify.
The DFG will only pay for the adaptations assessed by the Council as 'necessary and appropriate' and 'reasonable and practicable'. If you decide to carry out further works, or want the adaptations provided in a different way, you will be responsible for the difference in costs between our recommendation and your proposed scheme.
For example, if the recommendation is for the conversion of a ground floor room into a bedroom (say, £10,000) but you decide that you want an extension instead (say, £22,000), we would contribute the equivalent value of converting the ground floor room, and you would pay the rest. In this example, you would pay £12,000.

Does the Council have to give a Mandatory DFG?

We have to apply three tests to determine whether a DFG can be given:
1. Is the adaptation 'necessary and appropriate' to meet the disabled person's needs? (determined by an occupational therapist - OT in the form of recommendations in a report)
2. Is the adaptation 'reasonable and practicable', taking into account the nature, layout, age and condition of the property? (determined by the Private Sector Housing Team)
3. Based on a Test of Resources, does the applicant qualify financially? (determined by the Private Sector Housing Team)
If we are satisfied that all three criteria have been met, then a grant can be awarded. We must make a decision on your grant application within 6 months of the receipt of a valid application.
Please note that we will not award a DFG for any works started or completed before you receive written grant approval.

What if the works cost more than £30,000, or aren't feasible to carry out?

If the works cost more than £30,000, you may be able to apply for additional funding from Surrey County Council. Elmbridge Council's Private Sector Housing Team can discuss this with you if this situation arises.
If the works aren't feasible, for example because they cannot be carried out in your property or would be too costly, you may be able to apply for a Discretionary Disabled Facilities Grant instead.

Do I have to repay the DFG?

Only if you own your home, the DFG is for more than £5,000 and you sell or transfer your home or it is no longer your main residence, within 10 years of the work being completed.
You will only have to repay any part of the grant in excess of £5,000, and the maximum repayment is £10,000. For example, if you had a grant for £7,500 you would repay £2,500.

How do I apply for a DFG?

There are two ways you can apply for a DFG:

  1. You can contact Surrey County Council by calling 0300 200 1005 to request an Occupational Therapy (OT) assessment, who, if they think an adaptation is needed, will write a report and refer you to the Private Sector Housing team for a Disabled Facilities Grant. A preliminary Test of Resources is carried out at an early stage to give you an indication whether you qualify financially for a grant and what level of contribution you may have to pay. |Care and Repair Elmbridge (CARE) our in-house Home Improvement Agency can help you to organise drawings and quotes by preparing a specification for the works, assist you with completing the grant application form and overseeing the work once the grant is approved.
  2. Or, you can apply directly to the Private Sector Housing Team. We recommend that you seek the views of an OT before you start your application, to ensure that the adaptation you are applying for is considered to be 'necessary and appropriate'. Your application must include details of the premises to be adapted, details of the work to be carried out (including planning permission and building regulations approval if necessary), at least two detailed and itemised estimates from contractors, proof of ownership and/or consent of all owners, and details of any preliminary or ancillary fees. We will then carry out a Test of Resources to determine the level of any contribution that you may be required to make towards the cost of the works. A standard application form is available upon request.

Discretionary Disabled Facilities Grants

These grants may be made available to help disabled people to move to a more suitable home.

The Council, subject to available resources, will pay up to £5,000 assistance for the fees and charges associated with moving home. The disabled person and any partner are means-tested to determine the amount of any contribution towards the cost of the works. If the disabled person is a child, the parents or legal guardian are not means-tested. There is no requirement to repay this grant if it is awarded to you.


What will it pay for?

The fees and charges associated with moving home, including:

  • Estate agent fees
  • Removal costs
  • Legal fees, including searches etc.


Am I eligible to apply?

If you are eligible for a Disabled Facilities Grant and have been assessed by an occupational therapist as being in need of adaptations to your home, but we have determined that your home is not suitable or too costly to adapt, you can apply for a Discretionary Disabled Facility Grant.
You can apply if you are an owner-occupier or a tenant, and you can be moving to a property you are purchasing or renting. The property you are moving from must be within Elmbridge, but the property you are moving to does not have to be.
The property that you are moving to has to be more suitable for the needs of the disabled person and supported by an OT assessment.

For further information

More information on DFGs is available in the Council's Guide to Disabled Facilities Grants, but please contact the |Private Sector Housing Team should you have any further queries or if you wish to apply for a grant.

|Care and Repair Elmbridge can assist customers through the whole grant application process for both types of DFG - from identifying the necessary repairs and improvements, to recommending contractors and overseeing the works. It charges a fee for this service, which can be included within any grant given.
