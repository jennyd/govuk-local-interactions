Access Travel Scheme 

 

1. Who can apply for the Access Travel Scheme?

You are eligible for the Access Travel Scheme if you are resident in Rutland and have a physical, sensory or mental impairment, which has a substantial and long-term adverse effect on your ability to use public transport.

You may also qualify if:

- You have been refused a driving licence under section 92 of the Road Traffic Act

1988 (except for persistent drug abuse or alcohol misuse)

- You are unable to walk unaided for a distance of 200 metres (this only applies to

impairments that have or are likely to last longer than 12 months)

 

2. What type of concession can I claim?

You may claim either a national Free Travel Pass or £44 of National Travel Tokens if you are:

- Blind or partially sighted

- Without hearing or speech

- Have a mental impairment or learning difficulty

- Have a physical impairment with a severe walking difficulty

- Have a physical impairment involving the use of both arms

First time applicants will be required to provide one of the following relevant documents. (Photocopies are accepted)

- In receipt of a War Pension Mobility Supplement

- Registered as having a sight impairment

- Registered as having a hearing impairment or without speech

- Registered as having a disability with the local authority

- In receipt of a Blue Badge parking permit

- Alternatively, medical certification from a Doctor, Occupational Therapist, Health

Visitor, District Nurse, Hospital Specialist / Consultant or Optician

(Please note: individual doctors may charge for this service. Unfortunately, the Council cannot reimburse these costs)

 

You may qualify for £44 of National Travel Tokens as an alternative to the travel pass if:

- You have been refused a driving licence under section 92 of the Road Traffic Act

1988 (except for persistent drug abuse or alcohol misuse)

- You are unable to walk unaided for a distance of 200 metres (this only applies to

impairments that have or are likely to last longer than 12 months)

First time applicants will be required to provide one of the following relevant documents.

- A DVLA letter (D206) withdrawing/refusing a driving licence on medical grounds

- Medical certification from a Doctor

 

(Please note: individual Doctors may charge for this service. Unfortunately, the Council cannot reimburse these costs)

 

3. How Do I Apply?

If you are applying for the first time you should send a completed Access Travel Scheme application form (pdf file) with the relevant documents relating to proof of age, residency and evidence of having a relevant impairment to the address detailed on the application form. Please allow at least 10 days for issue.

 -Travel Passes

If you are applying for a travel pass you must send a passport-sized photograph along with the application form.

Passes are issued for a 3-year period.

 - National Travel Tokens

If you are applying for travel tokens, these are issued from April 1st of each year.

Applications received during the year will be allocated a reduced amount of tokens pro rata to the number of days left in that issue year as follows:

 

April 1st to June 30th - Full amount £44

July 1st to September 31st - ¾ amount £33

October 1st to December 31st - ½ amount £22

January 1st to March 31st - ¼ amount £11

 

4. Is There A Charge?

All concessions are provided free of charge.

 

5. How Do I Renew My Concession?

Your pass will have an expiry date on it. Please send us your pass approximately one month before its due to expire, to allow use time to process your application.

If you claim tokens you will be sent a renewal form in February / March of each year. Please complete this form and return it to us by post as soon as possible. We will then send your tokens to you by registered post.

 

6. What If My Pass Is Lost Or Stolen?

If your pass is lost or stolen please contact Rutland County Council for advice on 01572 722577. Please note that lost or stolen tokens will not be replaced.

Application forms are also available from the following outlets:

 

• Rutland County Council Offices

 

• Libraries including the mobile library

 

• By post – please telephone (01572 722577)
