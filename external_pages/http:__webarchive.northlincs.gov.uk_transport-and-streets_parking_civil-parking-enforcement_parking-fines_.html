Parking fines

Payments of a parking fine (penalty charge notice) can be made by any of the following ways:

  • Online
  • Cash
  • Debit card
  • Cheque
  • Postal order

Online payments

  • Car parking fine

Cash, Debit cards, Cheques and Postal orders

Cheque/cash/postal order payments can be made in person at Civic Centre, Scunthorpe and The Angel, Brigg.

Cheques (made payable to North Lincolnshire Council) can also be posted to:

Cashiers
North Lincolnshire Council
Civic Centre
Ashby Road
Scunthorpe
North Lincolnshire
DN16 1AB

Debit card payments can be made in person at Civic Centre, Scunthorpe and The Angel, Brigg or by telephone - 01724 297000.

Opening hours:

  • Monday to Thursday 9am to 4.30pm
  • Friday 9am to 4pm

Post Offices and PayPoint

The barcode on the penalty charge notice can be used when making payments in the following way:

  1. Cash, cheque or debit card at any Post Office
  2. Cash at a shop that has PayPoint

Simply hand over your penalty charge notice and payment.  Your payment will then be processed, the penalty charge notice returned to you together with a printed receipt.  Make sure you get a receipt for every payment.

You can pay at any Post Office or Pay Point outlet in the country.  A list of the local outlets is shown below:

  • Barton
  • Brigg
  • Isle of Axholme
  • Scunthorpe

A penalty charge must be paid no later than the last day of the period of 28 days beginning with the date on which it was served.

If paid within 14 days, beginning with the date on which the notice was served, a discount of 50 per cent will apply.

Please note: Civil enforcement officers cannot accept payments.

Objections

All appeals or objections must be made in writing by either:

  • Online objection form.
  • A letter addressed to Parking Services, Church Square House, PO Box 42, Scunthorpe, North Lincolnshire, DN15 6XQ.
  • Email: parking.services@northlincs.gov.uk.

Objections must include the penalty charge notice number, car registration number and the driver's full name and address.

Enforcement

Off-street parking is enforced by the North Lincolnshire Borough Council (Off-Street Parking Places) (Civil Enforcement and Consolidation) Order 2010.

On-street parking is enforced by the North Lincolnshire Borough Council (Various Streets) (Prohibition and Restriction of Waiting and Street Parking Places) (Civil Enforcement and Consolidation) Order 2010.
