How to view and comment on a planning application

How do I look at a planning application?

*** Changes to our Software System***

Please be aware that on the 28 March Pendle Borough Council will be transferring to a new software system. This will affect how you can view and comment on applications for approximately two weeks during that month. We will give further details at the beginning of March.

***

You have three choices

1. Text Based Searching 

For applications from 1991 - the present day, and ongoing, you can use our MySpacePlanning system. You can view planning applications, any associated documents, and where applicable, you can comment on those applications.

You can download a document showing you how to get the best out of the MySpacePlanning System.

2. Map Based Searching 

Applications from 2000 onwards can also be viewed using our web based mapping system. You can view planning applications plotted on maps (application sites outlined in red with reference numbers). There are also links through to the MySpacePlanning system, so that you can view any associated documents, and where applicable, you can comment on applications.

3. Historic Maps

For planning applications from 1947-2000, we have scanned images of old maps which you can download. These maps show application sites outlined in red, with the relevant reference numbers:

  • 1 to 2500 map of Pendle
  • 1 to 1250 map of Pendle
  • 1947 Map: Barley With Wheatley Booth, Blacko, Foulridge, Goldshaw Booth and Roughlee Booth
  • 1947 Map: Barrowford
  • 1947 Map: Brierfield
  • 1947 Map: Colne
  • 1947 Map: Nelson
  • 1947 Map: Trawden
  • 1947 Map: West Craven
  • 1947 Map: Wheatley Lane, Old Laund Booth, Higham and Reedley
  • Pre-1990 Town Centre Maps

Planning Weekly List

If you want to see a round up of all the planning applications that are submitted on a weekly basis, you can view our Planning Weekly List.

Important Information:

  • This search function should not be substituted for a formal Land Charges search
  • Although we make every effort to keep the archive up to date it does not constitute the Part 1 Planning Register

Scaling of Plans: If printing plans from these pages please note that they will not necessarily be to the exact scale specified on the plans. However, we are encouraging our applicants and agents to supply plans in PDF format. Providing such plans have been created in a recent version of Acrobat with the measuring function enabled then anyone with Adobe Reader Version 9.4 onwards will be able to scale plans using the measuring tool.

Monitoring: The Council monitors the effectiveness of its development management service by sending out questionnaires to some of those that have made representations on planning applications. This is to help improve service delivery.  The Council also holds a forum for planning agents on a twice yearly basis.

Copyright Warning: The documents have been submitted to the Council in order to apply for planning permission. They are protected by Section 47 of the Copyright Designs and Patents Act 1988. They are being made available to be viewed for consultation purposes only - copies must not be made without the prior permission of the copyright owner (i.e. the applicant or their agent).

Ordnance Survey Mapping: Unauthorised reproduction infringes Crown Copyright and may lead to   prosecution or civil proceedings.
