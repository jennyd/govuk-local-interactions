Kirkstall Household Waste Recycling Site closure

Kirkstall Household Waste Recycling Site will close for refurbishment from Monday 4 January 2016 for approximately 12 months. The facilities for trade and business non-chemical waste will remain open. Please visit the Seacroft (East Leeds) Recycling site to dispose of chemical trade and business waste.

Your nearest recycling sites to use during the closure will be Meanwood and Pudsey. To check for the site re-opening date, please periodically check this page.


The final design of the site includes:
Public recycling area
This will be a modern, purpose-built recycling area for residents of Leeds to recycle a wide range of materials and dispose of household waste, and will be similar in design to other sites in Leeds (like Meanwood Road and Pudsey recycling sites). Access will be from Evanston Avenue and a one-way system around the recycling area will be created within the site allowing plenty of room for cars, and preventing queuing on surrounding roads during busy periods. A totally separate operations area will be developed, where the site staff can change skips while the recycling site is being used by the public.

Re-use shop
Instead of throwing re-usable items into the skips at the recycling area, they can be given to the new, on-site re-use shop where donations can be sold and enjoyed by someone else – while raising funds for the organisation concerned. The shop – which will be accessible on foot from Evanston Avenue – is expected to be run by a ‘not for profit’ local community organisation. Profits raised will be used to support their work. The shop will also provide training, volunteering and apprenticeship opportunities for the local community, and will be a great place to bag a bargain.

Council and commercial waste area
This area is located in the far south-west corner of the site and will continue to be used by the Council’s waste management service for functions including bin storage, vehicle parking, and short-term storage of recyclable materials and bulky items prior to onward transfer. Leeds-based businesses and traders will be able to recycle and dispose of their waste materials in this area for an appropriate fee.
