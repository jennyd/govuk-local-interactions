If you live, work or study in the City of London there are a number of organisations that provide information, advice and support to help deal with many of the issues we face in everyday life.

  1. Age UK
    We offer a wide range of services including health and wellbeing, travel and lfestyle, home and care. We are here to help everyone make the most of later life
  2. Bettercaring
    Bettercaring offers a searchable database of all residential and nursing homes in the UK. You can search by location or type of home you need
  3. Care Home Selection
    Specialist service to help you find a care home. Our advice is free and confidential
  4. Care Quality Commission
    Information on home care providers and how to find a care home
  5. City 50+
    Toynbee Hall offers support services to anyone aged 50+ living in the City of London or Tower Hamlets. The Wellbeing Centre at Toynbee Hall is a friendly place to meet and access our services
  6. City Advice
    We offer impartial and confidential advice on consumer and employment rights, welfare benefits, debt, housing, legal issues, relationships and health and wellbeing
  7. City Carers Service
    We provide support, information and advice to adult carers of older people and adults with physical and learning disabilities, mental health difficulties or with other long-term conditions
  8. City Health
    Find your nearest pharmacy, GP, optician, dentist, walk-in clinic, gym or leisure centre or any other type of healthcare service
  9. GOV.UK
    The government is working to provide a social care system that provides care for those who need it, and which enables people to retain their independence and dignity
  10. Healthwatch
    Healthwatch is unique in that our sole purpose is to understand the needs, experiences and concerns of people who use health and social care services and to speak out on their behalf
