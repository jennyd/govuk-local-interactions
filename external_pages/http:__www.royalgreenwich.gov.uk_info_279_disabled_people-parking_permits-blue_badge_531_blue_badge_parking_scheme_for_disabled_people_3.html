Blue Badge parking scheme for disabled people

Where you can park

View pages in this section

There are parking spaces available to Blue Badge holders but there are also some restrictions.

If you are disabled, you can park for free in Royal Greenwich - as long as you correctly display your Blue Badge - at a:

  • Pay and display space
  • Disabled parking bay
  • Meter parking space
  • Car parks (in Royal Borough of Greenwich-managed car parks only. See our car parks directory for details of our public car parks)
  • Limited stay bays

Blue Badge holders can park in these places for an unlimited time, unless the signs specify otherwise.

If you park in a car park that is not managed by the Royal Borough of Greenwich, or a private car park, please check the regulations before parking.

Blue Badge booklet

For detailed information about where Blue Badge holders can park, please refer to Chapter 2 of the Blue Badge rights and responsibilities booklet (Department for Transport website).

Parking can be found throughout the Royal Borough of Greenwich, especially in the town centres of Eltham, Greenwich and Woolwich.

Yellow lines

If you are a Blue Badge holder, you will also be able to park on a yellow line for up to three hours, provided:

  • You do not cause an obstruction
  • There is not a loading ban

You will need to clearly display your badge, with the clock showing the arrival time.  

Parking restrictions and exceptions

Designated disabled bays may also be used by non-Blue Badge holders for loading and unloading.

Blue Badge holders are not allowed to park in bays reserved for specific users (examples of reserved bays include permit-holders only bays, resident permits only bays, loading bays, taxi bays, doctors' bays).

Generally, parking restrictions are shown by:

  • Single or double yellow lines on the kerb 
  • Red lines on the kerb

Every local authority has its own parking regulations and restrictions. Please check each authority's website for details.

Loading

Other vehicles may park in a disabled bay for the purposes of loading or unloading goods for a maximum period of 20 minutes.

Loading and unloading must:

  • Involve heavy or multiple goods 
  • Be to an adjacent property 
  • Be a continuous activity that is seen to be taking place by a civil enforcement officer

Congestion Charge

Blue Badge holders qualify for a 100 per cent exemption from London's Congestion Charge. This means that you do not have to pay the Congestion Charge, but you must register with Transport for London at least 10 days before your journey.

You can download a registration form on the Transport for London website.

Using the Blue Badge abroad

In non-EU countries, you should take the badge with you and ask whether you are entitled to use it.

The Institute of Advanced Motorists, an independent road safety charity, publishes a booklet with detailed information on using the Blue Badge in EU countries.

You can download the booklet from the Institute of Advanced Motorists website
