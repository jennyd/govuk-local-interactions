Libraries

 

Tell us what you think
If you are interested in our public engagement, Listening, Learning, Changing - the future of hubs and libraries, please visit www.somerset.gov.uk/hubsandlibraries - which runs from 17 December 2015 to 11 February 2016.


To join the library you just need to show current proof of your name and address. Library membership entitles you to borrow library items such as books, CDs and DVDs. Your membership will also allow you to request items, renew items and to access the Internet free of charge using the People's Network computers in your library.

If you have no proof of address, staff may be able to provide you with a limited, introductory membership. This will enable you to borrow one item at a time (excludes DVDs and computer games). Please go to the LibrariesWest website for renewals and borrower information.

If you wish to join the library you can either visit your local library or join online via the LibrariesWest website.

Joining in a library
When joining the library you will be asked to complete a membership joining form and show proof of your identity and current address.

Examples include:

  • Driving licence

  • Family allowance book

  • Medical card

  • Pension book

  • Current utility bill less than 3 months old

  • Bank statement

  • Building society book

If you are unable to provide proof of your address, staff may be able to offer you an 'introductory membership'. This will enable you to borrow one item at a time (excludes DVDs and computer games).

You will then be given a membership card and a PIN.

You will need your membership card every time you wish to borrow items, to renew or request items via our website or to use the People's Network computers.

You can use your card at any library or mobile library in Somerset, Bath and North East Somerset, South Gloucestershire, North Somerset and Bristol.

If you are under 16, a parent or guardian will have to come to the library with you, to sign for your membership card.

Joining Online
You can join online via the LibrariesWest website. When joining online you will be given a temporary membership number and PIN. You can immediately reserve items on our catalogue. To borrow items you will need to collect your membership card from the library.

To join children
To arrange for your child (under 16) to join the library to borrow items or to use the computers, please either visit the library or join online. In both instances you will be asked to complete a membership joining form. Then show proof of your identity and current address on behalf of your child. You will be asked to sign the membership card as 'guarantor' for your child's loans until he/she reaches the age of 16. As well as the membership card you will be given a PIN for your child's membership.

PIN
You will be issued with a PIN, your personal identification number, that has been automatically generated for your library card number. You need your PIN if you wish to renew books online or by telephone, if you wish to request items online or if you want to use the People's Network computers in libraries. If you do not know your PIN just ask your local library, they will ask you a few questions to confirm your identity. They will then inform you of your PIN. You can change your PIN at any time either at your library or online via the LibrariesWest website.

Change of Address
If you change your address please visit your local library and inform them, they will amend your record. You will not need proof of your new address.

Lost Library Cards
If you lose your library card please notify your library immediately, so we can prevent unauthorised use of your card. Failure to do so will result in you being responsible for all loans on your cards. You will be charged a small fee for a replacement card – see Library charges and fines. When going to the library to obtain a replacement card, please remember to take identification, such as:

  • Driving licence

  • Family Allowance Book

  • Pension Book

  • Current utility bill

  • Bank / Building Society book

Who can use it?
Library membership is open to anyone who would like to join.

How do I use it?
You will need your card whenever you wish to borrow any item or use a People's Network computer. You can borrow up to 20 items at any one time - with a limit of 8 DVDs and 2 Computer Games, from any library.

Your card and PIN will also enable you to:

  • Borrow from any library within the 5 partner library services of LibrariesWest. These are Somerset; Bath and North East Somerset; South Gloucestershire; North Somerset and Bristol.

  • Renew items online on the LibrariesWest website.

  • Check your loans and the progress of your requests, also by using the LibrariesWest website

  • Access the Internet - some free time is available see Book library facilities

  • Access a range of free e-resources

 

The loan period depends on the type of item you borrow:

  • Books - 21 days

  • Talking books - 21 days

  • Music CDs - 14 days

  • Videos - 7 days

  • DVDs - 7 days (boxed sets - 14 days) 

  • Computer games - 7 days

If you borrow an item from a non-Somerset library (for example, from a library in Bath and North East Somerset, North Somerset, South Gloucestershire, or Bristol), the local loan period for the lending library will apply. If in doubt, check with the library staff at the time of your visit. If you anticipate (at the time you borrow any items) being away when your loans are due back, it may be possible to arrange for a longer loan period. You can renew most items up to 20 times if no-one else has requested them. Books borrowed from mobile libraries are issued for 6 weeks, but other materials (DVDs, music CDs and talking books) are issued for 3 weeks, that is, one visit and for the standard loan charge. For example, the standard charge for a one-week loan of a DVD in a library will also apply to a 3-week loan of a DVD from a mobile library.

What happens next?
If, at the time you borrow any item, you anticipate that you will be away when your loans are due back, it may be possible to arrange for a longer loan period - please ask staff. If the loan you wish to extend is normally charged for, there will be an additional charge. Oh, and enjoy your holiday.

You can return your books and other items to any library within the LibrariesWest Consortium, that is, Somerset, Bath and North East Somerset, South Gloucestershire and Bristol.

Returning books and other items when the library is closed is not recommended. It is best to return loaned items during opening hours as that allows staff to confirm that items have been recorded as returned and the borrower record cleared and because of the potential damage to items returned through library letter boxes.

Faulty/damaged items
If the book or item you have borrowed is damaged and this wasn't noticed at the time you borrowed it, then please make a note of the damage, for example, the number of a faulty track on a CD, as soon as you notice it. When returning the item to the library, please hand over the note recording the damage. In the case of items for which there is a loan charge (CDs, computer games, talking books and DVDs) staff will assess the damage and, in appropriate cases, will offer a free loan of another item.

How much does it cost?
There is no charge for joining the library.

Single Membership
A member registered at any public library in Somerset, Bath and North East Somerset, Bristol, North Somerset and South Gloucestershire is now able to use their membership card at any other public library within these authorities. The main reason for introducing single membership is to provide a more flexible and seamless service to all our existing customers, and to make membership more attractive to potential customers.

What will single membership mean for me?
All those with a library membership of one of the LibrariesWest services will be able to borrow, return or renew items at any LibrariesWest library, place reservations for collection at any library, and use a People's Network terminal at any library.

Will I need a new membership card?
No, all existing cards will still be useable.

How many items will I be able to have out on loan at any one time?
You will be able to borrow up to a maximum of 20 items at any one time - with a limit of 8 DVDs and 2 Computer Games.

How do I access the People's Network in another library service within the Librarieswest consortium?
You can use your membership card to book and log on in the usual way. You will be asked to read and accept the Acceptable Use Policy each time you log on to a computer. The terms and conditions of the authority you are in at the time will apply.

Can I book a People's Network computer in another consortium authority?
Yes but only if you are using a library in that authority at the time, for example, if you are visiting a North Somerset library, such as Weston-super-Mare, you can book a computer in any North Somerset library. However, if you are in a Somerset library and want to book a computer in a North Somerset library this will not be possible at the moment. However, you will be able to do this through LibrariesWest later this year.

What happens if I am already a member of one LibrariesWest service and try to join another?
Staff will explain that your current library membership card is useable in any LibrariesWest library. You can update your membership record (for example, to notify the service of a change of address) in any LibrariesWest library, regardless of where you originally joined.

What happens if I already have a card for more than one library service, or wish to be a member of more than one?
Generally you will only need one membership card. However, if you are already a member of more than one LibrariesWest library service you will be able to continue to use both membership cards.

Which authority's fees and charges will apply, and when?
Hire charges - will be applied according to the library/authority the items are loaned at, for example, a DVD borrowed in Bristol by a library member who registered in Somerset will be charged at the Bristol rate.

Overdue charges - will be applied according to the library/authority the item was issued at, for example, an item borrowed from South Gloucestershire and returned late to a library in Bath and North East Somerset will be charged at the South Gloucestershire rate.

Reservation charges (placed on the web) - will relate to the library chosen as the pickup location. If the pickup is subsequently changed by the customer there will be no charge.

Reservation charges - if a member of staff places the reservation on your behalf, you will be charged according to the library you are in at the time. 

Will I be able to specify any library as a pick up location for reservations?
Yes, you can collect your reservation from any public library within the LibrariesWest consortium.

How will any membership concessions be applied?
Concessions will apply to the authority customers have registered with, for example, if you are registered with South Gloucestershire and use a South Gloucestershire library you will be entitled to South Gloucestershire concessionary rates, but those rates won't apply if you use your card in another authority.
