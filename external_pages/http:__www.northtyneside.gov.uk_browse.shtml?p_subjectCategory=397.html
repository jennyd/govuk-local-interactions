Sheltered housing

Sheltered housing schemes are unfurnished, rented homes, which are designed for older people.

There is a choice of sheltered housing in North Tyneside. We have 29 sheltered schemes and 7 group dwellings across the borough. The properties are ideal for older people who wish to live independently, in comfort and security with the opportunity to socialise and get involved with leisure activities when they choose

Click here for a map of our schemes and their contact details

Who is eligible for sheltered housing?

  • Anyone who is over 60 years of age
  • There is no upper age limit
  • People living alone or couples
  • For couples, at least one person must meet the age requirement.

Before we offer you a sheltered property we will check it meets your housing needs.

What does it provide?

We offer three types of sheltered housing:

  • There are Sheltered schemes, which are purpose built courts containing a number of flats
  • Bungalows, which are located next to sheltered schemes
  • Group Dwellings, which are a number of bungalows that can be found in various locations across the borough.

The flats and bungalows are self-contained with one or two bedrooms, a lounge and bathroom facilities.

Sheltered schemes have communal facilities, these may include:

  • A laundry
  • A kitchen
  • A lounge
  • Gardens
  • A guest room

Some group dwellings have a recreation room, generally located in the centre of the bungalows, which can be used for a variety of social activities.

Facilities within the recreation room may include:

  • A lounge area
  • A kitchen
  • Toilets

A Sheltered Housing Officer is available 37 hours per week, Monday to Friday to ensure the smooth running of the building and carry out Housing Management tasks associated with Sheltered Housing. In addition Sheltered Housing Officers will provide information and assistance to tenants.

The Sheltered Housing Officers are split into small local teams so that tenants get to know the Officers in their area and details of the team are clearly displayed for tenants, including photographs and contact details.

The Sheltered Housing Service aims to provide each scheme and Group Dwelling with an on site presence by a Sheltered Housing Officer for half a day, per day Monday to Friday. Whilst not on site, a Sheltered Housing Officer is contactable by telephone or via the Management Team.

In the event of an emergency, tenants can contact Care Call via a Pull Cord system. Care Call is a 24 Emergency Response team and are contactable 24 hours per day, 365 days per year.

North Tyneside Homes is the landlord of all sheltered accommodation owned by North Tyneside Council.

Quality Homes for Older People

The Council is currently in procurement to select a partner to deliver the Housing PFI Quality Homes for Older People project. The project aims to transform sheltered accommodation within the borough, with 10 new build schemes and high quality refurbishment of 16 of our existing schemes.

You can find out more about this exciting project in the section on the Quality Homes for Older People project.
