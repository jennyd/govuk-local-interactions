Finding post-16 opportunities

Home » My learning » Finding post-16 opportunities Share page:

Welcome to our hub of training and learning opportunities after Year 11

This section links to websites and directories of information about learning and training options for young people in secondary education, for after year 11 and opportunities for disabled young adults.

Search - UCAS Progress

A national website which includes profiles of all the sixth form colleges, sixth forms, further education colleges and training providers in Suffolk, plus details of their courses and programmes.

Who is it for?

  • young people, parents and carers, professionals who work with any young person who is looking into their post-16 learning options

Download - Information on your post 16 options

For an overview of all your options after leaving year 11, and information about the Raising of the Participation Age.

Download - Post-16 Options for SEND learners information guide

For information, advice and guidance around post-16 options for young people with special educational needs or disabilities (SEND).

Download - Post-16 SEND Learning Providers Guide

For a list of the learning providers offering alternative provision for young people with SEND.

Go to - The 16+ Directories: Informal learning & leisure opportunities for young people with SEND

There are three directories which pull together information about opportunities available for a disabled young person when they have finished at further education college, sixth form college or sixth form. They include details about social enterprises, personal development opportunities, care farms, leisure pursuits, volunteering and youth and social groups across Suffolk. They are updated twice a year.

Who are they for?

  • They can be used byparents and carers, professionals and young people
