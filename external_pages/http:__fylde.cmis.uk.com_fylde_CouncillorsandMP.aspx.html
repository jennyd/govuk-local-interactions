Councillors and MP

Councillors have three main components to their work.

a.Decision making - Through meetings and attending committees with other elected members, councillors decide which activities to support, where money should be spent, what services should be delivered and what policies should be implemented all of which have a direct impact on the local community.

b.Monitoring - Councillors make sure that their decisions lead to efficient and effective services by keeping an eye on how well things are working.

c.Getting involved locally - As local representatives, councillors have responsibilities towards their constituents and spend time at meetings of local organisations such as tenant's association, taking up issues on behalf of members of the public, running a surgery (question and answer session) for residents to bring up issues and meeting with individual residents in their own homes.

A composite directory of all the Councillors, with their contact details and a map of the borough showing all the wards can be found by following this link  http://fylde.cmis.uk.com/fylde/DocumentsandInformation.aspx

MEMBER OF PARLIAMENT (MP)

Mark Menzies was elected as member of parliament for Fylde at the general election on May 6 2010 and re-elected on 7 May 2015. You can find out more information about Mark, including how to contact him, by visiting his own website at www.markmenzies.org.uk  (external link).

Please ring 01253 739848 to make an appointment at his local constituency office.

Correspondence address: House of Commons, London SW1A 0AA.
