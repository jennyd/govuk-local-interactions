Housing Repairs and Maintenance

Rechargeable Repairs

Your registered provider does not bear the cost of all repairs to your property.  Where tenant's or visitors to their property, have caused damage (e.g. broken window) or been negligent (e.g. locked out of property) they will charge for the cost of works to rectify the problem.  Tenants should be advised that a repair is rechargeable when reporting.
The Contractor will ask you to sign a copy of a form which outlines the works carried out and the cost. An invoice will subsequently be raised for which you will be liable. It may be possible to pay for recharged repairs by instalments.
You might be able to claim back the cost of rechargeable repair done through your personal Contents Insurance. It is up to you to check your policy details to see if you are covered for accidental damage.  For further information on rechargeable repairs or to request a repair contact your registered provider.

Communal Areas

There are occasions when tenants identify repairs that are necessary that are not to their own home.  This could include things like the communal lighting in a block of flats, or the communal drainpipes to a block of garages.
Registered providers would always urge people to report these requests for repairs as soon as possible and they will be happy to undertake the work.
If tenants are aware that damage is being deliberately caused by an individual or groups of individuals they would welcome information that could lead to those people being charged with the costs rather than the costs falling to the rent payers.
To request a repair to a communal area contact your registered provider.

Garden Maintenance

Many registered providers are able to offer garden maintenance for those elderly tenants who are not capable of doing the work themselves. It is not possible to offer this service to all elderly tenants so to qualify tenants must meet the following criteria:-
  • Over 60 years of age and/or disabled
  • Physically unable to do the work themselves.
  • No other person living with you or within reasonable distance able to help maintain your garden.
In many instances the garden maintenance covers the cutting of grass and any hedges but does not include the maintenance of bushes, shrub beds and trees.
The garden maintenance is carried out by a contractor appointed by the housing association who will have appropriate identification. It is important that you ask to see this before allowing any workmen into your property. If you feel that you meet the criteria for garden maintenance, then please contact your registered provider for further information.
