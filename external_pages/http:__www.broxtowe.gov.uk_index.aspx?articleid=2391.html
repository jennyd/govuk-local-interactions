Supported Housing

Supported housing projects are designed to help individuals bridge the gap between homelessness and independent living. Individuals are often offered the opportunity to develop life skills such as cooking, literacy or IT through training provided by experienced members of staff.

This enables the transition to independent housing to take place smoothly and ensures the individual has the skills needed to sustain a tenancy. There are a variety of supported housing projects in Broxtowe and the surrounding areas.

Accommodation for Young People

  • Stepping Stones - Leicester Housing Association

           41 - 43 Church Street
           Eastwood
           Nottingham  NG16 3HP
           Tel: 01773 719 625

Provides accommodation for young, single homeless (or potentially homeless) people aged 16 - 25. It offers residents support, advice and training provided by Castle College. One of the rooms is potentially suitable for a disabled person.

  • The Canaan Trust

          Jordan House
          14 Main Street
          Long Eaton
          Nottingham
          NG10 1GR
          Tel: 0115 9464903

Provides supported accommodation for up to 12 single males.

  • Central Women's Aid

           PO Box 20
           Nottingham
           NG3 5BA
           Tel: 0115 960 7943

Provides refuge for women aged 16+ (and their children) who are fleeing violence.

  • Elizabeth House

          780 Mansfield Road
          Daybook
          Nottingham
          NG5 3GG 

Elizabeth House is a 21 bed, quick access supported accommodation for single homeless people aged from 16. The accommodation provides self-contained, secure facilities, 24 hour cover, individual support and access to move-on and permanent accommodation. The service is intended to be a short-term service with an average length of stay of about 3 months.

  • Villa Street
          1 Villa Street
          Beeston
          Nottingham
          NG9 2NY 

It is expected that residents will be in the move-on accommodation for an average of 9 - 12 months with a maximum stay of 2 years. Again facilities are self-contained and secure. Support is provided via floating support. Referrals can be made directly to the move-on accommodation where vacancies allow and where applicants have lower support needs or higher independent living skills.

  • Derby Road
          Tun Tun
          100 Derby Road
          Long Eaton
          Nottingham
          NG10 4LS
  • Framework Housing Association Community Houses Team
          2nd Floor
          Maville House - Hayley Harris
          Beech Avenue
          Nottingham
          NG7 7LS 

The DIP service provides supported housing for single homeless people who have current or recent involvement with the Criminal Justice System and a drug issue. People using this service need to follow a drug managed programme - be on a heroin replacement script and able to provide a number of negative drug test results in a set time period.
