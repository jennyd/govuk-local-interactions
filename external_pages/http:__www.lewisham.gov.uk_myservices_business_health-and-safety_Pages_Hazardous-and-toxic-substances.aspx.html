    Hazardous and toxic substances

    Page Content 1

    This section gives information to businesses and residents concerned about hazardous or toxic substances, including asbestos.

    Hazardous substances in the workplace

    Employers are required by law to control substances that are hazardous to health. There is more information on how to prevent or reduce workers' exposure to hazardous substances on the COSHH website.

    Asbestos

    If your property is more than 15 years old, it's possible that it contains materials made from asbestos.

    Asbestos in homes is commonly found in cement:

    • on garage or shed roofs
    • in shed and garage wall panels
    • in drain pipes
    • in insulation board soffit, infill panels and partitions
    • on the side of bath panels.

    Asbestos removal from homes

    The City of London operates a household hazardous waste collection service on our behalf.

    If you have a large amount of asbestos, and the City of London are unable to help you, then you should contact an asbestos removal contractor. Find asbestos removal contractors

    Information for businesses

    Our Health and Safety Team is responsible for checking offices, shops, warehouses, leisure centres and other non-industrial premises. Get information and advice about managing and working with asbestos on the Heath and Safety Executive website.

    If you intend to carry out any works relating to asbestos then you must notify the local authority that these works are due to take place. The ASB5 form can be used for this.

    Flytipping of asbestos

    If you see flytipped asbestos, or you see someone flytipping asbestos, please report this to our Environmental Health Enforcement Team.
