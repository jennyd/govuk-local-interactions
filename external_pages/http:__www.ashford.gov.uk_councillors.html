  3. Biography

    Cllr. Bell has represented Biddenden on Ashford Borough Council since 1995.

    After reading maths at Bristol University, he spent several successful years with IBM in the UK and the US.  He then started the small business in IT which he runs today.

    Cllr. Bell served on the Council’s Executive, successfully delivering the £200m Stanhope Housing PFI and the Council’s Blue Box and Green Waste recycling services.

    He strongly supports keeping the Council Tax down and recycling rates up.

    He is happily married with three children.

    Cllr. Bell enjoys taking on the establishment on behalf of the individual.

  3. Biography

    Councillor Burgess has represented the Isle of Oxney area on the Ashford Borough Council since 1991.

    After leaving the Royal Sussex Regiment he joined the road haulage industry eventually obtaining his Certificate of Management Competence and became a Transport Manager.   In the mid 1970s Cllr. Burgess joined a fuel distribution company and he has stayed within the oil industry retail market ever since.

    Councillor Burgess has a strong interest in Housing and Planning, which he has used to help maintain Local Needs Housing in the villages, in order to preserve village life.

    He strongly supports Parish Councils and assists them whenever requested, attending their meetings when possible.  Having lived in villages all his life, his knowledge is often helpful.

    Councillor Burgess is the Lead Member for the Romney Marsh.

  3. Biography

    Born Gerald Dawson Clarkson; educated at Westminster Technical College, followed by an electrical engineering apprenticeship. Joined the Royal Engineers (National Service), after which he enrolled into the London Fire Brigade as a professional Fire-fighter.  During his career, spanning 30 years, he was promoted through the ranks to become the Chief Fire Officer of the London Fire Brigade and then appointed Chief Executive Officer of the London Fire & Civil Defence Authority.

    He was adviser to the Association of Metropolitan Authorities 1987 to 1991 and was a Member of the Central Fire Brigades Advisory Council 1983 – 1991.  Director of the National Fire Protection Association of America 1986 - 1993.  President of the Commonwealth & Overseas Fire Service Association 1990 - 2000. He has written and presented professional papers in America, Hong Kong, Singapore, China, Switzerland, Australia, New Zealand and Japan.

    Awarded:       BA (Hons) in Economics & Industrial Relations - Polytechnic of Central London.

                            Fire Brigade Long Service Medal - 1981

                            Order of St John (Officer Brother) - 1988;

                            Queen’s Fire Service Medal for Distinguished Service - 1983 Birthday Honours:

                            Commander of the British Empire (CBE) - 1990 Birthdays Honours.

     

    Honoured:     Freeman of the City of London 1983.

                            Freedom of the City of Glasgow 2004.

     

    Prof. Bodies: Fellow of Royal Society of Health (FRSH) since 1969;

                            Fellow of Chartered Management Institute (FCMI) since 1978;

                            Fellow Institute of Management Specialists (FIMS) since 1997;

                            Hon Fellow The Institution of Fire Engineers (Hon.FIFireE) since 1989.

     

    Founder of:    The Worshipful Company of Fire-fighters in 1988.

                            The Fire-fighters Memorial Trust in 1990.

     

    Following retirement from the Fire Service formed a successful International Fire Engineering & Fire Safety Consultancy. Served on the Kent Police Authority as an Independent Member from 1995 to 2003.

    Cllr Clarkson was elected to Ashford Borough Council in 2006 and became Cabinet Member in 2007, the Deputy Leader in 2011 and in April 2013 he was elected as Leader of Ashford Borough Council. He has lived in the Charing area since 1991.  Happily married to Rose, they have a son Nicholas and a daughter Penelope, and eight grandchildren.

  3. Biography

    Cllr Paul Clokie OBE was elected to Ashford Borough Council in 1997 and served as Leader from 1999 to 2010. Originally from London, he moved to Tenterden in 1995 after a successful career in IT.

    Previously he was a Member of Leeds City Council and a long-standing Member of the Royal Borough of Kingston upon Thames both as a Member and Leader. He is the Chairman of the Audit Committee and the TENT1 Task Group.

    Paul's many hobbies and interests include music and gardening. He is widowed with two children, two step-children and three granddaughtesr.

    His priorities as a Councillor include ensuring all residents get the best service possible within the current limits on resources and that the growth of Ashford is not a tax on existing residents but brings benefits to both current and future residents.

    Paul was awarded an OBE in the Queen's New Year's Honours List in 2007 for services to Local Government in Kent.

  3. Biography

    Cllr. Aline Hicks was elected to the Borough Council in May 2007.  Born in Brighton, Aline lived in Tonbridge and Dover before moving to Stubbs Cross near Shadoxhurst in 1983.

    Aline has two children and three delightful grandchildren.  She is interested in youth development and the CARM (Caring All Together on Romney Marsh) project, and is also a governor at the local primary school.  Aline has a wide variety of interests and used to show English Setters but is now involved with their rescue charity.

    Cllr. Hicks is concerned that we protect and enhance our rural areas, that there should be sensitive development of the new urban areas and especially that we should promote family values.

  3. Biography

    Born in the village of Great Chart in 1953 Councillor Shorter attended Ashford South Secondary Modern School, in Jemmet Road.

    After starting as an instrument apprentice in 1969 at Dungeness A Power Station, he went on to work as an engineer within the nuclear power industry at Dungeness B Power Station, managing a team of professional engineers in the Reactor Protection and Electrical Systems Group. In addition to managing the Group he also managed significant engineering projects and contracts together with wider team management roles within the organisation.

    Councillor Shorter has been married to Jenny since 1975 and lived in Woodchurch and Greatstone before finally moving to Kingsnorth in 1997. He has two grown up children, and six granddaughters (!), one of which lives with them on their small holding in Magpie Hall Road, where they rear pedigree Sussex and Dexter cattle as well as other farm stock.

    Although Councillor Shorter retired 8 years ago he has retained an active interest in the community with membership of a number of local groups and organisations. He is a member of Kingsnorth Parish Council (Kingsnorth village ward), a Kent Play Clubs director (Kingsnorth), and member of Ashford Local Commissioning Group patient participation group, among others.

    Councillor Shorter’s  main objective as a Borough Councillor is to improve the well being of the residents in the Borough.

  3. Biography

    Councillor Wedgbury has been a fireman since 1984 and during this time has attended a huge variety of incidents, ranging from rescuing an iguana from a roof, being the first responder to an IRA bomb, rescuing a child stuck in railings to serious fires and road accidents.  He is the Fire Brigades Union Branch Secretary for his Fire Station.

    He is married with two children and enjoys participating in many sports including rugby and football and has recently taken up and gained his orange belt in Washinkai Karate.

    Councillor Wedgbury represents Park Farm South Ward on both the Borough and Parish Councils. He views his role as setting policies to provide services and facilities for the community that would otherwise be unavailable to many if they were not provided out of general taxation whilst at the same time providing value for money without excessive tax.

    He is proud to be the Vice-Chairman of the Planning Committee and the Chairman of the Nature Conservation Forum and believes that his skills and experiences as a fireman assist him to make decisions that are in the best interests of the community.

    He is a life member of English Heritage and a member of the National Trust.

    Councillor Wedgbury welcomes and actively invites residents views, ideas and suggestions relating to Park Farm. One such suggestion received to date was a safe cycle route to the town centre.
