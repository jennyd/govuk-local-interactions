What is an assessment?

What does an assessment involve?

An assessment is just a chat with you to help us understand what your daily life is like. We want to know what support you might require to make your life easier, so you can remain living independently in your own home if at all possible.

Where will the assessment happen?

Generally, an assessment takes place in your home or over the phone. If you want, you can have a family member, carer or friend with you when this happens. For those in hospital, we will visit you prior to your discharge to assess whether you will need any help at home while you recover.

Assessments are a two-way process. For instance, we’ll ask you to talk about yourself and you can tell us about the services you think you require. In addition, with your consent, we might talk to other professionals who know your circumstances, such as your GP. We also consider your family or carer’s needs and, if they request it, we can assess them separately.

Our only goal in the assessment is to understand what your daily life is like, the problems you have and how services might improve your life. We are not making judgements on any other aspect of your life.

How long do I have to wait for an assessment?

If you are at serious risk of coming to harm or in a crisis, we will try to make an assessment within a day of you making the request. We will try to see you within a week if your carer is struggling to cope. Otherwise, you will have an assessment within 28 days.

Is the assessment confidential?

our privacy is paramount and anything you tell us will be treated as confidential. Nothing will be shared with anyone else without your say so, except in exceptional cases when by law we will have to disclose it to another party.

What happens next?

Following the assessment what issues you have and what support that we can offer to meet those needs, which will depend on what difficulties you are experiencing. If after the assessment you are judged to be eligible to receive Adult Social Care services, they will be arranged within a week of the assessment happening.

But if it is found you do not qualify for Adult Social Care services, we will instead provide you some advice and information on other services you may be able to support you in your everyday life.

If you dispute the findings of the assessment you can ask for us to review your circumstances again with you. If you are still unhappy you can contact us and make a complaint or suggestion.

Eligibility Criteria

We will consider your needs against the national eligibility criteria that have been set by the Department of Health. These detail how severe the risk is to your independence if your needs are not met, as well as other potential outcomes. From April 2014, there is a new, national level of eligibility. This means that all Councils in England will determine whether any of the needs identified during the assessment are eligible for care and support using the same criteria. 

The eligibility threshold is based on identifying:

  • whether a person’s needs are due to a physical or mental impairment or illness;
  • to what extent a person’s needs affect their ability to achieve two or more specified outcomes;

and

  • whether and to what extent this impacts on their wellbeing
