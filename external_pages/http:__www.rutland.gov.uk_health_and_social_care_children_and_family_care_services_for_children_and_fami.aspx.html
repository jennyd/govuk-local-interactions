Services for Children and Families

Family life is full of challenges. Bringing up children is exciting and enjoyable, but it is also demanding, tiring and stressful. We all need help and support at times.

Other people expect so much from you as a teenager, but don't seem to understand you. This can mean being under pressure at home, at school, or with friends.

 

We may be able to help you to meet these challenges and to solve any problems.
Is your family life under stress? If it is then we may be able to help, through giving you the chance to talk about it. We can also provide information, advice, practical help in your home, and support.

Need help caring for a child? If you have children under school age, we have information about nursery provision, child minders, playgroups, and parent and toddler groups.

Are you a teenager under pressure at home, at school, or with friends? If it would help, we can provide information, someone to talk to, and the chance to discuss ways of helping you to solve problems.

Is something happening at home that is making you unhappy? Sometimes in families children get abused, physically, emotionally and even sexually. If this is happening in your family you need to tell someone. You can tell us, or someone else you trust, such as a teacher, doctor, the NSPCC, or the police. We all work together for the protection of children.

Need practical support caring for a child with special needs? If you child has special needs because of a physical, or other disability, we can help by providing information about services and support groups. We may also provide practical help in the home, day services, sitting services, holiday play schemes, short stays at a residential centre or with a family, and the chance to talk about planning for the future.

We believe that:

  • All services should be accessible and take into account the wide range of different cultural, religious and social needs of all the residents of Rutland.
  • Everyone has the right to lead a full and satisfying life.
  • Services should be provided locally.
  • People should be involved in the planning, decision making and management of the services they use.

Information about services for children with disabilities can be found on the Services for children with disabilities web page
