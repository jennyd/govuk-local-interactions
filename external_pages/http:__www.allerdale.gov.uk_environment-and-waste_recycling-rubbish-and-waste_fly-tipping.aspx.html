Fly tipping

The definition of fly tipping is the depositing of waste on an open space or public place without the permission of the land owner and which is not an authorised waste disposal site. Those caught flytipping face a maximum fine of £50,000 and/or imprisonment.

Dumping rubbish is illegal, dangerous and can be detrimental to the appearance of your neighbourhood.

Help us tackle this problem by reporting fly tipping. 

If you see someone fly tipping or would like to report an area where fly tipping has taken place, take note of the following:

  • The date, time and location of the occurrence
  • What the waste looks like and how much of it there is
  • A description of any vehicles involved, along with their vehicle’s registration number.

Householders are reminded that they have a duty of care to ensure that their waste is passed on to an authorised person (A registered waste carrier).  Householders may be prosecuted if fly tipped items can be traced back to them. 

  • 810 reports of fly tipping were received by the Council in 2012/13
  • 799 reports have been received between April 2013 and February 2014

In addition to the usual items that are fly tipped (sofas, fridges/freezers, builders rubble, carpets, mattresses and TV's), a number of unusual items have unfortunately been dumped in Allerdale:

  • Rowing machine
  • A fridge full of rotten meat
  • Garden shed
  • Portable toilet
  • Beer barrels
