Home maintenance - getting building works done

For the majority of people their house is the largest investment they will ever make. It makes sense, therefore to ensure that it is kept in the best possible condition.
Don’t take unnecessary risks with your health and safety and if you are in any doubt at all leave it to the professionals.

Contractors list

Finding a reliable contractor to carry out building work can be difficult for some people, particularly the vulnerable and the elderly. Central Bedfordshire Council has published a list of contractors (PDF 111KB) This is not a list of ‘recommended contractors’ and no warranty is offered, or should be implied in respect of the performance of contractors included in this list. However, the contractors on this list have carried out grant work previously for clients and the Council has checked to ensure that they have the necessary insurances and health and safety policies in place.

If you are considering works to historic buildings then please contact the conservation and design team on 0300 300 8307 to discuss the repairs and seek advice on appropriate specialist building contractors.

What work needs to be done?

Before getting estimates take a good look at your property and prepare a list of what needs to be done and how much of it there is to do both inside and out.

How much work is there to do?

Measure as best you can the work to be done in m2 [SQ Metres] for walls, floors, ceilings, roofs and for door and window sizes, measure in Linear Metres by length for soffits/fascias, guttering, drainage pipes and DPC’s; even a rough indication will assist you when dealing with a builder. Having drafted your list of works (or specification) get a few photocopies done ready for the builders. 

Contacting builders

Go by personal recommendations or approved lists of builders. Ask builders for the details of jobs they have just finished and follow them up with their clients. Look for local builders as travelling time has to be paid for and smaller builders who commonly carry out the sort of domestic work you need doing. Do not pay for estimates. 

You need at least two estimates.  Estimates should be on a builders headed note paper, dated and signed by the builder showing a price against each item of work, a sub total plus VAT and a grand total.  Ask when your builder can start work and the duration of the job and get written confirmation.

Works in progress

Whilst works are in progress look over the work and discuss it with your builder on a daily basis, be critical and ask questions. Don’t pay money up front.

Pay increments as individual items are completed to your satisfaction less retention of 10%. Pay promptly, following a final inspection of the works where you check that everything looks satisfactory and operates properly.

Housing Grants and Loan Assistance for Homeowners

Private sector housing renewal policy

The council recognises that some vulnerable homeowners may need help to carry out essential health and safety related works.

Central Bedfordshire Council has a renewal policy (PDF 124KB) This contains the types of loans and grants that the council can provide to help meet priority housing issues. All types of assistance are discretionary and completely dependent upon funding being available.

The two highest priorities within the policy are safety, security of older persons and other vulnerable groups, living within their home, and reducing cases of fuel poverty. When funding is scarce, the council will help meet the highest priorities.

Further information can be obtained by contacting 0300 300 8007 and asking for the housing solutions team, or by emailing  housingsolutions@centralbedfordshire.gov.uk

 What type of assistance can the Council provide?

The Council can provide  safety, security and emergency repair assistance (PDF 248KB)  at most times, to help meet the highest priorities. Home  Improvement Assistance (PDF 231KB) can sometimes be available for more significant works such as renewing a worn out roof, or dangerous wiring

Owners of long term empty homes may be eligible for Empty Homes Loan Assistance (PDF 197KB) in order to carry out works to enable the property to be re-occupied safely.  Residents of park homes may be eligible for smaller grants to help repair their homes or make them warmer or safer, but these homes are not eligible for loan assistance.
All types of grants or loans (except Empty Homes Loan Assistance) are subject to a financial test of resources, based upon the Government’s means test for benefits. Where possible, the council will guide residents to other potential sources of funding where it cannot help directly.

What type of assistance is currently available?

Due to the continually changing budget situation please contact the private sector housing team directly on 0300 300 8007, or by emailing  housingsolutions@centralbedfordshire.gov.uk, and an officer will advise you on the current availability. Even where financial assistance is not available the officer may be able to advise on how to deal with problems that you feel you have with your home. We can even provide you with details of contractors who you may wish to employ.

Other issues/need more information?

If you are the owner of a property which may be cold, unsafe, in disrepair or lacking basic amenities, please contact the housing solutions team on 0300 300 8007. For more information there are factsheets giving more details.

Means-Testing/Test of Resources

The council provides a range of grants, assistance, and loans to enable the repair and adaptation of homes within Central Bedfordshire. Many of these forms of assistance are means-tested so as to focus resources on those least able to pay themselves.

Clients can get an approximate idea of how much (if anything) they would need to contribute towards the cost of works by completing the on-line preliminary test of resources form. 

Loan assistance may also be available to landlords to enable them to raise the standard of their rented properties above the legal minimum, and for bringing empty properties into use.

You can also download a document detailing the council's  private sector housing renewal policy (PDF 124KB).
