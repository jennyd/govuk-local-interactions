Registering to vote

The new way we register to vote has changed.

How is the new system different?

It's individual:  under the old system the 'head of every household' could register everyone who lived at their address.  Now everyone is responsible for registering themselves, so the new system is sometimes called Individual Electoral Registration (IER).

It offers online registration:  you can now register online. It's simple, quick and secure.  You will need to have your national insurance number to hand, and to provide some other details such as your date of birth.  If you prefer, you can still register using a paper form.

How to register if you're not already registered

You can apply to register via www.gov.uk/register-to-vote. It's simple, quick and secure.  Remember to have your national insurance number to hand before you start.

The button on the top right of this page will also take you to www.gov.uk/register-to-vote if you need to register. (If you prefer, you can also download a paper registration form to complete and return in person or by post.)

How to register when you move house

When you move house within the district you need to re-register by visiting www.gov.uk/register-to-vote where you can enter the details of your new address and provide us with the details of the address you have moved from.

What you need to do if you're already registered

Most people who are already registered at their current property will stay on the register until they change address. Some will have been told that they need to register individually and will have been sent an "Invitation to Register". If that is the case, they must take action to register or they will be removed from the register after December 2015.

If you're a postal or proxy voter

People who are currently on the electoral register but have been told by the Electoral Registration Officer that they need to register individually will stay on the register until December 2015. They will not, however, be able to vote by post or proxy until they register individually.

What happens if you don't register

If you are sent an 'Invitation to Register' but don't respond to it, a reminder form will be sent to you. By law, we must also send a second reminder, followed by a personal visit to all residents who do not register to vote under the new system.

If you do not register you will not be able to vote in forthcoming elections. Not being registered may also adversely affect your credit rating.

What happens when you apply to register

When you register online or by returning a paper form your details will be checked against other records to confirm your identity.

If your details are confirmed, you will receive a letter telling you that you have been successfully registered.

If your details cannot be confirmed, we will write to you asking for more information to confirm your identity. The letter will explain what you will need to do.

Change of Address

Registering to vote does not automatically mean you are on the Council Tax register.  If you have either moved into the district or within the district, please click on this link to register for Council Tax at your new address:  Change of Address - Council Tax 

Further help and advice

Advice about registering to vote can be obtained from the electoral office.
