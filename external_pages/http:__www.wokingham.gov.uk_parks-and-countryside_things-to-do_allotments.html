Allotments

  • Print
  • Email

Read our tips on how to save water.

Wokingham Town Council currently has four sites at:

  • Ormonde Road (161 plots)
  • St Paul's Gate (18 plots)
  • Latimer Road (52 plots)
  • Gipsy Lane (77 plots)

Click on the image below to see a map of our allotments.

 Read our Gipsy Lane Allotments Management Plan.

If you are unsure of what can be grown on an allotment the average size plot (5 poles) can easily accommodate the following:

  • 2 rows new potatoes
  • 2 rows early carrots
  • 1 row parsnips
  • 2 rows runner beans
  • 1 gooseberry bush
  • 12 raspberry canes
  • 2 rows butternut squash
  • 1 row lettuce (throughout season)
  • 1 row French dwarf beans
  • 12 strawberry plants
  • 2 blackcurrant bushes

The Wokingham Horticultural Association is always pleased to give help and advice. Details of the organisation will be sent with the agreement if you decide to rent an allotment. The Horticultural Association also has a shed at the Ormonde Road site which is open mainly at weekends for advice, purchases of materials and such like.

The cost of an average plot (5 poles) is currently £40.00 per annum for 2012/2013. Concessions are available.

Our allotments are available to residents of the town of Wokingham  only. They are very popular and there is currently a waiting list of around eighteen months.  For further information please contact the Amenities Officer giving your name, postal address (with postcode), e-mail address and telephone number. Please note that if you live in other parts of the borough of Wokingham e.g. Earley, you should contact your relevant town or parish council for details of allotments to rent.

For residents of the Norreys / London Road area there is an opportunity to take advantage of a garden share scheme run by Transition Wokingham. Please click here for more details.

With effect from 10th May 2012 the Town Council has decided to limit the allocation of plots to one per household.
