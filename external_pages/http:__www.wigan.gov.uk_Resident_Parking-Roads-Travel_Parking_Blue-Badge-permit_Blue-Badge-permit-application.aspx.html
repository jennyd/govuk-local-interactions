Blue Badge applications

If you are disabled, a Blue Badge permits allow you to park legally near to places you need to visit.

The permit is for any eligible disabled person irrespective of whether you are travelling as a driver, or as a passenger.

It allows you to park free in some pay and display car parks, and on single and double yellow lines in most streets for up to three hours.

Although this is a national scheme that operates throughout the UK and is recognised in Europe, it’s delivered locally by us. We only issue badges in accordance with the guidelines and regulations provided by the Department for Transport (DfT).

  • View our frequently asked questions document for help and information about the scheme in Wigan.

How to apply for or renew a Blue Badge

You must renew your Blue Badge every three years.

We send out reminders 4 weeks before your badge is due to expire, but renewing a badge remains your responsibility.

You will need the details of your current badge to hand when applying for a renewal.

Part of the online application process is to check your eligibility before you complete the form.

  • Apply for or renew a Blue Badge online (external link) - do not send us a payment or documents or upload any documents or photographs.
  • Or call us on 01942 489004 - we will ask a series of questions to find out if you can get a Blue Badge permit
  • If your application is approved, you will need to pay the £10 fee, have your photograph taken and for us to check your documents which can only be done at:
    • Wigan Life Centre
    • Leigh Life Centre.
  • If we decide you need further assessment, an appointment will be arranged to see our independent occupational therapist for a face-to-face mobility assessment. This can only be done at Wigan or Leigh Life Centre.
  • If your application is refused you can appeal in writing by following the Blue Badge appeal procedure.

Who can have a Blue Badge?

People who may be issued with a badge are those who are more than two years old and fall within one or more of the following descriptions:

  • Are registered as severely sight impaired or blind
  • Receive the higher rate mobility component of the Disability Living Allowance
  • Receive a War Pension Mobility Supplement
  • Meet a ‘Moving Around’ descriptor for the Mobility Component of Personal Independence Payment (PIP) that indicates you cannot stand or can stand but walk no more than 50 metres. This is a score of 8 points or more.
  • Have been awarded a benefit under the Armed Forces Compensation Scheme and have been certified by the Service Personnel and Veterans agency as having a permanent and substantial disability which causes an inability to walk or very considerable difficulty in walking.

We may decide you need a further assessment with our independent occupational therapist if you:

  • Have a permanent and substantial disability which means you cannot walk, or have considerable difficulty walking
  • Drive a motor vehicle regularly, have a severe disability in both arms, and are unable to or would find it difficult to operate all or some types of parking meter
  • Apply on behalf of a child under the age of three who has a medical condition that requires bulky medical equipment or immediate access to a vehicle for treatment. You will also need to provide a letter from the child’s consultant to support this application.

Blue Badges for organisations

A Blue Badge can be issued to an organisation that transports disabled people who, if they applied for a Blue Badge as individuals, would qualify in their own right. It can only be used when transporting disabled people and must not be used for any other business. A senior representative may apply on behalf of their organisation.

Taxi firms are not eligible to apply. We would recommend that their clients apply for an individual badge.
