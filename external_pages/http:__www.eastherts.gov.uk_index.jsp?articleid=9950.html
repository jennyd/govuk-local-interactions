Neighbourhood Noise

A certain amount of noise is an inevitable consequence of modern life, but it can become a nuisance and, if it continues, or becomes loud enough, it becomes a major problem.

Noise at night or early in the morning is much more obtrusive, although sometimes it is designed to be, like a burglar alarm. However, it is particularly irritating if an alarm sounds continuously, as many people who have experienced a disturbed night's sleep can testify.

Common causes for complaint are from sources such as loud radios, hi-fis, DIY, late night parties and barking dogs. (For more information on keeping the peace with your neighbours for dog owners, visit our section on Animal Welfare.)

You must bear in mind that if you can hear sounds through the walls, your neighbours may hear sounds from you too. If the walls or windows are thin, it may be that a little consideration from everybody is all that's required to solve the problem.

Complaining about Noise

If a complaint about noise is received by this authority we will investigate the complaint in accordance with our procedures. Please see the information contained in the Noise Nuisance Help Pack  and the 'What the Council Will Do' section in the frequently asked questions section (FAQ's).

Please note that this is not an authoritative guide to the law, it is a guide only. If you are considering an action under Section 82 of the Environmental Protection Act, you are advised to contact a solicitor.

Need more information? Why not have a look at the Neighbours from Hell website link on the right hand side of the page.  The aim of this website is to be the first port of call for those who feel that they are suffering from hearing excessive or unwanted noise.
