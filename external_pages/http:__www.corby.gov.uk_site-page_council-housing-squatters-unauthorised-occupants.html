Council Housing - Squatters & Unauthorised Occupants

 UNAUTHORISED OCCUPANTS

Most people living in Council housing have a tenancy to their home. However, in some situations, a tenancy can end whilst someone is still living in the property. Any people in the property then become 'unauthorised occupants'.

For example, this can happen:

  • The tenant gives notice that they will be leaving the property, but then does not move out at the end of the notice period. The tenant and their household will be unauthorised occupants.
  • The tenant dies leaving household members in the property, but no household member has the legal right to take over the tenancy ('succeed to the tenancy'). Everyone in the household will be an unauthorised occupier.

Corby Borough Council is usually aware of unauthorised occupants, but if you think there is someone living at a property who we do not know about - let us know. We will be very grateful for the information, and you do not have to give your name and address. Email: landlord.services@corby.gov.uk

SQUATTERS

The law on squatting is complex, and we cannot just 'throw a person out' who is squatting: there is a legal process that we must go through to repossess a property.

If there are squatters in a Council property, we need to know about it so that we can start action to get them out, and then offer the property to a household on the waiting list.

If you believe that a Council property is being used by squatters, please contact us and let us know. We will be extremely grateful for the information and you do not need to give your name and address. Email: Landlord.Services@corby.gov.uk
