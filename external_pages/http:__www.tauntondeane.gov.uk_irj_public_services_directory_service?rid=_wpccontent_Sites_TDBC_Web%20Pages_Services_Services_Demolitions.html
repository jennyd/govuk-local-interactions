Demolitions

Service details for Demolitions
Service element Service details
Responsibility: Building Control
Anyone intending to carry out demolition work within Taunton Deane is required to notify Building Control. We control both public and private sector demolition. If you have concerns regarding either please contact us.

About this service

Generally the demolition of buildings (or parts of a building) over a certain size will need authority under the Building Act before any site work on the demolition commences. We may set out conditions relating to site safety and the way the work is undertaken. Although the legislation for dealing with notification of demolition allows six weeks for us to complete our processing, we will complete this process as quickly as possible.

What is covered?

When the owner of a building over 50m³ intends to have it demolished, the legal requirements of Sections 80-83 of the Building Act 1984 impose a requirement for formal notification to be given, at least six weeks before any demolition work starts. This is to enable all the right people and organisations to be advised of the demolition and to ensure that measures to protect the public and preserve public amenities are put in place.

 

The notice issued to us should specify the building that it relates to and the work of demolition proposed.
Copies of the notice should be given to:

 

  • the occupiers of any adjacent building
  • service providers (Water, Electricity, Gas and others);
  • Somerset Fire and Rescue Service.

 

It will assist us to process the application more speedily if a site plan is provided with the notice and all relevant contact details are included.

How long does it take?

At least six weeks notification is needed before the physical commencement of demolition. We aim to write back before the six weeks has elapsed, notifying you of our requirements under the Act. Once you have received notification you may commence the demolition works, otherwise you should wait until the six weeks has elapsed.
