Council housing and tenants

The availability of council housing in Central Bedfordshire has fallen whilst the demand for housing has increased in recent years. Most properties allocated by Housing Associations and the council are allocated through the Council’s Choice Based Lettings Scheme.

If you are on a low income and struggling to make ends meet, you may be entitled to claim benefits that will help you to manage your rent payments, whether you live in a private or council accommodation. If you chose, you can visit the Housing Options office for further information( one located in the North and the other the South of the county). Both locations operate a drop in service. It is important to know that the council can help you to keep a roof over your head if there is a risk of you losing your home.

Central Bedfordshire Council owns approximately 5,000 properties across the district. As a landlord we provide a range of service to our tenants.

Renting a council garage

Would you like somewhere to park your car or motorbike?

If so, we can help. We have garages available in the south of Central Bedfordshire. If a garage is not available in the area you want, we can add your name to our waiting list until one becomes available.

How to rent a garage

Central Bedfordshire Council has garages to rent in Dunstable, Houghton Regis, Leighton Buzzard, Linslade and surrounding villages. A garage application form or you can contact the estates management team by calling 0300 300 8302.

Garages in the north of Central Bedfordshire can be rented from Aragon Housing Association or download their application form for a garage.

Please note that council tenant applicants who owe rent arrears will not be offered garages, and that garages are for the parking/storage of vehicles only and should not be used to store goods, as a workshop or for business purposes.

The weekly price at present is:

Council tenant: £10.00*

Non-council tenant: £12.00*

Application

Once an application has been received and accepted, you will be offered the next available garage within the area you have requested. Some areas are very popular and have a long waiting list.

Signing of tenancy agreement

If you accept the garage offered to you, you will need to attend the council offices in Dunstable to sign a contract. We will need to see proof of identity such as a driving license, passport along with proof of address, such as your current council tax bill.

Along with ID, we currently require a month’s payment in advance which is £40.00* for council tenants, and £48.00* for non-council tenants. Future payments will be discussed at your appointment.

Your garage keys will be available after you have signed the contract. The contract will start on a Monday and you will be charged weekly on a Monday thereafter.

* subject to change

Re-possession

If you do not pay your rent, we will send you letters to remind you to pay, followed by a Notice to Quit. If you still do not clear the arrears on your account, we will re-possess the garage.

Termination

To terminate your garage contract, we require one week’s notice in writing, alternatively please complete the on-line termination form. The garage should be cleared and swept, and any additions that you may have added, removed. All rent owing on the property needs to be paid.

Garages - rent arrears

Most arrears cases can be dealt with relatively simply provided tenants get in contact early enough. Ignoring the situation can only make matters worse. If you are experiencing difficulties, please contact Central Bedfordshire Council estates management team on 0300 300 8302.

Due to the low level of rent compared to a house or flat, some may regard garage rent as low priority. However, we have a duty to recover all rent owed to the Council to fund the services we provide. We are also required to maintain overall levels of outstanding rent including garage rent, within limits set by Central Government

When the garage rent arrears are in excess of one months rent the Council will repossess the garage, by issuing a seven day notice to quit.

If a garage is repossessed and items have been left, you will be contacted and asked to remove them. The council will dispose of all items within 28 days following the termination. Should the council have to clear the garage, the cost of the clearance will be charged to the yourself, this is in addition to any arrears outstanding.

Nuisance and anti social behaviour

Both the community safety team and the housing services team are dedicated to dealing with anti social behaviour to make sure the residents of Central Bedfordshire enjoy a quiet and peaceful home environment.

The housing services team will take all reasonable steps to ensure that residents comply with their tenancy agreement, whilst the community safety team will ensure that residents comply with their obligations to the community not to behave in an anti social manner.

Central Bedfordshire Council will work in partnership with other agencies to ensure that intervention is tried before legal action is taken.

What is nuisance and anti social behaviour?

The law states that anti social behaviour is:

“Acting in a manner that caused or was likely to cause harassment, alarm or distress to one or more persons not of the same household as themselves”.

Central Bedfordshire Council goes one step further and classifies anti social behaviour as:

“Behaviour causing disturbance, distress, harm or fear which has a significant effect on people’s lifestyles or routines. Persistence, intensity and the numbers being involved are relevant factors. The behaviour need not be a breach of criminal law.”

Put simply, anti social behaviour is something that disturbs others and affects people’s quality of life and the peace and quiet of the neighbourhood.

Anti social behaviour can mean different things to different people, depending on their tolerance levels.

For example, people living on a large housing estate may be more tolerant to young people playing football on a green outside their property than elderly residents living in sheltered/warden controlled property who may find this intimidating and distressing. Although this is distressing it a nuisance and not anti social behaviour.

Acceptable levels of noise

Tolerance to noise may also be different. A family with children may accept higher noise levels than a person living alone working shifts, who may find the same noise levels unacceptable. Again although this is distressing it is a nuisance and not anti social behaviour.

To report noise nuisance out of hours, call 0845 849 5972.

Common problems

Anti-social behaviour can manifest itself in a variety of ways but some of the more common problems include:

  • intimidation, shouting and swearing
  • graffiti, vandalism and damage to property
  • using and selling class a drugs
  • using your home to sell drugs or for other illegal activity
  • verbal and physical threats and abuse
  • animals not being kept under control
  • harassment including racist, gender, religious and homophobic incidents
  • loud amplified noise

This is not an exhaustive list as there are many different types of anti social behaviour. For all criminal related incidents, the Police will lead the enforcement action. To report such incidents either call 999 if the matter is urgent alternatively call 101 or call into your local Police station. In these cases Central Bedfordshire Council will jointly work with the Police to resolve the situation.

Action

If the problem is persistent and ongoing, you will need to keep a diary log, in which you should note the date, the time, and nature of the incident. You also should note how the incident made you feel and what you did about it i.e. call the police or the environmental protection team. If you know other people who are affected by the problem you could ask them to keep diaries as well.

The officer dealing with the case will gather all the information together and make an assessment of the best way to resolve the problem.

This may include:

  • warning letters and home visits
  • mediation
  • acceptable Behaviour Contracts (ABCs)
  • Anti Social Behaviour Notice
  • Anti Social Behaviour Orders (ASBOs)
  • injunctions

Housing legal proceeding:

  • Notice seeking possession
  • Demotion of tenancy
  • Possession orders
  • Evictions
  • Criminal proceedings

Communal areas

Communal heating charges

Tenant service charges In addition to your rent you may also pay charges for:

  • communal heating
  • communal lighting
  • communal cleaning
  • communal electric 
  • site warden / manager

The council calculates the unit cost for each service and then recovers these costs by allocating the total cost between the number of tenants.

Costs are revised on an annual basis and these charges will be stated individually on your rent account statement.  The council will provide four weeks’ written notice of any change.

Services and communal repairs

Each estate has an Estates Officer who on their visits will ensure that  communal areas clean and tidy and report any repairs they become aware of. We will repair things such as:

  • lighting on the communal stairways and outside the block on the walls or main lights that are on estates
  • glazing on the communal stairways and walkways
  • roofing
  • gutters
  • rain water pipes
  • drains outside on the communal parts of the estate
  • communal boilers
  • doors
  • intercoms
  • fire exit doors
  • lifts and communal aerials
  • communal paths

This list is not exhaustive.

If you want to report an area that needs attention or repair please call 0800 074 0263

Insurance

Council housing insurance

Central Bedfordshire Council insures its council dwellings. In the case of a fire that destroyed a property the council would claim on its insurance for the cost of repairing the property.

Tenant contents are not insured

The council does not insure any of the contents that belong to the tenant. We strongly urge that every tenant should have contents insurance. Contents insurance is available from a variety of insurance companies and probably the best starting point is the Yellow Pages telephone directory. As always, the council is happy to give advice on home contents insurance should you require it.

Damage by the council

Council tenants may make insurance claims against the council for any damage that the council causes to the tenants' possessions, or injury to the tenant or members of their family. As an example, if a council tradesman were to hammer a nail into the wall and inadvertently burst a pipe which resulted in the floor being flooded and ruining the tenant's carpet. The council would then be responsible for the cost of replacing the carpet as clearly it has been negligent in its actions.

Damage not caused by the council

However, there are a variety of other issues for which the council may not be negligent. For example, if there is a particularly bad storm which lifts a tile off of a roof and as a result water comes through the roof, through the ceiling and damages a bedroom carpet, the council would claim that it has not been negligent as it could not have prevented that from happening. Under such circumstances the council would seek to reject any such claim.

Squatters and unauthorised occupants

Empty council properties are usually awaiting repair, redecoration or re-letting and we are keen to ensure they stay empty so that someone on the waiting list has the opportunity to find a home.

The laws on squatting are quite complex and the council cannot simply evict a person who is squatting.

If you see anyone suspicious or who appears to be squatting in a property please advise us as soon as possible so that we can resolve the matter quickly. Any contact will be strictly confidential.

Tenancy support

The role of the Tenancy Support Officer is to help tenants sustain their tenancies. Tenancy support cases can be referred to us for a variety of reasons such as:

  • It could be that someone is having trouble managing their money leading to rent arrears and they may benefit from some debt management advice.
  • There could be cases where the property is messy or repair work has not been carried out or where the contractors cannot get in to carry out essential maintenance.
  • It may be that the accommodation that the tenant is in, is no longer appropriate for them and they need help to move to a more suitable property, such as a larger, smaller, or a more accessible home or sheltered housing.
  • Or perhaps a person is struggling to cope with several things and could be classed as vulnerable and in need of support and guidance.

How can we help.

  • TSO’s can help guide you to manage your income, and advise on debt repayments.
  • Where there are cases of debt that need more in-depth work, we can refer you to the Citizen’s Advice Bureau or other accredited debt management charities.
  • We can source professional help from organisations such as the Community Mental Health Team or Social Services.
  • Where other support could be beneficial, we can refer to other agencies, such as the Community Alcohol Network, or Occupational Therapy.
  • We can provide help with accessing the housing register and help you to move to a more suitable property.
  • Put simply, we help you to maintain your tenancy, to reduce the risk of you losing your home.

How to contact the Tenancy Support team.

If you feel that you need our support you will need to be referred to us.

Please speak to a Council Officer or other Council professional who will carry out the referral.

If you do not know who to speak to please call 0300 300 8302
