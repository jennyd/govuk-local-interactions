Child performer licence

How to apply for a licence

View pages in this section

If a school-age child is going to take part in a professional performance - that is not simply playing Mary in the end-of-term nativity play - they will probably need a child performance licence.

Changes to child performance licensing

There are important changes to the existing child performance legislation. The changes came into force on 6 February 2015.

Any licences that were issued before 6 February 2015 were issued in accordance with the old regulations (The Children (Performances) Regulations 1968 as amended), and are subject to the conditions of the 1968 regulations for the duration of the licence.

If an applicant wishes to take advantage of the new regulations then a new application will need to be submitted and a new licence issued. This can only be done from 6 February 2015.

A licence will not be issued under the new regulations before 6 February.

Find out more about The Children (Performance and Activities) (England) Regulations 2014

We are currently preparing updates for these pages, which will be available as soon as possible.  

Who applies for it?

The company producing the performance should apply for any child performance licences that it needs. However, there is a section on the form that needs to be filled in by the parent.

How to apply

Send us your completed form and if we are satisfied that the child will be in safe hands and won't miss too much school - or they have alternative arrangements - we will grant the licence.

1. Contact the child's school

If the child is going to miss any school, you need to clear it with the headteacher. We need a letter from the school saying they are happy for the pupil to do the performance and take the time off.

If the child is going to miss a lot of school, you may need to arrange for a tutor to accompany them.

2. Download and complete the form

The application for a child performance licence must be made by the person responsible for the production - whether television, film, theatre, modelling or sport.

There is also a section that needs to be filled in by the parent.

If you would like the form to be send to you by post, call, write or email your request to us.

Download child performer and chaperone documents

3. Send us the form

Don't forget to include the following:

  • Two photographs of the child taken in last six months
  • Birth certificate
  • School letter
  • Copy of the contract
  • Parental health declaration

Try and submit the application at least 21 days before the performance.

How long does it take?

We will assess the application, and then, if satisfied, we will grant the licence. This usually takes about 21 days.

However, if you urgently need one quicker than that, get in touch. We are part of the London Child Employment Protocol Network which means we can turn a licence around in seven days.

The legislation

Before we issue a licence, we must be satisfied with arrangements for:

  • the supervision and protection of the child, and
  • the education of the child, for example if they will be missing school, need a tutor and so on.

This licensing is demanded by The Children and Young Persons Acts 1933 and 1963 and The Children (performances) Regulations 1968.
