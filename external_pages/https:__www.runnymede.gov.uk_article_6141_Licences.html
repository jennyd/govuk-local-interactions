Licences

Licences including personal, Premises, Temporary Events Notices and how to make representations

  • Decision notices

  • Current licensing applications

    Applications for public consultation are shown below, representations must be made as per the guidance on the linked and related pages.

  • Late night refreshment

    Late night refreshment is the supply of hot food or hot drink to the public, for consumption on or off the premises, between 11pm and 5am.

  • Licensing Act 2003 Guidance

    The Licensing Act 2003 modernised the licensing laws governing the sale and supply of alcohol, the provision of late night refreshment and various regulated forms of entertainment. Application forms, guidance and information on the Act can be found on this website.

  • Application forms (licences)

    A Premises Licence authorises the use of any premises (vehicle, vessel or moveable structure or any place or a part of any premises) for licensable activities.

  • Personal Licence

    A Personal Licence is needed by an individual who wants to sell or supply, or authorise the sale/supply of alcohol, at premises that already hold a Premises Licence. There are different rules for the supply of alcohol under a 'Club Premises Certificate' or 'Temporary Event Notice'. Personal Licences are 'portable' between premises throughout England and Wales, allowing the license holder to sell or authorise the sale of alcohol for consumption on or off any premises with a Premises Licence.

  • Licensing fees and charges

    Please see our information on fees and charges for applications. These have been at the current level since the introduction of the Licensing Act in 2003. They are set in statute and the same fees apply country-wide.

  • Licensing Policy

    The Licensing Act 2003 imposes a statutory duty upon councils to produce, develop and review (at least every five years) their licensing policies.

  • Responsible Authorities

    This page provides a list of organisations the Licensing Act 2003 describes as 'responsible authorities'. These are the bodies you must inform if you are applying for a licence or certificate. They can pass comment upon your application or call for a review of your licence.

  • Temporary event notices (TEN)

    A Temporary Events Notice (TEN) is required for an event where licensable activities will take place and fewer than 500 people will attend.
