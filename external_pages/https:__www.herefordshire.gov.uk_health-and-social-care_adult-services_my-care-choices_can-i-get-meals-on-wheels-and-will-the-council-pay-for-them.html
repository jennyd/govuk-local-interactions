Can I get meals on wheels and will the council pay for them?

The council does not provide a community meals service. Instead, we provide information about a range of meal providers, who can supply both hot and frozen meals, delivered direct to your home, as well as lunch clubs that operate in the community. These options are available in most localities in Herefordshire. This allows you to choose and purchase meals or services to suit your preferences and location.

The Royal Voluntary Service (RVS) provides community meals, also known as meals on wheels, to older people in the community. The charge is £4.75 per meal. The RVS will collect this payment from you. Further details are available direct from the RVS on their website or you can contact the RVS (01432 267555) and ask about this service

Other commercial meal delivery services that deliver meals to your home are available. You can find details on the internet, in your local paper or in the yellow pages.

Visit our community meals and lunch clubs page to see the options and prices.

FAQs

What is my support plan and how does it work?

Following a care assessment, we will draw up a support plan with you.

Your support plan will explain how you are going to organise and pay for your support. You can choose to have services organised by the council's social care practitioners or to have direct payments so that you can organise and make your own arrangements to pay for your care.

Ask your social care practitioner to make arrangements for you

If you choose to ask your social care practitioner to make your support arrangements for you, we will make arrangements with the organisations we have contracts with and use your personal budget to pay for the services we arrange for you.

Take your personal budget as direct payments

If you choose to take your personal budget as direct payments we will pay you the money directly each month and you will be responsible for organising and paying for the care you need. For more information about taking your personal budget as a direct payment please visit: Direct payments for social care.

What is a personal budget for care services?

If, following a care assessment, you need non-residential care, we will work with you to determine how much money is available for your support. This is your personal budget, to be used for the services and care that best suit your needs, help you stay independent and live your life the way that you want.

Your personal budget will be used either by the council to arrange care services on your behalf, or can be paid to you as direct payments if you choose to make your own arrangements for your care.

Can I get care in my own home?

We can offer care in your own home if you need help with personal care such as:

  • Washing
  • Dressing
  • Taking medications
  • Continence management
  • Assistance with going to the toilet

We can also provide help at home with food preparation and in some instances, managing finances.

Before we can do this we need to assess your needs to find out what help you are eligible for and, if you are eligible for financial assistance, tell you how much your direct payments will be. We can help you to find support, or organise your own using this money. If you can organise your own support through relatives or friends, you do not have to contact us.

Read more about the care assessment process and arrange an assessment.

What sort of help can the council help to arrange for me?

Examples of the kind of help we can arrange as part of your support plan include:

  • Help with personal care, like washing and dressing
  • Meals
  • Support for carers
  • A place at a day centre
  • Adaptations or equipment in your home to make managing easier
  • Short term breaks for you and for your carers
  • If necessary, longer term residential or nursing home care

There may be a charge for some of these services. How much you are asked to contribute towards the cost will depend on your needs, income, benefits and savings. We will work with you to complete a financial assessment and can then work out how much, if anything, you will be asked to pay.

Read more about the care assessment process and arrange an assessment.

What resources are available to me if I wish to arrange my own care?

If you choose to arrange your own care there are a number of resources you can use, including:

  • Social care section of the Service directory - which provides links to independent bodies and charities providing information and social care support
  • List of village websites, many of which provide information about local and community facilities, social events, and services
  • Community transport - information about local community transport if you do not have access to or cannot use public transport
  • National charity websites, such as those in the external links on this page, and those dedicated to particular conditions or circumstances which often also provide local information or support groups
