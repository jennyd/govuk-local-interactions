The Care Act

From April 2015 care and support in England is changing. The current systems used by councils and health care professionals to assess the care needs of an individual are complex, confusing and often managed differently across the country. The new Care Act will replace them with a simpler, single, method which will be the same wherever you live in England.

The Act takes account of best care and support practices from around the country and will make their use a legal requirement rather than just desirable guidelines.

What the Care Act means for you

 What the Care Act means for Staffordshire - transcript (22 KB)

Are you hard of hearing? Watch this video with British Sign Language from the Department of Health

Care and support is the term used to describe the help which adults of any age might need at any point in their lives. This may include washing, dressing, eating, getting out and about and keeping in touch with friends or family.

Many of us will need care and support at some point in our lives, and most people will pay at least something toward the cost of their care. The new changes are designed to help plan for the future and put you in control of the help you may need and receive. In the future, any decisions about your care and support will consider your wellbeing and what is important for you and your family.

The Care Act will change the way care and support is accessed, assessed and funded. It will make it clearer for people to know what their care and support options are, and what the council needs to do.

For Staffordshire County Council and Stoke-on-Trent City Council the Act will help us to more effectively promote the benefits of health and wellbeing to Staffordshire’s residents. By helping people to be as independent as possible we can help them to prevent, reduce or delay their need for care and support.

What is the council doing to implement the Care Act?

The changes introduced by the Care Act do not start to come into effect until April 2015. Some of our plans are well advanced. For example, we are already well placed to offer the advice and information recommended by the Care Act. In Staffordshire and Stoke-on-Trent we have Staffordshire Cares. This website provides care and support information, advice and guidance people of all ages. We also have Staffordshire Marketplace. This online directory of services and activities will be live from February 2015. It will be a key way to find out about the range of providers in your area.

Other responsibilites, such as the cap on care costs will require a lot of preparation to implement, as well as working in partnership with clients, carers and our partner organisations in the independent and voluntary sectors.
