Right To Buy

Right To BuyThe Right to Buy (RTB) scheme gives tenants the right to purchase their home at a discount.  As a council tenant you must have a secure public sector tenancy totalling a minimum of 5 years.  

 

Please note the length of time it would take from the Right to Buy application to the completion date varies as there are many varying factors which cannot be controlled despite the Right to Buy time frame being governed and set by Legislation. The duration from application to completion would roughly last for under a year. 

 

The main difficulty that tenants are facing during an application for the Right to Buy is with regards to obtaining the finances. It is advised, if possible, to prearranged/plan the finances before applying for the Right to Buy. 

 

We do acknowledge that in order to prearranged/plan the finances, tenants require a certain level of information from the Council. However please note the information that the Council provide is quite limited. Information such as the valuation which we cannot provide simply because we do not know the value until an applicant applies for the Right to Buy, qualifies, and then we are able to appoint a chartered surveyor to attend the property to carryout the survey .

 

We are also unable to provide an estimated value of the property.

 

Valuations 

 

It is advised, should you wish to know the value of the property, to be better informed to make a decision whether to proceed with the Right to Buy or not, to obtain the estimated value of your property via online sources or forward your query with the nearest estate agent. Both methods should be free of charge.  

 

The way the valuation is carried out on the Right to Buy, properties are valued as of current market in the condition that the property is in just like everywhere else. Whether the property is council owned or privately owned makes no difference as to how a property is valued under the Right to Buy. It is advised and suggested for you to obtain an estimate so that you can make an informed decision based on your finances whether it would be worth proceeding/whether you can afford to proceed.  

 

 Discounts 

The current maximum is £102,700.00  How the discount is calculated:

 

 Entirely dependent on the tenants years of tenancy and the value of the property. 

 HouseFlat
5 years (being the current minimum required years to claim for a Right to Buy)35%50%
 Every year(s) thereafter is calculated  @1% @2%
 Maximum 70% 70%

 

Whatever the end percentage it cannot exceed the maximum discount of currently £102,700.00 

 

Previous tenancies toward qualifying period and discount calculation

Should you wish to have your previous tenancies to be taken into account to claim for the Right to Buy and or for us to calculate the discount, please note only secure public sector permanent tenancies can only count.   

 

Time period that is spent as a private tenant, if you had temporary tenancy whether in another borough, in a hotel, or in a bed and breakfast, will not count towards the discount calculation nor the qualifying period in which to claim for the Right to Buy. 

 

 Family Members 

 

Should you wish to have family members who are not classed as "tenant" to be apart of the Right to Buy for the purposes of financing the purchase of the property, please do note this is allowed. The condition is that the family member is currently residing at the property and at least for 12 months and not registered at any other property. 

 

Please note although a family members name might appear on the tenancy agreement, it doesn't mean that that person is classed as a "tenant". For clarification with regards to this please call your Housing Officer for the confirmation.

 

Only the named tenant(s) have the right as a secure public sector tenant to claim for the Right to Buy. Anyone else residing at the property who are not classed as "tenant(s)" would have no such rights.  

 

Mortgage 

 

Should you wish to purchase the property under a mortgage please be advised that the named tenant(s) on the Right to Buy application form, as per tenancy agreement, must appear on the mortgage papers. There would be no exceptions regardless of age and or employment status.

 

If the named tenant(s) are wishing/relying to have family members to obtain the mortgage on their behalf without their name appearing on the mortgage offer then the banks/lenders will more than likely will reject and decline.

 

This is due to the fact that without the named tenant(s) there is no Right to Buy. Without a possibility of potentially obtaining a mortgage from the banks/lenders then the Right to Buy is unlikely to complete.  

 

Repairs

 

Please note while an application for the Right to Buy is active, no repairs or works are to be carried out unless classed as emergency.
