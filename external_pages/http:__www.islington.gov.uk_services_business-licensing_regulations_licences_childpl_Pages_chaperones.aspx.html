Chaperones

Page Content

The Chaperone is acting in loco parentis and should exercise the care which a good parent might be reasonably expected to give that child.  A Chaperone is the key person to whom the child looks to for guidance, protection, clarification and support. One of a Chaperone’s greatest strengths is her/his ability to negotiate with the production company ‘on site’ and be able to say ‘no’ when what is being requested of the child is contrary or detrimental to either the child’s health, well being and/or education.

The chaperone is required by law to keep a record for each child, per performance, the daily record sheet template is provided for this purpose (this is available from the downloadable documents section below). It is a requirement under the Regulations that these records be kept and made available, together with each child’s Licence, at every place of performance where a child is present, for inspection by an officer of the Local Authority in whose area the performance takes place. Upon completion of the production, the daily record sheet/s should be stored at the Licence Applicant’s main company address for a period of not less than 6 months after the final performance date for which the Licence has been granted. 

Becoming a Chaperone

To register as a chaperone you must have up to date knowledge of relevant legislation and guidance in relation to working with, and the protection of, children and young people, display commitment to the protection and safeguarding of children and young people and value and respect the views and needs of children and young people.

Chaperones are required to have attended Level 2 Child Protection Training, at a minimum in the last three years, and preferably attended a refresher course annually. The Islington Safeguarding Board runs regular Level 2 courses in Child Protection.

You will need to apply for an Enhanced Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) check as well as completing the Chaperone Application form.

The chaperone application pack consists of the following documents that can be downloaded from this page

  • Chaperone Application Form & Cover Letter
  • Chaperone Person Specification
  • Chaperoning - A Brief Guide
  • Safe Working Practice Guidance
  • What to do if  you are worried a child is being abused - Summary
  • Children in Entertainment Information
 
You will also need to provide the following:
 
  • Details of three people who can be contacted to act as referees, (this is your current or last employer and two character referees).
  • Two identical passport style photos taken in the 6 months prior to application 
  • One payment for a DBS check (if required).  Please refer to the Application Form cover letter for details & costs, (this is downloadable below).

If you require a hard copy of the chaperone application pack, please contact us using details below.

Please be aware that the Licensing Authority is not responsible or involved in any way in finding work for chaperones. However, if you would like to be put on a list of working chaperones which can be sent to theatres or production companies at their request, please indicate this on your application form.

Enhanced DBS Disclosure

The Licensing Authority requires an Enhanced Disclosure and Barring (DBS) clearance, this was formerly called a Criminal Records Bureau (CRB) check.  Proof of previous DBS / CRB clearance will not be sufficient.  The Enhanced DBS disclosure must be applied for through Islington Council, in person, with all relevant original supporting documents.  This DBS fee payment will need to be received at time of the chaperone application in order to proceed. If the Chaperone Licence is required only for unpaid volunteer work the DBS fee will be waived.

When you have received your DBS clearance

  • Inform Pupil Services of the clearance details in writing (email accepted).
  • Arrange to take the original copy of the DBS in to Pupil Services as soon as possible after you have received it.
 

Renewing your DBS disclosure and Chaperone licence

A DBS disclosure is valid for three years.

A chaperone licence is issued for one year and may be renewed twice for a maximum of one year each time.

Please note that chaperones may not receive reminder letters, but are responsible for ensuring any renewal is received by the Licensing Authority in good time.

For Chaperone Licence renewal please complete and return the ‘Renewal Application to act as a Chaperone’ form, at least one month before your current licence expires. After the licence has been renewed twice, you will need to re-apply, i.e. begin the process from the start, by completing a new ‘Application to act as a Chaperone’ form.

Due to the processing time of the DBS disclosure, chaperones should contact Pupil Services regarding re-application at least three months before the expiry of their current DBS disclosure &/ start date of any chaperone work.
