Abandoned vehicles

A vehicle could be considered abandoned by the council if it has:

  • flat tyres
  • broken windows
  • rusted brake discs
  • a build up of dirt around the wheels

It’s an offence to abandon a motor vehicle on open land or the public highway. You could be fined £2500 or sent to prison if you abandon a vehicle.

What the council does

If it’s established that the vehicle is abandoned, local enquiries will be made to identify an owner. This process can take up to 28 days.

If a vehicle has no commercial value then it will be removed within 24 hours of the council’s legal entitlement to do so.

Vehicles with some commercial value will be removed and stored for 7 days. If vehicles aren’t claimed in this time then they will be destroyed and the council will try to recover its costs from the registered keeper.

The council may also take action against any person where it can be proved that they abandoned the vehicle.

All burnt out vehicles are removed as soon as is possible for health and safety reasons.

Report an abandoned vehicle

The council has a duty to remove abandoned vehicles. It inspects all reported vehicles within 2 working days.If you would like to report an abandoned vehicle please our First Contact team.

If you’re concerned about a vehicle and think it’s abandoned, you should make a note of:

  • its exact location
  • registration number
  • make
  • model
  • colour
  • the length of time it’s been left

If your vehicle has had a notice stuck to it or it’s been removed by the council and you want to claim it, you must contact the Priority Intervention Team and prove your ownership of the vehicle.

Removal scheme

If you’re a registered owner of a vehicle that you no longer want, we offer a removal scheme. There is a charge for the service and you’ll be asked to supply us with the log book and keys to the vehicle. Please contact the Priority Intervention Team for details.

Vehicles for sale

Selling a vehicle from the public highway can constitute an offence. These vehicles can be reported to Customer Services.
