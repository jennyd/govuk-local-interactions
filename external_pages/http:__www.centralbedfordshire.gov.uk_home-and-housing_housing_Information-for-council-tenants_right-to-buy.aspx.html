Right to Buy

Right to buy your council house

The scheme is open to people who are secure tenants who have spent at least three years as a public sector tenant. You may apply jointly with family members over 18 years old who have lived with you for at least 12 months.  You may apply if you are currently receiving Housing Benefit or have in the past.

Right to Buy information

Please read this information before completing your application

The Right to Buy scheme was introduced in 1980 and is designed to help council and housing association tenants to buy their home at a discount. Once you have completed an application form, you will need return it, in person, to the Council Offices, at Watling House, along with original photo ID (acceptable forms of ID are; current passport, current driving licence or ID card) for all applicants, proof of previous tenancies and proof you are living at the property. We may request further documentary evidence and you may be invited to attend an interview at the Council Offices.

The Process

1. Check eligibility

2. Complete application form

3. Landlord confirms eligibility

4. Receive an offer

5. Over to you – mortgage etc.,

6. Complete the purchase

Property Exemptions Certain types of property are exempt from Right to Buy: · Elderly persons dwelling · Dwelling houses for persons of pensionable age · Dwellings for disabled persons · Dwellings for people suffering from a mental disorder · Property scheduled for demolition

Eligibility of Tenants

Your current tenancy status must be secure in order to be eligible for the Right to Buy. Certain types of tenancies are not considered as secure for the purposes of Right to Buy, see Section 79 (2) and Schedule 1 amended of the Housing Act 1985 for a full list. Where a tenancy is in joint names, both tenants must be named and sign the Right to Buy application form regardless of whether they wish to join in the purchase or not.

Any tenant who is subject to one of the following orders will not be allowed to join in the Right to Buy:

  • Possession order with a fixed date - including Suspended Possession Order
  • Ground 2 criminal nuisance order
  • Right to Buy suspension order
  • Bankruptcy order
  • Demotion order

Eligibility of Family Members

Certain family members have the right to join in a Right to Buy application, even if they are not tenants. In order for a family member to exercise their right to join in the Right to Buy they must satisfy all 3 of the following requirements:

  • They must be; the spouse or civil partner of the tenant; or the tenant and that person live together as if they were husband and wife or civil partners, or the tenant’s parent, grandparent, child, grandchild, brother, sister, uncle, aunt, nephew or niece.
  • They must live at the property as their only or principal home.
  • They need to have been living at the property for 12 months immediately preceding the date of application.

Family members are automatically excluded from the Right to Buy if they are subject to any of the Court Orders detailed in the ‘Eligibility of Tenants’ section.

The Qualifying Period

The tenant will need to have been a public sector tenant for a minimum of 3 years (doesn’t have to be 3 years in a row).

The Discount

The longer you have been a tenant, the bigger the discount you get off the market value of the property, up to a maximum discount of £77,900. Discount levels for houses and flats start at 3 years of eligible tenancy increasing after 5 years from the starting point of 35% for houses and 50% for a flat, up to the maximum of 70% for both.

Examples of Discounts:

BUYING A HOUSE

Discounts start at 35% for 3 to 5 years, at year 6 add 1% for each extra year of tenancy up to 70% or the cash maximum of £77,900

Things to Consider

Once we have received your RTB application the Council will only carry out essential repairs and the property will be excluded from any planned maintenance projects, such as bathroom & kitchen replacements. Once the sale of the property is complete, you will be responsible for all repairs and maintenance.

Buying a home can be a long process and can take several months. Central Bedfordshire Council must process your application within certain timescales:

  • Confirmation of Eligibility – up to 4 weeks to reply (8 weeks if you have been with CBC for less than 5 years)
  • Send out an Offer Letter – 8 weeks for a house and 12 weeks for a flat

If CBC do not meet these timescales, or delay without reason, you could get a reduction in the sale price.

Applying for a Reduction

  • Fill in an ‘Initial notice of delay’ form (RTB6) and send it to CBC. We must either move the sale along within 1 month or send you a ‘counter notice’. The counter notice will say that we have already replied or explained why they can’t speed things up.
  • If you don’t get a response to your first notice within 1 month, you should complete an ‘Operative notice of delay’ form (RTB8) and send it to CBC. Once you have sent that, CBC may have to refund rent monies paid during the delay period – you must continue to pay rent. Any amount relevant to the delay period(s) will be deducted when the sale is completed.

To find out more about delay notices please visit Dealing with delays

Risks

Do not forget that if you fail to make repayments towards your mortgage there is a risk that your property will be repossessed.

How do I apply

Please read the booklet ' Your Right to Buy your home ' and the 'Right to Buy information Pack' (PDF 187KB) and complete the application form RTB 1. These can be downloaded from GOV.UK You can also contact the Housing Rents department on 0300 300 5541 and request an application pack. Return the completed form to the Housing Rents Department, Watling House, High Street North Dunstable, Bedfordshire. LU6 1LF.

What happens next?

On receipt of your application the Housing Rents Team will arrange for a valuation of your property (there is no charge for this service).  The valuation is less any discount you are entitled to, depending on how long you have been a tenant.  From 6th April 2015 the discount amount has been increased to £77,900.00.  You will have the right to challenge the valuation of the property which will be considered by the District Valuer.

What if I decide to go ahead?

If your application is accepted and you decide to go ahead with the purchase, you will need to arrange for a mortgage or alternative method of payment. You will also be liable for the general costs associated with purchasing a property including Stamp Duty, Solicitors fees and Land Registry fees.

Whilst your application is going through the Council will only carry out essential repairs to your property.  On completion of the sale of your property you will be responsible for all of the repairs and upkeep.  If you buy a flat you will have to pay towards any major works to the building and an annual service charge.

Can I sell my house
You can sell your house at any time, but if this is within five years of the purchase date you will be asked to repay a proportion of the percentage discount you received.
Council house - deeds

Where a property has been sold under the Right to Buy, the Council does not retain the title deeds.

If you have bought your property with a mortgage or loan, the deeds will be retained by your lender.

Your title deeds are important documents. If you hold the deeds to your property yourself, you should ensure that they are kept in a secure place.

Right to Buy Mobility Fund

What is the Right to Buy Mobility fund?

The Social Mobility Scheme is aimed at tenants who would like to buy a home under the Right to Buy but may find that their current home is not suitable for their needs. Under the Right to Buy Social Mobility Fund, tenants that are eligible for Right to Buy will have access to a cash payment of £30,000/40,000, in place of their Right to Buy discount on their current social property.

Am I eligible?

Eligibility Criteria

  • Older tenants (40 plus)
  • Tenants wishing to move closer to work opportunities 
  • Tenants who pass the Right to Buy affordability test but are unable to raise a mortgage because of issues with the property in which they live

Essential Criteria

  • Eligible for Right to Buy, for more details visit our website  
  • Applicants will need to show that the purchase of a property on the open market will be a sustainable one; they must have a clear rent account for the past 12 months, be purchasing a property that meets their current housing needs (all of the household must move into the new property), their existing home will be returned vacant to CBC and prove that they can re-pay a mortgage.  There must also be a commitment to achieving a move within the 2015-16 financial year.

How do I apply?

You should contact Kim Perry – Housing Rents Officer 0300 3005541, who will be able to advise on the individual application criteria.

Further information

  • Applicants living in one or two bedroom properties will be given priority as these are most in demand in the Central Bedfordshire area.
  • CBC’s will not support applications from sheltered housing schemes and other older persons designated homes as these homes that are not eligible for Right to Buy.
  • CBC will support people to buy a home anywhere in the UK. 
  • If approved, funds will then be released to the applicant’s solicitor on completion of the sale of the property and once the council property is empty.


Questions & Answers

Q1. What is the Social Mobility Scheme and how do I know if I am eligible?
The Social Mobility Scheme is aimed at tenants who would like to buy a home under the Right to Buy but may find that their current home is not suitable for their needs.


If your application is successful, CBC will give you a cash payment to put towards the cost of buying a property on the open market rather than using the Right to Buy to purchase your current home.   Tenants must give up their social rented property when they receive the cash payment and cannot return to social housing if they still own a property bought with support from the Fund.


Q2. I am eligible for the Right to Buy and CBC is offering the scheme. How will I know if I can apply?
You should contact Kim Perry – Housing Rents Officer 0300 3005541, who will be able to advise on the individual application criteria.


Q3. Are there other criteria I will be expected to meet?
You should make sure that you understand the responsibilities and costs involved in buying a home.  You will need to satisfy CBC that home ownership is currently affordable and likely to remain affordable in the longer term.


Q4. How much discount could I get?
Unlike Right to Buy, you will not receive a discount on the cost of your current home.  Instead you will receive a cash payment to put towards the cost of buying another property on the open market rather than using your Right to Buy.  The cash payment will be between £20,000 to £40,000.


Q5. Do I ever have to pay the money back?
No. The cash payment is not a loan but a grant so you will not be expected to pay it back.


Q6. If I apply, and I am eligible, will I definitely receive a cash payment?
Funding is limited but CBC should be able to advise on whether funding is still available when you apply.
