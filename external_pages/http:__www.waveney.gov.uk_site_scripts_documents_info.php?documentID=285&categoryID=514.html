What is Listed Building Consent?

You need Listed Building Consent to:

  • Demolish a listed building
  • Alter a listed building
  • Extend a listed building
  • Some repairs (the materials you use may seriously damage your property, if in doubt please ask).

Guide for owners and occupiers of Listed Buildings

How do I apply for Listed Building Consent?

Complete a listed building application form and include full details of your proposal including existing and proposed drawings, specifications, block plan location map and photographs if possible.

There is no fee, but it is advisable to seek assistance from an architect/surveyor in drawing up your proposals. (Listed building consent will be in addition to any planning permission and/or consent under the Building Regulations you need).

Most applications are successful, especially where negotiations take place beforehand.

What if the application is refused?

You may appeal to the Secretary of State if listed building consent is refused. There are serious penalties for carrying out unauthorised work.

What we ask of you

Seek our advice before carrying out work on listed buildings, provide clear and detailed information, complete application forms with full details when required.
