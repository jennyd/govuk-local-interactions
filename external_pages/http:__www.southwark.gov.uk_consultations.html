We Asked, You Said, We Did

Below are some of the issues we have recently consulted on and their outcomes.

Timed Waste Collections

We Asked:

We're introducing timed waste collections on Walworth Road to make it a cleaner and more pleasant area. We asked when would be the most convenient time for you to leave your rubbish out for collection.

You Said:

We received 65 responses. The most popular times were 8 - 9 in the morning (45%) and 6:30 - 7:30 in the afternoon (38%).

We Did:

We will start timed collections at the most popular times in January 2016. Residents and businesses affected by this change will receive more information shortly.

Westmoreland Road footway scheme

We Asked:

We want to improve the shopping parade on Westmoreland Road (off Walworth Road). We asked what you thought of our proposals to improve the footway.

You Said:

We received 103 responses: 93% supported the proposals, 4% opposed them and 3% had no opinion.

We Did:

The proposals were approved by Borough, Bankside & Walworth Community Council in September 2015. We are preparing a more detailed design based on the initial proposals and statutory consultation will start in January 2016.

Walworth Town Hall Design

We Asked:

We asked members of the community questions about which services they used in the Newington Library and how frequent they visited, as well as what they used the services for.

This enabled us to gain an idea of whether or not Avanti, the appointed architects achieved the vision through their stage one designs, what the community liked about them and what could be improved.

We also asked what made the visits to the library important and encouraged comments on the provision of facilities that would make the new Walworth Town Hall and Newington Library a better place for the community to visit.

You Said:

The feedback stated that a lot of thought went into the stage one design which reflected an overall sense of community. 

It also stated that design achieved the vision for the redevelopment as it integrated the different functions of the building, the new building design with the original features of the building, and the main entrance with the new Walworth Square.

We Did:

We have taken on board the feedback and are starting to look in more detail at how the council heritage services will operate and be displayed in the building. Will be consulting the community on this between November 2015 and January 2016.

So far in stage 2 we have designated study space, looked more closely at how the library and heritage services work together and at the circulation flows through the building for different users.

We are still only at concept stage and there is a lot more work and testing of spaces to be done as information about the infrastructure and service requirements is gained.  

If you have any queries about the project you can email regen.info@southwark.gov.uk.

 

During the consultation process, we ask for your feedback, you tell us and then we make positive changes. Public participation is key to our work. See what happens with We Asked, You Said, We Did.
