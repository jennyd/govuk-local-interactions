Speed limit signs

Last updated: 21/11/2014

Signs have to be erected where there is a change in speed limit. If the national speed limit applies and the road changes between dual and single carriageway the speed limit change does not have to be signed.

30 mph limit

Restricted roads and their associated 30 mph speed limits are established by the presence of a “system of lighting furnished by means of lamps placed not more than 200 yards apart” (s81 and 82 of the Road Traffic Regulations Act 1984). If such street lighting were removed, a new Traffic Regulation Order would be required to sustain the 30 mph limit on that section of road. Repeater signs cannot be used where the speed limit is 30mph and there is a system of street lighting in place.
