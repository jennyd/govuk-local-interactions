Apply for free school meals

Free school meals

If your child attends a state school and you are currently getting one of the following benefits, you will be able to claim free school meals.

  • Income Support (IS)
  • Income-based Jobseekers Allowance (JSAIB) (If you receive contribution-based Jobseekers Allowance you will not qualify.)
  • Support under Part VI of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999
  • Child Tax Credit (CTC) provided you do not receive Working Tax Credit and that your annual household income (as assessed by her Majesty's Revenue and Customs) does not exceed £16,190.
  • Guaranteed Pension Credit (GPC)
  • Income Related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Working Tax Credit 'run on' - payment you receive for a further 4 weeks after you stop qualifying for Working Tax Credit

Young people who receive Income Support (IS) or Income-based Jobseekers Allowance (IBJSA) in their own right are also entitled to receive free school meals at sixth form.
(NB: Children who attend a further education or tertiary college will need to contact them directly to request assistance.)

The Government does not allow us to recognise any other benefit other than those mentioned.

 Free school meals leaflet [1010kb]

How to claim

  • From September 2014 all Reception, Year 1 and Year 2 pupils are entitled to a free school meal.
  • This will be in addition to the current free school meal service currently offered to pupils whose parents are in receipt of qualifying benefits (shown above).
  • Even if you don't want your child in one of these year groups to receive a meal, we would encourage you to complete a form so that their School/Academy receives as much funding as possible from the Government.
  • It is important to register for free school meals as you could raise an extra £1320 to help fund valuable support.
  • You can include other children in year 3 and above on this form if you receive the qualifying benefit

Please note: if you wish to apply for free school meals for a child attending  BOURTON ON THE WATER PRIMARY school, you should apply directly to the school.

Download an application form:

  •  Free school meals application form [88kb]

Or:

  • Pick up a form from Shire Hall, Westgate Street, Gloucester GL1 4UT
  • Phone us on 01452 425390 and we will send you a form by post

When you claim we will check that you are eligible with the Department of Education.

Change of circumstances

You must inform us if your circumstances change. For example, if you no longer receive benefits, move house or change school as this may affect your right to claim.

If your child is currently receiving free school meals at their primary school and transferring to secondary school in September 2014 you do not need to reapply. Entitlement will transfer automatically to their new school unless your financial circumstances change; if so you will be notified if they are no longer entitled.

Are over 16s entitled to free school meals?

The Education Funding Agency has confirmed that from September 2014 all 16-18 year-old learners who are, or whose parents are, in receipt of the benefits listed at the top of this page can apply for free school meals.

To be eligible for a free meal, students must be enrolled in further education provision funded via the Education Funding Agency at:

  • general further education colleges, including specialist colleges sixth form colleges
  • commercial and charitable providers
  • higher education institutions (HEIs) with 16 to 19 funding from the EFA
  • independent specialist providers (ISPs)
  • local authorities (LAs) and FE institutions directly funded for 16 to 19s
  • Skills Funding Agency (SFA) only traineeship 16 to 18 providers
  • European Social Fund (ESF) only institutions
  • 16 to 19 academies and free schools

Learners who are aged 19-25 years old who have a Learning Difficulty Assessment (LDA) or an Education Health and Care Plan (EHC Plan) are also entitled to free meals under this scheme.

All eligible 16-18 year-old learners who attend a sixth form at a school/academy can apply using the Free School Meals Application Form above.  For all 16-18 learners who attend other colleges/institutions please contact them for more information on how to apply.

Assistance with the cost of school uniforms

The County Council will no longer administer school uniform grants after 31 March 2014, when applications for the 2013/4 academic year are closed. The administration of assistance with the cost of school uniforms is being transferred to schools/academies from 1 April 2014. Parents/carers should contact the secondary school/academy that the pupil will be attending for further information regarding the eligibility criteria, which is linked to free school meals entitlement.
