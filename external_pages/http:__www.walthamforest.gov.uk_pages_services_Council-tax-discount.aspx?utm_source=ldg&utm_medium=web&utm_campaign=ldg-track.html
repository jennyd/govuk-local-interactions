Council Tax discounts, reductions and exemptions

Last updated 18 January 2016

Who can get a discount, exemption, reduction or relief

You may be able to claim a discount, exemption, reduction or relief if you:

  • live on your own and aged 18 or over

  • live with people not counted for Council Tax

  • have certain adaptations in your home for a person with a disability

  • are on a low income or benefits

  •  are in severe financial hardship

If you live on your own and are aged 18 or over, you’re entitled to 25% off your Council Tax bill.

 
Fill in our Single Person Discount application form
 

People not counted as liable to pay Council Tax

Council Tax discounts are calculated based on the number of adults in a property. However, some people such as students and care workers are "not counted" (disregarded) when we work out how many adults people live in a property.

If only 1 of the adults living in your home is counted, you can get a 25% discount. For example, a house with 1 care worker and one other adult would get this discount.

If all the adults living in your home aren’t counted, you can get a 50% discount.  For example, a house of 4 apprentices or a house with 1 carer and 1 student would get this discount.

People who aren’t counted as adults
 

Select the link below to apply for the situation that applies to you, otherwise contact the Council Tax team.

You won’t be counted if you:

  • are a student enrolled in a full time course for at least academic year

  • have a severe mental impairment

  • are the second adult in a home where the other adult is currently being detained in prison, hospital or other place of detention

  • are aged 18-20 and receiving Child Benefit

  • are a school or college leaver under 20 years who will leave school or college between 1 May and 31 October in any year – the disregard will last until 1 November)

  • are an apprentice or youth training trainee

  • have had to leave your home empty or with one adult because you have moved into a care home or hostel

  • have had a long stay in hospital

  • provide care and support (or both) to another person on behalf of a local authority or charity or to someone on certain benefits

  • are a member of religious community

  • are a member of visiting defence forces, foreign defence organisation or international headquarters

  • a non-British spouse of a student

Reductions for people with disabilities

You may be entitled to a reduction if your home has the following adaptations for an adult or child with a disability:

  • A room, excluding a bathroom, kitchen or toilet, used mainly by the person with a disability; or
  • A second bathroom or kitchen for the needs of the person with a disability; or
  • Extra floor space inside your home for using a wheelchair

Your bill will be reduced to the next band down. For example, if your home is in band D, your bill will be reduced to the amount for a band C property.


Apply for reductions for people with a disability
 

Discount when you leave your property to live in work accommodation ​

If you have to leave your property empty but furnished because you’re put in accommodation related to your employment, you may be entitled to 50% off your Council Tax bill.

You’ll need to give us confirmation from your employer that your accommodation is linked to your job. To apply for this discount complete our online form below.

Leaving your property to live in work accommodation
 
Apply for a Council Tax discount for an empty, furnished property
 

Exemptions - properties that aren’t charged Council Tax

In some cases, a property is exempt from Council Tax. This means you don’t have to pay anything for the time it qualifies for exemption.

Find out which types of properties are exempt and how to apply.

Council Tax Support

Council Tax Support is a local scheme to help you pay your council tax if you are on benefits or a low income, whether or not you are working.  

Council Tax Extreme Hardship scheme

If you are in severe financial hardship through no fault of your own, you can apply for a reduction from the Council Tax Extreme Hardship scheme. This budget is limited so the help available will be for a limited periods and amounts.
