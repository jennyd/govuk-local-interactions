Applying for a council or housing association home

We have brought in some changes to our Housing Allocations Policy.

You can find out more about the changes by downloading our leaflet  changes to our housing allocations policy (PDF 362 KB).

Or, you can download our  Housing Allocations Policy - 2015 (PDF 509KB)

What is the housing register?

The housing register is our register of people who would like to live in a council or housing association home. To apply to be on the housing register you must be 18 or over.

If we accept you on our active register you will be able to view and apply for properties that you are eligible for.

Applying for properties is often referred to as ‘bidding’ on properties. If you are not on the register you are not able to bid.

To apply and bid for properties please use our website: www.movingwithdacorum.org.uk 

Properties are offered based on housing need. We represent housing need by awarding points.

Social and housing association housing is in very high demand and there is not enough to house everyone who would like this type of housing. If you are thinking about applying to the housing register we suggest that you speak to our housing options team to get a realistic idea of how likely you are to be made an offer of housing. If this is not a realistic housing option for you, you can get more advice on meeting your housing need.

For more information, you can download our leaflets  your housing register application (PDF 374 KB) and  how to bid for council and housing association homes (PDF 430 KB).

Frequently asked questions about the housing register

  • I am 16 years old. Can I apply to the housing register?

    You can apply to the housing register but you are unlikely to get a home through this route, unless you are regarded as being part of a high-risk priority group, for example you are leaving care.

    You may want to think about your other housing options. For example, staying at home and saving to move into a privately rented home. This would give you more choice over where you live.

  • What does ‘bidding’ or ‘placing a bid’ mean?

    If you ‘bid’ for a property, your name goes onto a shortlist of people who want to live in that particular property. At the end of the properties bidding cycle, whoever is at the top of the shortlist (has the most points) will be allocated the property. 

    You can place a bid:

    • Online at www.movingwithdacorum.org.uk 
    • By telephone – 0300 111 3570
    • By text – 07786 201131

    There is no limit to the number of properties you can bid on in the bidding cycle. 

    You can only bid on properties that you are eligible for. Restrictions to bidding may include:

    • Size of property (number of bedrooms)
    • Village lettings 
    • Application type

  • Can you help me use Moving with Dacorum?

    We can offer you support if you are having difficulties using the Moving with Dacorum website.

    To make it easier to use the Moving with Dacorum website, please see the leaflet  How to bid for council and housing association homes (PDF 430 KB).  

    If you need further advice you can telephone us on 01442 228000, or email us at housingoptions@dacorum.gov.uk

    If you cannot fill in the online housing register application form, paper forms are available from the Civic Centre in Hemel Hempstead.

  • I have an application on Moving with Dacorum and my address has changed. What should I do?

    Log in to Moving with Dacorum with your application number and password and then change your details.

  • I can’t log in to Moving with Dacorum (I forgot my application number or password). What should I do?

    Please call 01442 228000 and one of our customer service representatives will be able to reset this for you.

  • What if I am not happy with the decision made about my housing register application?

    If you feel that the points we have given you are wrong, please check your application form to ensure that you have included all the relevant information.

    You can also ask for a senior officer review. You will need to request a review in writing within 21 days from the date of your letter stating your points.

  • If I accept a private tenancy can I stay on the housing register and bid for properties?

    There is no connection between help to rent or any other private sector scheme and the Council’s Housing Register. You can apply to the Housing Register as well as our help to rent scheme.

  • How many properties can I bid for each week?

    There is no limit to the number of properties you can bid on. However, you should still restrict your bidding to properties you would be happy to accept if offered. Refusing a property you have bid upon will count towards one of your three reasonable refusals.

  • Can I withdraw my bid once it’s been accepted?

    You can withdraw any bid within the period of the advert cycle by going to your ‘my account’ page on www.movingwithdacorum.org.uk

    Or phone 0300 111 3570. You will hear a list of numbered properties that you have bid on (for example, property one: 9591). To cancel a bid you must enter this number on your telephone keypad, followed by the hash key (e.g. 9591#).

    You will be read the property reference number and your current queue position. Press 1 if you are sure you want to cancel, press 2 to exit. 

  • What happens if I bid for a property and receive an offer but I have changed my mind?

    You have a choice of which properties you bid for. Therefore we expect you to accept a property offer, unless it is considered unreasonable. A property offer would be considered unreasonable if living in that property would cause harm to your health. An offer would not be considered unreasonable if you do not like the property or if you have changed your mind.

    If you refuse three reasonable property offers you will be placed on the deferred register. The first and second time you refuse a reasonable property offer, you will be informed in writing of the consequences of refusing a third time. 

    If you are placed on the deferred register for refusing a property offer you will be given the opportunity to appeal the decision. 

  • How do I find out where I am on the shortlist?

    Before you place a bid on Moving with Dacorum, you will be able to see where you would be placed on the shortlist for that property. Your position is likely to change as other applicants place bids. You can track your position on your Moving with Dacorum account or by calling 0300 111 3570.

    We will only tell you if your bid is successful. However, we do provide weekly reports of the number of bids made on each property and the number of points the successful bidder had. These are available in the Moving with Dacorum newsletter and on the Moving with Dacorum website.

  • Why do I keep dropping down the shortlist?

    On the housing register you are given points depending on your situation and your current housing need. Those with greater need receive more points.

    If you are bidding for properties but other people have more points, it is because they have a greater housing need. If you think the points you have do not reflect your situation or if your situation has changed, please change your housing register application on the Moving with Dacorum website or contact the housing options team on 01442 228000.

    If you have a low number of points, you may want to consider your other housing options.

  • I’ve seen an empty property. When will it appear on Moving with Dacorum?

    When a council house becomes empty, we work hard to get it ready for someone new to move in. Sometimes a property needs a lot of work, so we do not always know how long it will take. In some cases we make direct offers to people in emergencies, so not all homes that are empty will appear on Moving with Dacorum.

    The empty homes that you can bid on will be updated every week.

  • I need an adapted property, how long will I have to wait to get housed?

    Depending on your housing need, you may have to wait a long time for an appropriate home. Have you considered making adaptations to your current home? 

    If you need particular adaptations to a home and the home you bid on has already been adapted, you will be favoured for the property. However, if there is someone who needs the same adaptations and has more points than you, then they will be preferred.   

  • What happens next if I get an offer?

    In most cases you will hear within two weeks if your bid was successful. However, there are situations where it may take longer to inform successful bidders. Before we make an offer of accommodation, we may visit you at home to check your points. 

    You will not be notified if your bid is unsuccessful. However, we do provide weekly reports of the number of bids made on each property and the number of points the successful bidder had.

  • How can I move into the property straight away if I have no furniture?

    We do not provide furniture, but there are a few suppliers who provide inexpensive items within Dacorum.

    • Paradise Furniture Warehouse is a not for profit organisation which is run by Community Action Dacorum and provides reconditioned second hand furniture to customers on means tested benefit. Contact Wendy Dove on 01442 263197. 
    • DENS Charity Shop and Furniture Warehouse sells second hand furniture. They are open Monday - Friday 10am - 2pm (and by appointment at other times) at The Hub, Paradise, Hemel Hempstead, HP2 4TF. 
    • The British Heart Foundation furniture shop in Marlowes, Hemel Hempstead sells second hand furniture.

  • We are private tenants and our landlord will not carry out maintenance work. Our home is in disrepair, will we get extra points?

    You will be given points if the property is seen to be in a state of minor disrepair (and/or sharing kitchen or bathroom facilities) or in a state of major disrepair (and/or lacking kitchen or bathroom facilities). More points will be awarded to those living in homes considered to be in a state of major disrepair.

    This will be confirmed by a local authority Environmental Health officer and your landlord will be given an opportunity to improve the property before extra points are awarded.

  • The gas appliance in my house may be dangerous. Can I get more points on the housing register?

    You can get a Gas Safe Registered technician to assess your appliance.

    We use the Housing Health and Safety Rating System to assess the extent of hazards in a property. This system is used to determine whether a home is in a state of disrepair.

    The disrepair will need to be confirmed by a local authority Environmental Health officer and your landlord will be given an opportunity to improve the property before any points are awarded.

    You will be given points if the property is seen to be in a state of ‘minor’ disrepair (and/or sharing kitchen or bathroom facilities) or in a state of ‘major’ disrepair (and/or lacking kitchen or bathroom facilities). More points will be awarded to those living in homes considered to be in a state of major disrepair.

  • Will the council pay my rent if I don’t move into my property straight away?

    If you are offered a property, you will be given a tenancy start date. You will be expected to pay rent from that day. If you cannot, you will accrue rent arrears. If you are paying rent somewhere else, you may have to pay rent on both properties if you do not leave in a timely manner. If you have to give four weeks’ notice, housing benefit may be able to pay dual benefits during this time.

    The only exception to this is if there is an issue at the property we are responsible for. If that is the case, we will move you to temporary accommodation and give you rent credit while we resolve the issue.

  • What are points?

    Points are used to represent your level of housing need. The more points you have, the higher your priority for housing will be.

    We give points for a number of different categories including: 

    • overcrowding
    • medical conditions
    • care and support needs
    • the condition of your current property.
