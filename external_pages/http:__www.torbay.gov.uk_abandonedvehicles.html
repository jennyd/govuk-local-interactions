Abandoned Vehicles

  • Report - Abandoned Vehicle


Torbay Council has a duty to remove abandoned vehicles under the Refuse Disposal (Amenity) Act 1978. The Council investigates vehicles to ascertain whether they consider them to be abandoned or not, and issues removal notices to registered keepers/land owners when it is deemed by the abandoned vehicles officer that a vehicle has been abandoned.

What is an abandoned vehicle?

Abandoned vehicles often get confused with nuisance vehicles. A nuisance vehicle is not necessarily abandoned and often cannot be removed by the authority. Common ‘nuisance’ vehicles are:
  • Poorly parked
  • Causing an obstruction
  • Involved in residential parking disputes
  • Broken down
  • Untaxed
If a vehicle is untaxed but not abandoned, the local authority has no powers to remove it from the highway. In this instance, please contact the DVLA  to report the vehicle.
To assist with identifying vehicles that may be abandoned, the following checklist could be used:
  • Is the vehicle untaxed?
  • Are any of the tyres flat or wheels been removed?
  • Is there litter or detritus under the vehicle, indicating that it hasn’t been moved for some time?
  • Are any of the windows or windscreen broken?
  • Is there any mould on the inside or outside of the vehicle?
  • Does the vehicle contain items of waste e.g. tyres, old newspapers, general rubbish?
  • Does the vehicle have number plates?
  • Has the vehicle been vandalised? Exterior vandalism may include graffiti or dents, interior could be seats slashed or the radio removed.
  • Is the vehicle one that your neighbours or you have never seen before and suddenly appears in your road with no-one claiming ownership?

Reporting an abandoned vehicle

If you suspect a vehicle has been abandoned you should collect the following information before reporting it:
  • Vehicle make, model and colour.
  • Vehicle registration number (if known – it may be on the tax disc if the number plates have been removed).
  • Condition of the vehicle.
  • Presence or not of the tax disc and whether it has expired (tax disc expiry details are available online at Gov UK: Vehicle Enquiry )
  • Location of the vehicle – be as specific as you can. Also note if the vehicle has been left in a location that may cause obstruction or danger to pedestrians or other motorists.
  • How long the vehicle has been in its current location.

Report - Abandoned Vehicle

Please note that you will be required to provide your name and address, as anonymous reports will not be investigated.
If during the investigations an owner claims a vehicle that otherwise appears to be abandoned, the Council may not be able to remove it.

Abandoned vehicle investigation process

Once a report of a potentially abandoned vehicle has been received, an initial inspection will be carried out where the vehicles condition and location will be recorded, and then monitored. There is no specific timescale that can be given as each vehicle will be dealt with on its own merits depending on its condition, but generally the registered keeper of the vehicle will be written to advising that the vehicle has been reported as abandoned, and that the Council intend to issue a removal notice within a certain period if no contact is received from them.
Once the Council officer is satisfied that the vehicle has been abandoned, a removal notice will then be issued. The length of time given on the removal notice will depend on the location and condition of the vehicle, please see Removal notice periods for further guidance.
The vehicle will then continue to be monitored during the time period on the removal notice, if the vehicle remains in place and no contact is received from the registered keeper then the Council will look to remove the vehicle once the notice period has expired.

Removal notice periods

The amount of notice period given depends upon the vehicles location, and also the current condition of the vehicle.
Vehicles abandoned on the highway will usually be issued with a seven day notice which will be affixed to the vehicle, and a letter will also be sent to the registered keeper of the vehicle. However, in circumstances where the Council Officer who deals with abandoned vehicles deems that the vehicle poses a danger to members of the public, removal may take place within 24 hours.
For vehicles abandoned on private land a 15 day notice will be served on the land owner in the form of a letter, a removal notice may also be affixed to the apparently abandoned vehicle with a further letter being sent to the registered keeper of the vehicle informing them of the action taken. Removal will take place following the expiration of the 15 days notice, unless the landowner submits written representations within this period objecting to the removal of the vehicle. Alternatively if the land owner confirms in writing that they wish the vehicle to be removed after being served with the removal notice, then the Council may carry out immediate removal.

How do I retrieve an abandoned vehicle that has been recovered by the Council?

If your vehicle has been removed by Torbay Council you can call us to confirm its status, however, please note that vehicles are only generally held for a period of seven days prior to them being disposed of.

Charges due following removal, storage and disposal of abandoned vehicles

There are charges prescribed in ‘The Removal, Storage and Disposal of Vehicles (Prescribed Sums and Charges) Regulations 2008’ that can be applied to any abandoned vehicles that are removed, stored or disposed of by the Council. The charges vary dependant on the type of vehicle that is being removed, and also where the vehicle is being removed from. The standard charges for removing a vehicle from highway would be as follows:
Type of ChargeCarsMotorbikes
Removal charge£150£150
Storage charge per day£20£10
Disposal charge£75£50

Legislation

The legislation that allows local authorities to inspect, remove and dispose of abandoned vehicles is as follows:
  • Refuse Disposal (Amenity) Act 1978
  • Road Traffic Regulation Act 1984
  • Removal and Disposal of Vehicles Regulations 1986
  • Clean Neighbourhoods and Environment Act 2005
  • The Removal, Storage and Disposal of Vehicles (Prescribed Sums and Charges) Regulations 2008
