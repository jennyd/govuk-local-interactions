Dealing with dogs

Our community protection enforcement officers oversee matters relating to dogs, in particular stray dogs and fouling.

Lost and stray dogs

Herefordshire Council has the responsibility for stray dogs within the county. We have a dog warden service who operate weekdays only during hours (9am – 5pm) and we will collect if necessary, stray dogs which have been contained or tethered.

To report a stray/lost dog please call the number shown below and all details will be recorded. We have a reception centre (kennels) that are open to receive dogs taken in by a member of the public as a stray. Arrangements to take a dog to the kennels can be made by appointment between the hours of 9am and 9pm. 

Contact us by the following:

  • Telephone: 01432 261761
  • Write to: Community Protection Team, Blue School House, Blueschool Street, Hereford, HR1 2ZB

I have found a stray dog what do I need to do now?

A stray dog is any dog that appears to be without its owner and is straying on public or other land.

If you find what you believe to be a stray dog then first check to see if it has any identification showing the owners details. If you are able to make contact with the owner then arrange for them to collect it from you. If it has no identification please contact us on 01432 261761.

Before calling please make a note of some details regarding the dog. This will help us make an accurate record of the dog and will enable us to match the owner to the dog if it is reported as lost .

Please include details such as:

  • Breed of the dog (if you are not sure, then how big is it? And what does it look like?)
  • Male or female dog – if not sure then please advise
  • What colour is the dog?
  • Is the dog wearing a collar? Does the collar have any identification? (for example a tag or phone number on it) 
  • Has the dog any distinguishing marks or scars – docked tail?
  • Is the dog injured in any way?
  • What is the condition of the dog?
  • Where and when did you find the dog?

If you are unable to take the dog to the kennels the community protection team will be notified that you have found a dog and will get in touch to arrange collection (during office hours Monday to Friday only). Please be patient as the enforcement officers cover the whole county and they could be some distance from you, but they will endeavour to get to you as soon as possible.

What happens to a stray dog?

Our holding kennels will keep the dog until either the owner comes forward to collect or until the dog has been in our care for seven days. After seven clear days the dog will be passed through to our re-homing facility.

What happens if I find a dog out of office hours?

Our office hours are 9am to 5pm (Monday to Friday)

The open hours for the holding kennels are 8am to 9pm seven days a week (closed on Christmas Day)

If you take possession of a dog please call our out of hours service on 01432 260000 and log the details with them as they may get a call from the owners who will be looking for their dog.

When you call please have the details of the dog ready to pass to the operator. When you report that you have a dog in your possession you will be advised of the contact number of the council's reception centre (kennels) and asked to contact them to arrange a time when you can take the dog to the kennels where it will be treated as a stray.

If you are unable to take the dog to the kennels the community protection team will be notified and they will contact you the next working day to arrange collection.

Out of hours telephone number 01432 260000 – this will divert to a call centre who will record all the details.

Please note: 

  • We do not operate an out of hours collection service
  • The kennels will not collect a dog from you 

What if the dog has been injured and requires urgent medical treatment ?

In emergency situations or if an animal requires urgent medical assistance we have a contract with:

  • Brookfield Vets, 1 Old Eign Hill, Hereford. Telephone 01432 357222

(Please note any owner who claims a dog following veterinary treatment will be liable for any costs incurred)  

What if I have lost my dog?

If you have lost your dog then contact us on 01432 261761.

We will need a description of your dog, your contact details and where you lost your dog. You may also like to call the local veterinary practices and animal rescue centres as the person who found the dog may have contacted these first.

If your dog is seized as a stray and is being held at our holding kennels you will be required to pay a fee before the animal will be released to you.

For more information on how to be a responsible dog owner please visit the Defra website. 

How do I report dog fouling?

To request the clean-up of dog mess, please contact us on:

  • Online: Contact us form
  • Telephone: 01432 261761

If you fail to remove your dog's faeces, you may be issued with a Dog Control Order. This could mean an £80 fixed penalty notice, or even prosecution. 

There is no excuse for failing to clean up after your dog. We will take a robust approach when offences are committed.

How do I report dangerous dogs?

A dog is legally defined as dangerous if there are grounds for reasonable apprehension that it will injure any person.

A dog can be dangerous because of:

  • Behaviour, like growling, barking or running at a person
  • Location, such as a stray dog in a school playground, or on a busy road

If you think a dog may be dangerous then you must report it immediately - dial 999.

If it is not an emergency please call the dog warden service on 01432 261761 during office hours or, at other times, your local police office.

If you're reporting a dangerous dog, please tell us:

  • location and, where appropriate, direction of travel
  • time seen
  • description and breed
  • reason why you think it is dangerous 
  • owner's name and address if known
  • your own contact details
  • any other relevant information, e.g. dog injured

Banned types of dogs

If you believe the dog is one of the banned breeds in the list below or you know of the location of one of these dogs, please contact West Mercia Police on 0300 333 3000 and ask to speak to the dog section.

  • Pit Bull Terrier
  • Japanese Tosa
  • Dogo Argentino
  • Fila Brasileiro

How do I report noise from dogs?

The environmental health team investigate noise nuisance caused by dogs.
