Overview

Nationally lots of information is available about social care. In Surrey we understand that there are times when people need extra support to do every day things because of age, frailty, health condition, disability or changing home circumstance. You may also be a carer and need some help or a break from caring. Local Authority have a duty to assess a person in need of care and support. They may need care and support because of serious illness, physical disability, learning disability, mental health problems or frailty because of old age.

The local authority is obliged to carry out a needs assessment when they become aware that someone maybe in need of care and support 

The Care Act 2014 makes clear the local authorities must provide or arrange services that help to prevent people developing care and support needs, or delay people deteriorating to the point where they would need on going care and support. Even if you don't want or need financial assistance with your care, your local authority can still help you plan your care or the person you care for so it is worth contacting Adult Social Services of Surrey County Council to find out the options available to you and your family. For more information go to the national website.

Often other family members can help out, or friends or neighbours who live nearby are happy to lend a hand. You can use Surrey Information Point to search for local care and support services, including organisations, community and faith groups who do a great deal to support people in their own communities, offering assistance with, for example, transport, cooking, cleaning and shopping.

We recognise that people are happier and healthier when in their own community and various options are available to help you remain safe and independent for as long as possible, if this is what you would like. If you do feel you need to move from your home the Care and Support Services in Surrey, page identifies various options for you to consider depending on your needs and risk. 

Finding out more

If you are not sure where to start you could visit one of the local Surrey Hubs and meet face to face with someone who will help you understand the options open to you. You can also speak to an adult social care advisor on 0300 200 1005. Alternatively you can use SMS: 07527 182861 or Fax: 020 8541 7390. Our helpline is manned Monday to Friday, 8am to 6pm. You can also make a comment or enquiry online via our online enquiry form.

Carers provide unpaid care by looking after ill, or older person  or a person who has a disability including someone with a mental illness. This includes a disabled family member or friend or partner. Parents and grandparents of a disabled child are also termed as Carers as are children and young adults under 18 who provide unpaid care. Your local carers support scheme is there to help you. For local details and to see what support people in a caring role can get visit the Surrey Information Point website.

One Stop Surrey is a great new service you can use to get the right kind of support or service you need at home to live and age well. It could be domestic help, access to a befriending scheme to prevent loneliness or free loft insulation. Complete the online form. Based on the responses you provide the best placed organisation will get in touch with you and put in place your support.

If you think you may need some equipment for your home to make things easier for you or a loved one, try our online equipment assessment. It helps you find the exact equipment to suit you and directs you to retailers.

Alternatively, a telecare sensor could keep you safe and independent at home with the assurance that there's someone at the end of the phone if you need them, 24/7. A fire safety check is also recommended for people living alone or who are more at risk of fire.

Many people need help accessing extra money/benefits to help them with their situation. GetWise is a consortium of organisations that can help you maximise your income.

Some residents may benefit from independent financial advice around care choices especially those who already pay for their own care without council support. People often make a decision to move into a residential home too early or without understanding all the options available to them. We strongly recommend speaking to an independent financial adviser. Check out the Society of Later Life Advisors (SOLLA) website for local Surrey advisers. Please note that some services they provide are free, others are chargeable.

For people concerned about memory loss they should visit a local wellbeing centre for specialist support and access to appropriate services.

The eleven local borough and district councils provide a range of support and services to help older and disabled people manage daily things. For example, meals on wheels, community transport, aids and adaptations for the home to help people move around safely.

Keeping up to date

If you have a registered disability you may like to sign up to the Surrey Disability Register. Membership will give you a newsletter twice a year, discounts to certain activities, support during an emergency such as extreme weather, invitations to consultations and events and an opportunity to network with other local residents.

Or sign up to receive a copy of our Living and Ageing Well newsletter by emailing us at livingandageingwell@surreycc.gov.uk.

When and how Adult Social Care can help

There's a huge choice of support available provided by many organisations in Surrey.

The Adult Social Care service at the county council is here to support people who either live or care for someone in Surrey. Even if you care for someone in a different part of the UK you may be still eligible for support as a Carer. Sometimes we provide short term support to help you get back to full fitness or mobility after a hospital stay or recover from mental ill health.

We speak to individuals most in need of our support and ask them about the areas that matter to them and that would make the greatest impact on their lives. They then choose the support (with or without our help, or that of a carer of family member) to achieve those outcomes and we regularly review if the support is making a difference to their wellbeing. If you are unable to tell us what you need due to mental capacity or communication difficulties we can arrange an independent advocate to help you share your views.

Carers of all ages have a right to an assessment of their needs. To arrange an assessment, please contact the Adult Social Care helpline.

If there are any children in the household affected by the caring situation, they may be a 'young carer'. Surrey Young Carers supports young carers under 18, and offer a wide range of carer and support options aimed at young people.'

Will I have to pay?

The council may or may not go on to provide support; it could be provided by another organisation or person but we will work out a cost that is used to pay for the support. Depending on the services you receive there may be a charge incurred; the amount will depend on your capital and income. A financial assessment will take place early on to decide if you pay towards your care. This is a standard process that takes place across the country. Carers are not charged for services they receive directly and disabled people are not charged for equipment or minor adaptations to their property.

Can I complain or appeal if I do not agree with a decision?

Yes. If you have concerns about the decision we have taken, we would ask you, in the first instance, to contact the person (or team) you have been dealing with to let them know what your concerns are and ask them to review the decision.

For information about the complaints and appeals process see How to make a complaint about adult social care. You can also contact the Adult Social Care Customer Relations Team for information and advice about the complaints process. The contact details are:

Adult Social Care Customer Relations Team, Surrey County Council, Millmead House, Millmead, Guildford, Surrey GU2 4BB, e-mail: asc.customerrelations@surreycc.gov.uk.
