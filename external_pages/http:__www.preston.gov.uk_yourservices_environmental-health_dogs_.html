Dog Control

We deal with complaints from members of the public about dog related matters.

We investigate complaints about:

  • Dog fouling
  • Stray dogs (including frequently roaming dogs)
  • Lost dogs 
  • Dogs not on a lead
  • Less serious incidents of dog aggression (those that do not include injury to humans)

The Dog Control Team actively monitor parks, open spaces and areas known to be used by dog walkers. This is to educate, advise and, in some circumstances, enforce against dog owners who are not complying with the laws surrounding responsible dog ownership.  For more information please see our dog laws in Preston page.

We also play an important role in promoting responsible dog ownership by:

  • Providing free microchipping events
  • Organising educational events at local schools, community centres and organising events such as Bark 4 Life
  • Empower local residents to make a difference by becoming Canine Volunteers and support them to address irresponsible dog ownership in Preston
  • Providing information to dog owners on our website

New dog microchipping laws

On 1 April 2016 every dog in England must be micorchipped. The council will have powers to check that dogs are microchipped and if necessary fine owners who haven't microchipped their dogs as well as force owners to get their dogs chipped. For more information go to our microchipping section.
