Adult Social Care: assessing your needs

Information about the assessment process and eligibility criteria for adults in Social Services

We aim to help people who need extra support to access services and live independently. As only a small number of people with high levels of need will be eligible for direct help from Social Services, we also provide a wide range of ideas and information about the advice and support available from other organisations. 

Before Social Services can provide any support service we need to carry out an assessment of the needs of the person for whom the service is requested.  This means we talk to you about the difficulties you are experiencing so that we understand how they affect your day to day life.  We can then work out what sort of support would be most helpful for you, and whether you qualify for help from Social Services. 

We may be able to address some straightforward needs after a short assessment, perhaps over the telephone, but for more complex needs and for long-term support a full professional assessment will be required.

This assessment is an opportunity for you, and perhaps also any relatives or friends who care for you, to talk to a social care professional about the kinds of things you feel you need help with.  Colleagues from the health service may also be involved in the assessment process. We can then:

  • assess the degree of need
  • work out whether a service can be provided
  • discuss what service(s) would be most helpful.

There is high demand for the services and support we provide. Unfortunately we are not able to provide support to everyone who asks for it.  To make sure our resources go to those in greatest need we use a set of rules called Eligibility Criteria for Social Services Support  to work out who we are able to help and what help we are able to provide.  These rules are set for us by the Welsh Government.  

If you are not eligible for our support we will give you advice and information about other ways that your needs could be met.

How to ask for an assessment

To ask for an assessment for an older adult or an adult with a physical disability contact:

Intake Team for Health and Social Care on 01792 636519

or complete the Adult Services: Online Referral Form

For an assessment for an adult with a sensory loss contact the Sensory Services Team on 01792 315969.

For an assessment for an adult with a learning disability contact the Community Support Team on 01792 614100.

For an assessment for an adult with mental health difficulties contact the Community Mental Health Team for the area of Swansea in which you live.

For more about the assessment process go to Getting help from Social Services: A guide to assessment and eligibility for adults. You can also download/print this information as a factsheet  Getting help from Social Services (Factsheet 001) (PDF, 44kb)Opens new window .
