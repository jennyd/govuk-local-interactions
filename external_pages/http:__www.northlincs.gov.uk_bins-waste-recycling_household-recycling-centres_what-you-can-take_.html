What you can take

Find out what can be taken to our Household Recycling Centres. Please be aware that charges apply for some materials.

Residents of Lincolnshire County Council can only use the household recycling centres in Barnetby or Kirton in Lindsey. Facilities for plasterboard, tyres, asbestos and gas bottles are not available at these sites and residents are advised to contact their local council for information on how to dispose of these materials.

  • Scunthorpe Household Recycling Centre only

Asbestos can only be taken to the Household Recycling Centre at certain times of the day and have strict rules on how to dispose of this waste see below for further details. 

Please note residents of Lincolnshire County Council can only use the Barnetby or Kirton in Lindsey Household Recycling Centres, and are advised to contact their local council for information on how to dispose of gas bottles, and asbestos. 

  • Asbestos

  • Expand

Asbestos is potentially dangerous and needs to be handled with care. Please read the guidance on how to handle and bag up your asbestos [PDF, 15Kb] before you handle asbestos.

We only accept asbestos at the Household Recycling Centre, Cottage Beck Road, Scunthorpe and you will need to request a bag to dispose of your asbestos. 

Apply for a bag for asbestos disposal

Alternatively, you can contact the Customer Contact Centre to order a bag.

Residents will be sent two bags (one red and one clear) to double bag their asbestos, a letter authorising them to dispose of the asbestos and a handling asbestos guidance sheet. These will be sent within two to five working days.

The following criteria must apply:

  • Asbestos must have originated from your own property

  • That it is bonded white asbestos (chrysotile). No blue (crocidolite), brown (amosite) or fibrous asbestos will be accepted under any circumstances

  • The maximum weight accepted per request is 20kg

  • The total amount of asbestos to be disposed of must fit into the red bag and sealed,  then the red bag placed into the clear bag and sealed, resulting in double bagged waste

  • Asbestos will only be accepted at the Household Recycling Centre, Cottage Beck Road, Scunthorpe during the following times:

    • From 1 March to 31 October: 10am to 4pm

    • From 1 November to 28/29 February: 10am to 2pm

  • You must report to a site attendant on arrival at the Household Recycling Centre during the asbestos disposal times. Any asbestos taken to the site outside of the disposal times will be refused

  • You must hand the letter of authorisation to the site attendant on arrival at the Household Recycling Centre, asbestos can only be deposited with this original letter

  • You  must be able to handle the asbestos waste from your  own vehicle into the designated skip as site attendants  are not permitted to assist in moving asbestos waste

  • Any split, torn, damaged or unsealed bags of asbestos will be refused and you will need to reapply

  • Only correctly double bagged material in the bags provided by the council will be accepted

  • You are only permitted to make two deposits per year (1 April to 31 March) per household

Please note: Permits are required for vans and commercial type vehicles and large trailers to access the site, this will need to be applied for in advance. No signwritten vehicles are allowed on site.

Carpets can be recycled at the Scunthorpe, Winterton, Barton, Belton, Goxhill and Kirton in Lindsey Household Recycling Centres. 

  • Charges apply for the following materials

  • Expand

See fees and charges or contact the Customer Contact Centre.

Soil, hardcore and rubble can be taken to any of the Household Recycling Centres.

Plasterboard can only be taken to Scunthorpe Household Recycling Centre.

Tyres can only be taken to Belton, Barton and Scunthorpe Household Recycling Centres.

  • Plasterboard

  • Expand

Plasterboard can only be recycled at the Scunthorpe Household Recycling Centre.

By law it cannot be disposed of in landfill, and its not recognised as household waste. Therefore, there is a charge to dispose of this. Please see our fees and charges.

  • Soil and rubble

  • Expand

Soil and rubble can be recycled at any Household Recycling Centres. By law it cannot be disposed of in landfill, and it is not recognised as household waste. Therefore, there is a charge to dispose of this. Please see our fees and charges.

  • Tyres

  • Expand

You can dispose of car tyres at Belton, Barton, and Scunthorpe Household Recycling Centres.

As car tyres are not recognised by law as household waste there is a charge to dispose of these. Please see our fees and charges.

Bicycle tyres and inner tubes can also be recycled along with the car tyres, there is not a charge to dispose of these.

We do not accept tractor or lorry tyres.
