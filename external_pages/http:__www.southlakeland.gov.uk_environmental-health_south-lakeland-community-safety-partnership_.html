South Lakeland Community Safety Partnership (SLCSP)

SLCSP is made up of a mixture of organisations and voluntary groups who work together with a common aim. To tackle crime and disorder in the area and to maintain South Lakelandâ€™s position as one of the safest places in the North West. We are now holding joint meetings with Barrow Community Safety Partnership, sharing a co-ordinator and a Chair for a trial period until March 2016.

All Community Safety Partnerships have a duty to undertake an annual Strategic Assessment of local community safety and crime priorities. Partners are working to a shared Partnership Plan 2015/16 that clearly sets out how the Partnership will tackle key community safety priorities, agreed with partners and local communities.

The SLCSP Constitution remains unchanged until agreement is reached on future shared working arrangements.

Some partners have a legal responsibility to work with the SLCSP and these are South Lakeland District Council, Cumbria County Council, Cumbria Constabulary, Cumbria Clinical Commissioning Group, Cumbria Fire and Rescue Service and Cumbria and Lancashire Community Rehabilitation Company.
