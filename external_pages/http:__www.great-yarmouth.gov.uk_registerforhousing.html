Register for housing

The demand for social housing in Great Yarmouth is too high for us to meet it. We have been forced to restrict who can go on the waiting list and who can't. We offer a Housing Options service to help everyone with housing needs.

Read our  housing allocation scheme [214kb] to find out more.

Collapse allReveal all

How do I make a housing application?

You can apply by coming into Housing Options Service at Greyfriars House. We are open from 9am to 5pm (we close at 3pm on the first Thursday of the month).

We do not offer appointments. To avoid a long wait it is best to avoid 12 to 2.30pm.

Who can go on the waiting list?

Because of the huge demand for social housing we can only put those in the greatest need on the housing register.

You can go on the waiting list if you have a housing need and have lived in the Borough of Great Yarmouth for two years.  An example of housing need would be if you:

  • Are homeless or at risk of homelessness
  • Live in overcrowded or unsanitary conditions
  • Need to move for medical or welfare reasons

Who cannot go on the list?

We have no responsibility to house people who:

  • Are subject to immigration control
  • Cannot claim Housing Benefit due to the Immigration and Asylum Act, 1999
  • Are from abroad and ineligible as may be prescribed by the Secretary of State
  • Do not live in the Common Travel Area or European Economic Area

You are responsible for telling us if there is any change in your immigration status.

How many bedrooms will I be allowed?

We will let you know the size of property you will be allowed when you are accepted on to the housing list. The number of bedrooms you are entitled to is calculated using the national bedroom standard.

You can use the LHA calculator to see how many bedrooms we think you need.

You are not entitled to bedrooms for children if you are not their main carer.

We will consider any unborn child as part of your household when you reach 30 weeks' in your pregnancy. On some occasions we may have to wait until the baby is born to know if an additional bedroom is required.

Who should be included in my application?

You should include the details of:

  • Your partner
  • All dependent children or dependent relatives
  • A carer (if they are needed for personal care during the day and at night)
  • Any household member who moved in with you at the beginning of your tenancy and is still living with you
  • Any household member in prison due for release within four weeks
  • Children who normally live with you but are studying at university or serving in the Armed Forces

You cannot include children on your application if you do not have the main responsibility for them.

How long will I have to wait to be housed?

This depends on the size and location of housing you need. If you want to live in an urban area you will probably be housed sooner than someone wanting to live in a countryside area. If you need ground floor accommodation or a larger property such as a 4 bed property you will have a long wait and in some circumstances you may have to wait two to three years.

If you are accepted on the housing list and have asked to be housed in a particular area or type of housing and have not been made an offer of housing within three months we have the right to consider you for properties that do not match your preferences.

We will also backdate the application date of applicants who are in paid work for at least 24 hours per week by 12 months.

What happens if I accept an offer of social housing?

Before we can make a formal offer you need to provide proof of the information in your application form.  Once this is done we will make you agree a date for you to sign the tenancy agreement.

What happens if I refuse an offer of social housing?

We will make you up to 2 offers of suitable accommodation but it you refuse both these offers you will be removed from the housing list and you will not be able to re-apply for 12 months.

If you have been accepted as homeless by the Council you will be made one suitable offer. If you refuse this offer our homelessness duty to you will end and no further offer of accommodation will be made to you.  If the Council has accepted a homeless duty, you should have received a decision letter informing you.
