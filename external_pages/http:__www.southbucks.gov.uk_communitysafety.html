Community Safety Partnership

The Chiltern and South Bucks Community Safety Partnership is a statutory partnership formed as a result of the Crime and Disorder Act 1998, and includes senior officers from the following organisations:

  • South Bucks District Council
  • Chiltern District Council
  • Buckinghamshire County Council
  • Thames Valley Police (Chiltern and South Bucks Local Policing Area)
  • Chiltern Clinical Commissioning Group
  • Thames Valley Probation and Community Rehabilitation Company (MTCNovo)
  • Buckinghamshire Fire and Rescue Service
  • Buckinghamshire Youth Offending Service
  • Buckinghamshire Drug and Alcohol Action Team.
  • Housing Associations including L&Q, Paradigm Housing Group, Homes.
  • Voluntary Organisations including, CrimeStoppers and Victim Support.
     

The Partnership facilitates the co-ordination of local activities to reduce crime, disorder and the fear of crime by selecting the most appropriate course of action, ensuring agencies are not working in isolation and that resources are targeted.

Community Safety is about action to tackle crime and disorder. It is also about fear of crime, as perception can sometimes be worse than reality. Community Safety involves us all. It affects everyone who lives, works or visits South Bucks District.

  • Domestic Violence & Abuse

    Domestic violence and abuse (DVA) is any incident or pattern of incidents of controlling, coercive, threatening behaviour, violence or abuse between those aged 16 or over who are, or have been, intimate partners or family members regardless of gender or sexuality. The abuse can encompass, but is not limited to: psychological, physical, sexual, financial and emotional. DVA is rarely a one-off event and it tends to escalate in frequency and severity over time and is often used to maintain power and control over another person.
