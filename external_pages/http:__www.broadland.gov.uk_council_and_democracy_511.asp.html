complaints procedure

Listen

Broadland District Council is committed to providing quality services. Your comments and suggestions are important because they help us to know where we need to improve.

We want to put things right as quickly as possible. To enable us to do this please follow the complaints procedure detailed below:

what to do next...

  • First tell the staff providing the service why you are unhappy
  • If you do not want to do this, or you have done this and are not happy with the answer you received, please contact their manager
  • If you are still unhappy please make a formal complaint using one of the following methods:
    • Web: online complaints form
    • Email: complaints@broadland.gov.uk
    • Tel: 01603 430615
  • At any time during this process you can write to your local Councillor or MP

forms

online complaints form

further information

What will happen after I have made a formal complaint?

When we receive your complaint we will acknowledge receipt within five working days, stating what action we will take.

Your complaint will be passed to the Head of Service about which you have complained. The matter will be investigated and a written reply sent to you within 10 working days.

If the complaint is about the actions of the Head of Service, then your complaint will be passed to a member of the Senior Management Team - either the Chief Executive or the Deputy Chief Executive, as appropriate. Any complaints about the actions of the Deputy Chief Executive will be passed to the Chief Executive and complaints about the Chief Executive will be passed to the Council's Monitoring Officer.

Some complaints may take more time to investigate, but we will keep you informed of our actions and the progress we have made.

What action will Broadland take?

Where we have made a mistake then our reply will explain what went wrong and how we propose to put things right. If we have not provided information that we should have provided, it will be supplied as soon as possible.

If our procedures are at fault we will improve them. We review all complaints at a later date and, if necessary, change our procedures, so please help us by returning the follow-up form which we will send you after we have dealt with your complaint.

And if you are still not happy?

If you are dissatisfied with the outcome of your complaint then you are entitled to refer your complaint to the Local Government Ombudsman. For more information about this process a link is provided below.

Freedom of Information complaints

If your complaint is that the Council did not supply you with information then this will be reviewed by the Council's Deputy Chief Executive.  If you are dissatisfied with the outcome of this review then you are entitled to refer your complaint to the Information Commissioner.  For more information about this process a link is provided below.
