Accessing the archives

The centre holds a fantastic collection of historic material relating to the ancient county of Kent, most of which is accessible to the public. The 14 kms of collections comprise both printed and manuscript (archive) material and are all held in purpose-built archive repositories with controlled environments and extra security. If you're new to the archives, read our guide to the archive collections (PDF, 518.5 KB).

To access the archives:

  1. Use the archive catalogue to find the archives you'd like to see. Once you find something, make a note of the finding number. Each document in our collection has a unique number and must be ordered individually from the repositories.  The catalogue is extensive and provides information on a wide range of different historic records and so if you have any queries please ask for help. If you need further information or help we have other catalogues, indexes and finding aids kept behind the scenes which staff can access for you.
  2. Update your library account to allow you to view original archive material. To do this, visit the Community History Desk, on the left side of the Kent History and Library Centre. You'll need to provide ID which proves your name, address and signature (as an example: a plastic card driving licence is sufficient ID because it has your name, address, signature and photo all on the one document). Alternatively, you may need to bring more than one form of ID.
  3. Book a desk in the archive search room if you want to look at original archive material and some of the earlier printed material. You can book a desk by calling 03000 41 31 31 or by emailing your contact details and the date and time you'd like to book a desk for to historyandlibrarycentre@kent.gov.uk.

In the search room

Once you have booked into the search room, you will be given your desk number and locker key. The lockers also require a £1 coin to operate, which is returned to you after use. There are certain restrictions on what you can take into the archive search room. For example, you may only take in pencils (without erasers) to write with, not pens.

To order the archives from the repository you will need to fill in an orange request ticket. These can be found on the search room tables or at the enquiry desk. You then need to give the ticket to a member of staff or put it in the small tray on the enquiry desk marked Request Slips. The orange slips are collected at regular intervals. There will be a short delay while the items are located in the storage areas and brought to you. If you have to wait longer than 15 minutes please mention this to a member of staff. When you have finished with the document you are looking at please bring it back to the enquiry desk and you will be given a receipt for it.

If you are having trouble reading your document in the search room, we have a high resolution enlarger available to use. The enlarger will display the document onto a screen and you can adjust the text or screen colour to suit your needs. If you would like to use the enlarger, please ask a member of staff.

Copying material

Photocopying facilities are provided for material in both the community history area and the archive search room. This is subject to copyright regulations and providing that copying will not damage the originals. We can also make digital copies for you or you can buy a camera licence and take your own photos. There is some material which cannot be copied for various reasons including terms and conditions of deposit, data protection legislation or the format and condition of the material. You'll need to fill out a form which lists the items you want to copy and also states that the copies will only be used for your personal use and study.
