Help to Stay In Your Home

Help to Stay in Your Home

Your current home may be unsuitable for your needs for a variety of reasons and, under certain circumstances, assistance may be available to address these issues, enabling you to remain in your home.

Disrepair

If you rent your home and it is in a poor state of repair or lacks basic amenities such as a bath, toilet or sink, etc, the Council's Public Health and Licensing Department has powers to ensure that improvements are made so that your accommodation meets legal standards. 

The Public Health and Licensing Department can also assist with Disabled Facilities Grants, heating and energy efficiency, moving home and repair assistance.  For further information, please call the department on (01403) 215641 or email:publichealth.licensing@horsham.gov.uk

Affordability

If you are having difficulty paying your rent or mortgage, it is vital that you seek advice as soon as possible.  The longer you wait to sort out the problem, the more difficult it will be to resolve.  Housing and Council Tax benefits are assessed and administered by Census on behalf of Horsham District Council.  To find out whether you are entitled to help with your rent or council tax, visit http://revsandbens.centralsussex.gov.uk or ring 01444 477264. 

If you own your own home and receipt of certain income related benefits, you may be eligible for Support for Mortgage Interest.  For further details, contact Job Centre Plus or the Pension Service https://www.gov.uk/support-for-mortgage-interest/overview.

Domestic Violence

In an emergency, contact 999. 

Domestic violence can be physical, sexual, psychological or financial that takes place within an intimate or family type relationship and that forms a pattern of coercive and controlling behaviour.  This can include forced marriage and so-called "honour crimes".  Domestic violence may include a range of abusive behaviours, not all of which are in themselves inherently "violent".  If you or a friend need help, contact the freephone 24 hour National Domestic Violence Helpline on 0808 2000 247 or seek further advice on www.womensaid.org.uk.

There are a range of legal options available to enable people experiencing domestic violence to remain in their homes such as non-molestation orders and injunctions.  Horsham District Council's Safe At Home scheme may be able to help you with increased security measures in and around your home.  For further information on the Safe At Home scheme, ring 01403 215230. 

If you are not in imminent danger, it is very important that you seek advice to fully explore the options available to you before leaving your home.

Housing Advice

If you have a need to move and would like to be added to Horsham District Council's Housing Register; please check your eligibility using the online assessment which can be found using the following link https://www.horsham.gov.uk/housing/housing-register

Once you have checked your eligibility, if eligible you can proceed to submit an application form online.
