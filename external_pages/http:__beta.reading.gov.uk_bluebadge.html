Blue Badges & Concessionary Travel

The Blue Badge Scheme gives people with severe mobility problems better access to goods and services by allowing them to park closer to their destination. The scheme is open to eligible disabled people whether they are a driver or passenger and only applies to on-street parking.

To check your eligibility and apply online for a Blue Badge, please visit the Gov.uk Blue Badge Application page. 

Questions

Close allOpen all

Am I eligible for a badge?

Potential applicants are able to check whether they may be eligible at the Blue Badge section of Gov.uk.

You automatically qualify for a Blue Badge (ie. eligible without further assessment) if you:

  • Are registered as blind (severely sight impaired) by a Local Authority under the National Assistance Act 1948 
  • Receive the higher rate mobility component of Disability Living Allowance
  • Receive 8 points or more under the Moving Around component of Personal Independence Payment (PIP)
  • Receive a war pensioner's mobility supplement
  • Receive a benefit under the Armed Forces and Reserve Forces Compensation Scheme (within tariffs 1-8) and have been assessed as having a permanent and substantial disability which causes inability or considerable difficulty in walking.

You may also be eligible (subject to further assessment) if you:

  • Have a permanent and substantial disability which causes inability or considerable difficulty in walking
  • Drive a motor vehicle regularly and have a severe disability with both arms, making it very difficult or impossible to operate parking meters
  • Are a parent or carer of a child who is younger than three years old, who has a specific medical condition which means they must be accompanied by bulky medical equipment, which is difficult to carry around, or need to be kept near a vehicle at all times to get emergency treatment for their condition.

For applications that are based on your difficulties in walking, please note that medical conditions such as asthma, autism, psychological and behavioural problems, Crohn's disease, incontinent conditions and Myalgic Encephalomyelitis (ME) are not in themselves a qualification for a badge. If you have one of these conditions, you may be eligible for a badge - but only if you are unable to walk or have very considerable difficulty in walking in addition to the condition.

It is your responsibility to provide as much supporting medical evidence as possible to enable the Independent Mobility Assessor who screens your application to determine your eligibility. If eligibility is unclear from the information provided you may be required to attend an Independent Mobility Assessment carried out by a fully qualified healthcare professional.

How do I apply for or renew a badge?

Applications can be made by post or by visiting the Gov.uk Blue Badge Application page. Details of which supporting documents you need to provide can be found on the application form.

  •  Download and print an application form for individuals [116kb]
  •  Download and print an application form for organisations [74kb].

If your application is successful, we will let you know and ask you to come to the Civic Centre to collect it. You will have to pay the badge fee - we accept payments by credit and debit cards, cheque or postal order. You can also buy a wallet for your badge for 94p. If you are renewing your existing badge, you must return it to us when you come to collect your new one. 

Is there a right of appeal if my application has been refused?

If your application is refused you can appeal to request a review of the decision.

If your application is refused even after an appeal, you must wait six months before re-applying, unless there has been a substantial deterioration in your health which has severely impacted your mobility.

What are Organisational Blue Badges, and can I apply for one?

Organisational badges are meant for organisations that provide care for and transport disabled people who meet one or more of the eligibility criteria for an individual Blue Badge. Applicants should have a clear need for an Organisational Badge, rather than using the personal Blue Badge of the person (or people) it is transporting.

Badges are supplied directly to organisations, not individual members of staff. Misuse of an organisational Blue Badge carries a penalty of up to £1,000.

Can I apply for a temporary badge?

No. Blue Badges are only for people who have a permanent and substantial disability (ie. a condition that is likely to last for your entire life).

Where can I use my badge?

You can download and print a high resolution map of  blue badge parking locations [1Mb] in central Reading.

I am a taxi/private hire/community transport operator - can I apply for a badge?

It is very unlikely you will quality for a badge, as you do not directly provide care to disabled people. You can make use of an individual's Blue Badge to aid them with their journey - but only when you are carrying them as a passenger. 

I am moving to Reading with an existing badge - do I need to apply again or renew with the Council?

If you move to Reading, you should continue to use your existing badge until it expires. You must then apply to us to renew your badge eight weeks before it is due to expire. 

My badge has been lost/stolen.

Please complete the  lost or stolen Blue Badge form [38kb]. If your badge was stolen, you will also need the Crime Reference number given to you by the Police. There is a charge for all replacement badges and you will need to supply new photographs. 

My badge is damaged.

Please complete the  lost or stolen Blue Badge form [38kb]. There is a charge for all replacement badges and you will need to supply new photographs along with the damaged badge itself. 

My contact details have changed.

Please contact us on 0118 937 2033 as soon as possible. For a change in address, please complete the blue badge change of address form.

I no longer need my badge.

Please return the badge by post to the Blue Badge Department as soon as possible. Make sure to include a statement explaining why you are returning the badge. 

What should I do if I suspect that an individual is committing fraud?

Please see our fraud reporting page.

Can I get help with bus travel costs?

Yes - residents within Reading Borough are entitled to apply for a concessionary bus pass. Residents within the Reading urban area who live outside of Reading Borough should apply to their local authority - either Wokingham Borough or West Berkshire Councils.

 Concessionary Bus Pass Application Form [5Mb]

 Concessionary Bus Pass Replacement Pass Form [1Mb]

Concessionary Bus Passes

Reading Borough residents whose 60th birthday is on or before 5 April 2010 (date of birth on or before 5 April 1950) are entitled to a pass allowing free travel on all local buses in the Reading urban area between 9am and 11pm Monday to Friday, and at any time on weekends and bank holidays, from the date of their 60th birthday.

Reading Borough residents whose sixtieth birthday is on or after 6 April 2010 (date of birth on or after 6 April 1950) should use the Government's State Pension age calculator to check when they become eligible for a concessionary bus pass which is available here. Once eligible, applicants will be entitled to a pass allowing free travel between 9am and 11pm Monday to Friday and at any time on weekends and bank holidays, on all local buses in the Reading urban area.

In addition to travel on all local buses in the Reading urban area, your concessionary bus pass will allow you free travel on local buses anywhere in England between 9:30am and 11:00pm on weekdays and at any time on weekends and bank holidays.

Disability Bus Passes

This entitlement applies to Reading Borough residents who cannot hold a driver's license on medical grounds or who suffer from certain disabilities, including blindness, deafness and disabilities which seriously impair the person's ability to walk. If you hold a concessionary bus pass on disability grounds, you may use your pass at any time on weekdays, weekends and bank holidays and you may also be able to travel for free on Readibus if you meet their eligibility criteria. If you you are accompanying someone with an eligible disability, you may also qualify for free bus travel within the Reading urban area as a companion.

Are there any dial-a-ride services in Reading?

Yes - Readibus is an independent local charity recognised as a leader in community transport services. Readibus has been providing bus services for people with restricted mobility in the greater Reading area for over 25 years.

It offers a door-to-door assisted travel service and the buses are fully wheelchair accessible.

The service is available seven days a week from 7.30am to 11.15pm across a large part of central and west Berkshire, including Reading, Newbury and Wokingham.

Readibus' principal service is a dial-a-ride door-to-door, fully accessible, assisted bus service for people who cannot use, or have great difficulty in using, ordinary public transport.  You will need to register with Readibus to use the service. All you need to do is call the number below and say what you need. You can book your journey in the same call.

People with permanent mobility problems should apply for an Access Pass which will allow use of Readibus free of charge at all times. Please contact Reading Borough Council on 0118 9305522 for an Access Pass application form. If you do not have a bus pass, fares range from £1.50 to £3.00 per journey. Someone may also accompany you on the journey if you wish.

If you find the service of benefit and wish to make the same journey regularly, at roughly the same time each week, you can join the weekly user scheme at no extra cost. This means the bus will call each week so you won't need to phone each time.

For more information, or to book your journey, please contact Readibus directly.

The Readibus service is supported with community grant aid by Reading Borough Council and other councils in the area. 

Can I request a new disabled parking bay?

Yes - to have an Advisory Disabled Parking Bay created in a residential street (for use by any blue badge holder) you must:

  • Currently hold a Blue Badge
  • Live at the address
  • Not have any parking restrictions (except residents-only parking) outside your home
  • Not already have access to private off-street parking within or near the premises
  • Not have the option of alternative arrangements if you do not drive yourself (eg. your driver collects the vehicle and parks in front of the address where you need to use the vehicle).

Contact the Highways Team for more information.

Please note that these parking bays are advisory only and cannot be enforced.
