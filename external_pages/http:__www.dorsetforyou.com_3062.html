Direct payments

Direct payment of your personal budget gives you control over your care.

If you have been assessed as eligible to receive support from us, direct payments are one of the ways that you can choose to receive your personal budget.  You can then use this money to purchase your own care, rather than us arranging it for you.

Benefits of direct payments

A direct payment gives you more control over the support you receive.  This could include help with personal care and getting around, as well as social and leisure support.  With a direct payment you can:

  • employ a support worker or personal assistant
  • pay for a support agency of your choice
  • buy equipment to help you to stay safe and independent
  • fund activities or outings
  • provide respite care or have a short break.

You may know how you want to spend your allocated personal budget to meet your outcomes which were agreed in your support plan. If not, you can ask for help from a family member or friend.  Your social care worker or an independent support organisation can also give you advice.

What you can't use your direct payment for

Some of the things that direct payments cannot currently be used for include:

  • paying for residential care
  • buying food, clothing or other general living expenses
  • buying goods or services that do not relate to your agreed care needs
  • employing someone who lives with you - unless there is a special reason for this and it has been agreed
  • buying healthcare services
  • buying services directly from the Council.

Your social  care worker will be able to give you more information about restrictions and how using direct payments will benefit you.

Getting your direct payment

Your direct payment will need to be paid into a separate bank account that is only used for direct payments.  You will receive your money, paid in advance, every two or four weeks (depending on which council makes the payments).  In some cases, such as for short breaks, you may receive this as a lump sum.

You will also need to pay your part of your contribution into the direct payment bank account.

Managing your direct payment

You will be responsible for how your direct payment is spent.  You must be able to show that it has been used for the services and support agreed in your support plan.

This means you will have to keep records of your spending and we will ask you to:

  • keep receipts and invoices
  • send us information on how you have spent your budget
  • check with your social care worker if you wish to spend your budget on anything that is not in your support plan to make sure this still meets your outcomes
  • return any money not used to the council
  • if you employ a carer or personal assistant directly, you will be an employer and you will need to keep records on wages, tax and national insurance.

You will be required to sign a contract with the council which will tell you about your responsibilities.

Help to manage your direct payment

You may want help to manage your direct payment such as:

  • support with planning your care, helping you to understand what is available and what you may need
  • finding care and support options to help you make your decisions
  • getting costs
  • help to set up bank accounts
  • advice on how to use the payment and what your responsibilities are
  • help with paperwork.

If you feel you would like help to manage your direct payment you can ask your social care worker to make a referral to one of the agencies listed below on your behalf.

If you pay for your own care and you would like help to arrange care and support, you may contact these agencies directly.

If you live in Bournemouth:

  • Enham Trust

If you live in Dorset:

  • Dorset Advocacy
  • Enham Trust
  • Penderels Trust
  • PRO Disability (Poole Rights Organisation on Disability)

 If you live in Poole:

  • Poole Direct Payments Advice Service
