Stray and Lost Dogs

Page Content

The dog warden service deals with complaints and reports of stray dogs and lost dogs. If you find a stray dog you should contact us. Please always give a full description of the dog, where it was last seen and at what time.

If you find a stray dog outside our normal office hours, you should:

  • take the dog to our Stray Dog Reception Centre. This can be arranged by ringing 07525 988 061
  • return the dog directly to the owner, if details are known
  • keep the dog until the next working day

Please note that we do not operate a collection service outside office hours and the police do not accept stray dogs.

Lost your dog?

  • contact us
  • contact the police on 101, local veterinary surgeries, neighbours and, if your dog is micro chipped, your micro chipping service provider

Give a full description of the dog and where it was last seen, the lost dog form will assist you. If the dog warden service receive any reports of a dog matching the description they will assist you in finding it

What happens to a stray dog?

If we seize a dog we will take it to our Stray Dog Reception Centre and keep it for seven days. Before a dog is released, the owner must pay a stray charge of £65, plus the cost of kennelling at £10 per day and any other costs incurred.

A cash payment must be made before a dog can be released back to its owner. You can pay direct to staff at the Stray Dog Reception Centre or during office hours by ringing 01423 500600 extension 56633 and paying by credit or debit card before collection. Payment by cheque is no longer accepted for this service.

If a dog remains unclaimed after seven days, we become the owner of the dog. We will re-home it, or in certain circumstances, humanely destroy the dog.

Collar and identification tag

Apart from some very specific exemptions, a dog in a public place must wear a collar with the name and address of the owner attached to it; failure to do so is an offence against the Control of Dogs Order 1992. An owner can be prosecuted and fined up to £2000.

Aggressive or dangerous dogs

The primary enforcement body for such incidents are the Police and you should contact them on 101.

They will provide you with an incident number and advise what action is appropriate.
