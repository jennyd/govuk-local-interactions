Children with disabilities and special needs

If your child has special needs or a disability we can help you find support and guidance.

Special needs are medical conditions which are serious and long-term or may affect your child's life expectancy. The term covers a range of diseases, disorders and illnesses. You can find out more at the NHS website.

Please contact the Multi Agency Safeguarding Hub (MASH) which handles all referrals and information requests for children with disabilities services:

  • Telephone: 01432 260800

What is the short break scheme for disabled children?

Our short break scheme lets children with disabilities enjoy a break from routine and get involved with exciting activities.

The scheme used to be called Aiming High.

You can find out more about eligibility for the scheme and what is available on our Short breaks, activities and respite for disabled children page and from the documents on this page.

FAQs

What is the difference between special needs and special educational needs?

Special needs are serious medical conditions which are dealt with by health professionals.

Special educational needs are barriers to your child's progress in school. They mean that your child may need extra help with their schooling. Special educational needs are assessed by a school or the council - and they may only be short-term conditions. Find out more on the special educational needs page.

Can I home school a child with special educational needs?

Children with a statement of special educational needs who are registered pupils at a special school can only be removed from school with our consent.

Pupils with a statement who have been registered at a mainstream school can be educated at home providing arrangements are judged to be suitable and meet the child's needs as outlined in the statement.

You can find out more on our special educational needs page.
