Funding for Community Organisations

Whether you are looking for £100 or £1,000,000 to support your community group, activity or event we’ve put together some practical information to help you get the funding you need.  From the inside track on researching the latest funding opportunities, to handy tips on putting together a winning bid, we’re here to help.

Where to start?

From the National Lottery to private grant making trusts, there are lots of funding opportunities for you to explore.  Finding which one of them best meets your needs is a task in itself. 

To start off, you’ll need to think about what you need funding for.  Is it to support the day-to-day activities (revenue funding) or are you looking to fund assets, such as buildings or vehicles (capital funding)?

Consultation

You will usually be expected to carry out a consultation before you apply for funding for your community group/project.

Funding Programmes

Why not give your search for funding a head start?  We’ve compiled a list of some of the best websites to help you track down the best funding opportunities.

  • Neighbourhood Forum Grants (DDC)
    Community groups looking for funding for projects that can show benefit to the local community are invited to apply for grants of up to £1,000 from the Dover District Neighbourhood Forums.
  • Level 1: This funding is now closed.
  • The Coalfields Regeneration Trust
    'Coalfields Community Grants' programme is open for applications from Community Groups in the following wards:  Aylesham, Eastry, Eythorne and Shepherdswell, Little Stour and Ashstone, Middle Deal & Sholden, Mill Hill, North Deal, St. Margaret's-at-Cliffe.
  • Kent 4 Community
    A database of funding opportunities across the UK but with an emphasis on Kent.  It has a free search facility and you can sign up for regular updates on funding opportunities.
  • Funding Central
    Set up by the National Council of Voluntary Organisations (NCVO), Funding Central lists over 4,000 funding opportunities.
  • The National Lottery
    A database of programmes run by the 13 Lottery funders, including the Arts Council England, Big Lottery, Heritage Lottery Fund, Sport England, and UK Sport.
  • Vol Resource 
    Aims to make it quick and easy to get to useful information on anything to do with running a voluntary organisation, whether a community group, charity or other non-profit body. 
  • Up On The Downs Landscape Partnership Scheme
    The Landscape Heritage Grants are for projects that conserve and improve understanding of our built and natural heritage
  • Town and Parish Councils
    Details of funding opportunities available from some Town and Parish Councils.
  • Funding for All
    A charity that exists to mentor voluntary organisations and community groups across Kent (and Medway) in bid writing skills, funding strategy, business planning, tendering, legacies and developing budgets. 
  • Heritage Lottery Fund
    Information and advice sessions are available from Heritage Lottery Fund to help apply for their funding - 2016 programme of events
