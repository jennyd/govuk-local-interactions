Council Tax exemptions and discounts

Depending on your circumstances you may be able to apply for a discount or exemption from paying your Council Tax.

Collapse allReveal all

Can I get a Council Tax discount?

If you are an adult and live on your own (or with other people who don't count as adults), you can apply to get 25% off your bill.

Adults not counted for Council Tax include:

  • People aged 18 if child benefit is being paid
  • Students
  • Apprentices paid less than £195 per week
  • Severely mentally impaired people or people in hospitals/care homes
  • Members of religious communities
  • Carers working at least 24 hours per week for no more than £44 per week
  • Prisoners on remand or in prison
  • People staying in hostels or night shelters

Can I get a Council Tax exemption?

You may be eligible to apply for an exemption. The following properties are exempt from Council Tax while occupied:

  • Halls of residence
  • Accommodation occupied by full-time students
  • Accommodation occupied by armed forces
  • Accommodation occupied by visiting forces
  • Accommodation occupied only by people under the age of 18
  • Accommodation occupied only by severely mentally impaired people
  • The main residence of someone with diplomatic immunity
  • Annexes occupied by a dependant relative

A property is exempt from Council Tax while unoccupied if:

  • The previous occupant is in prison
  • The previous occupant now lives permanently in a care home or hospital
  • It cannot be occupied by law
  • It is waiting to be occupied by a minister of religion
  • The former occupant has moved to provide care
  • The former occupant has moved to receive care
  • It has been left empty by a student
  • It has been repossessed
  • It has been left empty by a bankrupt person
  • It is an empty caravan pitch or boat mooring
  • It is an unoccupied annexe which cannot be let separately due to planning restrictions
