Bulky Waste Collections  

If you have furniture or appliances that you no longer want, we will collect them from you and dispose of them for a small charge. Collections are made from the front of your property.

Examples of what we collect:

Furniture (such as sofas, chairs and tables)
Televisions
Washing machines
Carpets (underlay and carpet would be classed as two units)
Mattresses
Bathroom suites (bath, toilet, sinks) Please note we do not collect cast iron baths.

Cost
The bulky waste collection each item has a points value. The cost of the collection is a minimum of £50.00 for up to 8 points and £5.00 for each additional point.  Please see the Household item list  to clarify the points value of the items you would have for collection.

We cannot collect:
DIY waste such as fixtures or fittings

Kitchen units/worktops, Tiles

Black bags, Miscellaneous waste

Please note that if you pay someone to dispose of this kind of waste you need to check that they are a registered waste carrier and make sure they give you a waste transfer note to state where the rubbish is being taken from and to.  For more information on this please refer to the Environment Agency.

How to request a bulky waste collection

  • Online Form
  • By Telephone: contact our customer service team on 023 8068 8440 and ask to arrange a bulky waste collection.

Please note collections are made from the front of property.

Changes can be made to an order up to 48 hours before the collection.  Likewise we can cancel a booking and make a full refund as long as we are informed at least 48 hours before the collection is due.

Give your furniture to a good home
Alternatively, before throwing good quality furniture away you could contact a local charity and donate your furniture. 

SCRATCH (Southampton City and Region Action to Combat Hardship) can reuse some furniture, please visit their website for further information.

British Heart Foundation also collect unwanted furniture in the Borough, visit their website for more information. 

Hampshire Furniture Reuse Network (HFRN)

If you have some furniture items that you wish to dispose of but which are still in good condition, you could consider donating them for reuse so that someone else can make use of them. This is good for the environment as it saves raw materials and is a key element of the 'Waste Hierarchy'.

Hampshire Furniture Reuse Network, collects furniture and provides it to those who are in need across Hampshire, a sustainable way of reducing our waste.  If you would like to donate or enquire more, please visit their website.
