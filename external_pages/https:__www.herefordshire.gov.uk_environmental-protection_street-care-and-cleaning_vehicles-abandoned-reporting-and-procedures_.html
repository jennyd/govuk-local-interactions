Abandoned vehicles

We aim to keep the number of abandoned vehicles to a minimum and welcome public co-operation. When we receive a report of an abandoned vehicle we aim to provide a swift and efficient removal service.

What is an abandoned vehicle?

We consider a vehicle abandoned if:

  • It has been stationary for a significant amount of time and it appears that it is not being used
  • It doesn’t have a number plate
  • It doesn’t have a valid tax disc
  • It has one or more flat tyres
  • There are weeds or litter surrounding it, or mould and waste inside
  • Its brake discs are rusty, which shows the vehicle is unused
  • Its windows are broken
  • It is unroadworthy

Abandoning a vehicle is a criminal offence. The council has a legal duty to remove any vehicle that appears to have been abandoned within Herefordshire.  This includes vehicles on any open land or part of the highway.  We may only remove abandoned vehicles from private land with the land owner’s consent.

How do I report an abandoned vehicle?

Unless an unattended vehicle is a health and safety risk the council will not generally take any action with regards to abandonment until a vehicle has been in situ (without being moved) for at least 21 days.

To report an abandoned vehicle, please contact us on:

  • Online: Contact us form
  • Telephone: 01432 261761

With the following details:

  • The vehicle location
  • The vehicle make, model, colour and registration number
  • The expiry date of the tax disc
  • The vehicle condition
  • How long the vehicle has been there

How do I reclaim a vehicle?

Community protection team enforcement officers have the power to remove a vehicle if they believe it is abandoned. If we have removed your vehicle and you would like to get it back, you will need to provide evidence that the vehicle was not abandoned and that you are the legal registered owner or keeper. 

If we have placed the vehicle in storage, before we release the vehicle to you, you will need to pay any costs incurred by us in removing and storing the vehicle. If you are responsible for abandoning a vehicle, you may also face prosecution.

Who do I contact to get my vehicle back?

To get your vehicle back please contact us at:

  • Online: Contact us form
  • Telephone: 01432 261761

 Please provide:

  • The car registration
  • Where the car was parked when we claimed it

Please note: we only deal with the owner of the vehicle

Will I need to pay to get my vehicle back?

Yes, there will be a charge to reclaim your vehicle. The charge depends on your circumstances and increases on a daily basis. It is, therefore, a good idea to reclaim your vehicle as soon as you can. Payment can be made by card or cash.

What happens next?

We will tell you where to collect your vehicle. You can usually collect it within 24 hours (excluding weekends) of contacting us.

On collection you will need to provide:

  • Card or cash to make the full payment
  • The vehicle's log book or receipt of purchase to prove you are the registered keeper

 Also, if you plan to drive the vehicle away it has to be road legal, which means that you will need to provide:

  • A valid MOT certificate
  • Valid car insurance
  • Valid car tax
  • A full and valid driver's licence
