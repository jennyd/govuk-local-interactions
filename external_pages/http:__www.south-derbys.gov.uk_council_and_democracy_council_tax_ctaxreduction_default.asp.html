On 1 April Council Tax Benefit was abolished and replaced by a new scheme called Council Tax Reduction.

This provides for the reduction of Council Tax, in certain cases, under a local scheme set by South Derbyshire District Council and known as the Council Tax Reduction Schemes Regulations (South Derbyshire District Council Local Scheme).

This scheme reflects national amounts of pensions, tax credits, income related and non-income related social security benefits and allowances, component parts, applicable amounts, premiums and deductions and related items as originally set in accordance with the 2013 Regulations. It's amended annually in uprating Circulars and where appropriate by The Council Tax Reduction Schemes (Prescribed Requirements) (England) Amendment Regulations.

A copy of the latest approved version of the Council Tax Reduction Scheme is attached to this page.

Making a claim for Council Tax Reduction

You can claim Council Tax Reduction if:

  • you are on Income Support, Job Seekers Allowance or Pension Credits
  • you are on a low income (this includes people who are self employed)

You cannot get Council Tax Reduction if:

  • you, or your partner between you, have more than Â£16,000 in savings, unless you are over 60 and get the guaranteed pension credit.

A partner is someone you are married to or have a civil partnership with, or someone you live with as if you are married to them or have a civil partnership with.

Second Adult Rebate

You can claim Second Adult Rebate if you are responsible for the council tax bill and you are single but there are other people living in your home who are on a low income.

If your partner is a disregarded person, such as a student, you may also be able to claim second adult rebate. If you claim main Council Tax Reduction we will check entitlement to second adult rebate at the same time, and award you the higher of the two benefits.

How do I claim?

You can claim by contacting us.

If you are unable to come into the offices due to disability, illness, please tel: 01283 595795 to register your intention to claim and to ask for a home visit.
