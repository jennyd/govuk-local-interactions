Parks & open spaces

Sedgemoor District Council provides and maintains public open spaces within the Sedgemoor area.

  • Apex Park, Highbridge

    Covering approximately 42 acres, Apex Leisure and Wildlife Park, which straddles Burnham-on-Sea and Highbridge offers visitors a chance to relax, picnic, complete dog walks and enjoy the play and skate park facilities.

  • Blake Gardens, Bridgwater

    Blake Gardens is in a prime location in Bridgwater Town Centre and is a good surviving example of a simple municipal garden from the late Victorian/ Edwardian period.

  • Cranleigh Gardens/Eastover Park, Bridgwater

    Cranleigh Gardens/ Eastover Park is located within the Eastover area of Bridgwater and covers an area of three hectares. The park consists mainly of open grass areas which are surrounded by mature and semi mature trees.

  • King Square, Bridgwater

    This garden was designed and laid out in the early 19th century and the original square garden area served as a private amenity garden area for the regency style properties surrounding the square.

  • Manor Gardens, Burnham-on-Sea

    Manor Gardens is situated on the junction of Love Lane and Berrow Road, Burnham-on-Sea. The prominent whitewashed Manor House, surrounded by formal gardens is popular with all age groups and park users. Manor Gardens is available for hire.

  • Marine Cove, Burnham-on-Sea

    Tucked away on Burnham-on-Sea's Victorian Esplanade, next to St Andrew's Church, are the Green Flag award-winning Marine Cove gardens. These formally laid out gardens are a place of tranquillity and relaxation.

  • Rosewood Farm, Burnham-on-Sea

    Under the Section 106 Agreement and planning permission for the Rosewood Farm Development, Sedgemoor District Council were obligated to complete the remediation, laying out and landscaping of the former landfill site for use as a recreational open space.

  • St. Matthew's Field, Bridgwater

    St Matthews Field or the Fairfield (as it is locally known) is located within the Durleigh/West Street area of Bridgwater and covers an area of 5.6 hectares. The park consists of open grass areas and is very popular with local dog walkers.

  • Victoria Park, Bridgwater

    Victoria Park is located within the Newtown area of Bridgwater and covers an area of three hectares. The park consists mainly of open grass areas, which are surrounded by mature and semi mature trees.

  • Hire of Public Open space

    If you wish to hold an event or activity in a park or open space you need permission to do so.

  • Pitches

    Sedgemoor District Council is responsible for the maintenance of the playing fields used for football.

  • Memorial Seats

    Memorial seats are a long-lasting and appealing way to commemorate a special event or a loved one.

  • Parks Refurbishment Projects

    The following refurbishment projects have been completed by the Parks Development Team. Please click on the headings below to see a photo showing some of the new equipment.

  • Meads Eco-Park

    The Meads is an area of low-lying farmland to the south-west of Bridgwater currently used as grazing marsh. The footpaths through the park provide an opportunity to walk and enjoy the unique pastoral landscape.
