How to organise a street party

Let residents and affected properties know

You must consult all affected properties before we agree to any road closure. This will avoid unnecessary conflict at the event.

All residents in the road must be informed of (and most would need to be in agreement with) the proposed closure.

You will need to send round paper invitations - this is needed to show us that you have consulted everyone.

Most street parties are for residents only and there should be no outside 'publicity' - that would make it a public event.

Licence to serve alcohol or provide musical entertainment

Alcohol

When you don't need a licence: At a private party, sharing drinks with your neighbours does not require a licence.

When you do need a licence: If you are selling alcohol, you are required to apply for a licence. It will cost Â£21. Alcohol licensing information.

Music

When you don't need a licence: You are allowed to play music at your street party. If your street party is a private party for residents and the music is not advertised in advance to attract people (and you're not making money) then there is no need for a licence for your music, whether it's live or recorded.

When you do need a licence: However, if you have a programme of bands, etc, you would need to apply for one.

Serving food

As a private party, you do not need a licence under the Licensing Act 2003 to sell food, unless you want to only sell hot food and drink after 11 pm.

You can always ask neighbours to bake, make sandwiches, or bring food to share with one another. This is a good way of bringing different groups of people together.

Find out about food safety.

Insurance and safety

You will be responsible for the safety of the event.

We strongly recommend that you take out cover for any possible eventualities for the duration of your street party road closure.

Street parties are usually very low risk events, but you should review the risks involved. Planning a safe event is the best way of reducing risks.

Carry out a risk assessment: think about minimising any risks from electricity, accident, burns, damage, rain, breakages and so on.

Agree in advance that everyone should take responsibility for themselves and watch out for each other, especially children - you might say this on your invitation.

Tell emergency services

You must contact the emergency services (and Transport for London, if the street party affects a bus route) to tell them of your event.
