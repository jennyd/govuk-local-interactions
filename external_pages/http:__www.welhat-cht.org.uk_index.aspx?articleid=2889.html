Moving from your council home

If you are a council tenant there are several ways you can search for and move to a new council or housing association home.

Applying for a transfer

If you are a Welwyn Hatfield Borough Council tenant and would like to move to another council or housing association home, you can apply to join the transfer list. The Community Housing Trust manages the transfer list on the council's behalf. 

We have joined up with four other housing organisations to advertise and offer homes, the group is called Herts Choice Homes. Once your application has been registered and prioritised you will be able to search and bid for a new home using the Herts Choice Homes scheme.

You may also need to complete a  Medical form [312KB], if you have medical circumstances related to your need for housing.

Mutual Exchange

Mutual exchange occurs when one tenant swaps homes with another tenant. It may be a faster way to move than waiting for a transfer. Only those with secure tenancies are allowed to mutually exchange, and both parties will need written permission from their landlords to exchange.

You can search for a mutual exchange partner in several ways:

  • Homeswapper - a national scheme with free registration for council tenants, it allows council and housing association tenants to search for a mutual exchange partner across the country.
  • Checking the mutual exchange property lists at our Welwyn Garden City or Hatfield Housing Office
  • Searching online for other mutual exchange schemes
  • Checking the local papers and shop windows for private adverts.

The Assisted Transfer Scheme 

We run a limited scheme to help tenants in certain circumstances to move. The Assisted Transfer Scheme is operated entirely at the Council and Trust's discretion. Applications are prioritised to help tenants in exceptional circumstances to move to a home that is more suitable for their needs. For more information please contact our Welwyn Garden City or Hatfield Housing Office.
