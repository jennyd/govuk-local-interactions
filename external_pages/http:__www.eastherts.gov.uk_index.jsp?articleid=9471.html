Private Drainage

On 1 October 2011, water and sewerage companies in England and Wales became responsible for private sewers, which were currently the responsibility of property owners. Not all private pipes are included. There are some cases where the property owners remain responsible for the sections of pipe between the property / building and the transferred private sewer or lateral drain.

Public Sewers

These are the large sewers usually located under a main road. A public sewer is the responsibility of the sewerage undertaker. In East Herts this is Thames Water. If you find a public sewer is blocked you should report it to Thames Water on 0845 9200 800 (Thames Water will need your postcode in order to log your call), Textphone: 0845 7200 899,  via their website   Thames Water - Problems with your sewer pipes  or write to Thames Water, PO Box 286, Swindon, SN38 2RA .

Private Sewers and drains

You are still be responsible for any internal plumbing and the section of pipe leading to the transferred sewer.   Property owners are responsible for the section of pipe that serves only their property and lies within their boundary.

If you think you have a problem with a sewer pipe or drain, please carry out the following steps as it's important that you try and identify where the problem is as this will save you unnecessary callouts.

  • If you have a shared sewer, try asking your neighbours if they have a problem.
  • If they don't, it's likely that the problem is on your section of pipe or waste plumbing, meaning you will need to resolve it yourself.
  • If your neighbours do have a problem, it may be on a shared section of pipe

The legal status of your sewer type of information is usually found within the deeds to your property.   East Herts does have access to a sewer map; if you wish to inspect the sewer map please contact Engineering Services on 01279 655261.   

You may also wish to alert your insurance company if you believe your drain or private sewer is damaged.

Local Authority Powers

East Herts has powers to serve legal notices on responsible parties to repair or clear damaged or blocked drains. If a drain is blocked the notice requires it to be cleared in 48 hours. The Council may undertake the work to clear the blockage themselves after 48 hours if the recipients of the notice fail to do so.
