Arranging your care

What is care and support?

Due to a disability or long term condition, you may need some care and support to live as well and as independently as possible in their own home for as long as possible.

Care and support services include practical help with things like:

  • getting in and out of bed
  • washing
  • dressing
  • getting to work
  • preparing meals
  • eating
  • socialising
  • caring for families
  • being involved in community and recreational activities.

Care and support services could also include offering emotional support at times of difficulty or stress.

Am I eligible for care and support services?

You may be eligible for care and support if you are not able to do a combination of things that seriously affects your wellbeing and quality of life.

To qualify for City of London Corporation care and support services, you need to:

  • aged 18 or over
  • live in the City of London
  • meet the eligibility criteria. (If you're not eligible, we will still be able to signpost you to advice and information.)

What should I do if I feel I need care and support?

If you’re finding managing at home and getting out and about increasingly difficult, you can talk to a member of the Adult Social Care Team who can discuss your options and advise on whether you will need to have an assessment of your needs.

What does the needs assessment look at?

If you’re finding a combination of tasks difficult to achieve, which, without support, would affect your wellbeing and quality of life, we will be able to determine the level of help you will need through the assessment.

Your needs assessment will consider:

  • how your care and support needs affect your day to day life
  • your mental and physical health and wellbeing
  • the things that are important to you in how you live your life, such as being able to carry on working or volunteering or being able to meet friends.

Helping you with your needs assessment

If you require a needs assessment, one of our trained social workers will talk to you, your family and anyone who is involved in caring and supporting you to complete your needs assessment. This will help us to understand what your needs are and how you would like us to support you.

If you wish, a carer, friend or family member can represent you or, if you would prefer, we can arrange an independent advocate to help you.

Any decisions about your care and support will take into account your health and wellbeing as well as what things matter to you and those close to you, so that you can stay as healthy and remain independent for as long as possible.

A member of the Adult Social Care Team will also speak to your carer, if you have one, to find out if they would like a separate Carers Assessment

What happens after the needs assessment?

After a needs assessment, we will use the eligibility criteria and decide if you are eligible for our services. We will write to you about our decision explaining the reasons for it.

Those eligible for City of London Corporation social care services

If you have eligible needs we will discuss what care and support might be available and develop a care and support plan with you. This will focus on your needs, how they will be met and the outcomes you want to achieve along with how your care and support will be paid for.

Will I have to pay for the care and support I need?

If you’re eligible for care and support from the Adult Social Care Team, you may be asked to contribute something towards the cost.

To calculate any costs you would pay, we will ask you to complete a financial assessment, which will ask about any savings and regular income. This information will help us understand how much you can afford if anything to contribute to the cost of your care and support.

As part of your Care and Support Plan, we will draw up an individual budget which sets out exactly how much it will cost to meet the eligible care and support needs you have. The budget will include any amount that the local authority is going to pay towards those costs. It will also set out any financial contributions you will make to your care and support.

Find out more about paying for your care and support

Those not eligible for City of London Corporation social care services

If you’re not eligible for care and support through the Adult Social Care Team, we can still offer those living within the City free advice and guidance, enabling them to access a number of voluntary services that provide support to adults in the community.

These can be found on the FYi Directory
