Presenting bins/boxes for emptying

  • Please present your bins/boxes by 7am in the morning and retrieve them to your property as soon after emptying as possible.
  • Containers should not be left at their collection point longer than necessary and definitely not overnight, as they could cause an obstruction under the Highways Act.
  • If you are out on collection day, you might like to ask a neighbour to help retrieve your containers.
  • If the crew do not empty your containers on collection day, please report it as soon as possible and before 12 noon on the following working day.
  • The operations crew are informed to replace your emptied container at its collection point and are asked not to block entrances to drives and gateways.
  • The council suggests that you use a permanent marker to label each container with your house number and street name to help deter theft.

To find out when your collections are due type in your address on our Collections calendar. 

North East Lincolnshire Council does not collect side waste.

What is side waste?

Side waste is household refuse which is presented outside of a standard wheelie bin, or excess bags which keep the lid raised.


What if I present my bin with side waste?

The bin will be emptied and any side waste, or any bags that kept the lid up, will be put back inside it for the next collection. You can either wait for your next collection, or use one of our Community Recycling Centres (tips) in Grimsby and Immingham to dispose of additional waste.

What can I recycle?

In North East Lincolnshire recycling is collected every week.

Properties have three coloured boxes for :

  • cans with plastic bottles (orange box)
  • paper with card/cardboard  (blue box)
  • glass bottles and jars (green box)

For a more extensive list of recyclables please see the word document to the right of this page.

For storage ideas for these boxes please open the download on the right of this page - there are brackets for the wall,or a trolley suitable if you have the smaller orange box (but not for the large).

But I live in a flat?

Flats may have a mini recycling site for cans, paper and glass - which are blue wheelie bins with red lids.

What if I have excess recycling?

Excess recycling will be taken if it is kept separated into type (i.e. plastic bottles in one container, glass bottles in another separate container etc.) and if the collection crews can easily see that it is additional recycling. Do not put it in a black bin bag tied up or they can't see and would leave it as potential side waste.
Alternatively, please take your recycling materials to the the bring to sites around North East Lincolnshire.

Waste electrics

Please take all waste electronic and electrical equipment (WEEE) to the tip where it can be separated for recycling.

Collections can be arranged for large, heavy or bulky items: see useful web pages on the right of this page and click to find the collection details: cost, days and where to present it.

Textiles

Shoes and textiles can be taken to recycling banks around the area, or donated to charity shops.

Some charity shops have textile recycling options, so check with them if you have items that are not suitable for re-use (for example if they have bleach marks, or rips, they are still good for textile recycling so please don't put them in the wheelie bin).
