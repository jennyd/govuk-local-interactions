Join your library

Join your library now

  • It is free to join the library.
  • Anyone who lives, works or studies in Surrey, can join a library.
  • If you are temporarily in Surrey for at least a week, you can join the library as a visitor.
  • There is no age limit to becoming a member. Join your baby or toddler now and get our special Pebble card!

Adults

Visit a library, bringing along something with your name and current address on it - for example: a driving licence, bank statement or utility bill. If you have only just moved to the area and don't yet have anything with your name and address on it, you can still join as a visitor.

Under 16s

If you are under 16, ask a parent or guardian to come to any library in Surrey with an official document with your family name and address on it. You will then be given your own library card, which you can use in all Surrey libraries.

If you are under 5, you can join the library for free and get your own Pebble card. 

Visitors

If you are temporarily in Surrey for at least a week, you can join the library as a visitor.

Online

Anyone with an email address who lives in Surrey can join online, and completing the form only takes a couple of minutes.

Fill in your details on the following pages (if you are under 16 we will need details of your parent, guardian or carer). Please choose a convenient Surrey library for your home base: if you only want to use eBooks and eAudiobooks, select 'eLibrary'. You will be invited to choose a Personal Identification Number (PIN), after which we will give you a temporary borrower number. You can use this number straight away to reserve books (but not eBooks or eAudiobooks) from the library catalogue.

Your library card will be sent to you in the post, and your new membership number will also be emailed to you. This will then allow you to access a wider range of services including eBooks, eAudiobooks and our online reference shelf. Your library card can be used in any of the 54 libraries in Surrey: to enjoy the full benefits of library membership, simply take your card into any Surrey library for a free upgrade.

If you have not heard from us within two weeks of joining online, please contact the library service. 

Please now continue to join online.

Library Direct

If you are unable to get to a static library because of illness or disability we can arrange for books and audio material to be brought to you on a regular basis by a volunteer.

There is no charge for this service and we also offer a free request service.

Alternatively, if you have a friend, neighbour or relative who is able to take out books on your behalf we can register you at your local library for a Library Direct Friends and Family card. This will allow your friend, neighbour or relative to borrow up to 18 books (or 12 audiobooks) for you for up to nine weeks at a time.

For more information, please see our webpage: Libraries Direct Home Service.

Library service school registration project

Surrey Libraries are currently piloting a school registration project with St Andrews School in Leatherhead that will see pupils being signed up to their local library automatically, with parental consent.

If the scheme is successful, it could be rolled out to more schools across Surrey. The intention is to register pupils on the library system en-masse using data taken from the school's student database. We are currently in the early stages of the project and in discussion with St Andrews school and our library system supplier about how best to achieve this.

This initiative is part of a larger Arts Council England supported project involving 22 local authorities.

Surrey will be receiving £4,000 towards the running of the pilot from Arts Council England. The aim of the Arts Council England scheme is to increase the number of children and families using their local library by testing new ways to increase membership.

Director of Libraries at Arts Council England, Nicky Morgan commented: 'These projects will test out genuinely exciting, new ways of encouraging children, young people and families to join and access their local libraries.'

Replacement library cards

If you lose your library card, you should let any Surrey library know as soon as possible. We will cancel your card to prevent anyone else from using it. Failure to let us know could result in you incurring additional charges.

 When you next visit the library, you will need to bring some proof of identity and we will give you a replacement card.

There is a £2 fee for a replacement card.

Please see our webpages: Terms and conditions for libraries and Libraries policies and procedures, for more information.
