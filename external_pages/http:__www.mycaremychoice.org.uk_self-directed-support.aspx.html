Self Directed Support

Understanding Self Directed Support

It is easy to get confused with the jargon around Self Directed Support, but in essence it is a way of arranging social care with the aim of giving people more options about their support services and a greater say in how they arranged.

Our aim at Stockport Council is to ensure every Stockport resident has choice and control over their own support, and that the services, support or activities they choose enable them to lead a full and active life. Self Directed Support gives you:

  • Greater control over the money given to you for your support;
  • Greater choice and flexibility over how you spend your money and what you spend your money on;
  • Greater choice over who provides your support.

How does Self Directed Support work?

After you have had your social care assessment you might be offered a Personal Budget (also known as an Individual Budget), which you can spend on services, support or activities to help improve your quality of life and meet your care needs.

Your Personal Budget can be paid to you direct (as a Direct Payment), which means you would be in charge of arranging your own support, or it can be arranged by the council.

Our video about Self Directed Support

You may wish to consider having a Managed Account. This gives you the same choice and control as a Personal Budget, but is useful if you are uncomfortable with the idea of managing it on your own. Managed accounts are offered by Stockport Council and several other external organisations.

Will I need an assessment?

Yes. An assessment will be required to determine the type and amount of support you need. An assessment is usually a discussion between yourself and a social worker to determine your level of need and eligibility.

Once you complete your assessment and there is agreement as to how much you will receive for your Personal Budget, you can look at ways to spend the money.

  • You can ask the Council to look after it for you and plan how to spend it according to your care needs;
  • You can have the money paid directly to you to arrange your care and support;
  • The Council (or another named person such as a friend or relative) can manage the money on your behalf if you are not comfortable doing so yourself.

It is up to you how you spend your money, provided it meets your needs as agreed in your care plan. The Council will contact you occasionally to see that the arrangement is still fulfilling your requirements.

Prepaid Cards

You can also have your Personal Budget paid directly onto a Prepaid Card. A prepaid card is the same size and shape as a credit or debit card and works in the same way. Using one ensures you don’t have to worry about writing a cheque or carrying money around with you. You can use your Prepaid Card to pay for support or other activities.

More about Prepaid Cards

Who can I speak to about Self Directed Support?

During your assessment you should tell your Social Worker that you are keen on Self Directed Support and would like more information on the process.

If you remain unsure if it is the right option, you can speak to the Adult Social Care Team based at Stockport Direct Contact Centre.
