What do I do if I am homeless or threatened with homelessness?

If you are at risk of losing your home, you must get help straight away. Early action can prevent you from becoming homeless.

Our housing service can advise you on the best course of action, and help you to stay in your current home or find new accommodation.

For more information, read the frequently asked questions below or download our leaflet  What do I do if I am homeless or threatened with homelessness? (PDF 341 KB)

Homelessness frequently asked questions

  • Am I eligible for help?

    Most people are eligible for help. We will not be able to help you if:

    • you are an asylum seeker;
    • you are from abroad and have limits to your right to remain (stay) in the UK;
    • you are not entitled to ‘public funds’, such as benefits;
    • you do not normally live in the UK for tax purposes; or
    • you are living in the UK illegally.

  • Will the Council help me get re-housed?

    We only have a duty to house you if:

    • you are homeless or threatened with homelessness;
    • you are in priority need;
    • you are unintentionally homeless (you have not made yourself homeless deliberately); and
    • you have a local connection to Dacorum or special circumstances.

  • What is priority need?

    We will only consider you to be in priority need if you or someone who normally lives with you:

    • is pregnant;
    • has dependent children (aged under 16, or aged 17 to 19 and in full-time education) who normally live with you;
    • is aged 16 or 17, not living with a parent or guardian, and are not already being assisted by social services;
    • is under 21 and was previously in care;
    • is vulnerable because of old age, disability, learning difficulties, having left prison or the armed forces or has mental-health issues; or
    • is at risk of domestic violence or other violence.

    If you are not in priority need, we can still provide you with housing advice, or refer you to other housing organisations and support services.

  • How do I know if I have made myself intentionally homeless?

    You will be seen as intentionally homeless if within the last six months you, or a member of your household:

    • deliberately entered into an arrangement with the person you were living with or your landlord which resulted in you losing your home;
    • took part in any form of anti-social behaviour; or
    • failed to pay your rent, when you could afford to do so.

  • What is a local connection?

    To have a local connection you, or someone who normally lives with you, must:

    • have either lived in the borough for at least six out of the last 12 months, or at least three years out of the last five years;
    • have a permanent job in the borough and work 16 hours or more a week; or
    • have a close relative who has lived in the borough for the last five years (close relatives include parents, children, brothers or sisters).

    We may accept other exceptional circumstances as meeting the conditions for a local connection.

  • What information will I need to provide at an interview?

    If you approach us for housing advice, we will ask you to meet with one of our officers. They will ask you a number of questions about your circumstances so that they can best advise you on your housing options.

     For the meeting please bring:

    • proof of ID for yourself and all members of your household;
    • proof of circumstances (for example, proof of registered disability or pregnancy);
    • proof of income and benefits;
    • proof of address for the last five years;
    • tenancy agreements;
    • mortgage information; and
    • any relevant legal documents (for example, court papers).

    All the information you provide will be handled in confidence and in line with data-protection laws.

  • Will I be allocated temporary accommodation?

    If you are eligible for help, homeless and in priority need, we will offer you temporary accommodation while we investigate your circumstances.

    In most cases, accommodation is provided in Dacorum, but sometimes you will be placed further away.  While in temporary accommodation you will have to pay a weekly service charge.

    If we do not have temporary accommodation available, we may house you in bed-and-breakfast accommodation until space becomes available.

  • How long will I stay in temporary accommodation?

    The time it takes to be permanently re-housed will depend on:

    • the size of property you need;
    • the areas you are able to live in;
    • the demand for accommodation in those areas; and
    • your support needs.

  • If I become homeless due to an emergency and the office is not open, what can I do?

    If you become homeless outside of normal working hours and cannot make your own temporary arrangements, please call the emergency homeless line on 0800 018 60 50.

    Even if you can sort out your own temporary arrangements, you still should contact us the next working day.

Additional Support Services

We also work closely with a range of local and national charities, which provide housing and homelessness advice and support within the local area. For a directory of these services please open or download the following leaflet Directory of homelessness services.

Contact us

Our Housing Options Service can advise you on the best course of action, and help you to stay in your current home or find new accommodation.
