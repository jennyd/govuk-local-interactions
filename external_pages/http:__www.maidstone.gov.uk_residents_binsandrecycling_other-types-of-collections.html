Book a Bulky Collection

If you have things that are too big to go in your bins you can book a bulky collection.

How to pay
We accept Visa, Visa Debit and Mastercard.

Number of Items

Price

1 - 4 items

£23

5 - 8 items

£33

We need to know what all the items are when taking the booking. If we don't, we won't be able to collect them. Once a booking has been taken any changes or additions can only be made by 11am, on the working day before the collection. The collection must be from a residential property. The items must be left just inside the front boundary of your property for 7am on the day of collection. If you live in a flat the items need to be left in or by the bin store. We will not collect from commercial premises, including schools and nursing homes.

About your bulky items
Each item will need to be under two metres in length so it can be lifted safely by two people. A three-piece suite is classed as three items for example a sofa and two armchairs.

Bulky garden waste must be bundled and tied. Each bundle will be counted as one item. Any loose piles of garden waste won’t be collected. Small items, such as tiles, need to be put into containers so they can be lifted safely by two people. Fridges, freezers, televisions and computer monitors can be collected using the bulky collection service. But these will be collected by a separate vehicle as they can’t be crushed.

What we won’t collect

  • Builders Rubble
  • Car parts
  • Commercial Waste
  • Garage and patio doors
  • Hazardous waste including paint, oils etc
  • Industrial waste
  • Items that can't be lifted safely by two people
  • Lengths of metal or wood above two metres
  • Loose garden waste
  • Window units

Book a Bulky Collection

If your bulky items are in a good reusable condition, why not donate them to charity? There are many charities which take reusable furniture.

To donate your items contact the organisation directly. Some you may want to consider are:

  • British Heart Foundation
  • Heart of Kent Hospice
  • The Neighbourhood Furniture Store
  • Sue Ryder

If you'd like to suggest a charity or organisation to include email waste@maidstone.gov.uk

If your items can't be reused you can take them Tovil Recycling Centre or Book a Bulky Collection.

Clinical waste is collected on a Tuesday.

The service is only available for residents. Nursing homes, hospitals, doctor surgeries and clinics should make arrangements with their waste contractor.

We can’t supply the sharps box, but you can get one from your doctor or a pharmacy. Incontinence pads and nappies don’t need a special collection. These can go in your black bin.

Register
Before you book your first collection you will need to register for the service using our clinical request form. We will also need a supporting letter from your doctor or surgery which you can email to waste@maidstone.gov.uk or send to:

Environmental Services
Maidstone Borough Council Depot
Bircholt Road
Park Wood Trading Estate
Park Wood
Maidstone
Kent
ME15 9XY

When we've received the letter, we will email you to confirm the date of your first collection.

Book a collection
You can book a collection using by calling 01622 602600.

Leave what you want collected by your front door for 7am on the day of collection. Sharps boxes should be left out on their own and not in the orange sacks. Other clinical waste should be left out in the orange sacks provided.

If you need a new sharps box please contact your doctor.

 

Do you need help putting your bin out?

If you are elderly, disabled and live alone and need help putting your rubbish and recycling out call 01622 602600.

In addition to your rubbish and recycling, we now collect small electrical items and textiles.

Small electrical items
Small electrical items will be collected on your rubbish collection day. The items should fit into a carrier bag and put next to your black rubbish bin. We can't accept televisions, computer monitors or any items that won't fit into a standard sized carrier bag.

Textiles
Textiles are collected on your recycling collection day. Again these should fit into a carrier bag and put next to your green recycling bin. We can't accept pillows or duvets or any items in black sacks or charity bags.

Batteries
Batteries will be collected on your rubbish collection day. They should be placed in a sandwich bag on the closed lid of your bin. Unfortunately we can't accept car batteries.
