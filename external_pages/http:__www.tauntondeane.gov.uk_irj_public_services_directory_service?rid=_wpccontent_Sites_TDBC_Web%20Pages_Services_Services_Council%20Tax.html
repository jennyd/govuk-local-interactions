Council Tax is a local tax set by local councils to help pay for local services such as schools, libraries, policing and refuse collection.

Latest news - Checks on single person discounts - 0345 302 2311

Council Tax payers who receive a 25% reduction on their bill because they are a single resident are currently being reviewed to make sure their reduction is correct.  These checks form part of the Council's measures to prevent and detect fraud and protect the public purse.

We have appointed Capita Local Government Services to carry out a comprehensive review. They will work with Equifax, the credit reference agency, using data-matching technology to identify anyone claiming a discount they are not entitled to.

The Council is keen to ensure that people genuinely living alone are claiming the 25% discount to which they are entitled. However, those who claim the discount improperly place an unnecessary burden on other residents.

To speak to somebody regarding the review, please contact 0345 302 2311

About this service


Council Tax is charged to owners and occupiers of domestic dwellings, including houses, bungalows, flats, maisonettes, mobile homes and houseboats.

Council Tax is not payable on non-domestic properties such as shops, factories and offices.  These types of properties will get a Business Rates (or Non-Domestic Rates) bill instead.

Some properties have a mixture of domestic and non-domestic use (for example, a pub with a flat above). We call these 'composite properties'.  We will send these properties a Council Tax bill for the domestic part and a Business Rates bill for the non-domestic part.

 

How much does it cost?


The amount of Council Tax you have to pay depends on how much the Council and certain other public bodies in your area spend and how much money they get from elsewhere.

The amount you have to pay (before any reductions) also depends on which valuation band your home is in. The lower the value of your home the less you will normally pay.

We can reduce your Council Tax bill by:

  • Awarding a discount – for example, where the dwelling is only occupied by a single adult
  • Awarding Council Tax Support – for people on low incomes
  • Awarding a disabled persons band reduction because a resident adult or child needs to use a wheeelchair indoors or where they need a special room within the house because of their disability.


One or more of these reductions may apply to your home.

 

Laws and regulation


The Local Government Finance Act 1992 (external link) introduced the Council Tax.  The Council Tax Regulations 1992 (external link) set out how the Council is to collect the tax.

General


The Department for Communities and Local Government are responsible for setting the overall Government policy on local taxation.


The Valuation Office Agency, part of Her Majesty's Revenue and Customs, carries out the valuations of domestic properties and places each dwelling in one of the eight Council Tax bands which we use to work out how much you have to pay. 


The Valuation Tribunal Service is an independent, judicial body that deals with appeals relating to Council Tax.


Local Councils, such as Taunton Deane Borough Council, are responsible for billing and collecting Council Tax.
