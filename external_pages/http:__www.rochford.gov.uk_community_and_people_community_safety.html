Community Safety


Community safety is seen as being concerned with people, communities and organisations, including families, victims and risk groups, as well as with attempting to reduce particular types of crime and the fear of crime. Community safety should be seen as the legitimate concern of all in the local community.

Achieving community safety

This became a statutory requirement under the Crime and Disorder Act 1998. This sponsored the creation in 1999 of 376 statutory local community safety partnerships throughout England and Wales. Each partnership is now required to conduct an audit of crime and disorder every year within its area called a Strategic Assessment, alongside consulting the local community and delivering an Annual Partnership Plan containing multi-agency action plans to tackle the priorities highlighted within the Strategic Assessment.  Although the assessment is a restricted document, the Annual Partnership Plan is available for public viewing on the Castle Point & Rochford Community Safety Partnership (CSP) page on the link listed below.

Community Older Persons Event (COPE), Hockley

The Rochford District Community Safety Partnership and Hockley Parish Council are inviting Hockley residents to a special event aimed at helping them to ‘Stay Safe’. The event, funded by Hockley Parish Council and Essex Community Foundation, is taking place on Wednesday 8th April 2015 from 10.45am to 3.30pm at Hockley Community Centre, Westminster Drive, Hockley, SS5 4XD and will have lots of different stalls and presentations offering advice and help for older residents. Free transportation, refreshments and lunch are also on offer!

Whether you want to speak to the Fire Service, Essex Police, Neighbourhood Watch, Essex Trading Standards or Community Safety officers from the Council – they will all be on hand to offer advice and support on a number of issues aimed at helping older residents to stay safe. There will also be a wide range of other organisations at the event too, including The Harbour Club, Citizens Advice Bureau, Alzheimer's Society and Talking Newspapers, all offering help, advice and a friendly chat.

Places must be booked in advance. To reserve your place, please call the Rochford District Community Safety Team on 01702 318103.

NEW PCC 1-1 Surgeries

From 13th November 2014, the PCC has been trialling a one-to-one surgery model, in addition to his public meeting schedule. Further details can be found online using the link in the Related Links section below.

PCC surgeries will directly precede the PCC’s district public meetings, and will be a chance for members of the community – particularly those who may not be comfortable speaking up in a public meeting, to raise issues, ideas and concerns with the PCC directly and in a more private setting.

This will not replace the existing process for making complaints to the PCC or to Essex Police, and members of the public may be referred to these processes as part of their consultation.

To book an appointment to attend a surgery near you please send an email to pcc@essex.pnn.police.uk

Please put the word SURGERY REQUEST in the subject line of your email. Alternatively you can ring the PCC office to book an appointment on 01245 291 600.

Essex Crime Victim website

A new website which offers help, information and advice to victims of crime across Essex has been launched.

The website has been set up by Essex PCC Nick Alston. You can find the site in related links below

Designed in consultation with victims and their representatives, the Essex Victim Gateway offers direct access to many local support groups for people who have been subjected to crimes ranging from fraud to domestic abuse.

The website offers help to all victims, including those who have not reported the crime to the police or where offences have happened in the past.
