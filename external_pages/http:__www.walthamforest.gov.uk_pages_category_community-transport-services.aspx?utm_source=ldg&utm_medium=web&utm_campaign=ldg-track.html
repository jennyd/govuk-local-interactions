Getting out and about

There are various schemes to help disabled or older people get out and about whether it's a trip to the shops, to the doctor’s or visiting friends or places of interest.

Blue badge disabled parking scheme

Blue parking badges allow cars carrying people who are registered blind or who have severe walking difficulties to be parked near shops, stations and other facilities.

Blue badges can only be issued to people who meet the eligibility criteria, not to relatives or carers. They can be used in any car the badge holder is driving or is a passenger in.

For more information see:

  • Disabled person’s parking permit
  • Blue badges for organisations

Freedom passes for the over 60s and disabled people

If you are over 60 or have a disability and live in London you can apply for a Freedom Pass which means you can enjoy free off-peak travel on local buses, the tube and overground in London.

For more information see:

  • Freedom passes for disabled people
  • Freedom passes for older people

Taxicard scheme

The Taxicard scheme provides subsidised travel in licensed black taxis and private hire vehicles for residents with serious permanent mobility problems and those with severe sight impairment.

Taxicard users can journey when and where they want within the Greater London area. The Council pays towards a limited number of trips and you are expected to pay the first £2.50 towards the cost of each trip. If the journey goes further or above the subsidy then you will have to pay the extra.

For more information see Taxicard scheme.

Loan of a mobility scooter

You can loan a mobility scooter for up to one week from Shopmobility. Tel: 020 8520 3366.

Door to door transport to shops

This is a safe, reliable and accessible door to door transport service for older and disabled people to visit shops and supermarkets in and around Waltham Forest.

See Community Transport for more information, or ring 020 8521 0665 or email info@ctwf.co.uk.

Disabled persons railcard

If you have a disability that makes travelling by train difficult you might qualify for the Disabled Persons Railcard.

The Disabled Persons Railcard allows you to get 1/3 off most rail fares throughout Great Britain.

The railcard is not issued by the Council but can be bought online or at a local railway station.

If you're travelling with an adult companion they also can get 1/3 off their rail fare.

How much is it?

  • £20 for a one-year Railcard
  • £54 for a three-year Railcard

Who is eligible?

You need to give proof that you are eligible for a Railcard. You might qualify if you: have a visual impairment, a hearing impairment, have epilepsy or are in receipt of a disability-related benefit.

Older persons railcard

The Senior Railcard gives people aged 60 or over reduced cost train travel in Great Britain.

The railcard is not issued by the Council.

Am I eligible?

You're eligible to buy a Senior Railcard if you are 60 years old or over.

How much does it cost?

  • £30 for a 1 year card
  • £70 for a 3 year card

How to apply

Please visit www.senior-railcard.co.uk/buy-senior-railcard.
