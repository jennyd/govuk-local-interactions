Bins and Collections

All households in the Rochford District are provided with three bins, including flats and parkland homes. One for compostables, one for recyclables and one for non-recyclables.

Bins should be put at the boundary of your property by 7am on your collection day. The compostable bins are collected weekly, along with either the non-recyclable or recyclable bins, which are collected on an alternate week basis.

If you live in a flat there is no need to move the bins, they will be collected and returned to the allocated bin areas.

To find out your collection day and which bin is collected in which week in your area, please check the bin collection day tool and the calendar to the right.

Missed bin collections

Reasons why your bin may have been missed:

Do you have a red sticker on your bin?

This means your bin has incorrect items in it, and is 'contaminated'. Incorrect items must be removed from the bin to make sure the bin will be collected on your next scheduled collection day. Please see our pages 'What goes where?' and A to Z of Recycling for all the information on what goes in each bin.

Common wrong items

The items below should not be put in the recyclables or compostables bin.

  • black sacks
  • large plastic sacks
  • plastic wrapping
  • textiles
  • nappies

Also, please do not put plastic carrier bags into your compostables bin.

Were the correct bins put out at the boundary of your property by 7am on your specific collection day?

If not, this is the reason why your bin has not been collected. Please make sure that the correct bins are put out at the boundary of your property by 7am on your next collection day.

If you are not sure, please see the related documents below for the recycling & non-recycling collection calendar.

If yes, you can report the missed collection using the online form in related links below.

Special collections

The Council offers help to residents who have problems with their waste and recycling collections. This includes:

  • assistance with moving bins on collection days
  • help with residents who struggle with understanding the scheme
  • advice for residents who regularly produce extra waste due to specific circumstances

If you would like to speak to someone about the services we could offer you please call Customer Services on 01702 318111

Charges for new bins

Rochford District Council has introduced a £168 charge for providing wheeled bins to:

  • new build properties
  • converted properties*

*A converted property refers to a property which has been converted from a non domestic property to a domestic property, or where a domestic property has been converted into two or more domestic properties.

Each year the Council spends in excess of £60,000 providing and replacing wheeled bins .The new charges are a one-off cost, for the service of administering and delivering the bins. The charge for the collection of waste from your bins is already covered by your Council Tax.

Waste collection is one of the statutory services the Council must provide and as part of the Environmental Protection Act 1990, Councils are allowed to specify and charge for bins to be provided.

The charge for a set of three bins is £168, these are:

  • a non recyclables bin which is collected every two weeks
  • a recyclables bin which is collected every two weeks
  • a compostables bin for food and garden waste which is collected every week

If you have moved into a new property and wheeled bins have not been provided, please contact the developer who can then contact Rochford District Council to arrange payment and delivery of your wheeled bins.

If the developer does not pay for the bins, then the resident is liable for the payment.

The bins remain the property of Rochford District Council and cannot be taken with the resident if they move.

Keeping your bins safe

Once you have your bins, you should mark them clearly with your house name or number to prevent them from going missing or being stolen. This can be painted on your bins.

If your collection is from the rear alley you should ensure you have your house name or number on your gate.

For full details on this policy please see the bin charging questions and answers in related documents.
