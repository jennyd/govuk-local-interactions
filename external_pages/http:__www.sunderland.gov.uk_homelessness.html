I need somewhere to live

Jump to:

  • Related links
  • External links

The advice available will depend on your circumstances. Follow the link that best describes your circumstances to find out what help may be available to you.

Related links

  • I am sleeping rough - If you are faced with the possibility of sleeping rough tonight, contact us immediately.
  • I have been asked to leave by a family member or friend - If you have been asked to leave your current accommodation by a family member or friend, there are several ways in which organisations can give you help or support.
  • I am a Gypsy or traveller and need to find permanent accommodation - If you find yourself in one of the situations on this page, you should contact the council in the area that you have the strongest connection (for example, where you work, have family, or have stayed the longest).
  • I am leaving the armed services and need somewhere to live - If you have housing questions or problems, there is help available for you if you are in the Armed Forces, a veteran or a family member of someone in the Armed Forces or a veteran.
  • I am considering renting from a private landlord - The privately rented sector comprises accommodation which is privately owned and rented out by a landlord, letting agent or estate agent (that is, not by a council or housing association.
  • I am leaving hospital and need somewhere to live - If you are leaving hospital with no where to live, there is a joint agreement between Sunderland Hospital and Sunderland City Council.
  • I am leaving prison and need somewhere to live - If you are leaving prison and have no accommodation to return to, you should contact the Housing Officer within the prison service who will make contact with the council on your behalf.
  • I am considering lodging with someone - If you want to lodge with someone, you'll need to work out how both you and your landlord's rights are affected.
  • I am considering a mutual exchange - Mutual Exchange allows existing council or housing association tenants to swap their property with another local authority or housing association tenant.
  • I am considering shared ownership - If you live in England and can't afford to buy a home, you may be able to get some financial help through a home ownership scheme. There are 3 types of scheme: First Buy equity loans; shared ownership; or NewBuy.
  • I have been asked to leave my current accommodation which is linked to my employment - Tied accommodation is accommodation that is provided as a part of a person's job and is conditioned by the worker's continued employment with his/her employer.
  • I am frightened in my own home because there is a risk of violence, threats or other forms of harassment - If you are in immediate danger, please contact the police on 999. If you are in danger of violence resulting from neighbourhood problems, please use the contact details on this page.
  • I am part of a close family but we are currently living apart - If you and your family normally reside together but are forced to live apart due to your housing situation, you should consider the housing options on this page.
  • My home is overcrowded - If your accommodation is much too small for your household you may be considered to be living in overcrowded conditions under the law. Your home may be legally overcrowded if there are not enough rooms or space for the number of people who live there.
  • I am the victim of domestic violence or honour-based violence - Domestic Violence is described as any incident of threatening behaviour, violence or abuse (psychological, physical, sexual, financial or emotional) between adults, aged 18 and over, who are or have been intimate partners or family members, regardless of gender and sexuality.
  • My landlord is harassing me/trying to force me out illegally - It's a crime for your landlord to harass you or try to force you out of a property without using proper procedures (illegal eviction). If this happens, you may have a right to claim damages through the court.
  • My home is in a state of disrepair - If you feel your home is in disrepair, contact your landlord and allow reasonable time for repairs to be carried out. Examples of disrepair could include damp, failure of gas safety checks, structural repair, fittings and fixtures not working or unsafe electrical wiring.
  • I am considering taking in a lodger or subletting. Am I allowed? - A lodger is someone who rents a room in her/his landlord's home and who shares living space with her/him. Some lodgers receive services, such as meals or cleaning, as part of their agreement. Only certain people are allowed to take in lodgers. If you want to do so, you'll need to work out how both you and your lodger's rights are affected.
  • Homelessness assessment - If you have looked at the information in our other housing advice pages and are still at risk of being made homeless, this page will help you to determine if the council has a duty to re-house you.
