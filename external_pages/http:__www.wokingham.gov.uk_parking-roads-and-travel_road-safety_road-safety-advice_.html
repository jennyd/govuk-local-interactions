Road safety advice

Safety for all road users 

We help promote road safety for all our road users.

  • Child car seats and road safety for children

Walking to school

Use our School Crossing Patrol service when crossing the road to get to school. Read our School Crossing Patrollers fact sheet (PDF document) to find out more about their role.

Visit the 'be bright, be seen' advice on the Think! website to teach your child to wear high visibility clothing while out walking or cycling.

Watch the Sustrans video promoting a safer journey to school for children walking and cycling.

We can loan out teaching aids to schools and nurseries - such as zebra crossing mats, signs, books and games.      

Crossing the road

Read the green cross code page on the Think! website to teach your child how to cross the road safely.

Every Year 3 pupil takes home a safer steps DVD and workbook so they can do some pedestrian training with parents.

Visit the Tales of the Road website for advice about how to keep your child safe on the roads - includes films, games and interactive activities.  

We also run road safety shows over 4 weeks annually for primary age children. To find out more about our road safety events:

  • Email road.safety@wokingham.gov.uk
  • Phone 0118 974 6387

Child car seats

Children must be appropriately restrained when travelling in a car. Our Booster Boy campaign aims to raise awareness of the need to use booster seats in cars for children under the height of 135cm.

For more information visit:

  • Child Car Seats website - tips on how to choose a seat, information on the law and safety standards
  • Good Egg website - find out about how they are raising awareness of common child seat fitting errors, and how to rectify them to improve in-car child safety
  • Watch the YouTube video on child car seat safety - which demonstrates ‘common faults’ with the way child seats are fitted including loose or twisted harnesses, padded clothing, ‘buckle crunch’ (where the adult belt rests on the child seat) and incorrect seat belt routing and how easy it is to rectify these
  • Article by Good Egg website - learn about major concern with child car seats as recent research discovers 43% growth in incorrect fitment or incompatibility in the last five years
  • Child Accident Prevention Trust website - learn about how nearly a third of 3-10 year-olds use backless booster cushions which offer no side protection on impact warns Which?
  • Transport Research Laboratory child safety centre website - information about the latest child car seat tests

Tips for safe cycling

  • Cycle in a prominent position, so that you can be seen clearly
  • Signal your movements clearly
  • Make sure your lights are working and are fitted correctly
  • Wear high visibility clothing and reflective materials
  • You can use our helmet order form (PDF document) to order a cycle helmet
  • Take extra care when cycling at night, when you'll be less visible to motorists
  • Visit the urban limits website for more cycling advice for young adult cycling enthusiasts

Driver safety

All drivers and passengers must wear a seat belt, by law. Visit the Think! website seatbelt video to find out more about the importance of seat belts.

To raise awareness of the dangers of country roads, THINK! has partnered with a local farmer to turn potential hazards into helpful hazards. Watch the Think! country roads information video on the YouTube website.

To find out more about stopping distances visit the stopping distances simulator website.

How to safely overtake cyclists

Watch a film about how to overtake cyclists safely on the Road Safety Knowledge Centre website.

The video features British Cycling’s Chris Boardman and driving instructor Blaine Walsh.

Dangers of drink / drug driving    

In the last 50 years road casualties caused by drink driving have fallen dramatically. However in 2013 there were still 260 deaths due to drink driving. This accounts for 1 in 6 of all road deaths with 8000 casualties in all. 

By drinking and driving you risk your life, those of your passengers and others on the road. 

Drivers under the age of 24 are 54% more likely than average to be impaired by alcohol - men nearly 20% more likely.

The crashes involving impaired drivers are also more serious - twice as likely to result in someone being killed.

Watch the Thames Valley Police video where will you end up tonight on You Tube website to see the negative impact drink driving can have.

Don't drink / use drugs and drive

Alcohol affects your ability to drive. You risk a fine of up to £5,000, a minimum 12-month driving ban and a criminal record. 

Driving while unfit through drugs (illegal, prescribed or over-the-counter) is an offence which carries the same penalty as drink driving. If you take medicine for an illness, ask the doctor or chemist about side effects before you drive. New drug drive legislation came into force in March 2015 which includes 8 prescription drugs and makes it easier for police to catch and convict drug drivers.

To find out more visit:

  • Blazed and Wasted website - includes a drink drive calculator and drug drive advice
  • Think! website dangers of drink driving
  • Think! website dangers of drug driving
  • Morning after calculator on the Morning After website - helps you find out if your safe to drive after a night of driving, if in doubt don't drive

Don't drive and use your mobile phone

Research shows that using a mobile phone whilst driving - including hands free - is the worst distraction.

You are 4 times more likely to be involved in a fatal road traffic collision if you are using your mobile phone.

To find out more visit:

  • Think! website dangers of mobile phones
  • Safer Roads website dangers of mobile phones
