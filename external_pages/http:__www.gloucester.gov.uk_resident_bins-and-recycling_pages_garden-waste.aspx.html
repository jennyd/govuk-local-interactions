Green Wheeled Bin (collected fortnightly)

This is a chargeable service at a cost of £36 per bin per subscription period. The subscription period has been amended and now runs from 1 October to 30 September each year. A letter was sent to residents on 10 August 2015 who were currently taking part in the service detailing the changes being made.

If you are receiving Housing Benefit or Council Tax Support, then the cost is reduced to £18 per year.  However, you must provide your Housing Benefit or Council Tax Support reference number.

Once you have signed up, the service is continuous unless cancelled.  A renewal notice will be sent annually. Residents who have signed up to the service will receive a sticker, after payment has been received, to put onto the lid of their green garden waste bin. The property number should be written on the sticker to help with identification on collection day.

More information and FAQ’s about the garden waste service.

 Remember:

  • Gloucester City Council has a closed bin policy, so your bin should be presented, on the kerbside on the day of collection, by 7am, with the lid closed. 
  • Any additional bags left next to the bin will not be collected by the crew.
  • To remove your bin, from the kerbside, at the end of the collection day
  • Garden waste bins presented without a current sticker will not be emptied and may be retrieved.
  • The Garden Waste Service is suspended for two weeks over the Christmas and New Year period.

Garden Waste Service - Full Terms and Conditions

Is the service available to all householders?

The service is available for those properties where the wheeled bin can be safely stored away from the pavement and is not classed as a hard to reach property. If you think your property may have access problems, please call or email us.

Which items will you collect?

The items we collect include:

  • Grass Cuttings
  • Hedge Trimmings
  • Plants and cut flowers
  • Twigs and small branches
  • Weeds, plants, leaves and bark
  • Real Christmas trees cut to fit (these should be cut no thicker than your wrist and should be cut to fit into the bin)
  • Herbivore Waste (sawdust and straw bedding with droppings produced by small vegetarian animals, such as rabbits, Guinea pigs, hamsters etc).

Which items will you not collect?

The items we will not collect include:

  • Food Waste (use your brown food caddy)
  • Garden tools and furniture
  • Soil, bricks and rubble
  • Cardboard and paper
  • Plastic pots and labels
  • Vegetable peelings
  • Animal faeces
  • Plastic bags
  • Chemicals
  • Ash
  • Soiled newspaper used for animal bedding.

Preferred payment method

In order to reduce costs and to ensure continued service, we are recommending customers sign up to an annual Direct Debit payment. You will receive confirmation of the dates for collection of payment.

Other ways to pay for the Garden Waste Service

  • By phone (01452 396396) - for both new applications and paying an invoice, have your credit / debit card or bank details ready.
  • By post:
    • By cheque, new applications - complete a Cheque Application form, then send it to us along with your cheque.
    • By cheque, paying an invoice - write the invoice number and account reference number on the back of your cheque and send it to us.
    • By direct debit - for both new applications and paying an invoice, download and complete a Direct Debit Bank Instruction and an Application form and send them to us.
  • In person at Herbert Warehouse Reception
    • New application - ask at reception
    • Paying an invoice - pay at the kiosk in reception

Please Note:
If using a credit card, there will be a surcharge of 1.8%, which will add 65p per bin to the total amount.  If you are in receipt of Housing or Council Benefit, an extra 32p will be added, per bin, to the total amount.

Bin problems?

If you:

  • have recently moved house
  • need an assisted collection. An Assisted Collection is the collection of recycling or waste containers by the collection crew from an agreed point in the front of the property and the return of the empty container back to that point. It is provided for households where there is no one living at the property who is physically able to carry the waste and recycling to the boundary for collection. The service is not available for residents that use communal bin stores
  • have lost or damaged your bin
  • your bin has not been emptied (Please notify us within 2 working days). Remember bins presented without a sticker will not be emptied and may be retrieved.

    please email: heretohelp@gloucester.gov.uk or ring: 01452 396396
