asbestos

Listen

Asbestos is harmful and can cause serious diseases. It is a material used as an insulator in materials to build or refurbish properties before 2000. Materials that could contain asbestos include ceiling and floor tiles; window panels; pipe insulation (lagging); boilers and spray coatings. 

Every commercial premises has a legal duty to manage all asbestos within its control to prevent anyone from being exposed to it. The HSE Asbestos Essential Advice Information is available. Managing asbestos in your premises will involve:

  • Identifying whether there is asbestos in your premises, determining what type it is and what condition it is in. See the HSE free download on completing an asbestos survey;
  • Undertaking a risk assessment to determine the likelihood of exposure based on the condition and location of the asbestos. See the HSE risk assessment template;
  • Deciding if work needs to be done to the asbestos. Most work to asbestos must be done by a licensed contractor and these can be found using Yellow Pages or the HSE Register

what to do next...

A licensed contractor will need to provide 14 days notice about work with asbestos insulation, asbestos coating or asbestos insulating board in office, shops and warehouses. We have the right to assess the proposals and inspect the site before and during the work. If the work is in a factory or public building, you should send notification to the Health & Safety Executive (HSE).

All unlicensed work needs to be carried out with the appropriate controls in place. But for notifiable non-licensed work (NNLW), employers also have additional requirements to:

  • Notify work with asbestos to the relevant enforcing authority (see document below);
  • Ensure medical examinations are carried out; and
  • Maintain registers of work (health records).

further information

Asbestos in the home

Asbestos and related materials can be found in many areas of the home as well as in industrial and commercial premises. For further information to assist you in identifying where such materials may be found in houses, contact the Health & Safety Executive.

Householders cannot dispose of asbestos at the local recycling centres due to licensing restrictions as it is classed as hazardous waste.

Asbestos can be disposed of using a licensed disposal contractor - check the Yellow Pages or the Asbestos Removal Contractors Association (ARCA) or alternatively, you can take it to a licensed disposal site.
