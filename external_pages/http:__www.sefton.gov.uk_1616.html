GOING TO PRIMARY SCHOOL IN SEFTON

Primary Schools Admissions Information Booklet 2016-17

Sefton has produced a primary schools admissions information booklet to give you all the information about Primary Education in Sefton and to support parents/carers in choosing a primary school. We hope you find it helpful, particularly if your child is about to start school for the first time in September 2016.

Due to the large file size of the booklet, it has also been split into smaller files for easier access.

1. I cannot access my online application or I have forgotten my password?

Please contact the admissions team directly on 0151 934 3590.

2. Which school will be offered and when?
  • If your child can be offered a place at more than one of your preferred schools we will offer you a place at the highest preferred school listed on your application form
  • If your child cannot be offered a place at any of the schools listed as preferences on your application form we will offer you the nearest appropriate community or voluntary aided school to your home address with an available place
  • We will send you an email on 18 April 2016 informing you which school we have offered your child a place. You will also be able to access the offer information itself online from this date
  • If you applied via a paper application form your letter will be posted on 18 April 2016 informing you which school we have offered your child a place. You will usually receive this within 48 hrs.
3. How do I accept the place offered?

You will not have to accept the place offered. It is allocated automatically and you should only return a decline slip if you are declining the place offered. Full details will be enclosed with the email and letter.

4. After allocation, can I change my preferences?

Please complete the primary late application/change of preference form and submit it to the admissions team.

The changes will be dealt with in accordance with the published process highlighted in the admissions information booklet.

5. What if I don't have Internet access?

If you do not have access to the Internet you can complete a paper application form by contacting the Sefton schools admissions team, who will provide you with an application form and Primary admissions information booklet.

You can then return the paper application form to your preferred primary school, where you must ask for a receipt.

6. I want my child to be put on a waiting list, what should I do?

Your child will automatically be placed on a waiting list for a school you have been refused (providing it was a higher preference than the school you were offered). This is regardless of whether you appeal.

7. How can I appeal when I have been refused a place?

Full details will be contained in the letter posted on 18 April 2016 including an appeal form and how to appeal.
