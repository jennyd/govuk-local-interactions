Children's Performance Licences

Local Authorities are responsible for licensing children who take part in TV/theatre/radio, modeling and paid sporting activities in accordance with 'The Children and Young Persons Act 1933 and 1963' and The Children (Performances and Activities)(England) Regulations 2014.  Please check this site regularly for further updates. 

A licence for children taking part in Performances is issued to the Applicant, the Applicant being: "the person responsible for the production of the performance in which the child is to take part" (production/theatre company), for the specified performance and for the duration of the specified performance (not exceeding 6 months).

Once the application has been assessed and is in order, taking into account the current regulations including the number of hours the child will be required on set, whether there will be any late nights required, etc and the Central Attendance and Employment Support team (CAEST) is satisfied that all guidelines are being met, a licence will be issued.  If a child requires time off school to take part in a performance Hertfordshire County Council will only issue a licence if the absence has been agreed by the Headteacher.

Headteachers may agree to a child being absent from school under Regulation 7.(1)(2)(a)/(b) of The Education (Pupil Registration) (England) (Regulations) 2006.  This is not affected by recent changes to legislation preventing headteacher's authorising leave of absence for family holidays.

The application form(s) fully completed with all relevant documentation attached must arrive at our offices no later than 7 working days before the performance takes place. For Large scale productions eg. pantomimes, 21 days are required.

A complete application must include:

  • Part 1
  • Part 2
  • Chaperone information
  • Birth certificate
  • Parental Health Declaration
  • School letter giving permission to be absent
  • Photo.

Notification alone will not constitute an application within the deadline. It should be noted that chasing up of incomplete or missing paperwork is the responsibility of the Applicant/Agent and needs to be completed within the deadlines described above. Applications received with less than the due notice may not be processed. If you are unable to provide 7 days notice, please contact us on 01992 588605 or by email. All licences will be posted to Applicant, Parents, authorities - 1st class.

Applications should be returned to: the Children in Entertainment Officer, CAEST, Intergrated Services for Learning, Room 134, County Hall, Pegs Lane, Hertford, Herts, SG13 8DF or by email.

Children who have reached the end of their statutory education do not need a Child Performance Licence.  This is not affected by the raising of the participation age to 17/18.  

Performance and Activity (sport and modelling)

Part I of the form is completed by the applicant. Part II of the form is to be completed by the child’s parent. Once Part I and Part II have been fully completed, the following documentation must be attached:

  • One passport size photograph of the child taken within the last six months
  • A copy of the child's birth certificate or copy of passport
  • A letter from the child's headteacher authorising any absences they may need to have
  • The fully completed form with all relevant documentation attached can then be sent to the Children in Entertainment Officer at the address below.

Body of persons approval for a single production

The Children and Young Persons Act 1963 S37(3)(b) gives a Local Authority the power to issue an exemption licence to a Body of Persons ie a group of responsible adults (youth organisations, amateur drama societies, dance schools, church groups  etc ) to enable them to engage in performances without the need to apply for a separate licence for each child. This is known as a Body of Persons Approval.

The following criteria must also be met:

  • The child performers (or any other person on their behalf) cannot receive payment other than expenses
  • The child performers must not take time off school for rehearsals or performances
  • All performances relating to a Body of Persons Approval granted by HCC must take place within the county of Hertfordshire
  • The organisation must provide a copy of their child protection policy
  • The organisation must agree to Hertfordshire County Council’s terms and Conditions for Body of Persons Approval

How to Apply:

Application must be made by completing the Hertfordshire County Council Body of Persons Approval Application form and the Contract of Agreement. The application is normally made by the chairperson or secretary of the organisation. The applicant must have the authority to agree to HCC Terms and Conditions for Body of Persons Approval on behalf of the organisation and the application must be accompanied by the supporting documentation as detailed in the application pack.

Applications must be received no later than 21 days before the date of performance.

Further guidance on Body of Persons Approval is available on the Department for Education website: safeguarding child performers.

Applications should be returned to: the Children in Entertainment Officer, CAEST, Intergrated Services for Learning, Room 134, CH0136, County Hall, Pegs Lane, Hertford, Herts, SG13 8DF or by email as an attachment.

Licences for Matrons / Chaperones

Please note that these licences are subject to criminal background checks. To obtain an application pack or more information on how to apply, please contact the Children in Entertainment Officer, Anne Scott on 01992 588605 or by email.

For guidance and information on your responsibilities regarding chaperoning children in entertainment please read the Chaperoning Guide.

Hertfordshire County Council (HCC), Children's Services regards the lawful and correct treatment of personal information as very important to carrying out its operations successfully and to maintaining confidence between those with whom we deal and ourselves.  We ensure that our organisation treats personal information lawfully and correctly.

HCC, Children's Services needs to collect and use certain types of information about people with whom it deals in order to operate and any details of the services we provide or information about you or your family will be stored and used in strict accordance with our registration under the Data Protection Act 1998.

In order to provide effective services for children and young people, information provided may be shared with other Children's Services teams within HCC, where there is a service requirement.
