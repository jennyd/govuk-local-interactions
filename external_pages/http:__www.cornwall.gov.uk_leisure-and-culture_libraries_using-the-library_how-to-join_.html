How to join

Last updated: 11/12/2015

Library membership is free to everyone living, working or studying in Cornwall and Devon including Torbay and Plymouth, for more than one month. 

Join a library online

or visit your local library where staff will be happy to help you.

The first time you visit the library you will be asked to provide proof of your address by showing us one of the following:

  •  a utility or council tax bill showing your current address
  • driving licence showing your current address  
  • cheque or credit card together with a letter showing the same name and your address
  • if you have recently moved, a letter from a solicitor/estate agent or letting agent referring to the purchase or lease of the property 
  • for concessions, refugees, asylum seekers or full-time students, you will need proof of entitlement to benefits stating your address 

By joining the library you agree to abide by the library regulations and by-laws.

On request a courtesy email will be sent in respect of items due from Libraryservice.feedback@cornwall.gov.uk (please add to your safe list).   If an item is overdue, the fine will be recorded on your account.

To allow us to offer you this service, please note it’s your responsibility that we have your up to date email address.

Children's membership

If you are under 16 your parent or guardian must sign your joining form.

Concessionary membership

Concessionary membership offers lower overdue items and computer charges.You and your family living at the same address will qualify for concessionary membership if you provide evidence that you are in receipt of any of the following benefits:

  • council tax support
  • housing benefit
  • job seekers allowance (income based)
  • working tax credit
  • employment and support allowance
  • pension credit
  • full-time student (attending college for more than 12 hours a week)

Membership lasts for one year. On expiry you will be asked to produce evidence of continued entitlement. 

Access membership

If you suffer from visual impairment or a condition which makes it difficult to handle or read books, or you have a learning difficulty, including dyslexia, you are entitled to:

  • free loans of talking books and CDs
  • free reservations
  • concessionary rates on library charges 
  • Use of PC is free 60 minutes per day, with no extension; then £1.80 per hour (non-members £3.60 per hour)

Please print out and complete the Access membership form and get it signed by your doctor, optician or another professional such as a social worker or teacher who can confirm that you are eligible for Access membership. Take your completed form to your local library. If you are not already a library member, please also bring along some proof of your name and address.

Visitors card

Home library ticket holders (issued by a library in England, Wales or Northern Ireland

If you have a valid home library ticket (issued by a library in England, Wales or Northern Ireland), you can join the library as a visitor with full library membership.  The library will issue you with a visitor card. 

If you wish to use the library computers the library will also need a PIN issued by the library with your visitor card.

Non home library ticket holders

If you do NOT have a valid home library ticket (issued by a library  in England, Wales or Northern Ireland), you may join the library as a library visitor.  You will be asked to complete a visitor registration card with your current home address and length of stay. You will need to show proof of identification, for example, a passport, credit card, driving licence or cheque book.  The library will then issue you with a visitor card.  

If you want to use library computer, you can join library as a library visitor by completing the computer/PN signing in form (please ask in your library). You will need to complete the form every time you use a library computer.
