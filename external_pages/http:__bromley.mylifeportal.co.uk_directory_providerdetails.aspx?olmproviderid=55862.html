RVS Community Transport - previously WRVS

  Save to MyPad

What is MyPad?

Service summary

Royal Voluntary Service (previously the WRVS)  Community Transport is a friendly, community, door to door scheme designed to help you get out and about e.g.

  • to attend any health appointments
  • to visit family and friends
  • to go to a lunch club
  • to go shopping
  • or simply to go for a run

When you reach your destination, our volunteers can lend a hand if needed.

Client groups

This service is for the over 55s.

Service details

WRVS are not just providing the transport , they are offering a service of "care", by collecting the user from their doorstep to ensuring safe arrival at their destination, (especially useful in dementia cases) ensuring that each journey taken with them is a friendly , personal service. It can be arranged for the volunteer driver to accompany them on any visit they may require, such as medical appointments, shopping and where needed they would be happy to carry the bags into the house. 

Further information

WRVS also provide a Befriending service, which again is to ensure that users are less isolated by providing company for them to chat to.  All of our volunteers are DBS cleared (formerley known as CRB) and we will ensure that once the user is happy with  a particular volunteer we will assign him/her to the user for as long as is needed.
This is a Free service to anyone over the age of 55, however it is important that the it is understood that the volunteers are Not carers and will not be involved in any official caring duties, such as washing, feeding etc, the volunteers is there as a friend only

Area served

Greater London, Bromley

Accessibility

Some volunteers drive their own cars, while some will have WRVS lease cars and both are fully insured. All our drivers have been checked by the Criminal Records Bureau and they all carry identification so you can be sure they are genuine.
If you have any worries you can call us on our local number or the general enquiries number.

Price

The cost of the journey is mileage based and will depend on whether it is a one off journey or not. The idea being to keep elderly residents , independent, mobile and happy for as long as possible by providing assisted transport at a reduced cost (currently 60p a mile)
