School Transport

Last updated: 06/01/2016

School Transport is generally limited to those children attending their designated school or nearest school with a place available and when one of the following apply:

  • The school is more than two miles from the home address for primary pupils and more than three miles for secondary pupils;
  • The pupil is unable to walk to school by reason of their special educational need, disability or mobility problem;
  • the nature of the route is deemed by the Local Authority as being unsuitable for pedestrian use.

There are additional entitlements for children from families on low income (in receipt of Free School Meals or their maximum level of Working Tax Credits).  Children from low income families are eligible for free transport to one of their nearest three secondary schools within two to six miles or their nearest secondary school preferred on the grounds of religion or belief within two to fifteen miles.

Transport will occasionally be provided in other exceptional circumstances at the Local Authority's discretion.

Transport will be provided via the most cost-effective suitable method.

Transport is provided to and from school at the scheduled start and finish times Monday to Friday. Transport will not be scheduled to accommodate a child's individual timetable or extra-curricular activities.

Apply for Home to School Transport

If you need help completing the application or are unsure of your eligibility for free transport, the Pupil Transport Team will be able to advise you.  The team can be contacted on 0300 1234 101 or by email at pupiltransport@cornwall.gov.uk.
