Wigan and Leigh Homes/Tenants/The Right To Buy

Right To Buy

  • Right To Buy ProcessTo apply you need to complete a Right to Buy application form. Or they can request a form by either telephone or the contact us email form.
  • EligibilityAn explanation of specific criteria and related advice for eligibility.
  • DiscountsAny time you spend as a tenant of a public sector landlord will count towards your discount.
  • Costs to ConsiderAlthough there is no charge for processing your Right to Buy application, you need to be aware that there are other costs involved when you buy your home.
  • LeaseholdersWhen you were discussing the purchase of your flat with your solicitor, you will have been advised that the flat was being sold to you on a leasehold basis.
  • Paying Service ChargesYou can pay your quarterly service charge bills in several different ways.
  • Insurance ArrangementsThe Council insures your flat against the loss of or damage caused to the buildings. You do not need to take out additional buildings insurance.
  • Selling Your FlatWhen a Council flat is initially sold under the Right to Buy the Leaseholder is granted a 125 year lease. If you sell your flat in the future, the new owner will acquire the time remaining on the lease
