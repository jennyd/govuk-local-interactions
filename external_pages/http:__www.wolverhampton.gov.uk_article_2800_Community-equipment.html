Community equipment

View what equipment is available in Wolverhampton to help older people or people with a disability with daily living.

Independent Living Service

The Independent Living Service provides equipment and adaptations which help people of all ages who have problems with everyday tasks through illness, old age or disability. 

Equipment is available through an integrated service run in partnership by Adult Social Care services and Wolverhampton City Primary Care Trust.  The aim of the Independent Living Service is to maintain people's health, well-being and independence. 

Equipment and adaptations can only be provided following an assessment of your needs through the Neville Garratt Centre for Independent Living.

Available equipment

A range of equipment is available including:

  • home nursing equipment
  • equipment for daily living, like raised toilet seats, shower chairs or liquid level indicators
  • minor adaptations like grab rails or lever taps
  • equipment for people with a sensory impairment
  • wheelchair loans (short term)
  • communication aids for those with a speech impairment
  • telecare equipment such as fall detectors, wandering alerts, gas and smoke detectors, flood detectors, medication dispensers and temperature detectors.

Adaptations

Adaptations are undertaken if you qualify.  They are designed to meet individual needs, to promote independence within a safe environment.

An occupational therapist will assess you and your home, taking into account the needs of carers.  We will ask you whether you are happy with the adaptations.   Any work costing less than £1,000 is free.

Neville Garratt Centre

The Neville Garratt Centre displays and demonstrates a wide range of equipment of smaller equipment to assist with daily living.  The equipment is provided following an assessment by an occupational therapist who will come to your home or you can pop into the Neville Garratt Centre for Independent Living in Bell Street.  We also loan medical equipment, such as commodes and incontinence products, to people being cared for at home.

Those with a visual or sensory impairment (Deaf, Blind or deafblind) can speak to a technician who can demonstrate equipment to help.  This includes flashing doorbells and loop systems:

Telephone: 01902 553652 or 01902 553653
Textphone: 01902 553677
Fax: 01902 553643

Wolverhampton Wheelchair Service

Wolverhampton Wheelchair Service provides a wide range of wheelchairs and associated equipment, a detailed assessment of your needs, modifications to ensure that your wheelchair meets your needs, regular safety checks and a comprehensive repair, delivery and collection service.

A wheelchair voucher scheme enables you to contribute to the cost of your wheelchair so that you can choose from a wider range than normally available, though you can access the scheme for free.
