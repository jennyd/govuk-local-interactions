Homelessness

Homelessness Partnership Bulletin - view the Autumn 2014 issue of our information bulletin (PDF 393KB).

eastsuffolk.letshelpyou.co.uk - new website launched offering a new way of searching for affordable rented accommodation in the private sector. It’s free, quick, easy to use, and calculates your local housing allowance for a specified area and shows you an up-to-date list of available properties.

If you’re legally homeless, your council must provide you with help – this could range from giving advice to arranging accommodation for you.

You may be legally homeless if:

  • You have no accommodation available in the UK or elsewhere.
  • Your family has no home where you can live together.
  • You have a home but you cannot get in - eg your landlord has locked you out.
  • You have a home but it is considered unreasonable for you to live there - eg risk of violence or abuse.
  • You are living in very poor conditions - eg overcrowding.

We have a duty to house those who are:

  • Homeless or threatened with homelessness within 28 days.
  • Eligible for housing assistance.
  • In priority need.
  • Have not made themselves intentionally homeless.

If you do not meet all the above criteria, we do not have a duty to house you but can give you advice and assistance to help you find suitable accommodation. This could be in the private sector, supported housing or social housing.

Some households may be housed temporarily whilst their homelessness status is investigated. This could be in bed and breakfast accommodation and /or temporary housing. If enquires show that you have no local connection to our area we can refer you to an area where you do have one.

In general terms, 'in priority need' includes families with school age children, pregnant women, people with a disability and those people above state pensionable age.

We may be able to help you by way of our Rent Deposit Guarantee Scheme. Although no money is paid out it could help you to access a private sector let.

If you are homeless and need help with accommodation, contact the Housing Options Team on 01394 383789 or email housing.options.scdc@eastsuffolk.gov.uk

If you need help outside of office hours telephone 01502 527133 and speak to the operator who will contact a duty officer.
