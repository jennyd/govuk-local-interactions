How to claim housing benefit and council tax reduction

Housing benefit

Housing Benefit helps those on a low income pay rent on the property that they live in. It is means-tested, and administered by the council on behalf of the Department of Works and Pensions. To apply, you will need to complete a claim form and return this to us within 2 weeks of the date on the form.

You will also need to submit supporting evidence to us, dependant on your circumstances, click here for detailed information about what you should provide. A notification letter will be sent to you once your claim has been assessed, usually within 14 working days.

Make a claim for Housing Benefit

Council tax reduction

Pension age council tax payers

Council tax reduction for pension age council tax payers is a National Scheme administered by the council. It provides help for pensioners with low income to pay council tax. Visit the Government's Pension Age calculator page.

Working age* council tax payers

Council tax reduction for working age council tax payers is a Localised Council Tax Reduction Scheme. The scheme has been approved by the full council.

You can claim council tax reduction if you receive a council tax bill for the property you live in, and the bill is in your name or joint names with someone else.  To apply, you will need to complete a claim form and return this to us within 2 weeks of the date on the form.

You will also need to submit supporting evidence to us, dependant on your circumstances, click here for detailed information about what you should provide. A notification letter will be sent to you once your claim has been assessed, usually within 14 working days.

Make a claim for Council Tax Reduction

*Working age people - a claim is classed as working age if the applicant and their partner are under the State Pension Credit age. The age that you are classed as being a pensioner is gradually increasing from 60 to 68 over the years through to 2020. By 6 March 2013 you will be classed as being of State Pension Credit age if your birthday falls before 5 April 1951.

You will not qualify for Housing Benefit or Council Tax Reduction if your savings are over £16,000, unless you are receiving Guarantee Pension Credit.

Second Adult Rebate

You can claim Second Adult Rebate if you are of Pension Age and you receive a council tax bill in your name for the property you live in but you are unable to claim Single Person Discount because another adult lives with you, who is not your partner.

To make a second adult rebate claim contact the council or visit one of our council offices in Leek, Cheadle or Biddulph.

We will not need to know your income or savings but, to qualify for this benefit, the second adult in your property must have a total income lower than £243 a week, or be in receipt of Guarantee Credit, Income Support or income based Jobseekers allowance.

We aim to deal with all claims within two weeks.

Last updated: 2nd April 2015
