How to apply for a secondary school

Applying for a secondary school place

View pages in this section

If your child is due to transfer from primary to secondary school in September 2016, you will have needed to apply for a place between 1 September 2015 and 31 October 2015.

How to apply

You can apply for schools in any area, but if you live in the Royal Borough of Greenwich you need to make your application through us.

The easiest way to apply is through the eAdmissions portal - it is easy and secure - and it is the fastest way to hear the results. If you would rather complete a paper form, you must complete the Royal Borough of Greenwich's common application form.  

If you live in another borough, you need to make your application through your home authority even if you wish to apply for a place in a Royal Greenwich school. 

Apply for a secondary school place now (eAdmissions website)

Supplementary information forms for voluntary aided schools

If you are applying for a place at St Mary Magdalene CoE School, St Paul's Academy, St Thomas More Catholic School or St Ursula's Convent School as a foundation (faith) entrant, you will also need to complete a supplementary information form in addition to your main application form.

This must be returned to the school by the same date as the deadline for the application form.

You can find out more about what type of school a school is by looking at its entry in the secondary school admissions booklet. 

Download supplementary information forms for September 2016 secondary school admission

Deciding on your preferred schools

Before deciding on your preferences, you need to consider each school's admissions policy and previous years' application rates to see whether your child is likely to be offered a place. 

You are also encouraged to attend the secondary schools open days to learn more about schools you are interested in. 

Secondary school admissions booklet 2016 to 2017

Apply for up to six schools 

You can apply for up to six secondary schools, listing them in preference order.  

Your preference schools can be in Royal Greenwich or in other areas, but not independent (fee paying) schools.

If you wish to apply for schools in other areas, please bear in mind that there may be additional requirements such as entry exams. You should contact the local authority in which the school is located for details of their procedures and timetables.

You do not have to list six schools, but the more schools you list, the better your chances of being offered a place at one of them.  

Be realistic about your preferences 

Please do not list a school more than once. Your child can only be considered once for each school so any duplicates will be deleted.

Although it is for you to decide on your preference schools, your preferences do need to be realistic.  We recommend that you do not 'waste' a preference by naming a school at which your child is unlikely to be offered a place.

As any preference may be offered, you should not name a school you do not wish your child to attend.

Please make sure that you have ranked your preferences in the order you genuinely want them to be considered. Any changes you wish to make to your preferences after the closing date will only be considered once all applications, including those submitted late, have been processed.

Siblings already attending a school

If you have an older child or children who already attends the school, you must make this clear on your application.

Medical or social reasons for preferences

In most cases, a child's medical or social needs can be met in any mainstream school.

However, if you feel that there is an acute medical or social reason why your child should be offered a particular school, you need to state this in your application.  

When you apply you will need to:

  1. Give reasons why your child should attend a particular school.
  2. State the consequences for your child if they had to attend an alternative school.  
  3. Provide supporting documentation from the relevant professional, such as a qualified medical practitioner.

Please bear in mind that childcare arrangements do not fall under the category of an acute medical or social need.

Please also note that we do not normally consider moderate learning difficulties, such as delayed speech and language or dyslexia, as being an acute medical or social need.

We can only consider a request for a place in a school's Designated Specialist Provision (DSP) if it is supported by our Special Educational Needs Monitoring and Assessment Service.

Get help with your application

The Families Information Service offers support to parents and carers who may need support with the school admissions process. The service is available to everyone and provides: 

  • a wide range of information on local secondary schools
  • support with making informed and realistic preferences
  • help in completing the relevant forms
  • information about the appeals process. 

If you have questions about which schools will best meet your child's academic and developmental needs, or you require support with making your application, please contact the Families Information Service.
