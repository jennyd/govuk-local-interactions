Funding is available to make a real difference in your community

Oldham Council is inviting community groups and organisations to apply for £1,000 of funding to help improve the lives of the Borough’s young people.

Every year the Local Authority gives grants from the Kerrching Youth Opportunity Fund - a pot of money that aims to provide enjoyable leisure opportunities and activities.

The fund is for 11 to 19 year olds, or up to 25 if the young people have additional needs.  Oldham Youth Council manages the Kerrching fund and they will assess the nominations and decide which ones get the funding.

This year, we have the opportunity to offer some additional money to youth and community groups within Oldham in the form of the Thank YOUth awards.  

The Youth Council know that there are hundreds of organisations and groups working with young people, often doing good work without any expectation of thanks or recognition. 

A Thank YOUth award could be a way to say thank you to those who are making a real difference in their community.

For example a local sport club may need new kit or equipment; a scout organisation may need camp fees; or a youth group could require replacement IT equipment. 

Nominations are invited from groups that offer positive opportunities and activities for young people.  Nominations that support the Youth Council’s priority themes of tackling obesity, encouraging active lifestyles and improving emotional and mental health, will be especially welcome.

Anyone can make a nomination and the only conditions are the organisation must be already offering activities for young people and must have a constitution or be part of a statutory body.

The deadline for nominations is 5pm on Monday, January 6. All nominations will be reviewed and assessed by members of the Youth Council before any money is allocated. Successful nominees will be informed from the week commencing January 13.  

As this is an additional round of funding, all monies will have to have been spent and evidenced before March 31.

Councillor Jean Stretton, Cabinet Member for Cooperatives and Neighbourhoods, said: “We know we already have organisations across the Borough running lots of great youth projects.  

“This is an opportunity to get additional funding to support that work and £1,000 could make a really big difference to what they can offer to our young people.”

Daisy Murphy, Chair of Oldham Youth Council, said: “We are so excited about the Thank YOUth award as it’s such a fantastic way to support those working for the benefit of young people. 

“I’d urge people to nominate groups that you know are making a real difference in the lives of young people. Let’s recognise the work they do and give them a thousand thank yous.”


For more information or to request a nomination form please contact Jodie Barber, Senior Youth Work Manager at Oldham Council, on 0161 770 3117 or email Jodie.barber@oldham.gov.uk
