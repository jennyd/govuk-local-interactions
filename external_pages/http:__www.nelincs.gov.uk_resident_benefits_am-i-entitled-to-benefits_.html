The Housing Benefit and Council Tax Reduction schemes are there to help if you are working and on a low income, or if you receive any other type of welfare benefits. 

Am I eligible?

You can check your eligibility with our online Benefits Calculator.

The amount you can get depends on who lives with you, your total income, any savings you have and if you live in private rented accommodation the number of rooms your family require.

It will ask you a series of questions about your individual circumstances

  • Enter your details and you will be given a personalised indication of your entitlement, based on your circumstances
  • Please be as accurate as possible

This is not an application and is intended to give you an idea of what benefit would be available to you, if you put in an application. 

In order to apply you must complete our Housing Benefit and Council Tax Reduction form.

The form is also available from our Customer Access Points.

 

How to apply

Visit our making a claim page which will explain how to submit the application form and the supporting evidence you will need.  You only need to fill in one form to apply for both Housing Benefit and Council Tax Reduction. 

 

Housing Benefit 

You may be entitled to Housing Benefit if you:

  • are legally responsible for paying rent for the home you live in, and
  • have savings and investments of less than £16,000 (unless you receive Pension Credit Guaranteed Credit, as different rules apply).

return to top of page


Council Tax Reduction (previously Council Tax Benefit)

You may be entitled to this if you:

  • are legally responsible for paying Council Tax for the home you live in, and
  • have savings and investments of less than £16,000 (unless you receive Pension Credit Guaranteed Credit, as different rules apply).

When you fill in a joint Housing Benefit and Council Tax Reduction application form we will automatically consider your entitlement for both schemes. 

The Council Tax Reduction Scheme has replaced Council Tax Benefit.  Pensioners in North East Lincolnshire continue to receive the same levels of support as previously, but working age people may see a reduction in the benefit they receive, depending on their situation.  return to top of page
