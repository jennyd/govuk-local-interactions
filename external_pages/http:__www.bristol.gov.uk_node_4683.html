Home adaptations and equipment

Helping yourself

If you're looking for ideas to adapt your home yourself, or buy your own equipment, there are various organisations that can help:

Well Aware

Well Aware lists organisations that can help with home maintenance and adaptations.

WE Care and Repair

Help for homeowners and private tenants over 60 and disabled people of all ages.  They can carry out minor repairs, help when you leave hopsital, advice on adapting your home, free home safety checks.

Age UK - home and care

Age UK help people remain at home for as long as possible by offering tailored products, like stairlifts or help finding a local tradesperson.

EAC FirstStop

National advice service for older people, their families and carers.

Foundations

The national body for home improvement agency and handyperson services.

Charities

Some charities, like the Royal British Legion, will sometimes provide funding for specific equipment.

Visit displays

To see displays about how your home and facilities can be made more accessible, visit Waterloo Home Design Centre, West of England Care and Repair, Waterloo Street, St Phillips, Bristol.

Telephone: 0300 323 0700
Email: info@wecr.org.uk.

Get help from us

We can help you to remain living independently at home, and support carers.

Assessments and visits are prioritised depending on your needs. There are waiting lists, we'll let you know if there will be a delay before we can see you.

Request this service
using the Care Direct contact and referral form

Some examples of the help we can offer:

  • Providing advice about living independently at home.
  • Providing equipment like a raised toilet seat.
  • Arranging minor adaptations like a stair or grab rail.
  • Arranging major adaptations like a level-access shower or a stair lift.

Eligibility

Anyone who has needs which mean that they have difficulty in getting into or out of their home, or carrying out particular tasks in their home.

Charges

  • Advice, equipment and minor adaptations are free to those who qualify.
  • Major adaptations may need a financial contribution following a financial assessment.

How to return equipment

To return equipment that's no longer needed, please contact Medequip on 0117 957 9140.

Medequip website

Financial help

For most homeowners enquiries for financial help from us are administered by WE Care and Repair and in line with the Financial Assistance Schedule (revised January 2015) (pdf, 0.6 MB)(opens new window) (pdf, 565k) (opens new window)  This help is in the form of a low cost loan.

We have a range of affordable products available to suit the financial circumstances of eligible households and the term can be extended up to 15 years to make repayment as low as possible.

In some exceptional circumstances we might accept interest only loans or deferred payments as long as there is equity in your property to adequately cover the capital value of the loan.

All loans are secured against your property and your home is at risk if you do not keep up repayments as agreed.

To make an enquiry contact WE Care and Repair on 0300 323 0700 or by email info@wecr.org.uk. Help offered under this policy is limited to the budget available at the time of your application.

See the WE Care and Repair website for more information about this and other services.

All our low cost loans are administered by our partner Wessex Home Improvement Loans.

The Private Housing Service deals directly with enquiries for loans for specialist areas of work like:

  • energy efficiency improvements
  • bringing empty properties back into use
  • loans to private landlords in areas where Discretionary Licensing Schemes have been designated.
