02/02/2011 - Dont miss out on celebrating the royal wedding

With less than three months to go before this years big royal wedding, anyone planning to hold a street party in Allerdale is urged to ensure they have everything in place for the big day.

Prince William is set to marry Kate Middleton on Friday 29 April which means people in Allerdale have only a month in which to apply for a road closure as part of their celebrations.

Sarah Beattie, Allerdale Borough Councils Democratic Services Manager, said: An application for a road closure order needs to be made to Allerdale Borough Council no less than eight weeks before the starting date of the event; in the case of the royal wedding the deadline will be Friday 4 March. This is to ensure that there is enough time for the Council to consult with other authorities, including Cumbria Highways, Stagecoach North West and the local parish or town council, on the proposed road closure.

Consultees such as Cumbria Police, Cumbria Fire and Rescue Service and Cumbria Ambulance Service may need extra time to consider potential road closures and their possible impact on the emergency services, therefore we urge residents not to delay and contact Allerdale Borough Council with their street party plans today. By working together, we hope we can help people to plan the perfect celebration for the royal wedding.

The Council can also offer advice and support if a temporary events notice is required for licensable activities such as the supply of alcohol and performance of live or recorded music. And dont forget to clear the event with all of your neighbours too.

For more information on road closure orders, contact Allerdale Borough Councils Democratic Services team on 01900 702550, email: democratic.services@allerdale.gov.uk or visit the road closure orders page.

For more information on temporary events notices, contact Allerdale Borough Councils Licensing team on 01900 702720 or email licensing@allerdale.gov.uk
