What should I do if I'm homeless?

If you are homeless, in the first instance you need to contact us using the details below.

Telephone: 01473 825757

Email: customer.services@babergh.gov.uk

How do I apply for shared ownership?

You need to complete an online housing application form via the Gateway to Homechoice website. 

You will also need to apply to BPHA (Bedfordshire Pilgrims Housing Association) who, from the 1st April 2014, are the new Help to Buy Agent for this region. To find out more visit Help to Buy East & South East (opens in new window) or call them on 03333 214044

How do I get help with private renting?

We run a Rent Deposit Guarantee Scheme. The aim of the scheme is to provide support and assistance to enable people to rent a property within the Private Rented Sector.

For those who qualify, the scheme can provide assistance by guaranteeing a deposit and/or provide an interest free loan for Rent in Advance. Where the Council provides a Guarantee to assist with private renting this is a ‘promise to pay’, rather than a transfer of money. Alternatively, money may be paid direct to the Landlord to help secure the tenancy. Where assistance has been provided, ongoing support throughout the tenancy can be given if required by either Tenant or Landlord.

We regret that the Rent Deposit Guarantee Scheme cannot provide a guarantor for the tenancy nor can it assist with any agency non-refundable fees that may be required.

Telephone: 01473 825757

Email customer.services@babergh.gov.uk

How do I get help with the cost of private rent?

Local Housing Allowance

You may qualify for financial support towards you rental payments depending on your household income. Local Housing Allowance is the Housing Benefit payment made for someone renting a property within the Private Rental Sector.

The amount you could receive is dependent on a number of factors such as: income, savings, members of household, property and location.

The amount of Local Housing Allowance you are entitled to may not cover the full rent you need to pay, therefore it is important to check that the property is affordable and suitable for you.  

Where you are in receipt of Local Housing Allowance and you are in a tenancy secured with the Rent Deposit Guarantee Scheme, the payments will go to the Landlord directly
