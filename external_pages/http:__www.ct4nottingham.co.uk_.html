The Little Bus Company With The Big Heart

Nottingham Community Transport in conjunction with Nottingham City Council have launched a fleet of electric vehicles. The Optare Solo buses, joining four already in service, will work on the Locallink network and have initially been placed into service on the W3 and L64 services. The vehicles, which can do up to 90 miles on a single charge, have been painted into a new, updated Locallink Electric livery.

To go along with the new vehicles, Nottingham Community Transport have also undergone a rebranding exercise which includes a new logo, new uniforms, a brand new website and a social media presence on both Facebook and Twitter. We have also opened a new compound at Queens Drive Park and Ride.

Ian Combellack, General Manager, said “we are delighted with the new look Community Transport and the initial feedback from the customers has been very positive”.

Nottingham Community Transport thank Carbon Creative, Reveal Web Solutions, Progressive Safety and Neil Page Creative for their help in the whole rebranding exercise
