Adaptations to your home

If you are disabled and need adaptations in your home to increase your independence and safety, the route for arranging these will depend on the type of adaptation required, and the tenure of your property.

Types of Adaptation:

Minor adaptations include smaller, less costly items such as grab-rails, bannister rails, and half-steps. Many items are also easily available on the high street and it is possible to search on the internet for a variety of options.

You can also use the online SmartAssist equipment self-assessment tool for information about equipment and minor adaptations which may help you, and can be privately purchased.

If you rent your property from a registered social or private landlord, please also see the note below under ownership of property.

Major adaptations include larger, more extensive structural changes to your property.

These changes could include:

  • widening doors and installing ramps
  • improving access to rooms and facilities – e.g. by installing a stairlift, level access shower facilities or ramp
  • providing a heating system suitable for your needs
  • adapting heating or lighting controls to make them easier to use

Tenure (ownership) of your property:

Social Housing Landlord

If you live in a property which is owned by a social housing landlord (for example a local council or housing association), you should approach your landlord in the first instance who may be able to assist with the provision and funding of both minor and major adaptations.   For major adaptations, they may suggest you have an Occupational Therapy assessment initially to determine what you need.

Owner Occupier / Privately Rented

Contact Customer First initially for assessment and advice. If you live in a privately rented property, you will need to gain written permission from your landlord agreeing to any adaptations (major or minor) being made.  Major adaptations to your property can be funded via a Disabled Facilities Grant – see below forfurther information.

Tenure

Points to consider
Social Landlord (e.g. Council or Housing Association)
  • Contact local council / housing association for repairs
  • Contact local council / housing association for adaptations (may require OT/A assessment)
  • Gain permission for any alterations self-funded
  • Advice available from Customer First
  • OT/A assessment and recommendations available
Private Landlord
  • Contact landlord for repairs
  • Contact landlord for adaptations
  • Gain written permission for any adaptations / alterations
  • Advice available from Customer First
  • OT/A assessment and recommendations available
Owner occupier
  • Advice available from Customer First
  • OT/A assessment and recommendations available

Home Improvement Agencies

Home Improvement Agencies (HIAs) are not-for-profit organisations helping homeowners and private sector tenants who are older, disabled or vulnerable.  They arrange repairs, maintenance or adaptations to homes and provide handyperson services.

  • Suffolk Coastal District Council area: HIA services are provided by Helping Hand who can be contacted on 01394 444644
  • Orbit East Care and Repair provides HIA services for the rest of the county.  They can be contacted on 0800 121 7711

Grants and Funding

Disabled Facilities Grant 

If you are disabled and need to make changes to your home, you could get a grant from your local council to help with the costs.

These changes can include:

  • widening doors and installing ramps
  • improving access to rooms and facilities - eg by installing a stairlift, downstairs bathroom or level access shower
  • providing a heating system suitable for your needs
  • adapting heating or lighting controls to make them easier to use

Visit the GOV.UK website to find out about what you can get, eligibility and how to apply, or contact your local council:

  • Babergh 
  • Forest Heath
  • Ipswich
  • Mid-Suffolk
  • St Edmundsbury
  • Suffolk Coastal
  • Waveney

Repairs to existing adaptations

If you have adaptations already to your property because of your disability which need repair you should contact the landlord/housing provider who arranged for the adaptation. If the adaptation was through a Disabled Facilities Grant and you own your own home then you may need to organise the repair yourself (please see below for other sources of help), and check if you had a service warranty as part of the process.

Other sources of help and advice

You might find help with repairs, adaptations and maintenance to your home from Age UK Suffolk, the Royal British Legion Handyvan service, Checkatrade, Norfolk Trusted Trader and Which? Trusted Traders.

Information on sources of funding to help with equipment, repairs and adaptations is available from:

  • Age UK factsheet on funding repairs
  • Charity Search help to find sources of funding
  • Disabled Living Foundation grants for adaptations
  • Turn2us benefits and grants finder
  • some service associations such as the Royal British Legion may help with loans or grants
  • Friends of the Elderly provides one-off grants to help with the costs of household repairs, adaptations and mobility aids
  • Suffolk Disability Care Fund gives grants of up to £1000 to provide daily living equipment for people with disabilities
  • The ACT Foundation gives grants for building modifications, provision of specialised wheelchairs, mobility aids and equipment to assist independent living
  • In certain circumstances you may also be eligible to buy equipment or have adaptations made without paying VAT. Read more here.
