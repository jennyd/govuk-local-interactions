Highways licences & permits

Application forms are available below to obtain the specific licences or permits required when working on the highway.

Failure to obtain the necessary permissions may result in enforcement action being taken against you.

Full list of highways charges and fees (pdf, 156KB).

Only registered companies can apply for hoarding, scaffolding and skip permits. If you would like to become registered please email street.works@cambridgeshire.gov.uk.

Available application forms

  • Scaffolding and hoardings licences

Skip permit

Only registered skip companies can apply for skip permits.

Skips are not allowed to be placed on waiting restrictions (yellow lines). Skips may be permitted in resident permit holder bays or pay and display bays but additional charges will apply.

To apply and pay for a permit to place a skip on the highway please complete the online form.

Apply or renew a skip permit

The current charge for a skip permit is £30 (£31 from 1 April 2016) for 14 days. This payment is taken during the online application.

Applications by phone or email are no longer accepted.

It can take up to 5 working days to process an application for a skip permit.

Scaffolding and hoardings licenses

You need a licence to place scaffolding or hoardings on the highway.

Only registered companies can apply for scaffolding and hoardings licences. If you would like to become a registered company please email street.works@cambridgeshire.gov.uk.

To apply and pay for a licence to place scaffolding on the highway please complete the online form below.

Apply or renew a scaffolding licence


To apply and pay for a licence to place hoardings on the highway please complete the online form below.

Apply or renew a hoardings licence

The current charge for both scaffolding and hoardings licences is £105 (£110 from 1 April 2016) per street placed per 30 days, this payment is taken during the online application.

It can take up to 7 working days to process an application for a licence.

Applications by phone, email or paper are no longer accepted.

Licence renewals use the same online form as for new applications.

Tables and chairs

If you intend to sell food or drink from your business on any part of the public highway you will need to apply for a licence. Please read the following before applying.

  • Tables and chairs guidance notes (pdf, 80kb)
  • Pavement Licence Conditions (pdf, 43kb)

To apply and make the initial payment for a tables and chairs licence please complete the online form.

Apply or renew a tables and chairs licence

You may be asked to make an additional payment for your tables and chairs licence please use the online form.

Make an additional payment for your tables and chairs licence application 

Temporary Traffic Regulation Orders (TTRO) for road closures and speed reductions

If you need to reduce a speed limit or close a road or footway you will need to apply for a temporary Traffic Regulation Order (TRO).

Temporary Traffic Regulation Orders for road closures and speed reductions application form (doc, 124kb)

Banners over the highway

To place a banner over a highway to publicise a charitable or community event you will need a license.

Please read and complete banners over the highway application form (doc, 194kb).

Tower cranes

You need a licence for tower cranes that may 'oversail' the highway. Tower crane application form (pdf, 33kb)

Book a road space

If you want to work on the public highway you need to book road space. Make sure you include accurate descriptions and grid references. Book a road space application form (doc, 128kb)

Temporary traffic lights

If you want to use temporary traffic lights, you will need to complete the Temporary traffic lights application form (doc, 188kb)

Private apparatus on the highway

You need a licence if you want to place or retain apparatus on the public highway (such as sewer connection pipes). Private apparatus (street works) licence application form (pdf, 278kb)

Planting on the highway

You need a licence if you want to maintain an area of highway verge adjacent to your premises. Planting on the highway application form (pdf, 45.1kb).

Public event on the highway

If you are planning an event that will take place on a road or may cause disruptions to traffic please read the following:

  • The Good Practice Safety Guide (pdf, 1282kb)
  • Managing the traffic to your event (pdf, 1702kb)

Please contact us at least four months in advance of your event if you feel the event is likely to have an impact on the highway, so that there is time to arrange any necessary measures.

To apply for a road closure for your event please complete the event road closure application form (doc, 280kb).

Traffic counters on the highway

If you are using traffic counters on the highway please complete the Traffic Counters licence application form from 1 April 2015 (doc, 191kb)
