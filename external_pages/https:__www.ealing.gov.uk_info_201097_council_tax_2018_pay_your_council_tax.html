Paying by instalments

Payment of council tax is usually by 10 monthly instalments from April to January, but you can choose to pay 12 instalments over a full year.

Apply to pay over 12 instalments

Instalments are normally due on the first of the month, however direct debit payers can choose between the 1st, 8th, 15th or 22nd of each month.

You can also pay for the whole year at once or by two half-yearly instalments.

If the amount you have to pay changes during the year, eg because your entitlement to a discount, exemption or council tax support changes, you will receive a new bill. This will show the new amount for the year and the new instalments you will have to pay.

Direct debit

This is the most convenient way to pay your council tax, saving you time as your bills are paid automatically.

Set up a direct debit

Pay your council tax online

You can make a payment online using a credit or debit card.

Make a payment

Credit or debit card payments

We accept payment by Mastercard or Visa credit cards, Visa debit cards, or Delta or Maestro. 

A charge of 2.5% will be added to your payment if you use a credit card. No charge will be added to your payment if you pay by direct debit or pay using a debit card.

Telephone or internet banking and standing orders

You will need to instruct your bank to make payments to sort code 30-00-00 and account number 02390140. Please quote your council tax account number as the reference number.

For telephone payments call (020) 8825 9940. Open 24 hours a day, seven days a week.

Pay at a bank

You can pay at any bank using a payment slip. Some banks will charge you a fee for this service unless you hold an account with them. You can pay your council tax free of charge at branches of Lloyd's Bank in Acton, Ealing, Perivale or Southall.

If making the payment by cheque please quote your council tax account number on the back.

Pay at a post office

You can pay your council tax at any post office using a payment slip. You will have to pay a fee. Cheques should be made payable to 'post office counters' and please quote your account number on the back.
