Council Tax Discounts and Exemptions

Overview

The amount of Council Tax that you need to pay can be reduced in certain circumstances:

  • Exemptions may be granted on the property
  • Discounts may apply to the property and/or the occupants
  • Disability reductions may be granted
  • Council Tax Benefit can be claimed

The full Council Tax assumes that there are two adults living in a property. You may be able to get a discount if this is not the case, for example if you live on your own you are eligible for Single Person's Discount.

When deciding how many adults are resident, certain occupants can be disregarded for Council Tax purposes.

A detailed Guide to Council Tax discounts is available to Download. If you think that you may be entitled to a discount you should contact us immediately.

If your bill indicates that a discount or exemption has been applied you must tell us immediately of any change of circumstance that could affect your entitlement, for example, another adult joining the household. If you fail to do so within 21 days you may be required to pay a penalty.

Your bill will show if you have been granted a discount:

  • Single person - 25% discount because there is only one adult resident
  • Single with disregards - 25% discount because only one adult resident is counted
  • Second home discount - 10% discount if the property is a furnished second home
  • Disregards only - 50% discount because all of the residents are disregarded
  • Single who is disregarded - 50% discount because there is only one adult resident who is not counted

Prior to April 2004 a 50% discount was granted to empty properties after the 6 or 12-month exemption expired. New powers in the Local Government Act 2003 have allowed the Council to reduce the discount to 0% to encourage owners to bring long-term empty homes into use.
