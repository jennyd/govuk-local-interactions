Who Can Claim Benefits?

You can be working or not working, retired or a Student

Students must be either;

  • A lone parent or,
  • Disabled or,
  • Under 19 following a further (not higher) course of education or,
  • Qualifying State Pension Age

You can claim Housing Benefit if you are the named person liable to pay the rent for where you live. If you are a joint tenant we can also help you with your share of the rent. In some circumstances you can be treated as the liable person even though you are not the named person on the tenancy agreement.

You can claim Council Tax Support if you are the named person liable to pay a Council Tax for where you live. If you have a joint liability with someone else we can help you with your share of the Council Tax. Even though you may not be entitled to a Council Tax Support you may qualify if you have someone else living with you who has a low income.

We cannot help you with the payment of a mortgage; you will need to contact the Department for Work and Pensions if you require this help.  Please see related links.

Who is unable to apply for help?

  • You are a non-householder (and do not pay rent or Council Tax)
  • You have savings, investments or property (other than the home you live in) worth more than £16,000
  • Your status in the UK excludes you from receiving public funds
  • You are a full time student

Changes to Benefits from 2nd November 2009

Child benefit

From 2nd November 2009, anyone receiving child benefit should be aware that this will no longer be counted as income when calculating your housing benefit and council tax support. This change may mean that many families who may not have qualified in the past may now receive some help towards their rent and council tax.

Increase in pensioner age lower capital threshold

From 2nd November 2009 the lower capital threshold for pension age claim (those aged 60 or over) will increase to £10,000. This means that the first £10,000 of your capital will be ignored when we calculate your housing and council tax support entitlement.

What the above changes mean to you

If you already receive housing benefit and/or council tax support, your claim has automatically been re-calculated by the Benefits Service and you will have received notification letters advising you of your new weekly housing benefit and council tax support entitlement.

If you have not qualified for housing benefit and/or council tax support in the past, you may now be eligible for some help towards your rent and council tax.

To find out if you qualify please use our benefits calculator or contact the office.

Further Information

For further information regarding Housing Benefit and Council Tax Support, please refer to the 'Benefit Leaflets' link under the Offsite Links section of this page.
