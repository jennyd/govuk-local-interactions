How do I buy visitor/hotel guest scratch cards?

For residents with a parking permit

If you already hold a resident’s parking permit for your own vehicle(s), or a  registration card, you can buy scratch cards by either:

  • calling the Permits Office on (01482) 395411 who will take payment by either credit/debit card. The scratch cards will arrive within 10 working days.
  • visiting a  customer service centrewith your permit or registration card. You can take the scratch cards home the same day. View payment options.  
  • Unfortunately, this cannot be done online.

Each book contains 10 scratch cards and costs £10. Each household is entitled to five books of scratch cards per year.

For residents without a parking permit

If you don't hold a resident’s parking permit (because you don't have a vehicle which requires parking), you must first buy a registration card for £15 using the following application form:

Visitor scratch card application form (pdf 86kb opens in new window)

and  return it to us with your payment.

You will then be issued with a registration card, entitling you to  buy scratch cards.


For guest houses and B&Bs

You must first buy a registration card for £15:

Hotel and guesthouse scratch card application form (pdf 87kb opens in new window)

and  return it to us with your payment.

You will then be issued with a registration card, entitling you to  buy scratch cards.

Which documents do I need for my permit application?

Residents, contractors and carers require different types of documents:

Residents require:

One proof of address:

To prove that you live at a property in a CPZ area, you must provide your:

  • current Council Tax Bill Reference Number (if you are a named person on the bill); 

or one of the following:

  • driving licence;
  • bank statement or utility bill;
  • current flat or house contents insurance;
  • car insurance document;
  • solicitor’s letter showing exchange of contracts;
  • current tenancy agreement (not handwritten).

And one proof of vehicle ownership/hire:

You must also provide proof that you have a vehicle which requires parking in the CPZ area:

Own your vehicle?

If you own the vehicle, you must provide the 11 digit V5C Vehicle Registration Number showing that your vehicle is registered to a property in the CPZ.

Please note: if you have recently moved in to the CPZ, you will need to ensure the details on the V5C Vehicle Registration Certificate are changed as soon as possible. The council will grant a 6 week grace period for you to change the address details on the V5C. 

Hire or lease a vehicle?

If you hire, lease or rent a vehicle, you must produce the agreement that shows your name and your address in the CPZ.

Have a company vehicle?

If you have a company vehicle (includes a vehicle owned by a partnership or sole trader) you will need to produce a letter on the company’s headed paper from the company secretary or someone of a similar position (not from you). It needs to be addressed to the  Permits Office, stating you are an employee, partner or sole trader.

Vehicle registered abroad?

If you have a vehicle that is registered abroad you may apply for a 3 month temporary permit, however, in order to apply for a full resident parking permit, your vehicle must be registered in the UK.

Foreign plate temporary parking permit (pdf 82kb opens in new window)

 

Contractors require:

Contractors need proof of business such as a business letterhead and VAT number.


Carers require:

Either the person receiving care or the carer can complete the form.

The person receiving care needs:

  • a written confirmation (letter) that they are on the current published register of people eligible for care assistance.
  • proof of address


Or, the carer needs:

  • written confirmation (letter) from Children, Family and Adult Services section of East Riding of Yorkshire Council confirming that you are registered to administer care. Contact the council on (01482) 393939.
  • proof of vehicle registration or hire.

Please note: If applying by post, you can send copies of these documents.

If taking your application to a customer service centre, you must bring the originals for them to be checked.

How do I submit my application and pay?

Filling in the form

Please ensure you:

  • write clearly in black ink;
  • block capitals;
  • tick all the relevant boxes;
  • enclose the correct proof(s) of eligibility;
  • enclose payment.

Please note: To help you complete the form correctly and speed up your application, please ensure you read all the information on this page.

If your application form is not filled in correctly or you have not enclosed the relevant documents, we will return the form and documents to you by post.

If you have any questions or need help filling in the application form, please contact the Parking Permit Office:

  • Tel: (01482) 395411
  • Email: parkingnotifications@eastriding.gov.uk

Please note: we may record these calls for training purposes.


Submit and pay by post

Please return your completed application form along with a copy of the required documents and your cheque payment (made payable to 'ERYC' ) to:

Permits
PO Box 299
Beverley
HU17 6FH

You may wish to post your application by a signed service to ensure it's safe arrival. Your local Post Office will be able to help you with this.

We will return your permit and documents to you by normal post.


Submit and pay in person

You can also take your application to your local   customer service centre.

Your payment can be made by:

  • cash;
  • postal orders;
  • cheque (made payable to 'ERYC');
  • or by credit card (no fee applied) or debit card (not American Express or Diners Cards).

If applying in person for a permit (not scratch cards), you may ask for a 14 day temporary parking permit to allow parking within the CPZ whilst your application is processed.

Top of page

When will I receive my permit?

If the application was filled out correctly, the permit will take about 10 days to arrive and can only be posted to the address on the application form.

For new permits

If you have not filled in the application properly or have not enclosed copies of the relevant documents, we will return your form. This will cause a delay in issuing your permit.

Temporary permit

If your need your new permit urgently, or your existing permit is about to expire, you take your application form to your local  customer service centre instead of posting it, you can have a 14 day temporary permit.

  Top of page

Can I change the details on my permit (eg name, address)?

Name and/or address

If you move home and/or change your name within the same CPZ whilst your permit is valid, you may use the same permit but you need to inform us so that we can update our records. You must complete the following application form:

Apply for a new residents parking permit (pdf 97kb opens in new window)

and  return your application  to us with one  proof(s)of eligibility.

 

Vehicle – permanently

If you have changed your vehicle (owned or leased) you must not alter your existing permit. You must complete the following application form:

Apply for a new residents parking permit (pdf 97kb opens in new window)

and  return your application  to us with your current permit, together with one of the following:

  • a V5C Vehicle Registration Number

or, if you want your permit quicker:

  • a sales invoice together with insurance documents (showing your name/company name, vehicle registration number and your address).

Please note: As you will be without a permit whilst a new one is being issued, you can take your application to a  customer service centre so a 14 day temporary permit can be issued the same day. If you do not do this, you may be issued a parking fine for not having a valid permit.

 

Vehicle - temporarily

If your vehicle is off the road because of an accident/major repair or if it has been stolen, we can issue you a 14 day temporary permit for a shared, borrowed or leased vehicle.

You can only get one of these temporary permits by visiting your local  customer service centre. You must take your original permit with a letter from the garage or insurance company to confirm the damage, major repair or theft. 

Please note: You must display both the temporary permit and your normal permit (unless it was stolen with your vehicle) in the temporary vehicle.

  Top of page

Can I get a new permit if it has been lost or stolen?

If your parking permit is lost or stolen you should report it to the police who will issue you with a crime/lost property reference number. This number will enable you to get a replacement permit for free.

If the permit has been destroyed you will need to pay the original fee. See how much it will  cost.

To get a new one, you must complete the following application form:

Apply for a new residents parking permit (pdf 97kb opens in new window)

and return your application  to us with the crime reference/lost property number if you have one.
