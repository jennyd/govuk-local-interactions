What is a needs assessment?

Under the Care Act 2014, needs assessments are for people over the age of 18 requiring help to live in the best way possible as a result of:

  • disability
  • ill health
  • old age

You are entitled to a needs assessment as soon as Solihull Council is aware of your needs.

An assessment can be offered by Solihull Council Adult Social Care team or requested by you. You can also be referred for an assessment by a medical professional, such as your GP or a consultant surgeon.

Your local Community Advice Hub could help you understand needs assessments. The hubs are managed by Age UK Solihull and supported by other organisations including DIAL Solihull and Health Trainers from Health Exchange. Solihull Carers Centre also offer support to carers.

Assessing the care and support you need

Assessments allow Solihull social care services to understand whether a person is in need of help. A needs assessment also provides a full picture of the situation to allow the best help and advice to be given.

They also allow social care services to work with you, and your carer or another person you trust if you want, to decide what level of care and support is required and how to put it into place.

If you do not have someone to get involved in the assessment process with you, social care services may be able to provide you with an independent advocate. An independent advocate is someone who can help you represent yourself.

Alternatively, you can learn about arranging your own advocate.

If you are deaf, deafened, hard of hearing, BID Services Solihull can provide social care and support assessments, care and support planning and reviews.

The right assessment

There are different kinds of assessments to ensure that the right support is given to the right people in the right way.

In addition to needs assessments, there are:

  • carers assessments - for carers providing regular care
  • Children Act assessments for disabled children under 18, their parents and family, and young carer's under the age of 18

Parents and family members who care for disabled children can each have their own carer’s assessment (a parent carer's needs assessment). A carer’s assessment can be asked for if you feel an assessment under the Children’s Act does not cover your needs.

Young carer's can also request their own carer’s assessment.

Reablement and Promoting Independence

Before you have a needs assessment a period of reablement may be discussed with you. This is short term support from the Solihull Adult Social Care Promoting Independence team. The support is similar to rehabilitation and recovery. It can help you learn new skills, or regain and relearn any lost skills, to reduce your care needs.

The needs assessment process

You can request an assessment by contacting the Adult Social Care team by:

  • email to ccadults@solihull.gov.uk
  • calling: 0121 704 8007

When requesting an assessment you will need to have some details to hand, including your:

  • name
  • contact details
  • individual care needs

The assessment is conducted by a qualified assessor at a time and a place that is convenient to you. This is usually your own home but does not have to be.

The assessment will normally take place in private and face-to-face. However, it can also take place over the phone, but only if you are happy with that.

You are also able to carry out a self-assessment. If you would like to do this, Solihull Council Adult Social Care team will offer you as much support as you need. For example, you may want to talk to someone beforehand. You can also contact Solihull Connect, who will answer any questions and can put you in touch with support organisations if required. If you have difficulty understanding, remembering or using information, or have difficulty getting your point across, you may want to use an advocate. An advocate is someone who can help you be heard, represent you and support you with your assessment. For more information, you can:

  • read the Solihull MyLife Advocacy page
  • call Solihull Connect on 0121 704 8007

You can also visit your local hub for a supported self-assessment.

The assessment will focus on your emotional, mental and physical wellbeing, typically discussing:

  • your personal circumstances
  • the important elements of your life
  • what you want to achieve day-to-day

There will also be a financial assessment to see how much you can afford to help with paying for your care.

Social care services will also:

  • check that you live in Solihull or that you have your main home here. You  can take a look at the extra information below
  • draw up a personal care and support plan and estimated personal budget
  • if applicable, let you know what your contribution is to pay for your own care (which is also known as self-funding)

Resident of Solihull

This part of the process is to check that you live in Solihull with what is called ‘Ordinary residence’ status:

  • This looks at which council pays for your care
  • Understandably, it can be a complicated process
  • However, it is likely that you have lived in Solihull for most of your life and will not be affected by it

For further information, please take a look at:

  • the Think Local Act Personal jargon buster Ordinary residence definition
  • the Ordinary residence pages from the official GOV.UK website

Alternatively, you can ask the person completing your assessment with you for a full explanation of the Ordinary residence provision.

If you are not Ordinarily resident in Solihull, you will be supported by the social care team while we contact the appropriate Local Authority.

Save a copy of the assessment

You can download and open a file or document of your care and support assessment to your own computer. Selecting the links below will automatically start the download from the Solihull Council website. Some of these files are very large.

Once the file opens you can save or print it. Please remember some of these files are very large and may require a lot of paper and black and coloured ink to print successfully.
You can choose to:

  • download the Consent to Share Information document
  • download the How your budget is decided document
  • download the Full Review Assessment document
  • download the Overview Assessment document

After the assessment

Once your assessment has taken place you will be sent a copy of it. You will also be sent an estimate of your personal budget.

Your actual care and support budget will be confirmed once your personal plan has been put together. This allows the costs and care availability in your local area to be better understood.

Whether you can receive care and support from Solihull Council will be based on the results of your assessment against the outcomes in the national eligibility criteria.

Your care and support plan will be reviewed by Solihull social care services within 6 to 8 weeks.

Further reviews will take place a minimum of once every 12 months.

What if I do not agree with the assessment?

You will always have an opportunity to give feedback about your assessment and, if you disagree with the findings, you can ask for this to be looked at again.

If you do not meet the national eligibility criteria we will:

  • provide you with information and advice on what community care and support is available
  • discuss services which you can pay to receive
  • explore ways that you might find funding to pay for them
  • explain how the Solihull Directory and Solihull MyLife can help you find what you need

In the first instance, the reassessment should be completed by the person completing it with you the first time.

If you are still not satisfied, you can take the complaint to the Solihull Council Complaints Team.

To help with your complaint, you may find it useful to get support and advice from an advocacy service.
