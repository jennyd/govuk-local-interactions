Carers

If you provide unpaid support for someone because they are ill, frail, disabled or have a mental health condition or a substance misuse problem, you are a carer.

It does not matter how old you are or what your relationship is with the person you look after.

Ask us for a carers assessment where we can tell you about support to help you with your caring role. This might be from the council or other organisations.

What help can I get?

Use the links on this page to find out about support you may be able to get. This includes:

  • Carers allowance
  • Carers personal budgets
  • Council tax discount
  • Emergency help scheme

Salford Carers Centre

The Salford Carers Centre can help you access a wide range of services that could help make things easier for you. Carers have different needs and the centre can assist you with helpful advice, information or support in your caring role, or with concerns such as health issues, equipment, entitlements, carers personal budgets, carers assessments, practical or emotional support. You can also read the latest news in the Carers Newsletter.

All of the carers centre's services are independent, confidential and free to all carers across the city.
