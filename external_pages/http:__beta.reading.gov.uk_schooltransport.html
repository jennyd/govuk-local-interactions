Help with School Transport

We have a legal duty to provide free school transport for pupils when the nearest suitable school to your child's home is more than the statutory walking distance.

Questions

Close allOpen all

What is a 'suitable' school?

We have a legal duty to provide free school transport for pupils when the nearest suitable school to your child's home is more than the statutory walking distance.

What is the statutory walking distance?

The 'walking distance' is defined by The Education Act 1996 as two miles for a child less than eight years old and three miles for a child aged eight to school leaving age (ie. the end of year 11). The walking route may take into account footpaths and crossing points and may differ significantly from the distance recorded in a car.

What happens if my child does not attend the nearest suitable school?

If you choose to send your child to a school other than the nearest suitable school, Reading Borough Council does not have a duty to provide free school transport, regardless of the distance travelled.

Does my child quality for discretionary free transport?

Discretionary free transport may be available for:

  • Pupils with serious medical conditions (with supporting medical evidence from the Community Paediatrician)
  • Pupils who have a Statement of Special Educational Needs (conditional upon placement and need)
  • Pupils who attend suitable maintained denominational schools (with certified evidence from the religious body to which the parent belongs), and live beyond the statutory walking distance
  • Other exceptional circumstances (with supporting professional evidence).

Is there support for families on low incomes?

Children who receive Free School Meals or whose parents receive the maximum level of working tax credit will be entitled to transport assistance provided that the above criteria relating to distance and attendance at an appropriate school are fulfilled.

Proof of entitlement will need to be made before the start of each academic year. If benefit entitlement is withdrawn, transport assistance will continue to the end of the academic year and any further entitlement will be based on the general transport policy.

How do I apply?

You can apply by completing our Online Free School Transport Application Form. When the School Transport Service receives your application, we will send a letter to you confirming whether your application has been successful, or we may request supporting evidence.

Alternatively, you can  download an application form [108kb] and send the completed form to us by post. If you wish to request a paper copy from us, please contact the School Transport Service on 0118 937 2542.

Can I appeal the decision?

If your child is not offered assistance with transport, you can make an online appeal. Please read the  Criteria for Receiving Free School Transport [43kb] for guidance before submitting an appeal.

Is there any help available for post-16 students?

There is no legal entitlement to free home to school transport after Year 11.

However, transport assistance is provided for students attending a full-time course at Reading College if they had a Statement of Education Needs and their year 11 review clearly indicated that transport assistance would still be required for them to successfully access their course.

The  Transport Policy for Post 16 students [40kb] gives details of operators, agencies and concessionary fare schemes/discounts available in the Reading area.
