Conservation Areas

Conservation areas are designated for their ‘special architectural and historic interest’. They vary in character, form and size from a small group of buildings to a major part of a town, but their designation means that they are all worthy of protection as areas of special merit.

Ashford borough has 43 conservation areas. You can find where they are on our Interactive Map by clicking in the Conservation Area box and can zoom in to street level to see if your property is in one of these areas.

Conservation Area Reviews - Town Centre, KIngsnorth and Woodchurch

The council commissioned independent consultant Tony Fullwood Associates in August 2015 to undertake conservation area reviews in Ashford town centre, Kingsnorth and Woodchurch.

As part of the reviews, a new Conservation Area Appraisal and Management Plan will be prepared for each area, which provides a description of the historical development of the settlement and sets out the important features of the area that should be protected.

The benefits of the review are that it will provide a sound basis for decisions on any future developments, help to develop initiatives to improve the area and provide an educational and informative document for the local community. The boundaries of the conservation areas have also been assessed and revised.

The council is now seeking residents' comments on the draft appraisals and management plans of the three conservation areas. They are available to read on the 'Have Your Say' Consultations page and comments can be submitted up until Monday 21st December 2015.

An exhibition for each area’s review is also being held for residents that wish to come along to view the draft appraisals and management plans.

These are to be held:

  • Kingsnorth: Thursday 26th November 6-9pm – Kingsnorth Church of England Primary School Hall
  • Woodchurch: Friday 27th November 4.30-7pm – Woodchurch Memorial Hall
  • Ashford Town Centre: Saturday 28th November 10am -1pm – St Mary’s Church Hall

 

What impact does being in a conservation area have on my property?

Development in conservation areas is more strictly controlled than elsewhere, the intention being not to prevent change but to ensure that the main features of the area are conserved and that new development respects local character. You will need to apply for planning permission for demolition and for certain alterations which would normally be 'permitted development'. For example, planning permission is required when any of the following are proposed:

  • Erect or alter a building, such as a garden shed, in the curtilage of a dwelling house which is larger than ten square metres and more than twenty metres from the rear of the dwelling or sits beyond the side wall of the dwelling;
  • Install external cladding such as weather-boarding or false stone;
  • Install roof dormers.

This list is not definitive, so if you are in any doubt whether or not you need planning permission, please see our Making Planning Applications page.

Trees

Trees contribute greatly to the character and appearance of conservation areas and designation provides a general protection for all trees over a certain size within the area. View our Trees in Conservation Areas page for more information.
