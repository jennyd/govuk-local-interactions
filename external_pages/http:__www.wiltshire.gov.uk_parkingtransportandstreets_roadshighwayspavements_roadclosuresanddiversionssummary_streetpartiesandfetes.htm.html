Street Parties or Events on the Public Highway

Street parties and fetes to celebrate events are a traditional part of community life and are a great way for us to get to know our neighbours and meet members of our community.  

Wiltshire Council is keen to promote community activity where it can be accommodated conveniently and safely. With this in mind Wiltshire Council has adopted the following guidance and flow chart which is designed to make it straightforward for you to arrange and conduct these types of events.

Before making an application, A guide to organising a street party or small community event read our advice guide on organising events of this type. 292kb

The Wiltshire Public Events Safety Group has also produced a A Guide to Organising Safe Events from the Wiltshire Public Events Safety Group Guide to Organising Safe Events. 251kb

How do I make an application?

Having read through the guide, if you would like to hold a street party or fete you can use the Street party flow chart 114kb to identify:

  • How to apply,
  • What forms you need to complete and how to let the council know of your intended plans.

The number one tip for holding a party is to plan early, think about what you want to achieve and get in touch with the council 'at least 4-6 weeks in advance'.

More helpful tips, advice and support for organising a successful event can be found on the Streets Alive website
