Election information

Elections held in Harborough include:

Parish and district elections are held on the first Thursday in May, once every 4 years. View details for the district elections in May 2015 and the parish elections in May 2015.

County council elections are held once every 4 years. View details of the last county council election.

Parliamentary elections are held in response to Her Majesty the Queen dissolving Parliament and issuing a writ to hold an Election. The term of office of a Government is 5 years.  View details of the last parliamentary election. View details for the parliamentary elections in May 2015.

European Parliamentary elections are held every 5 years. View details of the last European Parliamentary election.

The administration of local elections within Harborough District is carried out by the Returning Officer at Harborough District Council.

Electoral register

Register to vote.

If you have not registered on the Electoral Register you will not be able to vote in any elections, polls or referendums.

You can check the Electoral Register at The Symington Building or by emailing e.services@harborough.gov.uk

There are 2 versions of the register of people that can vote:

  • Electoral register: includes everyone who has applied to be added, its use is very restricted and it can only be accessed by credit reference agencies, some government bodies and for political use
  • Open register: can be purchased by anyone for purposes such as mail shots; if you don’t want your details to be included on the open register you must tell us, email e.services@harborough.gov.uk
