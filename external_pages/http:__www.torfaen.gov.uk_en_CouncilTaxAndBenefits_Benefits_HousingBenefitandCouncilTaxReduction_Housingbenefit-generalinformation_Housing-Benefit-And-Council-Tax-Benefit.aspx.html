Housing Benefit and the Council Tax Reduction schemes

What is Housing Benefit?

The Housing Benefit scheme is run by local councils on behalf of the Government.

Housing Benefit is a benefit for people on a low income to help them pay their rent. You may qualify for Housing Benefit if you are in receipt of benefits, work part-time or work full-time and are on a low wage.

To get Housing Benefit you must pay rent to the council, a registered social landlord or a private landlord. You can also claim Housing Benefit if you are a joint-tenant, rent a room in a hostel or are a boarder.

You can not usually get Housing Benefit if you:

  • have savings of over £16,000
  • live in the home of a close relative
  • are a full-time student (unless you are disabled, have children or have a partner who is not a student )
  • are an asylum seeker or are sponsored to be in the UK

Help with your Council Tax

The Council Tax Reduction scheme replaces Council Tax Benefit from 1 April 2013.The UK Government has cut the funding for the new scheme by 10 per cent and devolved responsibility to the Welsh assembly Government.

The Council Tax Reduction scheme helps people on a low income pay Council Tax. You may apply whether you rent or own your home, or live rent-free. In Wales the maximum level of support that eligible claimants can receive will be 100 per cent of the Council Tax charge. This is in line with the previous Council Tax Benefit scheme and means that Council Tax payers will continue to get any benefit entitlement based on the full charge.

The amount of reduction you may receive depends on your income and household circumstances. You could qualify if you are in receipt of benefits or if you are working on a low wage. If you have savings of over £16,000 you can't normally get a Council Tax Reduction.

If you are entitled to a Council Tax Reduction, your Council Tax bill is reduced.

If you would like to know more information on Housing Benefit or the Council Tax Reduction scheme please contact us on 0800 028 2569 (Freephone). We will also be able to give you some idea whether you may qualify for one of these benefits.

Will I be entitled?

Use the Benefits Calculator to work out whether or not you will be entitled to Housing Benefit or a reduction in your Council Tax charge. Please note- this is only intended as a guide. Press ‘claim’ if you wish to make an application.

Making a claim for Housing Benefit and Council Tax Reduction

Claiming benefit may seem complicated so we have made it easier. 

Claim Online

You can make a claim online for Housing Benefit and Council Tax Reduction. Use the Benefits Calculator to make an application. 

We will then contact you to ensure we have all the information we need to assess your entitlement. 

Claim by Telephone 

You can now make a claim by phoning us on 0800 028 2569. We will need your name, address and contact phone number. We will be able to give you some idea of whether you will qualify for Housing Benefit and/or a Council Tax Reduction .We will then arrange to complete the claim with you either by personal interview at either Cwmbran or Pontypool Customer Care Centre.

Claim in Person 

Call in to one of our Customer Centres and make an appointment for a personal interview.

If you have difficulty getting into one of our offices or using the telephone we can visit you to complete the form. To arrange this contact us on 0800 028 2569.

Completing the Short Claim Form

You can print off and complete the short claim form. We may need to contact you for additional information and proof of income and capital

Further information is available in the Help with your rent and council tax leaflet.
