Council Tax Discount

How do I know if I qualify for a discount?

Council tax discount is not the same as Council Tax benefit (now replaced by Local Council Tax Support).
If you wish to apply for help paying your Council Tax go to the Local Council Tax Support (LCTS) webpage.

A standard Council Tax bill is based on an assumption that there are two adults living in a household. We can offer a 25% discount if you live alone, or if you or other people living with you do not count for Council Tax purposes (See below for a list of those who do not count for Council Tax purposes).

Alternatively read the Council Tax Discounts and Exemptions Explained webpage

Check if you get a discount

Follow these steps to work out if you should get a Council Tax discount.

  • Count the number of adults who live in your home as their main home.
  • Discount anyone in the list below
  • Your Council Tax bill will be reduced by 25% if you're left with 1 person who counts as an adult. in
  • Your bill will be reduced by 50% if this is your main home and you're left with no-one who counts as an adult.

Second homes and empty properties have different rules

See the Council Tax Discounts and Exemptions Explained webpage

Who are not counted?

Children and young people under 18 years of age are not counted for Council Tax purposes.
The following people do not count when we look at the number of adults living in a property:

  • 18- and 19-year olds who are at or have just left school or college;
  • full-time students, student nurses, youth training trainees;
  • apprentices training for a qualification and earning no more than £195 weekly;
  • people who live in hospital;
  • people who are being looked after in care homes;
  • people who are severely mentally disabled;
  • people living in certain hostels or night shelters;
  • care workers working for low pay, usually for charities;
  • people caring for someone with a disability who is not a partner, or a child under 18;
  • members of visiting forces and certain international institutions;
  • diplomats and their non-British husbands or wives;
  • monks and nuns, and;
  • People in prison (except those in prison for not paying Council Tax or a fine).

Please ask us for more information, if you would like help about how to decide if these categories apply to you or people in your household. (Email Council Tax).

How do I apply?

Please go to the Council Tax and Benefits Forms webpage or, if the application form you require is not available online or to download and print, telephone us on 01799 510510.

What happens when I apply?

We will send you a new bill showing your discount and the amount left to pay.
Until you get your new bill, you should pay in line with the one you already have.

When does it start?

Discount starts from the day you live alone or others in your household are no longer counted for Council Tax.

What if someone moves out or their circumstances change?

Please let us know if there are any changes that affect your discount. This includes where:

  • someone moves out
  • someone moves in
  • a trainee or student finishes a course or gives up training
  • a resident has to go into hospital for long term care
  • a young person becomes 18

For further information go to the Council Tax Discounts and Exemptions Explained webpage or read the  Council Tax Discounts and Exemptions Leaflet [67kb]

  • Notification of Change of Circumstances - online form

We have to check discounts regularly, so we may send you a review form asking you to confirm the details of people living in your home. If there is a change, we will adjust your Council Tax bill from the date of the change. If you don't, you may have to pay a fine.

New Council Tax Discount for Annexes

With effect from the 1st April 2014 there is a new Council Tax discount for Annexes occupied as part of the main home by the owner or where the annexe is occupied by a qualifying relative. The 50% discount will apply after any other discount or reduction is applied to the Council Tax liability. Please click on this link for more details.

Council Tax Flooding Relief

If you are a householder whose house has been flooded by the winter severe weather events you may consider asking your local authority for a discount from Council Tax. Please for more details.

Changes of circumstance

Any changes/corrections to your entitlement to any exemption or discount should be notified to the Council within 21 days. Failure to do so may result in a penalty fee of £70.

  • Report your change of circumstances online

Appeals

If you have not been granted a Council Tax discount and you believe this decision is incorrect you are entitled to you can appeal in writing to the Council and we will review your application. If you are still unhappy following this review you can appeal to the Valuation Tribunal who will undertake an independent review of your application. Details can be found on the Valuation Tribunal Services website Tel. 0300 123 2035 If you make an appeal you must continue to pay the instalments shown on your Council Tax bill until a final decision is made.
