Fostering

Sunderland City Council provides foster care for children and young people who cannot live with their parents.

  • Your introduction to fostering

    Children and young people could come into care for a short time during a family crisis or because it isn't safe for them to live with their own family.

  • Fostering options

    There are several different types of fostering available in Sunderland. This page gives more about them.

  • Interested in fostering?

    Sunderland has around 200 foster carers who care for children of all ages in their own homes as part of their family, on behalf of the council.

  • Watch a video

    Watch a video featuring three foster carers talking about their experiences of fostering in Sunderland.

  • Private fostering

    Private fostering is when a parent makes arrangements for their child to live with another family for a little while. If you are involved in or know about a private fostering arrangement in Sunderland, you must tell us about it.

  • Adoption

    Adoption is the legal process by which a child under 18 years gains new parents. Children in Sunderland need adopters who are willing to make a commitment to them for life, and who are: patient, flexible, realistic and with lots of love to give.

    Information about residential care and short breaks for disabled children that we provide

  • Sunderland Virtual School for Looked After Children

    Information about the personalised learning support we provide for looked after children

  • Information for foster carers

    This area of the website has been created to provide information for Sunderland City Council's foster carers.

  • Complaint in relation to Children's Services

    Complaints about Children's Social Care, including complaints about Social Workers, are classed as statutory complaints.
